## Scope

PAA 05-12 addresses misjoins at commands across the Marine Corps. A misjoin is a Marine joined in MCTFS to a unit other than the unit officially assigned by Manpower Management Division (MMEA) HQMC through the Web Orders System. MMEA identified 6,949 misjoins across the force at the time of release. The advisory restates that permanent movement between commands requires CMC authority (PCS or PCA orders); temporary movement uses TAD orders. Special orders are not authorized to move Marines between commands.

## Audience

- G-1 and S-1 staff joining inbound Marines in MCTFS.
- IPAC and servicing PAC personnel.
- Commanders monitoring unit accountability and staffing.
- 0102, 0111, and 0170 admin specialists.

## Content scope

- Misjoin definition and root cause analysis.
- Failure to execute orders cause and the MMEA non-compliance notification process.
- Untimely joins past the 5-day mandate cause.
- Special orders cause and the prohibition on using local special orders to move Marines between commands.
- Impact on unit staffing reports and HQMC staffing actions.
- Advisory directing PCS/PCA orders for permanent movement and TAD for temporary movement.

## Connection to higher policy

- MCO P1000.6G (Special Order Guidance).
- MCO 1300.8R (Permanent Change of Assignment and PCS Policy).
- ACTS Manual (pending revision at time of release reflected the special orders prohibition).
