91800.  BLENDED RETIREMENT SYSTEM – CONTINUATION PAY
(BRS-CP) (TTC 450/451/452/551) (ACTIVE COMPONENT (AC), ACTIVE RESERVE (AR),
& RESERVE COMPONENT (RC)

1. BRS-CP is
explained in MCBUL 1800 and MARADMIN(S) 543/17, 575/17, and 662/17.  Additionally, an annual Calendar Year
BRS-Continuation Pay (BRS-CP) MARADMIN is released each year with guidance and
specific updates for the given Calendar Year. 
DO/FO (Disbursing) related information is contained with the Financial
Management Policy (FPM).  Authorization
for BRS-CP payment is contained in the DODFMR, Vol 7A. Chapter 66.  Included within the MCBUL is the eligibility
criterion for opting into the Blended Retirement System, as well as defining
which population group of Marines are auto enrolled into the Blended Retirement
System.  Once a Marine has elected to
receive BRS-CP, and payment processing has occurred, this election is
considered to be irrevocable.  Marines
are subject to BRS-CP recoupment processing in the event four years of
obligated service are not completed/served in the appropriate component
(AC-Active Component, AR-Active Reserve, or RC-Reserve Component).  (I.E. If a Marine receives BRS-CP while
serving in the Active Component (AC) they are expected to complete four years
of obligated service in the AC.  If a
Marine receives BRS-CP while serving in the Active Reserve (AR) they are
expected to complete four years of obligated service in the AR.  Likewise, if a Marine receives BRS-CP while
serving in the Reserve Component (IMA or SMCR) they are expected to complete
four years of obligated service in the RC. 
(Note: IRR Marines are not eligible for BRS-CP.  Service in the IRR does not satisfy the obligated
service requirement for BRS-CP).  Failure
to complete the appropriate BRS-CP obligated service requirement (AC, AR, or
RC) will result in pro-rated recoupment processing unless being discharged for
reasons of Hardship, Medical Disability, or Death. 

2. The BRS-CP TTC’s
identified within are effective October 2018 with Software Release SR
2-18.   

3. The MCTFS 405 RMK
identifies BRS-CP election status as well as when BRS-CP credits/debits have been
expensed and/or liquidated.  Contained
within the MCTFS 405 RMK is the Bonus Award Level (Flat Rate Bonus Amount),
BRS-CP effective date (12 year Pay Entry Base Date Anniversary or PEBD plus 12
years), lump sum or installment payment indicator, BRS-CP Obligation Expiration
Date (last day of required obligated service to satisfy four year BRS-CP
agreement), BRS-CP Tax Code, installment payment amounts and anniversary
payment dates.  The following TTC’s
pertain to BRS-CP payment and recoupment processing:

450 001 (A) DECLINE

(NOTE:  BRS-CP SYSTEM GENERATED DECLINE ELECTION GUIDANCE
- In the event a BRS-CP TTC 450 SEQ 000 (CREDIT BRS-CP) or SEQ 001 (DECLINE
BRS-CP) is not reported to MCTFS, prior to the Marines 12 year PEBD anniversary
date, then MCTFS will issue a System Generated TTC 450 SEQ 001 (DECLINE BRS-CP)
transaction.  The System Generated TTC
450 SEQ 001 DECLINE BRS-CP transaction will in turn establish a D405 RMK with
D-APPROVE CODE equal ‘S’.  HQMC(MPO) is
the only Unit/RUC authorized to report DEL AS ERR TTC 450 SEQ 001 for a System
Generated BRS-CP Decline election (405 RMK D-APPROVE CODE equal ‘S’).  In the event an ADMIN RUC or IPAC attempts to
report DEL AS ERR TTC 450 SEQ 001 for a MCTFS System Generated Decline election
(D405 RMK D-APPROVE CODE equal ‘S’) the transaction will fail with the
following DFR/ERROR: “MR/D405 RMK REFLECTS SYSGEN BRS-CP DECLINE ELECTION.
CONTACT HQMC(MPO) FOR BRS-CP POLICY & TTC RPTING GUIDANCE”.  Marines are afforded one full year to make a
BRS-CP election.  If the Marine fails to
make a BRS-CP election, during this one year window of opportunity, and prior
to their 12 year PEBD Anniversary, then the Marine is not eligible to receive
BRS-CP.  Any questions regarding this
policy should be directed to HQMC(MPO) via email at MPO@USMC.MIL.)
