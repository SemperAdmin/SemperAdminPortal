## Scope

MCO 3000.13B establishes Marine Corps readiness reporting policy and procedures. The order implements CJCSI 3401.02B force readiness reporting and DoDD 7730.65, the DoD Readiness Reporting System, at the service level, and operationalized DRRS-MC reporting until MARADMIN 518-21 directed consolidation into DRRS. Readiness reporting is a commander's personal act, the order requires the commander's own assessment and remarks on every report, not a staff product signed unread.

## Audience

- Unit commanders assessing and signing monthly C-levels with remarks.
- S-3 operations officers running the reporting cycle and the T rating inputs.
- S-1 officers and chiefs feeding personnel readiness from MCTFS.
- Readiness Officers and SNCOs appointed in writing, and HQMC PP&O (POR) oversight staff.

## Operative content

- The monthly report carries commander remarks. A subjective override without written rationale is a violation the IGMC checklist looks for.
- Ratings run across the P, S, R, T, and C levels. Personnel readiness inputs flow from MCTFS, the T rating reflects T&R event completion against the METL.
- The commander appoints a Readiness Officer or SNCO in writing. Turnover delivers a binder with 12 months of records, the IGMC functional area 3000 checklist, appointment letters, and DRRS training certificates.
- Reporting continues through deployment, and individual augment departures and returns update the unit's personnel readiness state.
- Deployment sourcing feasibility validates against the DRRS personnel state before submission.

## Content scope

Readiness reporting framework, DRRS-MC architecture and reporting cycle, resource and training ratings, commander narratives and assessments, reporting cadence and reporting unit designation, and the cross-reference to GSORTS and joint force readiness.

## Connection to higher policy

- CJCSI 3401.02B. Force readiness reporting the order implements.
- DoDD 7730.65. The DoD Readiness Reporting System directive.
- CJCSM 3150 series. GSORTS reporting mechanics.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MARADMIN 518-21. Directs the DRRS-MC consolidation into DRRS.
- Commanders Readiness Handbook. The working companion for the commander's assessment.
- MCO 3120.12A and MCO 3000.18B. Global force management and force deployment planning, the demand side of the readiness picture.
- MCO P3500.72A and NAVMC 3500.3E. The T&R framework behind the T rating.
- MCTP 3-30G. Personnel administration doctrine feeding the personnel pillar.

Active. Signed 2020-07-14, sponsored by HQMC PP&O (POR). MCPEL lists MCO 3000.13B as Current with no C revision as of the 2026-07-15 check. The IGMC functional area registry's 2025-05-15 effective date belongs to the Unit Readiness checklist revision, not to the order.
