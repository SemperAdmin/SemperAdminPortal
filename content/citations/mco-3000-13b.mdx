## Scope

MCO 3000.13B establishes Marine Corps readiness reporting policy and procedures. The order implements CJCSI 3401.02B (Force Readiness Reporting), CJCSM 3150.02B (Global Status of Resources and Training System), DoDD 7730.65 (Department of Defense Readiness Reporting System), and the Defense Readiness Reporting System (DRRS) framework at the Marine Corps level. The order operationalized DRRS-MC reporting until MARADMIN 518-21 directed consolidation into DRRS.

## Audience

Marine Corps unit commanders. S-3 operations officers. S-1 officers and S-1 chiefs supporting personnel readiness inputs. DRRS-MC and DRRS reporting administrators. HQMC PP&O (POR) readiness oversight staff.

## Content scope

- Marine Corps readiness reporting framework.
- DRRS-MC architecture and reporting cycle.
- Resource and training readiness ratings (P, S, R, T, C).
- Personnel readiness inputs from MCTFS.
- Commander narratives and assessments.
- Reporting cadence and reporting unit designation.
- Cross-reference to GSORTS and Joint Force readiness.

## Connection to Marine Corps policy

- CJCSI 3401.02B. Joint Force Readiness Reporting.
- DoDD 7730.65. DoD Readiness Reporting System.
- MARADMIN 518-21. DRRS-MC Consolidation to DRRS.
- MCO 3120.12A. Global Force Management.
- MCO 3000.18B. Force Deployment Planning and Execution.
- MCO P3500.72A. Marine Corps Ground T&R Manual.
- Commanders Readiness Handbook.

## Status

Active. HQMC PP&O (POR) sponsors. MARADMIN 518-21 directs DRRS-MC consolidation into DRRS. Verify the current readiness reporting architecture against PP&O (POR) guidance.
