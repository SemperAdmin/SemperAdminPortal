## Scope

MCO 3574.2M, Marine Corps Combat Marksmanship Programs, signed 2022-04-06, governs rifle and pistol qualification for the Marine Corps. The order cancelled MCO 3574.2L, established the Annual Rifle Qualification (ARQ) as the controlling rifle course of fire for fleet Marines, and set the Combat Pistol Program (CPP) for pistol qualification. It also governs competitive marksmanship and specialty badges from national-level matches. Its cancellation section retires the End of Year Training Report (Report Control Symbol EXEMPT), enclosure (1) chapter 1 paragraph 2f.

## Audience

- Fleet Marines assigned a rifle or pistol as the T/O weapon, qualifying each fiscal year.
- Combat Marksmanship Trainers and Coaches running ranges and remediation.
- Unit S-3 and training sections entering scores in MCTIMS.
- S-1 and admin Marines verifying badge classifications post to MCTFS.

## Enclosure map

- Enclosure (1). Marine Corps Combat Rifle Program. Chapter 1 covers the fiscal-year cycle, exemptions, and score recording with re-qualification bars (paragraphs 4, 5.b, 5.f, 7). Chapter 2 covers initial qualification. Chapter 3 covers ARQ structure, classification, and re-evaluation (paragraphs 2.b, 6.d, 7).
- Enclosure (2). Marine Corps Combat Pistol Program, including who fires CPP (Chapter 5).

## Operative content

- Initial qualification at MCRD, School of Infantry, and OCS scores Tables 1 and 2 on a 350-point scale. Marksman 250-279, Sharpshooter 280-304, Expert 305-350, per Enclosure (1) Chapter 2.
- ARQ is a three-day event fired from the 500-yard line to the 15-yard line, per Enclosure (1) Chapter 3 paragraph 2.b.
- ARQ classification requires a minimum of 15 Destroys out of 50 plus at least one passing drill iteration. Expert is 43-50 destroys with one iteration in each drill type, Sharpshooter 31-42 with two, Marksman 15-30 with any, per Enclosure (1) Chapter 3 paragraph 6.d.
- Marines qualifying Expert receive a fiscal year exemption, per Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 5.f.
- All scores enter MCTIMS regardless of pass or fail, per Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 7. Classification flows to MCTFS through the Unit Diary.
- Units report rifle qualification data in MCTFS within 5 days of the effective date, per MCAAT Training checks and MCTFSPRIUM 5-51301.

## Connection to higher policy

SECNAV M-1650.1 Appendix 4A Tab 17 governs marksmanship awards, the Commanding Officer's awarding authority (paragraph b), and re-qualification bars (paragraph d).

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCRP 8-10b.2 and MCRP 8-10b.3. Combat marksmanship doctrine.
- NAVMC 3500.44D and NAVMC 3500.18D. Training and readiness.
- MCO 1650.19J w/ CH 1 and MARADMIN 077/25. Awards administration and current CMC delegation.
- MCO 6100.13A and MCO 1500.59A. Companion training-data orders in MCAAT checks, with MARADMIN 474/21.
- The Civilian Marksmanship Program and National Trophy Match procedures govern the competitive badge path through the Marksmanship Program Management Section at Weapons Training Battalion Quantico.
