## Scope

MARADMIN 613/25 provides advance notification of changes to the Marine Corps Physical Fitness Test (PFT) and Body Composition Evaluation (BCE) process. The message sets the framework for the waist-to-height ratio (WHtR) methodology effective 2026-01-01 and the transition away from height and weight as a body composition measure. MARADMIN 066/26, Change 1, amends the framework and carries the detailed implementation rules. Applicability is the Marine Corps Total Force.

## Audience

- Marines undergoing semiannual BCE. Apply the WHtR methodology. Undergo a body fat evaluation when exceeding the WHtR.
- Body composition evaluators. Apply the same-sex evaluator rule and the self-tensioning taping device.
- Commanders. Confirm Marines are evaluated under the revised process.
- Reporting officials. Apply the revised FITREP directed comments carried in Change 1.

## Announced changes, as amended by Change 1

- Effective 2026-01-01, the semiannual height and weight requirement is replaced with a WHtR methodology and a subsequent body fat evaluation for Marines exceeding the WHtR.
- Physical performance remains a consideration during body fat evaluation.
- During calendar year 2026 the Service continues to weigh Marines to assess the change. Weight is not used for body composition evaluation.
- Change 1 sets the WHtR standard at less than 0.52 regardless of sex and operationalizes it through a maximum waist measurement table.
- Marines exceeding both the WHtR and the maximum allowable body fat standard without a waiver receive an adverse FITREP under the directed comments in Change 1.

## Connection to higher policy
