## Scope

NAVMC 1720.1C carries the Marine Corps Suicide Prevention System procedures. The original prevention guidance lived in the MCO 1720 series and migrated to the NAVMC publication chain. The "MCO 1720.1" alias resolves to this entry so commander pages referencing the legacy MCO continue to render a live chip.

## What this directive is

NAVMC 1720.1C, MARINE CORPS SUICIDE PREVENTION SYSTEM PROCEDURES. Signed 2025-05-28 by M&RA (MF). Verified against the signed PDF on 2026-07-15.

## Stated purpose

> Suicide Prevention in the Marine Corps is executed in accordance with references (a) through (s) and this NAVMC. This NAVMC establishes procedures to support suicide prevention, intervention, and postvention efforts and to ensure consistency throughout the Marine Corps. This NAVMC details the activities involved in the DISTRIBUTION STATEMENT A: is unlimited.

Quoted from the document's opening section.

## Audience

Commanders signing suicide prevention program policy. Suicide Prevention Program Coordinators (SPPCs) appointed in writing. Force Preservation Council participants executing intervention and postvention steps. S-1 sections filing appointment letters and policy signatures.

## Operative content as used in the portal

- The commander oversight matrix carries a Suicide Prevention row under the legacy "MCO 1720.1" label. Summarized commander actions. Sign program policy incorporating SMITR functions. Appoint a uniformed SPPC in writing. The matrix marks its rows as IGMC FAC summaries pending a source-text walk.
- The commander battle rhythm page lists a suicide-related event as a trigger. Open the Suicide Response playbook with NAVMC 1720.1 follow-on actions, cited beside DoDI 6490.08.
- Commander FPC pages list "NAVMC 1720.1 Commander's Checklist for Response to Suicide-Related Events" among their references, pointing at the checklist enclosure role this NAVMC plays in postvention.
