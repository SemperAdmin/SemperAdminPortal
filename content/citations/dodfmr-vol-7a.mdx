## Scope

Volume 7A of DoD 7000.14-R, the DoD Financial Management Regulation, sets military pay policy for active duty and reserve component service members. The Office of the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller) publishes the FMR. Each chapter updates on its own cadence and carries its own effective date in the header. Every entitlement on a Marine's LES traces to a chapter of this volume, and most pay questions on this portal resolve to a specific paragraph of it.

## Audience

- Every Marine. Basic pay, allowances, deductions, and bonuses all live here.
- Disbursing clerks and pay technicians reading chapters directly on entitlement decisions.
- S-1 and IPAC personnel starting and stopping entitlements in MCTFS.
- Commanders approving special pays, advance pay, and certifying hostile fire pay.

## Chapter map

- Chapters 1 to 4. Basic pay, creditable service, and definitions. Chapter 1 carries the PEBD rules and rate tables.
- Chapters 5 to 24. Special and incentive pays. Chapter 8 SDAP, Chapter 9 enlisted bonuses, Chapter 10 hostile fire and imminent danger pay, Chapter 18 career sea pay, Chapter 19 foreign language proficiency bonus, Chapter 22 aviation incentive pays.
- Chapters 25 to 32. Allowances. Chapter 25 subsistence and basic needs allowances, Chapter 26 housing allowances, Chapter 27 family separation allowance, Chapter 32 advance, local, partial, and emergency pay.
- Chapters 33 to 52. Deductions, collections, and separations. Chapter 35 separation payments and leave sell-back, Chapter 44 tax withholding and the combat zone tax exclusion, Chapter 45 FICA, Chapter 47 SGLI, Chapter 48 court-martial sentences, Chapter 49 NJP, Chapter 50 bankruptcy, Chapter 51 savings programs including TSP, BRS, and flexible spending accounts, Chapter 52 priority of deductions.
- Chapters 55 to 58. Reserve pay. Chapter 57 reserve entitlements on active duty, Chapter 58 inactive duty training pay.
- Chapters 65 to 68. High-deployment allowance, continuation pay, CONUS COLA, OCONUS COLA and TLA.

## Operative content

Rules the portal leans on daily.

- Hostile fire pay is a flat 225 dollars a month, imminent danger pay prorates daily. Chapter 10.
- BAH dependency status changes the day of divorce and the day after other dependency events. Chapter 26.
- Accrued leave sells back against basic pay only, divided by 30, with a 60-day career cap. Chapter 35.
- Marine NJP forfeitures do not run concurrently. Chapter 49.
- Debt collection follows the 17 priority levels of Table 52-1. Chapter 52.
- An IDT period pays 1/30 of monthly basic pay, two periods maximum per day. Chapter 58.

## Connection to higher policy

- 37 USC. The statutory basis for pay and allowances, section by section under each chapter.
- 10 USC and 26 USC. Service statutes and tax code driving separations, forfeitures, withholding, and FICA.
- Annual NDAA public laws. Rate and authority changes flow into chapter revisions.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCTFS PRIUM. The reporting instructions turning these entitlements into transactions.
- MCO 5000.14D MCAP. The administrative structure executing them.
- MCO 7220.12R and MCO 7220.24P. Marine Corps SDAP and SRB implementation.
- MCO 4650.39A and the JTR. Travel pay rides Volume 9, entitlement interactions land here.
- MCO 1001R.1L MCRAMM. Reserve administration behind the Chapter 55 to 58 entitlements.

## Status

Active, revised continuously chapter by chapter. The externalUrl points at the canonical chapter index, individual chapter PDFs sit one level deeper. Chapter map and operative claims verified against the archived chapter PDFs on 2026-07-16, including the Chapter 10 hostile fire rate, Chapter 35 sell-back rules, and Chapter 58 one-thirtieth computation. Chapters revise independently, confirm the effective date in the chapter header before relying on a paragraph.
