## Scope

MCO 1616.1 establishes the Junior Enlisted Performance Evaluation System (JEPES). JEPES modernizes the way the Marine Corps identifies skills, performance, and future potential of Marines in the ranks of Private through Corporal. The order responds to the 2019 Commandant's Planning Guidance direction to change the way junior enlisted performance is captured.

## Audience

Reporting officials writing on Privates, Privates First Class, Lance Corporals, and Corporals. Reported-on Marines in the same ranks. NCOs and SNCOs supervising the reporting cycle. Commanders and OICs ensuring all reporting officials and junior Marines understand the spirit and intent of the order. Career planners and monitors using JEPES scores for retention decisions including the Commandant's Retention Program selection.

## Manual structure

The order establishes concepts and overview. Subsequent revisions add the detailed JEPES software help guide and periodic system upgrades.

- Chapter 1. Concepts and Overview. Scope, purpose, objectives of the system, key concepts, general responsibilities, evaluation cycle, authority.
- Chapter 2 and follow-on chapters. Reporting flow, system inputs, scoring framework, output products. Detailed software help guide ships as a subsequent revision.

## Concept

JEPES scores feed major retention decisions. The CRP uses JEPES retention scores to select up to 20 percent of a PMOS population for the streamlined retention pathway. Monitors and selection boards rely on the records JEPES produces for assignment and promotion-board context.
