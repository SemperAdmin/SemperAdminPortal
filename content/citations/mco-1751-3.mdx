## Scope

MCO 1751.3 publishes guidance and standard procedures for the Marine Corps Dependency Determination and Support Program. The order covers Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) entitlement determinations for dependents and Travel and Transportation Allowance authorizations tied to dependency status. Substantiating documents include marriage certificates, birth certificates, divorce decrees, and adoption decrees.

## Audience

S-1 and IPAC personnel making dependency determinations. Marines submitting NAVMC 10922 dependency applications. Commanders signing or designating attesting-officer authority for dependency determinations. The 0102 and 0111 MOS communities own the transaction-level work.

## Change history

CH-1 dated 1 July 2021. Two substantive changes.

- Chapter 1, paragraph 1.e. Admin personnel with authority to sign by direction now required to sign as the attesting officer.
- Chapter 1, paragraph 1.f. Added a time requirement for substantiating documentation.

## Connection to higher policy

- 37 U.S.C. Chapter 7. Statutory basis for the pay and allowances the dependency determinations feed.
- DOD FMR 7000.14-R Vol 7A Chapter 26. Sets the conditions of BAH entitlement. MCO 1751.3 supplies the procedural instructions for claiming BAH with dependents.
- DODI 1000.13. Dependency determinations under this order feed ID card eligibility for dependents.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCTFSPRIUM 8-80201. BAH reporting through TTCs 186, 187, 388, 389, and 896 executes the entitlements in MCTFS and points to this order for procedural instructions.
- NAVMC 10922. The dependency application form the order governs. Portal PAC pages on NAVMC 10922 processing, BAH with and without dependents, and personnel record audit source the order.
- MCO 5800.16 (LSAM). Carries the child custody information tied to dependency cases.

## Privacy posture

Misuse or unauthorized disclosure of PII exposes the discloser to civil and criminal penalties. The DON treats individual privacy as a personal and fundamental right protected during the collection, use, maintenance, and dissemination of dependency-related PII.
