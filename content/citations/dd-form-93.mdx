## What this form is

Form 93, Record of Emergency Data. Fillable PDF on file from the 2026-04-29 forms archive snapshot. The form is the single source for emergency notification and death benefit designations. It identifies the primary and secondary next of kin, the Person Authorized to Direct Disposition (PADD) of remains, the Death Gratuity designees, and the arrears of pay beneficiary.

## Purpose printed on the form

> This form is used by military personnel and Department of Defense civilian and contractor personnel, collectively referred to as civilians, when applicable. For military personnel, it is used to designate beneficiaries for certain benefits in the event of the Service member’s death. ## Who prepares, signs, and files

The Marine completes and updates the form through the unit S-1. MCO P1070.12K, the Individual Records Administration Manual, governs record administration. The portal's casualty affairs pages treat the form as the source for PNOK and SNOK identification during notification per MCO 3040.4 Chapter 4, and for PADD identification during mortuary affairs per Chapter 5 paragraph 4.

## When the form triggers and how it drives payments

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 36 paragraph 2.4.1, effective 2008-07-01, the Marine designates Death Gratuity recipients on this form in 10 percent increments. The 100,000 dollar Death Gratuity pays within 72 hours of death notification through this designation. Per paragraph 2.4.4, a bequest in a will does not constitute a legal designation. The form controls, and the Death Gratuity sits outside the estate. The form's SGLI section reconciles against elections made in SOES at milConnect. The arrears of pay beneficiary designation also lives on this form.

## Audit cadence

The IGMC casualty affairs checklist asks whether the command ensures all Marines update the form. The functional area guide directs an administrative audit of the DD Form 93 and SGLV 8286 at check-in, before temporary duty over 30 days, before every deployment regardless of length, before PCS departure, and triennially. Portal guidance adds an annual refresh and an update at every life event, since a stale form misidentifies the PNOK and delays payments.
