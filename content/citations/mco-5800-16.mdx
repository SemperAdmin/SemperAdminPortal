## Scope

MCO 5800.16 with changes 1 through 7 is the Legal Support and Administration Manual, short title LSAM, the Marine Corps legal services authority issued by CMC through the Judge Advocate Division. Seventeen volumes cover legal support structure, military justice, administrative law, and legal administration across the Marine Corps. The set replaced the LEGADMINMAN, MCO P5800.16A. Each volume signs and updates independently, so the manual behaves as a family of orders under one number.

## Audience

- Commanders exercising NJP, convening authority, and administrative separation powers.
- Legal clerks and S-1 personnel preparing the Unit Punishment Book and legal packets.
- Judge advocates, legal services specialists, and victims' legal counsel.
- Leaders navigating discipline documentation with the IRAM alongside.

## Volume map

- Volume 1. Legal Support Within the Marine Corps, the framework volume.
- Volume 2. Oversight of the Marine Corps Legal Community.
- Volume 3. Marine Corps Defense Services Organization.
- Volume 4. Marine Corps Victims' Legal Counsel Organization.
- Volume 5. Marine Corps Legal Assistance Program.
- Volume 6. International and Operational Law.
- Volume 7. Ethics.
- Volume 8. Gifts to the Marine Corps, Civil Litigation, and Claims.
- Volume 9. Dependent Support and Paternity.
- Volume 10. Indebtedness.
- Volume 11. Administrative Separations and Investigations.
- Volume 12. Command Responsibilities in the Provision of Legal Services.
- Volume 13. Compliance with Criminal Court Orders by Marines, Dependents, and Civilian Employees Serving Overseas.
- Volume 14. Enlisted Nonjudicial Punishment Matters and Preparation of the Unit Punishment Book.
- Volume 15. Officer Misconduct and Substandard Performance of Duty.
- Volume 16. Military Justice, 19 chapters, with Chapter 4 carrying the Victim and Witness Assistance Program.
- Volume 17. Judge Advocate Division Awards Program, which cancels MCO 1650.62.

Each volume resolves to its own registry entry with section maps and source excerpts.

## Operative content

- Volume 14 prescribes NAVMC 10132 as the Unit Punishment Book form, sets punishment limits by the grade of the imposing officer, and carries SMCR forfeiture formulas.
- Volume 16 Chapter 4 assigns VWAP responsibilities, RVWLO and IVWLO appointments, DD Forms 2701 through 2705 at each case stage, and quarterly reporting to the VWAP Director at JMJ.
- Volume 11 governs enlisted involuntary administrative separations support and administrative investigations.
- Volume 15 requires a verbatim transcript in officer NJP proceedings.

## Access

No public URL covers the full set. The externalUrl points at the combined marines.mil PDF. Pull current volumes from the MCPEL or the unit legal reference library.

## Connection to higher policy

- UCMJ, 10 USC Chapter 47, and the Manual for Courts-Martial. The military justice framework the LSAM administers.
- JAGINST 5800.7 JAGMAN. The Navy JAG manual the investigation volumes work beside.
- 10 USC 1044e. Statutory basis for the victims' legal counsel structure in Volume 4.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCO 5000.14D MCAP Enclosure 3. The commander discipline responsibilities the LSAM supports.
- MCO P1070.12 IRAM. NJP and UPB record entries cross-reference between the two manuals.
- MCO 1900.16 MARCORSEPMAN. Separation execution downstream of Volume 11.
- MCO 1001R.1L MCRAMM. Reserve discipline rides Chapter 11 with LSAM procedures.

## Status

Active with changes 1 through 7. Volumes signed individually between 2018-02-20 and 2021-08-26. Volume 1 opened the set on 2018-02-20. Confirm the volume's own sign date and change level before relying on a paragraph, the volumes revise on independent schedules.
