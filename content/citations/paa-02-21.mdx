## Scope

PAA 02-21 provides clarification to Unit Diary reporting requirements on personnel and units participating in the Unit Deployment Program (UDP) in accordance with MCO P3000.15B. UDP units use reach-back administration to the fullest extent possible. G-1, S-1, and Personnel Administration Center (PAC) personnel review the PAA to confirm appropriate entries are properly reported in a timely manner.

## Audience

- G-1 and S-1 staff running UDP unit diary entries.
- Personnel Administration Center (PAC) personnel processing UDP reports.
- Commanders establishing internal control procedures to capture and submit data elements to the servicing PAC.
- Reserve personnel admin desks reporting UDP participation.

## Content scope

- Personnel reporting instructions for deploying personnel.
- Personnel reporting instructions for personnel redeploying from UDP.
- Reserve personnel reporting instructions.
- Overseas Control Date entries.
- Best practices for commanders to support unit diary accuracy.
- MCTFSPRIUM paragraph 60610. STRENGTH CAT entries with TTC 112. Strength Category 4 (TAD over 30 days, not as student). Effective date is the date of departure from CONUS.
- MCTFSPRIUM paragraph 40902. START DEPLOY/OP ID entries with TTC 121. Deploy/OP ID Code (10-byte freeform), Type, MCC, RUC, From, To, Location, Description fields.
- Stabilization period reporting.

## Connection to higher policy

- MCO P3000.15B. Marine Corps Deployment Program order. Parent authority.
- MCTFSPRIUM. Source of paragraphs 60610 (STRENGTH CAT) and 40902 (START DEPLOY/OP ID).
- DODFMR Volume 7A Chapter 17. DoD pay framework for deployed personnel.
- MCO 1300.8. Marine Corps Assignment Policy.
- MARADMIN 481/03. Prior UDP guidance, in effect.

## Status

Released 2021. Source MISSA portal (CAC restricted). References (a) through (e) remain in effect. Distribution to all subordinate commands. Accurate and timely reporting directly impacts unit and personnel readiness.
