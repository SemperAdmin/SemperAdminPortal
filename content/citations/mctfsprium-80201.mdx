## Source text

1. The conditions of entitlement to BAH are described in the Department of Defense Financial Management Regulation (DoD FMR), Volume 7A, Chapter 26. Reserve Component-specific BAH entitlements are outlined in DoD FMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26, Section 2603. Procedural instructions for claiming entitlement to BAH with dependents are contained in MCO 1751.3 and Child Custody Information in MCO P5800.16. Entitlement to the various types of BAH is based upon pay or duty status, primary dependent status, Government quarter's status, pay grade, and location. See DoD FMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26 for Reserve Component BAH policies.

### Section 2

Every Marine is entitled to a type of BAH to pay a portion of their housing cost. The entitlement may be BAH Partial, BAH (formally BAQ and VHA) with or without dependents at the Military Housing area (MHA) locality rate, BAH RC/T (formally BAQ Own Right), BAH DIFF, and Overseas Housing Allowance (OHA). BAH Partial is automatically credited to the MMPA during the recruit accession process.  MCTFS BAH Rate tables have been expanded to allow unlimited retroactive reporting and processing to 1 November 1998.

Note: Reserves not on Active Duty and those on Active Duty for periods of duty for 30 days or less, are entitled to BAH RC/T unless ordered to Active Duty in connection with a contingency operation.

### Section 3

According to DoD FMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26, BAH is generally payable based on the member’s Permanent Duty Station (PDS). The Marine Corps defines a PDS as the MCC to which permanently assigned. Movement between MCC locations shall be conducted via PCS/PCA/LCM orders. RU's must pay particular attention to all personnel who are administratively joined, to ensure that the ZIP Code reported matches the location that the Marine was ordered to. The exceptions to this policy are for:

a. MCC 1UG (MMSL)

b. MCC M6A (MCLE)

c. MCC 077 (Intel)

d. MCRC RSS (Recruiting Sub-Stations)

e. RC members and Special Cases approved by HQMC (See Below)

- **Note 1: For further clarification on BAH at locations other than the permanent duty station, refer to MARADMIN 239/05.**
### Section 4

BAH Difference (BAH-DIFF). A Marine assigned to single-type quarters who is authorized BAH solely by reason of the Marine's payment of adequate child support, the member is entitled to BAH-DIFF. For additional information concerning BAH-DIFF, refer to DoD FMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26, Section 260202.

a. Public Laws 105-85 and 106-398 terminated the concurrent payment of BAH-Without Dependents and BAH-DIFF. Effective December 31, 2005, a member who is not assigned to Government Quarters and who is entitled to BAH-DIFF is entitled to BAH at the With Dependent Rate. BAH-DIFF will continue to be paid to members assigned to single type Government Quarters, who are entitled to BAH-DIFF based solely on the payment of child support.

b. For members living off base and receiving BAH-DIFF and BAH-Without, report Stop BAH Diff (TTC 186 001).  Then report CHAN BAH (TTC 388 001) at the With-Dependent Rate and the Locality ZIP Code that they are entitled.

c. Members not assigned to single type quarters, and receiving BAH-DIFF, are entitled to BAH at the With Dependent Rate. This BAH rate change was effective December 31, 2005.  As a result of this change, the BAH-RENT-OWN-CODE field in the 901 Remark is now the BAH-DIFF FIELD.  For the purposes of privatized housing (PAW) in the 901 Remark, the unused field BAH-OFFSET-DEDUCTION-AM will be used to display the BAH WITHOUT RATE.

### Section 7

BAH Rates During PCS Elapsed Time Period

**    **a. Marines with dependents receiving a BAH locality rate prior to detachment on PCS orders will continue to receive that rate during the elapsed time period. For those Marines that were occupying government quarters upon reporting TTC 186 STOP QTRS, MCTFS will automatically start BAH-RC/T.

b. When a member vacates Government quarters, the transferring command should report the BAH locality rate (old PDS) for that member. It is noted that close coordination between the Commanding Officer and Housing is essential. The joining command must review the member's record to determine if the member received the BAH locality rate for his old PDS. MCTFS will generate a BAH RC/T entitlement with the reporting of the STOP QTRS entry, however, the correct entitlement for the member is the BAH locality rate for the old PDS.  Determine if the rate in the member's record is BAH RC/T or BAH locality by accessing the website https://secureapp2.hqda.pentagon.mil/perdiem/bah.html using the ZIP Code for the old PDS, and entering the member's rank. Refer to PAAN 13-01 for additional information.

c. MCTFS will automatically stop BAH RC/T Without Dependent or Partial for Marines who were in receipt of BAH RC/T Without Dependent on the day prior to date of detachment.

d. BAH Entitlement-PCS when old and new PDS are inside CONUS. Marines are entitled to BAH at the old PDS locality rate beginning on the date of detachment through the day prior to reporting to the new PDS (without regard to a temporary duty assignment).  The only exception to this is if the member commutes daily to a temporary duty site from a permanent residence that will be occupied at the new PDS.  BAH for the PDS locality will begin the day after per diem has been stopped at the temporary duty site. This only applies when temporary duty is performed in the vicinity of a new PDS incident to a PCS when both old and new PDS are in the United States.

e. BAH Entitlement-PCS to or from outside CONUS. The following apply:

(1) A Marine ordered to an unaccompanied tour overseas is entitled to BAH based on either:

(a) The old PDS locality rate, provided the member was in receipt of BAH, through the day prior to arrival at the new PDS.

(b) A location designated by the member and approved by MMIA provided the dependents have a pre-existing tie to that location.

(c) on the dependent's location, not to exceed the BAH rate of the PDS immediately prior to the new PDS. If the member does not elect a designated location but moves dependents at no cost to the government, the housing allowance payable reflects the actual location of the dependents.

(2) A Marine accompanied by dependents to overseas area is entitled to BAH at the old PDS locality rate through the day prior to arrival at the new PDS.

(3) A Marine returning to CONUS from accompanied tour overseas is entitled to BAH-RC/T from the date of detachment overseas through the day prior to date of arrival at the new PDS. Upon arrival at the new PDS Marine is entitled to BAH at the new PDS locality rate. If the Marine has Temporary Additional Duty in route during the execution of the PCS orders the following applies:

(a) If the member performs Temporary Additional Duty in route at the new PDS, BAH for the new PDS begins the day of arrival in a 'Temporary Additional Duty' status at the new PDS.

(b) If the member performs Temporary Additional Duty in route at a location near, but outside the limits of, the new PDS and per diem stops, BAH for the new PDS begins the day the per diem stops.

### Section 8

Temp Visits. When a Marine is assigned to a permanent duty station outside the United States, and the member is authorized BAH for dependents residing separately from the member in the United States, and if the dependents visit at or near the member's PDS for more than 90 consecutive days, then the member is no longer entitled to BAH at the dependent locality rate.  Start BAH RC/T beginning on the 91st day.

### Section 9

Marines who are in the accession pipeline (date of entry through the day prior to reporting to first PDS) and are married prior to or during this period are entitled to BAH with dependent at the dependent Military Housing Area (MHA) locality rate.  If the Marine's dependents reside overseas, the BAH should be based on the Marine's duty station location. This entitlement will continue to change from duty location to duty location until the member arrives at his initial PDS.

### Section 10

A Marine in A Leave Status Awaiting Final Separation From The PDS Is Entitled to The Bah Locality Rate of The PDS.

### Section 11

BAH-RC Reserve BAH. If The Last PDS Was Outside The United States and The Marine Was on An Accompanied Tour The Marine Is Entitled to BAH-RC/T From The Date of Detachment From The Overseas PDS Through The Day Prior to The Arrival At The Processing Station.  After Arrival At The Processing Station The Marine Is Entitled to The Bah Locality Rate for That Processing Station. If The Marine Was on An Unaccompanied Tour Overseas Then They Would Receive Bah At The Dependents Location Through The Date of Discharge.

a.  A reserve member called or ordered to Active Duty for 30 days or less and not assigned to government quarters at the PDS (as defined by the DOD 7000.14-R Financial Management Regulation, Chapter 57) is entitled to BAH-RC/T.

b.  A reserve member called or ordered to Active Duty for more than 30 days (with no PCS authorized) and not assigned to government quarters at the PDS (as defined by the DOD 7000.14-R Financial Management Regulation, Chapter 57) is entitled to BAH-I at the Place Entered Active Duty (PLEAD) locality rate.

c.  A reserve member called or ordered to Active Duty for more than 180 days (PCS authorized) is authorized BAH/OHA for the PDS effective the day the member reports at that location. A reserve member called or ordered to Active Duty for more than 180 days when HHG transportation is not authorized is authorized BAH/OHA based upon the principal residency.

d.   A reserve member initially called or ordered to Active Duty for a period of more than 30 days, and orders are subsequently modified resulting in the performance of less than 30 days, the entitlement to BAH will continue through the date of release from Active Duty.

e. If a reserve member is initially ordered to Active Duty for a period of 180 days or more, and the orders are modified during the period of Active Duty, and the period of additional Active Duty, when coupled with the time remaining to be served, is 181 days or more, then the entitlement to BAH exists from the date of the modified orders. If a reserve member is initially ordered to Active Duty for a period of less than 20 weeks, and the orders are modified during the period of Active Duty, and the period of additional duty when coupled with the time remaining to be served is less than 20 weeks, then the member would remain entitled to BAH-RC/T.

f.  A reserve member called or ordered to Active Duty in connection with a contingency operation (regardless of the duration) is entitled to BAH. This entitlement only applies if the member maintains financial responsibility for the primary residence.

g.  To report a credit for a Marine's entitlement to BAH DIFF (Court ordered support for children) the reporting unit must contact the FO. The reporting unit cannot report a credit for BAH DIFF.

h.  To check BAH RC/T that was previously reported by CRED BAH, report the DEL AS ERR transaction for TTC 896 sequence 002.

- **Note 1: The period of BAH-RC/T entitlement being reported must equal or include dates within a previously or concurrently reported Active Duty period.**
i. Entitlement to partial BAH is automatic for Reservists not entitled to BAH with dependents, or in their own right, except under certain conditions. Specific conditions of entitlement are contained in the DOD 7000.14-R Financial Management Regulation, Volume 7A.

(1) A Reservist's entitlement to partial BAH will be automatically established in MCTFS upon processing the proper join transaction reported by a Selected Marine Corps Reserve, Individual Mobilization Augment or Mobilization Training Unit. Partial BAH will be automatically credited to the account of an Individual Ready Reserve, not a member of a Mobilization Training Unit, when Active Duty is reported per Chapter 7 of this manual.

(2) The applicable increase or decrease of the partial BAH rate occurring from promotion or reduction of pay grade is accomplished via computerized processes.

(3) Entitlement to partial BAH is automatically terminated upon processing of the entry reporting commencement of BAH on behalf of dependents and reestablished when BAH in behalf of dependents is terminated. Entitlement to partial BAH is automatically suspended upon processing a credit of BAH own right in conjunction with an Active Duty period.

j. When a Reserve Marine is mobilized or ordered to Active Duty for 31 days or more, the member's BAH entitlement data resident in MCTFS reserve record will automatically transfer to the member's MCTFS active record.

### Section 18

Refer to DoD FMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26 to determine the proper entitlement to BAH when a Marine and spouse are members of the United States Armed Forces on AD.

### Section 19

Personnel at home awaiting Physical Evaluation Board (PEB) proceedings.  A Marine ordered home to await the results of a PEB is entitled to the same BAH as if ordered to a new PDS (That is, place to which ordered is treated as the new PDS) based on no government quarters available for assignment at the place to which ordered.

### Section 20

Bah Rate Protection.  Member's BAH is rate protected to the locality rate in effect the date they reported to a new PDS.  This BAH rate is protected until the member's status changes as follows:

a.  Member performs a PCS to a new MHA.

b.  Member is reduced in pay grade.

c.  Member has a change of dependency status from with dependent to without dependent or from without dependent to with dependent.

### Section 22

Member Married to Member. Effective 1 July 1999, When Two Service Members Married to Each Other Are Assigned to The Same or Adjacent Base, Only One Member Is Entitled to Receive Bah At The With Dependent Rate. The Other Member Is Entitled to Bah At The Without Dependent Rate. This Applies to Members Married to Members Residing in The Same Household or Stationed in The Same Vicinity. Service Members Married to Other Service Members Who Meet The Conditions Below, Must Contact Their Personnel Office Concerning A Change in Entitlement. The Members Must Decide Which Member Can Claim The Dependent(S) in Order to Receive Bah At The With Dependent Rate. If Members Cannot Decide, The Higher-Ranking Member Is to Claim With Dependents. If The Entitlement to BAH Was Reported by The Commandant of The Marine Corps (MMRP), The Personnel Office Must Coordinate With Headquarters Marine Corps Regarding The Stopping or Starting A Member's Entitlement (Refer to The DOD 7000.14-R Financial Management Regulation, Volume 7A, Chapter 26). These Are The Conditions That Will Require An Entitlement Change:

a. Both members receiving BAH with dependents, one is claiming a dependent child (children) and/or an approved parent. If the spouses are co-located (same or adjacent duty stations) the Marine receiving BAH without dependent in addition to BAH-DIFF must have BAH-DIFF terminated.

b. One member is receiving BAH with dependents and the other member is receiving BAH without dependents and BAH difference (for child support).

c. Members who have a dependency status change from the with dependent rate to the without dependent rate or from without dependent rate to with dependent rate lose the rate protection. Therefore, if member married to member decide to flip-flop their dependents, both will lose the BAH rate protection.

### Section 23

Confinement. The Below Paragraphs Indicate Entitlement to or Not to Receive Bah While Member Is Confined.

a. Members with dependents who are entitled to basic pay and in receipt of BAH with dependents at PDS locality rate prior to entering a confined status, to include those members in pre-trial confinement (that is, confined awaiting higher authority, or confinement ATLSCM, confinement ATLSPCM, and confinement ATLGCM). These members are entitled to BAH at the with dependent rate while in confinement, unless forfeiture of all pay and allowances was awarded in the court martial sentencing. If total forfeiture of pay and allowances is awarded, the member is not entitled to any BAH.

b. Members without dependents are not entitled to BAH without dependents while confined (to include the categories of Pretrial confinement stated above). These members are entitled to BAH-partial while in confinement unless forfeiture of allowances was directed.

c. Members listed in paragraphs a and b above can retain their BAH for the period of confinement if they are acquitted, the charges withdrawn, or the sentence set aside or disapproved. This is provided the member's sentence did not include total forfeiture of all pay and allowances.

d. Those members that are living in the barracks and receiving BAH-Partial prior to entering a confined status will continue to receive BAH-Partial while in confinement unless forfeiture of allowances was directed (DoDFMR Volume 7A, Chapter 26 and PAAN 14-03 applies).

### Section 24

The Bah Entitlements Will Be Automatically Adjusted or Terminated When The Following Are Reported:

a. Change of pay grade (promotions/reduction or, changes between officer and enlisted).

b. When STRT QTRS is reported, BAH entitlement will automatically be stopped, effective the date of assignment to quarters.

c. When TTC 020 is reported and the Marine's MCTFS record contains an active BAH entitlement at the with dependents rate, BAH is automatically stopped at the old duty station rate on the join Effective Date minus 2 days, and the BAH will be automatically started at the new duty station rate on the join Effective Date minus 1 day.
