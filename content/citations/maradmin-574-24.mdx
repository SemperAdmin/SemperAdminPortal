## Scope

MARADMIN 574/24 publishes the implementation details for transport or shipment reimbursement of human breast milk expenses during a Permanent Change of Station (PCS). Eligible Service Members receive up to $1,000 reimbursement per PCS. The message implements Section 626 of the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023 and is effective immediately upon publication on 2024-11-26. Applicability is the Marine Corps Total Force.

## Audience

- Active duty Service Members who have given birth within the prior 12 months and are executing PCS. Eligible.
- S-1 staff and Defense Travel Administrators. Endorse PCS orders and process vouchers.
- Disbursing officers. Pay reimbursement claims.
- Commanders. Brief the entitlement during PCS counseling.

## Reimbursement rules

- The cap is $1,000 per PCS. The cap covers total shipment-related expenses, not a per-shipment amount.
- Eligible expenses include commercial shipping fees, disposable storage bags, cold shipping packages, refrigeration, excess baggage fees, and dry ice or ice.
- The reimbursement covers shipping expressed human breast milk from the Marine's PCS travel location to a designated recipient, typically the dependent infant at a separate location during the move.
- The reimbursement does not extend to unaccompanied tours. Unaccompanied OCONUS tours and dependent-restricted assignments fall outside the framework.
- Authorization flows through the PCS orders endorsement via the MOL Outbound Interview Module. The Marine secures the endorsement before reimbursement processing.
- The Marine submits the voucher through DTS within 5 working days of the completed shipment. The window starts the day the shipment is delivered.
- JTR Chapter 2, Table 2-24, Item 20 carries the entitlement.
