## Scope

MCO 1070.1 sets the policies, procedures, and responsibilities for the Official Military Personnel File (OMPF) across the Marine Corps. The OMPF is the authoritative record of a Marine's service. During service it drives assignment, training, retention, advancement, and separation decisions. After service it protects the legal and financial rights of veterans, their families, survivors, and the United States government.

## Audience

Every Marine owns the currency of their OMPF. Commanders assist with upkeep, review, and content. Manpower Management Records and Performance Branch (MMRP) creates and maintains the file at HQMC. S-1 and IPAC personnel upload documents during the service lifecycle.

## Concept of operations

- Marines ensure their OMPF is current and contains documents authorized for the file that adequately describe their Marine Corps service.
- Through MMRP, the Director, Manpower Management Division, and commanders assist Marines in the upkeep, review, and content of the OMPF.

## MMRP responsibilities

- Create and maintain the OMPF.
- Determine the contents of the OMPF and the documents authorized for inclusion.
- Provide records-management oversight for the Manpower Information Systems Support Office.
- Approve additions to the document inventory when deemed necessary and appropriate.

## Privacy posture

All collection, use, maintenance, or dissemination of PII complies with the Privacy Act of 1974, 5 U.S.C. 552a, and SECNAVINST 5211.5E. The order treats unwarranted disclosure as a record-management failure with potential civil and criminal exposure.
