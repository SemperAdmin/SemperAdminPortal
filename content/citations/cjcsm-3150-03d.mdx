## Scope

CJCSM 3150.03D established the Joint Reporting Structure (JRS) requirements for event and incident reports, specifically the OPREP-3 (Operational Report 3) reporting system. The manual defined flag word categories for OPREP-3 reports, established national-level interest thresholds triggering PINNACLE-level reports to the National Military Command Center (NMCC), and prescribed the serious incident report (SIR) criteria for significant but sub-PINNACLE events. The manual provided the standard OPREP-3 message format and flag word list used by commanders at all levels.

## Status

CJCSM 3150.03D is no longer listed as a current publication in the CJCS Directives system as of 2026. The document was not replaced by a direct successor in the 3150.03 series. OPREP-3 reporting requirements may now be addressed within CJCSM 3135.01 (Joint Planning and Execution System Reporting Structure Governance) or through current theater/component-specific reporting directives. Marine Corps portal content that cites CJCSM 3150.03D refers to OPREP-3 flag word and threshold rules previously codified in this manual.

## Connection to Marine Corps Policy

- NAVMC 3500.3E T-R event 0102-OPER-1101 Supervise Operations Event Incident Reporting.

## OPREP-3 Framework (Historical Reference)

OPREP-3 reports fall into two primary categories:

- PINNACLE. National-level interest events requiring immediate report to the NMCC within 15 minutes of commander awareness. Flag words include NUCFLASH, BROKEN ARROW, BENT SPEAR, DULL SWORD, EMPTY QUIVER, FADED GIANT, BLEEDING EDGE, LAME DUCK, HELPING HAND, and others tied to specific event types.
- SIR (Serious Incident Report). Significant events below the national-level interest threshold. SIRs route through the operational chain of command. When in doubt whether a threshold is met, commanders escalate to PINNACLE rather than SIR.
