## Scope

32 CFR Part 199 prescribes guidelines and policies for the administration of the Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Uniformed Services (CHAMPUS), now operating under the TRICARE brand. The regulation covers eligibility, benefits, claims, appeals, dental program, provider reimbursement, and program-specific extensions such as the Extended Care Health Option and Continued Health Care Benefit Program.

## Audience

Marines, family members, and survivors using TRICARE health coverage. S-1 personnel processing DEERS enrollment and TRICARE eligibility actions. Tricare beneficiary representatives at command level. Casualty Assistance Calls Officers (CACOs) advising surviving family on TRICARE continuation.

## Authority

The regulation is issued under 5 U.S.C. 301 and 10 U.S.C. Chapter 55. Source. 51 FR 24008, July 1, 1986. Applies geographically within the 50 States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, US possessions and territories, and all foreign countries unless specific exemptions are granted in writing by the Director, OCHAMPUS, or a designee. Applies throughout DoD, the Coast Guard, the Public Health Service, and NOAA Commissioned Corps.
