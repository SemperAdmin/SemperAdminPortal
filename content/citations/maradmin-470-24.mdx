## MARADMIN 470/24: Strategic Location Incentive Program (SLIP)

**Issued**: 1 October 2024  
**Effective**: 1 October 2024  
**Classification**: GENADMIN (General Administrative)

## Purpose

SLIP is an assignment and retention initiative for career enlisted Marines who accept orders to difficult-to-fill locations or critical billets in exchange for an agreed-upon duty station preference for their next assignment.

## Key Details

- **Program Origin**: SLIP was the winning crowd-sourced submission in MARADMIN 643/23 (Manpower Modernization Challenge II).
- **Orders Eligible**: All orders written after 1 October 2024 may be eligible.
- **PMOS Monitor Discretion**: Individual Primary Military Occupational Specialty monitors determine which assignments qualify as "difficult to fill" or "critical."
- **Program Focus**: Overseas locations, remote CONUS assignments, and billets requiring special training or qualifications.

## Eligibility Requirements

Enlisted Marines must meet ALL of the following:

1. Completed initial enlistment and eligible for subsequent reenlistment in Zones B, C, or D.
2. Not identified for Headquarters Marine Corps Special Duty Assignment Screening Team (HSST) or slated for special duty or screenable billets at time of SLIP orders.
3. Meet general eligibility requirements per MCO 1300.8, including time-on-station requirements.
4. Must not have RE3O reenlistment code.
5. Must obligate sufficient service to complete orders per Joint Travel Regulations.

## Program Mechanics

**Draw Case Code (DCC)**: Marines accepting a SLIP assignment receive a DCC of "DS" in the Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). This code:
- Stabilizes the Marine for the initial tour and follow-on tour.
- Prevents participation in other special programs until completion.

**Follow-On Assignment**: Upon successful completion of the SLIP-eligible assignment, Marines are assigned a follow-on duty station based on preferences agreed upon in their original SLIP orders.
