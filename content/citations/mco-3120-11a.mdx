## Scope

MCO 3120.11A, Marine Corps Parachuting Policy and Program Administration, signed 2014-12-17, establishes policy and procedural guidance for administering Marine Corps parachuting programs. Department of Defense executive agents guide individual service parachuting programs in accordance with references (a) through (aj) of the order. Reference (a) assigns the U.S. Army as the executive agent for all airborne matters common to both the Army and the Marine Corps, and the order takes precedence where the Army framework does not support Marine Corps equipment and doctrine. For the S-1 community the order is the substantiation authority for parachute duty (jump) pay.

## Audience

- Commanders of units with parachute billets, who endorse jump pay substantiation.
- Unit training sections maintaining jump logs and assignment orders.
- S-1 and IPAC Marines (0102, 0111, 0170) reporting jump pay in MCTFS.
- Reporting and supporting unit admin sections exchanging key supporting documents.

## Chapter map

Repo evidence names two chapters.

- Chapter 5 and Chapter 6. Jump pay eligibility, reporting, and documentation requirements, cited across MCAAT special and hazardous duty pay checks.
- Chapter 6 paragraph 9.e. Supporting unit submission timeline for jump pay documents.

## Operative content

- Jump pay reports in accordance with DoDFMR Volume 7A Chapter 24 paragraphs 2402 and 2403 with Tables 24-1, and MCO 3120.11A Chapters 5 and 6. MCAAT audits this at the IPAC and the reporting unit.
- Key supporting documents validating jump pay are the assignment order, the unit command endorsement, and the unit jump logs. Units maintain them on file for audit under retention policy per DoDFMR Volume 1 Chapter 9 and SECNAV M-5210.1.
- Supporting units submit jump pay substantiation to the reporting unit no later than 5 days after the end of the month, per Chapter 6 paragraph 9.e and MCTFSPRIUM 1-10100.6 and 2-20103.
- MCTFS reporting runs through MCTFSPRIUM 9-90101, Table 9-4.

## Connection to higher policy

DoD executive agency for airborne matters sits with the U.S. Army under reference (a). DoDFMR Volume 7A Chapter 24 sets the parachute duty HDIP entitlement the order substantiates. MCO 1000.6 Chapter 1 Section 2 paragraphs 4 through 6 govern the related assignment orders.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCAAT Special and Hazardous Duty Pay program, sponsor DCS M&RA. Audits jump pay at IPAC, reporting unit, and supporting unit levels.
- MARADMIN 601/24. Appears alongside the order in MCAAT jump pay documentation checks.
- MARADMIN 366/09, signed 2009-06-16. Announced publication of the base order MCO 3120.11.
- MCTFSPRIUM. MCTFS reporting procedures for jump pay transactions.
