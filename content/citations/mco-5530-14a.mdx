## Scope

MCO 5530.14A is the Marine Corps Physical Security Program Manual. Signed 2009-06-05. The order constitutes the Marine Corps Physical Security Program, prescribes policy, assigns responsibilities, and presents requirements for securing property and places under command authority. The document's opening section states:

> Physical security is a primary command responsibility. Reference (a) requires commanders to establish and issue regulations for the security of property and places under their command. This Order constitutes the Marine Corps Physical Security Program and prescribes policy, assigns responsibilities, and presents requirements. ## Audience

- Commanders at every echelon. Physical security is a primary command responsibility under this order.
- Physical Security Officers appointed in writing by the commander.
- Disbursing and finance officers whose facilities safeguard public funds.
- Arms, ammunition, and explosives (AA&E) account holders and armory personnel.

## Operative content

- The commander appoints a Physical Security Officer and approves and annually reviews the organization-specific Physical Security Plan, per the commander oversight matrix for the Physical Security functional area.
- The disbursing facility, safeguarding of public funds, security equipment such as alarm systems, and security procedures are inspected in accordance with DODFMR Volume 5 Chapter 3 paragraph 030302.B and MCO 5530.14A Chapter 3, per the MCAAT finance and administrative management checklist.
- At change of command, the outgoing commander reconciles a 100 percent serialized AA&E inventory by serial number, lot, and DODIC and provides TAMIS ammunition plan status, per the commander turnover checklist citing MCO 5530.14A with MCO 8023.3 and MCO 5100.29C Volume 8.
