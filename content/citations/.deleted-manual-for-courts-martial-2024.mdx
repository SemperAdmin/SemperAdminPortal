## Scope

The Manual for Courts-Martial United States (2024 Edition) sets the procedural rules implementing the Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ). The Manual covers preliminary proceedings, charges and specifications, pretrial restraint, trial procedures, sentencing, and post-trial action. Every court-martial across the Department of Defense applies these rules.

## Audience

Convening authorities. Military judges. Trial counsel. Defense counsel. Staff judge advocates. Commanders processing courts-martial. S-1 officers and legal clerks supporting courts-martial administration.

## Structure

- Part I. Preamble.
- Part II. Rules for Courts-Martial (RCM). Procedural rules covering charges (RCM 307), disposition (RCM 401-407), preliminary hearings (RCM 405), pretrial restraint (RCM 304-305), trial procedure, sentencing, and post-trial action (RCM 1106).
- Part III. Military Rules of Evidence (MRE).
- Part IV. Punitive Articles. UCMJ offenses and elements.
- Part V. Nonjudicial Punishment Procedures. Maximum punishments (paragraph 5), appeals (paragraph 7d), and procedural rules.
- Appendices. Forms and procedural support.

## Connection to Marine Corps policy

- MCO 5800.16 Volume 14. Enlisted NJP procedures implementing MCM Part V.
- MCO 5800.16 Volume 16. Marine Corps military justice procedures implementing MCM Part II.
- JAGINST 5800.7G. Manual of the Judge Advocate General cross-references the MCM.
