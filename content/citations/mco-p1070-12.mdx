## Scope

The Marine Corps Individual Records Administration Manual (IRAM) is the authoritative guide for Marine Corps records administration. The manual governs the Service Record Book (SRB) for enlisted Marines and the Officer Qualification Record (OQR) for officers. It sets the rules for what documents file inside the OQR/SRB, how the field folder gets maintained, and how Marine records move through the personnel system.

## Audience

Records clerks, unit administrators, IPAC personnel, and any Marine leader responsible for maintaining individual records. The 0102, 0111, and 0170 MOS communities use the IRAM daily. Officers reference it for OQR maintenance. Enlisted Marines reference it for SRB transparency.

## Key chapters

- Chapter 1. Field Folder, OQR, and SRB structure. Table 1-4 lists every document filed inside the field folder.
- Chapter 2. General care and maintenance of the OQR and SRB.
- Chapter 3. OQR-specific filing requirements and standard pages.
- Chapter 4. Unit Punishment Book (UPB) procedures. Paragraph 4007 carries NJP entry instructions. Cross-references MCO 5800.16 (LEGADMINMAN, now LSAM) Paragraph 3006 for detailed preparation rules.
- Chapter 6. Authorized awards and decorations. Table 70 lists approved entries.

## Change history

CH 1 published 31 May 2002 via MARADMIN 301/02. Replaced NAVMC 118(12) (SRB Page 12) with NAVMC 10132 (Unit Punishment Book) and modified UPB procedures.

## Reserve applicability

Applies to the Marine Corps Reserve.

## Notes

The manual does not amend Navy Regulations or Navy Department General Orders. The IRAM operates inside the framework those sources define.
