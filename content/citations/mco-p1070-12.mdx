## Scope

MCO P1070.12K with CH 1, the Individual Records Administration Manual, known as the IRAM, is the authoritative guide for Marine Corps individual records administration. The manual governs the Service Record Book for enlisted Marines and the Officer Qualification Record for officers, sets the rules for what documents file inside each, and directs how records open, move, and close across a Marine's service. The manual applies to the Marine Corps Reserve. Instructions in the manual do not amend Navy Regulations or Navy Department General Orders.

## Audience

- Records clerks, unit admins, and IPAC personnel. 0102, 0111, and 0170 admin specialists work from it daily.
- Leaders drafting Page 11 counseling entries and verifying record accuracy for their Marines.
- Commanders directing NJP documentation and record entries with legal effect.
- Every Marine. The record documents your service, and the IRAM defines what belongs in it.

## Chapter map

The manual carries seven chapters, verified against the signed K revision on 2026-07-15.

- Chapter 1. Contents of Headquarters Files. The OMPF folder structure. Table 1-1 Service Folder, Table 1-2 Commendatory/Derogatory Folder, Table 1-3 Performance Folder, Table 1-4 Field Folder OQR/SRB, Table 1-5 Health Folder.
- Chapter 2. Personnel Identification Information and General Instructions on the Care and Maintenance of the OQR and SRB. Paragraph 2007 covers closing and distribution of records. Table 2-1 carries the instructions for transfer of records.
- Chapter 3. Officer's Qualification Record (OQR). Paragraph 3005 carries the OQR Page 11 administrative remarks instructions.
- Chapter 4. Service Record Book (SRB). Paragraph 4005 carries the Record of Service, since superseded for marking by JEPES under MCO 1616.1. Paragraph 4006 carries the SRB Page 11 administrative remarks instructions. Paragraph 4007 carries the Unit Punishment Book (NAVMC 10132), reshaped by CH 1.
- Chapter 5. Audit of Records.
- Chapter 6. Abbreviations and Acronyms.
- Chapter 7. Identification Tags.

## Operative content

- Page 11 discipline. The IRAM gives the Marine a rebuttal right within a set timeframe, requires signature or a refusal-to-sign annotation, and is explicit on exclusions. Hearsay, unverified allegations, off-duty conduct without a command nexus, undocumented counseling, and adverse opinion absent fact stay out.
- The record split. The OMPF is the permanent HQMC-held file, pulled through the Online Records Management Application. The SRB and OQR are the local working records. The IRAM controls what files where and when originals route to the OMPF through the IPAC.
- Change 1. Published 2002-05-31 through MARADMIN 301/02. Replaced NAVMC 118(12), the SRB Page 12, with NAVMC 10132, the Unit Punishment Book, and modified UPB procedures.
- Interim changes ride Pay and Personnel Administrative Advisories. PAA 05-23 clarifies K-revision provisions, PAA 07-10 changes paragraphs 4006.3d, 5000.3, and 5002, PAA 09-11 changes paragraph 4006.3E to align with the Promotion Manual.

## Connection to higher policy

- MCO 5000.14D MCAP. Assigns the administrative structure the IRAM's record work runs inside.
- MCO 1070.1. The OMPF order the IRAM's filing rules feed.
- SECNAV M-5210.1 and MCO 5210.11. Records management and disposition authority over the documents the IRAM creates.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCO 5800.16 LSAM. NJP and UPB preparation rules cross-referenced from the discipline entries.
- MCTFS PRIUM. The reporting instructions for the same events the record documents.
- MCO 1616.1 JEPES. Superseded the paragraph 4005 marking system.
- MARADMIN 015/20. OMPF document submission requirements layered over the filing rules.
