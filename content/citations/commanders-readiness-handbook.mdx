## Scope

The Commanders Readiness Handbook is the Marine Corps quick-reference guide for unit commanders on readiness reporting requirements. The handbook covers Marine Corps readiness reporting structure, statutory responsibilities, force readiness ratings (P, S, R, T, C), command readiness reporting cadence, report content, commander narratives, and assessment frameworks. The handbook complements MCO 3000.13B and DRRS reporting procedures. Portal readiness pages treat it as the working companion for the commander's assessment, distinct from the order itself.

## Audience

Marine Corps unit commanders. S-3 operations officers. DRRS reporting administrators. New commanders receiving the initial readiness brief. S-1 sections feeding personnel data into the personnel pillar of the report.

## Content scope

- Marine Corps Readiness overview.
- Statutory responsibilities and reporting requirements.
- Force readiness rating definitions (P-1 through P-4, S, R, T, C).
- Command readiness reporting cadence and types.
- Report content overview.
- Commander narrative and assessment guidance.
- DRRS and DRRS-MC interface (prior to consolidation per MARADMIN 518-21).

## Operative content as used in the portal

Portal commander and admin pages anchor these working requirements to the handbook.

- Designate the Readiness Officer and Authorized Agents in writing. The incoming commander completes this inside the first 30 days of command.
- Complete the MarineNet DRRS-MC policy course within 30 days of appointment.
- Establish Commander Standing Notification Events (CSNEs) and conduct the first Readiness Board. CSNEs are predetermined readiness triggers requiring an out-of-cycle Readiness Board, regardless of the monthly cadence.
- Build the commander narrative at every reporting cycle. A DRRS submission without the narrative is a defect flagged on the portal personnel-pillar page.
- Rating definitions follow the Joint framework, P-1 highest readiness through P-4 lowest, with similar S, R, T, and C scales. Portal workflow pages pair the handbook with a P/S/R/T/C rating worksheet.
- At turnover, the outgoing commander briefs current C/P/S/R/T levels with rationale for subjective overrides and delivers the Readiness Officer Turnover Binder holding 12 months of records, the IGMC FAC-3000, appointment letters, and DRRS-MC training certificates.

## Connection to Marine Corps policy

- MCO 3000.13B. Marine Corps Readiness Reporting. The governing order.
- MARADMIN 518-21. DRRS-MC Consolidation to DRRS.
- DoDD 7730.65. DoD Readiness Reporting System.
- CJCSI 3401.02B. Joint readiness reporting framework cited beside the handbook on portal DRRS pages.

## Status

Active reference. September 2020 edition. HQMC PP&O sponsors. The edition predates the DRRS-MC consolidation directed by MARADMIN 518-21, so read system references against the consolidation. Verify against current MCO 3000.13B before relying on cadence or rating specifics.
