## Status

**CANCELLED.** This advisory notice has been cancelled. The entry remains in the citations registry for traceability of older content that cited it. Do not act on the operative paragraphs below as current policy. Verify the current pay reporting function alignment between Administration and Disbursing through the HQMC RFF Finance Policy Manual (FPM) Volume 1, the current PRIUM, PAA 02-24, and any newer MARADMIN or PAA on the topic.

## Scope

PAAN 04-21 establishes official policy for the transition of designated pay reporting functions from the Disbursing and Finance community to the Administration community. Initial Operating Capability (IOC) began 1 May 2021, permitting a phased migration of identified Type Transaction Codes (TTCs) after Software Release 1-21. Full Operational Capability is determined by M&RA. Initial rollout covered the fourteen active duty IPACs.

## Audience

- IPAC pay clerks and the broader administration community taking on transferred TTCs.
- Regional Disbursing Offices accepting NAVMC 11116 (Quads) during the transition period.
- 0102, 0111, and 0170 admin specialists.
- Commanders supervising pay accuracy on the PAC side.

## TTCs migrated under SR 1-21

- TTC/SEQ 597/003 Advanced Basic Allowance for Housing.
- TTC/SEQ 698/003 Change of Pay Option Election (POE).
- TTC/SEQ 699/000 Special Payment.
- TTC/SEQ 559/000 Liquidation of Indebtedness (LIQ). TTC 599/000 used by the PAC only after full training and confidence in LIQ execution.

## Operative paragraphs from the source

1. Purpose. Provide official policy for the transition of designated pay reporting functions from the Disbursing/Finance community to the Administration community.
2. Background. Periodic pay inaccuracies cause financial hardship for Marines and require further intervention by the local DO/FO. NAVMC 11116 (Quads) are generated by PACs and used by DO/FOs as the source authority to report offsets and adjustments. The superannuated process of DO/FOs accepting Quads at face value creates audit challenges due to lack of access to key substantiating documentation.
3. Determination. Some military pay reporting capabilities run more efficiently at the PAC rather than the DO/FOs. The intent is to migrate the reporting capability of less complex Type Transaction Codes (TTCs) in phases to ease the transition and build experience.
4. Implementation. IOC begins 1 May 2021 with phased migration after Software Release 1-21. FOC determined by M&RA. Initial rollout at the fourteen active duty IPACs: Camp Pendleton, Camp Lejeune, MCB Camp Butler Okinawa, MCB Hawaii Kaneohe Bay, MCAS Miramar, MCAS Yuma, MCAS Cherry Point, MCAS Beaufort, MCAS New River, MCAS Iwakuni, 29 Palms, MCRD Parris Island, MCRD San Diego, and MCB Quantico.
5. Training. Training plan and materials will be available on the MISSA and MISSO portal prior to SR 1-21. MISSOs and Regional Disbursing Offices provide training upon request after SR 1-21.
6. Metrics. Baseline metrics analyzed using MCTFS and mid-tier application data to monitor progress and ensure quality control.
