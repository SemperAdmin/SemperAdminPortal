## Scope

MARADMIN 518/21 directs the consolidation of the Defense Readiness Reporting System Marine Corps (DRRS-MC) into the Joint DRRS system, signed 2021-10-01. The message supports the DoD-wide readiness reporting unification effort and aligns Marine Corps readiness reporting with the Joint DRRS architecture per DoDD 7730.65 and CJCSI 3401.02B.

## Audience

- Marine Corps unit commanders. Sign the DRRS report and own the readiness ratings.
- S-3 operations officers and DRRS reporting administrators. Submit reports on the DRRS cycle.
- S-1 chiefs. Pull personnel readiness data from MCTFS for the personnel pillar.
- HQMC PP&O readiness oversight staff and MARFOR readiness coordinators. Sponsor the program and support the transition.

## Required action

- Transition DRRS-MC reporting to DRRS per the published timeline.
- Train designated reporting administrators on the DRRS architecture.
- Update unit reporting procedures to align with DRRS.
- Coordinate with MARFOR for transition support.
- Submit readiness reports through DRRS on the reporting cycle. Ratings cover Personnel (P), Supply (S), Equipment Condition (R), Training (T), and Equipment On-Hand (C) with a commander narrative assessment per MCO 3000.13B.

## Connection to higher policy

- DoDD 7730.65. DoD Readiness Reporting System.
- CJCSI 3401.02B. Joint force readiness reporting.
- CJCSM 3150.02B. GSORTS framework.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- MCO 3000.13B. Marine Corps Readiness Reporting. The DRRS-MC framework being consolidated.
- Commanders Readiness Handbook. Supports the commander narrative and the rating definitions.
- MCO P3500.72A. Supports T&R event computation for the training pillar.
- NAVMC 3500.3E T-R events 0102-MPMN-1003 and 0170-OPER-2003. Map the readiness reporting workflow in the portal's admin pages.
