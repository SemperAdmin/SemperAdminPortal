## Scope

MARADMIN 270/05 announced the Aviation Commanders Preparation Program. The program prepares aviation officers for command of aviation units and establishes the 6-month arrival rule. The incoming aviation commander arrives at the gaining command no later than 6 months before the scheduled change of command. Applicability is the Marine Corps Total Force.

## Audience

- Incoming aviation commanders. Arrive 6 months before the change of command and complete pre-assumption training.
- Aviation Wing scheduling staff. Coordinate slating around the MAWTS-1 course schedule.
- Manpower and Reserve Affairs (M&RA). Adjudicates waivers to the arrival window.
- HQMC Aviation. Owns the program.

## The 6-month arrival window

- The incoming aviation commander arrives at the gaining command at least 6 months before the scheduled change of command.
- The window provides time for flight refresher training in the unit's type-model-series, MAWTS-1 Aviation Commander's Course attendance, squadron observation in steady operations, Wing or Group integration, and the outgoing commander's context brief.
- Any deviation requires an M&RA waiver. The request routes through the chain of command with documented reasons.
- The window is the floor, not the operational ideal. A commander arriving inside 6 months compresses every preparation block.

## Connection to higher policy

- MCO P3500.72. Aviation Training and Readiness Program. Parent training framework.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- MCO 3500.27C. Paired with this message as authority for the arrival window in the portal's commander turnover checklists.
- MCO 5100.29C Enclosure 1 Chapter 3 paragraph 2.b and NAVMC 3500.109A Chapter 2 paragraph 1. Require flight refresher training and the MAWTS-1 Aviation Commander's Course before assumption.
- Cornerstone, the Commandant's Combined Commandership Course. General command preparation. The MAWTS-1 course is the aviation-specific pair. Both are required.
