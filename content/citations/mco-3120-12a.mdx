## Scope

MCO 3120.12A establishes the Marine Corps Global Force Management (GFM) and Force Synchronization framework, signed 2020-06-03. The order implements DoD Global Force Management Implementation Guidance (GFMIG), the Secretary of Defense Forces For (SECDEF Forces For) processes, the Global Force Management Allocation Plan (GFMAP), and Joint Staff allocation procedures at the Marine Corps level. GFM coordinates force sourcing across the Joint Force. Force Synchronization addresses conflicts when multiple taskings compete for the same forces.

## Audience

- MARFOR commands (MARFORCOM, MARFORPAC, MARFORRES) coordinating GFM through HQMC PP&O.
- Marine Corps GFM coordinators running GFM tasking at the unit, with HQMC PP&O staff.
- MAGTF commanders sourcing MEU and named-operation requirements.
- S-3 operations officers, S-1 officers, and S-1 chiefs supporting GFM tasking and personnel readiness validation.

## Content scope

- Marine Corps GFM framework and tasking process.
- SECDEF Forces For and force allocation procedures. The Forces For directive identifies the standing force allocation.
- Joint Staff GFM coordination and the GFMAP annual force allocation cycle.
- Force Synchronization across MARFORs, including conflict identification.
- Sourcing recommendations and feasibility.
- Operational and ad-hoc tasking, including individual augmentation.

## Operative content

- GFM tasking reaches the unit from MARFOR or HQMC PP&O. The unit GFM coordinator receives and runs the tasking.
- The sourcing feasibility recommendation reflects the unit DRRS state and operational tempo, submitted through MARFOR.
- Force Synchronization surfaces concurrent tasking conflicts. The higher-priority tasking prevails when conflicts arise.
- The GFMAP cycle drives annual force allocation. Units submit inputs through MARFOR.
- Individual augment tasking flows through GFM coordination. OSCAR is the Marine Corps Operational Support Augmentation Requirement process. Joint Individual Augmentee tasking flows from the Joint Staff through MARFOR to the Marine Corps source unit.
- Approved taskings execute through the deployment cycle under MCO 3000.18B.
- Standard products include the GFM Tasking Order, the Sourcing Feasibility Memorandum, and the Force Synchronization Conflict Notification.

## Connection to higher policy

DoD Global Force Management Implementation Guidance and the GFMAP set the DoD-level force management framework. SECDEF Forces For processes and Joint Staff GFM procedures govern allocation above the Marine Corps.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCO 3000.18B. Marine Corps Force Deployment Planning and Execution. Executes GFM-approved taskings through TPFDD and JOPES.
- MCO 3000.13B. Marine Corps Readiness Reporting. DRRS data supports sourcing feasibility.
- MCTP 3-30G. S-1 doctrine for augment and deployment personnel administration.
- NAVMC 3500.3E. The S-1 GFM workflow maps to T&R event 0170-OPER-2003.
