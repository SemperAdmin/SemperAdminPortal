## Scope

PAA 09-12 covers the Integrated Disability Evaluation System (IDES), the joint DoD-VA process by which DoD determines whether wounded, ill, or injured Service Members are fit for continued military service and by which DoD and VA determine appropriate benefits for Service Members separating or retiring for service-connected disability. The IDES goal is to complete the VA disability compensation delivery phase within 30 days from separation. The advisory directs timely OMPF placement and VA routing of the DD Form 214 to support the 30-day benefits delivery target. Effective date 2012-08-13.

## Audience

- IPAC retirement processors and DD 214 preparers.
- Wounded Warrior administration teams.
- S-1 desks tracking IDES-track separations.
- 0102, 0111, and 0170 admin specialists.

## Content scope

- IDES framework as the joint DoD-VA disability determination process.
- 30-day benefits delivery target measured from the date of separation.
- DD Form 214 OMPF placement and VA routing requirement. The DD Form 214 entry resolves in this registry.
- Coordination between DoD, VA, IPAC, and the Wounded Warrior Regiment.

## Connection to Marine Corps policy

- The Disability Evaluation System page cites this PAA and walks the full IDES pathway under MCO 1900.16 Chapter 2 and DoDI 1332.18.
- On the citing page, IDES completes all required VA exams during the MEB phase, and MCTFS carries duty limit code V for IDES MEB referral.
- The citing page routes disability separations through MMSR-4, the Disability and Limited Duty Section, with PEBLO support to the Marine.
- IODES extends the IDES framework to OCONUS Marines on the same page.

## Status

Released 131801Z Aug 12 by CMC Washington DC MRA MI MIF. Historical baseline for IDES timeliness expectations. Subsequent IDES guidance has reinforced and refined the 30-day delivery target. Verify current IDES timeliness standards through DoDI 1332.18 and current MMSR-4 guidance before citing the 30-day figure as an active standard. Source MISSA portal (CAC restricted).
