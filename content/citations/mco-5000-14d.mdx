## Scope

MCO 5000.14D, the Marine Corps Administrative Procedures order, sets the function and organization of Marine Corps administration. The order increases the quality of administrative support across the force and establishes uniformity at every level of administrative and personnel support. Six enclosures define the division of administrative responsibilities across individuals, leaders, and supporting staffs.

## Authority

Functional Advocate. Deputy Commandant, Manpower and Reserve Affairs (DC, M&RA). DC, M&RA owns occupational field sponsorship, administrative guidance, policies, orders, and the development and management of Marine Corps manpower and personnel information systems.

MAGTF Advocate. Deputy Commandant, Installations and Logistics (DC, I&L).

## Audience

Every Marine carries administrative responsibilities under this order. Leaders own the accuracy of their Marines' information. S-1s, G-1s, Administrative Officers, IPAC personnel, and MISSO staff perform the supporting functions.

## Enclosures

- Encl 1. Key Administrative References.
- Encl 2. Individual Marines' Administrative Responsibilities.
- Encl 3. Commanders' Responsibilities for Personnel Administration.
- Encl 4. Responsibilities of G-1s, S-1s, and Administrative Officers.
- Encl 5. Installation Personnel Administration Centers Responsibilities.
- Encl 6. Responsibilities of the Manpower Information Systems Support Office.

## Goal

Provide commanders, Marines, and family members effective administrative support and services. Enable commanders to focus resources on training and operations. Increase quality of life through efficient administrative support. Lift unit and individual readiness.

## Status

Active. The order supersedes MCO P5000.14C. The order remains the foundational reference for Marine Corps administration structure.
