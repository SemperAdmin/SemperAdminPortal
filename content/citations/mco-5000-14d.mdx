## Scope

MCO 5000.14D, the Marine Corps Administrative Procedures order, known across the force as the MCAP, sets the function and organization of personnel administration for the Marine Corps. The order assigns administrative responsibility at every level, from the individual Marine to the Manpower Information Systems Support Office, and defines the division of labor between operating force units and the Installation Personnel Administration Centers. The 1999 administrative consolidation moved most personnel administrative production out of unit S-1s and into IPACs in the supporting establishment. The MCAP governs how commanders, units, and IPACs share the work after consolidation, with the stated goal of lifting administrative burden off units so commanders focus resources on training and operations.

## Audience

- Every Marine. Enclosure 2 makes each Marine responsible for the accuracy of their own record.
- Commanders. Enclosure 3 lists command responsibilities for personnel administration, from IPAC support procedures through discharge processing.
- G-1s, S-1s, and Administrative Officers. Enclosure 4 defines the administrative functions retained inside the unit.
- IPAC directors and staffs. Enclosure 5 defines the production work owned by the supporting establishment.
- MISSO staffs. Enclosure 6 covers manpower information systems support.

## Enclosure map

- Enclosure 1. Key Administrative References. The library of orders and manuals the MCAP coordinates with.
- Enclosure 2. Individual Marines' Administrative Responsibilities. Record review in MCTFS, reporting life events, keeping dependency and contact data current.
- Enclosure 3. Commanders' Responsibilities for Personnel Administration. Paragraph a directs each command to establish procedures for receiving IPAC support. Later paragraphs assign commanders the Unit Morning and Status Report through MOL, Non-Judicial Punishment processing at paragraph j, and administrative discharge processing at paragraph m.
- Enclosure 4. Responsibilities of G-1s, S-1s, and Administrative Officers.
- Enclosure 5. Installation Personnel Administration Center Responsibilities.
- Enclosure 6. Responsibilities of the Manpower Information Systems Support Office.

## Operative content

Three rules carry most of the order's weight.

1. Commanders own the relationship with the IPAC. Enclosure 3 paragraph a puts establishing command procedures for IPAC support first on the commander's list. The order states the principle directly, "For administration to function efficiently, free and open exchange of information must exist between the unit and the IPAC."
2. The Marine owns the record. Enclosure 2 places responsibility for data accuracy on the individual. Errors in MCTFS feed pay computation, promotion eligibility, retirement points, and benefit qualification.
3. The IPAC owns production. Higher-level administrative functions sit in the supporting establishment. The unit submits complete, accurate source documents. The IPAC executes the transactions.

Functional Advocate. Deputy Commandant, Manpower and Reserve Affairs. Deputy Commandant, Installations and Logistics.

## Connection to higher policy

- Marine Corps Manual. Assigns the personnel administration duties the MCAP implements.
- DoD 7000.14-R, DoDFMR Volume 7A. Military pay entitlements computed from data the MCAP governs.
- SECNAV M-5210.1. Records management and disposition for the documents administration creates.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine and lower policy

- MCO P1070.12 IRAM. Individual records administration, the content standard for the records the MCAP assigns.
- MCTFS PRIUM. Unit-level reporting procedures for every MCTFS transaction the MCAP routes through the IPAC.
- MCO 1001R.1L MCRAMM. Reserve component administration, the MCAP's counterpart structure for the Marine Corps Reserve.
- MCO 5800.16 LSAM. Legal administration supporting the Enclosure 3 discipline responsibilities.

## Status

Active. Signed 2004-06-04. Supersedes MCO P5000.14C. The order remains the foundational reference for the structure of Marine Corps administration and the unit-to-IPAC division of labor. Confirm the current version at the Marine Corps publications site before relying on a specific enclosure paragraph.
