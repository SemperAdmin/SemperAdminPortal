## Scope

JAGINST 5800.7G publishes the Manual of the Judge Advocate General (JAGMAN). The JAGMAN regulates investigations, claims, commercial activities, court-martial procedures, military justice, and administrative procedures across the Department of the Navy. The instruction operates as a Department-wide regulation under the authority of the Secretary of the Navy and the Judge Advocate General.

## Audience

Staff judge advocates and legal officers across the Navy and Marine Corps. Convening authorities exercising court-martial jurisdiction. Commanders processing administrative investigations, line of duty determinations, command investigations, and Article 138 complaints. Legal administrative personnel preparing case files.

## Authority

Office of the Judge Advocate General, Department of the Navy. The instruction operates under the framework of US Navy Regulations 1990, SECNAVINST 5430.7R, and the JAG authorities set in JAGINST 5219.1G.

## Change history

- CH-1. Initial change after the G revision.
- CH-2. 1 December 2023. Incorporates the change to Chapter I published by ALNAV 095/23 (30 November 2023). Significant updates to regulations implementing and supplementing the Manual for Courts-Martial. Revised pages 1-1 through 1-172 and Appendix A-1-a through A-1-q.

## Chapter structure (parent enclosure 1)

- Chapter I. Military justice. Investigations and court-martial procedure.
- Chapter II. Claims. Federal Tort Claims Act, Military Claims Act, Foreign Claims Act, Article 139 claims, Personnel Claims Act.
- Chapter III. Investigations of incidents involving accidents, deaths, and high-visibility events.
- Chapter IV. Article 138 complaints of wrongs.
- Chapter V. Standards of conduct, ethics, and administrative procedures inside the JAG community.
- Subsequent chapters. Specialized procedures including environmental law, intelligence law, and operational law.
