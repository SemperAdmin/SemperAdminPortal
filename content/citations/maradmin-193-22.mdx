## Scope

MARADMIN 193/22 published an Inactive Duty Training (IDT) Travel Reimbursement Update for the Marine Corps Reserve, signed 2022-04-14. The message carried the IDT Travel Reimbursement Program rules in force from 2022 until early 2025. Under the framework it governed, reimbursement topped out at 500 dollars per round trip. MARADMIN 157/25 cancels the message and sets the current framework with the 750 dollar round trip reimbursement cap.

## Audience

- Reserve Marines traveling to IDT at a distant Reserve Training Center. Apply the current MARADMIN 157/25 framework.
- Unit administrators and MSC G-1 staff processing IDT travel claims. Route claims under the successor message.
- Finance and Disbursing Offices settling legacy claims. Round trips before 2024-12-27 settle at the 500 dollar limit.

## Cancellation and successor framework

- MARADMIN 157/25, signed 2025-03-24, cancels MARADMIN 193/22 and lists it in the portal supersedes chain.
- The successor raises the reimbursement limit from 500 to 750 dollars per round trip for round trips ending on or after 2024-12-27.
- The successor keeps the program built around the 150 mile distance rule from the Primary Residence Address, BIC match requirements, and 11 round trips per fiscal year, 22 for MOS 75XX aviators in DIFOP status.
- Current eligibility does not guarantee eligibility under future policy updates.

## Connection to higher policy

- Joint Travel Regulations. The program applies JTR rules for rental vehicles, mileage, and the 75 dollar receipt threshold under the successor framework.

## Connection to Marine Corps doctrine

- MARADMIN 157/25. Inactive Duty Training (IDT) Travel Reimbursement Update. Current framework. Cancels this MARADMIN.
- MARFORRES G-1 and the Reserve Affairs Policy Branch (RAP-1) administer the program under the successor message.
