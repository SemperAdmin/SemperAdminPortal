## Role and responsibility

The assigned sponsor runs the OCONUS welcome aboard outreach per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Enclosure (5) (Accompanied OCONUS) or Enclosure (6) (Unaccompanied OCONUS) under paragraph 4.b.(8) (Assigned Sponsors). The unit SC tracks the OCONUS milestones against the inbound roster. The installation IR&R Program Manager per paragraph 4.b.(9) coordinates installation-level OCONUS in-and-out processing. The unit commanding officer per paragraph 4.b.(6) signs the Command Welcome Aboard Letter per Enclosure (2) and routes accompanied tour command sponsorship requests. The receiving installation Welcome Aboard Center supports the OCONUS arrival.

Source documents flow from the OCONUS PCS orders citing the JTR<Citation source="Joint Travel Regulations" />, the dependent travel orders (for accompanied tours), the command sponsorship approval (for accompanied tours), the Exceptional Family Member (EFM) screening result (when applicable), the Family Advocacy intake (when applicable per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" />), the housing application, the country clearance request, and the OCONUS-specific medical and dental clearances. OCONUS sponsorship outputs include the signed Enclosure (5) or (6) letter, the coordinated housing and clearance plan, the IR&R OCONUS in-processing record, and the unit sponsorship roster entry showing OCONUS milestones.

Per MCO 1320.11H Enclosure (5), the Accompanied OCONUS Sponsor Welcome Aboard Letter covers command sponsorship, family travel, housing, schools, EFM, child care, employment, and medical care. Per Enclosure (6), the Unaccompanied OCONUS Sponsor Welcome Aboard Letter covers BEQ assignment, in-and-out processing, dependent care arrangements (if applicable), and OCONUS-specific risk topics.

## Trigger events

- Inbound OCONUS PCS order issued.
- Command sponsorship request for accompanied tour.
- EFM enrollment triggers OCONUS screening.
- Family Advocacy intake during OCONUS coordination.
- Country clearance routing opens.
- Housing application due.
- Receiving installation Welcome Aboard Center contact opens.
- OCONUS in-and-out processing brief schedule.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the OCONUS assignment type. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Enclosures (5) and (6), confirm accompanied or unaccompanied. The order designates the tour type.
2. Validate command sponsorship for accompanied tours. The accompanied OCONUS tour requires command sponsorship approval before dependent travel under the Joint Travel Regulations<Citation source="Joint Travel Regulations" />. The S-1 officer routes the command sponsorship request per the local procedure.
3. Draft the appropriate sponsor letter. Per Enclosure (5) for Accompanied OCONUS or Enclosure (6) for Unaccompanied OCONUS, the sponsor drafts using the matching sample.
4. Coordinate housing and clearance per the JTR. Per the JTR, OCONUS housing and clearance run on specific timelines. The sponsor coordinates housing application early.
5. Coordinate Family Advocacy Program intake. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" /> and paragraph 4.b.(9), inbound family advocacy intake routes through the IR&R Program Manager.
6. Coordinate Exceptional Family Member screening. When the inbound family has EFM enrollment, the OCONUS screening runs ahead of orders execution. The sponsor coordinates with the SC and the IR&R Program Manager.
7. Coordinate dependent travel and BAQ documentation. Per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" /> and the JTR, dependent travel runs on command sponsorship and the BAQ documentation reflects family status.
8. Brief the inbound Marine on OCONUS-specific risks. The sponsor briefs cost-of-living, currency, transportation, and OCONUS-specific topics covered in Enclosures (5) and (6).
9. Coordinate IR&R OCONUS handoff. Per paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R Program Manager runs the installation-level OCONUS in-processing.
10. Track OCONUS milestones. The SC logs the OCONUS milestones on the unit sponsorship roster.

## Common pitfalls

- Accompanied tour without command sponsorship approval. Per the JTR<Citation source="Joint Travel Regulations" /> and MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Enclosure (5), command sponsorship approval precedes dependent travel. Without approval, the family lacks travel and housing entitlements.
- Enclosure (5) used for unaccompanied tour or Enclosure (6) used for accompanied tour. The letter content fails to match the family scope. Validate at draft.
- EFM screening late. Per the EFMP order and paragraph 4.b.(9), the OCONUS EFM screening runs ahead of orders execution. Late screening blocks dependent travel.
- IR&R OCONUS handoff missed. Per paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R Program Manager runs the installation-level OCONUS in-processing. Coordinate the handoff at sponsor assignment.
- Family Advocacy Program intake missed for accompanied tour with prior advocacy history. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" />, the intake runs at OCONUS arrival.

## Decision points

- Accompanied versus unaccompanied tour. Per the order issuing authority, the tour type drives. The sponsor matches the letter type to the order designation.
- Command sponsorship approval routing. Per the JTR<Citation source="Joint Travel Regulations" />, command sponsorship runs through the receiving command. The sending S-1 officer routes the request early.
- EFM screening result outcome. Per the EFMP order, an EFM screening result not supporting the OCONUS location triggers orders deletion or modification. The S-1 officer routes per the outcome.
