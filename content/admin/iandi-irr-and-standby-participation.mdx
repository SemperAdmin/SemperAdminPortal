## Why These Components Matter

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 Sections 2.2 and 2.3, the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR) and Standby Reserve maintain participation requirements even though they do not drill. Failure to participate at minimum levels affects mobilization eligibility, retirement points, and component status.

I&I admin sees these members rarely but the requirements are real.

## IRR Participation Requirements

IRR members maintain remaining military service obligation (MSO) without active drilling. Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 and Chapter 8.

### Minimum Requirements

- Maintain current address with HQMC (Marine Corps Reserve Support Command)
- Respond to muster correspondence within specified timeframes
- Complete annual readiness data submission
- Maintain DEERS and SGLI elections
- Remain available for involuntary recall

### Annual Muster

Per 10 USC 10206<Citation source="10 USC 10206" />, IRR members participate in annual muster. The muster.

- Verifies member contact information and availability
- Updates readiness data
- Confirms member is alive and reachable
- Earns membership points (15 per anniversary year for IRR)

### Failure to Respond

A member who fails to respond to muster correspondence may.

- Be flagged for additional contact attempts
- Lose membership points for the year
- Be subject to involuntary order to active duty under specific conditions
- Be transferred to Standby Reserve if pattern persists

### Where to Process IRR Actions

Most IRR processing routes through Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC), not the local I&I.

When an IRR member contacts the I&I.

1. Verify their component (IRR, Standby, etc.) in MCTFS.
2. For most actions, direct to MCRSC at https://www.marforres.marines.mil.
3. For return to drilling status, screen and route through join procedures.
4. For mobilization orders, process through MROWS.

## Standby Reserve Participation

Standby Reserve members have completed obligated service and have specific reasons preventing drilling. Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 and Chapter 9.

### Two Categories

- **Active Status List (ASL)**: Members temporarily unable to drill due to civilian employment, hardship, or other approved reasons. Eligible to return to Ready Reserve after the limiting condition resolves.
- **Inactive Status List (ISL)**: Members who must be screened before return. Includes key civilian employees, members in extended hardship, and members with limiting medical conditions not warranting separation.

### Minimum Requirements

Both ASL and ISL members must.

- Maintain current address with HQMC
- Respond to muster correspondence
- Update readiness data periodically
- Maintain DEERS and SGLI
- Remain available for involuntary recall under full mobilization (10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(a))

### No Drilling Required

Standby members do not drill. They earn no drill pay. They earn limited retirement points (membership points only).

### Mobilization Limits

Standby members are subject to mobilization only under full mobilization authority (declaration of war or national emergency). They are not subject to.

- Partial mobilization (10 USC 12302<Citation source="10 USC 12302" />)
- Presidential Reserve Call-Up (10 USC 12304<Citation source="10 USC 12304" />)

This makes them a deeper, more limited mobilization pool.

## Performance Steps for I&I When Members Contact

### Step 1 - Identify Component

Pull the member's MCTFS record.

- Note the component (IRR, ASL Standby, ISL Standby, Retired)
- Note the pay group
- Note any flags or limiters on the record

### Step 2 - Identify the Action

What does the member need?

- Address update (member can do via MOL)
- Status change request (back to SMCR, between IRR and Standby)
- Mobilization order processing
- Pay or benefits question
- Retirement question

### Step 3 - Route to Correct Organization

Based on component and action.

| Action | Component | Route To |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Address update | Any | MOL or MCRSC |
| Return to SMCR | IRR | Local SMCR unit join procedures |
| Return to Ready Reserve | Standby ASL | MCRSC for screening |
| Return to Ready Reserve | Standby ISL | MCRSC for full screening |
| Mobilization orders | Any | HQMC RA or MROWS |
| Retirement pay | Any | DFAS |
| Membership status | Any | MCRSC |

### Step 4 - Document the Contact

Even when not processing the action.

- Log the contact in unit records
- Note the request and routing decision
- Provide member with contact information for routed organization

## Common I&I Confusion Points

These create frustration for both members and admin.

1. **IRR member walks in expecting drill pay.** IRR does not drill. Direct to SMCR join procedures if interested in returning to drilling status.
2. **Standby member confused with IRR.** Different categories with different rules. Verify component before routing.
3. **Member wants to leave IRR for Standby.** Possible per MCRAMM but requires specific justification (key civilian employee designation, hardship). Route to MCRSC.
4. **Member missed muster correspondence.** Late response possible but document to MCRSC. Ensures accurate participation record.
5. **Member returning from civilian world wants back into SMCR.** Screening required. Route through unit join procedures with MCRSC coordination.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Non-drilling component processing touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: Primary support for IRR and Standby admin
- **HQMC RA**: Policy and Standby Reserve management
- **Local SMCR units**: For members returning to drilling status
- **DFAS**: Retirement pay questions
- **VA**: Veteran benefits questions

## Related Roles

These components interact rarely with the I&I but interactions matter.

- **Marines** in IRR or Standby maintain their requirements directly with MCRSC.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units process returning members through join procedures.
- **Commanders** at I&I support occasional Standby and IRR member contacts.
