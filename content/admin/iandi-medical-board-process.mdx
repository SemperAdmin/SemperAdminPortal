## What the Medical Board Process Is

Per DoDI 1332.45<Citation source="DoDI 1332.45" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 6, the Disability Evaluation System (DES) determines fitness for duty for members with conditions affecting service. The system has multiple stages.

## Stage 1 - Medical Evaluation Board (MEB)

The MEB reviews the member's medical condition.

### MEB Purpose

- Document the member's medical condition
- Determine if condition affects duty
- Identify whether disposition is needed
- Recommend referral to PEB

### MEB Process

1. Medical provider identifies condition affecting duty
2. MEB convened to review
3. MEB reviews medical records
4. Member might submit additional documentation
5. MEB determines findings
6. Member receives MEB findings
7. Member might request review or accept findings

### MEB Findings

Possible findings.

- Member fit for full duty (no further action)
- Condition does not warrant referral to PEB
- Recommend referral to PEB for fitness determination

### Member's Rights at MEB

- Review of MEB findings
- Submit comments
- Request review
- Submit additional medical documentation

## Stage 2 - Physical Evaluation Board (PEB)

If MEB recommends, member proceeds to PEB.

### PEB Purpose

- Determine member's fitness for duty
- If unfit, determine disability rating
- Recommend disposition

### PEB Findings

Possible PEB findings.

#### Fit for Duty

- Member returns to full duty
- No disability rating
- Return to drilling status

#### Unfit - Disability Rating Below 30%

- Member separated with disability severance pay
- Specific separation processing
- VA might rate separately

#### Unfit - Disability Rating 30% or Higher

- Member retired with disability retirement
- Continuing pay (percentage of basic pay)
- TRICARE eligibility continues
- Specific retirement processing

#### Unfit - Temporary Disability Retirement (TDRL)

- Member temporarily retired
- 5-year maximum on TDRL
- Periodic re-evaluation
- Final disposition after re-evaluation

## Integrated Disability Evaluation System (IDES)

IDES integrates DOD and VA processes.

### IDES Purpose

- Single set of medical exams
- DOD and VA disability determinations made together
- Streamlined transition for separated members

### IDES Process

1. MEB completes
2. VA conducts disability examinations during DES
3. VA provides disability ratings
4. PEB uses VA ratings for fitness determination
5. Member receives both DOD and VA disability decisions

### IDES Benefits

- Faster overall process
- Single set of exams (rather than separate DOD and VA)
- Better coordinated care

## Reserve Member DES

Reserve members have specific DES considerations.

### Reserve LOD Determination

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6.

- LOD determination drives DES eligibility
- LOD-positive condition qualifies for DES
- LOD-negative does not

### Reserve DES Pay Status

- Might continue drilling status during DES
- Or might be on medical hold (active duty status)
- Specific situation drives pay

### Reserve DES Disposition

- Same disposition options as active duty
- Specific Reserve component considerations
- Coordinate with Reserve component

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Coordinate MEB Initiation

When member's condition triggers MEB.

- Medical provider recommendation
- Member counseling about MEB
- Coordinate with MEB processing

### Step 2 - Support Member During MEB

Provide assistance.

- Documentation gathering
- Member's rights briefing
- Specific procedural support

### Step 3 - Process MEB Findings

After MEB.

- Document findings in member's record
- Coordinate next steps
- Specific actions per findings

### Step 4 - Coordinate PEB if Required

If MEB recommends PEB.

- PEB scheduling coordination
- Continue documentation support
- Member counseling

### Step 5 - Process PEB Findings

After PEB.

- Document findings
- Coordinate disposition
- Specific actions per findings (return to duty, separation, retirement)

### Step 6 - Coordinate Disposition

Per PEB findings.

- Return to duty: UD/MIPS transaction
- Separation: Disability severance processing
- Retirement: Disability retirement processing

## Common DES Issues

### Long DES Process

DES take many months.

- Coordinate ongoing treatment
- Member status management during DES
- Pay implications

### Member Disagreement with Findings

Member might disagree with MEB or PEB findings.

- Specific appeal procedures
- Member's right to review
- Coordinate appeal support

### LOD Issues for Reserve Members

LOD determination critical for reserve DES.

- Coordinate LOD investigation
- Verify LOD-positive for DES eligibility
- Specific Reserve provisions

### DES vs Standard Separation

DES is medical-driven.

- Different from standard administrative separation
- Different timelines
- Different benefit implications

## DES and Pay

Pay during DES.

### Active Duty Member in DES

- Continues active duty pay
- BAH and BAS continue
- TRICARE Prime continues

### Reserve Member in DES

- Drilling status might continue with restrictions
- Or active duty if Medical Hold
- Specific situation drives pay

### Disposition Pay Impact

- Return to duty: Pay continues normally
- Disability separation: Severance pay (covered separately)
- Disability retirement: Continuing pay at percentage of basic pay
- TDRL: Continuing pay during temporary status

## Documentation Required

For DES processing.

- Medical evaluation supporting referral
- MEB findings document
- PEB findings document
- Member's responses and submissions
- Disposition documentation

## Coordination Touchpoints

DES processing touches.

- **MTF Medical Provider**: For medical evaluation
- **MEB**: For initial board review
- **PEB**: For fitness determination
- **VA**: For IDES coordination
- **Member's chain of command**: For disposition coordination

## Quality Control on DES

Common errors.

1. **Documentation incomplete.** DES requires comprehensive medical documentation.
2. **Member rights not communicated.** Member should know rights at each stage.
3. **LOD coordination missed.** Reserve DES depends on LOD finding.
4. **Disposition follow-through delayed.** Final disposition after PEB requires action.

## Related Roles

DES processing affects members with permanent medical conditions.

- **Marines** in DES process treatment, evaluation, and disposition.
- **Leaders** support member during long DES process.
- **Commanders** at I&I coordinate DES processing for unit members.
