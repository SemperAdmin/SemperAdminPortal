## IRR Recall Authority

Per MCRAMM Chapter 8 and Chapter 10, IRR members are subject to recall under specific statutory authorities.

### Authorities Under Which IRR Might Recall

#### Full Mobilization (10 USC 12301(a))

- Declaration of war or national emergency
- All Ready Reserve subject to recall (including IRR)
- Highest authority

#### Partial Mobilization (10 USC 12302)

- Up to 1 million reservists for up to 24 months
- IRR subject to recall
- Specific operational needs

#### Presidential Reserve Call-Up (10 USC 12304)

- Up to 200,000 for up to 365 days
- IRR subject to recall
- Specific operational missions

#### Voluntary Active Duty (10 USC 12301(d))

- Member volunteers
- Specific missions
- Active duty orders

### Authorities Under Which IRR Might NOT Recall

Specific limited authorities.

- Standby Reserve only authorities (12302 and 12304 limited)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10.

### Step 1 - Mobilization Authority Established

- Specific authority invoked

- Specific operational requirement

### Step 2 - Member Identification

- Member's mobilization profile
- Specific criteria

### Step 3 - Member Notification

- Mobilization orders issued
- Specific reporting instructions

### Step 4 - Pre-Mobilization Processing

Members prepare for mobilization.

- Medical and dental screening
- Administrative preparation
- Family preparation

### Step 5 - Reporting to Active Duty

Members report at designated location.

- Active duty status begins
- Initial processing

### Step 6 - Active Duty Mobilization

Member serves active duty.

- Active duty pay and benefits
- Mobilization mission performance
- Specific timeline per authority

### Step 7 - Demobilization

When mobilization ends.

- Active duty status ends
- Return to IRR or to SELRES
- TAMP coverage
- Reintegration support

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin During IRR Mobilization

I&I admin generally does not directly process IRR mobilization. HQMC and gaining commands handle most.

### When IRR Member Local

For locally-known IRR member receiving recall.

- Provide guidance on procedures
- Connect with MCRSC for support
- Specific local assistance

### When Mobilization Includes Local Members

For mass mobilizations affecting local members.

- Coordinate with MCRSC and HQMC
- Specific local support during mobilization
- Family support during member absence

### When IRR Member Returns

If demobilizing IRR member is local.

- Specific reintegration support
- Connect with civilian employer for USERRA
- Coordinate transition

## Coordination Touchpoints

IRR mobilization touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: For IRR administration
- **HQMC**: For mobilization processing
- **Member's gaining unit**: For active duty processing
- **Member's civilian employer**: For USERRA coordination
- **Specific Combatant Command**: For deployment processing

## Quality Control on IRR Mobilization

Common errors.

1. **Member's address not current.** Mobilization notification does not reach member.
2. **Member's medical readiness unknown.** Pre-mobilization issues delay mobilization.
3. **Family Care Plan missing.** Specific situations require FCP for mobilization.
4. **USERRA documentation incomplete.** Employer documentation issues.

## Related Roles

IRR mobilization affects IRR members and their families.

- **Marines** in IRR maintain readiness for potential recall.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely directly engage IRR mobilization.
- **Commanders** at I&I support local IRR members during mobilization.
