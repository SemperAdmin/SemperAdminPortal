## Role and responsibility

The S-1 clerk receives, logs, and routes every piece of inbound correspondence at the unit. The S-1 chief tracks the staffing chop chain, validates signature authority via DD Form 577, and reviews the correspondence register weekly per SECNAV M-5216.5<Citation source="SECNAV M-5216.5" /> Chapter 8 (Signing and Authenticating). The S-1 officer or adjutant signs by direction on behalf of the commander per the by direction delegation. The commander signs personal correspondence and policy-level letters and approves the unit signature authority delegation in writing. The Directives Control Point (DCP) holds the unit letterhead and the directives file per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" />. The records manager retains correspondence per SECNAV M 5210.1.

Source documents flow from inbound naval letters, endorsements, memoranda, business letters, multiple address letters, electronic mail, command tasking messages, congressional inquiries, and operational message traffic. Outbound correspondence flows from the action officer through the staffing chop chain to the signing official. Correspondence outputs include the certified outbound naval letter, the endorsement on received correspondence, the released naval message, the memorandum or business letter, the chop sheet documenting staffing, the SSIC-labeled file folder per SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" />, the OMPF Field Folder copy when the correspondence affects an individual Marine per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" />, and the correspondence register entry.

Per SECNAV M-5216.5 Chapter 8 (Signing and Authenticating), by direction signing requires written delegation captured on DD Form 577. Per Chapter 7 (SSIC), every correspondence file folder labels with the applicable SSIC from SECNAV M-5210.2. Per Chapter 1 (Naval Letter), the naval letter is the standard format for formal correspondence between DON activities and to other Federal agencies. Per Chapter 4 (Business Letter), correspondence with non-Federal recipients uses business letter format.

## Trigger events

- Inbound correspondence received at the unit S-1 mailbox.
- Command tasking message arrives with action and suspense.
- Congressional inquiry or special interest inquiry routed to the unit.
- Outbound correspondence triggered by an operational requirement.
- Endorsement required on forwarded correspondence.
- Naval message preparation triggered by operational tasking.
- By direction signing authority delegation cycle (annual or on commander change).
- Annual correspondence file retention review.
- Records destruction window per SECNAV M 5210.1.
- IGMC audit of unit correspondence file.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive inbound correspondence. The S-1 clerk pulls from the unit mailbox, the unit message account, or hand-carry. The clerk classifies receipt by action versus information.
2. Log the action. The clerk records the date received, the originator, the subject, the classification, the SSIC, the action officer, and the suspense date in the correspondence register.
3. Route to the action officer. The S-1 routes the action to the responsible staff section with the suspense date.
4. Draft outbound correspondence. The action officer drafts per the appropriate SECNAV M-5216.5<Citation source="SECNAV M-5216.5" /> chapter (Chapter 1 Naval Letter, Chapter 2 Endorsement, Chapter 3 Memorandum, Chapter 4 Business Letter, Chapter 5 Multiple Address Letter).
5. Apply Marine Corps supplement. Per MCO 5216.20B<Citation source="MCO 5216.20" /> apply the Marine Corps-specific format adjustments and signature delegations.
6. Run the staffing chop chain. The action officer routes through legal, operations, and other affected staff sections. The chop sheet captures every chop.
7. Validate signing authority. Per SECNAV M-5216.5 Chapter 8, confirm the signing official holds authority for the subject. By direction signing requires DD Form 577 delegation on file.
8. Cut the certified signed copy. Print on unit letterhead with the correct signature block. Capture wet signature or digital signature per command policy.
9. Release naval messages. Per Chapter 10 (Naval Messages), the message routes through the release authority. The release authority signs the release line. Classified messages route through the security manager before release.
10. Apply SSIC labels. Per Chapter 7 and SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" />, every file folder labels with the SSIC, the open and close dates, and the disposal instructions.
11. File the certified copy. Per SECNAV M 5210.1 Schedules Cross Walked by Chapter and MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, file in the unit records system per the retention schedule.
12. Maintain the register. Update the correspondence and message register at every action. The S-1 chief reviews the register weekly.

## Common pitfalls

- By direction signing without DD Form 577 on file. Per SECNAV M-5216.5<Citation source="SECNAV M-5216.5" /> Chapter 8, by direction signing requires written delegation on DD Form 577. Without the form, the signature voids.
- SSIC label missing on file folder. Per Chapter 7 and SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" /> Part I Chapter 2 paragraph 2-2, the SSIC, open/close dates, and disposal instructions print on the outside of the folder.
- Wrong correspondence format selected. Per SECNAV M-5216.5, naval letter routes to Federal recipients. Business letter routes to non-Federal recipients. Memorandum routes internal. Endorsement forwards received correspondence.
- Suspense missed because the register entry skipped. Update the register at every receipt.
- Naval message released without classification review. Per Chapter 10, classified content routes through the security manager.
- Outdated letterhead used. The DCP holds the current letterhead per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" />.
- Records retention floor missed. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, every record type carries a retention schedule.
- Signature block incomplete or wrong. Per Chapter 8, the signature block reflects the signing official's name, rank, title, and authority.

## Decision points

- Format selection. The recipient and the action drive the format. Naval letter for Federal-to-Federal formal correspondence. Business letter for non-Federal recipients. Memorandum for internal. Endorsement for forwarding action.
- Signing authority. The commander signs policy-level and personal correspondence. The S-1 officer or adjutant signs by direction on routine and procedural correspondence within the delegated scope.
- Classification at release. Operational content drives the classification. The security manager confirms before release.
- OMPF Field Folder routing. Correspondence affecting an individual Marine (commendation, counseling, formal recommendation) routes a copy to the OMPF Field Folder per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" />.
