## Purpose

The Unit Punishment Book (UPB), NAVMC 10132<Citation source="NAVMC 10132" />, is the legal document of record for Non-Judicial Punishment proceedings under Article 15 UCMJ. S-1 admin clerks prepare the UPB for the commander before mast and document results after. The UPB drives unit diary entries, OMPF filing, and pay impacts.

## When You Prepare a UPB

- Commander has accepted an NJP referral.
- Member has elected to accept NJP (or refused trial by court-martial).
- Charges have been documented and witness statements collected.
- The mast is scheduled and on the calendar.

## Conditions and Standard

Per T&R event 0111-GENA-1003, you perform this work with access to web-based systems (Command Legal Action System CLA) or database systems, the Electronic Service Record (ESR), and source documents on hand. The standard is to ensure no format, typographical, or grammatical errors.

## Performance Steps

### Pre-Mast (Blocks 1 through 5 and 18 through 21)

1. **Block 1 - Member identification.** Pull from MCTFS. Verify SSN, full name, rank, MOS, unit. Cross-check against ID card if available.
2. **Block 2 - Charges.** List each specification under the relevant UCMJ article. Use the language from the Manual for Courts-Martial. Do not paraphrase.
3. **Block 3 - Date and place of offense.** Extract from the witness statements and command investigation. Be precise.
4. **Block 4 - Notification.** Document when the member was notified of NJP referral. Member signs acknowledgment.
5. **Block 5 - Election.** Member elects acceptance of NJP or demand for trial by court-martial. Witness signature required.
6. **Block 18 through 21 - Procedural acknowledgments.** Member rights advisement, opportunity to consult counsel, opportunity to present matters in extenuation and mitigation.

### Post-Mast (Blocks 6 through 17)

1. **Block 6 - Findings.** Commander enters guilty or not guilty for each specification.
2. **Block 7 through 12 - Punishment.** Document each punishment imposed. Common entries include reduction in grade, forfeiture of pay, restriction, extra duties, correctional custody.
3. **Block 13 through 15 - Suspended punishments.** Note any suspended terms and the suspension period.
4. **Block 16 - Appeal rights.** Member acknowledges right to appeal. Member elects to appeal or waive.
5. **Block 17 - Commander signature.** Imposing commander signs. Date matches the mast date.

### Required Attachments

Per MCO P5800.16<Citation source="MCO P5800.16" /> LSAM, the UPB packet includes the following.

- Charge sheet with specifications
- Investigation summary or command investigation
- Witness statements (signed and witnessed)
- Member acknowledgment of rights
- Counseling Page 11 entries leading up to the offense
- Prior NJP records on file
- ESR printout showing service history

## Pay Impact Coordination

Forfeitures and reductions affect pay. Coordinate with the supporting PAC.

| Punishment | Pay System Impact |
|---|---|
| Reduction in grade | UD/MIPS reduction transaction. New rank and pay grade post next pay period. |
| Forfeiture of pay (one-time) | Single deduction next pay period via UD/MIPS. |
| Forfeiture of pay (suspended) | No immediate deduction. Track suspension period. |
| Restriction | No direct pay impact. Document for command records. |
| Extra duties | No direct pay impact. Document for command records. |
| Correctional custody | Pay status changes to confined. UD/MIPS confinement transaction. |

## Common Errors

S-1 audits surface these errors most often on UPB prep.

1. **Incomplete charge specification.** Specification language deviates from MCM. Fix by using MCM verbatim.
2. **Missing rights advisement signature.** Member did not sign Block 18 acknowledgment. Fix before mast.
3. **Wrong commanding officer signature.** Acting commander not properly designated in writing. Fix by attaching designation memo.
4. **Late forfeiture transaction.** UD/MIPS not submitted within the pay period. Fix by submitting with retroactive effective date.
5. **Page 11 entry not posted.** Required Page 11 documenting NJP did not make it into the SRB. Fix immediately and route to OMPF.

## Routing

After completion, the UPB packet routes through these stops.

1. S-1 review (admin chief and S-1 officer)
2. Commander signature (final)
3. Member receives copy
4. UD/MIPS transactions submitted to PAC
5. UPB filed in command legal records
6. Page 11 entry posted to SRB and OMPF via PAC
7. Promotion eligibility flag updated in MOL if required

## Related Roles

This event reads differently depending on your role.

- **Marines** facing NJP review their rights, election options, and the appeal process. They sign the UPB acknowledging procedural rights.
- **Leaders** counsel Marines pre-NJP, witness statements, and post Page 11 entries documenting the underlying conduct.
- **Commanders** preside at mast, render findings, impose punishment, and sign the UPB. They own the legal sufficiency of the proceeding.
