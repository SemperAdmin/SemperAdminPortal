## Role and responsibility

The 0170 Manpower Officer owns MMEA engagement at the unit level. The G-1 manpower officer at MEF level coordinates structure-level monitor briefings with HQMC MMEA leadership. The S-1 chief builds the manning gap report and routes the replacement requests to the assigned MMEA monitor. The Talent Marketplace pilot extends the monitor engagement to a Marine-initiated matching model under Talent Management 2030.

Source documents flow from the unit T-O pull, the MCTFS assigned-strength roster, the BIC vacancy report, the monitor projection, the manning gap report, the replacement request package, and the MMEA monitor brief slides. Outputs include the manning gap report submission, the replacement request log, the monitor brief record, the lateral-move package, and the Talent Marketplace pilot enrollment.

The HQMC MMEA branch organizes enlisted assignment monitors by MOS. The Officer Assignments Branch (OFCA) organizes officer monitors by MOS or community. The unit identifies the assigned monitor through the MMEA monitor directory or the HQMC M&RA portal.

## Trigger events

- BIC vacancy reaches the manning gap threshold (critical billet, gap exceeding 90 days, or grade or MOS shortage).
- Inbound replacement projection from the monitor lags the outbound rotation.
- Talent Marketplace pilot opens an assignment matching window for the unit.
- MOS divestiture or lateral-move window opens under Force Design 2030.
- Annual monitor brief cycle opens for the unit and command.
- Officer Selection Board approval triggers a PCS projection for officers.

## Processing workflow

1. Pull the manning gap report. The S-1 chief refreshes the BIC vacancy report against TFSMS and MCTFS. Flag critical billets, gap duration, and grade or MOS shortages.
2. Identify the assigned MMEA monitor. Pull the monitor assignment from the MMEA directory by MOS. Officer assignment monitors sit at OFCA. Enlisted monitors sit at MMEA branches by MOS.
3. Build the replacement request package. Include the BIC number, the gap reason (outbound, deactivation, vacancy), the requested grade and MOS, the report-no-later-than date, and the operational justification.
4. Route the request to the monitor. The 0170 officer routes through the unit chain to the MMEA monitor per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" /> Chapter 3 monitor coordination rules.
5. Track the monitor projection. The monitor returns a replacement projection with the inbound Marine name, grade, MOS, date of detachment from the losing unit, and the projected report date. The S-1 chief tracks against the unit gap.
6. Coordinate sponsor assignment. On monitor projection receipt, the S-1 chief assigns a sponsor through the sponsorship program per MCO 1320.11<Citation source="MCO 1320.11" />.
7. Run the monitor brief. The 0170 officer briefs the commander on the manning gap report, the monitor projection, and the gap closure plan on a fixed cycle.
8. Coordinate lateral-move under FD2030. For divested MOSs, coordinate the lateral-move offer with the monitor. The Marine receives the lateral-move package and the timing window.
9. Engage the Talent Marketplace pilot. For pilot-eligible units and MOSs, the S-1 chief tracks the Marine talent marketplace registration and the unit billet posting.
10. File the engagement record. Place the manning gap report, the replacement request, the monitor projection, the lateral-move package, and the Talent Marketplace pilot record in the unit manpower files.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MMEA Monitor Directory. HQMC M&RA portal listing monitors by MOS.
- Talent Marketplace. Pilot platform for Marine-initiated assignment matching under TM2030.
- MCTFS. Holds the projection and the actual assignment record.
- TFSMS. T-O baseline against which the gap reports run.
- MMEA monitor brief system. Internal HQMC system feeding monitor projections.

### Forms

- Manning Gap Report. Local report surfacing unfilled critical BICs.
- Replacement Request Package. Local package routing through the chain to MMEA.
- Monitor Brief Slides. Local brief covering manning posture and the monitor projection.
- Lateral-Move Package. Local package for FD2030 divested MOS lateral moves.
- Sponsor Assignment Letter. Sponsorship program letter per MCO 1320.11.

## Common pitfalls

- Manning gap report stale. The monitor runs without unit gap visibility and sourcing lags. Refresh the gap report at every cycle and tie to the monitor brief.
- Replacement request without operational justification. The monitor de-prioritizes the request. Include the operational justification at submission per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" /> Chapter 3.
- Wrong monitor assignment. The request routes to a monitor not holding the MOS. Verify the monitor assignment against the MMEA directory before submission.
- Sponsor assignment skipped at monitor projection. The inbound Marine arrives without a sponsor. Assign the sponsor at projection receipt per MCO 1320.11<Citation source="MCO 1320.11" />.
- Talent Marketplace pilot enrollment skipped. Pilot-eligible Marines miss the assignment matching opportunity. Track the pilot eligibility list at every reenlistment cycle.

## Decision points

- Critical billet versus standard vacancy. The gap rises to critical based on operational impact and the billet criticality. The 0170 officer escalates critical gaps to the commander and to MMEA per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" /> Chapter 3.
- Lateral-move acceptance under FD2030. The Marine in a divested MOS receives a lateral-move offer. The 0170 officer and the monitor coordinate timing and the receiving MOS.
- Talent Marketplace pilot engagement. The S-1 chief routes the Marine registration when the BIC, the MOS, and the operational rotation align.
