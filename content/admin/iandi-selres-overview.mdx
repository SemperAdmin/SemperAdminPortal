## SELRES Within USMCR Structure

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7 and Chapter 1, the Selected Marine Corps Reserve (SMCR) - also called Selected Reserve (SELRES) - is the active drilling component of the USMCR.

SELRES Marines.

- Drill monthly with assigned SMCR units
- Complete annual training (AT) of 12-14 days
- Maintain MOS qualification
- Subject to mobilization for war or national emergency
- Earn drill pay and retirement points
- Combat power of the USMCR

## SELRES Categories Within Chapter 7

### SMCR Unit Members

The largest SELRES category.

- Assigned to specific SMCR units
- Drill at unit drill location
- Geographic distribution across United States
- Most common SELRES path

### Individual Mobilization Augmentees (IMAs)

SELRES members assigned to active duty commands.

- Drill with gaining active duty command
- Different drilling pattern than SMCR units
- Fill specific augmentation billets

### Active Reserve (AR) Marines

Full-time active duty reservists supporting Reserve component.

- Recruiters in Reserve recruiting
- I&I staff supporting drilling units
- Instructors at Marine Corps schools
- Considered SELRES per MCRAMM Chapter 7

## SELRES Distinctions from Other Reserve Components

| Component | Drills | Drill Pay | Retirement Points |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| SELRES (drilling) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| SELRES (IMA) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| SELRES (AR) | Active duty | Active pay | Active points |
| IRR | No | No | Membership only |
| Standby | No | No | Membership only |
| Retired Reserve | No | No | None |

## SELRES Unit Structure

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7, SMCR units organize like active duty units.

### Unit Types

- Battalions and squadrons (parent units)
- Companies and detachments (subordinate units)
- Specific Marine Corps Reserve combat, combat support, and combat service support units

### MARFORRES Major Subordinate Commands

- 4th Marine Division
- 4th Marine Aircraft Wing
- 4th Marine Logistics Group
- Force Headquarters Group

These commands own SMCR units under MARFORRES.

### Geographic Distribution

SMCR units distributed nationwide.

- Reach largest possible recruiting base
- Minimize travel for drilling reservists
- Specific operational support locations

## SELRES Drilling Patterns

Standard SELRES drilling.

### Monthly Drill Weekend

Typical pattern.

- Saturday and Sunday
- 4 IDT periods (MUTA-4)
- 12 weekends per year (48 IDTs annually)

### Annual Training (AT)

- 12-14 consecutive days
- Active duty status
- At unit, training facility, or deployment

### Additional Training Periods (ATPs)

- Supplemental periods beyond regular drill
- Authorized for specific training needs
- Earn drill pay and points

### Readiness Management Periods (RMPs)

- Administrative or readiness work outside regular drill
- Specific authorization required
- Earn drill pay and points

## SELRES Eligibility for Specific Programs

SELRES members access specific programs.

- TRS (TRICARE Reserve Select)
- MGIB-SR education benefits
- LRP loan repayment
- Specific bonuses per SRIP
- Reserve Health Readiness Program

## Performance Steps for SELRES Member Management

### Step 1 - Initial Receipt

When new SELRES member arrives.

- NPS or PS receipt processing
- Initial admin setup
- Initial counseling

### Step 2 - Continuous Member Management

Ongoing.

- Drill attendance tracking
- Pay processing
- Records management
- Career counseling

### Step 3 - Annual Cycle Management

Per anniversary year.

- Track participation toward 50 points minimum
- Coordinate AT
- Annual administrative items

### Step 4 - Coordinate Major Actions

For specific events.

- Promotion processing
- Lateral moves
- Mobilization
- Separation or transition

## Common SELRES Issues

### Member Drilling Inconsistently

Member missing drills frequently.

- Counseling
- EDP coordination
- Possible Unsatisfactory Participation processing

### Member Civilian Job Conflicts

Member's civilian employment affecting drilling.

- USERRA brief
- Drill schedule coordination
- Specific accommodations

### Member Geographic Issues

Member moves to area without nearby SMCR unit.

- Unit transfer
- Specific transfer to IRR
- Coordinate with member

### Member Family Issues

Member's family circumstances affecting drilling.

- Hardship considerations
- Specific options per situation
- Counseling

Standard SELRES career.

### Initial Service

- IADT for NPS members
- Initial drilling at SMCR unit

### Mid-Career

- Promotions per cycle
- Career schools (NCO Academy, Career Course, etc.)
- Possible MOS change or specialty

### Senior Career

- Senior leadership at SMCR units
- Specific career capstones
- Approaching retirement

### Retirement

- 20 qualifying years achieved
- Retirement processing
- Reserve Retired status

## Coordination Touchpoints

SELRES management touches.

- **MARFORRES G-1**: For SELRES policy
- **HQMC**: For specific personnel actions
- **Member's SMCR unit chain of command**: For unit-level actions
- **PAC**: For specific pay processing
- **MCRSC**: For component transitions

## Related Roles

SELRES members are the active drilling force.

- **Marines** in SELRES drill, train, and serve.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units lead drilling Marines.
- **Commanders** at I&I support SMCR unit operations.
