## What Page 11 Is

Page 11 (NAVMC 118<Citation source="NAVMC 118" />) is the Administrative Remarks form. Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />, Page 11 entries document.

- Required acknowledgments by the member
- Counseling sessions (positive and negative)
- Specific events requiring permanent record
- Command actions affecting the member
- Member elections requiring documentation

Page 11 becomes a permanent OMPF document. It surfaces during promotion boards, selection processes, and separation reviews.

## When Page 11 Is Required

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />, Page 11 entries are required for.

### Required Acknowledgments

- Acknowledgment of orders to active duty
- Acknowledgment of pending administrative action
- Acknowledgment of training requirements
- Acknowledgment of medical findings affecting fitness for duty
- Acknowledgment of policy changes affecting the member

### Counseling

- Initial counseling for new arrivals
- Performance counseling (positive or negative)
- Counseling preceding adverse actions
- Career counseling per applicable schedule
- Pre-NJP counseling

### Discipline-Related

- Documentation of NJP results
- Page 11 supporting administrative separation
- Documentation of restricted access or duty
- Documentation of probation status

### Specific Command Actions

- Will Not Promote determinations
- Reenlistment recommendations or denials
- Loss of access to classified material
- Loss of weapons qualification
- Specific command climate items

## When Page 11 Is Optional

Page 11 may be used at command discretion for.

- General positive recognition (a Letter of Appreciation may serve same purpose)
- Information acknowledgments
- Routine training documentation
- Specific accomplishments not warranting an award

The unit decides based on local policy.

## Performance Steps

### Step 1 - Identify the Event

Determine that Page 11 is appropriate.

- Is this required by IRAM or other directive?
- Is the event significant enough to warrant a permanent OMPF document?
- Is there better documentation (award, fitness report, NJP)?
- Has command policy on Page 11 use been considered?

### Step 2 - Draft the Page 11 Entry

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" /> format.

- Header with member identification (name, SSN, rank, unit)
- Effective date
- Subject line clearly identifying the event
- Body text describing the event factually
- Reference to controlling authority if applicable
- Signature blocks for member, witness, and counseling officer

### Step 3 - Conduct the Counseling

Page 11 is signed during counseling.

- Schedule counseling session in advance
- Read the Page 11 to the member
- Discuss the entry and answer questions
- Allow member to add comments if applicable
- Document any clarifications discussed

### Step 4 - Obtain Member Acknowledgment

Member signs to acknowledge.

- Member signs and dates the Page 11
- Witness signs (typically Admin Chief or unit officer)
- Counseling officer signs
- If member refuses to sign, document refusal with witness

Refusing to sign does not prevent Page 11 from going into the record. Note refusal and proceed.

### Step 5 - Process for OMPF Inclusion

Per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />.

- Apply OMPF metadata
- Apply correct sub-category (positive Page 11, adverse Page 11, acknowledgment)
- Upload via UD/MIPS document storage per MARADMIN 604/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 604/24" />
- Verify upload completion

### Step 6 - File Locally and Confirm

Distribute copies.

- Original to OMPF via UD/MIPS
- Copy to member at counseling
- Copy to unit personnel file
- Verify OMPF posting within 5 business days

## Page 11 Format Requirements

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />.

### Header

- "ADMINISTRATIVE REMARKS" heading
- Member's full name (LAST, FIRST MI)
- Member's SSN
- Member's rank and pay grade
- Member's unit (UIC if available)

### Date Block

- Date the entry takes effect
- Date the counseling occurred (if different)

### Subject

- Clear, specific subject line
- Avoid vague or generic subjects

### Body

- Factual description of the event
- Specific dates, times, locations
- Reference to controlling authority
- Member's options or required actions

### Signature Block

- Member's signature and date
- Witness signature and date
- Counseling officer signature, name, rank, title

## Drafting Tips for Effective Page 11s

Strong Page 11s share characteristics.

### Be Factual

- State what happened, when, where
- Include specific dates and times
- Avoid opinion or characterization
- Stick to verifiable facts

### Be Specific

- Identify the specific behavior or event
- Reference specific dates of incidents
- Cite specific authorities (UCMJ articles, MCOs)
- Avoid generalizations

### Be Brief

- Page 11 is a record, not a narrative
- Short, clear paragraphs
- One subject per Page 11
- Save context for separate documentation

### Be Constructive

- For counseling, identify the corrective action expected
- Specify timelines for improvement
- Identify support available to the member
- Document follow-up plans

## Common Page 11 Errors

These hit during S-1 processing.

1. **Page 11 missing required signatures.** Member or counseling officer signature absent. Page 11 may not be valid for adverse purposes. Reissue with all signatures.
2. **Page 11 with characterization instead of facts.** "Member is lazy" instead of "Member arrived 30 minutes late on dates X, Y, Z." Vulnerable to challenge.
3. **Page 11 not OMPF processed.** Counseling occurred but Page 11 never reached OMPF. Same effect as no Page 11. Process via UD/MIPS storage.
4. **Multiple Page 11s for one event.** Same incident generates three Page 11s with slight variations. Choose one and discard duplicates.
5. **Page 11 dated incorrectly.** Backdating or using wrong effective date. Use accurate dates only.

## Page 11 vs Other Documentation

Page 11 is one tool. Others may apply.

- **Letter of Counseling**: Less formal, may or may not go to OMPF
- **Letter of Caution**: More formal than counseling, OMPF inclusion likely
- **Letter of Reprimand**: Formal command action, definite OMPF inclusion
- **Fitness Report adverse**: For SNCO and officers, formal performance documentation
- **Pro/Con marks**: For junior enlisted, quarterly performance documentation
- **NJP**: For UCMJ violations, formal disciplinary action

Choose the right tool for the situation.

## Member Rights and Page 11

Members have rights regarding Page 11s.

- Right to read the entry before signing
- Right to add comments if applicable
- Right to refuse to sign (with documented refusal)
- Right to request correction if factually wrong
- Right to consult counsel for adverse Page 11s

Respecting these rights protects the integrity of the entry.

## Related Roles

Page 11 entries affect Marines throughout their careers.

- **Marines** read and sign Page 11s acknowledging receipt and content.
- **Leaders** counsel Marines and draft Page 11s documenting the counseling.
- **Commanders** direct Page 11 entries for specific command actions.
