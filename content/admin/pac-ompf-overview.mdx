## What the OMPF Is

Per MCO 1070.1<Citation source="MCO 1070.1" />, the Official Military Personnel File (OMPF) is the permanent, lifetime record of a Marine's military service. It contains documents covering enlistment, training, performance, awards, discipline, separation, and other records of permanent value.

The OMPF travels with the Marine throughout their career and beyond. It is the source document for.

- Promotion board reviews
- Reenlistment eligibility
- Special selection processes (drill instructor, recruiter, embassy duty)
- Reserve and Retired Reserve transitions
- VA disability claims
- Survivor benefits processing

A complete and accurate OMPF protects the Marine's career and benefits. An incomplete OMPF causes problems that compound over time.

## Who Owns OMPF Processing

Per MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> and MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />.

- **Individual Marines**: Responsible for verifying their own OMPF is accurate
- **S-1 staff**: Processes routine document submissions to PAC
- **PAC**: Processes documents into the OMPF system
- **HQMC Manpower & Reserve Affairs**: Maintains the central OMPF system
- **National Personnel Records Center (NPRC)**: Holds OMPFs of separated Marines

## What Belongs in the OMPF

Per MCO 1070.1<Citation source="MCO 1070.1" /> and MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />, the OMPF contains documents in five major categories.

### Performance Documents

- Fitness Reports (officer and SNCO)
- Proficiency and Conduct (Pro/Con) marks (junior enlisted)
- Page 11 (Administrative Remarks) entries
- Letters of Appreciation, Commendation, and Caution

### Award Documents

- Personal awards (Navy Commendation Medal, Bronze Star, etc.)
- Unit award participation records
- Foreign decorations
- Service medals

### Training and Education

- School certificates from formal Marine Corps schools
- Civilian education credentials
- MOS school graduation records
- Special qualification certificates (jump, dive, demolition)

### Discipline

- Non-Judicial Punishment (NJP) records (UPB)
- Court-martial findings
- Administrative separation records (if separated)
- Letters of Reprimand

### Administrative

- Enlistment contracts (DD Form 4<Citation source="DD Form 4" />)
- Reenlistment contracts
- Promotion warrants
- Naval messages affecting personnel actions
- Career service date documents

## What Does NOT Go in the OMPF

Equally important. These documents do not belong.

- Routine personnel actions captured in MCTFS (those live in unit diary, not OMPF)
- Working papers and drafts
- Personal correspondence not affecting service
- Unsigned documents
- Photocopies when originals or certified copies required
- Member-generated documents without command endorsement

Submitting wrong documents creates clutter. PAC rejects improper submissions.

## OMPF Categories Within the System

The OMPF organizes documents into specific subcategories per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Personnel Actions | Promotion warrants, awards, transfer orders |
| Performance | Fitness reports, Pro/Con marks |
| Education | School certificates, civilian transcripts |
| Service Documents | Enlistment, reenlistment, separation |
| Discipline | NJP, Page 11 adverse, courts-martial |
| Medical (Limited) | Most medical in separate medical record, OMPF has limited medical |

## Performance Steps for OMPF Processing

### Step 1 - Receive Document

PAC receives documents from S-1, member, or other PAC sections.

- Log document with date and source
- Verify completeness (all signatures, dates, attachments)
- Check for OMPF inclusion criteria

### Step 2 - Verify OMPF Inclusion Criteria

Per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />, documents must meet specific criteria.

- Permanent value to Marine's service record
- Properly executed (signatures, dates, witnesses)
- Original or certified copy
- Properly identified with member SSN and name

Reject documents that do not meet criteria.

### Step 3 - Process Upload per MARADMIN 604/24

Per MARADMIN 604/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 604/24" />, OMPF documents now route through UD/MIPS document storage.

- Scan document if hard copy
- Upload to UD/MIPS with correct metadata
- Apply indexing tags (member SSN, document type, date)
- Submit for OMPF inclusion

### Step 4 - Verify Indexing

After upload.

- Confirm document appears in member's OMPF view
- Verify indexing tags applied correctly
- Test document retrieval
- Document any indexing issues

### Step 5 - Confirm Member Access

Ensure member can access via MOL.

- Member should see new OMPF entry within 5 business days
- Coordinate any access issues
- Provide member with document copy

## OMPF Access Authority

Per MCO 1070.1<Citation source="MCO 1070.1" />, access to the OMPF is restricted.

| Who | Access Level |
|-----|--------------|
| The Marine | Full access to their own OMPF via MOL |
| S-1 staff | Access to assigned unit Marines |
| PAC | Access for processing actions |
| Promotion boards | Access during board sessions |
| Selection processes | Access during selection only |
| Investigators | Access with proper authorization |
| Anyone else | Access requires specific written authorization |

Unauthorized access is a punishable violation per MCO 1070.1<Citation source="MCO 1070.1" /> and the Privacy Act.

## Common OMPF Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Document submitted without proper signature.** Member or command signature missing. Reject and request corrected version.
2. **Document submitted as photocopy when original required.** Marriage certificates, divorce decrees, and similar require originals. Request certified copy.
3. **Wrong indexing tag applied.** Document uploaded with wrong document type code. Fix by re-indexing or re-uploading.
4. **Member SSN incorrect on document.** SSN typo causes document to not associate with member. Critical to verify SSN before upload.
5. **Document not appearing in OMPF after upload.** UD/MIPS upload failed silently. Verify upload completed and re-process if needed.

## Quality Control

Before submitting any document to OMPF.

- Verify member SSN matches the record
- Verify all signatures present and dated
- Verify document type clearly identified
- Verify metadata complete for indexing
- Test that document retrieves from member's OMPF view

## Related Roles

OMPF is the permanent record everyone touches.

- **Marines** review their OMPF annually via MOL and report errors.
- **Leaders** ensure their Marines' performance documents reach OMPF.
- **Commanders** sign documents going to OMPF and own the accuracy of their Marines' records.
