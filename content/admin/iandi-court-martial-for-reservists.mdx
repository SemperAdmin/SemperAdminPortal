## Court-Martial Overview

Per UCMJ Articles 16-20 and MCRAMM Chapter 11, court-martial provides judicial process for serious military offenses. Reserve members may face court-martial under specific circumstances.

## Three Court-Martial Types

Per UCMJ.

### Summary Court-Martial

For minor offenses.

- Single officer (Summary Court-Martial Officer)
- Limited punishments
- Member may refuse and demand higher court
- Specific procedures

### Special Court-Martial

For intermediate offenses.

- Three or more officers
- Increased punishments
- Specific procedures
- Conviction creates federal criminal record

### General Court-Martial

For most serious offenses.

- Five or more officers (members) or military judge alone
- Most severe punishments (up to and including death)
- Specific procedures
- Major federal criminal record

For some serious offenses, alternatives exist.

### Court-Martial

- Federal criminal proceedings
- Specific UCMJ procedures
- Conviction creates criminal record

### Administrative Separation

- Specific administrative process
- Possible Other Than Honorable discharge
- Generally less severe than court-martial conviction
