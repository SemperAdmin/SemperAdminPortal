## IDT Period Limits

Per 10 USC 10147<Citation source="10 USC 10147" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 7 Section 1.

### Standard Limits

- 48 paid IDTs per fiscal year (most members)
- 60 paid IDTs per fiscal year (Reserve general officers)
- 4 paid EDPs per fiscal year (subset of total IDTs)
- 2 IDT periods per calendar day maximum
- 4-hour minimum per IDT period (2-hour for FHD)

These limits cap pay drilled annually.

### What Counts Toward 48 IDT Total

- Regular drill periods
- Paid Equivalent Duty Periods (EDPs) for missed drill
- Specific other paid IDT categories

### What Does NOT Count Toward 48 IDT Total

- Funeral Honors Duty (FHD) - separate count
- Additional Training Periods (ATPs) - separate authorization
- Readiness Management Periods (RMPs) - separate authorization
- Active duty days (AT, ADOS, mobilization)
- Unpaid EDPs (EINs)

This separation allows authorized supplemental periods beyond the 48 limit.

## Additional Training Periods (ATPs)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7.

### Purpose

Supplemental training beyond regular drill.

- Specific skills training
- Equipment training
- Range periods
- MOS-specific training

### Authorization

ATPs require specific authorization.

- Unit commander or operational sponsor
- Documented authorization
- Pre-approved per ATP plan

### Pay Treatment

- Earn drill pay at standard rate
- Earn retirement points
- Counted separately from 48-IDT total

## Readiness Management Periods (RMPs)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7.

### Purpose

Administrative or readiness work outside regular drill.

- Command admin
- Training preparation
- Equipment management
- Specific readiness improvement

### Authorization

RMPs require specific authorization.

- Unit commander or specific authority
- Documented authorization
- Specific readiness purpose

### Pay Treatment

- Earn drill pay at standard rate
- Earn retirement points
- Counted separately from 48-IDT total

## Additional Flight Training Periods (AFTPs)

For aviators.

### Purpose

Aviation-specific flight currency training.

### Authorization

- Restricted to aviation MOSs
- Specific aviation authorization
- Specific currency requirements

### Pay Treatment

- Earn drill pay
- Earn retirement points
- Aviation-specific tracking

## Equivalent Duty Periods (EDPs)

For making up missed drills.

### Paid EDP (EIO)

- Replace missed regular IDT
- Performed within 60 days of missed drill
- Same fiscal year as missed drill
- Counts toward 48-IDT total
- Earns pay and points

### Unpaid EDP (EIN)

- Used when paid EDP window expired
- Earns retirement points only
- Does not count toward 48-IDT pay total

## Performance Steps for IDT Tracking

### Step 1 - Track Each Member's IDT Total

Maintain tracker per member.

- Regular drill periods completed
- ATPs authorized and performed
- RMPs authorized and performed
- AFTPs (if applicable) performed
- FHD performed
- EDPs performed (paid and unpaid)

### Step 2 - Verify Authority for Supplemental Periods

For ATPs, RMPs, AFTPs.

- Check authorization documentation
- Verify within authorized period
- Coordinate with command

### Step 3 - Process Payments

Through Drill Manager.

- Specific pay codes per period type
- Verify each period before submission
- Coordinate with member's chain of command

### Step 4 - Track Annual Limits

Throughout fiscal year.

- Monitor approaching 48-IDT limit
- Coordinate with command if member approaching limit
- Specific actions if limit reached

### Step 5 - Address Limit Issues

If member approaches or reaches limits.

- Verify any further periods authorized as supplemental
- Specific decisions per member's situation
- Coordinate with command

## When IDT Limits Affect Drill Operations

### Member at 48-IDT Limit

Member completed 48 paid regular drill periods.

- Does not perform additional regular drill IDTs
- Would still perform supplemental ATP, RMP, AFTP, FHD
- Specific authorizations needed for supplemental

### Unit Operational Need Beyond 48

Unit needs member's continued participation.

- Authorize ATPs or RMPs as appropriate
- Specific operational sponsor approval
- Document authority

### Member Wants Additional Pay

Member requests additional drilling for pay.

- Limited to authorized supplemental periods
- Specific authorities needed
- Counseling on pay limits

## Common IDT Limit Issues

### Member Exceeds 48-IDT Limit

Pay processing for period 49+.

- Verify whether period was authorized supplemental
- If not authorized, coordinate correction
- Possible pay debt situation

### Authorization Documentation Missing

ATP or RMP performed but no authorization on file.

- Coordinate retroactive documentation
- Or treat as unauthorized
- Specific procedures per situation

### Member Confusion About Categories

Member confuses regular IDT, ATP, RMP, FHD.

- Brief member on different categories
- Help member track categories
- Provide annual summary

## Audit Implications

IDT limits are audit-relevant.

- Pay beyond 48 IDT without authorization creates findings
- ATP and RMP without proper authorization create findings
- Missing documentation creates audit issues

PAC and I&I admin coordination ensures compliance.

## Documentation Required

For IDT period processing.

- Authorization documents for ATPs, RMPs, AFTPs
- Drill batch documentation
- EDFR confirmation
- Annual member summaries

## Coordination Touchpoints

IDT limit administration touches.

- **MARFORRES**: For specific operational authorities
- **Member's command**: For drill authorization
- **PAC**: For pay processing
- **HQMC**: For policy questions

## Related Roles

IDT period limits affect every drilling SELRES (Selected Reserve, what most Marines call SMCR or the Reserves) member.

- **Marines** track their IDT participation toward annual limits.
- **Leaders** at units authorize supplemental periods within authority.
- **Commanders** at I&I support compliance with IDT limits.
