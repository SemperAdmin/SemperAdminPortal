## Role and responsibility

The DRRS administrator submits Marine Corps readiness reports per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" /> and MARADMIN 518-21<Citation source="MARADMIN 518-21" />. The unit commander signs the DRRS report and supports the personnel-driven readiness rating. The S-1 chief pulls personnel readiness data from MCTFS. The S-3 operations officer pulls T&R event completion data. The S-4 logistics officer pulls equipment readiness data. The Commanders Readiness Handbook supports the commander narrative.

Source documents flow from the MCTFS personnel readiness data, the T&R event status per MCO P3500.72A, the equipment readiness from S-4, the prior DRRS reports, the commander's narrative assessment, and the unit Mission Essential Task List (METL). DRRS outputs include the submitted DRRS report with P (Personnel), S (Supply), R (Equipment Condition), T (Training), and C (Equipment On-Hand) ratings, the commander narrative, the change report when ratings shift, the retained DRRS report per records schedule, and the audit trail.

Per MCO 3000.13B, Marine Corps unit DRRS reporting follows the Joint DRRS architecture under DoDD 7730.65 and CJCSI 3401.02B. Per MARADMIN 518-21, DRRS-MC consolidates into DRRS. Per the Commanders Readiness Handbook, the rating definitions follow the Joint framework (P-1 highest readiness through P-4 lowest readiness, similar S, R, T, C scales).

## Trigger events

- DRRS reporting cycle opens.
- Rating change at the unit (personnel, training, equipment).
- METL change triggers reassessment.
- New OPLAN tasking triggers readiness review.
- DRRS-MC to DRRS transition milestone per MARADMIN 518-21.
- Annual readiness baseline cycle.
- Commander change triggers commander narrative refresh.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the DRRS reporting cycle notification. Per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />, the cycle runs on the published cadence. Change reports run on rating shift.
2. Pull personnel readiness data. From MCTFS, pull strength, MOS distribution, grade distribution, and qualification data.
3. Pull equipment readiness data. From S-4 and the equipment readiness reports, pull equipment-on-hand and equipment condition.
4. Pull T&R event completion data. Per MCO P3500.72A, pull T&R event completion against the METL.
5. Compute P, S, R, T, C ratings. Per MCO 3000.13B and the Commanders Readiness Handbook:
   - P (Personnel) rating per MCTFS strength and qualification.
   - S (Supply) rating per supply on hand against authorized.
   - R (Equipment Condition) rating per equipment maintenance readiness.
   - T (Training) rating per T&R event completion against METL.
   - C (Equipment On-Hand) rating per equipment quantity against authorized.
6. Build the commander narrative. Per the Commanders Readiness Handbook, the narrative provides commander assessment of the unit's mission capability.
7. Commander signs. Per MCO 3000.13B, the unit commander signs the DRRS report.
8. Submit via DRRS. Per MARADMIN 518-21<Citation source="MARADMIN 518-21" />, the report submits through DRRS following the consolidation timeline.
9. Update at rating change. Per MCO 3000.13B, rating changes trigger an updated report.
10. Retain DRRS reports. Per the records retention schedule, retain DRRS reports per the SSIC disposition.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Defense Readiness Reporting System (DRRS). Per MCO 3000.13B and MARADMIN 518-21.
- Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). Personnel readiness data feed.
- MIRRORS, GCSS-MC, or equivalent equipment readiness systems. Per S-4.
- T&R event tracking system. Per MCO P3500.72A.
- Commanders Readiness Handbook. Per HQMC PP&O.

### Forms

- DRRS Report. Per MCO 3000.13B.
- P/S/R/T/C Rating Worksheet. Per Commanders Readiness Handbook.
- Commander Narrative. Per MCO 3000.13B.
- DRRS Change Report. Per rating change.
- METL. Per MCO P3500.72A.

## Common pitfalls

- DRRS rating reported without commander narrative. Per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />, the commander narrative supports the rating.
- Personnel readiness data not reconciled with MCTFS. The S-1 reconciles MCTFS data with DRRS inputs.
- T&R event completion data lagged. Per MCO P3500.72A, the T&R event status drives the T rating.
- Equipment readiness data lagged. The S-4 pulls current readiness for the R and C ratings.
- DRRS-MC reporting continued after consolidation deadline. Per MARADMIN 518-21<Citation source="MARADMIN 518-21" />, the consolidation timeline drives the transition.
- Rating change report not submitted. Per MCO 3000.13B, rating changes trigger an update.
- METL change not reflected in DRRS. The METL drives the T rating computation.
- DRRS report not retained per records schedule. Per the SSIC disposition, retention runs per the published rules.

## Decision points

- Rating computation versus commander assessment. Per MCO 3000.13B, the data-driven rating combines with the commander narrative. The narrative addresses non-quantitative factors.
- Change report timing. Per MCO 3000.13B, rating changes drive change reports. Standard cycles run on the published cadence.
- DRRS versus DRRS-MC. Per MARADMIN 518-21, DRRS is the consolidated reporting platform.
- METL update. Per the unit's mission and MCO P3500.72A, METL changes trigger DRRS reassessment.
