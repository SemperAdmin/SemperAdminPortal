## Communications and Reporting Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 5 and MCAP, Reserve administration generates substantial communications and reporting. The reports inform command decisions, drive resource allocation, and demonstrate compliance.

I&I admin generates many of these reports.

## Categories of Reports

### Personnel Status Reports

For tracking unit personnel.

- Unit Manning Status Report (UMSR)
- Daily personnel status (JPERSTAT)
- Specific manpower reports
- Mobilization readiness reports

### Pay Reports

For tracking pay-related actions.

- Drill pay reports
- Active duty pay reports
- Pay anomaly reports
- Audit-related pay reports

### Training and Readiness Reports

For tracking unit training and readiness.

- Drill performance reports
- AT participation reports
- Training currency reports
- Readiness reports per DRRS-style requirements

### Statistical Reports

For aggregate data.

- Demographic reports
- Component status reports
- Geographic distribution reports

### Operational Reports

For operational matters.

- Deployment status reports
- Mobilization tracking
- Specific operational requirements

## Common Reports

### Unit Manning Status Report (UMSR)

Per MCAP Enclosure 3, commanders submit UMSR via MOL.

- Frequency: Monthly typically
- Content: Unit personnel status by category
- Submission: Via MOL UMSR module
- Reviewed by: Higher headquarters

I&I admin coordinates UMSR data accuracy with the unit commander.

### Drill Performance Reports

After each drill weekend.

- Attendance report
- Participation status
- Specific issues for command attention

### JPERSTAT

Joint Personnel Status report for personnel accountability.

- Frequency: Daily during active operations
- Content: Personnel by status
- Submission: Per command requirements

### Pay Anomaly Reports

When members have pay discrepancies.

- Specific anomaly identification
- Resolution tracking
- Coordination with PAC

### Audit Reports

For audit findings and resolutions.

- Specific findings documentation
- Corrective action tracking
- Resolution verification

## Performance Steps for Reporting

### Step 1 - Identify Required Reports

Per current MARADMIN and command requirements.

- List of regular reports
- Frequency of each
- Specific submission requirements

### Step 2 - Gather Data

For each report.

- Pull data from MCTFS, MOL, Drill Manager
- Cross-check accuracy
- Coordinate with member data sources

### Step 3 - Format Report

Per specific report requirements.

- Use approved templates
- Complete required fields
- Provide supporting documentation

### Step 4 - Submit Report

Per submission channels.

- MOL for UMSR
- Email or specific portal for others
- Specific routing requirements

### Step 5 - Track Submission

After submission.

- Confirmation of receipt
- Response to any feedback
- Continued coordination

## Common Reporting Issues

### Late Reports

Missing submission deadlines.

- Establish reporting calendar
- Set internal deadlines before external
- Track submission compliance

### Inaccurate Data

Reports with errors.

- Cross-check data sources
- Review before submission
- Correct after-the-fact if needed

### Missing Reports

Required reports not submitted.

- Maintain reporting list
- Track each report
- Address gaps promptly

### Coordination Failures

Reports requiring multi-source data.

- Coordinate with all sources
- Establish lead and supporting roles
- Track each contribution

## Communication Methods

### Email

For most routine communications.

- Official email accounts
- Specific routing
- Appropriate classification

### Phone

For time-sensitive matters.

- Direct contact for urgent
- Specific phone trees for command
- Documentation of phone communications

### Written Correspondence

For formal communications.

- Naval letter format
- Specific routing
- Filed copies

### Specific Systems

For data-driven communications.

- MOL for personnel data
- Drill Manager for drill-related
- Specific systems for specific reports

## Common Communication Issues

### Wrong Audience

Communication sent to wrong recipients.

- Verify distribution list
- Verify intended audience

### Untimely Communication

Communication sent too late.

- Establish urgency criteria
- Use appropriate method for urgency

### Incomplete Information

Communication missing required details.

- Use templates where applicable
- Verify completeness before sending

### Member Not Reached

Communication not received by member.

- Verify member contact information
- Use multiple methods if necessary
- Document attempt

## Quality Control on Reporting and Communications

Before submission.

- Verify data accuracy
- Verify formatting correct
- Verify routing appropriate
- Verify timing meets requirements

## Coordination Touchpoints

Reporting and communications touch.

- **MOL administrators**: For MOL-based reports
- **Higher headquarters**: For routing and feedback
- **MCRSC**: For specific Reserve component reports
- **Member's chain of command**: For unit-level reports

## Related Roles

Reports and communications support command awareness and member service.

- **Marines** receive timely communications about their service.
- **Leaders** at units provide data for reports and direct communications.
- **Commanders** rely on reports for situational awareness.
