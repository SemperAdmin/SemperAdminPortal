## What IRR Is

Per MCRAMM Chapter 8 and 10 USC 10144<Citation source="10 USC 10144" />, the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR) consists of Reserve members with remaining military service obligation but who do not drill with SMCR units. IRR is part of the Ready Reserve, subject to mobilization but not actively training.

## IRR Composition

Members enter IRR through.

### Active Duty Completion

Most common path.

- Member completes active duty contract
- Discharged from active duty
- Remaining MSO served in IRR
- 4 years IRR following 4 years active typical

### SMCR Departure

Members leaving drilling status.

- Hardship cases preventing continued drilling
- Civilian employment changes
- Family circumstances
- Specific other reasons

### Standby Reserve Member Reentry

Standby Reserve member returning to Ready Reserve.

- Active Status List members eligible
- May go to IRR rather than directly to SMCR

### Inter-Service Transfer

Members from other Services entering Marine Corps Reserve.

- Initial entry to IRR
- May transition to SMCR with specific procedures

## IRR Member Status

IRR members have specific status.

### Not Currently Drilling

- No drill pay
- No retirement points from drilling
- No active military training
- Reduced military involvement

### Maintain Component Status

- IRR members are part of Marine Corps Reserve
- Subject to recall under specific conditions
- Continue to be Marines
- Specific obligations remain

### Limited Benefits

- Limited military medical access
- Specific TRICARE provisions
- Limited base access
- Reduced specific benefits

## IRR Pay Group

Per MCTFSCODESMAN.

- Pay Group P (no drill pay)
- Earns membership points (15 per anniversary year)
- No active duty pay unless on active duty orders

## IRR Participation Requirements

Per MCRAMM Chapter 8.

### Annual Muster

Members participate in annual muster.

- Verify member status and contact information
- Update readiness data
- Confirm member is reachable
- Earn membership points

### Address Maintenance

- Member must maintain current address with MCRSC
- Address changes update via MOL
- Annual verification

### DEERS and SGLI Maintenance

- DEERS enrollment current
- SGLI election current
- Specific updates as life events occur

### Readiness Updates

- Periodic readiness data updates
- Mobilization availability information
- Specific reporting requirements

## Mobilization Subject

IRR members are subject to mobilization under specific authorities.

### Full Mobilization (12301(a))

- All IRR subject to mobilization
- Declaration of war or national emergency
- Highest authority

### Partial Mobilization (12302)

- Up to 1 million reservists for up to 24 months
- IRR subject to recall
- Specific operational needs

### Presidential Reserve Call-Up (12304)

- Up to 200,000 for up to 365 days
- IRR subject to recall
- Specific operational missions

### Voluntary Active Duty (12301(d))

- Member volunteers
- Specific missions
- Active duty orders

## IRR Administrative Support

I&I admin generally does not directly support IRR members.

### Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)

Primary support organization for IRR.

- IRR member services
- Address updates
- Annual muster coordination
- Mobilization processing

### When IRR Members Contact I&I

I&I admin may receive IRR contacts.

- Member returning to drilling SMCR (join procedures)
- Specific local issue
- Reference to MCRSC for routing

### I&I Limited Authority

Most IRR actions require routing.

- To MCRSC for routine actions
- To HQMC for policy questions
- To member's gaining unit for SMCR return

## Performance Steps for IRR Member Contacts

When IRR member contacts I&I.

### Step 1 - Verify Member Status

Pull MCTFS record.

- Verify IRR component status
- Pay Group P
- Recent service actions

### Step 2 - Identify Member's Need

Determine what member needs.

- Address update
- Status change request
- Mobilization-related action
- Other specific need

### Step 3 - Route to Correct Organization

Based on need.

- MCRSC for routine actions
- HQMC for policy matters
- Local SMCR unit for joining as drilling member
- VA or DFAS for specific other needs

### Step 4 - Document the Contact

Even if not directly processing.

- Log the contact
- Note routing decision
- Provide member with contact information

## IRR Member Career Path

Members in IRR have specific options.

### Continue in IRR

- Maintain participation requirements
- Earn membership points
- Subject to recall

### Return to SMCR

- Transition back to drilling status
- Specific reentry procedures
- Available billet required

### Transition to Standby Reserve

- Specific qualifying conditions
- Different participation requirements
- May limit mobilization category

### Separation from Reserve

- End of MSO
- Specific separation processing
- Discharge from Marine Corps Reserve

### Recall to Active Duty

- Per mobilization authority
- Active duty orders processing
- Specific transitions

## Common IRR Member Questions

### "Can I get TRICARE?"

Generally no for IRR. Direct to civilian insurance.

### "Where is my drill pay?"

IRR members do not drill or earn drill pay. Brief on IRR status.

### "How do I get back to drilling?"

Direct to local SMCR unit join procedures.

### "Will I be activated?"

Subject to mobilization under specific authorities. Brief on possibilities.

### "How do I update my address?"

Update via MOL or contact MCRSC.

## Coordination Touchpoints

IRR member processing touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: Primary IRR support
- **HQMC RA**: For policy matters
- **Local SMCR units**: For SMCR return
- **MOL**: For self-service updates

## Related Roles

IRR members have limited Reserve involvement but specific obligations.

- **Marines** in IRR maintain status and remain available for recall.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely interact with IRR members.
- **Commanders** at I&I support occasional IRR member contacts.
