## Role and responsibility

The unit S-1 supports pre-deployment and redeployment personnel actions per MCO 3000.18B<Citation source="MCO 3000.18B" /> and MCTP 3-30G<Citation source="MCTP 3-30G" />. The IPAC outbound counselor processes deployment-related orders. The S-1 chief tracks personnel through the deployment cycle. The unit commander signs the deployment readiness check and the redeployment closeout documentation.

Source documents flow from the deployment timeline per MCO 3000.18B, the T&R event roadmap per MCO P3500.72A, the unit DRRS state per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />, the deployment readiness check list, the RIP/TOA package with the relieving unit, the redeployment package, and the reset cycle records. Outputs include the completed pre-deployment training certification, the deployment readiness check completion, the family readiness coordination record, the deployment orders, the RIP/TOA documentation, the redeployment transactions, the reset cycle personnel actions, the post-deployment health and pay reset, and the updated DRRS readiness.

Per MCO 3000.18B, the FDP&E Manual covers the deployment lifecycle from planning through execution and redeployment. Per MCTP 3-30G, the S-1 supports personnel administration across the deployment cycle. Per MCO 3120.12A<Citation source="MCO 3120.12A" />, GFM and Force Synchronization coordinate the deployment timing.

## Trigger events

- Deployment date approaches triggering pre-deployment training.
- Pre-deployment family readiness cycle.
- Deployment readiness check window opens.
- Deployment orders cut.
- RIP/TOA scheduled with relieving unit.
- Deployment closeout cycle.
- Redeployment transactions required.
- Reset cycle begins.
- Post-deployment health and pay reset.
- DRRS readiness update post-deployment.

## Processing workflow

1. Run pre-deployment training certification. Per MCO P3500.72A, the unit certifies on pre-deployment training requirements.
2. Coordinate deployment readiness check. Per MCO 3000.18B<Citation source="MCO 3000.18B" />, the deployment readiness check confirms unit personnel and equipment readiness before deployment.
3. Process pre-deployment family readiness. Per MCO 1754.9B (cross-reference to sponsorship and UPFRP), the unit supports family readiness during the pre-deployment phase.
4. Cut deployment-related orders. The IPAC outbound counselor processes deployment orders, TAD orders for individual augments, and PCS orders for permanent transfers.
5. Coordinate RIP/TOA with relieving unit. The Relief in Place / Transfer of Authority transfers the mission and personnel posture to the relieving unit.
6. Support deployment execution at the unit. The S-1 maintains contact with the deployed unit on personnel administration during operations.
7. Process redeployment transactions. Per MCTP 3-30G<Citation source="MCTP 3-30G" />, the redeployment transactions process at the home station.
8. Run reset cycle personnel actions. The reset cycle covers personnel reset (leave, PCS, separation, retirement) and unit reset.
9. Coordinate post-deployment health and pay reset. Per the post-deployment health assessment and the pay reset (HFP/IDP, HDP, CZTE termination), the unit completes reset actions.
10. Update DRRS readiness post-deployment. Per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />, the unit DRRS state updates with the post-deployment posture.

## Common pitfalls

- Pre-deployment training certification skipped. Per MCO P3500.72A, certification supports deployment readiness.
- Deployment readiness check skipped. Per MCO 3000.18B<Citation source="MCO 3000.18B" />, the check confirms unit readiness.
- Family readiness coordination late. The unit supports family transition during the pre-deployment phase.
- RIP/TOA documentation incomplete. The transfer of authority needs documented mission and personnel posture.
- Redeployment transactions skipped or late. Per MCTP 3-30G<Citation source="MCTP 3-30G" />, the home station processes the transactions on return.
- Reset cycle personnel actions delayed. The reset cycle supports the unit's recovery and the Marine's personnel actions.
- Post-deployment health assessment skipped. Per Defense Health Agency, the assessment supports Marine health.
- DRRS readiness not updated post-deployment. The unit posture changes after the deployment cycle.

## Decision points

- Pre-deployment training scope. Per MCO P3500.72A and the unit METL, the training scope addresses deployment-specific tasks.
- Deployment readiness check scope. Per MCO 3000.18B, the check covers personnel, equipment, training, and supply readiness.
- RIP/TOA timing. Per the deployed schedule, the RIP/TOA timing aligns with the relieving unit's arrival.
- Reset cycle priority. The unit weighs personnel reset (leave, PCS, separation) and unit reset (equipment, training).
- Family readiness scope. Per the unit's family posture, the unit supports family transition during pre-deployment and post-deployment.
