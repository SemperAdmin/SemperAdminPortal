## UCMJ Authority Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 11 and 10 USC Chapter 47, the Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ) provides legal authority over military members. For Reserve members, UCMJ jurisdiction depends on duty status.

## When UCMJ Applies to Reservists

Per UCMJ Article 2.

### Active Duty Status

UCMJ applies fully when reservist is on active duty.

- Mobilization (12301(a), 12302, 12304)
- AT (12301(b))
- ADT or ADOS (12301(d))
- Specific other active duty

During active duty, member subject to all UCMJ provisions.

### Inactive Duty Training (IDT) Status

UCMJ applies during IDT.

- Drill weekends
- Other IDT periods
- Specific training periods

UCMJ jurisdiction during IDT covers conduct during the duty period.

### Travel to/from IDT and AT

Per Title 10 portal-to-portal coverage.

- UCMJ might apply during travel to/from drill or AT
- Specific provisions per statute
- Specific application

### Off-Duty Periods

UCMJ generally does not apply when reservist not in duty status.

- Civilian off-duty periods generally outside UCMJ
- Specific exceptions for some serious crimes
- Specific off-duty conduct affecting service might have implications

## UCMJ Limits for Reservists

### Does not Be Tried for Pre-Service Offenses

UCMJ generally does not cover offenses committed before military service.

### Limits on Off-Duty Conduct

Most off-duty conduct generally outside UCMJ.

- Specific exceptions per statute
- Civilian conduct affecting service might have implications
- Specific procedures

### Status-Based Jurisdiction

UCMJ jurisdiction tied to member's status.

- Active duty: Full UCMJ
- IDT: UCMJ during IDT
- Off-duty: Limited UCMJ
