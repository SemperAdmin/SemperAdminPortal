## What MGIB-SR Is

The Montgomery GI Bill - Selected Reserve (MGIB-SR), also known as Chapter 1606, is the education benefit for drilling Selected Reserve members. Per 10 USC Chapter 1606, eligible reservists receive monthly payments while pursuing approved education programs.

This is one of the strongest reservist benefits. I&I admin counsels members on eligibility and application.

## Eligibility

A reservist qualifies for MGIB-SR when meeting all of these.

- Six-year reserve service obligation (initial enlistment or reenlistment)
- Completed initial active duty for training (IADT)
- High school diploma or equivalent before completing IADT
- Currently in good standing with unit
- Maintained satisfactory participation throughout obligation
- Not eligible for higher GI Bill benefits (member chooses one program)

The 6-year contract is the foundation. Without the 6-year SelRes commitment, the member does not qualify for MGIB-SR.

## Notice of Basic Eligibility (NOBE)

Eligible reservists receive a Notice of Basic Eligibility (NOBE) from HQMC. The NOBE.

- Confirms the member's eligibility for MGIB-SR
- Provides documentation for the VA application
- Establishes the start date of eligibility
- Required for VA Form 22-1990 application

I&I admin coordinates NOBE issuance for new SMCR members.

## Benefit Amounts and Duration

### Monthly Payments

MGIB-SR provides monthly payments while pursuing approved education. Specific amounts.

- Updated annually by Congress
- Tied to enrollment level (full-time, three-quarter time, half-time)
- Paid directly to the reservist
- Reservist uses the funds to cover education costs

Reference current VA rates for amounts.

### Duration of Eligibility

The benefit applies for.

- 36 months of full-time benefits
- Equivalent benefits for part-time enrollment
- 14 years from date of basic eligibility (or until separation if earlier)
- Until member separates from SelRes

The 14-year window matters. Members who delay using benefits might run out of time.

## Approved Education Programs

MGIB-SR pays for approved programs at.

- Colleges and universities (degree programs)
- Vocational and technical schools
- Apprenticeship and on-the-job training
- Flight training (with limits)
- Correspondence courses (limited rates)
- Cooperative training
- High school and equivalent programs

The VA maintains the list of approved programs.

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Verify Member Eligibility

When a reservist asks about MGIB-SR.

- Pull MCTFS to verify SelRes status
- Check enlistment contract for 6-year obligation
- Verify satisfactory participation
- Identify any disqualifying factors

### Step 2 - Provide Information

Brief the member on key points.

- Eligibility requirements
- Benefit amounts (current rates)
- 14-year window for using benefits
- Approved programs
- Application process

### Step 3 - Coordinate NOBE Issuance

For members new to SMCR or members needing the NOBE.

- Coordinate with HQMC for NOBE issuance
- Provide member with NOBE copy
- Verify member has documentation for VA application

### Step 4 - Direct Member to VA Application

Member applies through VA.

- Complete VA Form 22-1990 (Application for Education Benefits)
- Submit to VA via mail, fax, or online (VA.gov)
- Include NOBE
- Include enrollment information from school
- Wait for VA processing (typically 30 to 60 days)

### Step 5 - Track Member Continuing Eligibility

Throughout the member's service.

- Verify continued SelRes status
- Notify VA if member transfers to IRR (eligibility changes)
- Notify VA if member separates (eligibility ends)
- Maintain documentation

## When MGIB-SR Eligibility Changes

The benefit changes when.

- Member transfers to IRR: Loses MGIB-SR (eligibility tied to SelRes status)
- Member transfers to Standby: Loses MGIB-SR
- Member separates from Reserves: Loses MGIB-SR
- Member's 14-year window expires: Loses remaining benefits
- Member uses 36 months of benefits: Exhausts the entitlement
- Member becomes ineligible due to participation issues: Might lose benefits

## Common Member Questions

### "I use MGIB-SR while drilling?"

Yes. MGIB-SR is designed for use while drilling. Most beneficiaries take classes around drill weekends.

### "How much will I receive per month?"

Specific amount depends on enrollment level and current VA rates. Direct member to VA.gov for current rates.

### "I use it for graduate school?"

Yes. MGIB-SR covers graduate programs at approved schools.

### "What if I'm activated for AT or mobilization?"

MGIB-SR continues during AT (typically 12-14 days does not affect education enrollment). Mobilization to active duty over 30 days might transition the member to higher GI Bill programs (Post-9/11 GI Bill - Chapter 33).

### "What happens if I separate from the Reserves?"

Eligibility ends at separation. Use any remaining months before separation date if possible.

### "I combine MGIB-SR with civilian financial aid?"

Generally yes. Coordinate with school financial aid office on stacking benefits.

## MGIB-SR vs Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33)

Reservists might have access to both programs. Knowing the difference matters.

| Feature | MGIB-SR (1606) | Post-9/11 GI Bill (33) |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| Required service | 6-year SelRes obligation | 90+ days active duty (combined service) |
| Payment | Monthly to member | Tuition direct to school + housing allowance + book stipend |
| Amount | Lower monthly amount | Generally higher total benefit |
| Duration | 36 months | 36 months (for most) |
| Use during drilling | Yes | Yes if otherwise eligible |
| Transferability | No | Yes (to family with conditions) |

Members with both options should evaluate carefully. Post-9/11 generally provides more total benefit.

## Common I&I Errors on MGIB-SR

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Telling member they qualify without verifying 6-year contract.** Verify enlistment obligation before confirming eligibility.
2. **Wrong monthly amount quoted.** Rates change annually. Always reference current VA rates.
3. **Not flagging the 14-year window.** Members forget the window exists. Track and remind.
4. **MGIB-SR confused with MGIB Active Duty (Chapter 30).** Different programs. Verify which applies.
5. **NOBE not coordinated for new members.** New SelRes members eligible but lack NOBE. Process through HQMC.

## Coordination Touchpoints

MGIB-SR processing touches.

- **HQMC**: NOBE issuance
- **VA Education Office**: Application processing
- **Member's school certifying official**: Enrollment verification
- **DFAS**: For any related allotments
- **MCRSC**: For component status verification

## Related Roles

Education benefits affect every drilling reservist's career planning.

- **Marines** (drilling reservists) apply for MGIB-SR and use the benefit while drilling.
- **Leaders** counsel members on education benefits as career mentoring.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member education planning through pre-separation counseling.
