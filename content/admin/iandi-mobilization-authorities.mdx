## Mobilization Authority Framework

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10 and 10 USC, multiple authorities allow Reserve mobilization to active duty.

## Authorities Under 10 USC

### 10 USC 12301(a) - Full Mobilization

Highest authority for Reserve mobilization.

#### Trigger

- Declaration of war by Congress, OR
- Declaration of national emergency

#### Scope

- Total Force called up
- Includes all Ready Reserve, Standby Reserve, Retired Reserve
- Indefinite duration during emergency

#### Use

- War or imminent war
- Major national emergency
- Specific historical examples include WWII, Korean War, Vietnam War (specific aspects)

### 10 USC 12302 - Partial Mobilization

Major mobilization for declared emergencies.

#### Trigger

- President declares national emergency
- Congress need not declare war
- Specific operational need

#### Scope

- Up to 1 million Reserve members
- Up to 24 months of active duty
- Includes SELRES (Selected Reserve, which covers SMCR units, IMAs, and Active Reserve Marines) and IRR (not Standby for partial)

#### Use

- Major operations
- Specific national security needs
- Historical examples include Persian Gulf War, Iraq War, Afghanistan

### 10 USC 12304 - Presidential Reserve Call-Up (PRCU)

Limited mobilization for specific operations.

#### Trigger

- President orders call-up
- Specific operational mission
- No Congressional declaration required

#### Scope

- Up to 200,000 Reserve members
- Up to 365 days of active duty
- Includes SELRES and IRR

#### Use

- Specific operational missions
- Limited duration operations
- Historical examples include specific Operations

### 10 USC 12301(b) - Annual Training (AT)

Standard SELRES annual training.

#### Trigger

- Routine training authority
- Standard SELRES participation

#### Scope

- 12-14 days typically
- All SELRES members per cycle

#### Use

- Standard annual training
- Routine readiness maintenance

### 10 USC 12301(d) - Voluntary Active Duty

Member volunteers for active duty.

#### Trigger

- Member volunteers
- Command approval
- Specific operational need

#### Scope

- Specific to mission
- Member's voluntary commitment
- Specific duration per orders

#### Use

- Specific missions
- Member's career interest
- Specific operational support

### 10 USC 12305 - Stop-Loss

Suspends member separations during specific situations.

#### Trigger

- Specific national emergency
- Specific operational requirement

#### Scope

- Members otherwise eligible for separation
- Specific MOSs typically
- Limited duration

#### Use

- Maintain force during operations
- Specific historical use during wars

## Authority Comparison

| Authority | Trigger | Components | Duration | Numbers |
|-----------|---------|------------|----------|---------|
| 12301(a) | War declaration or NE | All Reserve | Indefinite | All |
| 12302 | NE declared | Ready Reserve | Up to 24 months | Up to 1M |
| 12304 | President orders | Ready Reserve | Up to 365 days | Up to 200K |
| 12301(b) | Routine | SELRES | 12-14 days | SELRES |
| 12301(d) | Volunteer | All Reserve | Per orders | Volunteer |
| 12305 | NE | Members eligible to separate | Specific | Specific |

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

When mobilization affects unit.

### Step 1 - Identify Specific Authority

Determine which 10 USC authority applies.

- Specific notice from MARFORRES or HQMC
- Specific operational mission
- Specific authority cited in orders

### Step 2 - Identify Affected Members

Per authority and operational requirement.

- Members in specific MOSs
- Members in specific units
- Specific selection criteria

### Step 3 - Process Mobilization Orders

Per MROWS or specific orders system.

- Mobilization orders generated
- Member notification
- Specific reporting instructions

### Step 4 - Coordinate Pre-Mobilization

Specific pre-mobilization processing.

- Medical and dental screening
- Administrative preparation
- Family preparation
- USERRA notification

### Step 5 - Process Active Duty Status Change

When member reports.

- UD/MIPS active duty start
- Active duty pay activates
- BAH and BAS at active duty rate
- TRICARE Prime active duty

### Step 6 - Coordinate During Mobilization

Throughout mobilization period.

- Maintain communication with deployed members
- Family support
- Specific issues during deployment
- Pay accuracy verification

### Step 7 - Process Demobilization

When mobilization ends.

- Active duty status ends
- Return to drilling status (or other component)
- TAMP coverage activated
- Reintegration support

- **MARFORRES G-3**: For mobilization planning

- **MCRSC**: For component-specific support
- **Local installation receiving members**: For active duty processing
- **Member's family**: For family support
- **Member's civilian employer**: For USERRA coordination

## Common Authority Confusion Points

### Annual Training vs Mobilization

AT (12301(b)) is not mobilization.

- AT is routine training
- Mobilization is specific operational duty
- Different processing

### Voluntary Active Duty vs Mobilization

12301(d) volunteer is not mobilization.

- Member volunteers
- Specific career interest
- Different from involuntary mobilization

### Standby Eligibility for Various Authorities

Standby members subject only to 12301(a).

- Not subject to 12302 partial mobilization
- Not subject to 12304 PRCU
- Specific exception

### IRR Eligibility

IRR subject to 12301(a), 12302, and 12304.

- Standard mobilization pool
- Specific selection per situation
- Not voluntary call-up category
