## UCMJ Jurisdiction Categories

Per UCMJ Article 2 (10 USC 802<Citation source="10 USC 802" />) and MCRAMM Chapter 11, UCMJ jurisdiction over Reservists varies by status.

### Full Jurisdiction (Active Duty)

When member is on active duty.

- All UCMJ provisions apply
- All offenses subject to UCMJ
- Same jurisdiction as active duty Marine

### Limited Jurisdiction (IDT)

When member is in IDT status.

- UCMJ applies during IDT period
- Limited to conduct during IDT
- Limited to offenses occurring during IDT

### No Jurisdiction (Inactive)

When member is off-duty (not in any military duty status).

- Generally no UCMJ jurisdiction
- Specific exceptions for some serious offenses
- Civilian conduct generally outside UCMJ

## Case Examples

### Member's Drill Weekend Misconduct

Member's misconduct during drill weekend.

- Status: IDT
- UCMJ: Applies during IDT
- Process: Specific UCMJ procedures during IDT

### Member's Travel to Drill Misconduct

Member's misconduct during travel to drill.

- Status: Specific travel status analysis
- UCMJ: Might apply per portal-to-portal coverage
- Process: Coordinate with SJA

### Member's Civilian Job Misconduct

Member's civilian misconduct.

- Status: Inactive (off-duty)
- UCMJ: Generally does not apply
- Process: Civilian legal authority, possible reporting

### Member's Mobilization Misconduct

Member's misconduct during mobilization.

- Status: Active duty (Title 10)
- UCMJ: Full UCMJ applies
- Process: Standard active duty UCMJ procedures
