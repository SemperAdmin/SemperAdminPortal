## Overseas Housing Allowance (OHA)

Per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 68, Marines stationed overseas receive OHA instead of standard CONUS BAH.

### How OHA Differs From BAH

- OHA based on actual rental costs (member submits lease)
- OHA includes reimbursement for utilities and other housing costs
- OHA covers move-in and move-out costs (specific allowances)
- BAH is fixed by zip code; OHA reimburses actual costs up to a cap

### OHA Components

OHA has three parts.

| Component | Coverage |
|-----------|----------|
| Rental Allowance | Actual rent up to maximum for location |
| Utility/Recurring Maintenance Allowance | Average utility costs for location |
| Move-In Housing Allowance (MIHA) | One-time setup costs at new station |

### OHA Eligibility

- Active duty Marines on overseas tours
- Marines not in government quarters at overseas station
- Specific accompanied vs unaccompanied rules apply

### OHA Documentation Requirements

Per DOD FMR Chapter 68, member submits.

- Signed lease agreement
- Utility cost documentation if claiming utility allowance
- Move-in receipts for MIHA claims
- Annual lease renewal documentation

### Performance Steps for OHA

When member arrives at overseas station.

1. Member receives PCS orders to overseas location
2. Member secures off-base housing
3. Member submits lease and OHA application
4. PAC verifies lease meets OHA standards
5. PAC processes OHA via UD/MIPS at correct rate
6. MIHA processed for one-time setup costs
7. Annual review of lease and OHA continuation

## Partial BAH

Per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />, partial BAH applies in specific limited situations.

### When Partial BAH Applies

- Member living in government quarters but with specific eligibility
- Specific transitional periods
- Limited geographical or duty situations

### Partial BAH Rate

- Smaller amount than standard BAH
- Set per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" />
- Different transaction code than standard BAH

Partial BAH is not common. Most members fall into standard BAH categories.

## BAH for Single SNCO and Senior Officers

Per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />, single SNCO (E-7+) and senior officers have specific BAH provisions.

### Standard Off-Base BAH

- Without-dependents rate at duty station
- Standard BAH processing
- No special category

### Government Quarters Eligibility

- Single E-7 and above may opt out of barracks
- SNCO quarters may apply at some installations
- Specific senior officer quarters at some locations

The category is standard BAH. The "special" aspect is that single SNCOs and senior officers have more housing options than junior enlisted.

## BAH for Single Junior Enlisted

E-1 through E-3 generally live in barracks (government quarters). BAH does not apply.

### Exceptions

- Hardship cases approved by command
- Members with specific health requirements
- Specific situations approved per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />

When approved, member draws standard without-dependents BAH or specific category per circumstance.

## BAH and Government Quarters

When Marines occupy government quarters, BAH typically does not apply.

### Types of Government Quarters

- Barracks (junior enlisted)
- BEQ (Bachelor Enlisted Quarters) for senior enlisted
- BOQ (Bachelor Officer Quarters)
- Family housing (for accompanied tours)
- Senior officer quarters

### BAH Rules with Government Quarters

- Member in government quarters: No BAH
- Member temporarily without quarters during transition: BAH may apply for transition period
- Member with quarters used for command convenience: Specific situations may apply

## BAH for Sea Duty

Marines on sea duty may have specific BAH situations.

### Sea Duty BAH

- BAH continues at home port for sea-duty Marines
- Family residence at home port draws standard BAH
- Specific sea duty allowances may layer on top

### BAH When Underway

- BAH does not change when ship is underway
- Member's home port BAH continues
- Combat zone or hostile fire pays may apply during deployment

## BAH for Senior Reserve Members

Per MCRAMM Chapter 12, reservists on active duty receive standard BAH.

### Reserve Member on Active Duty Over 30 Days

- BAH applies at active duty rate
- Use member's home zip code or duty location per orders
- Standard BAH processing

### Reserve Member on Drilling Status

- No BAH for inactive duty (drill weekends)
- Drill pay only
- BAH only during AT or active duty orders over 30 days

## Performance Steps for Special Categories

### Step 1 - Identify Applicable Category

Determine which special BAH category applies.

- Overseas: OHA processing
- Sea duty: Standard BAH at home port
- Government quarters with allowance: Partial BAH
- Reserve on active duty: Standard BAH at active duty rate

### Step 2 - Apply Specific Calculation

Each category has different calculation rules.

- OHA: Actual cost-based with documentation
- Partial BAH: Fixed reduced rate
- BAH-Diff: Fixed differential rate
- Standard BAH: Zip code, rank, dependent status

### Step 3 - Process via Correct UD/MIPS Code

Each category has its own transaction code per MCTFSCODESMAN.

- Standard BAH: Generic BAH codes
- OHA: OHA-specific transaction codes
- BAH-Diff: BAH-Diff transaction codes
- Partial BAH: Partial BAH codes

### Step 4 - Document the Basis

PAC retains documentation supporting the special category.

- OHA: Lease agreements, utility documentation
- Partial BAH: Justification for category
- Hardship-based BAH: Approved hardship documentation

## Common Special Category Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Member overseas drawing standard BAH instead of OHA.** OHA rate not applied at overseas station. Fix by processing OHA with effective date.
2. **OHA continued after member returns CONUS.** OHA stops, BAH starts at CONUS station. Fix by stopping OHA and starting BAH.
3. **Partial BAH applied when full BAH appropriate.** Member off-base but processed at partial rate. Fix by upgrading to full BAH.
4. **Reserve drilling member drawing BAH.** Drilling reservist not on active duty orders should not draw BAH. Stop and document.
5. **Sea duty BAH location wrong.** Member home port BAH at wrong zip code. Fix by updating home port zip code.

## OHA Annual Review

Per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 68, OHA recipients undergo annual review.

- Verify lease still active
- Verify utility costs current
- Verify OHA rate current
- Member confirms continued accuracy

## Coordination Touchpoints

Special BAH categories touch.

- **DTMO**: For OHA rates and BAH information
- **Local installation housing office**: For government quarters status
- **Overseas housing officials**: For OHA-specific assistance
- **Member's chain of command**: For hardship and special situation documentation

## Related Roles

Special BAH categories affect specific groups of Marines.

- **Marines** in special situations submit appropriate documentation and verify pay.
- **Leaders** counsel members on applicable category and route documentation.
- **Commanders** support hardship cases and unique situation processing.
