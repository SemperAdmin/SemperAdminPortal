## Pre-Mobilization Screening Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10 and Chapter 6, pre-mobilization screening verifies member readiness across five domains.

- Medical readiness
- Dental readiness
- Administrative readiness
- Training readiness
- Family readiness

Members not ready in any domain require remediation before mobilization.

## Medical Screening

Most comprehensive screening domain.

### Pre-Deployment Health Assessment (PDHA)

Per DoDI 6490.03<Citation source="DoDI 6490.03" />.

- Physical health screening
- Mental health screening
- Specific deployment-related health questions
- Updated immunizations
- Blood draws and labs as required

### Specific Medical Items

- Current PHA within 12 months
- Updated immunizations per deployment requirements
- Specific medical waivers for deployment-limiting conditions
- Pre-deployment medications and supplies

### Medical Disqualifying Conditions

Specific conditions may disqualify member from deployment.

- Significant chronic conditions
- Recent surgeries
- Specific psychiatric conditions
- Conditions requiring specific care unavailable in deployment area

## Dental Screening

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6.

### Required Dental Status

- Class 1 (no treatment needed) or
- Class 2 (routine treatment, can wait)

### Class 3 Members

- Significant dental issues blocking deployment
- Treatment required before mobilization
- Reserve Dental Care for deployment treatment

### Class 4 Members

- Dental status not assessed
- Requires assessment and possibly treatment
- Specific procedures

## Administrative Screening

For member's administrative readiness.

### MCTFS Data

- Member's MCTFS data current
- Address and contact information current
- Specific data elements verified

### Dependent Data

- Current NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />
- DEERS enrollment for dependents
- Specific dependent verification

### SGLI Election

- Current SGLI election (recommended max coverage)
- Beneficiary current
- Specific updates as needed

### Record of Emergency Data (RED)

- Current RED with valid contacts
- Specific updates for life events
- Verified during pre-mobilization

### Will and Power of Attorney

- Current will recommended
- Power of attorney for specific situations
- Coordinate with legal services

## Training Screening

For member's MOS and required training.

### MOS Qualification

- Current MOS qualification
- Specific MOS-related training current
- Refresher training if needed

### Required Training

Standard training requirements.

- Sexual Assault Prevention and Response (SAPR)
- Information Security (INFOSEC)
- Specific other annual training
- Training current within required intervals

### PFT and CFT

- Current PFT and CFT scores
- Within deployment standards
- Specific waivers if applicable

### Rifle Qualification

- Annual rifle qualification current
- Specific qualifications per assignment

### Specific Deployment Training

For specific deployments.

- Counter-improvised explosive device (C-IED) training
- Cultural awareness
- Language training (if applicable)
- Specific mission training

## Family Readiness Screening

For member with family.

### Family Care Plan

If applicable.

- Current Family Care Plan
- Specific care arrangements
- Documentation current

### Family Briefing

- Family briefed on mobilization
- Family resources identified
- Family support plan in place

### Specific Family Issues

- Health issues
- Special needs family members
- Specific family situations affecting member

### Family Information Update

- Family contact information current
- Emergency contacts verified
- Specific notification preferences

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

When unit alerted for mobilization.

### Step 1 - Identify Affected Members

Per mobilization roster.

- List of members to screen
- Specific timeline for screening
- Specific deployment information

### Step 2 - Schedule Screenings

For each member.

- Medical screening at MTF
- Dental screening
- Administrative review session
- Training validation
- Family readiness check

### Step 3 - Conduct Screenings

Coordinate completion.

- Medical and dental at MTF or providers
- Administrative review with member
- Training records review
- Family readiness check

### Step 4 - Identify Gaps

For members with readiness issues.

- Document specific gaps
- Identify remediation needed
- Specific timeline for resolution

### Step 5 - Coordinate Remediation

Address each gap.

- Medical treatment
- Dental treatment
- Administrative updates
- Required training completion
- Family preparation

### Step 6 - Verify Final Readiness

Before member reports for mobilization.

- All screenings complete
- All gaps addressed
- Member cleared for mobilization

### Step 7 - Document Screening

For audit and accountability.

- Specific screening completion
- Gaps identified and addressed
- Final clearance

## Common Pre-Mobilization Screening Issues

### Medical Issues

Members discover health issues during screening.

- Treatment required before mobilization
- Possible deferment if uncorrectable
- Specific procedures

### Dental Issues

Members in Class 3 status.

- Expedited dental treatment
- Reserve Dental Care for deployment
- Track to Class 1 or Class 2

### Administrative Gaps

Members with outdated records.

- Coordinate updates
- Specific document submissions
- Verify completion

### Training Gaps

Members missing required training.

- Coordinate completion
- AT or specific training periods
- Possible online training options

### Family Readiness Issues

Members with family situation issues.

- Family Care Plan completion
- Family support coordination
- Specific accommodations

## Pre-Mobilization Timeline

Standard timeline.

| Time Before Mobilization | Action |
|--------------------------|--------|
| 90 days | Initial member notification, screening scheduled |
| 60 days | Medical and dental screenings completed |
| 45 days | Administrative review completed |
| 30 days | Training gaps addressed |
| 14 days | Family preparation finalized |
| Reporting day | Member reports cleared |

## Coordination Touchpoints

Pre-mobilization screening touches.

- **Local installation MTF**: For medical and dental
- **Reserve Health Readiness Program**: For specific reserve provisions
- **Member's family**: For family preparation
- **Member's civilian employer**: For USERRA notification
- **Mobilization receiving unit**: For specific mobilization information

## Quality Control on Pre-Mobilization

Common errors.

1. **Insufficient time for screening.** Late notification creates compressed timeline.
2. **Medical issues not promptly addressed.** Member's health issues delay mobilization.
3. **Family Care Plan missing.** Member reports without required FCP.
4. **Training gaps not closed.** Member arrives without required training.

## Related Roles

Pre-mobilization screening affects every mobilizing Reserve member.

- **Marines** complete screening and address identified gaps.
- **Leaders** at units track member screening progress.
- **Commanders** at I&I coordinate unit pre-mobilization processing.
