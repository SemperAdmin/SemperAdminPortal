## Reserve vs Active Promotion Policy

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 Section 6 and MCO P1400.32<Citation source="MCO P1400.32" />, reserve promotions follow the same general framework as active duty promotions but with reserve-specific differences.

- Quarterly cycles for E-2 through E-5 (vs monthly for active duty corporal and below)
- Reserve-specific composite scores and eligibility computations
- Reserve promotion warrants generated by the unit and routed differently
- SNCO and officer promotions process through reserve-specific selection boards

I&I admin executes promotions every quarter and tracks members through eligibility windows.

## Enlisted Promotion Cycles

### Reserve Quarterly Cycle

Reserve enlisted promotions process quarterly per MCO P1400.32<Citation source="MCO P1400.32" /> Volume 2.

- Q1: October 1 (effective date for promotions earned through prior quarter)
- Q2: January 1
- Q3: April 1
- Q4: July 1

### Eligibility Computation

Per MCRAMM, reserve eligibility requires.

- Time in grade (TIG) per the published reserve TIG requirements
- Time in service (TIS) per the published reserve TIS requirements
- MOS qualification and currency
- Last fitness report or proficiency mark satisfactory
- No pending administrative or disciplinary actions
- Required training completion

Reserve TIG requirements differ slightly from active duty due to drilling status.

### Composite Score for Reserve

For LCpl through Sgt promotions, composite score calculation includes.

- Time in service points
- Time in grade points
- Conduct marks
- Proficiency marks
- Awards points
- Education points (high school, college, MarineNet courses)
- PFT and CFT scores
- Rifle qualification

The cutting score for promotion is established by HQMC and announced via MARADMIN.

## Officer Promotion Process

Reserve officer promotions follow MCO P1400.32<Citation source="MCO P1400.32" /> Volume 1 with reserve-specific provisions.

- Selection boards convene for promotion to grades from O-3 through O-9
- Reserve selection boards consider reserve performance and active duty equivalent factors
- Promotion to higher grades requires confirmation per applicable statute
- Mandatory removal dates apply per officer policy

I&I admin processes officer promotion records and supports board preparation but does not run the boards.

## Performance Steps for Quarterly Promotion Processing

### Step 1 - Pull Eligibility Roster

Before each quarterly cycle.

- Pull the eligibility roster from MOL Promotion Module
- Verify each member's MCTFS data is current
- Identify members with eligibility issues to resolve
- Review unit composite scores for accuracy

### Step 2 - Address Composite Score Issues

For members with low composite scores due to data errors.

- Verify all marks (Pro/Con) are correctly posted
- Verify all awards are posted
- Verify education credits are posted
- Verify PFT and CFT scores current
- Submit UD/MIPS corrections for any data errors

### Step 3 - Process Will Not Promote Decisions

Where commander does not recommend a member for promotion.

- Document via Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />
- Submit "Will Not Promote" entry via MOL Promotion Module
- Member acknowledges with signature
- Process before the cycle closes

### Step 4 - Generate Promotion Warrants

For members selected.

- Generate warrant from MOL or unit format
- Warrant includes member identification, new grade, effective date
- Route to commander for signature
- Print for member presentation

### Step 5 - Process Unit Diary Entries

After warrants signed.

- Submit UD/MIPS promotion transaction
- Verify EDFR returns clean
- Member's pay reflects new grade next pay period

### Step 6 - File and Distribute

Final processing.

- Original warrant to member at promotion ceremony
- Copy to OMPF via PAC
- Copy to unit personnel file
- Pay reflects new grade in next drill pay processing

## Common Promotion Errors

These hit on quarterly processing.

1. **Member's composite score wrong due to missing data.** Pro/Con marks, awards, education credits not posted. Fix by submitting UD/MIPS corrections before the cycle closes.
2. **Will Not Promote without Page 11.** Per policy, commander must document Will Not Promote with Page 11. Without Page 11, member appeal possible.
3. **Promotion processed for ineligible member.** Time in grade or service shortfall not caught. Audit eligibility carefully.
4. **Effective date wrong.** Reserve promotions effective on quarterly date, not the date of warrant signature. Use correct effective date.
5. **Unit diary entry missing or failed.** Promotion warrant signed but UD/MIPS transaction not submitted or failed. Member's grade does not update in MCTFS.

## SNCO Promotion Process

For staff sergeant and above, promotions process through HQMC selection boards.

- Eligibility based on time in grade, service, MOS qualification
- Selection board reviews master brief sheets and records
- Board produces selection list
- Promotions sequenced based on board outcome
- I&I admin supports board prep with record audits

## Coordination Touchpoints

Promotion processing touches.

- **HQMC Promotion Section**: Cutting scores, selection board results
- **MOL Promotion Module**: System for tracking and processing
- **Supporting PAC**: For complex eligibility cases
- **MCRSC**: For IRR and Standby promotion considerations
- **MCTFS Help Desk**: For processing failures

## Related Roles

Promotion processing involves multiple roles.

- **Marines** monitor their composite scores via MOL and ensure all data is current.
- **Leaders** verify Marines' record accuracy and recommend or do not recommend promotion.
- **Commanders** approve or do not approve promotions and sign warrants.
