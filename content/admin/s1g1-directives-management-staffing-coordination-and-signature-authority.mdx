## Role and responsibility

The drafting branch routes the directive draft through the chop chain per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 17 (Staffing and Clearing Directives). The DCP clerk tracks coordination receipts. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the route to the signing authority. The commander or signature authority validates authority to issue per Chapter 1 paragraph 6 and signs per the delegation per paragraph 7. The legal advisor reviews for sufficiency at the coordination stage.

Source documents flow from the directive draft, the chop chain coordination receipts, the signature delegation memorandum per Chapter 1 paragraph 7, the language and pronoun review per Chapter 1 paragraph 15 (Gender Specific Language and Personal Pronouns / Sex-Based Language and Biological Term Usage), the abbreviation and acronym review per Chapter 1 paragraph 16, the letterhead per Chapter 1 paragraph 18 (Letterhead Stationary and Style of Type), the Distribution Statement per Chapter 1 paragraph 19, and the printing and distribution plan per Chapter 1 paragraph 20. Coordination outputs include the chopped draft, the signature delegation validation, the joint coordination memoranda per Chapter 4 paragraphs 7-8 (when applicable), and the signed directive.

Per MCO 5215.1K Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 6 (Authority to Issue Directives), the authority to issue runs to specific commands and offices identified in the order rules. Per paragraph 7 (Delegating Signature Authority), the commander or principal delegates signature authority in writing per the criteria. Per paragraph 17 (Staffing and Clearing Directives), the chop chain runs through every affected staff section before signature.

## Trigger events

- New directive draft routed for staffing.
- Higher headquarters directive triggers downstream unit directive needing chop.
- Signature delegation memorandum requires update on assumption of command.
- Joint directive preliminary or final coordination opens per Chapter 4 paragraphs 7-8.
- Legal review identifies a chop-chain gap.
- Internal review per Chapter 1 paragraph 14 surfaces a stale signature delegation memorandum.

## Processing workflow

1. Validate authority to issue. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 6 (Authority to Issue Directives), confirm the issuing command holds the authority.
2. Validate signature authority delegation in writing. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 7 (Delegating Signature Authority), verify the written delegation memorandum on file. A missing delegation triggers a rebuild before routing.
3. Route the draft through the chop chain. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 17 (Staffing and Clearing Directives), route through every affected staff section with concur/non-concur tracking and a comment matrix.
4. Apply language and pronoun rules. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 15 (Gender Specific Language and Personal Pronouns / Sex-Based Language and Biological Term Usage, post-CH-3), apply the current usage rule. The Admin CH-3 update aligned the paragraph with E.O. 14168.
5. Apply abbreviation and acronym rules. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 16 (Abbreviations, Acronyms, and Reference Aids), expand acronyms at first use. Include an acronyms list when warranted.
6. Apply letterhead and type style. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 18 (Letterhead Stationary and Style of Type), use the prescribed letterhead and font.
7. Apply Distribution Statement. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 19 (Distribution Statements), set the Distribution Statement (typically Statement A or limited) per the content sensitivity.
8. Coordinate printing and distribution. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 20 (Printing and Distribution), the DCP coordinates printing and the distribution per the list.
9. Coordinate joint preliminary and final coordination. Per Chapter 4 paragraph 7 (Preliminary Coordination) and paragraph 8 (Final Coordination), joint directives route through partner commands at both phases.
10. Address supplemental directive promulgation. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 5 (Promulgation of Supplemental Directives), subordinate commands promulgate supplemental directives as needed. The S-1 officer validates supplemental scope.

## Common pitfalls

- Signature delegation memorandum not on file or stale. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 7, the delegation is in writing. Audit the file at command assumption per Chapter 1 paragraph 13.
- Chop chain skipped on an affected staff section. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 17, every affected section chops. A skipped section triggers post-issuance disputes.
- Distribution Statement omitted. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 19, the statement is mandatory. Set the statement on the letterhead.
- Acronyms not expanded at first use. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 16, acronyms expand at first use. A non-expanded acronym confuses readers and triggers audit finding.
- Joint preliminary coordination skipped. Per Chapter 4 paragraph 7, preliminary coordination identifies partner objections early. A skip risks a stalled final coordination.

## Decision points

- Signature delegation versus by-direction signature. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 1 paragraph 7, written delegation runs as standalone signature. By-direction signature runs under existing delegation per the order rules.
- Distribution Statement selection. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 19, Statement A is the standard. Limited Distribution applies when content sensitivity warrants restriction.
- Joint coordination versus single-principal coordination. Per Chapter 4 paragraphs 7-8 versus Chapter 1 paragraph 17, joint directives require partner principal coordination at preliminary and final phases.
- Supplemental directive scope. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 5, subordinate commands promulgate supplemental directives only when the parent leaves scope for supplemental rules. Beyond-scope supplements are out of bounds.
