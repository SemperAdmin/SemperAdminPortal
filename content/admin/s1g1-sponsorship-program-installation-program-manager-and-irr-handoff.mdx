## Role and responsibility

The Installation Information, Referral and Relocation (IR&R) Program Manager runs installation-level sponsorship coordination per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(9). The installation commander oversees installation-level program execution per paragraph 4.b.(5). The unit SC coordinates the handoff between the unit and the installation. The S-1 chief tracks IR&R handoff completion against the inbound roster. Marine and Family Programs Division (MF) at HQMC per paragraph 4.b.(2) oversees the IR&R program at the strategic level.

Source documents flow from the inbound PCS roster, the sponsor assignment letter per Enclosure (7), the Welcome Aboard Letters per Enclosures (2) and (4)-(6), family advocacy concerns surfacing during sponsorship, special-needs referrals (Exceptional Family Member Program when applicable), and the installation Marine Corps Community Services (MCCS) intake. IR&R outputs include the installation handoff record, the family advocacy or special-needs referral, the inbound brief attendance, and the IR&R follow-up actions logged on the unit sponsorship roster.

Per MCO 1320.11H paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R Installation Program Manager provides relocation assistance, family advocacy referral, special-needs coordination, and the installation-level inbound brief covering housing, schools, employment, child care, and medical care. MCO P1700.27B<Citation source="MCO P1700.27B" /> covers Marine Corps Community Services underwriting the IR&R function. MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" /> covers the Family Advocacy Program for inbound family advocacy needs. MCO 1754.10B<Citation source="MCO 1754.10B" /> covers related family programs.

## Trigger events

- Inbound PCS with family triggers IR&R handoff.
- OCONUS inbound triggers extended IR&R coordination.
- Family advocacy concern surfaces during sponsor outreach.
- Special-needs request (Exceptional Family Member, special education) surfaces.
- Inbound brief schedule opens.
- IR&R Program Manager rotation triggers point-of-contact refresh.
- Annual sponsorship metrics cycle.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the installation IR&R Program Manager. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(9), the installation IR&R office holds the Program Manager point of contact. The SC pulls the current name and contact information.
2. Coordinate inbound family handoff. Per paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R Program Manager receives the inbound family roster for relocation and family services planning.
3. Route family advocacy or special-needs referrals through IR&R. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" /> and paragraph 4.b.(9), route inbound family advocacy or special-needs concerns through the IR&R Program Manager.
4. Provide the IR&R Program Manager with PCS volume data. The SC shares volume data to support installation resource planning.
5. Coordinate IR&R inbound brief attendance. The IR&R Program Manager runs the installation portion of the inbound brief covering housing, schools, employment, child care, and medical care.
6. Track IR&R follow-up actions. The SC logs IR&R follow-up actions on the unit sponsorship roster (housing assignment, school enrollment, employment referral, family services intake).
7. Coordinate Family Advocacy Program touchpoints. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" />, the IR&R Program Manager routes Family Advocacy Program intake.
8. Maintain documentation of IR&R coordination. Stage the IR&R coordination record per the Privacy Act paragraph 5.c.
9. Brief the unit commander on installation handoff posture. The S-1 officer briefs the commander on installation coordination at the metrics cycle.
10. Audit handoff completion at the metrics cycle. The SC audits handoff completion at the Enclosure (3) metrics cycle.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Installation IR&R Office. Per paragraph 4.b.(9).
- Marine Corps Community Services (MCCS). Per MCO P1700.27B.
- Family Advocacy Program. Per MCO 1754.9B.
- Unit Sponsorship Roster. Tracks handoff completion.
- Installation Welcome Aboard Center. Per the installation order.

### Forms

- IR&R Handoff Memorandum. Local format documenting handoff.
- Family Advocacy Program Referral. Per MCO 1754.9B.
- Special-Needs Referral. Routes through IR&R per paragraph 4.b.(9).
- Inbound Brief Attendance Record. Local format.

## Common pitfalls

- IR&R Program Manager point of contact stale. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(9), the SC pulls the current point of contact at every cycle.
- Family advocacy or special-needs referrals routed outside IR&R. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" /> and paragraph 4.b.(9), the routing runs through IR&R.
- Inbound family arrival without housing or school coordination. Per paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R Program Manager runs the housing and schools coordination. Route early.
- Privacy Act breach in IR&R coordination. Per paragraph 5.c, PII rides through approved channels.
- Inbound brief attendance not logged. Per paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R portion of the inbound brief drives installation resource access. Log attendance.

## Decision points

- IR&R routing for unaccompanied inbound. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Enclosure (6) and paragraph 4.b.(9), unaccompanied OCONUS still coordinates with IR&R for in-and-out processing, even without family scope.
- Family Advocacy versus general IR&R intake. Per MCO 1754.9B<Citation source="MCO 1754.9B" /> versus paragraph 4.b.(9), Family Advocacy intake covers abuse, neglect, or domestic violence cases. General IR&R intake covers routine relocation services.
- Installation versus unit responsibility for inbound brief. Per paragraphs 4.b.(5) and 4.b.(9), the installation IR&R Program Manager runs the installation portion. The unit S-1 runs the unit-specific brief.
