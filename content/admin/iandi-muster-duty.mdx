## What Muster Duty Is

Per MCRAMM Chapter 3 Section 2.3 and 10 USC 12319<Citation source="10 USC 12319" />, Muster Duty (MD) is a short-duration assembly of reservists for purposes of administrative processing, screening, briefing, or other limited operational requirements. MD does not meet the criteria for regular IDT or active duty.

MD is not common. Most reservists perform regular IDT and AT. MD shows up for specific purposes.

## When Muster Duty Applies

Common MD scenarios.

- Annual screening musters for IRR members per 10 USC 10206<Citation source="10 USC 10206" />
- Record update musters for verifying member data
- Pre-mobilization screening
- Equipment turn-in or distribution musters
- Special command briefings or assemblies
- Standby Reserve recall screening

## Pay and Points

MD has its own pay treatment per DOD FMR 7000.14-R Volume 7A.

- Single muster duty pay rate (different from drill pay)
- One day of pay regardless of actual hours performed
- Earns retirement points (one per muster)
- Travel reimbursement might apply per orders

The pay rate is set in DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> and updates with annual pay tables.

## Conditions for Muster Duty

Per 10 USC 12319<Citation source="10 USC 12319" />, MD must meet these conditions.

- No more than 24 hours in duration
- Not for purposes of training (otherwise it would be IDT or AT)
- Authorized by competent authority
- Member receives orders or muster notification

## Performance Steps

### Step 1 - Verify the Authorization

Before processing MD pay, confirm.

- Authorization came from competent authority (HQMC, MARFORRES, or designated commander)
- Purpose meets MD criteria (not training)
- Duration falls within the 24-hour limit
- Member was properly notified

### Step 2 - Confirm Member Performance

Document the muster attendance.

- Sign-in sheet with member signature, date, and time
- Member identification verified
- Any briefings or actions completed during the muster
- Officer of the day or muster officer signature

### Step 3 - Process Through Drill Manager

MD pay processes via Drill Manager with specific MD pay codes.

- Open Drill Manager and select muster type
- Enter members who performed muster
- Apply MD pay code per MCTFSCODESMAN
- Submit batch for unit diary processing

### Step 4 - Verify EDFR

Confirm the muster posts cleanly to MCTFS via the Electronic Diary Feedback Report.

- Pull EDFR within 5 business days
- Verify each member's MD entry posted
- Identify and correct any failures

### Step 5 - File Documentation

Retain muster records per MCO 5210.11<Citation source="MCO 5210.11" />.

- Sign-in sheets
- Authorization correspondence
- Drill Manager batch confirmation
- EDFR confirmation

## MD vs IDT vs AT

Knowing the difference prevents misclassification.

| Aspect | MD | IDT | AT |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| Duration | < 24 hours | 4+ hours per period | Multi-day (typically 12-14) |
| Purpose | Admin, screening, briefings | Training | Training |
| Pay rate | MD pay rate (one day) | Drill pay (per period) | Active duty pay (per day) |
| Points | One per muster | One per period | Multiple based on duration |
| Status | Inactive | Inactive | Active |
| Orders | Muster notification | None for regular drill | Active duty orders |
| BAH/BAS | No | No | Yes (active rate) |

## Common Errors

These hit on MD processing.

1. **Treating MD as IDT.** Member came in for record update and admin processed as regular drill. Wrong pay code, wrong accounting. Fix by correcting to MD code.
2. **Treating training as MD.** If training occurred, it is IDT not MD. MD has narrow purpose.
3. **MD over 24 hours.** Exceeds statutory limit. Must convert to IDT or AT depending on duration and purpose.
4. **Missing authorization documentation.** No paper trail authorizing the muster. Reconstruct or accept the record gap.
5. **Late EDFR check.** MD entry failed and was not caught for weeks. Fix by submitting correction with retroactive date.

## When You Use MD vs Other Categories

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Member came for annual readiness screening (4 hours, no training) | MD |
| Member attended monthly drill weekend | IDT regular drill |
| Member attended supplemental skills training session | IDT ATP |
| Member performed annual training (12 days) | AT |
| Member processed administrative actions over weekend (2 days, 16 hours) | IDT regular drill or RMP |
| Member came in for pre-mobilization screening (one day) | MD |

## Coordination

MD touches a few external organizations.

- **MCRSC**: Authorizes IRR muster duty calls
- **HQMC RA**: Policy on MD authorization for reserve components
- **Local command**: For unit-level MD authorizations
- **Travel office**: When members travel to attend MD

## Related Roles

MD processing affects fewer roles than regular drill.

- **Marines** receive muster notification and report as directed.
- **Leaders** at the unit conduct the muster and document attendance.
- **Commanders** authorize unit-level musters and receive reports.
