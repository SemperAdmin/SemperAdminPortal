## Three Retirement Status Phases

Per MCRAMM Chapter 12, Reserve retirees move through distinct phases with different healthcare and ID privileges.

### Phase 1 - Reserve Retired Awaiting Pay

Member completed 20 qualifying years but has not reached retirement pay age (typically age 60 or reduced age).

- ID card: Reserve Retired (specific category)
- Healthcare: TRR (premium-based) or civilian insurance
- Base access: Limited
- No retirement pay

### Phase 2 - Reserve Retired Receiving Pay (Under 65)

Member has reached retirement pay age and draws retirement pay.

- ID card: Retired (full benefit category)
- Healthcare: TRICARE Select retiree or other plan
- Base access: Full retiree access
- Retirement pay active

### Phase 3 - Reserve Retired with Medicare (Age 65+)

Member is age 65 or older and Medicare-eligible.

- ID card: Retired (full benefit category)
- Healthcare: TRICARE for Life (TFL) with Medicare
- Base access: Full retiree access
- Retirement pay continues, coordinated with Medicare

## ID Card Categories

Per DoDI 1000.13<Citation source="DoDI 1000.13" />, military ID cards designate the holder's category and benefits.

### Reserve Retired Awaiting Pay ID

Reserve retirees not yet receiving pay receive a specific ID card.

- Indicates Reserve Retired status
- Limited base access privileges
- Limited commissary and exchange privileges per current policy
- Healthcare access through TRR if enrolled

### Retired ID (Full Privileges)

Retirees drawing pay (Reserve or Active component) receive standard Retired ID.

- Full base access
- Full commissary and exchange privileges
- Healthcare per applicable TRICARE plan
- All standard retiree benefits

### Dependent ID Cards

Eligible family members receive dependent ID cards.

- Spouse: Full retiree dependent ID
- Children under 21 (or 23 if full-time student): Dependent ID
- Disabled adult children: Continued dependent ID
- Each requires separate enrollment and verification

## Healthcare by Phase

### During Awaiting Pay

Members elect coverage from these options.

- TRICARE Retired Reserve (TRR): Premium-based, similar to TRS structure
- Civilian insurance (employer or marketplace)
- Some other federal coverage if eligible

### When Pay Begins (Under 65)

Coverage typically transitions to.

- TRICARE Select retiree: Premium-based, broader provider access
- TRICARE Prime retiree: Limited geographic availability for retirees
- Other federal coverage if eligible (FEHBP through civilian government work)

### Age 65 with Medicare

Coverage transitions to.

- Medicare Part A and B (member enrolls separately)
- TRICARE for Life (TFL): Wraparound to Medicare
- DEERS must reflect Medicare enrollment
- TRICARE pharmacy benefits continue

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Identify Member's Phase

When a Reserve retiree contacts the I&I.

- Verify member status in MCTFS (Awaiting Pay, Receiving Pay, etc.)
- Verify age for Medicare considerations
- Verify family status for dependent benefits

### Step 2 - Brief on Current Phase Benefits

Provide phase-appropriate information.

- ID card category and how to obtain
- Healthcare options for the current phase
- Base access privileges
- Family member benefits

### Step 3 - Coordinate Transitions

For members approaching phase transitions.

- Pre-retirement pay transition (around age 60)
- Pre-Medicare transition (around age 65)
- Brief on changes 90 days in advance
- Coordinate updates with DEERS and DFAS

### Step 4 - Process ID Card Updates

When member needs new ID.

- Verify member identity and current status
- Coordinate appointment at RAPIDS facility
- Provide documentation for ID category
- Update DEERS as needed

### Step 5 - Coordinate Family Updates

For family member changes.

- New marriage after retirement
- Family death
- Children aging out of dependent status
- Adult disabled child documentation

## ID Card Renewal

Retired ID cards are typically issued without expiration (INDEF). Earlier ID cards had expiration dates.

- Members with expiration dates renew at age 65 (transition to permanent)
- Damaged or lost cards replaced through RAPIDS
- DEERS must remain current for valid cards

## Base Access Privileges

Privileges depend on phase.

| Phase | Base Access | Commissary | Exchange | Other |
|-------|------------|------------|----------|-------|
| Awaiting Pay | Limited | Limited per policy | Limited per policy | TRR if enrolled |
| Receiving Pay | Full | Full | Full | All retiree benefits |
| Medicare-Eligible | Full | Full | Full | TFL coverage |

Specific privileges vary by installation and current DOD policy.

## Common Member Questions

### "I still use the commissary?"

Yes for retirees drawing pay. Limited access for awaiting pay members per current policy.

### "What about MWR facilities?"

Generally yes for all retirees. Specific facilities might have access rules.

### "my spouse keep their ID?"

Yes. Dependent ID continues while spouse remains married to retired member.

### "What happens to my family if I die?"

Spouse and eligible dependents continue to have ID and benefits as surviving family. SBP might provide annuity. TRICARE might continue depending on category.

### "I be recalled to active duty?"

Yes. Reserve retirees remain subject to recall in time of war or national emergency. Specific authority varies by retirement category.

## Common I&I Errors on Retirement Benefits

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Wrong ID category issued.** Awaiting Pay vs Receiving Pay vs Retired have different categories. Verify before requesting.
2. **Phase transitions not coordinated.** Member transitions from awaiting pay to receiving pay but DEERS not updated. Coordinate.
3. **Medicare enrollment timing missed.** Member should enroll in Medicare 3 months before age 65 to avoid coverage gap. Brief in advance.
4. **TRR vs TFL confusion.** Different plans for different phases. Clarify with member based on current phase.
5. **Family member benefits not addressed.** Spouse and dependents need separate updates. Coordinate all family changes.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Retirement benefits administration touches.

- **RAPIDS**: ID card issuance and DEERS updates
- **DEERS**: Authoritative database for benefits eligibility
- **DFAS**: Retirement pay and TRICARE premium coordination
- **TRICARE Regional Office**: Plan enrollment and questions
- **Social Security Administration**: Medicare enrollment
- **Veterans Affairs**: VA-specific benefits

## Pre-Retirement Counseling

I&I admin provides pre-retirement counseling per MCRAMM standards.

- Brief on retirement pay calculation
- Brief on TRR option for awaiting pay phase
- Brief on SBP election at retirement
- Brief on transition timing for Medicare
- Brief on ID card and base privileges
- Brief on family member benefits

This counseling sets the retiree up for success across decades of retirement.

## Related Roles

Retirement benefits affect every Reserve retiree's life.

- **Marines** (retiring reservists) elect benefits and use them throughout retirement.
- **Leaders** counsel members during pre-retirement on benefit options.
- **Commanders** at I&I support thorough pre-retirement counseling.
