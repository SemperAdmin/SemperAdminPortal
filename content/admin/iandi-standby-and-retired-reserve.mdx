## Standby Reserve

Per MCRAMM Chapter 1 Section 1.4, the Standby Reserve consists of Marines who have completed their initial military service obligation but have specific reasons preventing them from active drilling.

### Categories of Standby

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9, two subdivisions exist.

- **Active Status List (ASL)**: Marines temporarily unable to participate due to civilian employment, family hardship, or other approved circumstances. Eligible for return to Ready Reserve.
- **Inactive Status List (ISL)**: Marines who must be screened before return to Ready Reserve. Includes Marines designated key civilian employees and members in extended hardship status.

### Eligibility for Standby

A Marine moves to Standby Reserve when one of these applies.

- Designated as a key civilian employee in a critical industry
- Suffering temporary hardship preventing drill
- Has completed total obligated service period
- Has specific medical limitations preventing active drill but not warranting separation

### Participation Requirements

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4, Standby Reserve members must.

- Maintain current address with HQMC
- Respond to muster correspondence
- Maintain DEERS and SGLI
- Submit periodic readiness updates
- Remain available for involuntary recall in time of war or national emergency

No drill pay. Limited retirement point credit (membership points only).

### Mobilization Authority

Standby Reserve members mobilize only under full mobilization authority per 10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(a). They are not subject to partial mobilization or Presidential Reserve Call-Up.

### Admin Processing for Standby

I&I admin rarely processes Standby Reserve actions directly. Most route through Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC). When a Standby member contacts the I&I shop.

1. Verify their Standby category (ASL or ISL) in MCTFS.
2. Direct routine inquiries to MCRSC.
3. For return-to-drill requests, screen for ASL eligibility and route to MCRSC for SMCR placement.
4. For administrative changes (address, dependents), member updates via MOL or contacts MCRSC.

## Retired Reserve

Per MCRAMM Chapter 1 Section 1.5, the Retired Reserve consists of Marines who have retired from the Reserve but remain subject to recall under specific conditions.

### Eligibility for Retired Reserve

A Marine moves to Retired Reserve upon meeting one of these.

- Completed 20 qualifying years of service for non-regular retirement
- Completed mandatory removal date for officers
- Reached age 60 (Reserve retirement pay begins at age 60 absent qualifying active duty service)
- Voluntarily retired with required years of service

### Two Status Categories

- **Reserve Retired Awaiting Pay**: Member completed 20 qualifying years but has not reached age 60. Cannot draw retirement pay until age 60 (reduced based on qualifying active duty per MCRAMM Chapter 4).
- **Reserve Retired Receiving Pay**: Member is age 60 or older (or earlier per 10 USC 12731<Citation source="10 USC 12731" /> reductions) and is drawing retirement pay.

### Recall Authority

Retired Reserve members remain subject to involuntary recall to active duty under 10 USC 688<Citation source="10 USC 688" /> in time of war or national emergency. They are the deepest pool of mobilization manpower.

### Participation Requirements

Minimal. Retired Reserve members must.

- Maintain current address with HQMC
- Update beneficiary designations
- Maintain DEERS for spouse and dependents (medical and ID card eligibility)
- Notify HQMC of significant life events affecting recall capability

### Admin Processing for Retired Reserve

I&I admin generally does not process Retired Reserve actions. Routing depends on the request.

| Request | Where to Route |
|---------|----------------|
| Retirement pay questions | DFAS or VA |
| Address updates | MOL or HQMC RA |
| ID card renewal | RAPIDS facility |
| TRICARE eligibility | TRICARE for Life or TRICARE Retired Reserve |
| Recall to active duty | HQMC RA via formal orders |
| Medical board recall (medical issues) | Service Personnel Office |

## Differences at a Glance

The three Reserve components beyond active drilling SMCR have different characteristics.

| Aspect | IRR | Standby Reserve | Retired Reserve |
|--------|-----|----------------|-----------------|
| Drill required | No | No | No |
| Drill pay | No | No | No |
| Retirement pay | After 20 years and age 60 | Same as IRR if applicable | Drawing or awaiting |
| TRICARE | Limited (TRR available) | Limited | TRICARE for Life or Retired Reserve |
| Mobilization (war) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Mobilization (partial) | Yes | No | No |
| Mobilization (PRCU) | Yes | No | No |
| Career path open | Yes (return to SMCR) | Yes (return via screening) | No (retired status) |
| Admin contact | MCRSC | MCRSC | HQMC RA |

## Common I&I Decision Points

When a non-drilling Marine contacts the I&I.

1. **Pull MCTFS to verify component.** Do not guess. Pull the record.
2. **Identify the action requested.** Update, recall, return to drill, retirement question, etc.
3. **Route to the correct organization.** I&I supports SMCR. Other components route elsewhere.
4. **Document the contact.** Even if you cannot process the action, record the request and the routing decision.

## Coordination Touchpoints

- **MCRSC (Marine Corps Reserve Support Command)**: Primary support for IRR, Standby, and some Retired Reserve actions
- **HQMC RA (Reserve Affairs)**: Policy guidance and Retired Reserve recall actions
- **DFAS**: Retired pay processing
- **VA**: Veteran-related benefits and disability

## Related Roles

These components affect rare but important interactions.

- **Marines** in these statuses have minimal day-to-day admin interaction with I&I.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely interface with these components except for returning members.
- **Commanders** at I&I command may receive recall orders for these components during mobilization.
