## What the PHA Is

Per DODI 6025.19<Citation source="DODI 6025.19" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 6, the Periodic Health Assessment (PHA) is the annual medical screening for all military members including Reserve. The PHA establishes baseline health and identifies any conditions affecting readiness.

## PHA Components

The PHA includes.

### Self-Reported Medical History

Member completes questionnaire covering.

- Current medications
- Past medical history
- Current symptoms
- Mental health status
- Specific deployment-related concerns

### Physical Examination

Health care provider examines member.

- Vital signs
- General physical examination
- Specific deployment-relevant evaluation

### Specific Tests

As indicated.

- Vision screening
- Hearing screening
- Specific lab work
- Other tests per indication

### Mental Health Screening

Standardized mental health screening.

- Specific screening tools
- Follow-up if indicated

### Dental Status

PHA includes dental component or coordinates with separate dental exam.

### Immunizations

Update immunizations as needed.

### Documentation

PHA results documented in medical record.

## When PHA Required

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6.

- Annual cycle
- Pre-deployment (specific PHA-Pre-Deployment Health Assessment)
- Post-deployment (Post-Deployment Health Assessment)
- Specific other situations

## PHA for Reserve Members

Reserve members complete PHA annually.

- Might be completed at MTF or with civilian provider
- Specific Reserve Health Readiness Program supports access
- Documentation must reach official record

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Track Member PHA Status

Maintain tracker.

- Last PHA date per member
- Members approaching annual currency
- Members past due

### Step 2 - Schedule PHAs

For members approaching currency.

- Coordinate appointments with MTF or providers
- Notify members in advance
- Schedule during drill weekends or other availability

### Step 3 - Coordinate Documentation

After PHA completion.

- PHA results to member's record
- MCTFS update
- Specific findings addressed

### Step 4 - Address Findings

For members with PHA findings.

- Follow-up appointments scheduled
- Specialist referrals as needed
- Specific treatment coordination
- Update readiness category

### Step 5 - Track Until Resolution

For findings requiring follow-up.

- Track action items
- Verify completion
- Update readiness category

## Reserve Health Readiness Program

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6, the Reserve Health Readiness Program supports reserve member medical readiness.

- Specific provisions for Reserve member access to MTF
- Specific reimbursement for civilian provider medical care for readiness purposes
- Specific programs for dental readiness
- Coordination of various medical services

## Common PHA Issues

### Member Missing PHA Window

Member fails to complete annual PHA.

- Reserve Category changes
- Deployment eligibility affected
- Schedule promptly

### PHA Findings Requiring Follow-up

Member has issues identified during PHA.

- Specific follow-up required
- Treatment might be needed
- Track until resolved

### Member Refuses PHA

Rare but does occur.

- Specific procedures per MCRAMM
- Might affect continued drilling
- Coordinate with command

### Documentation Gaps

PHA completed but documentation missing from record.

- Coordinate with medical provider
- Update MCTFS
- Verify accuracy

## PHA and Deployment

PHA supports deployment readiness.

### Pre-Deployment Health Assessment (PDHA)

Specific assessment before deployment.

- Updated PHA components
- Deployment-specific screening
- Medical readiness verification

### Post-Deployment Health Assessment (PDHA)

After return from deployment.

- Specific post-deployment screening
- Mental health screening
- Specific conditions evaluation

### Post-Deployment Health Reassessment (PDHRA)

Several months after deployment.

- Follow-up assessment
- Identify delayed-onset conditions
- Specific reservist provisions

## Coordination Touchpoints

PHA processing touches.

- **Local installation MTF**: For Reserve member PHA access
- **Reserve Health Readiness Program**: For specific reserve provisions
- **Member's civilian providers**: For some PHA components
- **Member's chain of command**: For findings affecting duty

## Quality Control on PHA

Common errors.

1. **Tracker not maintained.** Members fall through open. Maintain current tracker.
2. **Findings not addressed.** PHA identifies issues but no follow-up. Coordinate resolution.
3. **Documentation gaps.** PHA completed but not in record. Verify documentation reaches official record.
4. **Member's deployment limited by overdue PHA.** Schedule before mobilization.

## Documentation Required

For PHA processing.

- PHA completion documentation
- Findings documentation
- Follow-up appointment records
- Specific treatment documentation
- Updated MCTFS records

## Related Roles

PHA affects every Reserve member's medical readiness.

- **Marines** complete annual PHA and address findings.
- **Leaders** track member PHA status at the unit.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit PHA compliance.
