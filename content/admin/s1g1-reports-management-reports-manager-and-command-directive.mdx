## Role and responsibility

The unit commander appoints the Command Reports Manager in writing per MCO 5214.2G<Citation source="MCO 5214.2G" /> paragraph 4b(4)(b) and signs the Reports Management Program directive per paragraph 4b(4)(a). The Command Reports Manager builds the continuity binder per paragraph 4b(4)(c) and runs daily program execution. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the routing of the appointment letter and the directive staffing. The Directives Control Point (DCP) publishes the unit directive per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" />. The HQMC AR (ARDB) program sponsor provides the template directive on the SharePoint site.

Source documents flow from the candidate's grade and billet, the AR Reports Management SharePoint template directive, the unit organizational reporting requirements, the staffing chop chain, and the continuity binder content. Reports Manager and command directive outputs include the signed appointment letter, the published Reports Management Program directive (unit order or bulletin), the continuity binder with section POCs, the orders (MCO 5214.2G and SECNAVINST 5210.16), the Reports Inventory, the 3-year review documentation, and the audit trail for IGMC inspection.

Per MCO 5214.2G paragraph 4b(4)(a), the unit commander promulgates the Reports Management Program directive to ensure organizational reporting requirements are documented, tracked, and in compliance with legal and statutory requirements. Per paragraph 4b(4)(b), the Command Reports Manager appointment runs in writing. Per paragraph 4b(4)(c), the continuity binder supports program execution and IGMC inspection.

## Trigger events

- New unit stand-up triggering Command Reports Manager appointment.
- Change of command triggering directive and appointment refresh.
- Command Reports Manager PCS or rotation triggering replacement.
- New MCO 5214.2G or SECNAVINST 5210.16 issuance triggering directive update.
- Annual continuity binder audit.
- IGMC Reports Management FA inspection notice arrives.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the Command Reports Manager candidate. The unit commander identifies the candidate from the unit S-1, AR section, or appropriate staff section. The candidate holds grade and experience supporting the program.
2. Draft and sign the appointment letter. Per MCO 5214.2G<Citation source="MCO 5214.2G" /> paragraph 4b(4)(b), the unit commander signs the appointment letter naming the Command Reports Manager with title, duty address, and telephone number.
3. Draft the Reports Management Program directive. Per paragraph 4b(4)(a), the unit publishes the directive as an order or bulletin. The directive documents organizational reporting requirements, tracking, and compliance with legal and statutory requirements (5 CFR Part 1320, SSN Reduction Act, Privacy Act).
4. Use the AR Reports Management SharePoint template. The HQMC AR (ARDB) program sponsor publishes a template directive on the SharePoint site. The unit adapts the template to local content.
5. Run the staffing chop chain. The action officer routes the draft directive through legal, the Directives Control Point, and affected staff sections.
6. Commander signs and publishes. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" />, the commander signs the directive. The DCP publishes per directives management rules.
7. Build the continuity binder. Per MCO 5214.2G paragraph 4b(4)(c), the Command Reports Manager builds the binder including:
   - Section POCs.
   - MCO 5214.2G.
   - SECNAVINST 5210.16<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5210.16" />.
   - Reports Inventory.
   - 3-Year Review documentation.
8. Stage binder contents. The Command Reports Manager organizes the binder for daily reference and IGMC inspection readiness.
9. Refresh appointment and directive on change of command. The new commander reviews and re-signs the appointment letter and the directive.
10. Audit the binder. The Command Reports Manager runs the annual audit before the IGMC inspection cycle.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Unit Reports Management Continuity Binder. Per paragraph 4b(4)(c).
- Directives Control Point (DCP). Per MCO 5215.1K. Publishes the unit directive.
- AR Reports Management SharePoint Site. Holds the template directive.

### Forms

- Command Reports Manager Appointment Letter. Per paragraph 4b(4)(b).
- Reports Management Program Command Directive (Order or Bulletin). Per paragraph 4b(4)(a).
- Continuity Binder Index. Per paragraph 4b(4)(c).

## Common pitfalls

- Appointment letter missing name, title, duty address, or telephone number. Per MCO 5214.2G<Citation source="MCO 5214.2G" /> paragraph 4b(4)(b), the letter records all elements.
- Command directive missing or out of date. Per paragraph 4b(4)(a), the unit publishes and maintains.
- Continuity binder incomplete. Per paragraph 4b(4)(c), the binder carries POCs, the orders, the inventory, and the 3-year review.
- Verbal appointment of Command Reports Manager. Per paragraph 4b(4)(b), the appointment runs in writing.
- Directive issued without proper staffing through DCP. Per MCO 5215.1K, the DCP publishes per directives management rules.
- New MCO or SECNAVINST issuance not reflected in the directive. The directive references the current orders.
- Appointment not refreshed on change of command. The new commander reviews and re-signs.

## Decision points

- Command Reports Manager source. The Command Reports Manager comes from the S-1, the adjutant section, or the AR section depending on the unit structure.
- Directive type (order versus bulletin). Per MCO 5215.1K, an order is the standing directive. A bulletin is a time-limited directive. The unit selects based on the directive's expected duration.
- Continuity binder paper versus electronic format. Per paragraph 4b(4)(c), the binder runs in either format provided it carries the required content.
- Refresh cadence. The unit refreshes the directive on order issuance changes and on commander change.
