## Standby Administration Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9, Standby Reserve administration involves specific tasks supporting member status maintenance.

I&I admin generally does not directly handle Standby administration. MCRSC primarily handles.

## MCRSC Standby Administration

Marine Corps Reserve Support Command primarily administers Standby Reserve.

### MCRSC Responsibilities

- Member status maintenance in MCTFS
- Periodic readiness updates
- Address maintenance
- Status change processing
- Coordination during mobilization
- Member benefit coordination

### MCRSC Member Contact

For standby members.

- Periodic correspondence
- Annual readiness checks
- Specific event communications
- Member-initiated contacts

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

When Standby member contacts I&I.

### Step 1 - Verify Member Status

Pull MCTFS record.

- Verify Standby category (ASL or ISL)
- Recent service history
- Specific member situation

### Step 2 - Identify Member Need

Determine what member needs.

- Address update
- Status change request
- Benefits question
- Mobilization-related action

### Step 3 - Route to MCRSC

Most Standby actions route to MCRSC.

- Provide MCRSC contact information
- Specific routing for various needs
- Document referral

### Step 4 - Local Coordination if Needed

For local situations.

- Coordinate with local SMCR if member returning to drilling
- Specific local support
- Specific accommodations

## Standby Member Status Changes

Members might request or experience status changes.

### Voluntary Status Changes

Member requests change.

- ASL to ISL (long-term condition)
- ISL to ASL (condition change)
- Standby to Ready Reserve (return to drilling)
- Standby to separation (end of MSO)

### Involuntary Status Changes

Imposed changes.

- Mobilization to active duty (full mobilization only)
- Stop-loss extension (during mobilization)
- Specific other situations

## Coordination Touchpoints

Standby administration touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: Primary Standby administration
- **HQMC RA**: For policy and specific Standby matters
- **MOL**: For self-service updates
- **Local SMCR units**: For Ready Reserve return
- **PAC**: For specific pay actions when applicable

## Quality Control on Standby Administration

Common errors.

1. **Wrong category communicated.** Member confused about ASL vs ISL.
2. **MCRSC routing skipped.** I&I tries to handle directly when MCRSC is correct.
3. **Member's status not verified.** Action proceeds without verifying current status.
4. **Local SMCR coordination missed.** Member returning to drilling without unit coordination.

## Related Roles

Standby administration affects Standby members occasionally.

- **Marines** in Standby maintain status with limited Reserve involvement.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely engage Standby members.
- **Commanders** at I&I support occasional Standby member contacts.
