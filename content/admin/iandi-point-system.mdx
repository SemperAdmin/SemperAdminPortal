## Why Points Matter

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 Section 1.2 and 10 USC 12732<Citation source="10 USC 12732" />, reserve service for retirement eligibility is measured in retirement points, not years of service alone. A reservist needs 20 qualifying years to retire and draws retirement pay based on accumulated points across their entire career.

I&I admin tracks points through MCTFS. Errors in point posting affect retirement eligibility and pay computation. This is one of the most consequential admin actions you perform.

## How Points Are Earned

Points come from five primary sources.

### Inactive Duty Training (IDT)

- One point per IDT period performed
- Standard drill weekend (MUTA-4) earns 4 points
- Maximum 130 paid IDT points per anniversary year (regular drill plus paid EDPs plus FHD)

### Active Duty (AD, ADT, AT, ADOS, mobilization)

- One point per day in active duty status
- AT period of 14 days earns 14 points
- Mobilization periods earn one point per day for the entire mobilization

### Correspondence Courses (Equivalent Training)

- Points per course completion based on course hours
- One point per 3 credit hours of approved correspondence
- MarineNet courses with assigned point value
- Maximum 130 inactive duty point total annually (combines IDT and correspondence)

### Membership Points

- 15 points per anniversary year for membership in the Ready Reserve, Standby Reserve, or other qualifying reserve component
- Earned regardless of drill performance for members who maintain reserve status
- Reduces in years of inactive component status

### Funeral Honors Duty

- One point per FHD performance
- Counts separately from IDT regular drill total

## Anniversary Year vs Calendar Year

Reserve points calculate by anniversary year, not calendar year.

- Anniversary year begins on the date the member entered the current reserve service obligation
- Each anniversary year produces an annual point total
- The anniversary year totals accumulate over the career

Example: A Marine entered the SMCR on 15 March 2020. Their anniversary year runs 15 March to 14 March each year. A drill weekend in February 2024 counts toward the anniversary year ending 14 March 2024.

## Satisfactory Year Threshold

Per 10 USC 12732<Citation source="10 USC 12732" />, a satisfactory year for retirement requires 50 retirement points minimum. Anniversary years with fewer than 50 points do not count toward the 20-year retirement requirement.

The 50-point threshold is critical. Tracking and ensuring members meet the threshold is a continuous I&I task.

## Maximum Annual Points

Per 10 USC 12733<Citation source="10 USC 12733" />, annual points cap at specific limits.

- Inactive duty points (IDT, correspondence, membership): 130 per anniversary year for most reservists
- Active duty points: One per day, no annual cap (a year of mobilization would be 365 points)
- Combined total: Active duty days plus inactive duty points (up to 130)

## Performance Steps for Point Tracking

### Step 1 - Establish the Anniversary Year

Pull the member's MCTFS record and identify the anniversary year start date. This drives the annual reporting cycle.

### Step 2 - Track Quarterly Performance

Each quarter, review the member's point accumulation against pace.

- Q1 target: 12-15 points
- Q2 target: 24-30 points
- Q3 target: 36-45 points
- Q4 target: 50+ points (satisfactory)

Members below pace need command attention.

### Step 3 - Process Each Drill, AT, FHD, and Course Completion

Each duty period generates a point credit.

- Drill weekend processed via Drill Manager populates IDT points
- AT period processed via UD/MIPS active duty transactions populates active duty points
- FHD processed via Drill Manager populates FHD points
- Correspondence course completion documented via MarineNet certificate

### Step 4 - Verify EDFR Posting

After each batch processing, pull the Electronic Diary Feedback Report.

- Verify each member's points posted
- Identify failures requiring correction
- Submit corrective UD/MIPS transactions

### Step 5 - Annual Reconciliation

At each anniversary year end.

- Pull the member's annual point total from MCTFS
- Compare against the 50-point threshold
- Generate Career Retirement Credit Report (CRCR) submission
- Validate point accuracy with member

## Career Retirement Credit Report (CRCR)

The CRCR is the official record of a member's reserve service points. Per MCRAMM Chapter 4.

- Generated annually after each anniversary year
- Validated by the member
- Final document for retirement application
- Discrepancies must be resolved while documentation exists

The CRCR locks in the year's points. Errors found years later are difficult to correct.

## Common Point Errors

These hit during admin processing.

1. **Drill processed but points not posted.** UD/MIPS transaction failed and was not corrected. Pull EDFR to identify failures.
2. **Active duty days not credited as points.** AT or ADT processed for pay but the active duty point transaction missed. Submit retroactive UD/MIPS.
3. **Correspondence course not credited.** Member completed MarineNet course but no point credit. Submit course completion documentation through unit.
4. **Membership points not posting.** Member in IRR for the year but membership points (15) did not show. Check pay group and MCRSC processing.
5. **Anniversary year miscalculated.** MCTFS shows wrong anniversary date. Submit correction request through PAC.

## Points Affecting Retirement Pay

Total accumulated points (across all anniversary years) drive retirement pay computation.

- Retirement pay formula: (total points / 360) x 2.5% x retired pay base
- Higher point accumulation produces higher retirement pay
- Mobilization periods (high active duty points) significantly boost retirement pay

A 20-year reservist with average drill participation accumulates roughly 1500-2000 points. A reservist with extensive mobilization service accumulates 3000+ points.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Point tracking touches several systems and organizations.

- **Drill Manager**: Daily IDT and FHD point processing
- **MROWS**: Active duty period orders
- **UD/MIPS**: Final point posting to MCTFS
- **MCRSC**: Membership points for IRR and Standby
- **MarineNet**: Correspondence course completion records
- **MCAAT**: Policy clarification on point calculation edge cases

## Related Roles

The point system affects retirement eligibility for every reservist.

- **Marines** verify their annual point total via MOL and review the CRCR each year.
- **Leaders** monitor section participation rates and counsel members below pace.
- **Commanders** review unit participation metrics and address satisfactory year shortfalls.
