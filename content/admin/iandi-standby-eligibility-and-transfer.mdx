## Standby Transfer Eligibility

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9, transfer to Standby Reserve requires meeting specific eligibility conditions.

### General Requirements

- Completion of initial obligated service period
- Specific qualifying reason for transfer
- Command endorsement
- MCRSC approval
- Honorable status

### Specific ASL Eligibility

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9.

- Civilian employment temporarily preventing drill
- Family hardship temporarily preventing drill
- Specific personal circumstances
- Other approved temporary reasons

### Specific ISL Eligibility

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9.

- Designated key civilian employee
- Extended hardship
- Long-term medical conditions not warranting separation
- Specific other long-term qualifying conditions

## Transfer From SELRES to Standby

Most common Standby transfer.

### Member Initiates Request

Per MCRAMM.

#### Step 1 - Member Submits Request

Member submits formal request through unit.

- Specific request package
- Justification for transfer
- Command endorsement requested
- Documentation supporting reason

#### Step 2 - Unit Review and Endorsement

SMCR unit reviews and endorses.

- Verify member's situation
- Endorse request based on circumstances
- Forward to MCRSC

#### Step 3 - MCRSC Review

MCRSC reviews request.

- Verify eligibility
- Confirm specific category (ASL vs ISL)
- Process or return

#### Step 4 - Transfer Processing

If approved.

- UD/MIPS transfer transaction
- Member's component status changes
- Notification to member
- Documentation in OMPF

#### Step 5 - Member Notified

Member notified of transfer.

- New component status
- New participation requirements
- Specific procedures

## Transfer From IRR to Standby

Less common but does occur.

### When Applicable

- IRR member's situation matches Standby criteria
- Specific qualifying conditions
- Long-term situation not temporary

### Transfer Process

- Member request
- MCRSC coordination
- Documentation review
- Specific transfer procedures

## Transfer Within Standby (ASL to ISL)

When member's ASL condition becomes long-term.

### When Applicable

- ASL member's temporary condition becomes long-term
- Member's situation changes
- Specific reasons supporting ISL designation

### Transfer Process

- Member request or MCRSC determination
- Documentation review
- Specific transfer per MCRAMM

## Transfer From Standby to Ready Reserve

Members returning from Standby.

### ASL to Ready Reserve

- Easier procedure
- Member's temporary condition resolved
- Specific availability of billet

### ISL to Ready Reserve

- Screening required
- Verify member's continuing reserve fitness
- Specific procedures per MCRAMM

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

For local Standby transitions.

### Step 1 - Member Counsels on Options

When member considering Standby.

- Brief on Standby implications
- Compare with other options (IRR, separation)
- Specific counsel on member's situation

### Step 2 - Coordinate Request Process

If member proceeds with request.

- Help with request package preparation
- Verify documentation supporting reason
- Coordinate command endorsement

### Step 3 - Forward to MCRSC

For MCRSC processing.

- Submit complete package
- Track to MCRSC decision
- Coordinate any follow-up

### Step 4 - Process Local Transition

When transfer approved.

- Update local records
- Coordinate member departure from drilling
- Specific transition processing

### Step 5 - Member Returning From Standby

If local member returns.

- Coordinate with MCRSC
- Local SMCR unit coordination
- Specific receiving procedures

## Standby Implications for Member

Transferring to Standby has implications.

### Lost Benefits

- No drill pay
- Limited military benefits
- Limited base access
- Limited TRICARE eligibility

### Continued Obligations

- MSO continues
- Subject to mobilization (limited authorities)
- Annual readiness updates required
- Address maintenance

### Retirement Impact

- No drilling points
- Membership points only
- Affects retirement calculation
- Specific retirement implications

### Career Impact

- Specific career considerations
- Possible career path changes
- Specific opportunities limited

## Common Transfer Issues

### Member Doesn't Qualify

Member's situation doesn't meet Standby criteria.

- Counsel on alternatives
- Specific procedures per situation
- Possible IRR transfer instead

### Documentation Insufficient

Request package missing supporting documentation.

- Coordinate documentation collection
- Specific requirements per category
- Resubmit when complete

### Command Disagreement

SMCR command disagreeing with transfer request.

- Specific endorsement procedures
- Member's right to request despite disagreement
- Coordinate through chain

### MCRSC Denial

MCRSC denies transfer request.

- Specific appeal procedures
- Member counseled on options
- Document decision

## Coordination Touchpoints

Standby transfer processing touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: Primary processor
- **Member's SMCR unit**: For initial endorsement
- **HQMC**: For specific policy questions
- **PAC**: For pay status changes

## Quality Control on Standby Transfers

Common errors.

1. **Wrong category requested.** ASL when ISL more appropriate (or vice versa).
2. **Documentation gaps.** Request package incomplete.
3. **Pay status not updated.** Member's pay continues incorrectly after transfer.
4. **Member's expectations unrealistic.** Member doesn't understand Standby implications.

## Related Roles

Standby transfers affect members changing component status.

- **Marines** consider Standby and submit requests when appropriate.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units endorse and route requests.
- **Commanders** at I&I support transfer processing.
