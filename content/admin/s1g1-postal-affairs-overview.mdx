## Role and responsibility

The Postal Officer (MOS 0161) runs Marine Corps postal operations at the installation per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />. The S-1 officer supports unit mail room operations and coordinates official mail through the Postal Officer. The S-1 chief tracks unit mail room accountability and the unit mail room standards. The unit commander appoints the Postal Officer in writing and approves official mail management procedures. The Defense Department's Military Postal Service Agency (MPSA) oversees DoD postal operations per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" />. The United States Postal Service (USPS) provides the mail transportation and processing under 39 U.S.C.

Source documents flow from the Postal Officer appointment letter, the unit mail room standing operating procedures, the registered and certified mail records, the official mail authorization, the postal inspection cycle, the mail accountability records, and the postal directives and forms. Postal outputs include the documented postal operations, the certified registered and certified mail handling, the official mail management compliance, the postal inspection records, the mail routing coordination, the postal directive and form management, the SSIC-labeled postal file folder, the postal records retained per SECNAV M-5210.1, and the audit trail.

Per MCO 5110.4B and DoDI 4525.09, the Marine Corps operates MPOs (Military Post Offices) at land installations and FPOs (Fleet Post Offices) at sea-based units. Unit mail rooms (UMRs) operate under the installation Postal Officer's oversight for unit-level mail receipt and distribution.

## Processing workflow

1. Appoint the Postal Officer. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the unit commander appoints the Postal Officer in writing.
2. Train postal personnel. Per MCO 5110.4B, postal personnel complete the required postal training before assuming duties.
3. Run MPO or FPO operations. Per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" />, the installation Postal Officer runs the MPO. The FPO operates under sea-based unit command.
4. Process unit mail room accountability. Per MCO 5110.4B, the unit mail room maintains accountability for incoming and outgoing unit mail.
5. Handle registered and certified mail. Per USPS DMM and IMM, registered mail and certified mail require signature on receipt and accountability through the mail chain.
6. Manage official mail. Per DoDI 4525.09 and MCO 5110.4B, official mail uses penalty mail privileges or paid postage per the authorization.
7. Coordinate mail routing and transportation. The Postal Officer coordinates with USPS, the Defense Transportation System, and contract carriers for mail movement.
8. Run postal inspection cycle. Per MCO 5110.4B, postal inspections cover mail accountability, security, and standing procedures.
9. Document postal records. Per SECNAV M-5210.2 SSIC for postal subjects and SECNAV M 5210.1 retention schedule, postal records retain per the SSIC disposition.
10. Brief commander on postal posture. The Postal Officer briefs the commander on the unit postal posture and any inspection findings.

## Common pitfalls

- Postal Officer not appointed in writing. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the unit commander appoints in writing.
- Postal personnel untrained. Per MCO 5110.4B, training completes before duty assumption.
- Registered or certified mail accountability broken. Per USPS DMM, the accountability chain documents every transfer.
- Official mail abuse (personal mail using penalty privileges). Per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" />, penalty mail is official-only.
- Mail routing not coordinated. The Postal Officer coordinates with USPS and the Defense Transportation System.
- Postal inspection skipped. Per MCO 5110.4B, the inspection cycle runs per the published cadence.
- Postal records retention floor missed. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, the retention schedule controls.

## Decision points

- MPO versus FPO. The installation type (land base or sea-based unit) drives the post office designation.
- Penalty mail versus paid postage. Per DoDI 4525.09, official mail uses penalty mail by default. Paid postage applies in specific cases.
- Registered versus certified mail. Registered mail provides higher security and tracking. Certified mail provides delivery confirmation.
- Unit Mail Room scope. The unit decides the UMR operating scope per command policy and MCO 5110.4B.

## Authority
