## Mobilization Orders Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10, mobilization orders direct Reserve members to active duty for specific operations. Orders processing through Marine Reserve Orders Writing System (MROWS) coordinates the formal authorization for member's active duty.

## Orders Components

A mobilization order includes.

### Member Identification

- Full name and rank
- SSN
- MOS
- Current unit
- Specific member identification

### Authority Citation

- Specific 10 USC authority (12301(a), 12302, 12304, etc.)
- Specific operational mission
- DOD or HQMC tasking reference

### Reporting Information

- Reporting date and time
- Reporting location
- Specific reporting unit or POE (Port of Embarkation)
- Specific instructions

### Active Duty Period

- Start date
- Estimated end date
- Specific duration per authority
- Possible extensions

### Pay and Allowances

- Active duty pay rate
- BAH location
- Specific pay categories
- Travel reimbursement information

### Specific Mission Information

- Operation name
- Specific deployment area
- Specific mission requirements
- Specific authorities

## Performance Steps for Orders Processing

### Step 1 - Receive Mobilization Tasking

When higher headquarters tasks unit for mobilization.

- HQMC or MARFORRES tasking received
- Specific members identified
- Mobilization timeline established

### Step 2 - Generate Orders in MROWS

For each affected member.

- Open MROWS
- Build order with required elements
- Specific authority and mission cited
- Specific reporting and pay information

### Step 3 - Route for Approval

Per MROWS approval process.

- Unit commander signature
- Higher authority approval if required
- Specific approval routing

### Step 4 - Issue Orders to Member

After approval.

- Member notified
- Orders provided
- Member acknowledgment
- Specific reporting information

### Step 5 - Coordinate Travel

For member's travel to reporting location.

- Government Transportation Request (GTR) if applicable
- Per diem coordination
- Specific travel arrangements

### Step 6 - Track Member Movement

Through reporting and active duty.

- Reporting confirmation
- Active duty status change in MCTFS
- Specific tracking through deployment

### Step 7 - Process Modifications

For specific changes.

- Order modifications via MROWS
- Specific amendments
- Member notification of changes

## Orders Modifications

Common modifications.

### Date Changes

- Reporting date adjustments
- End date extensions
- Specific timeline changes

### Location Changes

- Reporting location adjustments
- Mission location changes
- Specific deployment changes

### Authority Changes

- Switch between authorities (e.g., 12304 to 12302)
- Specific authority extensions
- Member notification

### Pay Changes

- Pay updates for changes in deployment
- Specific allowance adjustments
- Coordinate with PAC

## Orders Coordination

Orders processing involves multiple coordination points.

### MROWS Administrators

- System access for orders processing
- Specific MROWS training
- Coordinate complex orders

### HQMC

- Mobilization tasking
- Specific authority guidance
- Major decisions

### MARFORRES

- Operational coordination
- Specific Reserve unit tasking
- Member selection

### Receiving Unit

- Coordinate member arrival
- Specific reporting procedures
- Unit assignment

### PAC

- Pay status changes
- Specific pay processing
- Coordination throughout active duty

## Common Orders Processing Issues

### Late Order Generation

Orders not generated in time.

- Member receives late notification
- Specific scrambling for travel
- Affects family preparation

### Order Errors

Orders contain errors.

- Wrong member information
- Wrong dates or locations
- Specific corrections needed

### Member Cannot Comply

Member unable to report per orders.

- Specific exemption procedures
- Possible order modification
- Coordinate with HQMC

### USERRA Documentation

Member needs USERRA notification documentation for civilian employer.

- Orders sufficient for USERRA notice
- Specific employer coordination
- Coordinate documentation

## Order Types

Different order types for different situations.

### Standard Mobilization Orders

For involuntary mobilization.

- 12301(a), 12302, or 12304 authority
- Standard mobilization period
- Standard mobilization pay

### Voluntary Active Duty Orders

For voluntary service.

- 12301(d) authority
- Specific volunteer mission
- Standard active duty pay

### Active Duty for Operational Support (ADOS) Orders

For specific operational missions.

- 12301(d) authority typically
- Specific mission per orders
- Active duty pay

### Annual Training (AT) Orders

For routine AT.

- 12301(b) authority
- Standard 12-14 day AT period
- Active duty pay

### Active Duty for Training (ADT) Orders

For specific training requirements.

- 12301(d) authority typically
- Specific training mission
- Active duty pay

## Documentation Required for Orders

For each set of mobilization orders.

- MROWS-generated orders
- Member acknowledgment of receipt
- Specific tasking documentation
- USERRA notification documentation
- Family notification documentation

## Coordination Touchpoints

Orders processing touches.

- **MROWS administrators**: For system access
- **HQMC and MARFORRES**: For tasking and authority
- **Member's gaining unit**: For reporting coordination
- **PAC**: For pay status changes
- **Member's family**: For family notification

## Quality Control on Orders Processing

Common errors.

1. **Wrong authority cited.** Verify specific authority for the mobilization.
2. **Date errors.** Verify reporting and end dates.
3. **Member information errors.** Verify SSN, name, MOS, etc.
4. **Late orders.** Generate in time for member preparation.

## Related Roles

Orders processing affects every mobilizing Reserve member.

- **Marines** receive orders and acknowledge.
- **Leaders** at units coordinate orders processing.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit mobilization processing.
