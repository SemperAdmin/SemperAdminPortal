## Role and responsibility

The Sponsorship Coordinator runs the inbound sponsor program per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Paragraph 4.b.(7) (Sponsorship Coordinators). The unit commander signs the welcome aboard letter per Enclosure (2). The assigned sponsor per Paragraph 4.b.(8) executes contact with the inbound Marine. The IR&R Installation Program Manager per Paragraph 4.b.(9) coordinates installation-level resource referral. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the sponsor designation letter per Enclosure (7).

Source documents flow from the gain notification 30-60 days before inbound arrival, the Sponsorship Coordinator designation per Enclosure (1), the sponsor designation per Enclosure (7), the welcome aboard letters per Enclosures (2), (4), (5), and (6), and the IR&R handoff record per Paragraph 4.b.(9). Sponsor handoff outputs include the designated sponsor, the issued welcome aboard letters, the documented sponsor contact, the IR&R coordination record, the metrics entry per Enclosure (3), the in-person arrival brief, the closeout record, and the Page 11 documenting sponsorship completion.

Per MCO 1320.11H Paragraph 4.b.(7), the Sponsorship Coordinator matches sponsor to inbound Marine. Per Enclosure (7), the sponsor designation runs in writing. Per Enclosures (2) and (4)-(6), the welcome aboard letter type aligns with the assignment (CONUS, Accompanied OCONUS, Unaccompanied OCONUS).

## Trigger events

- Gain notification arrives 30-60 days before inbound arrival.
- Sponsor candidate identified for matching.
- CONUS assignment triggering Enclosure (4) letter type.
- Accompanied OCONUS assignment triggering Enclosure (5) letter type.
- Unaccompanied OCONUS assignment triggering Enclosure (6) letter type.
- Family handoff to IR&R per Paragraph 4.b.(9).
- Marine arrives at the unit triggering arrival brief.
- Sponsorship closeout at completion per Paragraph 4.b.(6).

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the gain notification. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Paragraph 4.b.(6) and (7), the Sponsorship Coordinator receives the inbound notification 30-60 days before arrival.
2. Designate the sponsor. Per Paragraph 4.b.(8) and Enclosure (7), the Sponsorship Coordinator matches sponsor to inbound Marine by grade, MOS, and family status.
3. Cut the sponsor designation letter. Per Enclosure (7), the designation letter runs in writing.
4. Issue welcome aboard letters. Per Enclosures (2), (4), (5), and (6), the unit and the sponsor issue welcome aboard letters per the assignment type.
5. Coordinate IR&R handoff. Per Paragraph 4.b.(9) and MCO 1754.10B<Citation source="MCO 1754.10B" />, the IR&R Installation Program Manager coordinates installation-level resource referral.
6. Confirm sponsor contact. The Sponsorship Coordinator confirms the sponsor reached the inbound Marine.
7. Track sponsor execution. Per Enclosure (3) metrics, track the sponsor coverage and contact.
8. Brief the inbound Marine on arrival. The S-1 and the sponsor brief the inbound Marine and family at arrival.
9. Close out sponsorship at completion. Per MCO 1320.11H Paragraph 4.b.(6) and 4.b.(8), sponsorship closes at the agreed milestone.
10. Document sponsorship completion. Per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006 cross-reference and MCO 1320.11H, document on Page 11.

- Welcome Aboard Letter Unaccompanied OCONUS. Per Enclosure (6).
- Command Welcome Aboard Letter. Per Enclosure (2).

## Common pitfalls

- Sponsor not designated 30-60 days before arrival. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> Paragraph 4.b.(8), the designation runs ahead of arrival.
- Welcome aboard letter type mismatch. Per Enclosures (4)-(6), the assignment type drives the letter type.
- IR&R handoff missed. Per Paragraph 4.b.(9), the IR&R coordination supports family transition.
- Sponsor contact not confirmed. The Sponsorship Coordinator confirms the sponsor reached the inbound Marine before arrival.
- Sponsorship completion not documented. Per the cross-reference to MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006, the completion records on Page 11.
- Metrics not tracked. Per Enclosure (3), the metrics support unit posture.

## Decision points

- Sponsor match. Per MCO 1320.11H Paragraph 4.b.(8), grade, MOS, and family status drive the match.
- Welcome aboard letter selection. CONUS, Accompanied OCONUS, or Unaccompanied OCONUS per Enclosures (4)-(6).
- IR&R timing. The handoff runs at sponsor designation per Paragraph 4.b.(9) so the family receives installation-level support.
