## Why This Matters

Per MCRAMM Chapter 3, every reserve duty period falls into a specific utilization category determines pay, points, and reporting. Get the category wrong and the Marine gets paid wrong. Worse, retirement point credit might not post correctly.

I&I admin classifies duty periods correctly before any pay processing. This is not an afterthought.

## Reserve Utilization Categories

The MCRAMM organizes reserve utilization into broad categories.

### Inactive Duty (ID)

Duty performed by reservists not in active duty status. Includes.

- Inactive Duty Training (IDT) - regular drill periods
- Muster Duty (MD)
- Funeral Honors Duty (FHD)
- Equivalent Duty Periods (EDPs) - both paid and unpaid

### Active Duty (AD)

Duty in full active duty status. Includes.

- Active Duty (AD) - typically mobilization or specific orders
- Active Duty for Training (ADT) - extended training periods like Annual Training
- Active Duty for Operational Support (ADOS) - specific operational missions
- Muster Duty Active Duty Other Than for Training - specific short-duration active duty

### Continuing Service Status

Reserve members not currently performing duty but maintaining their reserve component status.

## Types of Reserve Duty

Five primary duty types appear in pay and personnel processing.

| Duty Type | Status | Pay Type | Typical Use |
|-----------|--------|----------|-------------|
| Regular Drill (IDT) | Inactive | Drill pay | Monthly drill weekend, 4 periods per weekend |
| Annual Training (AT) | Active | Active duty pay | Annual 12-14 day training period |
| Active Duty for Training (ADT) | Active | Active duty pay | Extended training beyond AT |
| Active Duty for Operational Support (ADOS) | Active | Active duty pay | Operational missions, special projects |
| Mobilization Active Duty | Active | Active duty pay | Involuntary recall under 12302 or similar |

## Reserve Pay Group Categories

Pay groups appear as a single character in MCTFS and drive Drill Manager processing. Per MCRAMM and MCTFSCODESMAN.

### Pay Group A

Drilling SMCR member earning drill pay. Standard drilling reservist.

- Earns drill pay for each IDT period
- Earns retirement points for each period
- Eligible for AT pay
- Eligible for SDA pays where qualified

### Pay Group B

SMCR member with reduced participation. Limited drill participation.

- Earns drill pay for periods performed
- Limited annual periods
- Eligible for partial year retirement credit
- Subject to Unsatisfactory Participation review if performance falls below minimum

### Pay Group F

IMA in active drilling status. Individual Mobilization Augmentees.

- Drills with their gaining active duty command, not a SMCR unit
- Earns drill pay processed through the gaining command's IPAC
- Earns retirement points at gaining command rates
- Subject to gaining command's training requirements

### Pay Group P

IRR or Standby with no drill pay.

- No drill pay
- Earns membership points only (15 per anniversary year)
- Annual readiness reporting required
- Subject to involuntary recall under specific authorities

### Pay Group R

Retired Reserve.

- No drill pay
- No retirement point accumulation
- Drawing retirement pay if age 60 or older
- Awaiting pay if under age 60 with 20 qualifying years

## Decision Tree for Pay Code Selection

When processing a duty period, walk this decision tree.

1. **Is the Marine in active duty status (orders signed)?**
   - Yes: Active duty pay codes
   - No: Continue
2. **Is this a regular scheduled drill weekend?**
   - Yes: IDT regular drill pay codes
   - No: Continue
3. **Is this an Equivalent Duty Period replacing missed regular drill?**
   - Yes: EDP pay codes (paid or unpaid based on timing)
4. **Is this Funeral Honors Duty?**
   - Yes: FHD pay codes (4 hours minimum, special category)
5. **Is this Muster Duty?**
   - Yes: MD pay codes
6. **Is this an Additional Training Period (ATP) or Readiness Management Period (RMP)?**
   - Yes: ATP or RMP pay codes (separate from regular drill total)
7. **Is this Associate Duty or Appropriate Duty?**
   - Yes: Points only, no pay

## MUTA Codes

Drill periods use Multiple Unit Training Assembly (MUTA) codes to indicate how many periods were performed. Common codes.

| MUTA | Periods | Hours | Typical Use |
|------|---------|-------|-------------|
| MUTA-1 | 1 | 4+ hours | Single period, partial drill day |
| MUTA-2 | 2 | 8+ hours | One full day of drill |
| MUTA-4 | 4 | 16+ hours | Standard drill weekend (Saturday and Sunday) |
| MUTA-5 | 5 | 20+ hours | Extended drill weekend |
| MUTA-6 | 6 | 24+ hours | Three-day drill weekend |

Drill Manager applies MUTA codes when processing the drill batch.

## Common Errors on Category Assignment

These hit most often.

1. **Regular drill processed as ATP.** Regular monthly drill is IDT regular drill, not ATP. ATP is supplemental beyond regular drill.
2. **AT processed as IDT.** AT is active duty for training. Different pay rate and entitlement structure. Use AT pay codes.
3. **Wrong pay group for IMA.** IMAs are Pay Group F, not A. Processing through the wrong category breaks pay.
4. **EDP timing miscalculated.** Paid EDPs must be performed within 60 days of the missed drill and within the same fiscal year. Late EDPs become unpaid.
5. **MUTA code mismatched to actual hours performed.** Member did 8 hours but MUTA-1 (single period) submitted. Fix by correcting MUTA code.

## Quality Control on Category Selection

Before submitting a drill batch, verify these.

- All members listed have correct pay group in MCTFS
- All duty types match what the unit performed
- MUTA codes match actual hours per member
- EDP dates fall within the 60-day window
- ATP and RMP authorizations are documented
- FHD missions are documented in the funeral honors record

## Related Roles

Category selection drives every downstream pay action.

- **Marines** verify their pay matches what they performed by reviewing the LES.
- **Leaders** confirm muster attendance and duty period type at the drill weekend.
- **Commanders** approve ATP, RMP, and special duty period authorizations.
