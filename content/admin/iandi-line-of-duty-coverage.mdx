## What LOD Coverage Is

When a reservist is injured during inactive duty status (drill, AT, FHD, etc.), Line of Duty (LOD) determination establishes whether the injury occurred in qualifying duty. LOD-positive determinations open access to.

- Medical care for the injury at military or VA facilities
- Incapacitation pay if the injury prevents civilian employment
- Disability evaluation if the injury causes lasting impact
- Survivor benefits if the injury results in death

LOD-negative determinations leave the reservist with civilian insurance for treatment.

This is high-stakes for injured reservists. Get the documentation right.

## When LOD Investigation Triggers

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6 and JAGMAN, an LOD investigation is required when.

- A reservist is injured during inactive duty
- A reservist contracts a disease during inactive duty
- A reservist is killed during inactive duty
- The injury or condition manifests during qualifying duty even if cause is unclear

The investigation establishes facts about how the injury occurred and whether the reservist was in qualifying duty status.

## Qualifying Duty Status for LOD

Status during the injury determines coverage.

| Duty Type | LOD Eligible |
|-----------|--------------|
| Regular IDT (drill weekend) | Yes |
| Annual Training (AT) | Yes (active duty status, separate authority) |
| Active Duty for Training (ADT) | Yes (active duty) |
| Funeral Honors Duty (FHD) | Yes |
| Muster Duty (MD) | Yes |
| Travel to/from drill (specific conditions) | Yes (portal-to-portal coverage where statute applies) |
| Travel to civilian work | No |
| Personal time at home | No |
| Civilian employment | No |

## Coverage Categories Following LOD Determination

### LOD-Positive (Injury In Line of Duty)

The reservist receives.

- Medical care at military treatment facility (MTF) or VA
- Continued treatment until determined fit or unfit for duty
- Incapacitation pay if injury prevents civilian employment per DoDI 1241.01<Citation source="DoDI 1241.01" />
- Disability evaluation if condition warrants
- Continued status as drilling reservist until determination

### LOD-Negative (Injury Not In Line of Duty)

The reservist receives.

- No military medical coverage for the injury
- Civilian insurance is the primary coverage
- May still continue drilling if able
- May separate or transfer based on condition

### LOD-Pending

While investigation is open.

- Initial medical care typically authorized
- Continued coverage subject to investigation outcome
- Documentation gathered for final determination

## Performance Steps for LOD Processing

### Step 1 - Identify the Incident

When an injury occurs.

- Document the incident immediately with witnesses
- Identify the duty status at time of injury
- Notify the chain of command
- Begin medical care as needed

### Step 2 - Initiate Investigation

Per JAGMAN.

- Convening authority orders investigation
- Investigating officer appointed
- Investigation begins documentation gathering
- Witnesses interviewed
- Medical evaluation completed

### Step 3 - Document the Duty Status

The investigation establishes.

- Was the reservist in qualifying duty at time of injury?
- Were there any deviations from authorized duty?
- Was misconduct involved?
- What was the cause of injury?

### Step 4 - Complete Investigation Report

The investigation report includes.

- Findings of fact
- Determination of duty status at time of injury
- Determination of misconduct involvement (if any)
- Recommended LOD determination
- Supporting evidence

### Step 5 - Forward for Final Determination

The package routes through.

- Convening authority review
- Higher command review
- HQMC for complex cases
- Final LOD determination issued

### Step 6 - Process Coverage Based on Determination

Once LOD determined.

- LOD-positive: Coordinate continuing medical care, incapacitation pay if applicable, disability evaluation
- LOD-negative: Coordinate transition to civilian insurance, document case
- File determination in member record

## Incapacitation Pay

Per 10 USC 12302<Citation source="10 USC 12302" /> and DoDI 1241.01<Citation source="DoDI 1241.01" />, an LOD-positive reservist may receive incapacitation pay when.

- Injury or illness prevents performance of military duty
- Injury or illness prevents civilian employment
- Reservist would otherwise have earned civilian income

Incapacitation pay matches what the reservist would have earned in civilian employment, up to specified limits.

## Common Errors on LOD Processing

These hit at the I&I shop and unit level.

1. **Investigation delayed.** Documentation gets stale. Witnesses become unavailable. Begin investigation immediately upon injury.
2. **Witnesses not interviewed promptly.** Memories fade. Interview while events fresh.
3. **Status documentation insufficient.** Cannot prove qualifying duty at time of injury. Maintain detailed muster and accountability records.
4. **Misconduct evaluation skipped.** If reservist's misconduct contributed to injury, this affects LOD determination. Investigation must address.
5. **Member coordination failed.** Member is injured and uncertain about coverage. Communicate clearly throughout the process.

## Pre-Mobilization Coverage

Per MCRAMM Chapter 12, reservists alerted for mobilization may receive pre-mobilization coverage.

- Medical and dental review before mobilization
- Coverage for conditions discovered during pre-mobilization screening
- Limited duration coverage to prepare for mobilization
- Different rules than LOD coverage for incidents during drill

## Post-Mobilization Coverage (TAMP)

After demobilization, reservists transition to.

- Transitional Assistance Management Program (TAMP) coverage for 180 days
- Continued medical coverage for service-connected conditions through VA
- Family coverage during the transition period
- Potential transition to TRS or TRR depending on retirement status

## Coordination Touchpoints

LOD processing touches multiple organizations.

- **Investigating Officer**: Conducts the investigation
- **Convening Authority**: Orders investigation and reviews findings
- **SJA**: Legal review of LOD determination
- **Medical Treatment Facility**: Provides care during investigation
- **MARFORRES G-1 / HQMC**: Final determination authority
- **DFAS**: Incapacitation pay processing if applicable
- **VA**: For disability evaluation if applicable

## Related Roles

LOD processing affects every party involved with the injured reservist.

- **Marines** (injured reservists) cooperate with the investigation and access medical care.
- **Leaders** at the unit document the incident and witness statements.
- **Commanders** convene investigations and recommend LOD determination.
