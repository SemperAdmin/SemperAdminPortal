## Role and responsibility

The CACO coordinates mortuary affairs with the PADD (Person Authorized to Direct Disposition) per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 4. The HQMC Casualty Section coordinates national-level mortuary affairs. The S-1 officer signs by direction on mortuary affairs routing. The Marine Corps mortuary affairs office processes the actual disposition of remains.

Source documents flow from the confirmed casualty, the DD Form 93 PADD identification, the PADD election on disposition, autopsy reports when applicable, and shipment documentation. Mortuary affairs actions land as the disposition order, the shipment record, the burial allowance authorization, and the escort travel orders. Outputs include the disposition of remains, the burial allowance processed, the National Cemetery interment when elected, and the family travel via ITO when applicable.

Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 4 (Person Authorized to Direct Disposition), the PADD makes the disposition election. Per paragraph 5 (Disposition of Remains), the disposition options follow the PADD direction. Per paragraph 7 (Burial Allowances), authorized allowances apply. Per paragraph 15 (Invitational Travel Orders), family travel is authorized.

## Trigger events

- Confirmed casualty triggers mortuary affairs activation.
- PADD identified per DD Form 93 and MCTFS records.
- Autopsy required per Chapter 5 paragraph 2.
- Organ donation election per Chapter 5 paragraph 3.
- Disposition direction received from PADD per paragraph 4.
- Shipment of remains required per paragraph 6.
- National Cemetery interment elected per paragraph 9.
- Burial at sea elected per paragraph 10.
- ITO requested for family travel per paragraph 15.

## Processing workflow

1. Coordinate autopsy when required. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 2 (Autopsies), determine autopsy requirement per the casualty circumstance.
2. Process organ donation. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 3 (Organ Donations), process per the order rules when applicable.
3. Identify the PADD. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 4 (Person Authorized to Direct Disposition), identify the PADD from DD Form 93 and MCTFS records.
4. Process disposition. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 5 (Disposition of Remains), follow the PADD election.
5. Coordinate shipment. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 6 (Shipment of Remains), arrange shipment to the disposition location.
6. Process burial allowance. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 7 (Burial Allowances), authorize the burial allowance.
7. Process reimbursement. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 8 (Reimbursement for Funeral/Interment), process family reimbursement when applicable.
8. Coordinate National Cemetery. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 9 (Burial in a National Cemetery), arrange National Cemetery interment when elected.
9. Process ITO. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 15 (Invitational Travel Orders), authorize family travel.
10. Coordinate escorts. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 16 (Escorts), assign escorts for remains transport.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Marine Corps Mortuary Affairs office. Coordinates national-level mortuary affairs.
- HQMC Casualty Section. Coordinates Casualty Section mortuary support.
- DCIPS. National-level casualty processing.
- Unit casualty case file. Local record of mortuary affairs.

### Forms

- PADD Election Form. Captures PADD disposition direction.
- Burial Allowance Authorization. Per Chapter 5 paragraph 7.
- DD Form 1419 Application for Burial Allowance. When applicable.
- Invitational Travel Order (ITO). Per Chapter 5 paragraph 15.
- DD Form 2 Family Travel Order. Family travel authorization.
- Escort Travel Order. Per Chapter 5 paragraph 16.

## Common pitfalls

- PADD identification stale. The DD Form 93 lags actual family circumstance. Refresh DD Form 93 annually.
- Disposition acted on without PADD direction. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 4, the PADD directs. The CACO confirms.
- Burial allowance not processed. The family bears unauthorized expense. Process per Chapter 5 paragraph 7 timeline.
- ITO travel processed without authorization. Family travel reimbursement requires the ITO per Chapter 5 paragraph 15. Confirm before family departs.
- Escort assigned without proper coordination. Per Chapter 5 paragraph 16, escort duties require coordination with the mortuary affairs office.

## Decision points

- Autopsy versus no autopsy. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 5 paragraph 2, autopsy determination follows the casualty circumstance and PADD election where applicable.
- Burial location. National Cemetery (paragraph 9), private cemetery, or burial at sea (paragraph 10). The PADD elects.
- National Cemetery selection. The PADD elects the specific National Cemetery. The Mortuary Affairs office coordinates capacity and timing per Chapter 5 paragraph 9.
- Escort assignment. The Mortuary Affairs office assigns the escort per Chapter 5 paragraph 16. The unit provides candidates when applicable.
