## Role and responsibility

The losing unit S-1 processes the leave-in-conjunction-with-PCS authorization per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 10. The S-1 chief validates accrued leave and processes the travel order leave authorization. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the leave order when delegated. The gaining unit S-1 processes the receiving endorsement per Chapter 4 Section 6.

Source documents flow from the PCS order, the Marine's MCTFS accrued leave balance, the travel order, and the leave-in-conjunction request. PCS leave transactions land in MOL and MCTFS. Outputs include the leave order, the travel order leave authorization per Chapter 4 Section 3, the Delay En Route entry per Chapter 4 Section 4, and the receiving endorsement at the gaining unit per Chapter 4 Section 6.

Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 10 (Leave In Conjunction With Permanent), Marines taking leave between PCS detach and PCS join are in chargeable status throughout the leave period. Per Chapter 4 Section 3 (Travel Order Leave Authorization), the travel order documents the leave authority. Per Chapter 4 Section 4 (Delay En Route), the rules apply to authorized travel pauses.

## Trigger events

- PCS order issued with leave-in-conjunction request.
- Marine requests Delay En Route between detach and join.
- Travel order leave authorization required per Chapter 4 Section 3.
- Receiving endorsement triggers at gaining unit per Chapter 4 Section 6.
- Marine extends leave during PCS travel.
- Return to military control after extended leave during PCS.
- Marine fails to report to gaining unit within the leave window.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the PCS order. Pull the order with leave-in-conjunction request. The losing unit S-1 clerk runs receipt.
2. Validate accrued leave. Pull current accrued leave from MCTFS. Confirm balance covers requested duration per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 10.
3. Process Travel Order Leave Authorization. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 4 Section 3 (Travel Order Leave Authorization), document the leave authority on the travel order.
4. Coordinate Delay En Route. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 4 Section 4 (Delay En Route), apply the rules to authorized travel pauses.
5. Cut the leave order. The S-1 chief processes the leave order per Chapter 2 paragraph 10.
6. Process Receiving Endorsement. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 4 Section 6 (Receiving Endorsement), the gaining unit S-1 endorses the travel order on arrival.
7. Track chargeable status. The Marine is chargeable throughout the leave period per paragraph 10.
8. Coordinate arrival timing. Confirm the Marine reports to gaining unit within the leave window.
9. Document the leave period in MCTFS. Submit through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> leave transaction format.
10. File the PCS leave packet. Stage in the OMPF Field Folder leave tab.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds chargeable leave state during PCS travel.
- MOL. Captures PCS leave order.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts the leave transaction.
- TFSMS. Holds the PCS gain and loss state.

### Forms

- PCS Order. Source for the leave-in-conjunction request.
- Travel Order Leave Authorization. Per Chapter 4 Section 3.
- Delay En Route Authorization. Per Chapter 4 Section 4.
- Receiving Endorsement. Per Chapter 4 Section 6.
- NAVMC 10274 Application for Leave or Liberty. Standard leave request.

## Common pitfalls

- Leave authorized exceeding accrued balance during PCS. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 10, leave runs against accrued balance. Validate before authorization.
- Travel Order Leave Authorization skipped. Per Chapter 4 Section 3, the travel order documents the leave authority. Process at PCS order receipt.
- Delay En Route not authorized formally. Per Chapter 4 Section 4, Delay En Route requires authorization. The S-1 chief processes.
- Receiving endorsement skipped at gaining unit. Per Chapter 4 Section 6, the endorsement closes the travel order. Process on arrival.
- Marine arrives at gaining unit past leave window. UA status risk emerges. The S-1 chief tracks via PCS leave roster.

## Decision points

- Leave duration during PCS. The Marine requests leave longer than typical. The losing commander authorizes per accrued balance and per Chapter 2 paragraph 10.
- Delay En Route versus regular leave. Per Chapter 4 Section 4, Delay En Route is a specific authorized pause. The S-1 officer routes per the right category.
- Extension during PCS travel. The Marine requests extension. The losing or gaining commander authorizes per paragraph 10 timing.
