## Role and responsibility

The unit training manager identifies service-directed and unit-directed training requirements from MCO 1500.63 and command guidance. The S-1 chief tracks completion per Marine in MCTIMS. The S-1 officer briefs shortfalls to the chain and routes make-up training scheduling. The commander adds unit-directed requirements per discretion under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2.

Source documents flow from MCO 1500.63 (annual training requirements derived from reference (j) of MCO 1553.3C), unit-type-specific policy, and command guidance. Requirements land in the unit training plan and as MCTIMS calendar events. Outputs include completion tracking per Marine, readiness reporting roll-ups, and shortfall briefs to the chain.

MCAAT and the IGMC UTM functional area inspectors check MCTIMS for current-year service-directed training completion. A Marine without current-year service-directed training triggers a finding under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2. Service-directed requirements are not optional.

## Trigger events

- Annual MCO 1500.63 update releases.
- Inbound Marine arrives requiring current-year service-directed training.
- Unit type change brings new unit-directed requirements.
- Pre-deployment cycle adds deployment-specific requirements.
- Command guidance change adds unit-directed requirements.
- Quarterly review identifies completion shortfalls.
- Marine reaches the annual cycle for sustainment training.

## Processing workflow

1. Pull annual training requirements. The unit training manager pulls the current MCO 1500.63<Citation source="MCO 1500.63" /> from MARADMIN updates per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2.
2. Identify service-directed events. Mark events applicable to the entire Marine Corps Total Force per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2.
3. Identify unit-directed events. Mark events tied to specific unit type or community per the same paragraph.
4. Add commander-discretion unit-directed events. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2, commanders may determine additional unit-directed requirements.
5. Map to MCTIMS calendar. Schedule each requirement as a calendar event per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5).
6. Track completion per Marine. Use MCTIMS UTM or IMM Scoresheets per paragraph 4.c.(6) for individual completion.
7. Roll up completion rates. Generate completion reports per requirement and per Marine.
8. Brief shortfalls. The S-1 officer presents shortfalls to the commander and subordinate leaders.
9. Schedule make-up training. The unit training manager schedules make-up sessions for delinquent Marines.
10. Refresh on update. Repeat the process at every MCO 1500.63<Citation source="MCO 1500.63" /> update.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTIMS UTM Module. Calendar events for service-directed and unit-directed requirements.
- MCTIMS UTM or IMM Scoresheets. Per-Marine completion tracking.
- MOL. Surfaces required training to the Marine.

### Forms

- Annual Training Requirements List (per MCO 1500.63). Service-directed event source.
- Unit-Directed Training Memo. Local memo documenting commander-discretion requirements.
- Completion Report. Generated in MCTIMS per Marine and per requirement.

## Common pitfalls

- Service-directed requirements skipped on inbound Marines. Inbound briefs miss the service-directed list. Build into the inbound brief checklist.
- Unit-directed requirements documented in informal memos only. The requirement does not show in MCTIMS. Build calendar events for every unit-directed requirement per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(7).
- MCO 1500.63 update missed. The unit trains to last year's requirements. Subscribe to MARADMIN updates for the order.
- Make-up training not scheduled. Delinquent Marines accumulate. The unit training manager schedules make-ups within 30 days of delinquency.
- Completion rates not briefed to the commander. Commander-level visibility lags. Brief on a fixed cycle.

## Decision points

- Commander-discretion versus service-directed. The requirement is unit-specific only. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2, commander-discretion requirements are permitted in addition to service-directed requirements. The commander decides scope.
- Pre-deployment additions. Deployment cycle drives additional unit-directed requirements. The commander adds per paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2.
- Waiver for individual requirement. The Marine has medical or other limitation. The S-1 officer routes a documented waiver per command policy.

## Authority

This sub-page sits under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(d).2 which defines service-directed requirements (typically derived from MCO 1500.63<Citation source="MCO 1500.63" />), unit-directed requirements (specific to certain Marines by unit type or community), and commander-discretion additions. MCO 1500.63<Citation source="MCO 1500.63" /> is the authoritative source for service-directed annual training. MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(7) requires documentation in MCTIMS. NAVMC 3500.3E<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.3E" /> T-R event 0170-OPER-2001 covers the 01XX training plan including service-directed integration.
