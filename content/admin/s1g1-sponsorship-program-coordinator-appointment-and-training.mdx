## Role and responsibility

The unit commanding officer signs the Sponsorship Coordinator (SC) appointment letter per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(7) (Sponsorship Coordinators) and Enclosure (1) (Sample Sponsorship Coordinator Appointment Letter). The S-1 officer signs by direction on the SC training routing. The S-1 chief tracks the SC appointment file and the SC training completion. The SC owns daily program execution per paragraph 4.b.(7).

Source documents flow from the unit commanding officer's identification of the SC candidate, the SC's qualifications (grade, MOS, tour length), the Enclosure (1) appointment letter format, the SC training material, and the SC training completion record. SC actions land in the SC appointment file. Outputs include the signed SC appointment letter per Enclosure (1), the SC training completion record, the SC handoff at rotation, and the audit trail for IGMC Sponsorship Program FA inspection.

Per MCO 1320.11H paragraph 4.b.(6) (Unit Commanding Officers), the unit commanding officer appoints the SC. Per paragraph 4.b.(7) (Sponsorship Coordinators), the SC runs the unit program. Per Enclosure (1), the appointment letter standardizes the formal designation, the scope, and the duration.

## Trigger events

- New SC appointment required at unit standup or SC PCS.
- Annual SC refresh cycle opens.
- SC training cycle opens.
- IGMC Sponsorship Program FA inspection notice arrives.
- Change of command triggers SC appointment refresh.
- SC removed from role triggering replacement.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the SC candidate. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(6), the unit commanding officer identifies the SC candidate from the unit S-1 or admin section.
2. Validate candidate grade and tour length. Per paragraph 4.b.(7), the candidate holds grade and tour length sufficient to run the program through the appointment period.
3. Draft the appointment letter. Per Enclosure (1) (Sample Sponsorship Coordinator Appointment Letter), draft using the sample as the template.
4. Sign and distribute the appointment letter. The unit commanding officer signs. The SC, the S-1, the unit family readiness section, and the installation IR&R receive copies.
5. Run the SC initial training. Per paragraph 4.b.(7), the SC completes the required training covering program scope, sponsor assignment, welcome aboard letters per Enclosures (2) and (4)-(6), metrics per Enclosure (3), and OCONUS-specific procedures.
6. Brief the SC on Privacy Act and records management. Per paragraph 5.b (Records Management) and paragraph 5.c (Privacy Act), the SC handles PII per MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, SECNAVINST 5211.5F<Citation source="SECNAVINST 5211.5F" />, and 5 USC 552a<Citation source="5 USC 552a" />.
7. Maintain the SC appointment file. Stage the appointment letter, the training completion record, and any updates.
8. Refresh the appointment on SC PCS or rotation. Cut a new appointment letter on SC change.
9. Audit SC appointment posture. Run the annual audit cycle against the appointment letter and the training completion record.
10. Report SC appointment status to the commander. The S-1 officer briefs the commander on SC posture annually.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- SC Appointment File. Local file holding the signed letter and training records.
- Unit Sponsorship Roster. Local register driven by the SC.
- Installation IR&R Office. Coordination partner per paragraph 4.b.(9).
- HQMC Marine and Family Programs Division (MF). Per paragraph 4.b.(2).

### Forms

- Sponsorship Coordinator Appointment Letter. Per Enclosure (1).
- SC Training Completion Record. Local format documenting training.
- SC Handoff Memorandum. Local format used at SC PCS or rotation.

## Common pitfalls

- SC appointment not in writing. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(7) and Enclosure (1), the appointment is a written letter signed by the unit commanding officer. A verbal designation breaks on IGMC inspection.
- SC tour length insufficient to cover the appointment period. The SC rotates without handoff. Validate tour length at candidate selection.
- SC training skipped. Per paragraph 4.b.(7), the SC completes the training before executing. A non-trained SC misapplies welcome aboard letter types or skips metrics.
- Privacy Act brief omitted. Per paragraph 5.c, the SC handles PII. Without the brief, the SC risks Privacy Act breach.
- Appointment file out of sync with current SC. Per paragraph 4.b.(6), the unit reflects the current SC on file. A stale file shows the prior SC.

## Decision points

- SC candidate grade. Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> paragraph 4.b.(7), the SC holds sufficient grade to interface with sponsors and inbound Marines. The unit commanding officer matches grade to scope.
- Multiple SCs versus single SC. Larger units appoint multiple SCs at the company or section level. Smaller units run a single SC. The unit commanding officer decides per paragraph 4.b.(6).
- SC PCS or rotation timing. Per paragraph 4.b.(7), the SC handoff runs ahead of departure. The S-1 chief coordinates the handoff window.
