## Role and responsibility

The unit commander signs DD Form 577 as the appointing authority per MCO 4650.39A<Citation source="MCO 4650.39A" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(b). The S-1 officer signs by direction on the routing of the appointment package. The LDTA holds the DD Form 577 file for the unit per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.f and reconciles the Complete Traveler Information List monthly against the appointments. The ODTA validates the routing list against the AO and RO appointments per Chapter 1 paragraph 3.d. The FDTA validates voucher reconciliation under the same DD Form 577 framework per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.b.(5).

Source documents flow from the unit organization structure, the candidate's grade and billet, the role requirement per Chapter 1 paragraphs 2-4, the current DD Form 577 version, the digital signature workflow, the by-name waiver letter per paragraph 4.c when grade requires, and the AO submission to DFAS per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.c. Outputs include the digitally signed DD Form 577 in the LDTA file, the DFAS submission record for AO appointments, the by-name waiver letter file, the Assumption of Command Letter file, the Delegation of Authority file, and the audit trail for IGMC inspection.

Per Chapter 1 paragraph 2 (LDTA), the order names LDTA as the senior DTA holding org-level oversight. Per paragraph 3 (ODTA), the order names ODTA as the unit-level DTA running daily execution. Per paragraph 4 (FDTA), the order names FDTA as the disbursing-side DTA running financial reconciliation. Per paragraph 4.b.(6)(b), the commander appoints these roles in writing using DD Form 577.

## Trigger events

- New unit standup requires the LDTA, ODTA, FDTA, BDTA appointment cycle.
- Inbound AO check-in triggers a new DD Form 577.
- Change of command triggers Assumption of Command Letter and refresh of the appointment file.
- LDTA, ODTA, FDTA, or AO PCS or rotation triggers replacement.
- AO grade below SNCO or GS-7 triggers by-name waiver letter per paragraph 4.c.
- LDTA or ODTA placement in routing list triggers by-name waiver letter per paragraph 4.c.
- Annual permission and access review opens.
- IGMC Defense Travel System FA inspection notice arrives.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the role and grade requirement. Per MCO 4650.39A<Citation source="MCO 4650.39A" /> Chapter 1 paragraphs 2-4, confirm the role need and the grade floor.
2. Validate the candidate. The candidate holds grade, billet, and tour length sufficient for the appointment scope.
3. Draft DD Form 577. Per Chapter 2 sections 2-3 and Chapter 11 paragraph 2.a, use the current DD Form 577 version. Pen changes are not authorized.
4. Process by-name waiver letter. Per paragraph 4.c, the first O-6 or GS-15 in the supervisory chain signs a by-name standard naval correspondence waiver approval letter for an AO below SNCO or GS-7 grade, or for an LDTA or ODTA placed in a routing list. Per SECNAV M-5216.5 Chapter 2, the letter uses naval correspondence format.
5. Sign digitally. The commander or designated representative digitally signs. The appointee digitally signs. Both signatures use a current valid CAC certificate.
6. Submit AO DD Form 577 to DFAS. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.c and DoD FMR Vol 5 Chapter 5 paragraph 4.2.1.1, the LDTA emails the AO DD Form 577 to DFAS with the AO name. Retain the email evidence.
7. Retain the appointment file. Per Chapter 2 and MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, retain DD Form 577 for 10 years. Retain Assumption of Command Letter and Delegation of Authority documents on file.
8. Verify separation of duties. Per Chapter 1 and Chapter 2 paragraph 2, hold one role per individual where the routing list demands separation.
9. Run monthly permission and access alignment. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2 and Chapter 6 paragraph 1-2, the LDTA reconciles permissions and access against DD Form 577 appointments during the monthly Complete Traveler Information List review.
10. Audit the appointment posture. Run the annual audit against the DD Form 577 file, the DFAS submission record, the waiver letter file, and the Complete Traveler Information List.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- DTS Permissions module. Driven by DD Form 577.
- DFAS travel email box. Receives the AO DD Form 577 submission per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.c.
- LDTA Appointment File. Local file holding signed forms and waiver letters.
- Complete Traveler Information List. Monthly reconciliation surface per Chapter 6 paragraphs 1-2.

### Forms

- DD Form 577 Appointment / Termination Record - Authorized Signature. Current version required per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.a.
- By-Name Standard Naval Correspondence Waiver Letter per paragraph 4.c.
- Assumption of Command Letter. Retained on file per Chapter 2 paragraph 2.
- Delegation of Authority document. Retained on file per Chapter 2 paragraph 2.
- ROA Request form per Chapter 2 paragraph 2.l. Valid for two years.

## Common pitfalls

- Pen change on DD Form 577. Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.a, pen changes are not authorized. Cut a new digitally signed form.
- Outdated DD Form 577 version. Per Chapter 2 sections 2-3, the most current DD Form 577 version applies at the time of appointment.
- AO DD Form 577 not submitted to DFAS. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.c, the LDTA emails the AO DD Form 577 to DFAS. Without the submission, the AO lacks DFAS recognition.
- Missing by-name waiver letter. Per paragraph 4.c, an AO below SNCO or GS-7 grade or a DTA in a routing list requires a signed waiver letter from the first O-6 or GS-15 in the supervisory chain.
- DD Form 577 not retained for 10 years. Per Chapter 2 and MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, the retention floor is 10 years.
- Group or organization access outside the designated organization without justification. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2, justification accompanies the appointment.
- Separation of duties broken. Per Chapter 1 and Chapter 2 paragraph 2, the same individual does not hold conflicting roles in the routing list.

## Decision points

- AO grade. Per paragraph 4.c, AO grade SNCO or higher does not require a waiver. AO grade below SNCO or GS-7 requires the waiver letter.
- LDTA or ODTA in routing list. Per paragraph 4.c, placement in a routing list triggers a waiver letter for the LDTA or ODTA.
- DMO reviewer placement. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 2.j, the LDTA inserts Marine Corps DMO reviewers into the appropriate DMO authorizations routing list. The LDTA coordinates with DMO when the function runs externally.
- Single LDTA versus multiple ODTAs. Larger units appoint multiple ODTAs at the section level under a single LDTA. The unit commander decides the structure.
