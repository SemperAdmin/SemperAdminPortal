## Role and responsibility

The promotions clerk owns daily promotion processing. The S-1 chief tracks the unit promotion roster against zone and selection cycles. The S-1 officer signs by direction on promotion ceremony coordination and Page 11 entries. The commander pins the rank at the promotion ceremony and signs reduction or denial actions.

Source documents flow from MMEA promotion warrants, MMPB selection lists, meritorious or remedial promotion recommendations, time-in-grade and time-in-service rosters, and the unit composite score worksheets. Promotion actions land in MCTFS as grade changes with effective date and pay impact. Outputs include the executed promotion order, the updated pay grade, the Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006, the unit awards register entry, and the ceremony record.

Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1201 (Promotion System), the enlisted promotion system runs across the active component, the Selected Marine Corps Reserve and Active Reserve (SMCR/AR), and the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR). Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1202 (Time in Grade and Time in Service), eligibility flows from the TIG and TIS rules. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1203 (Promotion Policies), promotion authorities and approval routes are set. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204 (Promotion Restrictions), MMEA and the commander apply restrictions tied to discipline, medical, or performance state. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1205 (Promotion Records), the S-1 documents the promotion in the OMPF and MCTFS.

## Trigger events

- Promotion warrant received from MMEA (enlisted) or MMPB (officer).
- Time-in-grade reached for non-board zone promotion.
- Selection board results released.
- Meritorious promotion recommendation routed.
- Remedial promotion documentation required per MCO P1400.32D Chapter 2 paragraphs 2600-2604.
- Frocking authorized for officer or SNCO.
- Reduction in grade order received.
- Promotion deferred, withheld, or removed from list.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive promotion warrant. Pull from MMEA, MMPB, or selection board release.
2. Validate eligibility. Cross-check time-in-grade, time-in-service, MOS qualification, and education requirements against the warrant per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1202.
3. Apply promotion policies. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1203, validate the promotion authority and approval route.
4. Screen restrictions. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, screen for discipline, medical, performance, or administrative holds.
5. Cut MCTFS transaction. Submit through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> promotion transaction format.
6. Coordinate ceremony. Schedule the promotion ceremony per command tradition.
7. Process Page 11. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6 and MCO P1400.32D Chapter 1 paragraph 1205, document the promotion.
8. Update pay grade and entitlements. New pay grade triggers base pay change. BAH and BAS shift on the effective date.
9. Process meritorious or remedial recommendations. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 2 paragraphs 2300-2306 (special programs) and paragraphs 2600-2604 (remedial), recommendations follow specific routing.
10. Track promotion zones. Maintain unit roster of Marines in promotion zone against MMEA published cutting scores per Chapter 2 paragraphs 2500-2501.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds grade and pay state.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts promotion transactions through Unit Diary.
- MOL. Surfaces promotion warrant and selection results to the Marine.
- MMEA. Issues enlisted warrants and cutting scores.
- MMPB. Issues officer warrants and selection board results.

### Forms

- Promotion Warrant. Issued by MMEA or MMPB.
- NAVMC 11070 Recommendation for Promotion. Local meritorious recommendation.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting promotion or non-recommendation.
- Composite Score Worksheet. Per Chapter 2 paragraphs 2200-2203.
- Frocking Letter. Per MCO P1400.32D Chapter 2 (and MCO 1400.31C for officers).

## Common pitfalls

- Promotion warrant processed without eligibility validation. The Marine promotes without meeting criteria. Validate per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1202.
- Page 11 omitted on promotion. The OMPF lands without record. Cut Page 11 at promotion per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6 and MCO P1400.32D Chapter 1 paragraph 1205.
- Restriction screen skipped. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, discipline, medical, or performance state suspends the promotion. Screen at warrant receipt.
- Pay grade transition lag. New pay does not post on next cut. Cut the promotion transaction at the effective date per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.
- Frocking processed without authorization. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1203, frocking requires specific authorization.
- Reduction in grade not processed promptly. The Marine continues at higher grade past the order. Process at the order effective date.

## Decision points

- Frocking versus promotion. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1203, frocking allows wear of grade without pay. The commander authorizes per the criteria.
- Meritorious versus board promotion. Per MCO P1400.32D Chapter 2 paragraphs 2300-2306 and MCO 1400.31C, meritorious or special programs route through specific authority versus the standard board cycle.
- Promotion deferral, withhold, or removal. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204 and Chapter 2 paragraphs 2600-2604, the action routes through MMEA with documentation.
