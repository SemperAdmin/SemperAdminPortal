## Role and responsibility

CG MEF and MARFORRES carry responsibility for the conduct of formal evaluations per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.2.b. Authority delegates to Wing, Division, or Force Service Support Group commanders for evaluations not requiring MAGTF-element coordination. The S-1 officer designates evaluators in writing per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(c). The S-1 chief tracks evaluator credentials and event scheduling.

Source documents flow from the published METL, the T-R Manual event standards, evaluator credential files, and the MCTIMS calendar. Evaluations land in MCTIMS as performance evaluation checklists tied to T-R event entries. Outputs include the evaluator findings, the AAR report, the formal evaluation report when applicable, and the readiness picture update to DRRS-MC.

MCAAT and the IGMC UTM functional area inspectors check evaluator designation letters against MCTIMS event entries and validate evaluator qualifications against event level. Per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7001.3, evaluators must have accomplished at least the level of the events being evaluated. Designation without qualification triggers a finding.

## Trigger events

- Assessed T-R event scheduled requires evaluator assignment.
- Pre-deployment formal evaluation cycle opens (typically 18 to 24 months prior to deployment per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.1).
- MCCRE scheduled per MCO 3501.1E<Citation source="MCO 3501.1E" />.
- Real-world contingency reduces the training cycle requiring compressed evaluation per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.1.
- Change of command requires fresh evaluator designations.
- New T-R Manual release changes evaluator requirements.
- Cross-unit evaluator support requested by a sister unit.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the event and required level. Pull the T-R event from the T-R Manual including event code, level (1000 through 8000), and standard per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 4002.1.a.
2. Match evaluator candidates. Identify Marines who meet the event-level qualification per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7001.3.
3. Verify prior accomplishment. Confirm the candidate has accomplished at least the level of the event being evaluated. The S-1 chief verifies against MCTIMS individual event history.
4. Designate the evaluator in writing. The commander signs the designation per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(c).
5. Brief the evaluator. The unit training manager briefs the evaluator on the event standard, condition, and grading criteria from the T-R Manual per MCRP 7-20A.4<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.4" />.
6. Conduct the evaluation. The evaluator runs the evaluation against the T-R event standard per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7000.3.
7. Document findings. The evaluator completes the performance evaluation checklist per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(h).
8. Conduct the AAR. Run the AAR with the evaluator present to capture sustains, improves, and lessons learned per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7000.3.
9. File evaluator findings. Upload the performance evaluation checklist and AAR to MCTIMS per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5).
10. Schedule formal evaluations. Plan formal evaluations on the 18 to 24 month pre-deployment cycle per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.1.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTIMS UTM Module. Holds the calendar event entry and the performance evaluation checklist.
- T-R Manual (per occupational field). Defines the event standard against which the evaluation runs.
- Evaluator credential file. Local file documenting evaluator qualifications.

### Forms

- Evaluator Designation Letter. Local format documenting evaluator, event, and qualification basis.
- Performance Evaluation Checklist. T-R event-specific checklist completed by the evaluator.
- Formal Evaluation Report. Per MCO 3500.11G and MCO 3501.1E for unit-level formal evaluations.
- AAR Report. Local AAR template completed with the evaluator present.

## Common pitfalls

- Evaluator designated without prior accomplishment of the event. Per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7001.3, evaluators must have accomplished at least the level. Verify against MCTIMS individual history before designation.
- Performance evaluation checklist not uploaded to MCTIMS. The evaluation does not count toward CRP. Upload at the moment of evaluation completion per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(h).
- Formal evaluation cycle compressed without HHQ coordination. Real-world contingency drives compression but the S-1 officer must brief HHQ per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.1.
- Evaluator briefs without the T-R event standard. Evaluation runs against memory, not standard. Pull the T-R Manual event standard at every evaluation per MCRP 7-20A.4<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.4" />.
- Evaluator missing from the AAR. Lessons learned lack the evaluator perspective. Schedule the AAR with the evaluator present per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7000.3.

## Decision points

- Internal versus cross-unit evaluator. The unit lacks an internal evaluator at the required level. Request cross-unit evaluator support through the chain per MCO 3500.11G<Citation source="MCO 3500.11G" />.
- Formal versus informal evaluation. Per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7000.3, most unit training evaluations are informal. Formal evaluations apply for verification of readiness prior to deployment per paragraph 7002.1. The unit training manager calls the level.
- Compressed cycle response. Real-world contingency cuts the training cycle. Per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 7002.1, planners adjust schedules to ensure evaluation of mission essential tasks key to assigned mission.
