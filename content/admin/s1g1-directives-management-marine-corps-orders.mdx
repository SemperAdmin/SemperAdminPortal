## Role and responsibility

The drafting branch builds the MCO per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 2 paragraph 1 (Introduction). The DCP clerk supports the staffing routing and the front matter format. The S-1 chief reviews against Chapter 2 paragraph-by-paragraph. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the routing to the signing authority. The signing authority signs the MCO per Chapter 1 paragraph 6 (Authority to Issue Directives) and paragraph 7 (Signature Delegation).

Source documents flow from the lasting policy or procedural area driving the MCO, the parent directive (when implementing higher policy), the references list per Chapter 1 paragraph 24 (Ref Section), the enclosures list per Chapter 1 paragraph 26 (Encl Section), the reports required list per Chapter 1 paragraph 29 (Reports Required Section), the staffing inputs from the chop chain, and the legal review. MCO outputs include the signed five-paragraph order per Figure 2-1, the enclosed procedural guidance (when applicable) structured per Chapter 1 paragraphs 50-51, the locator sheet per Chapter 1 paragraph 28 and Figure 1-3, the table of contents per Figure 1-5, and the record of changes page per Figure 1-4.

Per MCO 5215.1K Enclosure (1) Chapter 2 paragraph 1 (Introduction), MCOs carry lasting Marine Corps policy and procedural guidance. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 3 (Mandatory Paragraphs), every MCO carries the five mandatory paragraphs: 1 Situation, 2 Mission, 3 Execution, 4 Administration and Logistics, 5 Command and Signal. Per Chapter 2 Figure 2-1, the standard five-paragraph format applies. Per Figure 2-2, field commands use a field-command variant.

## Trigger events

- Lasting policy or procedural guidance requires formal MCO issuance.
- Parent MCO update triggers unit MCO update.
- Marine Corps reorganization changes policy ownership.
- Internal review per Chapter 1 paragraph 14 identifies new policy gap.
- Recurring guidance previously carried by repeated bulletins warrants consolidation into an MCO.
- Joint policy not warranting a joint directive but requiring lasting Marine Corps guidance.

## Processing workflow

1. Validate MCO scope. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 2 paragraph 1 (Introduction), confirm the content fits the MCO category. Time-limited information goes to a Bulletin per Chapter 3.
2. Apply letterhead. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2 (Letterhead Page), use the standard letterhead with the SSIC and the sponsor code per Chapter 1 paragraph 23 (Designation Line).
3. Build the five paragraph order. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 3 (Mandatory Paragraphs) and Figure 2-1 (Five Paragraph Order Format) or Figure 2-2 (Field Command Format), structure paragraphs 1 Situation, 2 Mission, 3 Execution, 4 Administration and Logistics, 5 Command and Signal.
4. Draft the Administration and Logistics paragraph. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 4, address records, forms, reports, and administrative support.
5. Draft the Command and Signal paragraph. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 5, address command relationships, effective dates, and communications.
6. Set the signature block. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 6 (Signature Block), build the signature block per the signing authority.
7. Set distribution and copy to. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 7 (Distribution and Copy to), set distribution per the scope.
8. Apply Chapter 1 front-matter paragraphs. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 21 (Preparing Directives), paragraph 22 (Reserve Designation), paragraph 23 (Designation Line), paragraphs 24-25 (Ref Section and Citing References), paragraphs 26-27 (Encl Section and Citing Enclosures), paragraph 28 (Locator Sheet), paragraphs 29-30 (Reports Required, Forms Management), and paragraph 31 (Records Disposition).
9. Structure the procedural guidance enclosure. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 50 (Main Segments of Procedural Guidance Enclosure) and paragraph 51 (Structuring Major Paragraph Numbers), build the enclosure with the chapter-section-paragraph structure.
10. Route through staffing and signature. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 17 (Staffing and Clearing Directives), route the draft through the chop chain. The signing authority signs.

## Common pitfalls

- Mandatory paragraphs missing. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 2 paragraph 3, every MCO carries the five mandatory paragraphs. A missed paragraph triggers finding on inspection.
- Time-limited information drafted as an MCO. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 1 and Chapter 3 paragraph 1, time-limited content belongs in a Bulletin.
- Records Disposition paragraph omitted. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 31 (Records Disposition), every MCO addresses records disposition for the data generated by the directive.
- Forms Management paragraph omitted when forms are introduced. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 30 (Forms Management), the MCO addresses forms management when the directive introduces or modifies forms.
- Signature delegation not validated against Chapter 1 paragraph 7. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 6, the signature block reflects valid signature delegation. Validate before routing for signature.

## Decision points

- MCO versus Bulletin. Per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" /> Enclosure (1) Chapter 2 paragraph 1 versus Chapter 3 paragraph 1, the lasting versus time-limited nature drives.
- Five paragraph standard versus field command variant. Per Figure 2-1 versus Figure 2-2, the issuing command type drives the format choice.
- Embedded procedural guidance enclosure versus simple five-paragraph order. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 50, complex procedural detail goes in an enclosure with the chapter-section-paragraph structure. Simple orders use the five paragraphs without an enclosure.
