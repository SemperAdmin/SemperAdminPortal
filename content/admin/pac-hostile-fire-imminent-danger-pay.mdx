## What HFP and IDP Are

Per 37 USC 310<Citation source="37 USC 310" /> and DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 10, two related pays compensate members exposed to combat or hazardous duty.

### Hostile Fire Pay (HFP)

Pay for actually being exposed to hostile fire.

- Applies when member is subjected to hostile fire or explosion
- $225 per month
- Activated at the moment of hostile fire exposure

### Imminent Danger Pay (IDP)

Pay for assignment to designated imminent danger areas.

- Applies when member is assigned to area DOD designates as imminent danger
- $225 per month (same rate as HFP)
- Activated by area designation, not actual hostile fire exposure

### Combined Pay

A member never draws both HFP and IDP simultaneously.

- The pay is structured so member receives one or the other based on situation
- Single $225 per month rate
- Often referred to combined as "HFP/IDP"

## Eligibility for HFP

A member receives HFP when meeting these.

- On active duty
- Subjected to hostile fire or explosion in the performance of duty
- HFP designation by command

The actual exposure is the trigger. Member exposed to hostile fire on a single day in a non-IDP-designated area receives HFP for that month.

## Eligibility for IDP

A member receives IDP when meeting these.

- On active duty
- Assigned to a DOD-designated imminent danger area
- Member is in the area for at least one day of the month
- Specific area designation per DOD instruction

The area designation is the trigger. Areas include specific operational zones, embassy posts, and other locations.

## DOD-Designated IDP Areas

Per DODI 1340.09<Citation source="DODI 1340.09" /> and current MARADMINs, areas are designated for IDP based on threat assessment.

- Areas of active military operations
- Specific embassy duty locations with elevated threat
- Specific contingency operations
- Areas with documented hostile activity

The DOD-designated area list updates periodically. Always reference current designations.

## When HFP/IDP Pays

The pay activates when.

- Member's location is on the IDP-designated list (IDP)
- Or member is exposed to hostile fire (HFP)
- Member is in qualifying status for at least one day during the month

The pay is monthly. One day in the month qualifies for the full $225.

## Performance Steps for HFP/IDP Processing

### Step 1 - Identify Deployment or Assignment

Pull member's orders.

- Identify deployment dates
- Identify deployment location
- Verify location designation (IDP or not)

### Step 2 - Verify Designation

Cross-reference DOD-designated IDP list.

- Member location on current IDP list
- Or member experienced hostile fire (HFP)
- Verify command verification

### Step 3 - Process UD/MIPS Transaction

Apply HFP/IDP via UD/MIPS.

- Specific transaction code per MCTFSCODESMAN
- Effective date when member entered qualifying status
- Monthly $225 pay activated

### Step 4 - Verify EDFR

Confirm processing within 5 business days.

- HFP/IDP posts at $225
- Member sees pay on next LES

### Step 5 - Verify Continuation

During deployment.

- Pay continues monthly
- No partial month adjustment for full month presence
- One-day-in-month standard for monthly payment

### Step 6 - Stop at Return

When member returns from deployment.

- Stop HFP/IDP retroactive to last day of qualifying status
- Verify accurate end date
- Process via UD/MIPS

## Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (CZTE) Coordination

HFP/IDP often pairs with CZTE for the same area.

- HFP/IDP pays $225 monthly
- CZTE excludes pay from federal income tax
- Different programs but often co-occur
- Each has its own designation and processing

CZTE is detailed in its own page.

## HFP/IDP and Other Pays

HFP/IDP coordinates with other deployment pays.

### HFP/IDP and HDP-L

- HDP-L for hardship location
- HFP/IDP for combat zone
- Both may apply if location qualifies for both
- Each is independent

### HFP/IDP and FSA

- FSA for family separation over 30 days
- HFP/IDP for combat zone
- Both apply during deployment
- Independent allowances

### HFP/IDP and CSP

- CSP for sea duty
- HFP/IDP for combat zone
- Both may apply if Marine is on combat-zone sea duty
- Independent allowances

## Common HFP/IDP Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **HFP/IDP not started at deployment.** Member arrived in IDP-designated area but pay never started. Process retroactive start.
2. **HFP/IDP continues after return.** Member returned from deployment but pay continues. Critical error. Stop retroactive to return date.
3. **Wrong location designation.** Member at location not on current IDP list. Verify and correct.
4. **HFP applied for non-hostile-fire incident.** HFP requires actual hostile fire exposure. IDP is the area-based pay. Verify.
5. **Partial month confusion.** One day in qualifying status earns full $225. Some PAC clerks pro-rate incorrectly.

## Audit Implications

HFP/IDP is audit-relevant.

- HFP/IDP after return creates significant overpayment
- Wrong location designation creates pay where none should apply
- Multiple month overpayment compounds quickly
- Coordination with CZTE creates complex audit chains

PAC accuracy on HFP/IDP supports unit audit posture.

## Documentation Required

For HFP/IDP actions, PAC needs.

- Deployment orders showing destination
- Command verification of arrival in IDP area
- Command verification of return date
- HFP-specific incident documentation if applicable

## Quality Control

Before submitting HFP/IDP transactions.

- Verify location on current IDP-designated list
- Verify deployment dates accurate
- Verify rate matches DOD FMR ($225 standard)
- Verify start and end dates precise

## Coordination Touchpoints

HFP/IDP processing touches.

- **Member's deploying unit**: For deployment dates and orders
- **Combatant Command**: For specific deployment IDP designations
- **DOD designation lists**: For current IDP areas
- **DFAS**: For pay processing questions

## Recent Designation Changes

DOD updates IDP-designated areas based on threat assessments.

- New areas added when threats emerge
- Areas removed when threats diminish
- MARADMINs and DOD instructions document changes
- Always reference current designation when processing

## Related Roles

HFP/IDP affects every deploying Marine.

- **Marines** verify HFP/IDP on LES during deployment.
- **Leaders** at deploying units track member arrival and return dates for processing.
- **Commanders** ensure unit deployment processing routes timely to PAC.
