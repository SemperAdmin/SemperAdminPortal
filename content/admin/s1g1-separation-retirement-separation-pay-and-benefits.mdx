## Role and responsibility

The S-1 chief computes separation pay eligibility per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1305 (Policy). The S-1 officer signs by direction on the separation pay computation and the benefits brief. The IPAC outbound counselor briefs the Marine on benefits at separation per Appendix K (Benefits at Separation Based on Characterization). The disbursing office cuts the separation pay payment per DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35<Citation source="DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35" />. The Marine signs receipt of the benefits brief and acknowledges the repayment obligation per paragraph 1311.

Source documents flow from the separation order with characterization, the SPD code from Appendix A, the YOS computation from the MCTFS active service record, the separation pay computation worksheet per DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35, the repayment obligation acknowledgment per paragraph 1311, the transition benefits checklist per paragraph 1312, and the Appendix K benefits matrix. Outputs include the computed separation pay, the disbursing office payout, the transition benefits brief record, the repayment obligation Page 11 per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, and the final pay closeout.

Per MCO 1900.16 Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1305 (Policy), separation pay is non-disability separation pay paid to involuntarily separated Marines who are fully qualified for retention. Per paragraph 1306 (Rates), the rate computes from base pay and years of service per the DoD formula. Per paragraph 1307 (Full Separation Pay), Marines involuntarily separated under specific qualifying conditions receive full separation pay. Per paragraph 1308 (Half Separation Pay), specific conditions limit the rate to half. Per paragraph 1309 (Not Eligible), separation pay does not apply to retirement, voluntary separation, punitive discharge, and other excluded categories. Per paragraph 1311 (Repayment), receipt of separation pay triggers repayment obligation against future military retired pay or VA disability compensation per the order rules.

## Trigger events

- Involuntary separation under qualifying conditions per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraphs 1307 or 1308.
- Separation pay computation request from disbursing.
- Reservist meets additional eligibility criteria per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1310.
- Marine queries repayment obligation tied to prior separation pay per paragraph 1311.
- Pre-separation counseling triggering transition benefits brief per paragraph 1312.
- Characterization decision impacting Appendix K benefits matrix.
- VA outreach or TRICARE follow-up driven by characterization.

## Processing workflow

1. Validate eligibility. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraphs 1305 (Policy), 1307 (Full Separation Pay), 1308 (Half Separation Pay), and 1309 (Not Eligible), confirm the separation qualifies for pay.
2. Apply full versus half rules. Per paragraphs 1307 and 1308, the basis controls full versus half. Apply the rule.
3. Compute separation pay. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1306 (Rates) and DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35<Citation source="DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35" />, compute as 10 percent times years of service times 12 months of base pay (full rate) or one-half of the full figure (half rate).
4. Validate reserve eligibility. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1310 (Reserve Personnel Additional Eligibility), reservists meet additional criteria per the order rules.
5. Process repayment obligation. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1311 (Repayment), prior receipt of separation pay, severance pay, or readjustment pay triggers offset against future military retired pay or VA disability compensation.
6. Process transition benefits. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1312 (Transition Benefits), brief and document Transition Assistance Program (TAP), TRICARE transitional, Veterans Group Life Insurance (VGLI), and other applicable benefits.
7. Apply characterization-driven benefits matrix. Per Appendix K (Benefits at Separation Based on Characterization of Service), the matrix sets entitlements (E), non-entitlements (NE), and to-be-determined (TBD) by VA across Honorable and other characterizations.
8. Coordinate separation pay payout through disbursing. The disbursing office cuts the payment per DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35.
9. Document Page 11 entries. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, document the repayment obligation acknowledgment.
10. Brief the Marine. The IPAC outbound counselor walks the Marine through Appendix K, the separation pay computation, the repayment obligation, and the transition benefits.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds the active service basis for separation pay computation.
- Disbursing System. Cuts the separation pay payment per DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35.
- MOL Pre-Separation Counseling. Surfaces transition benefits to the Marine.
- VA Pre-Discharge Programs. Per Appendix K cross-reference.
- TRICARE Transitional Assistance Management Program (TAMP). Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1312.

### Forms

- Separation Pay Computation Worksheet. Per DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35.
- Repayment Obligation Acknowledgment. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1311.
- Transition Benefits Brief Record. Per Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1312.
- DD Form 2648 Pre-Separation Counseling Checklist. Per MCO 1700.31 citation hook.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting acknowledgment.

## Common pitfalls

- Separation pay computed without YOS validation. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraph 1306, the computation runs on validated YOS. A YOS error inflates or deflates the payment.
- Full versus half rate misapplied. Per paragraphs 1307 and 1308, the basis controls. The IPAC counselor and disbursing reconcile the applied rate.
- Repayment obligation not acknowledged in writing. Per paragraph 1311, the obligation runs against future retired pay or VA compensation. Cut the Page 11 acknowledgment per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6.
- Transition benefits brief skipped. Per paragraph 1312 and MCO 1700.31, the brief is mandatory pre-separation. Document attendance.
- Appendix K matrix misapplied to the characterization. Per Appendix K, each characterization (Honorable, General, Other Than Honorable, Bad Conduct, Dishonorable, Uncharacterized) drives a specific benefits posture. Apply the correct row.

## Decision points

- Full versus half separation pay. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 1 Section 3 paragraphs 1307 (Full) versus 1308 (Half), the basis controls. The IPAC counselor walks the Marine.
- Eligibility versus exclusion. Per paragraph 1309 (Not Eligible), retirement, voluntary separation (with certain exceptions), and punitive discharge exclude separation pay.
- Repayment offset against retired pay versus VA disability. Per paragraph 1311, the offset runs against both. The order of offset and the schedule follow DODFMR Vol 7A Chapter 35.
- VA benefits eligibility by characterization. Per Appendix K, VA benefits eligibility varies. The Marine with Other Than Honorable receives VA determination on case-by-case basis ("TBD" in the Appendix K matrix).
