## BAH and Permanent Change of Station

When a Marine PCS, BAH coordinates with the move per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" /> and DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 26.

### General PCS Rule

The member's BAH location.

- Old duty station BAH stops at PCS departure date
- New duty station BAH starts at PCS arrival date
- Rate adjusts based on new zip code
- Dependent status remains unless changed during move

### Specific Effective Dates

- BAH stops at old location: Day before report date at new station (typically)
- BAH starts at new location: Day of report date at new station
- Travel days: Member entitled to BAH during travel per JTR

## BAH and PCS with Dependents

When member has dependents and PCS.

### Dependents Move with Member

- BAH transitions per standard PCS rules
- New zip code rate applies upon arrival
- No BAH-Diff
- Standard with-dependents rate

### Dependents Stay in Old Location

Different scenarios apply.

- Member married, dependents stay at old location during housing search: BAH-Diff or BAH at new location based on specific rules
- Member single PCS, dependents move later: Coordinated BAH transitions
- Specific accompanied vs unaccompanied tour rules

### Dependents Travel Separately

If dependents travel to new station after member.

- Member arrives, BAH starts at new location
- Dependents arrive later, no rate change (with-dependents continues)
- Specific JTR rules for dependent travel reimbursement

## BAH for Unaccompanied Tours

Some PCS orders are unaccompanied tours where dependents do not move.

### Common Unaccompanied Tours

- Korea unaccompanied (1-year tour)
- Specific overseas locations with limited family support
- Sea duty in some cases

### BAH During Unaccompanied Tour

The Marine has options.

- Maintain home where dependents reside (BAH at home location, primary BAH)
- Live in barracks at unaccompanied tour location (no BAH at tour location)
- Specific BAH-Diff or other entitlement may apply based on tour type

### Designated Location Move

Member may designate dependents to a location during unaccompanied tour.

- Family moves to designated location
- BAH applies at designated location
- Specific procedures per JTR and MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />

## BAH and Temporary Duty (TDY)

Per JTR, TDY does not generally affect BAH.

### Standard TDY

- BAH at home station continues
- Per diem covers TDY lodging at TDY location
- No change to BAH category

### Long TDY

For TDY exceeding specific durations.

- BAH may continue at home station
- Specific JTR provisions for extended TDY housing
- Coordinate with PAC for unusual situations

## BAH and Deployment

Deployment is treated as TDY for BAH purposes in most cases.

### CONUS Deployments

- BAH at home station continues during deployment
- No change to BAH category
- Deployment-related allowances (HFP/IDP, FSA) layer on top

### OCONUS Deployments

- BAH at home station continues during deployment
- Deployment per diem applies separately
- Combat zone tax exclusion may apply

### Partial Month Issues

Most BAH actions during deployment do not require partial month adjustments.

- BAH paid full monthly amount
- Deployment status does not pro-rate BAH
- Coordinate with PAC for unusual situations

## Performance Steps for BAH PCS Processing

### Step 1 - Receive PCS Orders

When member receives PCS orders.

- Identify report date at new station
- Identify accompanied vs unaccompanied tour
- Identify dependents' planned residence

### Step 2 - Determine BAH Action

Based on PCS specifics.

- Standard PCS: BAH stop at old, start at new with effective dates
- Unaccompanied tour with designated location: Specific BAH actions
- Government quarters at new station: BAH stops on quarters move-in date

### Step 3 - Process Transactions

Apply UD/MIPS transactions.

- BAH stop at old station with effective date
- BAH start at new station with effective date and new rate
- Verify dependent status correct
- Coordinate timing to avoid pay gaps

### Step 4 - Verify EDFR

Confirm processing.

- Pull EDFR within 5 business days
- Verify BAH posts correctly at both stations
- Address any failures or gaps

### Step 5 - Member Coordination

Brief the member.

- Effective dates for old and new BAH
- Total amount during transition month
- Any gaps or overlaps

## Common PCS BAH Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Old station BAH not stopped.** Member PCS but old station BAH continues. Creates overpayment. Stop retroactive to PCS departure.
2. **New station BAH not started.** Member arrives but new station BAH never starts. Member underpaid. Process retroactive start.
3. **Wrong zip code at new station.** Member's new BAH at wrong rate due to zip code error. Update zip code and recalculate.
4. **Government quarters not coordinated.** Member moves into base housing but BAH continues. Stop BAH retroactive to quarters move-in.
5. **Unaccompanied tour BAH miscalculated.** Specific unaccompanied tour rules not applied. Fix per tour type.

## Common TDY BAH Errors

These hit on TDY-related processing.

1. **BAH stopped during TDY.** TDY does not stop BAH. Restore BAH retroactive to incorrect stop date.
2. **TDY treated as PCS for BAH.** TDY does not change BAH location. Maintain home station BAH.
3. **Long TDY housing not coordinated.** Extended TDY may have specific provisions. Verify per JTR.

## BAH Continuity During Major Moves

Members in transition deserve continuous coverage. PAC ensures this through.

- Pre-PCS coordination with member on dates
- Same-day processing of PCS BAH transactions when possible
- Direct contact with member if delays occur
- Monthly LES verification by member

## Member Self-Audit During PCS

Brief members on what to verify during PCS.

- Old station BAH stops at correct date on LES
- New station BAH starts at correct date
- Total monthly BAH for transition month adds up correctly
- New zip code BAH rate matches DTMO calculator
- Report any discrepancies to S-1 immediately

## Coordination Touchpoints

PCS BAH processing touches.

- **PSD or supporting MTF**: For housing eligibility
- **Travel office**: For travel reimbursement
- **Gaining S-1**: For arrival-side processing
- **Member's chain of command**: For PCS coordination

## Related Roles

PCS BAH affects every Marine on orders.

- **Marines** verify BAH transitions during PCS via LES.
- **Leaders** at S-1 process the transactions and coordinate timing.
- **Commanders** ensure unit PCS rosters route to PAC timely.
