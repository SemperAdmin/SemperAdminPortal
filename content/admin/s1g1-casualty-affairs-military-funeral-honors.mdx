## Role and responsibility

The CACO coordinates MFH with the family and the MFH detail. The S-1 officer signs by direction on MFH authorization. The unit provides the MFH detail when assigned. HQMC Casualty Section coordinates regional MFH support per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6.

Source documents flow from the family or PADD request, the Marine's service record validating honorable service, the discharge or DD Form 214 evidence, and the casualty case file. MFH actions land as the authorization, the detail assignment, and the honors execution record. Outputs include the MFH detail dispatch, the honors execution, and the documentation in the casualty case file.

Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 1 (Statutory Requirements), MFH is a statutory entitlement under 10 USC 1491<Citation source="10 USC 1491" /> for eligible veterans. Per paragraph 2 (Eligible Personnel), eligibility runs per the order rules. Per paragraph 3 (Ineligible Personnel), specific categories are excluded. Per paragraph 4 (Verification of Honorable Service), service verification is required before MFH dispatch. Per paragraph 5 (Denial of Funeral Honors Support), denial follows the order procedure when applicable.

## Trigger events

- Family or PADD requests MFH for a deceased Marine or veteran.
- DD Form 214 or service record evidence required for verification.
- MFH detail assignment routed to the unit.
- Mass casualty event triggers multiple MFH coordinations.
- Eligibility question requires verification per Chapter 6 paragraph 4.
- Denial of MFH support routed per Chapter 6 paragraph 5.
- Annual MFH detail training cycle.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the MFH request. Pull the request from family, PADD, or CACO coordination.
2. Verify eligibility. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 2 (Eligible Personnel), validate against the order rules.
3. Confirm honorable service verification. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 4 (Verification of Honorable Service), pull DD Form 214 or service record evidence.
4. Validate against ineligibility criteria. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 3 (Ineligible Personnel), confirm the Marine does not fall under exclusion categories.
5. Process denial when applicable. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 5 (Denial of Funeral Honors Support), if denial applies, route per the procedure.
6. Coordinate MFH detail. The unit assigns the detail. HQMC Casualty Section coordinates regional support where the unit lacks capacity.
7. Brief the detail. The detail leader briefs members on the MFH standard.
8. Conduct the MFH. The detail executes honors per the standard.
9. Document execution. Capture date, location, detail members, and family reception.
10. File the MFH packet. Stage in the casualty case file.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- HQMC Casualty Section. Coordinates regional MFH support.
- DCIPS. Tracks MFH at national level.
- Unit casualty case file. Local record of MFH execution.
- DD Form 214 archive. Source for honorable service verification.

### Forms

- MFH Request Form. From family or PADD.
- DD Form 214 Certificate of Release or Discharge. Source for verification.
- MFH Detail Order. Local order assigning the detail.
- Denial of Funeral Honors Memorandum. Per Chapter 6 paragraph 5 when applicable.

## Common pitfalls

- MFH dispatched without honorable service verification. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 4, verification is required. Pull evidence before dispatch.
- Ineligible person provided MFH. Per Chapter 6 paragraph 3, ineligibility categories are specific. Validate before dispatch.
- Denial of MFH support routed informally. Per Chapter 6 paragraph 5, denial procedure is required. Document the basis.
- Detail dispatched without briefing on standard. The detail arrives without clarity. Brief at detail assembly.
- MFH execution not documented. The audit trail breaks. File the MFH packet in the case file.

## Decision points

- Eligibility determination. Per MCO 3040.4<Citation source="MCO 3040.4" /> Chapter 6 paragraph 2, eligibility runs per statute. The S-1 officer validates.
- Service verification path. The Marine has long service history. Pull DD Form 214 from OMPF or VA records per Chapter 6 paragraph 4.
- Denial basis. The Marine falls under an ineligibility category. The S-1 officer routes the denial memorandum per Chapter 6 paragraph 5.
