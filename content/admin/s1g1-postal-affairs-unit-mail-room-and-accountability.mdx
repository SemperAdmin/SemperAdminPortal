## Role and responsibility

The unit mail clerk runs the unit mail room (UMR) per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" /> under the installation Postal Officer's oversight. The S-1 chief tracks the unit mail room posture and runs inspection coordination. The S-1 officer signs by direction on mail clerk appointments and standing operating procedures. The unit commander approves the unit mail room procedures and signs the mail clerk appointment letter.

Source documents flow from the inbound mail at the installation Postal Officer, the outbound mail from unit sections, the accountability log for registered and certified mail, the unit mail room standing operating procedures, the secure storage records, and the inspection findings. Unit mail room outputs include the processed inbound mail with unit distribution, the routed outbound mail through the installation Postal Officer, the accountability records, the secure storage compliance, the inspection cycle compliance, and the audit trail.

Per MCO 5110.4B, the unit mail room operates under the installation Postal Officer's oversight per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" /> framework. The unit mail clerk holds custodial responsibility for the unit's incoming and outgoing mail.

## Trigger events

- New unit mail room standup.
- New unit mail clerk appointment.
- Daily inbound and outbound mail cycle.
- Registered or certified mail receipt at the unit.
- Annual unit mail room inspection.
- Mail security incident at the unit.

## Processing workflow

1. Appoint unit mail clerk. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the unit commander appoints the unit mail clerk in writing.
2. Establish unit mail room operating procedures. Per MCO 5110.4B, the procedures document mail handling, accountability, security, and distribution.
3. Process inbound mail daily. The unit mail clerk receives inbound mail from the installation Postal Officer, sorts by section, and routes for unit distribution.
4. Process outbound mail through installation Postal Officer. Per MCO 5110.4B, outbound mail routes through the installation Postal Officer per USPS DMM rules.
5. Maintain accountability for registered and certified mail. Per USPS DMM and the registered-and-certified-mail sub-page, the accountability chain captures every transfer.
6. Secure mail storage. Per MCO 5110.4B, the unit mail room maintains secure storage for incoming mail awaiting distribution.
7. Coordinate unit-level mail distribution. The unit mail clerk distributes mail to sections per the unit distribution schedule.
8. Run unit mail room inspection cycle. Per MCO 5110.4B, the inspection covers mail handling, accountability, security, and standing procedures.
9. Document mail room records. Per SECNAV M-5210.2 SSIC and SECNAV M 5210.1 retention, retain mail room records per the SSIC disposition.
10. Brief commander on unit mail room posture. The S-1 officer or unit mail clerk briefs the commander on volume, compliance, and any incidents.

- USPS tracking for registered and certified mail. Per USPS DMM.
- Installation Postal Officer coordination records. Per MCO 5110.4B.
- Records retention file per SECNAV M 5210.1.

### Forms

- Unit Mail Clerk Appointment Letter. Per MCO 5110.4B.
- Unit Mail Room Standing Operating Procedures. Per MCO 5110.4B.
- Accountability Log. Per MCO 5110.4B and USPS DMM.
- Mail Distribution Roster. Per the unit.
- Unit Mail Room Inspection Checklist. Per MCO 5110.4B.

## Common pitfalls

- Unit mail clerk not appointed in writing. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the appointment runs in writing.
- Unit mail room operating procedures undocumented. The procedures support compliance and inspection readiness.
- Inbound mail distribution lagged. The daily cycle drives the cadence.
- Registered or certified mail accountability broken at unit level. Per USPS DMM and MCO 5110.4B, the accountability chain extends through the unit.
- Secure storage breach. Per MCO 5110.4B, the unit mail room secures mail awaiting distribution.
- Inspection cycle skipped. Per MCO 5110.4B, the cycle runs per the published cadence.
- Records retention floor missed. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, the retention schedule controls.

## Decision points

- Unit mail clerk appointment scope. Per MCO 5110.4B, the unit commander designates the scope of the mail clerk's duties.
- Daily mail cycle cadence. The unit decides the inbound and outbound mail cycle cadence per command policy.
- Secure storage standard. Per MCO 5110.4B, the storage standard supports the mail security requirements.
- Inspection cycle preparation. The annual cycle drives preparation.
