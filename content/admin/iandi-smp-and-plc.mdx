## Simultaneous Membership Program (SMP)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 2 Section 3.4, the Simultaneous Membership Program (SMP) allows enlisted Reserve members to participate in college ROTC programs while continuing SMCR drilling.

### Who SMP Serves

- Enlisted SMCR Marines pursuing officer commission
- College students with prior or concurrent enlisted Reserve service
- Marines balancing drilling and college ROTC

### SMP Program Structure

- Member maintains SMCR drilling status
- Member participates in college NROTC unit
- Different drilling schedule might apply
- Pay structure includes both drill pay and ROTC pay

### Performance Steps for I&I Admin

When SMP member is in unit.

#### Step 1 - Verify SMP Status

Verify member's enrollment in SMP.

- College NROTC enrollment confirmed
- HQMC SMP designation in MCTFS
- Continued enlisted SMCR status

#### Step 2 - Coordinate Drilling Schedule

SMP members might drill on adjusted schedule.

- Might not drill all standard weekends
- Coordinated with NROTC training schedule
- Specific accommodation per MCRAMM

#### Step 3 - Process Pay Coordination

SMP members might have specific pay structure.

- Drill pay for SMCR participation
- ROTC subsistence allowance
- Coordinate per program requirements

#### Step 4 - Coordinate Eventual Commission

When member completes ROTC and graduates.

- Commission as Marine officer
- Transition from enlisted to officer status
- New officer assignment

## Platoon Leaders Class (PLC)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 2 Section 3.5, Platoon Leaders Class (PLC) is a college program leading to Marine officer commission. Different from NROTC in structure.

### Who PLC Serves

- College students seeking Marine officer commission
- Members not enrolled in NROTC
- Members typically not yet enlisted Marines

### PLC Program Structure

PLC consists of summer training periods at OCS.

- Junior year summer: 6-week PLC junior course
- Senior year summer: 6-week PLC senior course (or equivalent)
- College graduation: Commission as Marine officer

PLC members are not enlisted during college. They are college students with future commission obligation.

### Performance Steps for PLC Receipt

When PLC graduate arrives at I&I.

#### Step 1 - Coordinate Initial Receipt

Treat as new officer commissioning per ROCP procedures.

- Receive officer's records
- Establish OQR
- Verify all training documentation

#### Step 2 - Welcome and Integration

- Brief on unit operations
- Connect with senior officers
- Establish drill schedule

#### Step 3 - Process Officer Status

- Active duty status during initial schools
- Reserve drilling after initial training completion
- Pay system setup

## SMP and PLC Comparison

These programs both commission Reserve officers but differ.

| Aspect | SMP | PLC |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Member's status during college | Enlisted SMCR drilling | College student (not enlisted) |
| Pay during college | Drill pay plus ROTC subsistence | OCS pay during summer training |
| Drilling | Yes (with accommodations) | No |
| Summer training | Continuous SMCR | PLC summer courses |
| Commission upon graduation | Yes | Yes |
| Reserve obligation | Officer Reserve obligation after commission | Officer Reserve obligation after commission |

Both programs produce Marine Reserve officers ready for SMCR unit assignment.

## NROTC vs SMP vs PLC

A Marine officer commissioning candidate has multiple paths.

### NROTC (Non-SMP)

- College student in NROTC
- Receives NROTC scholarship or non-scholarship status
- Commissioning upon graduation
- Active or Reserve obligation per program type

### NROTC with SMP

- Same as NROTC plus enlisted SMCR drilling
- Provides drill pay during college
- Specific eligibility for SMP

### PLC

- College student seeking commission
- Summer training at OCS
- No college-time enlisted service
- Commission upon graduation

### Direct Commission OCS

- College graduate or experienced civilian
- 10-week OCS
- Commission upon completion

Each path has specific application procedures and benefits.

## Documentation Required

For SMP and PLC processing.

### SMP Members

- Original enlistment contract
- NROTC unit enrollment documentation
- HQMC SMP designation
- Continuing SMCR drill records

### PLC Graduates

- College graduation
- PLC course completion certificates
- Initial officer assignment documentation
- All in OQR via UD/MIPS document storage

## Common SMP Issues

### Drilling vs College Schedule Conflicts

SMP members might have conflicts.

- Specific accommodation procedures
- Drilling schedule flexibility
- Coordinate with NROTC unit

### Member's College Performance

If college performance affects ROTC eligibility.

- Might affect SMP designation
- Might affect future commissioning
- Specific procedures per MCRAMM

### Member's Drilling Performance

If SMCR drilling performance affects standing.

- Might affect SMP eligibility
- Standard SMCR participation rules apply

## Common PLC Issues

### PLC Course Failure

If candidate fails PLC summer course.

- Might result in disenrollment
- Specific procedures for failure
- Possible enlistment contract instead

### Successful Commissioning

When PLC candidate completes program.

- Commission upon college graduation
- Initial officer training
- Reserve unit assignment

## Coordination Touchpoints

SMP and PLC processing touch.

- **NROTC Units**: For SMP college coordination
- **OCS Quantico**: For PLC summer training
- **HQMC Officer Manpower**: For policy and selections
- **The Basic School (TBS)**: For initial officer training

## Related Roles

These programs commission Reserve officers.

- **Marines** (SMP members or PLC candidates) pursue commission while in college.
- **Leaders** counsel members on commissioning options.
- **Commanders** at I&I support SMP drilling and PLC graduate integration.
