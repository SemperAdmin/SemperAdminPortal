## Demobilization Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10, demobilization is the process of transitioning Reserve members from active duty back to drilling status or other component status. The process must address.

- Active duty status termination
- Pay transition
- Healthcare transition (TAMP)
- Records updating
- Mental health and physical screening
- Family reunification support
- Reintegration support

## Demobilization Stages

### Stage 1 - Theater Departure

Member leaves deployment area.

- Specific theater procedures
- Travel to demobilization site
- Initial post-deployment processing

### Stage 2 - Demobilization Site Processing

At demobilization site.

- Post-Deployment Health Assessment (PDHA)
- Administrative processing
- Specific transition activities
- Travel to home station

### Stage 3 - Home Station Processing

Back at I&I or home unit.

- Active duty status end processing
- Pay transition
- Records update
- Continued reintegration

### Stage 4 - Continued Transition (180 Days)

After demobilization.

- TAMP coverage active
- PDHRA at 90-180 days
- Specific reintegration programs
- Monitoring for adjustment issues

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

When member returns from mobilization.

### Step 1 - Confirm Member's Return

Coordinate with member.

- Verify return date
- Confirm specific demobilization processing completed
- Review any documentation provided

### Step 2 - Process Active Duty Status End

Critical UD/MIPS transaction.

- UD/MIPS active duty end transaction
- Effective date of demobilization
- Coordinate with PAC

### Step 3 - Coordinate Pay Transition

Active duty pay ends.

- Active duty BAH and BAS deactivate
- Specific deployment pays end (HFP/IDP, FSA, CZTE)
- Drill pay categories reactivate (if continuing SMCR)

### Step 4 - Activate TAMP Coverage

Per 10 USC 1145<Citation source="10 USC 1145" />.

- 180 days TAMP
- Family covered during TAMP
- Specific TAMP transaction in MCTFS

### Step 5 - Coordinate Healthcare Transition

For long-term healthcare.

- TRICARE Prime active duty (during TAMP)
- TRS transition planning (after TAMP)
- Specific care for service-connected conditions
- VA enrollment if applicable

### Step 6 - Update Records

Per IRAM and MCRAMM.

- Mobilization period documented
- Awards earned during mobilization
- Specific service updates
- All filed in OMPF

### Step 7 - Coordinate Reintegration Programs

Per Yellow Ribbon and other programs.

- Yellow Ribbon Reintegration events
- Specific reintegration support
- Family reunification programs
- Mental health screening if needed

### Step 8 - Process Travel Vouchers

For demobilization travel.

- DD Form 1351-2<Citation source="DD Form 1351-2" /> within 5 days
- Per diem and travel reimbursement
- Specific procedures via DTS

## TAMP Coverage Specifics

Per 10 USC 1145<Citation source="10 USC 1145" />.

### TAMP Eligibility

Member meets these.

- Involuntarily mobilized over 30 days
- Or active duty over 30 days for contingency operation
- Specific other qualifying service

### TAMP Coverage

During 180 days.

- TRICARE Prime or Select coverage continues
- Family members covered
- No premiums during TAMP
- Cost-sharing per elected plan

### TAMP Coordination

I&I admin coordinates TAMP enrollment.

- Specific TAMP transaction in MCTFS
- Member's TRICARE access continues
- Family understanding of coverage

## Post-Deployment Health Assessments

Required per DoDI 6490.03<Citation source="DoDI 6490.03" />.

### Post-Deployment Health Assessment (PDHA)

Completed at demobilization site.

- Physical health screening
- Mental health screening
- Specific deployment-related questions
- Member completes within 30 days of demobilization

### Post-Deployment Health Reassessment (PDHRA)

90-180 days after demobilization.

- Follow-up health screening
- Identify delayed-onset conditions
- Mental health follow-up
- Specific Reserve member emphasis

### Health Findings

For specific health issues.

- Coordinate care
- VA referral if applicable
- Specific treatment as needed

### Active Duty Status Not Ended

## Coordination Touchpoints

Demobilization touches.

- **PAC**: For pay status changes
- **MTF**: For PDHA and continued care
- **TRICARE Regional Office**: For TAMP and continued coverage
- **VA**: For long-term benefit coordination
- **Yellow Ribbon Program**: For reintegration events
- **Family Readiness Officer**: For family reintegration
- **Member's civilian employer**: For USERRA reemployment

## Quality Control on Demobilization

Common errors.

1. **Active duty status change delayed.** Pay continues incorrectly.
2. **TAMP not activated.** Member loses healthcare access.
3. **PDHA not completed.** Required health screening missed.
4. **Reintegration support lacking.** Member transitions without support.

## Related Roles

Demobilization affects every mobilized Reserve member's return.

- **Marines** complete demobilization processing and reintegrate.
- **Leaders** at units welcome returning members.
- **Commanders** at I&I coordinate demobilization processing.
