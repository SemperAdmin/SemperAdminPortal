## Role and responsibility

The medical board at the medical treatment facility (MTF) generates the LIMDU recommendation per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 8 paragraph 8104 (Limited Duty Overview). The MTF commander signs the LIMDU determination per paragraph 8110 (Limited Duty Responsibilities). The unit commander approves the LIMDU duty status and applies the duty restrictions. The S-1 chief tracks the LIMDU period and the re-evaluation gate per paragraph 8105. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the LIMDU transaction routing. The IPAC outbound counselor cuts the duty status transaction in MCTFS through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.

Source documents flow from the medical condition, the LIMDU board package, the MTF commander signature, the unit commander approval, the duty restrictions list, the LIMDU duration recommendation, the Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, and the re-evaluation appointment per paragraph 8110. LIMDU outputs include the approved LIMDU duty status, the documented LIMDU period (initial 6 months with 2-month extension), the Page 11 entry, the re-evaluation gate schedule, the medical extension if recommended per paragraph 8111, and either the fit-for-full-duty return or the DES referral per Chapter 8 Section 2.

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8102 (Purpose), LIMDU exists to allow a Marine to recover from a medical condition while remaining on active duty. Per paragraph 8104 (Limited Duty Overview), LIMDU runs when the medical officer expects the Marine to return to full duty within the LIMDU period. Per paragraph 8105 (Limited Duty Policy), standard LIMDU runs 6 months. The MTF recommends a 2-month medical extension when warranted per paragraph 8111. LIMDU does not normally exceed 8 months total without DES referral.

## Trigger events

- MTF medical board recommends LIMDU after a covered medical condition.
- Light duty period exceeds the threshold triggering LIMDU referral.
- Marine returns from convalescent leave with continued medical limitation.
- LIMDU board package routes through the MTF commander.
- 6-month LIMDU re-evaluation gate opens.
- 8-month LIMDU total duration approaches DES referral threshold.
- MTF commander recommends extension or DES referral at re-evaluation.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the MTF LIMDU board recommendation. The MTF medical board generates the recommendation with the medical scope, the expected recovery time, and the recommended duty restrictions.
2. Validate the LIMDU period. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 8 paragraph 8105 (Limited Duty Policy), standard LIMDU runs 6 months. Per paragraph 8111, the MTF recommends a 2-month medical extension when warranted. LIMDU does not normally exceed 8 months total.
3. Route through the MTF commander. Per paragraph 8104 (Limited Duty Overview), the MTF commander signs the LIMDU determination.
4. Unit commander approves. The unit commander approves the LIMDU duty status and applies the duty restrictions per the medical recommendation.
5. Cut the LIMDU duty status transaction. Per Appendix E (Automated Processing under MCTFS) and MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />, the IPAC cuts the LIMDU transaction in UD-MIPS.
6. Document on Page 11. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, cut a Page 11 documenting the LIMDU period, the medical scope, the duty restrictions, and the re-evaluation gate.
7. Track the LIMDU re-evaluation gate. Per paragraph 8110 (Limited Duty Responsibilities), the unit IPAC and the S-1 chief monitor the 6-month re-evaluation appointment cycle.
8. Coordinate referral to DES. Per Chapter 8 Section 2 paragraphs 8201-8208 and DODI 1332.18, the MTF refers to the Disability Evaluation System when the medical condition warrants PEB review.
9. Process LIMDU extension. Per paragraph 8111 (Medical Extension), the MTF commander recommends extension within the defined limit. The unit commander approves within the policy.
10. Close LIMDU. On fit-for-full-duty return, the unit closes the LIMDU duty status in MCTFS. On DES referral, the LIMDU status transitions per Section 2.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). Holds LIMDU duty status.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts LIMDU transactions per Appendix E.
- Disability Evaluation System (IDES) Workflow. Per Chapter 8 Section 2.
- MTF Records. Hold the LIMDU board package.
- Unit LIMDU Roster. Local register tracking LIMDU Marines.

### Forms

- LIMDU Board Package. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8102 and 8104.
- MTF Commander LIMDU Determination Letter. Per paragraph 8104.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting LIMDU per MCO P1070.12K Chapter 6.
- LIMDU Re-Evaluation Appointment Memorandum. Per paragraph 8110.
- Medical Extension Request. Per paragraph 8111.

## Common pitfalls

- LIMDU period set past 8 months without DES referral. Per Chapter 8 paragraphs 8105 and 8111, LIMDU does not normally exceed 8 months total. Past the threshold, DES referral per Section 2 applies.
- MTF commander signature missing. Per paragraph 8104, the MTF commander signs the LIMDU determination.
- Re-evaluation gate missed. Per paragraph 8110, the unit tracks the 6-month gate.
- LIMDU duty status not cut in MCTFS. Per Appendix E and MCTFSPRIUM, the IPAC cuts the transaction.
- Page 11 omitted on LIMDU action. Per MCO P1070.12K Chapter 6, document every LIMDU action.
- DES referral skipped on PEB-warranting findings. Per Chapter 8 Section 2 paragraphs 8201-8208, PEB-warranting medical conditions refer to DES.
- LIMDU continued past extension without re-evaluation. Per paragraph 8111, the extension carries a defined limit. Past the limit, the case routes to DES.
- Unit commander approval missing. Per paragraph 8110, the unit commander approves the LIMDU duty status.

## Decision points

- LIMDU versus DES referral. Per paragraphs 8104 and Section 2, LIMDU runs when the Marine is expected to return to full duty within the LIMDU period. DES runs when the condition warrants PEB review.
- 6 months versus 8 months. Per paragraph 8105, standard LIMDU is 6 months. The 2-month extension per paragraph 8111 brings the total to 8 months maximum without DES referral.
- Fit-for-full-duty return versus extension. The MTF medical board recommends. The MTF commander approves.
- DES referral timing. Per Section 2 and DODI 1332.18, the referral runs when the medical condition does not support a fit-for-full-duty return within the LIMDU period.
