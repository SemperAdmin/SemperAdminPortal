## Role and responsibility

The promotions clerk runs the automatic PFC and LCpl promotion process per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 2100 (General), paragraph 2101 (Active Component), paragraph 2102 (SMCR/AR), and paragraph 2103 (IRR). The S-1 chief reconciles the monthly promotion roster against MCTFS and surfaces non-recommendation actions. The S-1 officer signs by direction on Page 11 entries. The commander pins the new rank at the ceremony.

Source documents flow from the unit MCTFS grade and date-of-rank roster, the time-in-grade and time-in-service rosters per Chapter 1 paragraph 1202, the non-recommendation Page 11 entries, and the unit promotion register. Promotion actions land in MCTFS as a grade change with effective date and pay impact. Outputs include the executed PFC or LCpl promotion order, the updated pay grade, the Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, and the unit promotion register entry.

Per MCO P1400.32D Chapter 2 paragraph 2100, PFC and LCpl promotions follow automatic TIG and TIS criteria across the three components. Per paragraph 2101, the active component runs the monthly automatic cycle. Per paragraph 2102, the SMCR/AR runs the parallel automatic cycle adjusted for reserve service. Per paragraph 2103, the IRR runs the IRR-specific automatic cycle.

## Trigger events

- Marine reaches the TIG/TIS gate for Pvt to PFC or PFC to LCpl per Chapter 1 paragraph 1202.
- Monthly automatic promotion cycle runs.
- Marine transfers between active, SMCR/AR, or IRR components.
- Non-recommendation Page 11 routed by the chain of command.
- Marine returns from extended limited duty status restoring promotion eligibility.
- Adverse FITREP or conduct mark triggers restriction screen per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204.

## Processing workflow

1. Pull the unit promotion zone roster. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1202, surface Marines at or near the TIG/TIS gate.
2. Validate TIG and TIS. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1202, confirm the Marine meets the Pvt-to-PFC or PFC-to-LCpl criteria.
3. Identify the component. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2101 (active), paragraph 2102 (SMCR/AR), or paragraph 2103 (IRR), apply the component-specific rule.
4. Screen restrictions. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, screen for discipline, medical, performance, or administrative holds.
5. Confirm no non-recommendation. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, a chain-of-command non-recommendation Page 11 suspends the automatic promotion. Verify the OMPF and the unit register.
6. Cut MCTFS promotion transaction. Submit through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> promotion transaction format.
7. Coordinate the ceremony. Schedule the promotion ceremony per command tradition.
8. Process Page 11. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6 and MCO P1400.32D Chapter 1 paragraph 1205, document the promotion.
9. Update pay grade and entitlements. New pay grade triggers base pay change.
10. Brief the Marine. The S-1 chief briefs the Marine on the new responsibilities and pay.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds grade, date-of-rank, and component picture.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts PFC and LCpl promotion transactions.
- MOL. Surfaces promotion eligibility to the Marine.
- Unit promotion roster. Local roster of zone Marines.

### Forms

- Automatic Promotion Roster. Monthly MMEA publication for active, SMCR/AR, and IRR.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting the promotion or non-recommendation.
- Non-Recommendation Page 11. Chain-of-command suspension of automatic promotion per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204.

## Common pitfalls

- Non-recommendation Page 11 not cut at the decision point. The Marine promotes automatically despite chain-of-command intent. Cut the Page 11 at decision per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6.
- Component rules conflated. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 2 paragraphs 2101-2103, the active, SMCR/AR, and IRR rules differ. Apply the correct component rule.
- Restriction screen skipped. Per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, the screen runs at every cycle. A missed screen lets a restricted Marine promote.
- Pay grade transition lag. New pay does not post on next cut. Cut the promotion transaction at the effective date per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.
- Ceremony skipped. The Marine never receives the rank in front of the unit. Coordinate the ceremony at promotion.

## Decision points

- Automatic promotion versus non-recommendation. Per MCO P1400.32D<Citation source="MCO P1400.32D" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1204, the chain of command non-recommends via Page 11. The promotion suspends until the chain lifts the non-recommendation.
- Component transition timing. The Marine transfers between active, SMCR/AR, or IRR mid-cycle. Per Chapter 2 paragraphs 2101-2103, the receiving component rules apply on the effective date of transfer.
- Restriction clearance timing. The restriction clears mid-cycle. The Marine receives the promotion on the next cycle per Chapter 1 paragraph 1204.
