## Role and responsibility

The Installation Postal Officer runs the Military Post Office (MPO) at land installations per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" /> and MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />. The Fleet Post Office (FPO) Postal Officer runs the FPO at sea-based units. The Military Postal Service Agency (MPSA) oversees DoD-wide postal operations. USPS provides the mail transportation infrastructure under 39 U.S.C. The Defense Transportation System supports military mail movement per DoD 4500.9-R.

Source documents flow from the USPS mail manifests, the DoD mail accountability records, the MPSA operational guidance, the unit and installation mail volume data, the inbound and outbound mail logs, the postal incident reports, and the MPSA inspection findings. Outputs include the daily MPO or FPO operations, the documented mail accountability, the coordinated mail transportation, the MPSA reporting submission, the diplomatic post office handling for OCONUS locations, the operational records, and the inspection cycle compliance.

Per DoDI 4525.09, MPO operations support military installations with USPS-equivalent mail service. Per MCO 5110.4B, the Marine Corps Postal Officer operates the installation MPO with installation commander oversight. FPO operations support sea-based units with mail routing through fleet logistics.

## Trigger events

- New MPO or FPO standup at an installation or sea-based unit.
- Daily mail processing cycle.
- MPSA reporting cycle.
- Mail transportation coordination with USPS.
- Diplomatic post office (DPO) coordination for OCONUS locations.
- Mail security incident.
- MPSA inspection cycle.
- USPS rate or service change.

## Processing workflow

1. Operate the MPO at land installations. Per DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" />, the MPO handles incoming and outgoing mail at the installation under USPS-equivalent service.
2. Operate the FPO at sea-based units. Per DoDI 4525.09, the FPO handles mail for sea-based units with routing through fleet logistics.
3. Coordinate with USPS for mail transportation. Per 39 U.S.C. and the USPS DMM, the MPO interfaces with USPS for mail movement.
4. Run the MPSA reporting cycle. Per DoDI 4525.09, the MPO submits operational reports to MPSA per the published cycle.
5. Manage mail volume against capacity. The Postal Officer tracks volume metrics against MPO or FPO capacity.
6. Process inbound and outbound mail. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the MPO processes mail per the daily cycle including sorting, routing, and unit-level distribution.
7. Coordinate Diplomatic Post Office (DPO) handling. For OCONUS locations using DPO routing, the Postal Officer coordinates per DoDI 4525.09.
8. Maintain MPO/FPO operational records. Per SECNAV M 5210.1 records retention, retain operational records per the SSIC disposition.
9. Run MPSA inspection cycle. Per DoDI 4525.09, MPSA conducts periodic inspections of MPO operations.
10. Report mail anomalies and security incidents. Per DoDI 4525.09 and MCO 5110.4B, mail anomalies (lost mail, theft, damage) report through MPSA and command channels.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MPSA operational systems. Per DoDI 4525.09.
- USPS systems (Mail Tracking, DMM, IMM). Per 39 U.S.C.
- MPO or FPO operational records. Per MCO 5110.4B.
- Defense Transportation System. Per DoD 4500.9-R.

### Forms

- MPSA Daily Operational Report. Per DoDI 4525.09.
- Mail Manifest. Per USPS DMM.
- Mail Anomaly Report. Per DoDI 4525.09 and MCO 5110.4B.
- MPSA Inspection Report. Per DoDI 4525.09.

## Common pitfalls

- MPO operations without trained Postal Officer. Per MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" />, the Postal Officer trains before duty assumption.
- USPS coordination lagged. Per 39 U.S.C. and DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" />, the coordination supports mail movement.
- MPSA reporting cycle missed. Per DoDI 4525.09, the cycle runs per the published cadence.
- DPO routing skipped for eligible OCONUS locations. Per DoDI 4525.09, DPO handles OCONUS routing per location-specific rules.
- Mail security incident not reported. Per DoDI 4525.09 and MCO 5110.4B, mail incidents route through MPSA and command channels.
- Mail volume capacity exceeded without action. The Postal Officer tracks volume and adjusts operations.

## Decision points

- MPO versus FPO designation. Land installations operate MPO. Sea-based units operate FPO.
- DPO routing applicability. Per DoDI 4525.09, DPO handles specific OCONUS routing including embassy and diplomatic mission destinations.
- Mail transportation channel selection. USPS, contract carrier, or Defense Transportation System options drive the routing.
- Inspection cycle preparation. Per DoDI 4525.09, the MPSA inspection cycle drives preparation.

## Authority

This sub-page sits under MCO 5110.4B<Citation source="MCO 5110.4B" /> Marine Corps Postal Affairs and Official Mail Program. DoDI 4525.09<Citation source="DODI 4525.09" /> sets parent DoD Military Postal Service framework. USPS DMM and IMM cover mail processing rules. 39 U.S.C. sets postal service statutory authority. DoD 4500.9-R covers Defense Transportation Regulation.
