## Role and responsibility

The commander pins the award at the presentation per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 5. The S-1 chief processes initial issue of medals per paragraph 6. The awards clerk runs certificate and presentation folder preparation per paragraph 9. The Marine is responsible for proper wear per the display rules and MCO P1020.34H Marine Corps Uniform Regulations.

Source documents flow from the approved award, the citation, the presentation folder template, and the unit ceremony schedule. Presentation actions land as the ceremony record, the certificate issuance, the initial medal issue, and the MCTFS effective-date update. Outputs include the ceremony documentation, the certificate handed to the Marine, the presentation folder, and the MCTFS award entry effective the presentation date.

Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 5 (Presentation of Decorations), presentation follows the order rules. Per paragraph 6 (Initial Issue), the unit issues the initial medal at presentation. Per paragraph 7 (Display Medals and Awards), display rules govern wear. Per paragraph 9 (Certificates and Presentation Folders), certificates and folders accompany the award.

## Trigger events

- Award approved and ready for presentation.
- Unit ceremony scheduled requiring award presentation.
- Marine retires or separates requiring final award presentation.
- Initial issue of medal required for newly awarded Marine.
- Foreign award presentation following CMC (MMMA) authorization.
- Posthumous award presentation to PNOK.
- Display compliance review.

## Processing workflow

1. Schedule the presentation. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 5, schedule the presentation per the order rules and unit ceremony cycle.
2. Process initial issue of medals. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 6, issue the medal at presentation.
3. Apply display rules. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 7 and MCO P1020.34H<Citation source="MCO P1020.34H" />, the Marine wears the award per uniform regulations.
4. Issue certificates. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 9, issue the certificate and presentation folder.
5. Coordinate ceremony format. The S-1 officer coordinates with the unit chain for ceremony content.
6. Validate uniform display. Per MCO P1020.34H<Citation source="MCO P1020.34H" />, confirm the Marine's uniform wear matches the new award.
7. Document presentation date. Update the unit awards register.
8. Update MCTFS entry. Cut the MCTFS award transaction effective the presentation date per MCTFSPRIUM.
9. Provide certificate and folder. Hand to the Marine at the ceremony.
10. File presentation record. Stage in the OMPF Field Folder awards tab.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds award entries with presentation date.
- OMPF. Final record of awards.
- Unit awards register. Tracks presentations.
- Marine Corps medal supply. Source for initial issue.

### Forms

- Citation Certificate. Per Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 9.
- Presentation Folder. Per paragraph 9.
- Initial Medal Issue Receipt. Per paragraph 6.
- Ceremony Record. Local format documenting presentation event.

## Common pitfalls

- Initial issue skipped at presentation. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 6, initial issue is part of the presentation.
- Certificate not provided. Per paragraph 9, the certificate accompanies the award. Provide at presentation.
- Display rules misapplied. Per paragraph 7 and MCO P1020.34H<Citation source="MCO P1020.34H" />, uniform wear follows the order. Brief Marines on display.
- MCTFS entry effective date incorrect. The pay and personnel record lags the presentation. Cut the transaction effective the presentation date.
- Posthumous presentation handled without PNOK coordination. The PNOK receives the award. Coordinate with the CACO per MCO 3040.4 Chapter 4.

## Decision points

- Presentation timing. The Marine separates before scheduled ceremony. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 5, accelerate presentation or arrange after-separation presentation per the order rules.
- Foreign award display. Per MCO 1650.19J<Citation source="MCO 1650.19J" /> Enclosure 1 Section 1 paragraph 4.a and SECNAV M-1650.1<Citation source="SECNAV M-1650.1" /> Chapter 7, foreign award display follows CMC (MMMA) direction in the acceptance response.
- V, C, R device wear. Per ALNAV 055/17<Citation source="ALNAV 055/17" /> and MARADMIN 665/17<Citation source="MARADMIN 665/17" />, the Valor (V), Combat Conditions (C), and Remote Impact (R) devices apply per the qualifying action. ALNAV 055/17 sets the DoN criteria. MARADMIN 665/17 provides Marine Corps implementation guidance including iAPS support for the new devices.
- Posthumous award presentation. The Marine died in service. The CACO coordinates with the PNOK on presentation per MCO 3040.4 Chapter 4.
