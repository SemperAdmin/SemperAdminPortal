## Role and responsibility

The medical officer issues the PFT and CFT authorization scope per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 8 paragraph 8106 (Authorization for Partial or No PFT). The unit commander applies the PFT and operational restrictions per the medical scope. The S-1 chief tracks the restriction against the re-evaluation gate. The Monitor at HQMC M&RA coordinates the PCS restriction per paragraph 8107 (Deployability and PCS). The IPAC outbound counselor cuts the duty status and the related coding in MCTFS through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.

Source documents flow from the LIMDU board package, the medical officer chit per paragraph 8106, the unit commander approval, the BCP and PFT/CFT records, the Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, the Monitor PCS coordination per paragraph 8107, and the re-evaluation appointment. Operational restriction outputs include the documented PFT or CFT authorization (partial or none), the BCP status, the deployability hold, the PCS restriction, the Page 11 entry, and the audit trail.

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8106 (Authorization for Partial or No PFT), the MTF medical officer authorizes partial or no PFT for Marines on LIMDU when the medical condition supports the authorization. Per paragraph 8107 (Deployability and PCS), Marines on LIMDU are not normally deployable and PCS holds apply during the LIMDU period.

## Trigger events

- Medical officer issues PFT authorization (partial, no, or modified events).
- MTF supports fitness test alternatives per medical scope.
- Marine on LIMDU requires PFT or CFT cycle.
- Deployment notification arrives on a Marine on LIMDU.
- PCS orders cut on a Marine on LIMDU.
- BCP cycle opens with a Marine on LIMDU.
- Annual review of PFT authorization scope.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive medical officer PFT authorization. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 8 paragraph 8106, the medical officer documents the partial or no PFT authorization on the chit.
2. Apply PFT authorization. The unit applies the authorization to the next PFT or CFT cycle. The S-1 records the partial events or the no PFT status.
3. Document on Page 11. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, cut a Page 11 documenting the PFT authorization scope and duration.
4. Code MCTFS BCP status. If the LIMDU includes BCP implications, the IPAC codes the BCP status per MCTFSPRIUM and MCO 6100.13A.
5. Apply deployability hold. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8107 (Deployability and PCS), the unit applies the deployability hold during the LIMDU period.
6. Apply PCS restriction. Per paragraph 8107, the PCS restriction holds the Marine at the current duty station during the LIMDU period unless the Monitor coordinates a billet supporting the medical scope.
7. Coordinate with the Monitor. The S-1 coordinates with the Monitor at HQMC M&RA for any PCS movement supporting the medical scope.
8. Notify the PFT/CFT systems. The fitness test systems reflect the partial or no PFT authorization.
9. Track operational restriction against re-evaluation gates. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8110, the unit tracks the re-evaluation appointment.
10. Close restriction on fit-for-full-duty return. On fit-for-full-duty return, the unit closes the partial or no PFT authorization and lifts the deployability and PCS holds.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). Holds duty status, BCP status, and PFT/CFT records.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts duty status transactions per Appendix E.
- PFT/CFT Tracking System. Reflects the partial or no PFT authorization.
- Monitor Coordination Records. Hold PCS coordination per paragraph 8107.
- Unit LIMDU Roster. Local register tracking LIMDU Marines and their restrictions.

### Forms

- Medical Officer PFT Authorization Chit. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8106.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting the operational restrictions per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006.
- Deployability Hold Notice. Local format per paragraph 8107.
- PCS Restriction Notice. Local format per paragraph 8107.
- Monitor Coordination Memorandum. Local format for PCS coordination.

## Common pitfalls

- PFT authorization applied without medical officer chit. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8106, the medical officer documents the authorization.
- Marine on LIMDU deployed. Per Chapter 8 paragraph 8107, Marines on LIMDU are not normally deployable. Deployment breaks the LIMDU framework.
- PCS executed on Marine on LIMDU without Monitor coordination. Per paragraph 8107, the Monitor coordinates the PCS movement to a billet supporting the medical scope.
- Page 11 omitted on PFT authorization. Per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006, every operational restriction records on Page 11.
- BCP status not coded with LIMDU scope. The MCTFS coding reflects the operational restrictions for accountability.
- Restriction continued past fit-for-full-duty return. The unit lifts restrictions at the return.

## Decision points

- Partial versus no PFT. The medical officer's chit drives the authorization scope. Partial PFT identifies the events the Marine performs. No PFT covers cases where any PFT participation is medically unsupportable.
- Deployability hold scope. Per paragraph 8107, the hold applies during the LIMDU period. The unit weighs short-term deployability against the medical scope.
- PCS restriction exception. The Monitor coordinates a PCS to a supporting billet on a case-by-case basis. Standard PCS movement holds during LIMDU.
- BCP coding. The unit IPAC weighs the BCP status with the medical scope.
