## Role and responsibility

The S-1 officer owns officer retirement processing per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 2001 (General). The S-1 chief tracks officers reaching voluntary retirement eligibility and mandatory retirement gates per Chapter 2 paragraph 2003 (Voluntary Retirement) and paragraph 2005 (Mandatory Retirement). The IPAC outbound counselor coordinates the application package per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004 (Administrative Procedures). The commander pins the retirement at the ceremony per Chapter 2 paragraph 2008 (Retirement Ceremony). MMSR-2 issues the retirement order.

Source documents flow from the MCTFS active federal commissioned service (AFCS) record, the officer's commission date, the retirement preapplication checklist per Appendix J, the application memorandum per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004, the SBP election per MCO 1900.16 Chapter 1 paragraph 1406, the DD Form 2656 per Appendix H, the health assessment per Chapter 2 paragraph 2007, and the MMSR-2 retirement order. Retirement actions land in MCTFS as a status change per Appendix E. Outputs include the retirement order, the certificate of retirement, the DD Form 214 per Chapter 1 Section 2, the ceremony record, and the closed OQR per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 3.

Per MCO 1900.16 Chapter 2 paragraph 2003 (Voluntary Retirement), officers with 20 or more years of active federal service apply for voluntary retirement. Per paragraph 2005 (Mandatory Retirement), officers retire under statutory authority at specific age and grade combinations or upon failure of selection per 10 USC Chapter 65. Per paragraph 2009 (Advancement in Grade on the Retired List), officers are permitted to advance to the highest grade satisfactorily held per the statutory criteria.

## Trigger events

- Officer submits voluntary retirement application per Chapter 2 paragraph 2003.
- Officer reaches statutory mandatory retirement gate per paragraph 2005.
- Officer second failure of selection for promotion triggering Chapter 5 separation versus Chapter 2 retirement decision.
- Modification of the retirement date requested.
- Retirement ceremony coordination required per paragraph 2008.
- Officer medical condition triggers retirement decision versus DES referral.
- Advancement-in-grade application per paragraph 2009.

## Processing workflow

1. Validate voluntary retirement eligibility. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 2003 (Voluntary Retirement), confirm the officer meets the 20-year AFS gate.
2. Apply mandatory retirement rules. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2005 (Mandatory Retirement), validate against statutory age and grade combinations.
3. Build the application package. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004 (Administrative Procedures) and Appendix J (Retirement Preapplication Checklist), build the package with the application memorandum, the DD Form 2656, the SBP election, and the supporting documentation.
4. Run administrative procedures. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004, route the application through the chain to MMSR-2.
5. Coordinate health assessment. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2007 (Health Assessments), schedule the separation health assessment.
6. Process leave. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2006 (Leave), the officer sells or carries leave per the order rules.
7. Coordinate the retirement ceremony. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2008, schedule per command tradition. Use Figure 2-1 (Format for Orders) as the template.
8. Process advancement in grade on the Retired List. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2009, advancement applies to officers who satisfactorily held a higher grade per the statutory criteria.
9. Cut MCTFS officer retirement transaction. Per Appendix E and MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.
10. Prepare the DD Form 214. Per Chapter 1 Section 2 paragraphs 1200-1205, reflect the transfer to the Retired List.

## Common pitfalls

- SBP election skipped or made without spouse concurrence. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 1 paragraph 1406 (Survivor Benefit Plan), the spouse concurs in any election less than full coverage. A missed election triggers default full coverage.
- Appendix J preapplication checklist incomplete. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004 and Appendix J, the checklist drives the package.
- Advancement-in-grade application skipped. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2009, advancement applies per the statutory criteria. Run the application for officers who held a higher grade satisfactorily.
- Health assessment skipped. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2007, the health assessment is mandatory prior to retirement.
- DD Form 214 Block 12 (record of service) understated. Per Chapter 1 Section 2 paragraph 1202 and Appendix B, the service record reflects the full commissioned service.

## Decision points

- Voluntary versus mandatory retirement. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> Chapter 2 paragraphs 2003 versus 2005, the voluntary path runs through application. The mandatory path runs from statutory gate.
- Chapter 4 show cause versus Chapter 2 retirement. The officer faces a Chapter 4 show cause action. Per Chapter 4 paragraph 4104 (Retirement or Resignation), the officer is permitted to submit retirement in lieu of show cause per the order rules.
- Modification of the retirement date. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 2004, modification routes through MMSR-2 per the order rules.
