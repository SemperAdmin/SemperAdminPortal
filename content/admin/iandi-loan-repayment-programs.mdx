## What LRP Is

The Reserve Loan Repayment Program (LRP) pays down qualifying student loans for reservists in eligible MOSs as part of enlistment, reenlistment, or affiliation incentives. Per MCRAMM Chapter 2 and Chapter 12, LRP is one component of the Selected Reserve Incentive Program (SRIP).

LRP differs from GI Bill education benefits.

- GI Bill pays for future education
- LRP pays down existing student loans

A reservist with significant student debt benefits more from LRP than a reservist without student loans.

## Eligibility

LRP eligibility depends on current SRIP guidance, which updates via MARADMIN. Common eligibility patterns.

### Initial Enlistment LRP

A new SMCR member may receive LRP for.

- Enlistment in a critical MOS designated for LRP
- Initial enlistment contract (typically 6-year SelRes obligation)
- Existing qualifying student loans
- Specific service categories per current SRIP MARADMIN

### Reenlistment LRP

An existing SMCR member may receive LRP for.

- Reenlistment in a critical MOS
- Continued qualifying student loans
- Specific time-in-service or rank requirements
- Specific service categories per current SRIP MARADMIN

### Officer Programs

Officers may receive LRP through.

- Reserve officer commissioning programs
- Specific officer affiliation programs
- Critical specialty designations

## Qualifying Loans

Per 10 USC 16301<Citation source="10 USC 16301" />, LRP repays specific federal student loans.

### Qualifying Loan Types

- Federal Family Education Loans (FFEL)
- Federal Direct Loans (Subsidized, Unsubsidized, PLUS, Consolidation)
- Perkins Loans
- Health Education Assistance Loans (HEAL) for eligible programs

### Non-Qualifying Loans

- Private student loans
- State loans (some exceptions)
- Loans incurred by parents for student
- Loans for non-education purposes

Members with qualifying loans get LRP value. Members with private loans cannot.

## Benefit Amounts

LRP amounts depend on current SRIP MARADMIN.

- Annual repayment amounts (paid annually after qualifying year of service)
- Maximum total repayment over the obligation period
- Specific MOS-based bonuses for critical specialties
- Differences between officer and enlisted programs

Reference current SRIP MARADMIN for actual amounts.

## How LRP Pays

LRP is paid annually upon completion of each qualifying year.

- Year 1 service: First annual LRP payment after Year 1 anniversary
- Year 2 service: Second annual LRP payment
- Continues per the agreement

Each payment goes directly to the loan holder, not the member.

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Verify Current SRIP Guidance

Before discussing LRP with a member.

- Pull current SRIP MARADMIN
- Verify LRP is currently authorized
- Verify member's MOS qualifies
- Verify member's status (enlisting, reenlisting, etc.)

### Step 2 - Verify Member's Loan Eligibility

The member should have.

- Documentation of qualifying federal student loans
- Loan balances current
- Loan account information for direct payment

### Step 3 - Coordinate Contract Documentation

LRP is contractual.

- LRP terms included in enlistment or reenlistment contract
- Member acknowledges agreement
- Service obligation documented
- Payment schedule documented

### Step 4 - Process Annual Payment

Each year of qualifying service.

- Verify member completed satisfactory year
- Submit LRP payment request
- Payment processed to loan holder
- Documentation filed in member record

### Step 5 - Track Total Repayment

Over the obligation period.

- Track cumulative LRP payments
- Verify approaching cap
- Final payment processing at end of obligation

## Annual Year Verification

Each LRP payment requires the member completed a satisfactory qualifying year.

- 50+ retirement points in the anniversary year
- Continued SelRes status
- Satisfactory participation
- Continued MOS qualification

A failed year delays or terminates LRP payments.

## When LRP Stops

LRP terminates when.

- Member completes the contracted obligation
- Member separates from SelRes (loses eligibility)
- Member transfers to IRR (loses eligibility)
- Unsatisfactory participation triggers termination
- Maximum LRP cap reached

## Common Member Questions

### "How much can I get for LRP?"

Depends on current SRIP MARADMIN, MOS, and service obligation. Counsel member to reference current MARADMIN.

### "Can I use LRP and GI Bill together?"

Yes. LRP pays down loans (existing debt). GI Bill funds future education. Different programs, different timing.

### "What if my loan is paid off before LRP completes?"

LRP only pays loan balance. If loan is paid off, no remaining LRP payments.

### "What if I miss a year of qualifying service?"

The missed year does not earn LRP payment. Future qualifying years may continue but cumulative cap still applies.

### "Are my private loans covered?"

Generally no. LRP is for federal student loans. Verify specific loan eligibility through DFAS or MCRSC.

### "What if my MOS changes?"

MOS change may affect future eligibility but not past payments. Coordinate with HQMC for specifics.

## Common I&I Errors on LRP

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Outdated SRIP guidance cited.** SRIP updates via MARADMIN regularly. Always reference current.
2. **Telling member private loans qualify.** Most private loans do not. Verify specific loan eligibility.
3. **Wrong amount quoted.** Amounts vary by MOS, rank, contract type. Reference current MARADMIN.
4. **Annual qualification not tracked.** Member misses qualifying year and assumes payment continues. Track each anniversary year.
5. **LRP confused with GI Bill.** Different programs. LRP pays existing loans. GI Bill funds education. Counsel member on distinction.

## Coordination Touchpoints

LRP processing touches.

- **HQMC SRIP**: Program eligibility and authorization
- **DFAS**: Payment processing to loan holders
- **Member's loan servicer**: For payment coordination
- **MCRSC**: For component status verification
- **Member's school certifying official**: For loan documentation

## Related Roles

LRP affects reservists with qualifying student loans.

- **Marines** (reservists with student loans) coordinate with I&I for LRP eligibility verification.
- **Leaders** counsel members on LRP as part of incentive program briefings.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member benefit planning during enlistment and reenlistment.
