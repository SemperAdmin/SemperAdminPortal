## What Tuition Assistance Is

Per MCO 1560.25<Citation source="MCO 1560.25" /> and DoDI 1322.25<Citation source="DoDI 1322.25" />, the Marine Corps Tuition Assistance (TA) Program pays up to $4500 per fiscal year for active duty Marines pursuing approved college courses. Reservists qualify when serving on qualifying active duty orders.

This is a current-service benefit. Members use TA while serving rather than earning it for later use.

## Eligibility for Reservists

A reservist qualifies for TA when meeting all of these.

- Currently serving on active duty orders for over 30 consecutive days
- Member is in good standing
- Course is at an approved institution
- Course is part of an approved degree or credential program
- Member has not exceeded annual TA cap
- Member meets first-term active duty qualifications (newly enlisted Marines have specific waiting periods)

Reservists drilling without active duty orders generally do not qualify for TA. They might use MGIB-SR or Post-9/11 GI Bill instead.

## Benefit Limits

### Annual Cap

- Up to $4500 per fiscal year per Marine
- Capped at $250 per semester credit hour
- Capped at $166 per quarter credit hour

### Lifetime Cap

- 130 semester credit hours
- 195 quarter credit hours

These limits keep TA focused on supporting active duty career development rather than complete degree funding.

## Approved Programs

TA pays for courses at.

- Accredited colleges and universities
- Approved vocational and technical schools
- Online programs from accredited institutions
- Foreign schools meeting specific accreditation

The Defense Activity for Non-Traditional Education Support (DANTES) maintains the list of approved institutions.

### Program Requirements

- Course must contribute to an approved degree or credential program
- Member must declare a specific degree or credential
- Course must apply to program

Random elective courses outside the declared program do not qualify.

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Verify Eligibility

When a reservist on active duty asks about TA.

- Verify current active duty orders (over 30 days continuous)
- Verify good standing
- Verify member has not exceeded annual or lifetime cap
- Verify school and program are approved

### Step 2 - Counsel on Process

Brief the member on TA process.

- Application via MarineNet TA system
- Approval required before course start
- Member pays course costs upfront in some cases
- Reimbursement process if applicable
- Grade requirements (must complete with passing grade)

### Step 3 - Direct to MarineNet TA

The member applies through MarineNet.

- Login to MarineNet
- Select Tuition Assistance module
- Submit application for the specific course
- Provide course details (school, course number, credit hours, dates)
- Wait for approval before course start

### Step 4 - Monitor Course Completion

After course completion.

- Member submits grade to MarineNet TA
- Failing grade or course drop might require reimbursement
- Successful completion documented for lifetime cap tracking

## Grade and Reimbursement Requirements

TA requires successful completion. Failure consequences.

- C grade or better for undergraduate: TA covers
- B grade or better for graduate: TA covers
- Course drop after add deadline: Member might owe reimbursement
- Failing grade: Member owes reimbursement
- Withdrawal mid-course: Member owes reimbursement

Tracking and following through on reimbursement is part of admin responsibility.

## TA vs GI Bill

Reservists with multiple education benefits should know the difference.

| Feature | TA | MGIB-SR | Post-9/11 GI Bill |
|---------|----|----|----|
| Used during | Active duty service | While drilling SelRes | After active duty (or while drilling) |
| Pays for | Tuition (with cap) | Monthly to member | Tuition + housing + books |
| Cap | $4500/year, 130 SH lifetime | 36 months entitlement | 36 months entitlement |
| Required service | Current active duty 30+ days | 6-year SelRes obligation | 90+ days qualifying active duty |
| Reimbursement on failure | Yes | No | No |

Members on extended active duty often use TA for courses while serving and reserve GI Bill for after.

## Common Member Questions

### "I use TA and Post-9/11 together?"

Generally yes, if both apply. TA pays first, GI Bill might cover gap. Member should consult VA and MarineNet TA office.

### "What if I drop a class?"

Drop after add deadline typically requires reimbursement. Drop within add window does not.

### "What grade do I need?"

C for undergraduate, B for graduate. Lower grade requires reimbursement.

### "I take online classes?"

Yes. Online courses at approved institutions qualify if part of approved program.

### "What about books?"

TA does not pay for books. GI Bill includes book stipend.

### "What if my deployment interrupts the class?"

Coordinate with school and MarineNet TA. Withdrawal due to military obligation might not require reimbursement.

## Common I&I Errors on TA

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Telling member without active duty orders they qualify.** TA requires 30+ days continuous active duty. Drilling reservists do not qualify.
2. **Wrong cap amount.** Caps might change. Reference current MCO 1560.25<Citation source="MCO 1560.25" /> and program updates.
3. **Not flagging the lifetime cap.** Member with 130+ semester hours used does not use more TA.
4. **Failing to advise on grade requirements.** Member fails course and is surprised by reimbursement requirement.
5. **Recommending TA when GI Bill is better choice.** TA caps tuition. GI Bill plus housing allowance might be better total benefit. Counsel member on options.

## Coordination Touchpoints

TA processing touches.

- **MarineNet TA Office**: Application processing
- **DANTES**: Approved institution verification
- **Member's school**: Enrollment verification and grade reporting
- **DFAS**: Reimbursement processing if applicable
- **MCRSC**: For reservist active duty status verification

## Related Roles

TA affects reservists on active duty orders.

- **Marines** (reservists on active duty) apply for TA via MarineNet for courses during active duty.
- **Leaders** counsel members on TA as part of professional development.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member education planning during active duty periods.
