## Why Family Readiness Matters

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7 and MCO 1754.6<Citation source="MCO 1754.6" />, family readiness affects member retention, deployment success, and Marine Corps mission readiness.

Reserve families face unique challenges.

- Geographic separation from active duty installation
- Civilian community without nearby military support
- Member balancing civilian career and Reserve service
- Mobilization or deployment change
- Limited military culture exposure

Strong family readiness supports member retention and welfare.

## Quality Control on Family Readiness

Common errors.

1. **New family not briefed.** New SELRES family without orientation.
2. **Family contact information not current.** Does not reach family in emergency.
3. **Family Care Plan missing.** Member with FCP requirement without one.
4. **Family programs underutilized.** Resources available but not promoted.

## Related Roles

Family readiness affects every SELRES member's family.

- **Marines** keep family informed and engaged with unit family programs.
- **Leaders** support member family readiness.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit family programs.
