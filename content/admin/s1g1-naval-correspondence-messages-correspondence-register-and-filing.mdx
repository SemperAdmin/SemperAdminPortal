## Role and responsibility

The S-1 clerk logs every inbound correspondence on the unit register and routes to the action officer with suspense. The S-1 chief reviews the register weekly and the records manager runs the records retention cycle. The S-1 officer signs by direction on register-driven correspondence actions. The records manager applies the retention schedule per SECNAV M 5210.1 and the SSIC labeling per SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" /> Part I Chapter 2 paragraph 2-2. The OMPF clerk routes OMPF Field Folder copies for individual Marine actions per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6.

Source documents flow from every inbound and outbound correspondence (naval letter, endorsement, memorandum, business letter, multiple address letter, email, naval message), the SSIC matching the subject from SECNAV M-5210.2, the action officer assignment, the suspense date, the OMPF impact decision, and the retention schedule from SECNAV M 5210.1. Correspondence register and filing outputs include the maintained unit register with daily entries, the SSIC-labeled file folders with open/close dates and disposal instructions, the OMPF Field Folder routing record, the records retention review record, the destruction log, and the audit trail for IGMC inspection.

Per SECNAV M-5216.5<Citation source="SECNAV M-5216.5" /> Chapter 7 (SSIC), every correspondence carries the SSIC from SECNAV M-5210.2. Per SECNAV M-5210.2 Part I Chapter 2 paragraph 2-2, every file folder labels with the SSIC, open and close dates, and disposal instructions on the outside. Per SECNAV M 5210.1 page III-1 paragraph 2.b, every record type maps to a retention schedule under Schedules Cross Walked by Chapter.

## Trigger events

- Inbound correspondence arrives at the unit S-1.
- Outbound correspondence prepared for signature.
- New action officer assignment.
- Suspense date approaches.
- OMPF Field Folder impact decision.
- Quarterly register review cycle.
- Annual records retention review cycle.
- Records destruction window per SECNAV M 5210.1.
- IGMC inspection covers the correspondence register and filing.

## Processing workflow

1. Log every inbound correspondence. The S-1 clerk enters the action in the unit register on receipt.
2. Capture register fields. Per command policy and SECNAV M-5216.5<Citation source="SECNAV M-5216.5" /> Chapter 7, the register captures the date received, the originator, the subject, the classification, the SSIC, the action officer, the suspense date, and the close-out date.
3. Route to the action officer. The S-1 routes the action with the suspense date. The S-1 chief tracks against the suspense.
4. Apply SSIC labels on file folders. Per SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" /> Part I Chapter 2 paragraph 2-2, every folder labels with the SSIC, open and close dates, and disposal instructions on the outside.
5. Record open and close dates. The open date marks the start of the file. The close date marks the close-out of the correspondence action.
6. Apply disposal instructions. Per SECNAV M 5210.1 Schedules Cross Walked by Chapter, every record type carries a disposal instruction. The label captures the rule.
7. Route OMPF Field Folder copies. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, correspondence affecting an individual Marine (commendation, counseling, formal recommendation, adverse action) routes a copy to the OMPF Field Folder.
8. File certified copies. Per SECNAV M 5210.1 and MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />, file the certified copy in the unit records system per the SSIC retention schedule.
9. Run quarterly register review. The S-1 chief reviews the register quarterly to identify open suspenses, stale actions, and registration gaps.
10. Run annual records retention review. The records manager pulls the file inventory, identifies records past retention, and processes disposal per SECNAV M 5210.1.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Unit Correspondence Register. Local register tracking all S-1 correspondence.
- Unit Records System. Holds the certified correspondence per SECNAV M 5210.1.
- OMPF Field Folder. Receives copies of individual Marine correspondence per MCO P1070.12K Chapter 6.
- Directives Control Point (DCP). Coordinates correspondence affecting unit directives per MCO 5215.1K<Citation source="MCO 5215.1K" />.
- SSIC Database. SECNAV M-5210.2.

### Forms

- Unit Correspondence Register. Local format documenting daily correspondence actions.
- File Folder Label. Per SECNAV M-5210.2 paragraph 2-2.
- Records Retention Review Memorandum. Per SECNAV M 5210.1.
- Records Disposal Memorandum. Documents destruction or transfer.

## Common pitfalls

- Register entries missing or skipped. Without the register entry, the suspense and the action history break.
- File folder label missing SSIC, open/close dates, or disposal instructions. Per SECNAV M-5210.2<Citation source="SECNAV M-5210.2" /> paragraph 2-2, all three are required on the outside.
- Wrong SSIC applied. Per SECNAV M-5210.2, the SSIC matches the subject of the correspondence. A wrong SSIC misroutes the retention schedule.
- OMPF Field Folder routing skipped on individual Marine action. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, correspondence affecting an individual Marine routes a copy.
- Records destroyed before retention. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, the retention schedule controls the destruction date.
- Records retained past retention without authorized extension. Past-retention records inflate the records system without authority.
- Annual records retention review skipped. Without the review, expired records persist or current records get destroyed early.
- Electronic records held without disposal rules. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, electronic and paper records carry the same retention and disposal.

## Decision points

- SSIC selection. Per SECNAV M-5210.2, the subject of the correspondence maps to a specific SSIC. Mixed-subject correspondence routes to the primary subject SSIC.
- OMPF Field Folder impact. Per MCO P1070.12K Chapter 6, the action's effect on an individual Marine drives the routing decision.
- Paper versus electronic format. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, both formats carry the same retention and disposal rules. Format selection drives the storage system.
- Disposal versus transfer. Per SECNAV M 5210.1, disposition covers destruction or transfer to the records center per the schedule.
