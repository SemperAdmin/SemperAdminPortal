## Standby Reserve Within USMCR

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9 and 10 USC 10146<Citation source="10 USC 10146" />, Standby Reserve consists of Marines who have completed obligated service and have specific reasons preventing active drilling. Two lists organize Standby members.

## Active Status List (ASL)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9.

### ASL Eligibility

Members on ASL meet these.

- Completed initial obligated service
- Have specific reason temporarily preventing drilling
- Eligible to return to Ready Reserve when condition resolves
- Meet basic readiness standards

### Common ASL Reasons

- Civilian employment temporarily preventing drill
- Family hardship temporarily preventing drill
- Specific personal circumstances
- Other approved temporary reasons

### ASL Privileges

- Maintain reserve component status
- Subject to mobilization (limited authorities)
- Eligible for return to Ready Reserve
- Limited specific benefits

### ASL Participation

- No drilling required
- No drill pay
- Limited membership points
- Annual readiness updates

## Inactive Status List (ISL)

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9.

### ISL Eligibility

Members on ISL meet these.

- Members designated key civilian employees
- Members in extended hardship
- Members with specific medical conditions not warranting separation
- Specific other long-term situations

### Common ISL Reasons

- Key civilian employee designation (industries critical to national defense)
- Extended hardship preventing drill
- Long-term medical conditions
- Specific other approved reasons

### ISL Privileges

- Maintain reserve component status
- Subject to mobilization (limited authorities)
- Specific return to Ready Reserve requires screening
- Limited specific benefits

### ISL Participation

- No drilling required
- No drill pay
- Limited membership points
- Annual readiness updates

## Key Differences

| Aspect | ASL | ISL |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Return to Ready Reserve | Eligible after condition resolves | Screening required |
| Time on list | Generally shorter | Generally longer |
| Reason | Temporary | Long-term or specific designation |
| Specific support | Standard | Specific to category |

## Standby Mobilization Subject

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9 and 10 USC.

### Subject to

- 10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(a) Full Mobilization (declaration of war or national emergency)

### Not Subject to

- 10 USC 12302<Citation source="10 USC 12302" /> Partial Mobilization
- 10 USC 12304<Citation source="10 USC 12304" /> Presidential Reserve Call-Up

This makes Standby a deeper, more limited mobilization pool than IRR or SELRES (Selected Reserve, which covers SMCR units, IMAs, and Active Reserve Marines).

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

I&I admin rarely directly handles Standby Reserve. MCRSC primarily handles.

### When Standby Member Contacts I&I

For specific situations.

- Verify member's component status
- Identify need
- Route to MCRSC for most actions

### When Standby Member Returns to Ready Reserve

For local member.

- Coordinate with MCRSC
- Specific return procedures per ASL or ISL
- Local SMCR unit coordination if returning to drilling

### When Mobilization Affects Standby Members

During full mobilization.

- Coordinate with HQMC and MCRSC
- Specific mobilization procedures
- Local support for affected members

## Standby Member Career Options

Standby members have specific options.

### Continue in Standby

- Maintain participation requirements
- Periodic readiness updates
- Subject to specific recall authorities

### Return to Ready Reserve

- ASL: Eligible after condition resolves
- ISL: Requires screening
- Specific procedures via MCRSC

### Separation from Reserve

- End of MSO
- Specific separation procedures
- Discharge characterization

### Mobilization to Active Duty

- Per full mobilization authority only
- Specific procedures per MCRAMM Chapter 10

## Common Standby Issues

### Member Wants to Return to Drilling

ASL member ready to return.

- Coordinate with MCRSC
- Verify eligibility
- Local SMCR unit coordination

### Member's ASL Condition Continues

Member's temporary condition persists.

- Continued ASL status
- Possible transition to ISL if long-term
- Coordinate with MCRSC

### Member Confusion About Categories

Member doesn't understand status.

- Brief on ASL vs ISL
- Provide MCRSC contact
- Specific category procedures

### Member Wants to Update Standby Status

Member wants to change ASL or ISL status.

- Coordinate with MCRSC
- Specific procedures
- Document changes

## Coordination Touchpoints

Standby Reserve administration touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: Primary Standby administration
- **HQMC RA**: For policy and specific Standby matters
- **MOL**: For self-service updates
- **Local SMCR units**: For Ready Reserve return

## Related Roles

Standby Reserve members have minimal Reserve involvement.

- **Marines** in Standby maintain status without active drilling.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely engage Standby members.
- **Commanders** at I&I support occasional Standby member contacts.
