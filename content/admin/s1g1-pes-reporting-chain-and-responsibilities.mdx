## Role and responsibility

The Reporting Senior (RS) writes performance evaluation per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 3. The Reviewing Officer (RO) reviews and signs per Chapter 2 paragraph 4. Third Officer Sighting (3OS) applies in specific cases per Chapter 2 paragraph 5. The S-1 chief maintains the unit PES register of RS, RO, 3OS, SMR, and SEA assignments. The S-1 officer signs by direction on PES register updates.

Source documents flow from the unit T-O, the chain of command, designation orders, and personnel turnover events. PES chain actions land as register entries, RS and RO designation memos, and OMPF documentation. Outputs include the PES register, the RS and RO assignments per Marine, the 3OS designation when applicable, and the SMR and SEA roster.

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 2 (General Description), the reporting chain consists only of the RS, the RO, and where applicable the 3OS. Per paragraph 8, Senior Marine Representatives (SMRs) carry specific responsibility outside the reporting chain. Per paragraph 9, Senior Enlisted Advisors (SEAs) provide input but are not in the reporting chain.

## Trigger events

- Marine joins the unit requiring RS and RO assignment.
- RS change triggers Change of Reporting Senior (CRS) report.
- RO change triggers report routing update.
- 3OS designation required for specific circumstance per Chapter 2 paragraph 5.
- SMR designation cycle.
- SEA assignment change.
- Special situation per Chapter 2 paragraph 10.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the RS. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 3, the RS is the senior in the chain who writes the performance evaluation.
2. Identify the RO. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 4, the RO reviews the RS evaluation and signs.
3. Determine 3OS. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 5, 3OS applies in specific cases. The S-1 chief identifies when 3OS is required.
4. Validate SMR. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 8, the SMR is the senior Marine officer in a joint or non-Marine command. Identify per the unit type.
5. Coordinate SEA input. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 9, SEAs provide input outside the reporting chain.
6. Process special situations. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 10, special situations override the standard chain.
7. Brief incoming Marines. At inbound brief, identify the Marine's RS and RO.
8. Document assignments. Capture RS and RO per Marine in the unit PES register.
9. Process RS or RO change. Cut the CRS report at RS change per Chapter 3 reporting occasions.
10. Refresh the PES register. Update on personnel turnover, command change, or assignment change.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- PES (HQMC Performance Evaluation System). Holds fitness reports and reporting chain data.
- MCTFS. Holds chain-of-command data.
- Unit PES register. Local register of RS and RO per Marine.
- MMSB Performance Evaluation Branch. Maintains national-level PES records.

### Forms

- RS and RO Designation Memorandum. Local format documenting chain assignment.
- 3OS Designation Memorandum. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 5 when applicable.
- SMR Designation Letter. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 8.
- PES Register. Local format tracking chain per Marine.

## Common pitfalls

- RS not in the direct chain of supervision. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 3, the RS must be in the supervisory chain. Validate at assignment.
- RO outside Chapter 2 paragraph 4 criteria. Validate seniority and chain position before assignment.
- 3OS overlooked when required. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 5, identify 3OS at assignment.
- SMR role confused with RS or RO. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 8, SMR is outside the reporting chain.
- PES register stale. The register diverges from actual chain. Refresh on personnel turnover.

## Decision points

- RS designation. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 3, the RS is the senior in the supervisory chain. The S-1 officer validates.
- 3OS requirement. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 5, specific situations require 3OS. The S-1 officer determines per the situation.
- Special situation handling. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 10, special situations apply outside standard rules. The S-1 officer routes per the specific paragraph.
