## Mission of the Marine Corps Reserve

Per MCRAMM Chapter 1, the mission of the Marine Corps Reserve (USMCR) is to provide trained units and qualified individuals available for active duty in the Marine Corps in time of war or national emergency. The USMCR rounds out the operating forces and reinforces the active component when authorized.

This is not optional staffing. It is doctrinal force structure backed by Title 10 statute.

## Three Reserve Components

The USMCR organizes into three statutory components per 10 USC Chapter 1005.

### Ready Reserve

Active drilling reservists and ready-to-mobilize members. The largest component. Subdivides into.

- **Selected Marine Corps Reserve (SMCR)**: Active drilling units and Individual Mobilization Augmentees (IMA). The combat power of the USMCR.
- **Active Reserve (AR)**: Full-time active duty reservists supporting the reserve component. Recruiters, I&I staff, school instructors.
- **Individual Ready Reserve (IRR)**: Reservists with remaining military service obligation but not currently drilling.

### Standby Reserve

Members who have completed obligated service and have specific reasons preventing active drill. Smaller force pool. Available for mobilization but not actively training.

### Retired Reserve

Members who have retired from the Reserve but remain subject to recall under specific conditions.

## Marine Corps Forces Reserve (MARFORRES)

MARFORRES is the operational headquarters for the Marine Corps Reserve. Located in New Orleans, Louisiana. Commanded by the Commander, Marine Corps Forces Reserve (COMMARFORRES). MARFORRES owns the SMCR units geographically distributed across the United States.

The COMMARFORRES is also the Commanding General of the 4th Marine Division, 4th Marine Aircraft Wing, 4th Marine Logistics Group, and Force Headquarters Group when the Reserve is not federalized.

## Geographic Distribution

SMCR units are distributed by region to support local recruiting and minimize travel burden on drilling reservists. Each unit has an Inspector and Instructor (I&I) staff of active duty Marines providing administrative, training, and logistical support.

## Total Force Concept

The Marine Corps operates as a Total Force. Active and Reserve components train to common standards, deploy under common authorities, and serve under common policies. The USMCR is not a separate Marine Corps. It is part of the same fighting force.

This shapes how I&I admin work flows. You execute the same MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" /> (IRAM) records management, the same MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" /> BAH policy, and the same DOD FMR 7000.14-R pay regulation as active duty admin.

## Why I&I Admin Cares About Organization

Knowing the structure helps you process the right kind of action.

- A SMCR drilling Marine processes IDT pay, AT pay, and unit diary entries through Drill Manager.
- An IRR Marine has limited admin actions and uses MROWS for any orders.
- A Standby Reserve Marine has minimal action types.
- An Active Reserve Marine processes like active duty for pay and entitlements.
- A Retired Reserve recall processes through different channels.

Misrouting an action wastes time and creates pay errors.

## Reserve Pay Group Categories

Reserve Marines fall into pay group categories that determine drill pay and training participation. Pay groups appear in MCTFS as a coded value. The most common.

| Pay Group | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| A | Drilling SMCR member earning drill pay |
| B | SMCR member with reduced participation |
| F | IMA in active drilling status |
| P | IRR or Standby with no drill pay |
| R | Retired Reserve |

These codes drive Drill Manager processing, retirement point credit, and pay calculations.

## Common I&I Confusion Points

These create errors at the I&I admin shop.

1. **SMCR vs IMA processing.** Both drill but route differently. SMCR member uses unit Drill Manager. IMA member processes through their gaining command.
2. **IRR member walks in for help.** IRR members are not active drillers. Limited actions available. Direct to MROWS or HQMC depending on the request.
3. **Active Reserve confused with Active Duty.** Active Reserve Marines are reservists on full-time active duty supporting the Reserve. Pay and admin process like active duty but career path differs.
4. **Standby Reserve recall.** Standby reservists do not drill but remain subject to recall. Process under different policies than SMCR.

## Coordination Touchpoints

I&I admin coordinates with these external organizations.

- **MARFORRES G-1**: Reserve personnel policy and MROWS support
- **HQMC RA**: Reserve Affairs policy guidance
- **Supporting IPAC**: For active duty-equivalent processing during AT and mobilization
- **MCRSC (Marine Corps Reserve Support Command)**: IRR management and orders
- **MCAAT**: Policy interpretation when MCRAMM is unclear

## Related Roles

The Reserve organization affects every role.

- **Marines** in the Reserve track their pay group, drilling status, and component.
- **Leaders** at the unit understand which Marines drill what kind of duty.
- **Commanders** at I&I command Reserve units and own readiness reporting.
