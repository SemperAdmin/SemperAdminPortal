## Role and responsibility

The unit watch officer or operations duty officer owns the OPREP-3 PINNACLE synchronous voice report at the moment of event awareness. The commander assesses the national-level interest threshold. The S-3 operations staff drafts the asynchronous message report within the 1-hour timing window. The S-1 officer and adjutant support records retention.

Source documents flow from the event or incident notification, the duty officer report, the commander assessment of national-level interest, the Enclosure 1 OPREP-3 PINNACLE flag word matrix, and the Enclosure 3 NMCC contact information. Outputs include the synchronous voice report log, the OPREP-3 PINNACLE message report, the supplemental reports, and the retained record per SECNAV M-5210.1.

OPREP-3 PINNACLE is the highest-priority OPREP-3 report category. The 15-minute synchronous timing window applies from the moment of event awareness. Reports route directly to NMCC, bypassing the MCOC.

## Trigger events

- Nuclear weapons accident, incident, or significant deficiency.
- Loss of nuclear weapons custody or unauthorized nuclear weapons handling.
- Outbreak of war between major powers.
- Significant US military operation or imminent military operation.
- Loss of a major weapon system with national-level implications.
- Significant terrorism event with national-level implications.
- Other event meeting the national-level interest threshold per CJCSM 3150.03D.

## OPREP-3 PINNACLE flag words

OPREP-3 PINNACLE reports use specific flag words tied to event type per CJCSM 3150.03D and Enclosure 1 of MCO 3504.2A:

- OPREP-3 PINNACLE (OPREP-3P). Immediate notification of any incident or event where national-level interest is determined.
- OPREP-3 PINNACLE LERTCON STATUS OF ACTIONS. Alert condition status report.
- OPREP-3 NUCFLASH. Nuclear weapons accident or incident.
- OPREP-3 PINNACLE LERTCON. Alert condition declaration.
- OPREP-3 OPSCRITICAL. Operational critical event.

Flag words drive the routing priority and the addressee list. Use the correct flag word per the Enclosure 1 matrix.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the national-level interest. The commander assesses the event against the national-level interest threshold per CJCSM 3150.03D and MCO 3504.2A<Citation source="MCO 3504.2A" /> Paragraph 3c(1).
2. Determine the OPREP-3 PINNACLE flag word. Match the event type against the Enclosure 1 flag word matrix.
3. Submit the synchronous voice report to NMCC. The unit watch officer calls NMCC via the voice number in Enclosure 3 within 15 minutes of awareness per MCO 3504.2A<Citation source="MCO 3504.2A" />.
4. Submit the synchronous chat report (secondary method). Broadcast on the NMCS command net chat room on DISA Defense Connect Online Jabber Chat via the SIPR network as a backup or supplementary synchronous channel.
5. Draft the OPREP-3 PINNACLE message. Use the standard OPREP-3 message format with the flag word in the identification line per CJCSM 3150.03D.
6. Apply Joint Reporting Structure addressing. Include NMCC, the affected combatant command, and the appropriate Service operations centers per Enclosure 1.
7. Route the message report. Submit within the 1-hour asynchronous timing window per MCO 3504.2A<Citation source="MCO 3504.2A" /> Enclosure 1.
8. Submit supplemental reports. Supplemental reports update or correct the initial report. Submit as required to keep NMCC and the combatant command informed.
9. Coordinate with the Marine Corps Operations Center (MCOC). Notify MCOC of the PINNACLE report routing for HQMC awareness.
10. Retain the record. Archive the OPREP-3 PINNACLE report per SECNAV M-5210.1 and NAVMC DIR 5210.11E records retention rules.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- National Military Command Center (NMCC). OPREP-3 PINNACLE receiving authority.
- Marine Corps Operations Center (MCOC). HQMC awareness channel for PINNACLE reports routed by Marine Corps units.
- NMCS command net chat room on DISA Defense Connect Online Jabber Chat (SIPR). Backup synchronous channel.
- Combatant command operations centers. Information addressees per Enclosure 1.

### Forms

- OPREP-3 PINNACLE Message. The standard OPREP-3 message format with the PINNACLE flag word.
- Voice Report Log. Local log capturing the synchronous voice report content and time.
- Enclosure 1 OPREP-3 Reporting Requirements Matrix. Flag word matrix.
- Enclosure 3 OPREP-3 Reporting Contact Information.

## Common pitfalls

- 15-minute window missed. The initial voice report exceeds the 15-minute timing per MCO 3504.2A<Citation source="MCO 3504.2A" />. Submit the voice report at the moment of awareness with the information on hand.
- Wrong flag word applied. The OPREP-3 PINNACLE routing fails to align with the event type. Match the event against the Enclosure 1 flag word matrix before submission.
- MCOC unnotified on PINNACLE routing. HQMC loses awareness on the Marine Corps unit PINNACLE submission. Notify MCOC after the NMCC voice report.
- Synchronous chat used instead of voice. Voice is the primary synchronous channel. Use chat as backup per Paragraph 4a(2)(a)1.
- National-level interest threshold under-assessed. The commander treats a significant incident as SIR when it meets PINNACLE criteria. Apply CJCSM 3150.03D thresholds at the commander assessment.

## Decision points

- National-level interest determination. The commander assesses the event against CJCSM 3150.03D thresholds. When in doubt, escalate to PINNACLE rather than SIR per MCO 3504.2A<Citation source="MCO 3504.2A" /> "when in doubt, submit a report" framing.
- Flag word selection. Match the event type against the Enclosure 1 matrix. NUCFLASH, OPSCRITICAL, and LERTCON have distinct trigger criteria.
- Sister Service operational control PINNACLE routing. When operating under a sister Service component headquarters, route PINNACLE per Paragraph 5d with CMC WASHINGTON DC PPO as an information addressee.
