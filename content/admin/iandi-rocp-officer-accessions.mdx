## What ROCP Is

Per MCRAMM Chapter 2 Section 3, the Reserve Officer Commissioning Program (ROCP) commissions officers for the Marine Corps Reserve. ROCP supports SMCR officer billets by training and commissioning members directly for Reserve service.

ROCP is one of several officer commissioning paths.

## ROCP vs Other Commissioning Paths

Marine Corps officers commission through multiple paths.

| Path | Description |
|------|-------------|
| ROCP | Direct Reserve officer commissioning |
| ROTC (NROTC) | College program leading to active or Reserve commission |
| USNA | U.S. Naval Academy |
| OCS Active Duty | Officer Candidate School with active duty obligation |
| Mustang/LDO | Limited Duty Officer from senior enlisted ranks |
| Warrant Officer | Specific warrant officer program |

ROCP specifically commissions officers for Reserve service.

## ROCP Path

Standard ROCP path for a candidate.

### Step 1 - Enlisted Service or College Graduation

- Member is enlisted Marine seeking commission via ROCP
- Or college graduate applying directly to ROCP

### Step 2 - Application

Member submits ROCP application package.

- Officer application
- Recommendations
- Transcripts
- Specific specialty application if applicable

### Step 3 - Selection Board

HQMC convenes selection board.

- Candidates reviewed by board
- Selections made for ROCP slots
- Selection results published

### Step 4 - Officer Candidate School (OCS)

Selected candidates attend OCS.

- 10-week intensive officer training
- Quantico, Virginia
- Completion qualifies candidate for commission

### Step 5 - Commissioning

After OCS completion.

- Commission as Reserve Second Lieutenant
- Initial reporting to first assignment

### Step 6 - Initial Officer Schools

New Reserve officers attend.

- The Basic School (TBS) for 26 weeks
- MOS school based on assigned MOS
- Total initial officer training of 6-12 months

### Step 7 - Join SMCR Unit

After initial training.

- Officer joins assigned SMCR unit
- Begins drilling status
- Reserve officer pay structure

## Performance Steps for ROCP Receipt at I&I

When new Reserve officer arrives at I&I.

### Step 1 - Coordinate Initial Receipt

- Receive officer's records from TBS or MOS school
- Establish OQR locally
- Verify all initial documents in MCTFS

### Step 2 - Process Status Transition

Officer transitions from active training to drilling Reserve.

- UD/MIPS active duty end transaction
- Reserve drilling status begin transaction
- Drill pay system activated

### Step 3 - Initial Counseling

Per IRAM.

- Brief on SMCR officer expectations
- Brief on participation requirements
- Brief on drill schedule
- Document counseling

### Step 4 - Verify Documents in OQR

- TBS completion
- MOS school certificate
- Initial enlistment or commission contract
- All in OQR per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />

## Officer Specific Considerations

### Officer Drilling Requirements

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4, officers have specific participation requirements.

- Same minimum drill participation as enlisted
- Additional officer-specific responsibilities
- Officer Tour requirements per MCRAMM

### Officer Pay Structure

- Drill pay at officer rate
- Officer BAS rate
- Officer-specific bonuses where applicable

### Officer Career Path

- Promotion through Reserve officer ranks
- Officer-specific selection boards
- Lieutenant through general officer progression

## Common Reserve Officer Programs

Beyond standard ROCP.

### Direct Commission

For specific specialties.

- Medical Corps officers
- Judge Advocate General Corps
- Chaplain Corps
- Other specialty professions
- Direct commission without OCS

### Mustang/LDO Commission

For senior enlisted Marines.

- Selection through specific boards
- Limited Duty Officer (LDO) designation
- Specialized officer duty within member's enlisted MOS

### Warrant Officer

For technical specialty Marines.

- Specific warrant officer program selection
- Technical expertise focus
- Warrant officer pay grades W-1 through CW-5

## ROCP Documentation Required

For ROCP officer receipt, admin needs.

- Original commission documents
- TBS graduation certificate
- MOS school graduation certificate
- College transcripts
- Initial enlistment or service contract
- DEERS verification
- SGLI election

All filed in OQR via UD/MIPS document storage.

When Reserve officer arrives.

### Welcome and Integration

- Welcome new officer to unit

- Connect with senior officers in unit

### Documentation Coordination

- Establish OQR per IRAM
- Verify all training and qualification documents
- Address any documentation gaps

### Pay System Setup

- Drill pay activation at officer rate
- Officer BAS rate
- Coordinate any specialty bonuses

### Career Planning

- Career counseling for Reserve officer career
- Promotion timing and requirements
- Education benefits planning

## Common ROCP Issues

### TBS Performance Issues

If officer has issues at TBS.

- Might result in discharge or admin action
- Specific procedures for officer accession failures
- Coordinate with HQMC

### MOS Reclassification

If officer needs different MOS.

- Specific officer MOS reclassification procedures
- Different from enlisted lateral moves
- Coordinate with HQMC

### Successful Commission and Joining SMCR

When officer arrives at SMCR unit successfully.

- Welcome and integration into officer leadership
- First drill weekend important
- Officer responsibilities established

## Quality Control on ROCP Receipt

Common errors during officer receipt.

1. **Documents not received from TBS/MOS school.** Coordinate transfer.
2. **OQR vs SRB confusion.** Officers have OQR, not SRB. Process correctly.
3. **Officer pay rate incorrect.** Verify rank and apply correct officer rate.
4. **Officer-specific counseling skipped.** Officers have specific counseling requirements.

## Coordination Touchpoints

ROCP receipt touches.

- **HQMC Officer Manpower**: For policy and decisions
- **Officer Candidate School (OCS)**: For initial training
- **The Basic School (TBS)**: For officer initial training
- **MOS School**: For initial MOS qualification

## Related Roles

ROCP brings new officers to SMCR units.

- **Marines** (newly commissioned officers) join SMCR units and lead.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units integrate new officers.
- **Commanders** at I&I support new officer integration.
