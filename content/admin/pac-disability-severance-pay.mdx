## What Disability Severance Pay Is

Per 10 USC 1212<Citation source="10 USC 1212" /> and DOD FMR Volume 7B<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7B" /> Chapter 9, disability severance pay compensates members separated due to medical disability not qualifying for disability retirement. The pay is a lump sum based on years of service.

This is different from separation pay (for involuntary non-medical separations) and from retirement pay (for 20+ years service).

## When Disability Severance Pay Applies

A member receives disability severance pay when meeting all of these.

- Member separated due to medical disability
- Disability rating below 30% (the threshold for disability retirement)
- Less than 20 years of service
- Disability is not service-connected misconduct
- Specific separation processing through Disability Evaluation System (DES)

## Disability Evaluation System (DES) Outcomes

The DES evaluates members with medical conditions and determines outcomes.

### Findings

- **Fit for duty**: Member returns to duty
- **Disability rating below 30%**: Disability severance pay (typical separation)
- **Disability rating 30% or higher**: Disability retirement (continuing pay and benefits)
- **Permanent disability retirement**: Specific permanent retirement
- **Temporary disability retirement (TDRL)**: Temporary retirement with future re-evaluation

Members in disability separation processing receive disability severance pay if rated below 30%.

## Calculation of Disability Severance Pay

Per 10 USC 1212<Citation source="10 USC 1212" />.

### Formula

```
Disability Severance Pay = Years of Service x 12 x Monthly Basic Pay
```

### Years of Service Calculation

- Years of active service
- Plus reserve years counted at proper credit rate
- Minimum threshold per DOD FMR (typically 6 years)
- Maximum threshold (typically 19 years to avoid overlap with retirement)

### Example

Marine separated for medical disability rated 20% with 8 years active service and $4500 monthly basic pay.

- Disability severance pay: 8 x 12 x $4500 = $432,000

This is a substantial lump sum.

### Maximum Calculation

Per DOD FMR, maximum disability severance pay typically capped at the member's last 19 years of service.

## VA Coordination

VA disability compensation often follows or coincides with DOD disability separation.

### VA Compensation Eligibility

- Member receives VA disability compensation for service-connected conditions
- Specific monthly amounts based on VA disability rating
- Potentially long-term benefit

### Recoupment of Disability Severance Pay

Per 10 USC 1212<Citation source="10 USC 1212" />(d), VA may recoup some disability severance pay from VA compensation.

- Recoupment offsets duplicate compensation
- Specific conditions trigger recoupment
- VA reduces monthly compensation until severance pay recouped
- Specific conditions may exempt from recoupment

This complicates planning for some members.

## Performance Steps for Disability Severance Pay Processing

### Step 1 - Verify Disability Separation

Confirm separation through DES.

- Disability rating documented
- Rating below 30%
- Member not eligible for disability retirement
- Separation orders cite disability separation

### Step 2 - Calculate Pay

Apply disability severance formula.

- Identify years of service from MCTFS
- Identify monthly basic pay
- Apply formula
- Verify against current DOD FMR

### Step 3 - Process via DFAS

Coordinate with DFAS for pay processing.

- Specific separation pay transaction
- Lump sum disbursement
- Federal tax withholding (specific rules apply)
- Member account for direct deposit

### Step 4 - Verify Documentation

Ensure documentation complete.

- Disability finding from DES
- Member acknowledgment of separation
- VA referral for ongoing benefits
- Specific separation orders

### Step 5 - Brief Member on VA Coordination

Critical counseling for member.

- Member should apply for VA disability compensation
- VA recoupment may apply
- Specific implications for monthly VA benefits
- Coordination with VA case manager

## Tax Treatment

Per IRS rules.

- Disability severance pay generally subject to federal income tax
- Specific conditions may exempt portions per Combat-Related Special Compensation rules
- Service-connected disabilities may have specific tax treatment
- Coordinate with member's tax preparer

## Common Disability Severance Pay Errors

These hit during processing.

1. **Wrong rating threshold applied.** Member at 30% rating is disability retirement, not severance pay. Verify rating.
2. **Years of service miscalculated.** Reserve years not properly counted. Recalculate.
3. **Monthly basic pay wrong.** Use member's actual monthly basic pay at separation.
4. **VA recoupment not addressed.** Member receives both disability severance and VA compensation without recoupment coordination. Brief member.
5. **Tax withholding errors.** Federal tax not properly withheld from lump sum. Coordinate with DFAS.

## Audit Implications

Disability severance pay is audit-relevant.

- Wrong amount creates significant overpayment or underpayment
- VA recoupment errors create dual compensation
- Tax withholding errors create year-end issues

PAC accuracy on disability severance supports both unit audit posture and member welfare.

## Documentation Required

For disability severance pay, PAC needs.

- DES findings document
- Member's disability rating
- Years of service from MCTFS
- Monthly basic pay history
- Member acknowledgment of separation

## Quality Control

Before submitting disability severance pay.

- Verify rating below 30%
- Verify years of service accurate
- Verify monthly basic pay correct
- Verify DES findings final
- Verify member receives clear explanation

## VA Coordination Touchpoints

Disability separation involves coordinating with VA.

- VA pre-discharge program
- VA disability rating coordination
- TAP (Transition Assistance Program) participation
- VA case manager assignment
- Specific VA programs for disabled veterans

## Special Cases

### Combat-Related Disability

Members with combat-related disabilities may have specific provisions.

- Combat-Related Special Compensation (CRSC)
- Specific tax exemptions for combat-related portions
- Different recoupment rules in some cases

### Service-Connected Aggravation

If existing condition worsened during service.

- Specific disability rating considerations
- Disability severance pay still applies for ratings below 30%
- VA may rate separately for service-connection

### Permanent vs Temporary Disability Retirement

Members rated 30% or higher.

- Receive permanent or temporary disability retirement
- Continuing monthly pay (not lump sum)
- Different calculation
- Continuing TRICARE eligibility

## Related Roles

Disability severance pay affects medically separated members.

- **Marines** facing disability separation receive severance pay and apply for VA benefits.
- **Leaders** counsel members during disability evaluation process.
- **Commanders** ensure proper DES processing and member care.
