## Separate Rations (Sep Rats)

Per MCO 10110.47<Citation source="MCO 10110.47" />, separate rations is a specific BAS category for enlisted members eligible for chow hall meals but authorized to subsist separately.

### When Sep Rats Applies

A member is on separate rations when.

- Member is enlisted (junior enlisted typically)
- Eligible for meal card but not assigned one
- Authorized by command to subsist separately
- Specific work shift, duty location, or family circumstances

The sep rats designation allows the member to draw BAS while ineligible for routine BAH-without-dependents (for example, junior enlisted in barracks).

### Sep Rats Authorization

Command authorizes sep rats based on.

- Duty schedule incompatible with chow hall hours
- Geographic separation from chow hall
- Command-approved family or personal circumstances
- Specific duty assignments requiring flexibility

Authorization is in writing per MCO 10110.47<Citation source="MCO 10110.47" />.

### BAS Rate for Sep Rats

Members on sep rats receive enlisted BAS rate same as off-card enlisted.

- Standard enlisted BAS rate
- Same as members not assigned meal cards
- Annual rate adjustment applies

## Field Rations

When members operate in field conditions, BAS may have specific rules.

### Field Conditions

- Training exercises in field environments
- Deployment to operations
- Specific duty types requiring field subsistence

### BAS During Field Operations

- BAS continues at standard rate
- Field rations (MREs) provided by command at no cost to member
- BAS does not stop simply because rations are issued
- Specific deployment regulations may modify

### Combat or Operational Conditions

In combat or specific operational conditions.

- Government-provided meals do not stop BAS in most cases
- Command-issued rations are operational support, not pay reduction
- Specific exceptions per current operational orders

## Member Hospitalized or In Medical Care

When a member is hospitalized.

### Inpatient Care

- BAS may continue or be adjusted based on length and type of hospitalization
- Government-provided hospital meals may affect BAS
- Specific provisions per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" /> Chapter 25

### Outpatient Care

- BAS continues normally
- Member responsible for own meals during outpatient care

### Medical Hold

- BAS continues at member's standard rate
- Specific provisions may apply if member residing at MTF

## BAS and Confinement

When a member is confined.

### Restriction

- BAS continues during restriction
- Member still responsible for own meals
- Restriction does not affect BAS

### Correctional Custody

- BAS may stop during correctional custody
- Member receives institutional meals
- Specific provisions per UCMJ punishment rules

### Pretrial Confinement

- BAS rules vary based on specific circumstances
- Coordinate with PAC and SJA on specific cases

## BAS During Specific Pay Situations

### NJP Forfeitures

- BAS not directly forfeited (forfeitures apply to base pay)
- BAS continues normally during NJP unless other circumstances apply

### Reduction in Grade

- BAS continues at enlisted rate (officer or enlisted based on new grade)
- Rate does not change for grade reductions within enlisted ranks

## Performance Steps for Special BAS Categories

### Step 1 - Identify the Specific Category

Determine which BAS category applies.

- Standard BAS for off-card enlisted
- Officer BAS
- Sep Rats BAS
- Field condition BAS
- Hospital BAS
- Confinement BAS

### Step 2 - Verify Documentation

Each category has specific documentation requirements.

- Sep rats: Command authorization letter
- Hospitalization: Medical documentation
- Confinement: Confinement orders
- Field conditions: Operational orders

### Step 3 - Process Correct Transaction

Each category has specific UD/MIPS transaction codes per MCTFSCODESMAN.

- Standard BAS: Generic BAS codes
- Sep rats: Sep rats specific codes
- Stop or modify BAS: Specific codes for the situation

### Step 4 - Document Basis

PAC retains documentation supporting the action.

- Authorization letters
- Medical or confinement documentation
- Effective dates

## Common Errors on Special BAS

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Sep rats authorization expired but BAS continues.** Annual review missed. Verify authorization current. Adjust BAS as needed.
2. **Member moved to barracks but sep rats continues.** Member should be on meal card. Stop sep rats and transition to meal card.
3. **Field operation BAS stopped incorrectly.** BAS does not stop because of field rations. Restore retroactive.
4. **Hospital BAS adjustment not applied.** Long inpatient care may have specific provisions. Verify and apply.
5. **Confinement BAS not adjusted.** Correctional custody may stop BAS. Verify and process correctly.

## BAS Coordination with Other Pay

BAS interacts with other entitlements.

- BAH: Independent of BAS, both may apply or neither
- Special Duty Assignment Pay (SDAP): Independent of BAS
- Hostile Fire Pay (HFP): Independent of BAS
- Family Separation Allowance (FSA): Independent of BAS

Each allowance has its own eligibility and processing.

## Documentation Standards

For special BAS categories, PAC retains.

- Original authorization documents
- Medical or operational documentation supporting the category
- Member acknowledgment if applicable
- Periodic review documentation for ongoing categories

## Audit Implications

Sep rats is an audit-relevant category.

- Sep rats authorization without proper documentation creates audit findings
- Sep rats continued past expiration creates findings
- Member in barracks with sep rats creates inconsistency

PAC accuracy on sep rats supports unit audit posture.

## Quality Control

Before submitting special BAS transactions.

- Verify documentation present
- Verify rate matches DOD FMR
- Verify effective date matches the triggering event
- Verify authorization current (for sep rats)

## Coordination Touchpoints

Special BAS processing touches.

- **Member's chain of command**: For sep rats authorization
- **Local installation chow hall**: For meal card coordination
- **MTF**: For hospital-related BAS questions
- **Confinement facility**: For confinement BAS

## Related Roles

Special BAS categories affect specific situations.

- **Marines** in special situations submit appropriate documentation.
- **Leaders** counsel members and verify BAS accuracy.
- **Commanders** authorize sep rats and oversee unit BAS posture.
