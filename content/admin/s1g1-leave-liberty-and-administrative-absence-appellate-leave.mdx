## Role and responsibility

The convening authority and the legal advisor route the Appellate Leave action. The S-1 officer processes the Appellate Leave authorization per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26. The S-1 chief tracks the Appellate Leave status against the MCTFS record. The IPAC pay clerk coordinates pay-side adjustments.

Source documents flow from the court-martial appellate notification, the legal advisor's case status, the Marine's MCTFS record, and the convening authority's direction. Appellate Leave transactions land in MOL and MCTFS with the right chargeable or non-chargeable code per paragraph 26. Outputs include the Appellate Leave authorization, the MCTFS transaction, the chargeable status entry, and the legal records integration.

Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26 (Appellate Leave or Leave Awaiting), Appellate Leave covers Marines pending court-martial appellate review or final action. Status follows the court-martial result and the appellate process per MCO 5800.16<Citation source="MCO 5800.16" /> Volume 16.

## Trigger events

- Court-martial conviction triggers appellate review.
- Marine pending final action awaits separation or punishment execution.
- Legal advisor routes Appellate Leave action.
- Appellate authority issues decision affecting leave status.
- Marine separation directed following appellate review.
- Return-to-duty directed by appellate authority.
- Conversion to Excess Leave per the order rules.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the notification. Pull the court-martial appellate notification from the legal advisor or convening authority.
2. Validate eligibility. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26, confirm against the order rules.
3. Coordinate with legal advisor. Per MCO 5800.16<Citation source="MCO 5800.16" /> Volume 16, route through legal for appellate status validation.
4. Process the Appellate Leave authorization. The S-1 officer signs the authorization per paragraph 26.
5. Cut the Appellate Leave transaction. Submit through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> with the right Appellate Leave code.
6. Track chargeable status. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26, status follows the program rules.
7. Coordinate Marine duty status. Per the appellate process and legal direction, Marine duty status follows the court-martial result.
8. Process return-to-duty or separation action. On appellate resolution, route per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> when separation applies.
9. Document the period in MCTFS. Capture Appellate Leave start, end, and resolution.
10. Coordinate with MMSR. On separation routing, coordinate with MMSR per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" />.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds Appellate Leave state and pay impact.
- MOL. Captures Appellate Leave order.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts Appellate Leave transactions.
- Legal records system. Holds the court-martial appellate case file.

### Forms

- Appellate Leave Authorization Memorandum. Local format signed by the S-1 officer.
- Court-Martial Appellate Notification. From the convening authority or legal advisor.
- DD Form 214 Certificate of Release or Discharge. Cut on separation following appellate review.

## Common pitfalls

- Appellate Leave processed without legal advisor coordination. Per MCO 5800.16<Citation source="MCO 5800.16" /> Volume 16, legal advisor coordination is required.
- Chargeable status misapplied. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26, status follows the program rules. Cut the right transaction.
- Marine duty status confusion during appellate period. Coordinate with legal advisor for clear duty status guidance.
- Separation routing skipped following appellate resolution. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" />, separation follows appellate finality. Route to MMSR.
- Documentation skipped in MCTFS. The pay record diverges from leave state. Update at every transition.

## Decision points

- Appellate Leave versus Excess Leave. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 26 and Chapter 5 Section 2, the legal advisor calls based on appellate status.
- Return-to-duty versus separation. The appellate decision triggers the path. The S-1 officer routes per the right outcome.
- Pay-side implications. Per the punishment and appellate decision, pay-side adjustments follow. Coordinate with IPAC pay clerk.
