## Role and responsibility

The unit S-1 supports individual augment tasking per MCO 3120.12A<Citation source="MCO 3120.12A" /> GFM framework and MCO 3000.18B<Citation source="MCO 3000.18B" /> FDP&E Manual. The IPAC outbound counselor processes PCS or TAD orders for the augment. The S-1 chief tracks individual augments against the unit roster. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the augment package. The Marine's chain of command supports the augment selection. HQMC and MARFOR coordinate the tasking through OSCAR (Operational Support Augmentation Requirement) and joint individual augmentee channels.

Source documents flow from the OSCAR tasking, the MOS and grade requirement, the duration of the tasking, the gaining unit identification, the Marine's qualification record, the Marine's PCS or TAD orders, and the personnel deployment transactions. Outputs include the matched Marine for the tasking, the cut PCS or TAD orders per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" />, the pre-deployment training certification, the personnel deployment transactions, the gaining unit coordination record, the in-stride tracking, the redeployment and return record, and the unit DRRS readiness update.

Per MCO 3120.12A, individual augment tasking flows through GFM coordination. OSCAR is the Marine Corps Operational Support Augmentation Requirement process. Joint Individual Augmentee (JIA) tasking flows from Joint Staff through MARFOR to the Marine Corps source unit. Per MCTP 3-30G<Citation source="MCTP 3-30G" />, the S-1 supports the personnel administration aspects.

## Trigger events

- OSCAR tasking arrives at the unit.
- Joint Individual Augmentee (JIA) tasking from Joint Staff.
- HQMC individual augment requirement.
- Marine selected for the tasking.
- Pre-deployment training certification cycle.
- PCS or TAD orders cut.
- Individual augment redeployment.
- Unit DRRS readiness update after augment departure.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the individual augment tasking. The S-1 receives from MARFOR or HQMC per MCO 3120.12A<Citation source="MCO 3120.12A" />.
2. Match the tasking to qualified Marine. The S-1 chief identifies a Marine matching the MOS, grade, and qualifications.
3. Coordinate with the Marine on personal and family readiness. The S-1 confirms the Marine's personal readiness for deployment.
4. Process pre-deployment training certification. The unit certifies the Marine on pre-deployment training requirements.
5. Cut PCS or TAD orders. Per MCO 1300.8<Citation source="MCO 1300.8" />, the IPAC outbound counselor cuts the orders per the tasking duration.
6. Process personnel deployment transactions. Per MCTP 3-30G<Citation source="MCTP 3-30G" />, the S-1 processes deployment transactions through UD-MIPS.
7. Coordinate with the gaining joint or Marine Corps command. The S-1 communicates the Marine's qualifications and arrival date with the gaining command.
8. Track the individual augment through deployment. The S-1 maintains contact and tracks the augment's status.
9. Process redeployment and return to home unit. The IPAC processes the return transactions on completion of the tasking.
10. Update unit DRRS readiness. Per MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />, the unit DRRS reflects the augment departure and return.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- OSCAR database. Per HQMC tasking.
- Joint Individual Augmentee (JIA) tasking system. Per Joint Staff.
- MARFOR command coordination systems. Per MCO 3120.12A.
- Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). Personnel deployment transactions.
- DRRS. Per MCO 3000.13B for readiness updates.

### Forms

- Individual Augment Tasking Order. Per MCO 3120.12A and HQMC.
- Augment Selection Memorandum. Per the S-1.
- Pre-Deployment Training Certification. Per the unit.
- PCS or TAD Orders. Per MCO 1300.8.
- Redeployment and Return Memorandum. Per the IPAC.

## Common pitfalls

- Individual augment selected without qualification match. Per MCO 3120.12A<Citation source="MCO 3120.12A" />, the tasking carries specific MOS and qualification requirements.
- Pre-deployment training certification skipped. The unit certifies before the augment deploys.
- PCS or TAD orders late. The IPAC cuts orders to support the tasking timeline.
- Gaining command coordination lagged. The gaining unit needs the Marine's qualifications and arrival date.
- DRRS readiness update missed at augment departure or return. Per MCO 3000.13B, the augment status affects unit personnel readiness.
- Individual augment family readiness skipped. The Marine and family need transition support.

## Decision points

- Augment selection. The S-1 chief identifies the Marine matching MOS, grade, qualifications, and operational availability.
- PCS versus TAD orders. The tasking duration drives the orders type. Long-duration taskings route through PCS. Short-duration route through TAD.
- Family readiness scope. Per the Marine's family situation, the unit supports family transition.
- Return-to-unit timing. Per the tasking duration, the return aligns with the operational cycle.
