## What the SOU Is

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 Section 2.5, the Statement of Understanding (SOU) is a written acknowledgment by a reserve member of their participation requirements and the consequences of failure to participate. The SOU formalizes expectations beyond what the standard enlistment or commissioning contract covers.

The SOU exists to remove ambiguity. When a member signs the SOU, the unit has documented evidence the member knows what is required.

## When the SOU Applies

Per MCRAMM, the SOU is used in specific situations.

### Non-Mandatory Members

Members serving beyond their initial military service obligation but continuing in the SelRes voluntarily. The SOU documents continued voluntary participation commitment.

### Members with Modified Participation

Members whose normal participation is modified due to.

- Geographic relocation requiring different unit assignment
- Family or civilian employment circumstances
- Returning from extended absence or deactivation
- Cross-training or MOS conversion

### Members on Notice for Performance Issues

Members previously flagged for participation concerns. The SOU sets clear expectations for restored good standing.

## Required SOU Content

Per MCRAMM Figure 4-5 (sample SOU), the document covers.

### Member Identification

- Full name, rank, SSN, MOS
- Unit assignment
- Anniversary year start date

### Participation Requirements Acknowledged

- Number of IDT periods required per fiscal year
- AT requirement
- Required training completion
- Physical fitness standards
- Admin readiness expectations

### Consequences Acknowledged

- Unsatisfactory Participation processing trigger thresholds
- Possible separation from SelRes
- Possible transfer to IRR
- Possible involuntary order to active duty under 10 USC 12303<Citation source="10 USC 12303" />
- Loss of drill pay for missed periods
- Loss of retirement points for missed periods

### Member Signature

- Member signs and dates
- Witness signature (typically Admin Chief or unit officer)
- Counseling officer signature

## Performance Steps for SOU Processing

### Step 1 - Determine SOU Need

Identify when an SOU is appropriate.

- Member entering modified participation status
- Member returning from extended absence
- Member with prior Unsatisfactory issues now restored
- Officer or commander direction based on specific circumstances

### Step 2 - Prepare the SOU

Use the MCRAMM Figure 4-5 template.

- Fill in member-specific information
- Customize participation requirements per their situation
- Verify the consequences section reflects current policy
- Print clean copy for signing

### Step 3 - Conduct Counseling

The SOU is signed during a counseling session.

- Read through the document with the member
- Verify member understanding of each section
- Answer member questions
- Document any clarifications discussed

### Step 4 - Obtain Signatures

After counseling.

- Member signs and dates
- Witness signs (Admin Chief or unit officer)
- Counseling officer signs
- Make copies for distribution

### Step 5 - File and Track

Distribute copies and file.

- Original to member's OMPF via PAC
- Copy to member
- Copy to unit personnel file
- Track expiration or review date if applicable

### Step 6 - Periodic Review

For SOUs with conditions or duration.

- Review at agreed intervals (quarterly, annually)
- Verify member is meeting commitments
- Update the SOU if conditions change
- Process Page 11 entries documenting compliance

## SOU vs Page 11 Counseling

Both document member acknowledgment but differ in purpose.

| Aspect | SOU | Page 11 Counseling |
|--------|-----|---------------------|
| Purpose | Formal acknowledgment of participation commitment | Specific incident or behavior counseling |
| Trigger | Modified status, voluntary continuation, performance recovery | Specific incident or behavior |
| Scope | Forward-looking commitment | Document specific event |
| Filed | OMPF | SRB and OMPF |
| Member signature | Required | Required |
| Witness | Required | Required |
| Used as | Condition of continued participation | Record of counseling |

Both serve the unit's documentation needs but apply in different situations.

## Common SOU Errors

These hit on SOU processing.

1. **SOU used when not required.** Routine SelRes members do not need SOU. Use Page 11 for incident counseling.
2. **Generic template not customized.** SOU should reflect the specific member's situation. Generic templates without customization signal lack of counseling effort.
3. **No witness signature.** SOU requires witness. Without witness signature, validity is questionable.
4. **Not filed in OMPF.** SOU must be filed in the OMPF for permanent record. Local file copy alone is insufficient.
5. **No periodic review for conditional SOUs.** SOUs with ongoing conditions need review. Without review, the SOU loses force.

## When to Use Other Tools Instead

The SOU is one tool among several. Other options.

- **Page 11 Counseling**: For incident-specific counseling and documentation
- **Verbal counseling**: For minor issues not warranting paper
- **Letter of Caution**: For more serious performance concerns
- **Letter of Reprimand**: For administrative actions short of NJP
- **Unsatisfactory Participation processing**: For pattern of failures
- **Adverse fitness report or proficiency mark**: For documented poor performance

The right tool depends on the situation.

## Coordination Touchpoints

SOU processing touches.

- **Member**: Primary participant in counseling
- **Commander or designated counseling officer**: Conducts the counseling
- **Admin Chief**: Witnesses and processes the document
- **Supporting PAC**: Files original in OMPF
- **MCRSC**: For SOUs related to component transitions

## Related Roles

The SOU process involves multiple roles.

- **Marines** review and sign the SOU during counseling.
- **Leaders** conduct the counseling and witness signature.
- **Commanders** direct SOU processing in specific circumstances and review compliance.
