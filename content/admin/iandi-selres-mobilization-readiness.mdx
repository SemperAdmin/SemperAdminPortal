## Mobilization Readiness Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 7 and Chapter 10, SELRES members must maintain mobilization readiness for potential activation to active duty during war or national emergency.

A mobilizable SELRES member must be ready in five readiness domains.

## Five Readiness Domains

### Medical Readiness

- Current PHA (annual)
- Class 1 or Class 2 dental status
- Current immunizations
- No deployment-limiting medical conditions
- Specific deployment medications and accommodations addressed

### Dental Readiness

- Class 1 or Class 2 dental status
- No deployment-blocking dental conditions
- Treatment current

### Administrative Readiness

- Current MCTFS data
- Current dependent data and DEERS enrollment
- Current SGLI election
- Current RED with valid contacts
- Member personal records current

### Training Readiness

- MOS qualification current
- Required training complete (SAPR, INFOSEC, etc.)
- PFT and CFT scores current
- Annual rifle qualification current
- Specific MOS training requirements met

### Family Readiness

- Family Care Plan if applicable
- Family briefed on mobilization possibility
- Family support resources identified

## Mobilization Authorities

Per 10 USC.

### Full Mobilization (12301(a))

Declaration of war or national emergency.

- All ready reserve members subject to recall
- Including IRR and Standby

### Partial Mobilization (12302)

Up to 1 million reservists for up to 24 months.

- SELRES and IRR primarily affected
- Specific to declared emergencies

### Presidential Reserve Call-Up (12304)

Up to 200,000 for up to 365 days.

- Specific to operational needs
- President orders

### Voluntary Active Duty (12301(d))

For specific missions.

- Member volunteers
- Specific operational needs

## Pre-Mobilization Screening

When unit alerted for mobilization.

### Step 1 - Receive Mobilization Alert

Unit receives notification.

- Mobilization timeline established
- Member roster identified
- Pre-mobilization screening initiated

### Step 2 - Screen Each Member

Per readiness domains.

- Medical screening at MTF
- Dental screening
- Administrative review
- Training validation
- Family readiness check

### Step 3 - Identify and Address Readiness Gaps

For members with gaps.

- Medical treatment
- Dental treatment
- Administrative updates
- Required training completion
- Family preparation

### Step 4 - Process Cleared Members

For members ready to mobilize.

- Mobilization orders processing
- Travel coordination
- Specific deployment processing

### Step 5 - Process Members Not Cleared

For members with significant gaps.

- Determination of mobilization possibility
- Specific deferment if applicable
- Coordinate with HQMC for unique cases

## Post-Mobilization Processing

When mobilized members return.

### Step 1 - Demobilization Processing

When member returns from active duty.

- Active duty status ends
- Drilling status begins
- Specific transition processing

### Step 2 - TAMP Coordination

Per 10 USC 1145<Citation source="10 USC 1145" />.

- 180 days of TAMP coverage
- Family covered during TAMP
- Coordinate continued healthcare

### Step 3 - Post-Deployment Assessments

Per DODI requirements.

- PDHA completion
- PDHRA at 90-180 days
- Mental health screening

### Step 4 - Transition Coordination

For members continuing service.

- Return to drilling SMCR
- Specific mobilization veteran considerations
- Reintegration support

## Common Mobilization Readiness Issues

### Medical Readiness Gaps

Members with deployment-limiting medical conditions.

- Coordinate treatment
- Specific waivers if applicable
- Possible deferment

### Dental Class 3 Status

Members blocking deployment due to dental.

- Expedited treatment
- Reserve Dental Care for deployment treatment
- Track to Class 1 or Class 2

### Training Gaps

Members missing required training.

- Identify gaps
- Coordinate training completion
- AT or specific training periods

### Administrative Gaps

Members with administrative issues.

- Update records
- Process required updates
- Coordinate dependent enrollment

### Family Care Plan Issues

Members without proper Family Care Plan.

- Coordinate Family Care Plan completion
- Brief member on requirement
- Specific accommodations if needed

## Family Care Plan

Per Marine Corps policy.

### When Required

- Single parent
- Dual-military spouse
- Specific other family situations

### Components

- Identification of family caregiver
- Care arrangements during mobilization
- Financial arrangements for family
- Specific contingency plans

### Documentation

- Family Care Plan documented and filed
- Reviewed periodically
- Updated as family situation changes

## Mobilization Documentation

For mobilization processing.

- Mobilization orders
- Pre-deployment health assessment (PDHA)
- Family Care Plan if required
- Member acknowledgment of mobilization
- Family notification documentation

## Coordination Touchpoints

Mobilization readiness touches.

- **MARFORRES G-3**: For mobilization planning
- **HQMC**: For policy and major actions
- **Local installation MTF**: For medical screening
- **Member's family**: For family coordination
- **PAC**: For specific pay processing
- **DOL VETS**: For USERRA support

## Quality Control on Mobilization Readiness

Common errors.

1. **Annual PHA missed.** Members not current on PHA.
2. **Dental status not tracked.** Members in Class 3 status not addressed.
3. **Training gaps not identified.** Required training not current.
4. **Family Care Plan missing.** Members requiring FCP without one.

## Related Roles

Mobilization readiness affects every SELRES member.

- **Marines** maintain medical, dental, training, and admin readiness.
- **Leaders** at units verify member readiness.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit mobilization readiness posture.
