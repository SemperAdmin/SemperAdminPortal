## Role and responsibility

The commander is responsible for the unit's training per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1. Marines throughout the unit perform required UTM functions. Functions assign to Marines in training management roles per MCRP 7-20A.5<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.5" /> chapter 2. The S-1 officer drafts role designations. The S-1 chief tracks designations against the UTM roster.

Source documents flow from MCRP 7-20A.5 (UTM roles and functions list), NAVMC 3500.37D (UTM training standards), the unit billet roster, and the published designation letters. Roles produce observable indicators of an effective UTM Program per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1. Outputs include the UTM roster in MCTIMS, the designation letter file, and the role performance audit.

Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(d), required UTM training completes prior to designation. The MARFOR Commander, MSC, and Subordinate Commander designate Marines in writing per paragraphs 4.b.(6)(b) and (c).

## Trigger events

- Inbound Marine assigned to a billet carrying a UTM role.
- Outbound Marine departs requiring designation transfer to successor.
- Annual designation refresh window opens.
- New MCRP 7-20A.5 release changes role definitions.
- Commander direction adds or removes UTM roles.
- IGMC UTM inspection notice requires current designation file.
- Role indicator failure triggers performance review.

## Processing workflow

1. Pull the UTM role list. Reference MCRP 7-20A.5<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.5" /> chapter 2 for the authoritative role list.
2. Map roles to billets. Identify which unit billets carry which UTM roles per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1.
3. Identify functions per role. Pull function list from MCRP 7-20A.5<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.5" /> chapter 2.
4. Capture observable indicators per role. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1, each role produces observable indicators of an effective UTM Program. Document the indicators per role.
5. Designate Marines in writing. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(b), the MARFOR Commander designates in writing.
6. Confirm prereq UTM training. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(d) and NAVMC 3500.37D<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.37D" />, training completes before designation.
7. Brief role expectations. The unit training manager briefs each designated Marine on functions and indicators.
8. Track role performance. Audit role performance against the indicators on a fixed cycle.
9. Refresh designations on personnel change. Update the UTM roster at every detach or billet change.
10. Reconcile UTM roster against MCTIMS. Cross-check the local roster against MCTIMS each month per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(a).

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTIMS UTM Module. Holds the UTM roster.
- Unit training folder. Local repository for designation letters and indicator audits.
- OMPF Field Folder. Holds copies of designation letters per Marine.

### Forms

- UTM Role Designation Letter. Local format per role.
- Role Performance Audit. Local format documenting indicator performance.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 documenting designation and training.

## Common pitfalls

- Roles assigned without prereq UTM training. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(d), training completes before designation. Confirm before signature.
- Functions assigned without observable indicators captured. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1, each role produces indicators. Document the indicators per role.
- Designation lapses on detach without successor. The role sits vacant. Designate at the outbound brief.
- UTM roster diverges from MCTIMS. The local roster is wrong. Reconcile each month per paragraph 4.b.(6)(a).
- Commander believes UTM functions sit only with the unit training manager. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1, Marines throughout the unit perform UTM functions.

## Decision points

- Role consolidation. The unit lacks personnel for full role coverage. The commander consolidates roles into fewer designations per MCRP 7-20A.5<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.5" /> chapter 2.
- Cross-billet assignments. A role spans multiple billets. The S-1 officer routes a multi-billet designation per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(b).
- Indicator failure response. A designated Marine fails the role indicators. The commander calls retraining, replacement, or removal per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1.

## Authority

This sub-page sits under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(b).1 (commander responsibility, Marines throughout unit perform functions, observable indicators per role) and paragraphs 4.b.(6)(a) through (d) (designation, written, prereq training). MCRP 7-20A.5<Citation source="MCRP 7-20A.5" /> chapter 2 lists the UTM roles. NAVMC 3500.37D<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.37D" /> sets the training standards prerequisite to designation. MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> covers the T-R framework supporting UTM. NAVMC 3500.3E<Citation source="NAVMC 3500.3E" /> T-R event 0170-OPER-2001 covers the 01XX training plan informed by UTM roles.
