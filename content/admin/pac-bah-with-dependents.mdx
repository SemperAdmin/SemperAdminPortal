## What Qualifies as a Dependent

Per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />, a dependent for BAH purposes includes.

### Spouse

- Legally married spouse
- Same-sex spouse (recognized in jurisdiction of marriage)
- Common-law spouse if recognized by state of legal residence
- Spouse legally separated may still qualify in specific circumstances

### Children

- Biological children under age 21 (or 23 if full-time student)
- Adopted children with final adoption
- Stepchildren in member's household
- Court-ordered custodial children
- Disabled adult children with continuing dependency

### Parents and Wards

- In specific circumstances per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />
- Requires specific qualifying conditions
- Less common than spouse and children

## When With-Dependents Rate Applies

A Marine receives BAH at the with-dependents rate when.

- Has at least one qualifying dependent
- Dependent resides with member at duty station (or eligible separate location)
- Member is not in government quarters
- Documentation establishes dependent status (NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with supporting docs)

## Documentation Required

PAC processes with-dependents BAH only with proper documentation.

### For Spouse

- Marriage certificate (original or certified copy, not photocopy)
- Spouse Social Security Number
- Completed NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />
- Spouse photo ID for ID card processing

### For Children

- Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
- Adoption decree (if applicable)
- Court custody order (if applicable)
- Child Social Security Number (or application receipt)
- Completed NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### For Disabled Adult Children

- Medical documentation of disability
- Documentation of continuing dependency
- Specific certifications per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />

Without proper documentation, the BAH transaction fails or creates audit issues.

## Performance Steps for With-Dependents BAH

### Step 1 - Validate Dependent Documentation

When member submits NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />.

- Verify all required supporting documents present
- Verify documents are originals or certified copies (not photocopies)
- Verify documents are properly executed
- Verify member identification on documents

### Step 2 - Verify Dependent Resides at Duty Station

The dependent must reside at the member's duty station for standard with-dependents BAH.

- Verify family residence address
- Verify the address matches the duty station zip code area
- For dependents at separate location, evaluate BAH-Diff eligibility

### Step 3 - Determine Effective Date

The BAH change effective date.

- Marriage: Date of marriage (not date of submission)
- Birth: Date of birth (not date of submission)
- Adoption: Date adoption finalized
- Other dependent: Per applicable triggering event

### Step 4 - Process UD/MIPS Transaction

Apply the BAH change.

- UD/MIPS dependency transaction with effective date
- BAH category change to with-dependents
- Verify rate updates to with-dependents rate for zip code

### Step 5 - Coordinate Member ID and DEERS

Direct member to RAPIDS facility.

- Spouse and children eligible for ID cards
- DEERS enrollment for TRICARE eligibility
- 30-day target for completion

### Step 6 - Verify EDFR and Member Communication

Confirm processing.

- Pull EDFR within 5 business days
- Verify BAH posts at with-dependents rate
- Brief member on effective date and amount

## Annual Dependency Revalidation

Per MARADMIN 661/19<Citation source="MARADMIN 661/19" />, the Marine Corps validates dependency annually.

- Each member's dependents reviewed
- Member submits or confirms current status
- Discrepancies trigger re-validation
- Failure to revalidate may stop BAH

## Common Scenarios

### Marriage Adds Dependent

Member marries. Has no other dependents.

- Was: Without-dependents rate
- Becomes: With-dependents rate (spouse only)
- Effective date: Date of marriage
- Documentation: Marriage certificate, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### Birth Adds First Child

Member already has spouse. Now has child.

- Was: With-dependents rate (spouse)
- Remains: With-dependents rate (no rate change for additional dependent within same category)
- Effective date: Date of birth (DEERS enrollment date for ID card processing)
- Documentation: Birth certificate, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### Divorce Removes Last Dependent

Member divorces. No children. No other dependents remaining.

- Was: With-dependents rate
- Becomes: Without-dependents rate
- Effective date: Date of divorce decree
- Documentation: Divorce decree, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### Divorce With Custodial Children

Member divorces. Member has custody of children.

- Was: With-dependents rate (spouse and children)
- Remains: With-dependents rate (children alone)
- Effective date: No change in BAH rate
- Documentation: Divorce decree, custody order, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### Member Adopts Dependent

Member adopts a child.

- Was: Without-dependents rate
- Becomes: With-dependents rate
- Effective date: Date adoption finalized
- Documentation: Adoption decree, child SSN, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

## Common Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Marriage processed but BAH not changed.** NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> submitted but BAH category transaction missed. Fix by submitting separate BAH change.
2. **Effective date wrong.** BAH effective on submission date instead of marriage or birth date. Causes retroactive pay issues. Fix by reversing and resubmitting with correct date.
3. **Dependent not in DEERS.** Dependent processed for BAH but not enrolled in DEERS. Coordinate with member for RAPIDS visit.
4. **Annual revalidation skipped.** MARADMIN 661/19<Citation source="MARADMIN 661/19" /> cycle not completed. BAH may stop. Process revalidation.
5. **Photocopy submitted as supporting document.** Marriage cert or birth cert not certified copy. Reject and request original.

## When With-Dependents BAH Stops

BAH stops or changes when.

- All dependents removed (divorce with no children, all children aging out)
- Member moves to government quarters
- Member separates or retires
- Dependency revalidation fails

## BAH-Diff Comparison

BAH-Diff is different from with-dependents BAH.

- BAH-Diff: Member has dependents not at duty station (separated locations)
- BAH-Diff is a fixed rate, not zip-code based
- Less common but applies in specific situations

## Documentation Standards

Original or certified copy required for.

- Marriage certificates
- Birth certificates
- Divorce decrees
- Adoption decrees
- Death certificates
- Custody orders

Photocopies do not satisfy MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" /> requirements. Reject and request properly certified version.

## Related Roles

With-dependents BAH affects every Marine with family.

- **Marines** submit dependency documents and verify BAH on LES.
- **Leaders** ensure Marines submit life event documents within 10 working days.
- **Commanders** support timely processing through proper routing and documentation.
