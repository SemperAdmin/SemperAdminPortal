## Why Medical Standards Matter

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6, Reserve members must maintain medical standards for.

- Continued drilling status
- Mobilization eligibility
- Active duty deployment
- Specific MOS qualification
- Career continuation

A member who fails medical standards may face restricted duty, transfer to IRR, or separation.

## Reserve Medical Categories

Per DODI 6025.19<Citation source="DODI 6025.19" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 6, members fall into specific medical readiness categories.

### Category 1 (Fully Ready)

- Member meets all medical standards
- No deployment-limiting conditions
- Current PHA, dental, immunizations
- Eligible for full deployment

### Category 2 (Partial)

- Member meets most standards
- Minor deployment-limiting conditions
- Specific work needed to reach Category 1

### Category 3 (Not Ready)

- Member has deployment-limiting medical conditions
- May not deploy without specific waivers
- Requires significant medical work

### Category 4 (Indeterminate)

- Medical readiness cannot be determined
- Often due to incomplete information
- Requires action to reach a category

## Performance Steps for Medical Readiness Tracking

### Step 1 - Track Each Member

Maintain medical readiness data per member.

- Last PHA date
- Last dental exam
- Current immunization status
- Current category

### Step 2 - Identify Members Approaching Currency

For members with expiring requirements.

- Schedule physicals
- Coordinate dental appointments
- Update immunizations

### Step 3 - Address Findings

For members with medical issues.

- Follow-up appointments
- Specialist referrals
- Specific treatment

### Step 4 - Coordinate with Medical Personnel

I&I admin coordinates with medical staff.

- Local installation MTF
- Reserve Health Readiness Program
- Specific medical providers

### Step 5 - Update MCTFS

After medical actions.

- Updated PHA dates
- Dental status updates
- Immunization records
- Category changes

## Common Medical Issues

### Failure to Complete PHA

Member misses scheduled PHA.

- Reschedule promptly
- Member's category may shift
- Coordinate completion

### Dental Issues

Member's dental status not deployment-ready.

- Coordinate dental appointments
- Address findings
- Track until ready

### Specific Conditions Limiting Duty

Member with conditions affecting duty.

- Coordinate with medical staff
- Specific waivers if applicable
- DES referral if permanent

### Mobilization Medical Issues

Member's medical status discovered during mobilization screening.

- Specific procedures during pre-mobilization
- Treatment or deferral
- Coordination with mobilization timeline

## Coordination Touchpoints

Medical readiness touches.

- **Local installation MTF**: For routine care
- **Reserve Health Readiness Program**: For specific reserve medical
- **Member's civilian providers**: For routine civilian care
- **Specialty providers**: For specific medical needs

## Related Roles

Medical readiness affects every Reserve member's career.

- **Marines** complete PHA, maintain dental readiness, address medical issues.
- **Leaders** track member medical readiness at the unit.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit medical readiness posture.
