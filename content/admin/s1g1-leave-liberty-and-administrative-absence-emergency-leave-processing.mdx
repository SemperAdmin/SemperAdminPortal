## Role and responsibility

The leave granting authority (typically the commander or designated by direction signer) authorizes emergency leave based on judgment of the circumstance and the Marine's desires per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.a. The S-1 chief processes the leave transaction. The S-1 clerk runs documentation and Red Cross coordination where applicable. The S-1 officer signs by direction on emergency leave authorization when delegated.

Source documents flow from family notifications, Red Cross messages, hospital reports, and the Marine's statement. Emergency leave transactions land in MOL and MCTFS. Outputs include the leave order, the MOL leave entry, the chargeable leave count, and the OMPF Field Folder leave tab entry.

Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.a, swift and sensitive action on emergency leave requests is essential. Most family emergencies are highly time-dependent. The leave granting authority is permitted to authorize emergency leave greater than 60 days without CMC (MMEA/MMOA) approval provided advance leave balance stays at or under 30 days per paragraph 12.b.

## Trigger events

- Death of a member of the Marine's or spouse's immediate family per paragraph 12.c(1) (father, mother, person in loco parentis, son, daughter, brother, sister, including step or half relationships).
- Return of the Marine contributes to the welfare of a dying immediate family member per paragraph 12.c(2).
- Serious illness or injury of an immediate family member placing important responsibilities on the Marine per paragraph 12.c(3).
- Severe or unusual hardship on the Marine, household, or immediate family per paragraph 12.c(4).
- Marine who is a non-US citizen is in the final stages of US citizenship processing per paragraph 12.c(5).
- Red Cross message arrives requiring immediate response.
- Extension request received on previously authorized emergency leave.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the request. Pull the emergency leave request, supporting documentation, and any Red Cross message. Cross-check EDIPI and identification of the family member. The S-1 clerk runs receipt.
2. Validate the qualifying circumstance. Match against paragraph 12.c(1) through 12.c(5) per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2.
3. Verify loco parentis status. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.d, loco parentis normally requires a continuous 5-year period prior to the Marine's initial entry into the Marine Corps. Final determination rests with the commander.
4. Route for commander authorization. The leave granting authority makes the call based on circumstance and Marine's desires per paragraph 12.a.
5. Process Red Cross verification. Coordinate when in-person family verification is impractical.
6. Cut the leave transaction. Enter in MOL with start date, projected end date, and emergency leave designation.
7. Coordinate travel. Work with the unit for any TDY travel authorization or chargeable leave travel.
8. Track advance leave balance. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.b, leave greater than 60 days is permitted without CMC approval only if advance leave balance does not exceed 30 days.
9. Process extensions. Per paragraph 12.b, leave granting authorities authorize emergency leave extensions.
10. Document in MCTFS. Cut the leave transaction in MCTFS through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> leave transaction format.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MOL. Self-service leave module captures the leave request and order.
- MCTFS. Holds the master leave balance and pay state.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts leave transactions with KSDs.
- American Red Cross Emergency Communication Service. Provides verified family emergency messages.

### Forms

- NAVMC 10274 Application for Leave or Liberty. Standard leave request.
- Red Cross Emergency Communication. Provides verified emergency message routing.
- Leave Order. Generated in MOL.

## Common pitfalls

- Emergency leave denied for circumstance outside paragraphs 12.c(1) through 12.c(5). The Marine should resubmit under annual leave or special leave categories. The S-1 chief routes per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.c.
- Loco parentis status not verified against the 5-year continuous standard. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.d, the commander makes final determination. Document the basis.
- Emergency leave extension authorized without verifying 30-day advance leave cap. Per paragraph 12.b, leave exceeding 60 days without CMC approval requires advance leave balance at or under 30 days.
- Red Cross verification waited on before authorization. Per paragraph 12.a, swift action is essential. The commander is permitted to authorize before verification when circumstance is clear.
- Leave not entered in MCTFS within reasonable time. Pay impact emerges. Cut the MCTFS transaction within 1 working day of authorization per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />.

## Decision points

- Leave granting authority decision. The commander or designee judges based on circumstance and Marine's desires per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 12.a. Document the rationale.
- Loco parentis determination. The 5-year continuous standard is presumptive. The commander is permitted to determine status outside the standard with documented basis per paragraph 12.d.
- Extension versus new request. The Marine needs more time. The leave granting authority authorizes extension per paragraph 12.b within the 30-day advance leave cap, or routes to CMC for cases exceeding the cap.

## Authority
