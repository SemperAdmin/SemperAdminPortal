## Role and responsibility

The deployed commander authorizes R&R Leave per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 13. The S-1 chief processes the R&R Leave order and coordinates with the IPAC for travel arrangements. The S-1 officer signs by direction on routine R&R cases. The HHQ casualty or operations branch designates qualifying operations.

Source documents flow from the deployment order, the qualifying operation designation, the Marine's deployment duration, and the unit R&R quota. R&R Leave transactions land in MOL and MCTFS. Outputs include the R&R Leave order, the travel coordination, the chargeable status entry, and the return-to-deployment record.

Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 13 (Rest and Recuperation Leave Programs), R&R Leave is authorized for Marines deployed to qualifying operations. Per paragraph 14 (Special R&R Leave Programs), Special R&R programs apply additional rules per qualifying operation. Per paragraph 16 (Leave Travel In Connection With), the travel coordination follows.

## Trigger events

- Marine deploys to a qualifying R&R operation.
- Marine reaches the deployment duration threshold for R&R eligibility.
- HHQ designates a new qualifying operation.
- Special R&R Leave Program authorized per paragraph 14.
- R&R Leave travel order required for coordinated travel.
- Return-to-deployment date approaches.
- Annual R&R quota cycle opens.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify eligibility. Pull the Marine's deployment record and validate qualifying operation status per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 13.
2. Confirm qualifying operation designation. Pull HHQ designation per the order rules.
3. Validate deployment duration. Confirm the Marine meets the duration threshold per paragraph 13.
4. Determine R&R or Special R&R. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 14, Special R&R applies in specific circumstances. The deployed commander calls.
5. Coordinate travel arrangements. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 16 (Leave Travel In Connection With), arrange travel through the unit travel coordinator.
6. Process the R&R Leave order. The S-1 chief cuts the leave order per paragraph 13.
7. Track chargeable status. R&R chargeable rules differ from standard annual leave. Apply per paragraph 13.
8. Process return-to-deployment timing. Coordinate with the deployed unit on return date.
9. Document R&R Leave in MCTFS. Submit through UD-MIPS per MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" /> leave transaction format.
10. File R&R Leave packet. Stage in the OMPF Field Folder.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTFS. Holds R&R Leave balance and pay state.
- MOL. Captures R&R Leave order.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts the R&R Leave transaction.
- DTS. Coordinates R&R travel where applicable.

### Forms

- NAVMC 10274 Application for Leave or Liberty. R&R Leave request format.
- R&R Travel Order. Generated for travel coordination per Chapter 2 paragraph 16.
- HHQ Qualifying Operation Designation. Source for eligibility.

## Common pitfalls

- R&R Leave authorized without qualifying operation designation. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraph 13, the operation designation is required.
- Special R&R applied without paragraph 14 basis. Per paragraph 14, Special R&R requires specific authorization.
- Travel arrangements skipped. R&R travel requires coordination per Chapter 2 paragraph 16. Process before the Marine departs.
- Chargeable status misapplied. R&R chargeable rules differ. Apply per paragraph 13.
- Return-to-deployment date not enforced. The Marine extends beyond the R&R window. The deployed commander tracks.

## Decision points

- R&R versus Special R&R selection. Per MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 2 paragraphs 13 and 14, Special R&R applies in specific circumstances. The deployed commander selects.
- Travel coordination path. Per Chapter 2 paragraph 16, travel runs through the unit travel coordinator. Coordinate timing with deployment cycle.
- R&R extension. The Marine requests extension. The deployed commander authorizes per paragraph 13 or routes through HHQ per the order rules.
