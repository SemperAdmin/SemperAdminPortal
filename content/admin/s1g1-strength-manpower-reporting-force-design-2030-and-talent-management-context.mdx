## Role and responsibility

The 0170 Manpower Officer translates Force Design 2030 structural decisions and Talent Management 2030 retention initiatives into local manpower posture. The G-1 manpower officer at MEF level coordinates structure-level FD2030 transitions with HQMC Total Force Structure Division. The S-1 chief tracks deactivation timelines, MOS divestiture impacts on the unit T-O, and retention program enrollments under Talent Management 2030.

Source documents flow from the CMC Force Design 2030 Report (Phase I 2020 and Phase II 2022 updates), the Talent Management 2030 publication (November 2021) and the March 2023 update, MARADMINs implementing FD2030 unit deactivations and MOS divestitures, and the HQMC manpower decisions briefing the structure. Outputs include the unit deactivation timeline, the MOS divestiture impact assessment, the talent management retention initiative log, and the briefing slides to the commander on manpower posture against FD2030.

This sub-page is doctrinal and contextual. It anchors the strategic posture of the Marine Corps manpower program and frames decisions in the BIC management, TOECR, and MMEA coordination sub-pages.

## Trigger events

- HQMC announces a FD2030 unit deactivation or reactivation impacting the unit.
- MARADMIN announces a MOS divestiture or new MOS creation under FD2030.
- Talent Management 2030 program update changes retention initiative rules.
- Annual manpower posture briefing window opens with the commander.
- HQMC MMEA monitor projection shifts the unit MOS or grade mix.

## Force Design 2030 manpower implications

Force Design 2030 drove the divestiture of tank battalions, three reductions in infantry battalions (from 24 to 21), reduction in artillery cannon battalions, reduction in tiltrotor squadrons, and elimination of bridging companies and assault amphibian battalions. FD2030 added Marine Littoral Regiments (MLRs), additional rocket artillery batteries, and unmanned aircraft systems squadrons.

The manpower implications run across:

- BIC structure changes. Divested unit BICs delete from the T-O. New unit BICs add. The TOECR submission process under MCO 5311.1E routes the structure change.
- MOS divestiture and lateral move. Divested MOSs (tank crewman 1812, bridging 1371 elements, assault amphibian crewman 1833) lateral-move to retained MOSs. The S-1 chief tracks the lateral-move window and the MMEA monitor projection.
- Grade rebalancing. Force Design 2030 favors a higher senior NCO grade mix in the infantry battalion and the MLR. Grade gaps appear in the unit T-O when senior grade fills lag the structure.
- Geographic redistribution. MLR stand-up at MCBH Kaneohe, 3d MLR formation, and III MEF restructuring drive PCS volume.

## Talent Management 2030 retention implications

Talent Management 2030 launched career retention initiatives reshaping the manning roster. The S-1 chief tracks enrollment and impact across:

- Selective Retention Bonus (SRB) program rebalancing by MOS and grade.
- Career Designation reform for first-term enlisted Marines.
- Officer career path flexibility under the Officer Promotion and Retention modernization initiative.
- Lateral entry program for technical MOS.
- Permeability between Active Component, Reserve Component, and civilian workforce.
- Talent Marketplace pilot for assignment matching.

## Processing workflow

1. Pull the current FD2030 implementation MARADMIN. The S-1 chief tracks the MARADMIN library for FD2030 unit deactivations and MOS divestitures impacting the unit.
2. Pull the Talent Management 2030 update. The G-1 manpower officer pulls the current TM2030 update and tracks retention initiative changes.
3. Build the manpower posture brief. The S-1 chief assembles the unit deactivation timeline, the MOS divestiture impact, the lateral-move window, and the retention initiative log.
4. Brief the commander. The S-1 officer briefs the commander on the FD2030 posture and the TM2030 retention initiative status on a fixed cycle.
5. Coordinate with the operations officer. Operations endorses the FD2030 implementation timeline against the unit mission and the deployment cycle.
6. Coordinate with the manpower monitor. The 0170 officer engages MMEA on the MOS divestiture impact and the retention initiative enrollment.
7. Update the manning roster. Refresh the BIC roster with the FD2030 structure changes and the retention initiative enrollments.
8. Track the talent marketplace pilot. The S-1 chief tracks assignment matching outcomes against the unit fill and the MMEA monitor projection.

## Common pitfalls

- FD2030 deactivation date missed in the manpower brief. The commander runs without visibility on the structure change. Pull the FD2030 MARADMIN at every briefing cycle.
- MOS divestiture lateral-move window missed. Marines in divested MOSs miss the lateral-move window and face administrative separation. Track the lateral-move window per the MARADMIN and the MMEA monitor projection.
- TM2030 retention initiative enrollment lapsed. Eligible Marines miss the SRB or the Talent Marketplace pilot. Pull the eligibility list at every reenlistment cycle.
- Grade rebalancing impact unflagged. Senior grade gaps under FD2030 structure go unreported. Surface grade gaps at the BIC reconciliation cycle.
- Manpower posture brief skipped. The commander runs without visibility on FD2030 posture. Brief on a fixed cycle (quarterly at minimum) per the unit reporting cadence.

## Decision points

- Lateral-move acceptance for divested MOS. The Marine receives a lateral-move offer under FD2030. The S-1 chief and the monitor coordinate the timing and the receiving unit.
- Career Designation timing for first-term enlisted. The TM2030 reform shifts the Career Designation decision earlier. The S-1 officer briefs the first-term Marines on the new decision window.
- Talent Marketplace pilot participation. The 0170 officer endorses the Marine talent marketplace registration when the BIC and the monitor projection align.
