## Role and responsibility

S-1 admin specialists frame Marine pay debt within the broader DoD debt management policy. The S-1 chief understands the statutory framework, the key definitions, and the responsibilities of DFAS, the Defense Office of Hearings and Appeals (DOHA), and the Treasury Offset Program. The IPAC pay clerk applies the right framework based on the debt type (military pay debt, transportation debt, commissary debt, military exchange debt, etc.). The S-1 officer signs by direction on the debt management framework brief to the commander.

Source documents flow from the DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 general provisions, the Chapter Definitions section, the Debt Collection Improvement Act of 1996 statutory framework, the Federal Claims Collection Standards (31 CFR Parts 900-904), the DoD Component debt management responsibility matrix, and the DFAS debt management operating procedures. Outputs include the debt classification, the routing decision (military pay versus non-pay debt), the framework brief, and the unit debt posture report.

DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 establishes the scope, the statutory authority, and the responsibilities. It does not contain the operational procedures. The operational procedures sit in Chapters 2 through 7. The Marine pay-specific procedures sit in Vol 7A Chapter 50.

## Trigger events

- New S-1 chief assumes duties and requires the debt management framework brief.
- New IPAC pay clerk arrives and requires DoD debt framework training.
- Marine Corps Comptroller publishes a debt management framework guidance memorandum.

- Commander requests the debt management posture brief.

## Key statutory framework

DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 operationalizes the following statutory framework at the DoD level:

- Debt Collection Improvement Act of 1996 (DCIA). Federal-wide debt collection reform statute.
- 31 USC 3711 Collection and Compromise. Federal claims collection authority.
- 31 USC 3716 Administrative Offset. Treasury offset authority for federal payments.
- 31 USC 3717 Interest and Penalty on Claims. Interest, penalty, and administrative cost framework.
- 31 USC 3719 Reports on Debt Collection Activities. Annual reporting requirement.
- 31 USC 3720A Tax Refund Offset. Federal tax refund offset authority.
- 31 USC 3720D Administrative Wage Garnishment. Post-separation wage garnishment.
- 5 USC 5514 Federal Salary Offset. Current federal employee salary offset.
- 5 USC 5584 Civilian Employee Waiver. Civilian employee waiver authority.
- 10 USC 2774 Military Member Waiver. Military member waiver authority.
- 10 USC 6161 Navy and Marine Corps Enlisted Remission. Enlisted remission authority.
- 31 CFR Parts 900-904 Federal Claims Collection Standards. Federal collection regulations.

## Key responsibilities

DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 assigns the following key responsibilities:

- Office of the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller). DoD-wide debt management policy.
- Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS). Operational debt collection across DoD Components.
- DoD Components (Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, Space Force, Coast Guard). Component-specific debt collection support and waiver remission recommendations.
- Defense Office of Hearings and Appeals (DOHA). Independent hearing officer for due process hearings under DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 4.
- Treasury Offset Program (TOP). Federal payment offset for delinquent debt.
- Treasury Cross-Servicing. Long-term collection of delinquent federal debt.

## Debt classification

DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 and the Definitions section classify DoD debt by type:

- Military pay debt. Erroneous payment of pay and allowances. Routes through DoDFMR Vol 7A Chapter 50 for the waiver remission path.
- Travel debt. Travel advance, travel claim rejection. Routes through DFAS Travel.
- Transportation debt. Household goods overage, transportation entitlement violation.
- Commissary debt. Commissary check return, commissary credit account.
- Military exchange debt. Exchange credit account, exchange returned check.
- Contractor debt. Vendor overpayment, contract dispute. Routes through DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 5.
- Other federal debt. Per the specific framework applicable to the debt type.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the debt type at debt creation. The S-1 chief or IPAC pay clerk classifies the debt against the DoDFMR Vol 16 Chapter 1 classification framework.
2. Route the debt to the correct framework. Military pay debt routes through Vol 7A Chapter 50. Non-pay debt routes through Vol 16 Chapter 2 due process and Chapter 3 collection mechanisms.
3. Apply the statutory framework. The IPAC pay clerk applies the right statutory authority based on the debt type and the Marine status.
4. Coordinate with DFAS Indianapolis. DFAS owns the operational debt collection across the DoD Components.
5. Coordinate with DOHA for hearings. The Marine elects a hearing on the debt validity. The hearing routes through DOHA per DoDFMR Vol 16<Citation source="DoDFMR Vol 16" /> Chapter 4.
6. Track the debt posture. The S-1 chief maintains the unit debt posture report and surfaces aging debt to the commander on a fixed cycle.
7. Brief the commander. The S-1 officer briefs the commander on the debt management posture, the active waiver remission cases, the salary offset state, and the Treasury referral pending.

## Common pitfalls

- Debt routed to the wrong framework. Military pay debt routed through Vol 16 Chapter 4 instead of Vol 7A Chapter 50. The IPAC pay clerk classifies the debt at creation per DoDFMR Vol 16<Citation source="DoDFMR Vol 16" /> Chapter 1.
- Statutory authority confused between 10 USC 2774 and 10 USC 6161. 10 USC 2774 covers waiver on government error. 10 USC 6161 covers remission on hardship for enlisted Marines on active duty. The S-1 chief identifies the authority at package build.
- DOHA hearing right overlooked. The Marine elects a hearing under DoDFMR Vol 16<Citation source="DoDFMR Vol 16" /> Chapter 4. The S-1 chief routes the hearing request through DFAS to DOHA.
- DoD Component responsibility confused with DFAS responsibility. The Marine Corps Comptroller supports the debt collection and recommends waivers. DFAS operationalizes the collection and adjudicates the application.

## Decision points

- Military pay debt versus other federal debt. The classification drives the framework. Military pay debt routes through Vol 7A Chapter 50 waiver remission. Other debt routes through Vol 16 Chapter 4.
- DOHA hearing election. The Marine has the right to a DOHA hearing under DoDFMR Vol 16<Citation source="DoDFMR Vol 16" /> Chapter 4. The S-1 chief counsels the Marine on the hearing option versus the waiver remission application.
