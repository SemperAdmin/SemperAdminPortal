## Role and responsibility

The Component Program Manager (CPM) at HQMC P&R-RFF owns Marine Corps GTCCP execution per MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" />. The Agency Program Coordinator (APC) runs the unit GTCCP per MCO 4600.40C Enclosure 2 and DoD GTCC Regulations Section 0411 (Agency Program Coordinator Duties). The CBA Manager runs the unit Centrally Billed Account program per Section 040205 and Annex 11. The Authorizing Official inside DTS verifies card activation before approval per DoD DTS Regulations section 030401.B.9. The unit commander enforces compliance per Section 040103, takes corrective action, and reports misuse, abuse, or fraud through the chain of command and through the personnel security system (DISS). The cardholder pays undisputed Individually Billed Account (IBA) charges by the due date per Section 040206.

Source documents flow from the IBA application package per Section 040901, the CBA application package per Section 040903, the APC appointment letter per Annex 10, the CPM appointment letter per Annex 9, the CBA appointment letter per Annex 11, the Read Only Access template per Annex 12, the GTCC vendor IBA Listing Report, the Travel Card Management Reports per Section 0414, the Visa Intellilink case data per MARADMIN 115/19<Citation source="MARADMIN 115/19" />, the delinquency timeline data per Annexes 2 and 3, the sample misuse memoranda per Annexes 4 through 7, and the JTR<Citation source="JTR" /> for authorized travel expenses. GTCCP outputs include the issued IBA, the activated CBA, the trained cardholder, the cleared delinquent account, the misuse case file, the Visa Intellilink monthly review, the I through X reports per MCO 4600.40C Enclosure 2, and the IGMC GTCCP FA audit trail.

Per MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" /> paragraph 4 (Execution), the order operationalizes DoD GTCC policy. Per Enclosure 2 GTCCP Management Procedures, Appendices A through C cover Individually Billed Account procedures, Centrally Billed Account procedures, and reporting. Per DoD GTCC Regulations Section 040101 (Overview), the GTCC pays for all costs related to official government travel except where exempt under Section 0406.

## Trigger events

- Inbound traveler arrives without an IBA.
- New CPM or APC appointment cycle opens.
- Annual APC and CPM refresher training window opens.
- Pre-suspension notification arrives at 45 days past billing.
- IBA suspension notification arrives at 61 days past billing.
- 91-day or 121-day delinquency notification triggers commander action.
- Monthly Visa Intellilink case review window opens.
- Quarterly GTCCP Program Review report cycle opens per MCO 4600.40C Enclosure 1 Chapter 1 paragraph 1.b.
- Annual Root Cause Analysis with Performance Improvement Plan due per paragraph 2.
- IGMC GTCCP FA inspection or Defense Travel System FA inspection notice arrives.
- Separation, retirement, or transfer triggers IBA closure or hierarchy transfer.

## Processing workflow

1. Appoint the CPM and APCs. Per DoD GTCC Regulations Annex 9, the Department of the Navy nominates the CPM. Per Annex 10, the CPM appoints the APC at Hierarchy Level (HL) 3 and HL4. The unit commander signs the APC appointment letter.
2. Process IBA and CBA applications. Per Section 0409 and Section 040301 (IBAs), the APC processes IBA applications inside the GTCC vendor Electronic Access System (EAS). Per Section 040302 (CBAs) and Section 040903, the CBA Manager processes CBA applications. Per 10 U.S.C. 2784a and Section 041001, new IBA applicants undergo a credit-score check. Per Section 041002, non-qualifying applicants receive a Restricted card.
3. Train APCs, CPMs, and cardholders. Per Section 0408, the APC, CPM, and IBA cardholders complete the required training before card issuance. Per Section 040802, IBA cardholder training pairs with Travel Card 101.
4. Enforce mandatory use. Per Section 040501 (Requirement for Use) and the Travel and Transportation Reform Act of 1998 (Public Law 105-264), DoD personnel use the GTCC for official travel except where exempt under Section 0406. Per Section 040504, Leave in Conjunction with Official Travel (LICWO) holds the IBA out of personal use status.
5. Run delinquency management. Per Section 0413 and Annexes 2 and 3, the APC processes pre-suspension at 45 days, suspension at 61 days, 91-day notification, 121-day notification, and charge-off per Section 041305. The APC drafts the 61-day, 91-day, and 121-day memoranda per Annexes 4 through 6.
6. Run Visa Intellilink case review. Per MARADMIN 115/19<Citation source="MARADMIN 115/19" /> and DoD GTCC Regulations Section 041502, HQMC CPM and HL3 and HL4 APCs review GTCC transactions in Visa Intellilink monthly. Cases route through HL4 to HL3 to CPM for resolution.
7. Process misuse, abuse, and fraud. Per Section 041005 and Annex 7, the APC drafts a suspected misuse memorandum. The commander notifies the responsible personnel security system (DISS) per Section 040103. Public Law 112-194 (Government Charge Card Abuse Prevention Act of 2012, Annex 8) sets the parent statute.
8. Process split disbursement and reimbursement. Per Section 041007 and Section 0412, the cardholder submits the DTS voucher with split disbursement routing IBA charges to the GTCC vendor. Per Section 041201, reimbursement runs within the JTR<Citation source="JTR" /> and DoD FMR Vol 9 timelines.
9. Submit required reports. Per MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" /> Enclosure 1 Chapter 1 paragraph 1.b and paragraph 2 and Enclosure 2 Appendix B paragraph 4.b(1) and Appendix C paragraph 23.b(3), submit Report I (Program Review), Report II (Root Cause Analysis with PIP), Report III (IBA Listing Report), Reports IV through IX (aging and delinquency), and Report X (CBA Aging Analysis Report).
10. Coordinate IBA closure on separation. Per Section 041106 and MCO 4650.39A<Citation source="MCO 4650.39A" /> paragraph 4.b.(6)(d), the APC closes the IBA on separation. The Debt Management Monitor clears DTS-side debt before the cancellation.

## Common pitfalls

- APC appointment letter outside Annex 10 format. Per Annex 10, the appointment letter template is required. Deviations break the appointment.
- Credit-score check skipped on new IBA. Per Section 041001 and 10 U.S.C. 2784a, the credit-score check runs on every new IBA applicant. Without the check, the applicant receives a Restricted card per Section 041002.
- Visa Intellilink case review skipped. Per MARADMIN 115/19, HL3 and HL4 APCs review Visa Intellilink monthly. Skipping the review breaks the misuse-detection cycle.
- Mandatory-use rule not enforced. Per Section 0405 and Public Law 105-264, the GTCC pays for official travel expenses. Non-GTCC payment outside exemptions breaks the rule.
- Misuse reporting not routed through DISS. Per Section 040103, the commander updates the personnel security system with the final disciplinary action.
- Split disbursement skipped on the voucher. Per Section 041007 and DTS Regs section 030604, the cardholder routes IBA charges to the GTCC vendor through split disbursement. Without the split, the IBA falls delinquent.
- Delinquency notifications cut outside the Annex format. Per Annexes 4 through 6, the memoranda follow the required template.
- IBA not closed on separation. Per Section 041106, the APC closes the account. A stale IBA stays exposed to misuse.

## Decision points

- Standard IBA versus Restricted card. Per Section 041001 and 041002, the applicant authorization for the credit-score check drives the card type.
- CBA versus IBA payment. Per Section 040302, the CBA pays for centrally arranged transportation and selected lodging. The IBA pays for everything else.
- Mission-Critical Travel status during delinquency. Per Section 041306, the unit commander designates Mission-Critical Travel to hold suspension on otherwise-delinquent accounts during TDY.
- PCS Status hold. Per Section 041307, PCS status holds the IBA out of suspension during the PCS travel window.
- Exemption application. Per Section 0406, the exemption flows from the GSA list, the DoD list, the expenses list, or additional component-level exemptions. The APC documents the exemption in the cardholder file.
