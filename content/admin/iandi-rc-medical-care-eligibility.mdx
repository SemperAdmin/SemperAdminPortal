## Reserve Medical Care Categories

Per MCRAMM Chapter 6 and DoDI 1241.01<Citation source="DoDI 1241.01" />, Reserve members access military medical care based on status.

### Drilling SMCR Member

During drill weekends and AT.

- Limited military medical care
- Care for service-incurred or aggravated injuries
- LOD coverage if injured during qualifying duty
- TRS for routine care if enrolled

### Mobilized Reserve Member

On active duty over 30 days.

- Full TRICARE Prime active duty
- Family TRICARE Prime active duty
- Comprehensive coverage during mobilization
- TAMP coverage after demobilization

### Active Reserve Member

On full-time active duty.

- Same as active duty
- Full TRICARE Prime
- Family TRICARE Prime
- All active duty benefits

### IRR Member

Generally limited.

- Specific TRICARE coverage during muster duty
- Limited access otherwise
- Specific provisions per program

### Standby Reserve Member

Generally limited.

- Specific access per program
- Most rely on civilian insurance

### Retired Reserve Member

Per retirement status.

- Awaiting Pay: TRR if enrolled
- Receiving Pay: TRICARE Select retiree or other
- Age 65+: TRICARE for Life with Medicare

## Line of Duty (LOD) Coverage

Critical for drilling reservists. Detailed in its own page.

- LOD-positive injuries qualify for military medical care
- Treatment continues until determined fit or unfit
- Specific LOD provisions per MCRAMM Chapter 6

## TRICARE Reserve Select (TRS)

For drilling SMCR. Detailed in its own page.

- Premium-based coverage
- Comprehensive medical coverage
- Family coverage available
- Member-paid premium

## TRICARE Active Duty Coverage During Mobilization

When mobilized, full TRICARE Prime applies.

- Member: TRICARE Prime active duty
- Family: TRICARE Prime active duty family
- No premium during mobilization
- Comprehensive coverage

## Common Coverage Issues

### Drilling Member with Routine Health Need

Member with routine health need (not service-related).

- TRS coverage if enrolled
- Civilian insurance otherwise
- Limited military medical access for routine

### Drilling Member with Service-Related Injury

Member injured during drill.

- LOD investigation
- Military medical care for LOD-positive condition
- Coordinate treatment

### Mobilized Member Family Coverage

Family coverage during member mobilization.

- Family TRICARE Prime active duty
- TRS family conversion if applicable
- Coordinate with member's family

### Returning from Mobilization

Member completing mobilization.

- TAMP coverage 180 days
- Transition planning needed
- Coordinate post-TAMP coverage

### IRR Member with Medical Issue

IRR member contacts I&I for medical issue.

- Limited military medical access
- Direct to civilian provider typically
- Specific exceptions per LOD or specific program

## Reserve Health Readiness Program

Per MCRAMM, the Reserve Health Readiness Program supports member access to medical care for readiness purposes.

### Program Components

- Coordination of medical readiness exams
- Specific support for PHA completion
- Specific reimbursement for civilian medical care for readiness

### Program Access

- Coordinate through I&I or MTF
- Specific eligibility per program
- Updated provisions per current MARADMIN

## Family Coverage Coordination

Family coverage varies by member status.

| Member Status | Family Coverage |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Drilling SMCR | TRS family if enrolled, civilian otherwise |
| Mobilized | TRICARE Prime active duty family |
| Active Reserve | TRICARE Prime active duty family |
| IRR | Limited or none |
| Retired Reserve Awaiting Pay | TRR family if enrolled |
| Retired Reserve Receiving Pay | TRICARE retiree family |

I&I admin coordinates family coverage transitions during mobilization and demobilization.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Reserve medical care coordination touches.

- **Local installation MTF**: For Reserve member access
- **TRICARE Regional Office**: For TRICARE plan administration
- **Reserve Health Readiness Program**: For specific reserve provisions
- **Member's civilian providers**: For routine civilian care
- **Member's family**: For family coverage coordination

## Quality Control on Coverage

Common errors.

1. **Wrong eligibility cited.** Member status affects coverage. Verify current status.
2. **TRS enrollment not coordinated.** Members without coverage may need TRS.
3. **Mobilization coverage transitions missed.** Family may lose coverage during mobilization.
4. **TAMP coverage not coordinated.** Members returning from mobilization need TAMP awareness.

## Related Roles

Reserve medical care coordination affects every member.

- **Marines** access medical care per their current status.
- **Leaders** brief members on medical care options.
- **Commanders** at I&I coordinate medical care access for unit members.
