## Role and responsibility

The unit training manager owns the HRT risk assessment process. The unit safety officer chops every HRT risk assessment per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />. The commander approves residual risk above the unit threshold. The S-1 chief tracks assessment uploads against MCTIMS calendar entries.

Source documents flow from the T-R event standard, the safety control list from the T-R Manual references, the unit risk worksheet, and the unit safety officer chop. Risk assessment documents land in MCTIMS as required event documentation per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(f). Outputs include the certified risk assessment, the safety brief to participants, and the post-event AAR safety findings.

MCAAT and the IGMC UTM functional area inspectors check MCTIMS for risk assessment documents on every HRT event. A required event without risk assessment documents triggers a finding under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(f). HRT executed without commander approval of residual risk above threshold triggers a finding under MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.

## Trigger events

- T-R event meets the HRT definition under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(e).
- Live fire event scheduled regardless of HRT status.
- Mountain warfare, cold weather, or hot weather training scheduled.
- Helicopter operations, fast rope, or rappel training scheduled.
- Combat marksmanship beyond standard range scheduled.
- Combatives or hand-to-hand training scheduled.
- Water survival or amphibious training scheduled.

## Processing workflow

1. Identify the HRT designation. Per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(e), HRT is basic or advanced training essential for combat that exposes Marines and trainers to risk of death or permanent disability despite proper safety controls. The unit training manager flags the event.
2. Pull the T-R event standard. Pull condition, standard, and references including required safety controls per MCO P3500.72A<Citation source="MCO P3500.72A" /> paragraph 4002.1.
3. Conduct the risk assessment. Run the assessment using the Operational Risk Management process per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.
4. Document on the unit risk worksheet. Capture hazards, severity, probability, controls, and residual risk per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.
5. Route through the unit safety officer. Capture the safety officer chop on the worksheet.
6. Obtain commander approval where required. The commander approves residual risk above the unit threshold per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.
7. Upload to MCTIMS. Attach the risk assessment to the MCTIMS calendar event per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(f).
8. Brief safety controls. The senior trainer briefs participants on hazards and controls before execution.
9. Capture safety findings in AAR. The post-event AAR records any control failures or near-misses for safety officer review.
10. File the certified risk assessment. Stage in the unit safety folder and the MCTIMS event entry.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- MCTIMS UTM Module. Holds the calendar event with risk assessment documents per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(f).
- Unit safety folder. Local repository for risk assessments and safety findings.

### Forms

- Unit Risk Worksheet. Local form documenting hazards, controls, and residual risk per MCO 5100.29C.
- Safety Officer Chop Sheet. Documents the safety officer review and signature.
- Commander Risk Acceptance Memo. Required where residual risk exceeds unit threshold.

## Common pitfalls

- HRT executed without risk assessment in MCTIMS. MCAAT writes the finding under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.c.(5)(f). Upload the assessment before execution.
- Risk assessment without safety officer chop. The assessment lacks safety review. Route through the safety officer per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.
- Residual risk above threshold accepted without commander approval. The unit acts outside risk acceptance authority. Route to the commander per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.
- HRT designation skipped on a qualifying event. The event runs without HRT controls. The unit training manager flags every qualifying event per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(e).
- Safety brief skipped before execution. Participants enter without hazard awareness. Brief at every HRT event regardless of repetition.

## Decision points

- HRT designation call. The event is borderline against the HRT definition. The unit training manager calls HRT when risk of death or permanent disability persists despite controls per MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraph 4.a.(2)(e).
- Residual risk acceptance level. The risk worksheet shows residual risk above the unit threshold. Route to the commander per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" /> for acceptance.
- Cancellation versus mitigation. Conditions degrade between assessment and execution. The senior trainer cancels or applies additional controls per MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" />.

## Authority

This sub-page sits under MCO 1553.3C<Citation source="MCO 1553.3C" /> paragraphs 4.a.(2)(e) (HRT definition), 4.b.(6)(j) (risk assessment for required events), and 4.c.(5)(f) (risk assessment in MCTIMS).
