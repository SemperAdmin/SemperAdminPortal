## What BAH Is

Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) is monthly cash payment to military members not residing in government quarters. Per DOD FMR 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 26 and MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />, BAH offsets housing costs in the local civilian market.

BAH is one of the largest pay categories. It also generates the most pay audit findings. PAC processes BAH actions daily.

## Why BAH Is Audit Critical

The Marine Corps audit risk includes $171M in pay-related findings, with BAH the largest single category. Common BAH audit issues.

- Members drawing BAH at wrong rate (with vs without dependents)
- BAH not stopped when member moved to government quarters
- Wrong zip code driving wrong rate
- Dependent changes not processed timely
- BAH not coordinated with PCS orders

PAC accuracy on BAH directly affects the unit's audit posture.

## BAH Categories

Per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />, BAH has three primary categories.

### BAH-With Dependents

Member with dependents at the duty station receives.

- BAH at the with-dependents rate for the duty station zip code
- Larger amount reflecting family housing costs
- Most common category for married Marines and Marines with children

### BAH-Without Dependents

Member without dependents (single or with dependents not at duty station) receives.

- BAH at the without-dependents rate for the duty station zip code
- Smaller amount reflecting individual housing
- Common for single Marines living off-base

### BAH-Differential (BAH-Diff)

Member with dependents in a separate location not allowed to draw with-dependents BAH receives.

- BAH-Diff at a fixed differential rate
- Used in specific situations like dependents living overseas
- Less common but important for affected members

## Eligibility for BAH

A Marine qualifies for BAH when meeting these.

- Active duty status (or qualifying reservist on active duty over 30 days)
- Not residing in government quarters
- Has dependents (for with-dependents rate)
- Specific dependent and household situations per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />

## How BAH Rate Is Determined

Rate calculation involves three factors.

### Factor 1 - Rank

BAH rates increase with rank.

- E-1 through E-9 progression
- O-1 through O-7+ progression
- Senior ranks have higher BAH rates

### Factor 2 - Dependent Status

Two main rates per location.

- With Dependents (W-rate): Higher amount
- Without Dependents (S-rate): Lower amount

### Factor 3 - Zip Code

Each duty station zip code has specific BAH rates.

- DOD surveys local housing markets
- Rates updated annually based on housing data
- High-cost areas (San Diego, Honolulu, Washington DC) have higher rates
- Low-cost areas have lower rates

## Performance Steps for BAH Processing

### Step 1 - Verify Eligibility

Pull member's MCTFS record.

- Verify active duty status
- Verify housing status (government quarters or off-base)
- Verify dependent status from NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> history
- Identify zip code from PCS orders or current address

### Step 2 - Determine BAH Category

Based on member's situation.

- With dependents at duty station: With-Dependents rate
- Without dependents: Without-Dependents rate
- Dependents at separate location: Possible BAH-Diff

### Step 3 - Look Up Rate

Use DOD's BAH calculator at DTMO.

- Input zip code
- Input rank
- Input dependent status
- System provides current monthly rate

### Step 4 - Process UD/MIPS Transaction

Apply the BAH action.

- BAH start (new entitlement)
- BAH stop (no longer eligible)
- BAH change (rate or category change)
- BAH zip code change

### Step 5 - Verify EDFR

Confirm transaction processed.

- Pull EDFR within 5 business days
- Verify BAH posts at correct rate
- Identify and correct failures

### Step 6 - Member Coordination

Brief the member.

- Notify of BAH amount
- Provide effective date
- Verify member's housing situation matches the action

## When BAH Changes

BAH rate or status changes when.

- Member promotes (higher BAH rate at new rank)
- Member's dependent status changes (marriage, divorce, child added or removed)
- Member PCS to new duty station (new zip code, new rate)
- Member moves into or out of government quarters
- Annual BAH rate adjustment (typically January 1)
- Member separates or retires

Each trigger requires UD/MIPS action.

## Member Self-Audit Tips

I&I admin briefs members on BAH self-monitoring.

- Review BAH amount on each LES
- Verify with-dependents vs without-dependents matches reality
- Check rate against DTMO BAH calculator
- Report any discrepancies to S-1 or PAC immediately

## Common BAH Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Wrong rate after promotion.** Member promoted but BAH not updated. Fix by submitting BAH rate change effective with promotion date.
2. **Dependent change not processed.** Marriage or divorce occurred, NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> submitted, but BAH category not changed. Submit BAH change transaction.
3. **Member moved to government quarters but still drawing BAH.** Critical error. Member is overpaid. Stop BAH retroactive to move-in date. Member owes back-payment.
4. **Wrong zip code on PCS.** Member transferred but BAH still based on old zip code. Update zip code with PCS effective date.
5. **BAH-Diff not processed when applicable.** Member has dependents at separate location but BAH still showing without-dependents. Process BAH-Diff if eligible.

## BAH and PCS

When member PCS, BAH coordinates with the move.

- Old duty station BAH stops on PCS departure
- New duty station BAH starts on arrival
- Member may have brief gap or overlap requiring action
- New zip code drives new rate

## BAH and Government Quarters

Member moving to or from government quarters triggers BAH action.

- Move into government quarters: BAH stops effective move-in date
- Move out of government quarters: BAH starts effective move-out date
- Members in government quarters do not draw BAH
- Some categories of quarters have specific BAH rules

## Documentation Required

For BAH actions, PAC needs.

- PCS orders (for zip code changes)
- NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with supporting documents (for dependent status changes)
- Government quarters documentation (for moves to/from)
- Promotion warrant (for rate changes)

## Quality Control

Before submitting BAH transactions.

- Verify supporting documentation present
- Verify rate matches DTMO calculator
- Verify effective date matches the triggering event
- Verify member's zip code accurate

## Related Roles

BAH affects every off-base Marine's pay.

- **Marines** verify BAH amount on each LES and report discrepancies.
- **Leaders** counsel Marines on BAH and verify accuracy during reviews.
- **Commanders** review unit BAH for audit posture and member welfare.
