## What Triggers Unsatisfactory Participation

Per MCRAMM Chapter 4 Section 3 and 10 USC 10148<Citation source="10 USC 10148" />, a SelRes Marine is processed for Unsatisfactory Participation when one of these occurs.

- **Pattern of unexcused absences**: 9 or more unexcused IDT absences in any 12-month period
- **Missed AT without authorization**: AT skipped without approved excuse
- **Failure to report**: Member ordered to active duty fails to report
- **Inadequate participation pattern**: Command judgment based on overall performance
- **Failure to maintain admin readiness**: Despite counseling, member continues to fail readiness requirements

The 9-absence threshold is the most common trigger. Track it precisely.

## What Counts as an Unexcused Absence

Per MCRAMM, an unexcused absence is missed IDT without one of these.

- Authorized excuse approved by command before the drill
- Documented illness with medical evidence
- Documented family emergency with command approval
- Civilian employment conflict approved in advance under specific provisions
- Other excuse approved per local command policy

A missed drill becomes excused only if approved per command policy. Otherwise it is unexcused.

## Three Potential Outcomes

When Unsatisfactory Participation processing initiates, three outcomes are possible.

### Outcome 1 - Return to Good Standing

The member responds to counseling and returns to satisfactory participation. No further action.

### Outcome 2 - Transfer to IRR

The member is removed from SelRes and placed in the Individual Ready Reserve. They lose drilling status and drill pay. They retain MSO and remain subject to mobilization.

### Outcome 3 - Involuntary Order to Active Duty

Per 10 USC 12303<Citation source="10 USC 12303" />, members who have not completed initial military service obligation may be involuntarily ordered to active duty. This is the most severe outcome and is reserved for members under initial agreement.

## Performance Steps for Unsatisfactory Processing

### Step 1 - Track and Verify

Before initiating, verify the trigger.

- Pull member's drill attendance record
- Count unexcused absences in the prior 12 months
- Verify each absence was correctly classified (excused vs unexcused)
- Confirm 9-absence threshold or other trigger met

### Step 2 - Initial Counseling

Before formal processing, counsel the member.

- Schedule formal counseling session
- Review attendance pattern with member
- Identify root causes (employment, family, personal issues)
- Document counseling on Page 11
- Set specific improvement expectations
- Provide member opportunity to remedy

### Step 3 - Pre-Processing Counseling

If member does not improve after initial counseling.

- Conduct second formal counseling
- Review prior counseling and outcomes
- Brief member on Unsatisfactory Participation processing consequences
- Explain transfer to IRR vs involuntary active duty options
- Document via Page 11 with member signature

### Step 4 - Generate Unsatisfactory Participation Letter

If processing proceeds, generate the formal letter per MCRAMM Figure 4-6.

- Member identification
- Specific absences cited (dates, periods missed)
- Prior counseling history
- Action recommended (transfer to IRR or involuntary AD)
- Member rights and response opportunity

### Step 5 - Member Response Opportunity

Per MCRAMM, the member has opportunity to.

- Submit written matters in opposition or mitigation
- Request meeting with commander
- Provide evidence of authorized absence
- Identify mitigating circumstances

Allow specified response period (typically 30 days).

### Step 6 - Commander Review

After member response, commander reviews and decides.

- Affirm processing and recommend action
- Modify processing based on member response
- Withdraw processing if member's matters justify

### Step 7 - Forward to MARFORRES or HQMC

Final action authority varies.

- Transfer to IRR: Often unit commander authority
- Involuntary order to active duty: HQMC authority (10 USC 12303<Citation source="10 USC 12303" />)
- Forward complete package to higher authority for decision

### Step 8 - Process Final Action

Once decided, execute.

- IRR transfer: UD/MIPS transaction shifting member to IRR pay group
- Active duty order: MROWS orders for involuntary AD
- Member notification and final processing

## Documentation Requirements

The Unsatisfactory Participation package requires.

- Drill attendance log showing each absence and classification
- All Page 11 counseling entries
- Initial and pre-processing counseling documentation
- Member's written response (if submitted)
- Commander's review and decision
- Final action orders or transactions

## Common Errors in Unsatisfactory Processing

These hit during processing.

1. **Threshold miscounted.** Counting 12-month absences incorrectly. Use rolling 12-month window from the most recent absence backward.
2. **Excused absences counted as unexcused.** Verify each absence's classification. Excused absences (with approval) do not count toward the 9-absence threshold.
3. **No prior counseling on record.** Without documented counseling, processing has weak foundation. Document early and often.
4. **Member not given response opportunity.** Per MCRAMM, member has right to respond. Skipping this step invalidates the action.
5. **Wrong final action.** Transfer to IRR is appropriate for many cases. Involuntary AD applies primarily to members under initial agreement. Choose correctly.

## Decision Framework

Before initiating, ask these questions.

- Has the trigger been objectively met (count verified)?
- Has the member been counseled on prior absences?
- Are there mitigating circumstances we should address first?
- Is the member under initial agreement (eligible for involuntary AD) or beyond (transfer to IRR only)?
- Does the member's overall record support the action?

## Pay and Records Impact

Unsatisfactory Participation has consequences beyond drill pay loss.

- Loss of drill pay for missed periods (already absorbed)
- Potential loss of retirement points for the year
- Page 11 entries become permanent record
- Possible Career Retirement Credit Report (CRCR) impact
- Possible bar to reenlistment if processed
- Possible characterization of separation if separated

## Coordination Touchpoints

Unsatisfactory Participation processing touches.

- **Member**: Primary subject of the process
- **Unit chain of command**: Counseling and commander decision
- **Supporting PAC**: Final UD/MIPS processing
- **MARFORRES G-1**: For transfers and major actions
- **HQMC RA**: For involuntary AD orders
- **SJA**: For legal review of processing actions

## Related Roles

The process involves multiple roles.

- **Marines** facing Unsatisfactory processing have right to counsel, response, and review.
- **Leaders** conduct counseling sessions and document attendance issues.
- **Commanders** initiate processing, review responses, and recommend final action.
