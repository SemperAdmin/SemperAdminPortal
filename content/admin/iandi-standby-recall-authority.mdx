## Standby Recall Authority

Per MCRAMM Chapter 9 and 10 USC, Standby Reserve members are subject to recall to active duty under specific authorities.

### Subject to Full Mobilization Only (10 USC 12301(a))

Standby members might be recalled only under full mobilization.

- Declaration of war or national emergency
- Highest mobilization authority
- All Reserve component members subject

### Not Subject to Other Authorities

Per 10 USC.

- 10 USC 12302<Citation source="10 USC 12302" /> Partial Mobilization (NO)
- 10 USC 12304<Citation source="10 USC 12304" /> Presidential Reserve Call-Up (NO)
- 10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(d) Voluntary Active Duty (typically not applicable)

This makes Standby a deeper, more limited mobilization pool.

## Why Limited Recall

Standby Reserve serves as deeper mobilization pool.

- Members have specific reasons preventing routine drilling
- Recalled only when SELRES and IRR insufficient
- Specific national emergency triggers recall
- Standby members are last resort mobilization

## Standby Recall Process

When full mobilization invoked.

### Step 1 - Mobilization Authority Established

Per 10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(a).

- Declaration of war or national emergency
- Specific authority invoked
- DOD identifies need

### Step 2 - Standby Members Identified

HQMC identifies Standby members for recall.

- MOS-based selection
- Specific operational needs
- Member readiness considerations
- Specific selection criteria

### Step 3 - Member Notification

Selected Standby members notified.

- Recall orders issued
- Specific reporting instructions
- Travel arrangements
- Pre-mobilization preparation timeline

### Step 4 - Pre-Mobilization Processing

Members prepare for recall.

- Medical and dental screening
- Administrative preparation
- Family preparation
- Civilian employment notification (USERRA)

### Step 5 - Reporting to Active Duty

Members report at designated location.

- Active duty status begins
- Initial processing
- Unit assignment
- Specific deployment processing

### Step 6 - Active Duty Mobilization

Member serves active duty.

- Active duty pay and benefits
- Mobilization mission performance
- Specific timeline per authority

### Step 7 - Demobilization

When mobilization ends.

- Active duty status ends
- Return to Standby or to Ready Reserve
- TAMP coverage
- Reintegration support

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

I&I admin generally does not directly process Standby recall. HQMC and MCRSC primarily handle.

### When Standby Member Local

For locally-known Standby member receiving recall.

- Provide guidance on procedures
- Connect with MCRSC for support
- Specific local assistance

### When Mobilization Includes Local Standby Members

For mass mobilizations affecting local members.

- Coordinate with MCRSC and HQMC
- Specific local support during mobilization
- Family support during member absence

### When Standby Member Returns

If demobilizing Standby member is local.

- Specific reintegration support
- Connect with civilian employer for USERRA
- Coordinate transition

## Pre-Mobilization Issues for Standby

Standby members might have particular pre-mobilization issues.

### Medical Readiness

Standby members might have less recent medical screening.

- Comprehensive pre-mobilization medical
- Treatment for issues found
- Possible deferment

### Specific ISL Member Issues

ISL members might have long-term reasons preventing mobilization.

- Re-evaluation of ISL status during mobilization
- Specific accommodations
- Possible deferment

### Family Care Plan

Many Standby members have not maintained current FCP.

- Specific FCP development for mobilization
- Coordinate with family
- Specific accommodations

### Civilian Employment

Standby member's civilian job often more established.

- USERRA notification
- Employment protection during mobilization
- Specific employer coordination

## Standby Mobilization Pay

During Standby mobilization to active duty.

- Active duty pay at member's grade
- Active duty BAH and BAS
- Full TRICARE Prime active duty
- All standard active duty pays
- Specific deployment pays where applicable

## Demobilization Considerations

When Standby mobilization ends.

### TAMP Coverage

- 180 days TAMP
- Family covered
- Specific transition support

### Status Decision

Member decides post-mobilization status.

- Return to Standby
- Transfer to Ready Reserve (if available and eligible)
- Separate from Reserve component

## Stop-Loss Considerations

During specific national emergencies.

- Standby members' separation might be involuntarily delayed
- Stop-loss authority specific to mobilization
- Member's discharge date might shift

## Common Standby Recall Issues

### Member Does not Be Located

Standby member unreachable.

- Specific search procedures
- Might affect recall availability
- Possible AWOL status if non-responsive

### Member Medically Unable

Member with disqualifying medical condition.

- Pre-mobilization screening identifies
- Possible deferment
- Specific procedures

### Member Refuses Recall

Member refusing to report.

- Possible AWOL/desertion
- Specific consequences
- Coordinate with HQMC

### ISL Member's Original Reason Continues

ISL member's reason for ISL status persists.

- Re-evaluation of recall eligibility
- Possible deferment
- Specific procedures

## Coordination Touchpoints

Standby recall touches.

- **Marine Corps Reserve Support Command (MCRSC)**: For Standby administration
- **HQMC**: For mobilization processing
- **Member's gaining unit**: For active duty processing
- **Member's civilian employer**: For USERRA coordination

## Quality Control on Standby Recall

Common errors.

1. **Member's address not current.** Recall notification does not reach member.
2. **Member's medical readiness unknown.** Pre-mobilization issues delay recall.
3. **Family Care Plan missing.** Specific situations require FCP.
4. **USERRA documentation incomplete.** Employer documentation issues.

## Related Roles

Standby recall affects Standby members and their families.

- **Marines** in Standby maintain readiness for potential recall.
- **Leaders** at SMCR units rarely directly engage Standby recall.
- **Commanders** at I&I support local Standby members during mobilization.
