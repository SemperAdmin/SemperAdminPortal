## VA Home Loan Eligibility for Reservists

Per 38 USC Chapter 37 and MCRAMM Chapter 12, reservists qualify for VA Home Loan benefits under specific service requirements. Two paths to eligibility.

### Path 1 - Six Years Selected Reserve Service

A reservist qualifies after 6 years of honorable service in the Selected Reserve.

- Continuous SELRES (Selected Reserve, what most Marines call SMCR or the Reserves) service totaling 6 years
- Honorable discharge if separated
- Currently serving counts (good standing)
- Combined service across components counts (Reserve plus active)

### Path 2 - Active Duty Mobilization

A reservist qualifies after specific active duty service.

- 90 days of active duty (not for training) during a war period
- Mobilization to active duty for specific operations
- Active duty service generally treated as veteran service for VA Home Loan

The mobilization path might apply faster than the 6-year SELRES path for reservists with deployment history.

## What VA Home Loan Provides

The VA Home Loan is a guarantee program. The VA does not loan money. The VA guarantees the loan with private lenders.

### Key Features

- No down payment required (in most cases)
- No private mortgage insurance (PMI)
- Competitive interest rates
- Limited closing costs
- Loan amount limits per county (some loans exceed standard limits)
- Refinance options available

### Loan Types

- Purchase loan: For buying a home
- Refinance loan: For refinancing existing mortgage
- Cash-out refinance: Drawing equity from existing home
- Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL): Streamlined refinance for existing VA loans

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Verify Eligibility

When a reservist asks about VA Home Loan.

- Pull MCTFS to verify service history
- Check for 6 years of SELRES service
- Check for any qualifying active duty (90+ days non-training)
- Identify any factors affecting eligibility (discharge characterization)

### Step 2 - Brief on Process

Provide member with overview.

- Eligibility based on service
- VA Form 26-1880 application for Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
- COE provided to lender
- Lender processes loan application
- Standard real estate transaction

### Step 3 - Coordinate COE Application

Member applies for Certificate of Eligibility.

- Submit VA Form 26-1880 to VA
- Or apply online via VA.gov
- Provide service documentation (DD-214s, NGB-22, points statement)
- Wait for VA processing (typically 30 to 60 days)

### Step 4 - Direct to VA-Approved Lender

Member chooses a lender.

- VA does not lend directly
- Member chooses participating lender
- Lender processes loan application using COE
- Standard loan closing process

## Other VA Benefits for Reservists

### VA Disability Compensation

For service-connected disabilities.

- Filed via VA Form 21-526EZ
- Reservists eligible for service-connected conditions
- Compensation tax-free
- Might affect retirement pay (offset rules apply)

### VA Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment (VR&E)

For reservists with service-connected disability affecting employment.

- VA pays for retraining, education, or job placement
- Specific eligibility based on disability rating
- Coordinated through local VA office

### VA Burial Benefits

For deceased reservists with qualifying service.

- Burial in national or state veterans cemetery
- Burial allowance for non-service connected deaths
- Higher allowance for service-connected deaths
- Headstone and marker provided

### VA Health Care

For specific groups of reservists.

- Combat veterans (5-year enrollment from discharge)
- Service-connected conditions (lifetime enrollment)
- Some other categories per VA priority groups

### Other VA Benefits

- Caregiver Support Program
- Survivor Pension
- Dependents and Indemnity Compensation (DIC)
- Specific transition assistance programs

## Common Member Questions

### "How much I borrow with a VA Home Loan?"

VA loan limits per county. As of recent years, no overall cap if using full entitlement (varies by area). Check current limits.

### "I use the VA loan more than once?"

Yes. Members might use the benefit multiple times if previous loans paid off (entitlement restored).

### "Does my service-connected disability affect my home loan?"

Service-connected disability rating might waive the VA funding fee. Specific rules apply.

### "What if my discharge wasn't honorable?"

Less than honorable discharges might affect VA benefit eligibility. Some categories of discharge are barred from VA benefits.

### "my surviving spouse use the VA loan?"

Yes in specific cases. Surviving spouse of certain veterans qualifies.

### "Do I need to be discharged to use VA benefits?"

For VA Home Loan, currently serving SELRES members with 6 years qualify. For other VA benefits, varies.

## Common I&I Errors on VA Benefits

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Telling member without 6 years they qualify.** Verify SELRES service or qualifying active duty before confirming eligibility.
2. **Confusing VA benefits with TRICARE.** VA benefits and TRICARE are different programs administered by different agencies.
3. **Not documenting service for VA application.** Member needs DD-214s, NGB-22, and points statement. Coordinate documentation.
4. **Discharge characterization not verified.** Less than honorable might affect eligibility. Confirm.
5. **VA Home Loan funding fee not mentioned.** Member might pay funding fee at closing (varies based on category). Brief on cost.

## Coordination Touchpoints

VA benefits processing touches.

- **VA Regional Office**: Application processing for all VA benefits
- **VA Loan Guaranty Service**: Specific to home loans
- **VA Healthcare**: Enrollment for healthcare benefits
- **VA-approved lenders**: For home loan processing
- **MCRSC**: For service documentation
- **Member's local Veterans Service Organization (VSO)**: For benefits counseling

## Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs)

Members benefit from VSO assistance for VA claims and benefits.

- American Legion
- Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW)
- Disabled American Veterans (DAV)
- Marine Corps League
- Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America (IAVA)

VSOs help members navigate VA processes at no cost. Direct members to VSOs for complex claims.

## Related Roles

VA benefits affect reservists during and after service.

- **Marines** apply for VA benefits when eligible. Use VA Home Loan for major purchases.
- **Leaders** counsel members on VA benefits as part of career development.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member benefit awareness through pre-separation counseling.
