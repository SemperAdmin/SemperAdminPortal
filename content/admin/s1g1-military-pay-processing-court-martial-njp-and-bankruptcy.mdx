## Role and responsibility

The IPAC pay clerk cuts court-martial pay forfeitures per DoDFMR Vol 7A<Citation source="DODFMR Vol 7A" /> Chapter 48 and NJP forfeitures per Chapter 49 through UD-MIPS. The legal clerk validates the UPB or court-martial sentence against the forfeiture transaction. The S-1 chief tracks active forfeitures. The S-1 officer signs by direction on the routing. The unit commander signs the NJP imposition per Manual for Courts-Martial 2024 Part V. The disbursing technician processes the forfeiture per DoDFMR Vol 5 and MCO 7300.21B<Citation source="MCO 7300.21B" />. The Marine notifies the unit of bankruptcy proceedings per Chapter 50.

Source documents flow from the court-martial sentence per MCO 5800.16<Citation source="MCO 5800.16" /> Volume 16 and Manual for Courts-Martial 2024 Rule for Courts-Martial 1004, the NJP UPB per MCO 5800.16 Volume 14, the bankruptcy filing notification per 11 U.S.C. Bankruptcy Code, the convening authority post-trial action, and the prior MCTFS pay history. Outputs include the executed UD-MIPS forfeiture transaction, the documented KSD packet, the verified LES reflecting forfeiture or deduction, the tracked forfeiture duration, the Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, the bankruptcy coordination record per Chapter 50, and the audit trail.

Per DoDFMR Vol 7A Chapter 48, court-martial pay forfeitures apply per the sentence and become effective per the convening authority action timing. Per Chapter 49, NJP forfeitures apply per the UPB and the imposing commander's signature. Per Chapter 50, bankruptcy proceedings affect pay through automatic stays, debt discharge, and creditor priorities.

## Trigger events

- Court-martial sentence with forfeiture component.
- NJP imposition with forfeiture component per Manual for Courts-Martial Part V paragraph 5.
- Convening authority post-trial action triggering forfeiture effective date.
- Bankruptcy filing notification from the Marine.
- Forfeiture period completion.
- Bankruptcy discharge order.

## Processing workflow

1. Receive the court-martial sentence or NJP UPB. The IPAC pay clerk receives the sentence package from the legal advisor (court-martial) or the UPB copy from the imposing commander (NJP).
2. Identify the forfeiture type. Per DoDFMR Vol 7A<Citation source="DODFMR Vol 7A" />:
   - Chapter 48 court-martial forfeiture (Summary, Special, General).
   - Chapter 49 NJP forfeiture per Manual for Courts-Martial 2024 Part V paragraph 5 maximum punishments.
   - Total forfeiture (all pay and allowances) versus partial forfeiture (specified amount per month).
3. Validate per the controlling chapter. Per Chapter 48 or 49, confirm eligibility against the sentence or UPB.
4. Cut the forfeiture transaction in UD-MIPS. Per Appendix E and MCTFSPRIUM<Citation source="MCTFSPRIUM" />, submit the transaction at the appropriate code.
5. Process bankruptcy notification. Per Chapter 50 and 11 U.S.C., the bankruptcy filing triggers an automatic stay on collection actions. The S-1 routes the notification to disbursing and to the legal advisor.
6. Coordinate with disbursing. Per DoDFMR Vol 5 and MCO 7300.21B, the disbursing technician processes the forfeiture or the bankruptcy-related deduction.
7. Verify LES. The next LES reflects the forfeiture or deduction.
8. Track forfeiture duration. Per the sentence or UPB, forfeitures run for a specified period (NJP) or indefinitely (court-martial total forfeiture pending punitive discharge).
9. Document forfeiture on Page 11. Per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006, court-martial and NJP forfeitures record on Page 11.
10. Close forfeiture at completion. Per the sentence or UPB duration, the forfeiture terminates and pay restores.

## Systems of record and forms

### Systems

- Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). Holds forfeiture records.
- UD-MIPS. Cuts forfeiture transactions per Appendix E.
- LES. Validates forfeiture.
- Legal Records System. Holds court-martial sentences and UPBs.
- DFAS Pay Processing. Disbursing-side action.

### Forms

- Court-Martial Sentence (DD Form 490 / 491). Per MCO 5800.16 Volume 16.
- Unit Punishment Book NAVMC 10132. Per MCO 5800.16 Volume 14 and the NJP build.
- Convening Authority Post-Trial Action. Per Manual for Courts-Martial.
- Bankruptcy Filing Notification. Per Chapter 50.
- NAVMC 118 (11) Administrative Remarks. Page 11 per MCO P1070.12K paragraph 4006.

## Common pitfalls

- Court-martial forfeiture cut without convening authority action. Per Chapter 48, the convening authority acts on the sentence before forfeiture takes effect.
- NJP forfeiture exceeds maximum per Manual for Courts-Martial Part V paragraph 5. Per Chapter 49, the imposed punishment fits within the maximum.
- Bankruptcy notification not routed to disbursing. Per Chapter 50, the bankruptcy triggers an automatic stay affecting deductions.
- Forfeiture continued past sentence duration. The S-1 closes at completion.
- Wrong forfeiture type applied (total versus partial). Per Chapter 48, total forfeiture applies on punitive discharge sentences. Partial forfeiture applies per the specified monthly amount.
- Page 11 documenting forfeiture omitted. Per MCO P1070.12K<Citation source="MCO P1070.12K" /> Chapter 6, the entry records.
- Pay restoration past forfeiture end. The S-1 cuts the restoration on the end date.

## Decision points

- Total versus partial forfeiture. Per Chapter 48, total forfeiture applies on punitive discharge sentences pending appellate review. Partial forfeiture applies per the specified monthly amount and duration.
- NJP forfeiture cap. Per Manual for Courts-Martial Part V paragraph 5 and Chapter 49, the maximum NJP forfeiture varies by grade and the imposing commander's authority.
- Bankruptcy automatic stay scope. Per Chapter 50, the automatic stay affects pre-petition debt collections. Post-petition debts continue.
- Effective date timing. Per Chapter 48, the convening authority post-trial action drives the effective date for court-martial. Per Chapter 49, the imposition date drives the effective date for NJP.
