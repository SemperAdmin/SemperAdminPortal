## BAH Without Dependents

Per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />, BAH at the without-dependents rate applies when.

- Marine is single (never married)
- Marine is divorced with no qualifying dependents
- Marine is separated and dependent does not live with member
- Marine has no children or children have aged out of dependency
- Marine resides off-base (not in government quarters)

The without-dependents rate is lower than with-dependents but still substantial.

## Eligibility for Without-Dependents BAH

A Marine qualifies for without-dependents BAH when meeting these.

- Active duty status
- Has no qualifying dependents (or dependents not eligible to be claimed at duty station)
- Resides off-base (not in government quarters)
- Specific rank thresholds and other rules per MCO 7220.56<Citation source="MCO 7220.56" />

### Junior Enlisted Restrictions

Junior enlisted Marines (E-1 through E-3) generally live in barracks (government quarters) and do not draw BAH.

- E-1 through E-3 in barracks: No BAH
- E-1 through E-3 off-base: BAH may apply but limited
- E-4 and above: BAH off-base standard

Specific rules for junior enlisted with hardship or specific situations.

## How Without-Dependents Rate Is Determined

Same factors as with-dependents.

- Rank
- Zip code (duty station)
- Annual rate adjustments

Without-dependents rate is roughly 75-85% of with-dependents rate at the same zip code.

## BAH-Differential (BAH-Diff)

BAH-Diff is a separate category for Marines with dependents in a separate location.

### When BAH-Diff Applies

A Marine receives BAH-Diff when meeting all of these.

- Has qualifying dependents per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />
- Dependents do not reside at member's duty station
- Member is in government quarters at duty station
- Specific situations like dependents overseas while member CONUS

### BAH-Diff Rate

BAH-Diff is a fixed differential rate per DOD FMR.

- Set by DOD annually
- Not based on zip code
- Same rate worldwide
- Substantially less than with-dependents BAH

The differential reflects the additional cost of supporting dependents away from the member.

### Common BAH-Diff Scenarios

- Member CONUS in barracks, dependents living overseas
- Member sea duty, dependents in CONUS family housing not at member's home port
- Specific deployment or assignment situations creating geographic separation

## Performance Steps for Without-Dependents BAH

### Step 1 - Verify No Qualifying Dependents

Pull MCTFS dependency record.

- Verify dependent count is zero
- Or verify dependents not at duty station (BAH-Diff territory)
- Verify dependent records reflect current reality

### Step 2 - Verify Off-Base Residence

For without-dependents BAH eligibility.

- Verify member not in government quarters
- Confirm housing situation (off-base apartment, house, etc.)
- For E-1 through E-3, verify hardship waiver if applicable

### Step 3 - Apply Without-Dependents Rate

Use DTMO BAH calculator.

- Input zip code, rank, and dependent status (without)
- System provides current monthly rate
- Apply via UD/MIPS BAH transaction

### Step 4 - Verify EDFR

Pull EDFR within 5 business days.

- Verify BAH posts at without-dependents rate
- Identify and correct failures

## Performance Steps for BAH-Diff

### Step 1 - Verify BAH-Diff Eligibility

Confirm specific conditions.

- Member in government quarters at duty station
- Has qualifying dependents not at duty station
- Standard BAH (with or without dependents) does not apply

### Step 2 - Apply BAH-Diff Rate

Per DOD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DOD FMR Volume 7A" />.

- Apply current BAH-Diff fixed rate
- Process via UD/MIPS BAH transaction
- Different transaction code than standard BAH

### Step 3 - Document Justification

PAC retains documentation.

- Member's living situation
- Dependent location
- Reason for BAH-Diff vs standard BAH

## Common Scenarios

### E-3 in Barracks, Single, No Dependents

- BAH: None (member in government quarters)
- Member receives BAH only if circumstances support off-base living

### E-5 Off-Base, Single

- BAH: Without-dependents rate at duty station zip code
- Standard processing

### E-6 in Barracks, Married, Spouse Lives Overseas

- BAH: BAH-Diff (member in quarters, dependent at separate location)
- Specific BAH-Diff rate

### E-4 Off-Base, Recently Divorced, No Children

- BAH: Was with-dependents, becomes without-dependents
- Effective date: Date of divorce decree
- Submit divorce decree and NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />

### O-3 Off-Base, Married, Family Lives in Different City for Civilian Job

- BAH: Standard with-dependents BAH at duty station rate
- Family residence does not have to be at duty station
- Member supports family financially

### E-7 Off-Base, Children Aged Out of Dependency

- BAH: Was with-dependents (when children eligible), becomes without-dependents
- Effective date: Last child's loss of eligibility
- Member must submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> documenting change

## Common Errors

These hit during PAC processing.

1. **Member married but still drawing without-dependents BAH.** NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> submitted late or BAH transaction missed. Fix by submitting BAH change with marriage date as effective date.
2. **Member divorced but still drawing with-dependents BAH.** Critical error creating overpayment. Stop with-dependents BAH retroactive to divorce date. Member owes back-payment.
3. **Children aged out but member still drawing with-dependents BAH.** Verify child status annually. Stop with-dependents when last child loses eligibility.
4. **BAH-Diff applied when standard BAH appropriate.** Member off-base but processed as BAH-Diff. Fix by correcting to standard with-dependents.
5. **Junior enlisted in barracks drawing BAH.** Member in government quarters should not draw BAH. Stop BAH retroactive to barracks move-in.

## Audit Implications

Without-dependents and BAH-Diff are common audit findings.

- BAH-Diff applied incorrectly creates overpayment
- Member with no dependents drawing with-dependents creates overpayment
- Member with dependents not at duty station and BAH-Diff not applied creates underpayment

PAC accuracy on these categories is audit-critical.

## When Status Changes

BAH category changes per these triggers.

| Trigger | Change |
|---------|--------|
| Marriage | Without becomes With-Dependents |
| Birth/adoption (no spouse) | Without becomes With-Dependents (children) |
| Divorce (no children) | With becomes Without-Dependents |
| Last child ages out | With becomes Without-Dependents |
| Move from barracks to off-base | None to BAH (verify rate type) |
| Move from off-base to barracks | BAH to None |
| Spouse relocates to dependent-prohibited area | With becomes BAH-Diff |
| Spouse rejoins at duty station | BAH-Diff becomes With-Dependents |

## Related Roles

Without-dependents and BAH-Diff affect specific Marines.

- **Marines** verify BAH category on LES and report when life events occur.
- **Leaders** counsel Marines on BAH and dependent status changes.
- **Commanders** review unit BAH for accuracy and audit posture.
