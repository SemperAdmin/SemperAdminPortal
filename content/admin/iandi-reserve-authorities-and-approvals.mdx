## What Authorities Mean

Per MCRAMM Chapter 5 and MCAP, authorities determine who has approval power for specific actions. Knowing the authority structure prevents.

- Misrouted packages
- Approval delays
- Repeated rework
- Member frustration

## Authority Hierarchy

The Marine Corps Reserve uses a hierarchical authority structure.

### Headquarters Marine Corps (HQMC)

Highest authority for policy and major actions.

- Policy direction (MCOs, MARADMINs)
- Selection boards
- Promotion approvals (officer)
- Major separation actions
- Specific waivers

### Marine Corps Forces Reserve (MARFORRES)

Operational headquarters for the Reserve component.

- Reserve unit operational decisions
- Specific personnel actions
- Specific approval authorities
- Coordination across SMCR units

### Major Subordinate Commands (Divisions, Wings, Logistics Group)

Major commands within MARFORRES.

- Subordinate unit decisions
- Specific personnel actions
- Approval for specific moves and assignments

### Regiment, Group, MEU, Battalion

Operational unit levels.

- Direct unit personnel actions
- Specific approval authorities
- Day-to-day administrative decisions

### Battalion, Company, Detachment

Working level units.

- Direct member actions
- Daily administrative decisions
- Specific delegated authorities

### I&I Staff

Administrative support to drilling units.

- Coordination of administrative actions
- Specific delegated authorities
- Member services

For routine actions.

|--------|---------------------------|
| Routine pay actions | PAC level |

## Specific Authority Considerations

Some authorities require specific situations.

### Unit Commander Authority

Unit commander typically authorizes.

- Drill attendance approvals
- EDP authorizations
- Page 11 entries
- Counseling sessions
- Specific operational actions

### Commanding General Authority

Commanding generals (Division, Wing, MEF) typically authorize.

- Major personnel actions
- Convening boards for various proceedings
- Specific approvals beyond unit commander

### Service Secretary Authority

Service Secretary or designated representative typically authorizes.

- Mobilization
- Specific waivers
- Convening boards for senior officer actions
- Major personnel actions affecting Service component

## Performance Steps for Authority Verification

When processing actions.

### Step 1 - Identify the Action

Be specific about what is being approved.

- Pay action
- Personnel action
- Disciplinary action
- Operational action
- Specific request

### Step 2 - Identify the Authority

Per MCRAMM, MCAP, and current MARADMINs.

- Authority specifically named
- Hierarchical level required
- Any delegation in effect

### Step 3 - Route Through Proper Chain

For multi-level actions.

- Initial review at unit level
- Endorsement at higher levels
- Final approval at appropriate authority

### Step 4 - Document Approvals

For each action.

- Specific approving authority documented
- Date of approval
- Specific approval details

### Step 5 - File Documentation

Per IRAM.

- Approval documentation in member's record
- Audit trail maintained
- Specific filing requirements

## Common Authority Issues

These hit during admin processing.

### Routing to Wrong Authority

Action routed to authority that does not have approval power.

- Returns from incorrect authority
- Reroute to correct level
- Delays for member

### Authority Level Skipped

Higher authority approves without lower-level endorsement.

- Specific procedural failure
- May invalidate approval
- Coordinate proper routing

### Delegation Not Recognized

Higher authority delegates to lower level but admin doesn't recognize.

- Delegation may be in effect via memorandum
- Specific delegation period
- Verify before requiring higher routing

### Authority Not Available

Required authority is unavailable (deployed, on leave).

- Acting designation typically applies
- Acting authority handles approvals during absence
- Specific delegation procedures
