## Quality of Life Benefits Overview

Per MCRAMM Chapter 12, reservists and their families have access to military quality of life benefits with eligibility based on component status.

These benefits provide cost savings and support family readiness.

- Commissary (military grocery stores)
- Exchange (BX, NEX, military retail stores)
- Morale, Welfare, and Recreation (MWR) facilities
- Space-Available travel
- Lodging on military installations
- Recreation areas

## Commissary Privileges

Commissaries offer groceries at lower prices than civilian stores.

### Drilling SelRes Members and Families

- Full commissary access
- Use as often as desired
- Family members accompanied or with valid ID

### IRR Members and Families

- Limited commissary access per current policy
- Specific use frequency might apply
- Verify current privileges before counseling members

### Retired Reserve Members and Families

- Full commissary access for retirees drawing pay
- Full access for awaiting pay retirees per current policy
- Family members with valid ID

### Recent Policy Changes

DOD has expanded commissary access for additional groups in recent years. Veterans with VA disability ratings have specific access. Verify current policy before counseling.

## Exchange Privileges

Military exchanges offer retail goods at competitive prices, often without state sales tax.

### Drilling SelRes Members and Families

- Full exchange access
- All exchange types (BX, NEX, MCX, military clothing sales)

### IRR and Standby Members

- Limited exchange access per current policy
- Verify current privileges

### Retired Reserve Members

- Full exchange access for all retirees
- Family members with valid ID

### Online Exchange (Shop Military)

Recent expansions allow online shopping access for honorably discharged veterans and others. Direct members to ShopMyExchange.com or specific service exchange websites.

## MWR Facilities

Morale, Welfare, and Recreation facilities include.

- Recreation centers (gyms, libraries, recreational equipment)
- Outdoor recreation (campgrounds, equipment rental)
- Information, Tickets, and Travel (ITT) offices
- Family programs (single Marine programs, family counseling)
- Auto skills centers
- Hobby and craft centers

### Eligibility

- Drilling SelRes members and families: Full access
- IRR and Standby: Limited access per facility policy
- Retired Reserve: Full access for retirees
- Family members: Access with sponsoring member or with valid ID

## Space-Available Travel

Space-Available (Space-A) travel allows military members and families to fly on military aircraft for free or at minimal cost when seats are available.

### Eligibility

- Active duty: Yes (Category I or II)
- Drilling SelRes: Yes for specific categories per Air Mobility Command rules
- Retired Reserve receiving pay: Yes (Category VI or similar)
- Awaiting Pay Reserve: Limited
- IRR and Standby: Generally not eligible

### How Space-A Works

- Member registers at military passenger terminal
- Categories prioritize boarding (combat zone, emergency leave, drilling, retired, etc.)
- Show up, sign up, and wait
- Travel where space available

Space-A is free or very low cost but unpredictable. Members budget time for delays.

## Military Lodging

Military lodging on installations offers reduced rates compared to civilian hotels.

### Eligibility

- Active duty traveling: Full eligibility
- Drilling reservists on AT or ADOS orders: Full eligibility
- Retired members: Full eligibility for most lodging
- Family members: With sponsoring member

### Lodging Types

- Standard transient lodging on most installations
- Distinguished Visitor Quarters (DVQ) for senior members
- Specific cottages and recreation lodging
- Some have leisure travel options

## Performance Steps for I&I Admin

### Step 1 - Verify Member Status

When a reservist asks about benefits.

- Identify component (drilling SelRes, IRR, Retired Reserve, etc.)
- Verify good standing
- Identify family members eligible

### Step 2 - Brief on Specific Benefit

Provide accurate information.

- Eligibility for the specific benefit
- Family member access rules
- Process for accessing the benefit
- Any current policy changes

### Step 3 - Coordinate ID Card if Needed

Some benefits require updated ID.

- Member ID might need updating
- Family member dependent IDs might need updating
- Coordinate RAPIDS appointment

### Step 4 - Direct to Specific Resources

Different benefits have different administrators.

- Commissary: DeCA (Defense Commissary Agency)
- Exchange: AAFES, Navy Exchange Command, Marine Corps Exchange
- MWR: Local installation MWR office
- Space-A: Air Mobility Command

## Common Member Questions

### "I bring my friend to the commissary?"

No. Commissary access requires valid ID. Members might bring authorized family with their own ID.

### "What about my parents?"

Parents are not authorized dependents for benefits. Specific exceptions for ITO travel and survivor benefits.

### "I use the commissary as a Reserve retiree awaiting pay?"

Yes per current policy expansion. Verify current rules.

### "How do I shop at the exchange online?"

Direct member to ShopMyExchange.com. They will need military or veteran ID verification.

### "I take Space-A flights as a drilling reservist?"

Yes for many destinations. Categories and priorities apply. Direct to Air Mobility Command Space-A page.

### "What MWR facilities my spouse use?"

Full access for spouse with valid ID, accompanied or unaccompanied per facility rules.

## Common I&I Errors on QOL Benefits

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **Outdated commissary policy cited.** Recent expansions have changed access. Verify current policy.
2. **Saying friends accompany.** Only authorized ID holders access most facilities.
3. **Wrong category for Space-A.** Different components have different priority. Verify.
4. **Family member access overstated.** Some benefits require sponsoring member presence. Some allow standalone access. Verify.
5. **Online shopping access not mentioned.** Recent ShopMyExchange expansion is a benefit many do not know about.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Quality of life benefits administration touches.

- **DeCA (Commissary)**: Commissary questions
- **AAFES, NEX, MCX**: Exchange questions
- **Local Installation MWR**: MWR program access
- **Air Mobility Command**: Space-A travel
- **RAPIDS / DEERS**: ID card and dependent verification

## Family Programs

Beyond facilities, MWR includes family programs supporting reservist families.

- New Parent Support Program
- Family Advocacy Program
- Marine Corps Family Team Building
- Specific deployment support programs
- Resource and Referral services

These programs are particularly valuable for reservist families who might be geographically separated from active duty resources.

## Related Roles

Quality of life benefits affect every reservist family.

- **Marines** access benefits with their own ID. Family members access with appropriate IDs.
- **Leaders** brief members on QOL benefits as part of family readiness.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member family programs and benefits awareness.
