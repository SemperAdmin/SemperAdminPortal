## Federal Hiring Preference

Per 5 USC 2108<Citation source="5 USC 2108" /> and the Veterans Preference Act, reservists with qualifying service receive preference in federal hiring. Two main categories.

### 5-Point Preference

Members qualify for 5-point preference with.

- Honorable service during a period of war
- Specific qualifying campaigns or expeditions
- Service in specific theaters of operation
- Specific medals or decorations

The 5 points add to the candidate's score on civil service rating.

### 10-Point Preference

Members qualify for 10-point preference with.

- Service-connected disability
- Purple Heart recipient
- Specific other categories per veterans preference rules

The 10 points add to the candidate's score, providing significant hiring advantage.

### Veterans Recruitment Appointment (VRA)

Some agencies hire under VRA authority for specific positions.

- Members eligible based on service
- Direct hiring without competitive examination
- Limited to specific position grades and types

## How Federal Preference Works

When applying for federal jobs.

1. Member applies for federal position
2. Application includes verification of service (DD-214s)
3. Federal HR applies preference points to candidate's score
4. Candidate receives priority based on combined score
5. Hiring decision considers preference per rules

## USERRA Protections

Per the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act, reservists have civilian employment protections.

### Reemployment Rights

A reservist absent from civilian employment for military service has rights to.

- Reemployment in the position they would have attained had military service not interrupted
- Same seniority, status, and pay
- Continued health insurance coverage during service (with limits)
- Pension benefit accrual during service

### Eligibility

USERRA covers most military service.

- Active duty (mobilization, AT, ADOS, etc.)
- Inactive duty training (drill weekends)
- Funeral honors duty
- Initial active duty for training

### Time Limits

Reemployment must be requested within specific timeframes after military service ends.

- Less than 31 days service: Next regular work period
- 31-180 days: Within 14 days
- 181+ days: Within 90 days

Members exceeding these timeframes may lose USERRA protection.

### Member Responsibilities

To use USERRA, the member must.

- Provide notice to civilian employer of military duty (oral or written)
- Have served honorably (or be still serving)
- Apply for reemployment within the time limit
- Total military service does not exceed 5 years (with exceptions for specific categories)

### Employer Obligations

Civilian employers must.

- Hold the member's job (or equivalent) during military service
- Reemploy upon return per USERRA rules
- Continue benefits during specific periods
- Not discriminate against reservists for military service

USERRA enforcement is through the Department of Labor, Department of Justice, and federal courts.

## State Benefits

Each state has unique veteran and reservist benefits. Common categories.

### Education Benefits

Many states offer.

- Tuition waivers at state colleges and universities
- State GI Bill programs
- Loan repayment for state employment
- Apprenticeship programs

Examples vary widely. Texas, California, New York, Florida, and others have substantial state programs.

### Tax Benefits

Many states offer.

- State income tax exemption on military pay (varies)
- State income tax exemption on retirement pay (varies)
- Property tax reductions for veterans (often disabled veterans)
- Sales tax exemption for specific purchases

### Professional Licensing

Many states streamline professional licensing for veterans.

- License waivers based on military training
- Reduced licensing fees
- Expedited processing for veterans
- Recognition of military credentials

Common licenses affected: Commercial drivers license, healthcare provider, electrician, plumber, etc.

### State Employment Preference

Many states have hiring preference for veterans similar to federal preference.

- Points added to civil service scores
- Direct hiring authorities
- Veterans-only positions in some agencies

### Other State Benefits

- State park access (free or reduced fee)
- Hunting and fishing license discounts
- Free or reduced cost vehicle registration
- Specialized license plates
- State-funded burial benefits
- State-specific welcome home programs

## Common Member Questions

### "Does my civilian employer have to give me my job back?"

Yes per USERRA, with conditions. Member must apply within timeframes and have served honorably.

### "How do I claim federal hiring preference?"

Apply for federal jobs with documentation showing eligible service. Federal HR applies preference automatically.

### "What state benefits am I eligible for?"

Varies by state. Direct to your state Department of Veterans Affairs.

### "Can my civilian employer fire me for going to drill?"

No per USERRA. Termination for military service is unlawful discrimination.

### "What if my employer doesn't reemploy me?"

USERRA enforcement through Department of Labor Veterans Employment and Training Service (VETS). File complaint or seek legal counsel.

### "Do I need to tell my employer I'm in the Reserves?"

USERRA requires notice of military service obligations. Specific timing varies.

## Common I&I Errors on Federal and State Benefits

These hit at the I&I shop.

1. **State benefits not addressed.** Members assume only federal benefits exist. Direct to state resources.
2. **USERRA basics not briefed.** Members do not know their employment protections. Brief during initial counseling and pre-mobilization.
3. **Federal preference confused with VA benefits.** Federal hiring preference and VA disability are different. Brief separately.
4. **USERRA time limits not communicated.** Members miss reemployment deadlines. Brief on timing.
5. **Documentation not pre-coordinated.** Members need DD-214s and service records for applications. Coordinate in advance.

## Coordination Touchpoints

Federal and state benefits processing touches.

- **Federal Office of Personnel Management (OPM)**: Federal hiring preference
- **Department of Labor VETS**: USERRA enforcement
- **State Departments of Veterans Affairs**: State benefits
- **Member's civilian employer HR**: USERRA coordination
- **MCRSC**: For service documentation

## Pre-Mobilization Civilian Employment Counseling

Before mobilization, brief members on civilian employment matters.

- Notify civilian employer of mobilization in writing
- Identify key documentation to take with you
- Brief on USERRA reemployment rights
- Brief on continued health insurance options
- Coordinate any benefit decisions with civilian employer

This counseling protects the member's civilian career during military service.

## Related Roles

Federal and state benefits affect every reservist's civilian life.

- **Marines** apply for federal jobs with preference, use USERRA when needed, and access state benefits.
- **Leaders** brief members on civilian employment protections during pre-mobilization.
- **Commanders** at I&I support member benefit awareness through career counseling.
