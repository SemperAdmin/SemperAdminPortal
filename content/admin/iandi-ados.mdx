## ADOS Overview

Per MCO 1001.59<Citation source="MCO 1001.59" /> and MCRAMM Chapter 10, Active Duty for Operational Support (ADOS) authorizes Reserve members on active duty for specific operational missions distinct from.

- Annual Training (AT) - routine training
- Active Duty for Training (ADT) - specific training requirements
- Mobilization - involuntary recall during emergency

ADOS specifically supports operational missions.

## ADOS Authority

Per 10 USC 12301<Citation source="10 USC 12301" />(d).

- Voluntary active duty authority
- Member volunteers for specific mission
- Command and HQMC approve
- Specific operational need documented

## ADOS vs Other Active Duty Categories

| Category | Authority | Trigger | Voluntary |
|----------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| AT | 12301(b) | Routine training | No (mandatory) |
| ADT | 12301(d) | Training need | Member volunteers |
| ADOS | 12301(d) | Operational mission | Member volunteers typically |
| Mobilization | 12302/12304 | Emergency | No (involuntary) |

ADOS is similar to ADT but for operational vs training purposes.

Common errors.

2. **Active duty status delayed.** Member reports but UD/MIPS delayed.
3. **Pay processing issues.** Active duty pay not activated correctly.
4. **End of ADOS not processed.** Active duty pay continues after ADOS.
