## Mobilization Family Impact

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10 and MCO 1754.6<Citation source="MCO 1754.6" />, mobilization significantly affects member's family. The family experiences.

- Member's extended absence
- Income changes (typically increase due to active duty pay)
- Healthcare transitions (TRICARE Prime active duty family)
- Childcare and family responsibilities shifting to remaining parent or caregiver
- Specific emotional and practical adjustments

Strong family support reduces stress and supports member's mobilization performance.

## Quality Control on Family Support

Common errors.

1. **Family not briefed.** Family unprepared for mobilization.
2. **FCP missing.** Member with FCP requirement deployed without one.
3. **Healthcare transition not coordinated.** Family loses access during transition.
4. **Family Readiness Group not engaged.** Family disconnected from unit support.

## Related Roles

Family support affects every mobilizing member's family.

- **Marines** coordinate with family before mobilization and during deployment.
- **Leaders** at units support member family preparation.
- **Commanders** at I&I oversee unit family support programs.
