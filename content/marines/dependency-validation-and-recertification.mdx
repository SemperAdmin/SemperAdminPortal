## What Dependency Validation Is

Dependency validation is the periodic review of dependent status. It exists to.

- Confirm that claimed dependents still meet eligibility criteria.
- Identify dependents who have aged out, married, become self-supporting, or otherwise lost status.
- Prevent erroneous BAH and other entitlement payments.
- Address audit findings around dependency.

### Who Owns Validation

- HQMC Dependency Determination Section. Adjudicates and tracks at HQMC level.
- IPAC. Processes recertifications at installation level.
- S-1. Coordinates with Marine and tracks recertification compliance.
- Marine. Initiates recertification when notified or when triggered.

## The Annual Recertification Cycle

### MARADMIN 661/19 Framework

MARADMIN 661/19<Citation source="MARADMIN 661/19" /> established the annual dependency validation cycle. Subsequent MARADMINs may update timing or scope.

### Annual Recertification Categories

- All DD 137 secondary dependency cases.
- Student dependents (age 21-22).
- Incapacitated adult child dependents.
- Court-ordered support BAH-Diff cases.
- Family Care Plan (annual NAVMC 11800<Citation source="NAVMC 11800" /> update via MOL).

### Marines Not Subject to Annual Recert

Most spouse and biological/adopted child cases under age 21 do NOT require annual recertification. Status changes (marriage, divorce, death, custody) trigger updates, but no annual cycle.

## Which Dependents Require Recertification

### Secondary Dependency (DD 137 Cases)

ALL DD Form 137<Citation source="DD Form 137" /> cases require annual recertification. This includes.

- Incapacitated adult child.
- Student (age 21-22).
- Legal custody ward.
- Parent.
- Parent-in-law.
- Stepparent.
- In-loco parentis.

### Student Dependents (Specific Documentation)

For students (21-22), require ANNUAL.

- Letter from school registrar OR National Student Clearinghouse enrollment verification.
- Confirmation of full-time enrollment for the new academic year.
- Updated Worksheet for Determining Financial Support.

### Incapacitated Adult Child (Specific Documentation)

For incapacitated adult children, require ANNUAL.

- Updated medical sufficiency statement from approved medical provider.
- Updated Worksheet for Determining Financial Support.
- Confirmation of unmarried status.

### Parent, Parent-in-Law, Stepparent, In-Loco Parentis

Require ANNUAL.

- Updated Worksheet for Determining Financial Support.
- Confirmation of residence (if USIP card issued, residence confirmation; if BAH only, no residence confirmation needed).
- Updated supporting documents if any have changed.

### Legal Custody Ward

Require ANNUAL.

- Confirmation court order remains in effect.
- Updated Worksheet for Determining Financial Support.
- Confirmation ward still resides with sponsor (or institutional care exception).
- Confirmation ward is unmarried.

### Court-Ordered Support BAH-Diff

Require ANNUAL.

- Updated court order if amount has changed.
- Confirmation support amount still meets BAH-Diff threshold.
- Confirmation Marine is still without dependents (BAH-without-dependents rate).

### Family Care Plan

Annual NAVMC 11800<Citation source="NAVMC 11800" /> update via Marine Online FCP module. See [Family Care Plan (FCP)](/marines/family-care-plan).

## Recertification Triggers and Timelines

### Annual Cycle Trigger

- Approximately 12 months from prior validation or initial approval.
- HQMC publishes specific timing per MARADMIN.
- IPAC notifies Marine via S-1 with submission deadline.

### Event-Based Triggers Outside Annual Cycle

- Student status change (graduation, withdrawal).
- Marriage of student or incapacitated adult child (loss of dependency).
- Death of dependent.
- Custody modification.
- Sponsor PCS.
- Significant change in support amounts (court-ordered or voluntary).

### Submission Deadline

- Typically 30 to 60 days from notification.
- Specific deadline in IPAC notification letter or MOL message.
- Late submission triggers DEERS removal proceedings.

## Documentation Refresh Requirements

### What To Refresh

- Worksheet for Determining Financial Support (always).
- Type-specific documents (medical sufficiency for incapacitated child, school letter for student, etc.).
- Tax return showing dependent claim (if used in lieu of Worksheet).
- Court orders (custody, support).

### What Does NOT Need to Refresh

- Birth certificates (unchanging).
- Marriage certificates (unchanging unless new marriage).
- Adoption decrees (unchanging).
- Death certificates (unchanging).

### How to Submit

- Through S-1 to IPAC.
- IPAC routes to HQMC Dependency Determination Section.
- DEERS update upon approval.
- OMPF document submission per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />.

## Common Loss of Entitlement Scenarios

### Student Aging Out (Age 23)

Student dependent ages out at 23 regardless of continued enrollment. DEERS removal automatic.

### Student Marrying

A married individual cannot be a student dependent. Marriage of the student triggers loss of dependency.

### Incapacitated Adult Child Recovering

If medical sufficiency statement no longer supports incapacitation, dependency lost.

### Parent or Parent-in-Law Self-Supporting

If parent's income exceeds the over-half (50%) test threshold, dependency lost.

### Ward Reaching Age 21

Wards age out at 21 regardless of continued residence or support.

### Court Order Expiration (Ward)

If the legal custody court order expires or is rescinded, dependency lost.

### Failure to Recertify

The most common loss. Marine ignores recertification notice or misses deadline. DEERS removal automatic.

### Death of Dependent

Triggers immediate removal upon documentation, not at next recertification cycle.

## Audit Weight in IPAC Inspections

### High-Weight Category

The dependency category is one of the highest-weight audit categories in IPAC inspections.

- $63M annual audit category across the Marine Corps.
- Findings impact unit and IPAC ratings.
- Repeated failures can trigger investigations.

### What Auditors Check

- Timeliness of NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> submissions for life events (10 working day rule).
- Annual recertification compliance for required cases.
- Supporting document completeness and currency.
- DEERS-MCTFS alignment.
- OMPF document presence per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />.
- Loss-of-entitlement timeliness (BAH stopped on time after dependent loss).

### Why It Matters at the Unit Level

- S-1 personnel face career impact for poor case files.
- Marines face debt collection for overpayments identified during audit.
- Unit leadership accountable for systemic issues.

## The HQMC Dependency Determination Section Role

### What They Do

- Adjudicate complex cases.
- Manage the annual recertification cycle.
- Investigate non-support complaints.
- Provide policy guidance.
- Coordinate with DFAS for entitlement adjustments.

### How They Coordinate

- IPAC routes cases to HQMC.
- HQMC adjudicates and notifies IPAC.
- IPAC implements DEERS and MCTFS updates.

### Marine and Family Programs Branch (MF) Context

The HQMC Dependency Determination Section sits within the Marine and Family Programs Branch (MF). MF Branch operates programs that may inform or follow from dependency decisions.

- Casualty Assistance Program. For Marine death (CACO, LTAP, Gold Star, Funeral Honors, Repatriation).
- Child and Youth Programs (CYP). Childcare and youth programs.
- Exceptional Family Member Program (EFMP). For dependents with special medical or educational needs.
- School Liaison Program (SLP). For school-aged children.
- Career Services. FMEAP, SkillBridge, COOL, USMAP, TRP.
- Education Services. LSP, VolEd, Library.
- Family Readiness Services. IR&R, MCFTB, PFMP, Sponsorship, UPFRP.
- Marine for Life Network.
- CREDO. Religious enrichment.

For dependency decisions involving special needs, EFMP coordination is recommended. For dependency decisions affecting financial readiness, PFMP can support.

## How to Respond to a Recertification Notice

### Step 1. Read the Notice

- Identify which dependent requires recertification.
- Note the specific documentation required.
- Note the submission deadline.

### Step 2. Gather Documents

- New school letter (for student).
- New medical sufficiency statement (for incapacitated adult child).
- Updated tax return or Worksheet for Determining Financial Support.
- Type-specific updates per DD 137.

### Step 3. Complete Forms

- Updated DD Form 137<Citation source="DD Form 137" /> if required.
- Updated NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> if dependent information changed.

### Step 4. Submit Through S-1

- S-1 reviews for completeness.
- S-1 routes to IPAC.
- IPAC routes to HQMC Dependency Determination Section.

### Step 5. Confirm Approval

- Watch for DEERS confirmation.
- Watch for LES showing continued BAH.
- Verify in milConnect that dependent enrollment remains active.

### Step 6. Document Retention

- Keep approved recertification documents.
- Update personal calendar for next annual cycle.

## Common Errors

### Ignoring the Notice

Marines often think recertification is optional or assume someone else handles it. The Marine is responsible.

### Late Submission

Submitting after the deadline triggers DEERS removal. Re-establishment requires full re-application, often with retroactive entitlement loss.

### Stale Documentation

Submitting last year's school letter or last year's medical sufficiency statement. Recertification requires CURRENT documents.

### Failure to Confirm Approval

Marine submits but never confirms approval. Issues identified months later when LES changes or DEERS access denied.

### Failure to Coordinate Multiple Cases

Marines with multiple secondary dependents may need to recertify all simultaneously. Track each.

### Ignoring Loss of Eligibility Triggers

Student graduates but Marine continues to claim. Audit finding and back-debt likely.
