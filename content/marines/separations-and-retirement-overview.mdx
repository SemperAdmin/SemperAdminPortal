## Start Here If You Are New to Separations and Retirement

The Marine Corps separations and retirement framework is governed by MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> (MARCORSEPMAN). At HQMC, the Manpower Management Separation and Retirement (MMSR) branch operates four sections that handle different aspects of the lifecycle. MMSR-2 handles enlisted and officer separation matters. MMSR-4 handles disability separations, retirements, and limited duty. MMSR-5 handles reservist matters. MMSR-6 handles retired services and pay. The framework covers two main paths. Active duty separation (voluntary and involuntary) and active duty retirement (length of service or TERA when authorized). The Reserve component has its own retirement framework based on qualifying service, retirement points, and age-based pay start. The Disability Evaluation System (DES) overlaps both separation and retirement. Common failure points include incorrect SPD or RE code assignment, missing Transition Readiness Seminar completion, late retirement request submission, incomplete medical documentation, leave balance errors at EAS, and outdated dependency data. Per MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> and MMSR published guidance.

## The Governing Manual

### MCO 1900.16 (MARCORSEPMAN)

The Marine Corps Separation and Retirement Manual. Sometimes called MARCORSEPMAN. Covers.

- Separation procedures and characterizations.
- Retirement procedures and types.
- Disability separation and retirement framework.
- Limited duty framework.
- Document standards.
- Procedural rights.

The MARCORSEPMAN is the primary reference for any separation or retirement question.

### Title 10, U.S.C. (Federal Statutory Authority)

DoD-wide separation and retirement framework derives from Title 10 of the U.S. Code.

- 10 U.S.C. 12731<Citation source="10 U.S.C. 12731" />-12732 govern Reserve retirement.
- Title 10 Chapter 65 governs disability separation.
- Various other Title 10 chapters cover specific scenarios.

### DoD Financial Management Regulation (DoD FMR)

Volume 7 covers retired pay framework including high-3, BRS, and Reserve pay computation.

## The Lifecycle Perspective

A Marine's career ends through one of three primary pathways.

### Pathway 1. Active Duty Separation

Voluntary or involuntary departure before retirement eligibility.

- Voluntary. EAS, Early Release, Convenience, Hardship, Education programs.
- Involuntary. Misconduct, Performance, Standards failure, Drug abuse.

The Voluntary Separations and Involuntary Separations leaves cover these pathways.

### Pathway 2. Active Duty Retirement

Departure with pension eligibility.

- Length of service (20+ years).
- Temporary Early Retirement Authority (TERA, 15-19 years when authorized).

The Retirement Types and Eligibility, Retirement Timeline, and Retired Pay leaves cover these pathways.

### Pathway 3. Reserve Retirement

Reserve Marines retire under a different framework.

- 20 qualifying years.
- Pay starts at age 60 (with NDAA 2008/2011 reductions possible).

The Reserve Retirement leaf covers this framework.

### The Disability Evaluation System (DES)

Crosses both separation and retirement.

- DES outcomes can result in separation (Unfit with severance, Unfit without severance) OR retirement (TDRL, PDRL).

The Disability Evaluation System and Limited Duty leaves cover this framework.

## Active Duty vs Reserve Retirement Distinctions

### Active Duty Retirement

- Based on YEARS OF ACTIVE SERVICE.
- 20+ years required for length of service retirement.
- Pay begins immediately on retirement.
- High-3 or BRS pay calculation.

### Reserve Retirement

- Based on QUALIFYING YEARS (50+ retirement points per anniversary year).
- 20 qualifying years required.
- Pay begins at age 60 (with NDAA 2008/2011 reductions for qualifying active duty service).
- Pay calculation based on retirement points (P/360 × 0.025 × B = $/MONTH).

The Reserve Retirement leaf covers the qualifying year and points framework in detail.

## Common Failure Points

Per MMSR published guidance, the following are common errors that delay or compromise separations and retirements.

### Incorrect SPD or RE Code Assignment

The Separation Program Designator (SPD) and Reenlistment Eligibility (RE) codes drive future eligibility. Incorrect codes can prevent reenlistment in another component or affect VA benefits.

The SPD and RE Codes leaf covers the framework.

### Missing TRS Completion

Transition Readiness Seminar (TRS) completion is required for transition. Missing TRS delays the separation or retirement.

The Transition Requirements leaf covers TRS.

### Late Retirement Request Submission

Retirement requests should be submitted 6-18 months before the requested separation date. Late submissions risk processing delays.

The Retirement Timeline leaf covers the recommended timing.

### Incomplete Medical Documentation

Medical clearance, final physical (active duty only), and documentation requirements must be complete before separation or retirement.

### Leave Balance Errors at EAS

Leave sell-back is a one-time framework with a 60-day career cap. Errors in leave balance can affect final pay.

The Terminal Leave page in the Leave and Liberty parent group covers the framework.

### Awards and OMPF Not Finalized

Final awards must be processed and entered in the OMPF before separation. Awards processed after separation create administrative complications.

### Dependency Data Not Updated

DEERS dependency data must be current at separation. Outdated data affects survivor benefits, transitional healthcare, and other entitlements.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What governs separations and retirement?

MCO 1900.16<Citation source="MCO 1900.16" /> (MARCORSEPMAN). Plus Title 10 of the U.S. Code and DoD FMR Volume 7<Citation source="DoD FMR Volume 7" /> for retired pay.

What is MMSR?

Manpower Management Separation and Retirement at HQMC. Four sections. MMSR-2 (enlisted/officer personnel), MMSR-4 (disability/LimDu), MMSR-5 (reserve), MMSR-6 (retired services).

How do active duty and Reserve retirement differ?

Active duty. Years of active service. 20+ years for length of service retirement. Pay begins immediately. Reserve. Qualifying years (50+ retirement points). 20 qualifying years required. Pay begins at age 60 (with NDAA 2008/2011 reductions possible).

What if I am on limited duty or in DES?

Coordinate with MMSR-4. Limited Duty and Disability Evaluation System leaves cover the framework.

What if I am a Reserve Marine?

Coordinate with MMSR-5. Reserve Retirement leaf and Reserve Component Survivor Benefit Plan leaf cover the framework.

What if I am already retired?

Contact MMSR-6 for retired services and pay. Phone (703) 784-9311. Email smb_manpower_mmsr6@usmc.mil.

What are the common failure points?

Incorrect SPD or RE codes, missing TRS, late retirement request, incomplete medical documentation, leave balance errors, unfinalized awards, outdated dependency data.

How does TERA work?

Temporary Early Retirement Authority. Retirement after 15-19 years when authorized via MARADMIN. Requires specific authorization from CMC. The Retirement Types and Eligibility leaf covers TERA.

When should I start planning my retirement?

36 months before the requested retirement date for the most thorough planning. The Retirement Timeline leaf covers the seven-phase framework.

How does my Reserve service count toward retirement?

Through the qualifying year framework. 50+ retirement points (including 15 membership points) per anniversary year. The Reserve Retirement leaf covers the points framework in detail.
