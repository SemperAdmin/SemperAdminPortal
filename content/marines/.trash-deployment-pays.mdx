## Start Here If You Are New to Deployment Pays

When you deploy to a designated area, three pay items typically activate beyond your regular basic pay, BAH, and BAS. Hostile Fire Pay or Imminent Danger Pay at $225 per month for being in a designated area or exposed to a hostile fire event. Family Separation Allowance at $250 per month for being separated from dependents over 30 days. The Combat Zone Tax Exclusion makes most of your pay during the deployment federally tax-free.

These three deployment pays are the primary financial reason deployment income hits harder than garrison income. This page walks you through them from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- Hostile Fire Pay (HFP) and Imminent Danger Pay (IDP) framework, including the no-concurrent-payment rule
- The three Family Separation Allowance (FSA) types and which one applies to your situation
- The Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (CZTE) tax treatment
- How HFP/IDP gets certified through the chain of command
- The "transit through" exception that disqualifies IDP for travelers passing through designated areas
- Common LES errors that produce missing deployment pay

Every fact on this page comes from DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 10 (October 2024), Chapter 27 (October 2025), Chapter 13 (PAC Program), Title 37 U.S.C. sections 351 and 427, Title 26 U.S.C. section 112 (CZTE), and current DTMO and DFAS Marine Corps Pay guidance.

## Hostile Fire Pay (HFP) and Imminent Danger Pay (IDP)

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 10 (October 2024) and Title 37 U.S.C. section 351, HFP and IDP are two distinct entitlements paid to Marines exposed to hostile fire events or assigned to designated imminent danger areas.

### The No-Concurrent-Payment Rule

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.1, a Marine is not authorized to receive concurrent payments for hostile fire and imminent danger duty. In any given month, the Marine receives HFP OR IDP, not both.

When both situations apply (a Marine is in a designated IDP area AND is exposed to a hostile fire event during the same month), the HFP rate (full $225 monthly) typically supersedes the IDP daily prorated amount because HFP is paid as the maximum monthly rate for the month of the hostile fire event.

### HFP Eligibility

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.1.1, HFP is paid at the rate of $225 per month when, as certified by the appropriate commander, a Marine is subjected to hostile fire, explosion of hostile mines, or other hostile action (a "hostile fire event"), and who.

- Performs duty in a hostile fire area, OR
- Is exposed to a hostile fire event, OR
- Is on duty during a month in an area where a hostile event occurred that placed the Marine in grave danger of physical injury, OR
- Is killed, injured, or wounded by a hostile fire event.

HFP is the maximum monthly rate ($225) for the entire month of the hostile fire event. HFP is NOT prorated.

### IDP Eligibility

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.1.2, IDP is paid on a daily prorated basis, not to exceed $225 per month, when a Marine is on official duty in a designated IDP area. The threshold for designation is the threat of physical harm or imminent danger based on civil insurrection, civil war, terrorism, or wartime conditions in a designated foreign area.

The DTMO publishes the current IDP areas list at https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Imminent-Danger-Pay/. Recent additions to the IDP areas (per Chapter 10 Table 10-1).

- Ukraine (added per OASD M&RA Memo dated 13 June 2023).
- Bab-al-Mandeb Strait, Gulf of Aden, and Red Sea (added per ASD M&RA Memo dated 18 December 2024).
- Gaza Strip and Israel's Mediterranean Territorial Seas (added per OASD M&RA Memo dated 7 May 2025).

The full list of designated areas, including land areas, water areas, and airspace, is in Chapter 10 Table 10-1.

### IDP Daily Proration

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.2, IDP is payable on a prorated daily basis not to exceed the monthly rate of $225. Proration does not apply to the 31st of a month for Active and Reserve component Marines on active duty for 30 days or more.

Examples per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.2.

- A Marine on active duty for more than 30 days in an IDP area for the period 31 March through 29 April receives IDP only for 1 April through 29.
- A Marine on active duty for more than 30 days in an IDP area for the same period AND exposed to a hostile fire event on 31 March is entitled to HFP for the entire month of March AND IDP for 1 April through 29.
- A Marine on active duty for less than 30 days in an IDP area for the period 29 March through 20 April is entitled to IDP for 29 March through 31 March AND 1 April through 20, totaling 23 days.

### IDP Geographic Coverage

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 3.2.

- Airspace coverage. When an IDP area designation specifically includes airspace, Marines who perform official duty while flying over the area are eligible for IDP, even without landing in the area. When airspace is not specifically designated, Marines performing duty over the area are not entitled to IDP unless they land.
- Vessel coverage. A Marine performing duty on a vessel performing operational duty while in a designated IDP area is eligible for IDP.
- Transit Exception. A Marine is NOT considered on official duty in a designated IDP area if the Marine is in the area while on leave from a duty station outside the IDP area, while merely transiting through the area between two points both outside the IDP area, or solely for personal convenience.

### HFP Certification Flow

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 3.1, the appropriate commander certifies that the Marine met the requirements for HFP for a given month.

Certification requirements.

- The lowest level of command that includes all the vessels, aircraft, or units subjected to the hostile fire event.
- For a single vessel, the vessel's commanding officer certifies for all members on board.
- For two or more vessels, the commanding officer of the unit including all the vessels certifies.

The certification includes the name and payroll identification number of each Marine entitled, a brief description of the incident, and when and where the incident occurred. The certification routes through the geographic Combatant Commander exercising operational control over the Marine at the time of the hostile fire event to the servicing financial support office.

A death certificate or injury report substitutes for the certification if the document establishes that the cause of the death or injury was due to hostile fire or a hostile fire event.

### Hospitalization Continuation (PAC Program)

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.2, a Marine entitled to HFP and/or IDP who incurs a wound, injury, or illness in the line of duty while serving in a combat operation, combat zone, hostile fire area, or while exposed to a hostile fire event (regardless of location), and who is hospitalized for treatment, continues to receive HFP and/or IDP for up to 12 months from the date of hospitalization under the Pay and Allowance Continuation (PAC) Program. Chapter 13 governs PAC Program eligibility.

### Captured or Missing Status

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.1, a Marine entitled to HFP and/or IDP immediately before entering a status of missing, missing-in-action, interned in a foreign country, or captured by a hostile force continues to be credited with HFP and/or IDP for each month while in that status. Chapter 34 governs the broader missing-status pay framework.

### IDP and Leave

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.3.

- A Marine who takes leave in the IDP area where assigned for duty remains entitled to IDP.
- A Marine who takes leave in an IDP area where NOT assigned for duty is NOT eligible for IDP.
- A Marine who takes leave in an area not designated as an IDP area is not eligible for IDP for the period of absence.
- A Marine assigned for duty in an IDP area who performs temporary duty in an area NOT designated as an IDP area is not eligible for IDP for the period of absence.

## Family Separation Allowance (FSA)

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 27 (October 2025) and Title 37 U.S.C. section 427, FSA compensates a Marine with dependents for added expenses incurred because of an enforced family separation.

### Effective Rate

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.3.2, effective 1 October 2002, FSA is payable in a monthly amount of $250.

### General Eligibility

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.1, FSA is payable to.

- Marines with dependents.
- Marines married to another member of the uniformed services regardless of any other dependency status.

FSA is payable in addition to any other allowance or per diem to which a Marine is entitled. The Marine is not allowed to receive more than one FSA payment for the same period, even if qualified for multiple FSA types.

The Marine submits DD Form 1561 (Statement to Substantiate Payment of Family Separation Allowance) to substantiate entitlement.

### The Three FSA Types

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.3.1, three distinct FSA types apply to specific separation situations.

#### FSA-R (Restricted)

Per paragraph 2.3.1.1.

- The Marine's dependents (including dependents acquired after the effective date of PCS orders) do not live in the vicinity of the Marine's homeport or PDS, AND
- Their transportation to or near the PDS is not authorized at government expense.

Or alternatively. Transportation of dependents IS authorized at government expense, but the Marine elects an unaccompanied tour because a dependent cannot accompany due to certified medical reasons.

FSA-R is the typical case for a Marine on a dependent-restricted overseas tour.

#### FSA-S (Ship)

Per paragraph 2.3.1.2. The Marine is on duty aboard a ship and EITHER.

- The ship is away from the homeport continuously for more than 30 days, OR
- The Marine is under orders to remain on board a ship while at homeport, and the duty on board the ship is for a continuous period of more than 30 days.

When a Marine is under orders to remain on board a ship while at homeport, the days on the ship while at homeport count toward the more than 30 continuous days, except when the Marine is required to stay on board for disciplinary reasons.

For FSA-S, the dependents are NOT required to reside in the vicinity of the homeport.

#### FSA-T (Temporary)

Per paragraph 2.3.1.3. The Marine is on TDY or TAD away from the PDS or homeport continuously for more than 30 days, AND the dependents do not reside at or near the TDY or TAD station.

Daily commute exception. If the Marine is able to commute daily (or commutes daily) to the location of the dependents, the dependents are considered to be at or near the temporary duty location. The Marine does not qualify for FSA-T.

### Dual Military FSA

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.3.4.1, FSA is payable to a Marine married to another Service Member regardless of any other non-active-duty dependents, provided the Service Members were residing together immediately before being separated by reason of military orders.

#### One FSA Per Married Couple Per Month (General Rule)

Per paragraph 2.3.4.1.1, except as provided in paragraph 2.3.4.2, only one monthly FSA is paid with respect to a married military couple for any month. Payment goes to the Service Member whose orders resulted in the separation. If both members receive orders requiring departure on the same day, payment goes to the senior member.

#### Sequential Entitlement

Per paragraph 2.3.4.1.2, if a Marine meets the requirements for FSA but the entitlement is precluded by an existing entitlement status of the spouse, the second member is allowed to immediately become entitled to FSA upon termination of the spouse's status. The couple is allowed to qualify for sequential entitlements as long as military orders keep them continuously separated.

#### Subsequent Entitlement

Per paragraph 2.3.4.1.3, to qualify for a subsequent FSA, a married military couple no longer separated by military orders must reestablish a joint household and reside together.

#### Both Members Eligible Simultaneously (Dual Allowance)

Per paragraph 2.3.4.2, FSA is payable to BOTH married Service Members when they reside together with their dependents immediately before being separated from dependents by competent orders to assignments meeting the FSA-R, FSA-S, or FSA-T conditions. Each member's entitlement is determined individually. The dual allowance continues until one of the members is no longer assigned to a qualifying duty.

### Computation of Single or Multiple Periods

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 4.4, FSA is paid for periods of more than 30 days. A Marine on multiple short-duration TDY periods is allowed to combine the periods if they exceed 30 days continuous separation.

### Stop and Start Rules

Per Chapter 27 Table 27-1 (commencement) and Table 27-3 (stop dates), FSA starts on the date the separation begins and stops on the date the Marine returns to or near the dependent location, with specific rules covering ship return to homeport, temporary leave during deployment, and dependent travel.

## Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (CZTE)

Per Title 26 U.S.C. section 112 and Executive Orders designating specific combat zones, the Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (CZTE) is the tax treatment that excludes military pay from federal taxable income while a Marine serves in a designated combat zone.

### CZTE Is a Tax Treatment, Not a Separate Pay

The CZTE does not appear as a line item on the LES. Instead, the federal income tax line is reduced (or zeroed out) for the months the Marine qualifies. Marines see the effect on their LES as reduced or zero federal income tax withholding during qualifying months.

### Eligibility

Per IRS guidance and the underlying statute, CZTE applies to a Marine who serves in a combat zone designated by Executive Order, in a qualified hazardous duty area designated by statute, or in direct support of a combat zone.

Designated combat zones include (subject to current Executive Order designations).

- Afghanistan and Pakistan area.
- Arabian Peninsula area (Iraq, Syria, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, UAE, Bahrain, Jordan, Egypt, parts of Persian Gulf, Gulf of Aden, Gulf of Oman, Red Sea).
- Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (historical, related to Kosovo operations).

The full list and current status are at https://www.irs.gov/individuals/military/combat-zones.

### Exclusion Limits

For enlisted Marines, all military pay earned during any month of qualifying CZTE service is excluded from federal taxable income.

For officer Marines, the exclusion is capped at the amount of basic pay equivalent to the highest enlisted pay grade (E-9) plus HFP/IDP. Officer pay above this cap remains federally taxable.

The CZTE includes basic pay, special pays, bonuses paid during qualifying months (subject to specific timing rules), and reenlistment bonuses received in a qualifying month.

### Combat Zone vs. IDP Area

CZTE designation and IDP designation are separate. A Marine in a CZTE-designated combat zone is typically also in an IDP-designated area, but not always. Verify both designations against the current authoritative lists. The geographic boundaries are also different in some cases.

### Qualifying Service Days

A single qualifying day in a designated combat zone during a calendar month produces CZTE for the entire month for enlisted Marines (subject to the officer cap). The Marine does not have to serve the full month in the combat zone.

## Common Deployment Pay Errors and Fixes

Per Chapters 10, 27, and the Pay Problems and Fixes page, the four most common deployment pay errors.

### 1. HFP or IDP Not Started After Deployment Began

Cause. The reporting unit at the deployed location did not certify HFP/IDP eligibility through the chain to the financial support office. Fix. The unit commander certifies eligibility per Chapter 10 paragraph 3.1 (HFP) or paragraph 3.2 (IDP). The certification routes through the geographic Combatant Commander to the financial support office.

### 2. FSA Not Paid During Deployment Over 30 Days

Cause. The Marine did not submit DD Form 1561 to the reporting unit, or the reporting unit did not process the form. Fix. Submit DD Form 1561 immediately. Verify the FSA line item appears on the next LES at $250/month.

### 3. CZTE Not Applied to Federal Tax Withholding

Cause. The reporting unit did not flag the Marine's pay record as deployed to a CZTE-designated combat zone. Fix. Coordinate with the S-1 to verify the CZTE flag is set. Federal tax withholding adjusts in the next pay cycle. Tax already withheld during qualifying months is recovered through the Marine's federal tax return for the year.

### 4. IDP Stopped When the Marine Took Leave Outside the IDP Area

Cause. Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.3, IDP stops for the period of absence when the Marine takes leave in an area not designated as IDP. Fix. This is typically NOT an error. Verify the leave dates and accept the prorated IDP for the duty days only. If the Marine took leave in a different IDP area where assigned for duty, the IDP continues per paragraph 4.3.1.

## Verifying Deployment Pays on the LES

A monthly LES check during deployment catches errors fast.

### Step 1. Pull Your LES

Through MyPay each payday during deployment.

### Step 2. Verify the Deployment Pay Lines

Look for these line items.

- HFP or IDP (typically labeled HFP, IDP, or HFP/IDP combined). $225/month flat (HFP) or daily prorated up to $225 (IDP).
- FSA (FSA-R, FSA-S, or FSA-T). $250/month flat.
- CZTE indicator. Check the federal income tax line. Reduced or zero withholding during qualifying months indicates CZTE is applied.

### Step 3. Verify Against Deployment Orders

Compare the deployment orders, the geographic Combatant Command's designation list, and the LES line items. Confirm the dates align.

### Step 4. Resolve Discrepancies

Coordinate with your S-1. The reporting unit (I&I S-1 with direct UD/MIPS access, or a battalion S-1 routing through their PAC) routes corrections through DFAS Marine Corps Pay. The Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site covers the recovery flow.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

I deployed to Afghanistan in the middle of a month. Do I get the full $225 HFP that month?

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.1.1 and 2.2, if you were exposed to a hostile fire event during the month, yes. HFP pays the full monthly rate ($225) for the month of the hostile fire event without proration. If you were in the area but no hostile fire event occurred, you draw IDP at the daily prorated rate (capped at $225).

I deployed to a designated IDP area for 5 days. How much IDP do I get?

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 2.2 and the example calculations, IDP is prorated daily. 5 days produces 5/30ths of $225, or approximately $37.50. The proration does not apply to the 31st of a month for Marines on active duty for 30 days or more.

I deployed for 6 months but my dependents are at our PDS. Do I get FSA?

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.3.1.3 (FSA-T), no. FSA-T applies when the Marine is on TDY/TAD away from the PDS for more than 30 days AND the dependents do not reside at or near the TDY location. If the dependents remain at the original PDS and the Marine deploys, the Marine is technically on TDY/TAD away from the PDS, with dependents not at the TDY location. This produces FSA-T eligibility. Verify with your S-1.

I am dual military and we both deployed at the same time to different locations. Do we both get FSA?

Per Chapter 27 paragraph 2.3.4.2, yes. When both Service Members reside together with dependents immediately before being separated from dependents by competent orders to qualifying assignments, both members are eligible for the dual FSA allowance.

I am in the Gulf of Aden in a port visit but not on operational duty. Do I get IDP?

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 3.2.3.2, no. The transit-only exception disqualifies IDP for Marines merely transiting through an IDP area between two points both outside the IDP area. A port visit during transit is not "official duty in a designated IDP area." If the vessel is performing operational duty in the area, paragraph 3.2.2 applies and IDP is paid.

How does CZTE interact with my reenlistment bonus paid during deployment?

Per IRS guidance and Title 26 U.S.C. section 112, a reenlistment bonus paid during a qualifying CZTE month is fully tax-excluded for enlisted Marines. For officers, the bonus counts toward the officer-pay-cap calculation. The timing of bonus payment matters. Receipt during a CZTE qualifying month is the trigger.

I was hospitalized after a hostile fire event. Will my HFP continue?

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.2 and Chapter 13 (PAC Program), yes for up to 12 months from the date of hospitalization. HFP/IDP continues if the wound, injury, or illness was incurred in the line of duty while serving in a combat operation, combat zone, hostile fire area, or while exposed to a hostile fire event.

I went on R&R leave outside the IDP area. Did I lose IDP for those days?

Per Chapter 10 paragraph 4.3.3, yes. A Marine who takes leave in an area not designated as an IDP area is not eligible for IDP for the period of absence. R&R leave outside the area produces an IDP gap for the leave days.

## Where to Go for Help

### Routing by Issue

- HFP certification questions during a deployment. The unit commander at the lowest level including the affected unit. Routes through the geographic Combatant Commander to the financial support office.
- IDP eligibility questions. The DTMO IDP areas list at https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Imminent-Danger-Pay/. Verify with your S-1 against your deployment orders.
- FSA submissions and corrections. DD Form 1561 through the reporting unit (I&I S-1 with direct UD/MIPS access, or a battalion S-1 routing through their PAC).
- CZTE tax treatment questions. Internal Revenue Service guidance at https://www.irs.gov/individuals/military/combat-zones. Tax-specific questions to a tax professional or military legal assistance.
- DFAS Marine Corps Pay direct contact. Phone 1-888-332-7411 (DSN 312-571-9450 from overseas).
- PAC Program eligibility for hospitalized Marines. Chapter 13 framework, routed through the medical treatment facility and the unit S-1.
