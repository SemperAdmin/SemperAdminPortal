## Start Here If You Are New to Child Custody Changes

Child custody changed by court order. You have 10 WORKING DAYS to submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> to S-1 with a certified court order. The order may add a child to your dependents or remove a child. BAH-with-dependents triggers if a child becomes your dependent and was not previously. BAH-Diff applies if you pay court-ordered child support but the child is not a dependent (above the BAH-Diff threshold). DEERS enrollment for added dependents, removal for lost dependents. Family Separation Allowance considerations if custody affects family location. Per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />, MCO 7220.24<Citation source="MCO 7220.24" />, and DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 26.

This page walks you through child custody changes from your seat as the Marine. By the end you will know.

## Required Documents

### Primary Documents

- NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> (Dependency Application/Record of Emergency Data)
- Certified court order with raised seal

### Supporting Documents

- Birth certificate of the child
- Prior custody order (if this is a modification)
- Child support order if separate from custody order
- DD Form 137-3<Citation source="DD Form 137-3" /> (when claiming child as ward) for non-marital custody arrangements

## Custody Types

### Sole Legal and Physical Custody

One parent has both legal authority and physical residence of the child. Other parent typically has visitation rights and pays support.

### Joint Legal Custody

Both parents share legal decision-making authority. Physical custody may be sole or joint.

### Joint Physical Custody

Child resides with both parents on a court-ordered schedule.

### Split Custody

Multiple children, with different children residing with different parents.

### Primary Custody

One parent is designated primary residential custodian. Other parent has visitation. Common terminology in many state courts.

### Why It Matters

Marine Corps dependency determination follows the court order. The Marine claiming a child as a dependent must have either physical custody or court-ordered support exceeding the BAH-Diff threshold.

## The 10 Working Day Timeline

Day zero is the date of the court order.

### Steps Within 10 Working Days

- Day 1 to 3. Obtain certified copies of court order from the court.
- Day 4 to 5. Complete NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> to add or remove child.
- Day 6 to 8. S-1 appointment for review and submission.
- Day 9 to 10. S-1 routes to IPAC.

### Within 30 Days

- RAPIDS appointment for child DEERS enrollment if child is being added.
- Child must appear in person with documents for DEERS enrollment.

## BAH and BAH-Diff Implications

### Custody Adds a Child

If the court order grants you custody of a child you did not previously claim, BAH-with-dependents may activate (if you were at without-dependents rate). If you were already at with-dependents rate (married, other children), no rate change.

### Custody Removes a Child

If the court order removes a child from your custody, BAH-with-dependents continues IF you have other dependents (spouse, other children). If no other dependents remain, BAH adjusts to without-dependents rate the day AFTER the order.

### BAH-Diff for Court-Ordered Child Support

Per MCO 7220.24<Citation source="MCO 7220.24" /> and DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 26, BAH-Diff applies when.

- You are without dependents (BAH-without-dependents rate).
- You pay court-ordered child support.
- The support amount equals or exceeds the BAH-Diff threshold (currently set per DoD FMR).

BAH-Diff is a partial BAH amount, less than full BAH-with-dependents but more than BAH-without-dependents.

### Documenting Court-Ordered Support

- Submit court order to S-1.
- Include amount of monthly support.
- Annual recertification required.
- Update if support amount changes.

### Common Mistake - Voluntary Support

Voluntary support payments (not court-ordered) do not qualify for BAH-Diff. Only court-ordered support qualifies. If you make voluntary payments, get them formalized in a court order to qualify.

## Family Separation Allowance Considerations

### When FSA Applies

Family Separation Allowance (FSA) applies when a Marine is separated from dependents for 30 or more days due to military orders.

### Custody Changes and FSA

- Adding a custody-based dependent who lives away from your duty station may activate FSA if you are deployed or on long TDY.
- Removing custody may terminate FSA if the child was the basis for FSA.

### FSA Documentation

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 27, FSA requires.

- Dependent recognition (NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> on file).
- Orders separating Marine from dependent for 30+ days.
- Geographical separation documented.

See [Family Separation Allowance](/marines/family-separation-allowance) for full framework.

## DEERS Enrollment or Removal

### Adding a Child

- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with court order to S-1.
- Schedule RAPIDS appointment within 30 days.
- Child must appear with Marine and documents.
- DEERS enrollment activates TRICARE eligibility.

### Removing a Child

- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with court order to S-1.
- IPAC processes DEERS removal.
- Child's USIP card returned to RAPIDS if previously issued.
- TRICARE coverage terminates effective court order date.

### Joint Custody and DEERS

In joint custody, only ONE parent may claim the child as a TRICARE-eligible dependent in DEERS at a time. The court order or parental agreement determines which parent claims.

## Court Order Specifics Required

### What S-1 and IPAC Need

- Date of order
- Court name and jurisdiction
- Marine's role (custodial parent, non-custodial parent, joint)
- Child's name and date of birth
- Custody designation language
- Child support amount and schedule (if applicable)
- Effective date

### Vague or Incomplete Orders

If the court order is vague (e.g., "shared custody as agreed"), IPAC may request clarification.

- Modified order from court
- Affidavit from both parents (if cooperative)
- Legal Services consultation

### Foreign Court Orders

Custody orders from foreign courts require.

- Authenticated translation if not in English.
- Recognition under U.S. or state law.
- Coordination with Legal Services.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1. Becoming Custodial Parent After Divorce

Original divorce gave former spouse primary custody. Modified order now gives Marine custody.

- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with modified order.
- BAH adjusts upward (if previously at without-dependents).
- DEERS enrollment for child.
- Family Care Plan required if you become single parent.

### Scenario 2. Losing Custody After Modification

Marine had custody, court modifies to former spouse.

- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with modified order.
- BAH adjusts (depends on other dependents).
- DEERS removal for child.
- BAH-Diff may apply if court orders Marine to pay support.

### Scenario 3. Establishing Custody (Unmarried Marine)

Marine and child's other parent never married. Court establishes custody for the first time.

- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with court order.
- DD Form 137-3<Citation source="DD Form 137-3" /> may be required as supplemental documentation for ward determination.
- BAH-with-dependents activates if Marine has primary custody.
- Paternity must be established (see [Birth and Adoption](/marines/birth-and-adoption)).

### Scenario 4. Joint Physical Custody

Court orders 50/50 physical custody.

- One parent designated as primary custodian for dependent purposes (court order specifies, or parents agree).
- That parent claims child as dependent.
- Other parent typically does not claim simultaneously (DD 137 question 3g - "does any other person claim").

### Scenario 5. Custody Modification Through ROTC, Deployment, or PCS

Court modifies custody due to Marine's deployment, training, or PCS.

- Submit modified order with NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" />.
- Coordinate with Legal Services for Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) protections.
- Family Care Plan adjustment may be required.

## Coordination With Civilian Family Court

### Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA)

SCRA provides protections in custody and support proceedings during military service.

- Stay of proceedings if military service materially affects ability to participate.
- Default judgment limitations.
- Court appointment of attorney for absent service member.

### Legal Services Support

Marine Corps Legal Services provides limited assistance with family law matters.

- Consultation on legal rights and options.
- Document review.
- Notarization.
- Referral to civilian attorneys for representation.

See [Legal Services Overview](/marines/legal-services) for full framework.

### Permanent Change of Station Considerations

PCS may trigger custody disputes. Coordinate with civilian attorney and inform the court of military orders well in advance.

## Common Errors

### Submitting Without Certified Court Order

Photocopies are not enough. Court-issued certified copies with raised seal are required.

### Late Submission Creating BAH Discrepancy

Late submission of custody change may create BAH overpayment (if BAH-with-dependents continued for a child no longer in your custody) or missed entitlement.

### Voluntary Support Without Court Order

Voluntary payments do not qualify for BAH-Diff. Get any support arrangement formalized in court order.

### Failing to Update FSA

Custody changes affecting dependent location may activate or terminate FSA. Update with each custody change.

### Both Parents Claiming Same Child

If you and the other parent both claim the same child as a dependent, IPAC will eventually identify the discrepancy. The court order or DEERS designation determines which parent legitimately claims.

### Failing to Update Family Care Plan

Custody changes affecting single-parent or dual-military status trigger Family Care Plan requirements. See [Family Care Plan (FCP)](/marines/family-care-plan).

## Quick Reference

- Submission. NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with certified court order within 10 working days.
- BAH. With-dependents activates or deactivates per court order.
- BAH-Diff. Court-ordered support exceeding threshold qualifies.
- DEERS. Enrollment or removal per court order.
- FSA. Re-evaluate if custody affects dependent location.
- Family Care Plan. Required if single parent or dual-military.
- Primary contact. S-1, with Legal Services support.

## Records To Keep

- Certified court order (original plus extra certified copies)
- Prior custody order (if modification)
- Child support order if separate
- Signed NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with S-1 endorsement
- DEERS enrollment or removal confirmation
- DD Form 137-3<Citation source="DD Form 137-3" /> if applicable
- LES showing BAH or BAH-Diff change
- Family Care Plan if applicable

If you have a question about custody documentation, S-1 is your first stop. For court order interpretation, support disputes, or SCRA protections, coordinate with Legal Services.
