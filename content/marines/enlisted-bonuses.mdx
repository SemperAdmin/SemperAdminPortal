## Start Here If You Are New to Enlisted Bonuses

Marine Corps enlisted Marines see seven distinct bonuses tied to written agreements. Enlistment bonus at recruiting. SRB at reenlistment. CSRB for critical skills. Prior service reenlistment. Conversion bonus to switch MOS. Transfer between services. Transfer between components. Each is governed by a written agreement and subject to recoupment if you fail to fulfill the conditions.

This page walks you through Enlisted Bonuses from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The seven bonus types and their maximum amounts
- The $360,000 lifetime SRB cap and $200,000 combined CSRB+SRB cap
- The early-discharge exception (12 months of EAS per Title 10 U.S.C. section 1171<Citation source="Title 10 U.S.C. section 1171" />)
- Restrictions including the misconduct disqualifier and 28-year SRB ceiling
- Recoupment framework cross-reference

Every fact on this page comes from DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 9 (August 2024), DoDI 1304.31<Citation source="DoDI 1304.31" />, and Title 37 U.S.C. section 331<Citation source="Title 37 U.S.C. section 331" />.

## Repayment Framework

Per Chapter 9 paragraph 1.3, an enlisted Marine who does not complete the agreed term of service or otherwise meet the conditions of service for the bonus is subject to the repayment provisions of Chapter 2. The early-discharge exception per Title 10 U.S.C. section 1171<Citation source="Title 10 U.S.C. section 1171" /> applies. A Marine discharged 12 months or less before EAS is considered to have completed the term for which the bonus was paid (covered on the Pay Problems and Fixes page).

## Bonus 1. Enlistment Bonus (EB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 2.0 through 2.4.

- Purpose. For initial enlistees in the Regular Component for service in a designated military skill, career field, unit, grade, or other condition imposed by the Secretary concerned.
- Eligibility per paragraph 2.1. Possess HS diploma, completion certificate in lieu of HS diploma, or GED. Be an initial enlistee. Enlist for at least 2 years (or extend an initial obligation to a total of at least 2 years) in the Regular Component. Execute a written agreement specifying the bonus amount, payment method, period of obligated service, and designated skill/career field/unit/grade.
- Disqualifiers per paragraphs 2.1.5 and 2.1.6. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received an enlistment bonus, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received or be eligible to receive an SRB or CSRB.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 2.2. $75,000 for a minimum 2-year service obligation. This rate is authorized from 11 January 2024 through 10 January 2027.
- Method of payment per paragraph 2.4. Periodic installments or single lump sum. Initial payment is not paid before completion of basic recruit training. For specialties requiring formal training, the Service is allowed to withhold initial payment until skill qualification.

## Bonus 2. Selective Reenlistment Bonus (SRB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 3.0 through 3.7.

- Purpose. To mitigate significant personnel shortages or changing force structure requirements through targeted reenlistment incentives.
- Eligibility per paragraph 3.1. Pay grade E-3 or higher. Reenlist for at least 3 years OR voluntarily extend an enlistment for at least 1 year. Execute a written agreement.
- Indefinite reenlistment requirements per paragraph 3.1.8. Pay grade E-5 or above, 10 or more years of military service, sign agreement to remain on active duty for at least 3 years.
- Reenlistment window per paragraph 3.2.1. Re-entry or reenlistment is required no later than 3 months after discharge or release (or shorter period prescribed by the Secretary).
- Broken service / prior service re-entry SRB per paragraph 3.2.2. Veterans with more than 3-month but less than 4-year break in active duty are allowed to qualify under separate Service regulations.
- Disqualifiers per paragraph 3.2.7. The Marine is not eligible if previously discharged or released based on misconduct, substandard duty performance, or moral or professional dereliction.
- 28-year service ceiling per paragraph 3.2.8. SRB payments to a Marine are not allowed past 28 years of active duty unless waived by the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Military Personnel Policy.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 3.3. The statutory cap is $50,000 per year of obligated service. DoD policy limits the SRB to $30,000 per year of obligated service. The maximum single SRB is $180,000.
- Lifetime career cap per paragraph 3.3.2. Total combined SRB payments over a career are not allowed to exceed $360,000.
- Method of payment per paragraph 3.5. Lump sum or periodic installments. Initial installment after reenlistment with break in service is paid no earlier than 30 days after arrival at the first permanent duty station following reenlistment.

## Bonus 3. Critical Skill Retention Bonus (CSRB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 4.0 through 4.4.

- Purpose. For enlisted Marines qualified in a critical military skill designated by the Secretary of Defense.
- Eligibility per paragraph 4.1. Serving on active duty in a Regular Component, qualified in the critical skill, not on indefinite enlistment, reenlist or voluntarily extend for at least 1 year.
- 25-year service limit per paragraph 4.2.1. CSRB is not authorized for a Marine who has completed more than 25 years of active duty (or who will reach 25 years before the bonus period ends). Exception. Marines qualified in a special operations forces skill.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 4.3.2. $30,000 annually unless PDUSD M&RA grants an exception.
- Combined CSRB and SRB cap. The combined total of CSRB and SRB during an individual's career is not allowed to exceed $200,000 unless PDUSD M&RA authorizes an exception for the specific skill.
- Method of payment per paragraph 4.4. Single lump sum or periodic installments.

## Bonus 4. Prior Service Reenlistment Bonus

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 5.0 through 5.3.

- Purpose. For Marines who reenlist in a Regular Component after a break in active duty.
- Eligibility per paragraph 5.1. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received an SRB or CSRB. The Marine is not allowed to have more than 16 years of total military service. The Marine must have an honorable discharge for all previous periods. The Marine must reenlist for at least 3 years.
- Documentation. Original DD-214 (copy 1 or copy 4), or certified true-copy reproduction, or other official documentation verifying satisfactory participation.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 5.2. $20,000 per year of obligated service in a Regular Component.
- Method of payment per paragraph 5.3. Lump sum or periodic installments. The Marine Corps is not authorized to pay any portion of the bonus prior to completion of training.

## Bonus 5. Conversion Bonus

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 6.0 through 6.3.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for a Marine to convert to and serve in a designated military skill experiencing a shortage of trained and qualified personnel.
- Eligibility per paragraph 6.1. At time of agreement, pay grade E-6 or below with no more than 12 years of service. Have completed all service obligations from prior EB, SRB, or CSRB. Extend the existing enlistment to qualify if less than 3 years of active duty remaining. Execute a written agreement.
- Disqualifier per paragraph 6.1.6. The Marine is not allowed to be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 6.2. $10,000.
- Method of payment per paragraph 6.3. Lump sum or periodic installments. The Marine Corps is not allowed to pay any portion before completion of conversion training.

## Bonus 6. Transfer Between Military Services

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 7.0 through 7.2.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for an enlisted Marine to transfer to and serve in another Military Service.
- Eligibility per paragraph 7.1. Execute a written agreement with the gaining Service for a minimum 3-year period. Have satisfactorily completed any term of enlistment in the current service. Qualify for reenlistment in the gaining service. Not be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period. Not be in receipt of separation pay per Title 10 U.S.C.<Citation source="Title 10 U.S.C." /> Chapter 59.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 7.2. $10,000 lump sum at time of transfer approval, or annual installments totaling no more than $10,000.

## Bonus 7. Transfer Between Components of a Military Service

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 8.0 through 8.2.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for a Marine to transfer between the Regular Component and the Ready Reserve (or vice versa) of the same service.
- Eligibility per paragraph 8.1. Execute a written agreement specifying period and conditions. Have satisfactorily completed all terms of enlistment in the current component. Qualify for reenlistment in the gaining component. Agree to remain in the gaining component for at least 2 years. Have fewer than 15 years of active service.
- Disqualifier per paragraph 8.1.3. The Marine is not allowed to be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 8.2. $10,000.

## Career Cap Summary

- Single EB. Up to $75,000 (current authorization period 11 Jan 2024 to 10 Jan 2027).
- Single SRB. Up to $180,000 (DoD policy of $30,000/year times the maximum 6-year obligation under SRB).
- Lifetime SRB. Up to $360,000.
- Lifetime combined CSRB and SRB. Up to $200,000 unless waived by PDUSD M&RA.
- Conversion Bonus. Up to $10,000 per agreement.
- Transfer Between Services. Up to $10,000.
- Transfer Between Components. Up to $10,000.
- Prior Service Reenlistment Bonus. Up to $20,000 per year of obligated service.

The various bonuses are not allowed to overlap for the same period. EB disqualified by prior SRB or CSRB. SRB disqualified by current EB. Read the written agreement before signing to confirm the period covered and the disqualifying interactions.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

I recently enlisted in a critical-skill MOS. What is my bonus?

Per Chapter 9 paragraph 2.2, EB is up to $75,000 for a minimum 2-year obligation in the current authorization period. The actual rate depends on the specific MOS and Service guidance. Verify with your S-1 against the controlling Marine Corps SRB Program MARADMIN.

I am at the end of my first enlistment. Should I take SRB or CSRB?

If your MOS is on the SRB list, you draw SRB at the rate published in the current MARADMIN. CSRB is for SECDEF-designated critical skills, not MOS-level. Your S-1 routes the determination based on the published lists.

I have 25 years of active duty. Am I eligible for another SRB?

Per Chapter 9 paragraph 3.2.8, SRB payments are not allowed past 28 years of active duty unless waived by DASD MPP. At 25 years, you might qualify if reenlisting before the 28-year point.

I broke service for 2 years and want to come back. Can I get a bonus?

Per Chapter 9 paragraph 5.0, the Prior Service Reenlistment Bonus applies. Up to $20,000 per year of obligated service for a minimum 3-year reenlistment. Less than 16 years total military service required.
