## Start Here If You Are New to FITREP Appeals

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the Performance Evaluation Review Board (PERB) at HQMC M&RA adjudicates FITREP appeals. The MRO files the petition within established time limits when there is a substantive error in the FITREP. Grounds for appeal include factual errors, procedural failures (e.g., missing initial counseling, no addendum opportunity), RS or RO bias, and inconsistency with the RS profile or unit pattern. The petition routes through the chain of command with supporting documentation. The PERB outcomes include affirmation (no error found), remand to the unit for procedural cure, partial correction of factual errors, or full removal of the FITREP from the record. The PERB does NOT substitute its judgment for the RS's marks. The PERB only addresses substantive errors. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7.

This page walks you through the PERB process from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

## Substantive vs Administrative Correction Distinction

Before filing a PERB petition, determine whether the correction is substantive or administrative. Each follows a different process.

### Administrative Corrections (Submit via Appendix G)

Administrative corrections cover anything in Section A of the FITREP and factual data in Sections I and K (e.g., name, rank, billet title). These items are verifiable through official records.

- Submit via Appendix G of MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />.
- Email to smb.manpower.mmrp-31@usmc.mil (MMPB-23 Policy, Compliance and Corrections section).
- The MRO must initiate (it is the MRO's report).
- The MRO signs Appendix G.
- The RS or RO of the specific report must sign Appendix G endorsing the request.
- Attach all supporting documentation.

The A-PES and FITREP Administration leaf covers Appendix G in detail.

### Substantive Corrections (Submit to PERB)

Substantive corrections cover items requiring opinion or judgment. Examples.

- Attribute marks (Sections D and E).
- Sections I and K opinion comments.
- Comparative assessment markings (Section G).
- MRO's status selection in Section A.
- RS's grade recommendation for Gunnery Sergeants.

These items require submission to the PERB at MMPB-21.

- Email to smb.manpower.mmrp-13@usmc.mil for PERB process details.
- The remainder of this page covers the PERB framework.

## What the PERB Is

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7, the Performance Evaluation Review Board (PERB) is the Marine Corps body that adjudicates FITREP appeals based on substantive errors.

### PERB Composition

PERB members are senior officers selected by the Commandant of the Marine Corps (CMC). The board reviews petitions, supporting documentation, and the underlying FITREP. The board issues a written decision.

### PERB Authority

The PERB has authority to.

- Affirm the FITREP (no error found).
- Remand the FITREP to the unit for procedural cure.
- Partially correct factual errors.
- Direct full removal of the FITREP from the official record.

### PERB Standard of Review

The PERB reviews for SUBSTANTIVE error. The PERB does NOT re-mark the FITREP. The PERB does NOT substitute its judgment for the RS's marks. The PERB asks only whether the FITREP is procedurally and substantively correct.

## Grounds for PERB Appeal

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the PERB will consider the following grounds.

### Factual Error

A specific, verifiable factual error in the FITREP. Examples.

- Wrong dates of observation.
- Misattributed accomplishments (credited to or removed from the wrong Marine).
- Documented event that did not occur.
- Numerical errors (wrong personnel count, wrong dollar value).

### Procedural Failure

A procedural failure that violates MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />. Examples.

- No initial counseling within 30 days.
- No mid-period counseling.
- No mandatory adverse counseling before submission.
- No MRO addendum opportunity.
- Wrong RS or RO (chain of command violation).
- Missing Third Officer Sighter when required.

### RS or RO Bias

Documented bias by the RS or RO. Examples.

- RS or RO with personal animus (documented prior conflicts, public statements, etc.).
- RS or RO with conflict of interest (financial, family, prior litigation).
- Pattern of disparate treatment across same-grade peers.

### Inconsistency With RS Profile or Unit Pattern

The FITREP is significantly inconsistent with the RS's broader marking pattern (the Reporting Senior Profile) or the unit's calibration pattern.

### NOT Grounds for Appeal

- Disagreement with the RS's professional judgment about marks.
- Disappointment with comparative assessment.
- Belief that the marks should have been higher.
- Frustration with the RS or RO.

The PERB will reject petitions based solely on disagreement with marks.

## Time Limits

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the MRO must file the PERB petition within established time limits.

### Standard Time Limit

The MRO has a defined window after FITREP receipt to file the petition. The exact time limit is in MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7. Coordinate with the unit S-1 PES Manager for the current deadline.

### Late Petitions

Petitions filed after the time limit are typically rejected. The PERB may consider late petitions in extraordinary circumstances (e.g., the MRO was deployed and unable to access the FITREP, the MRO was incapacitated). Late petitions require justification.

### Why Time Limits Matter

The Marine Corps cannot maintain personnel records indefinitely subject to challenge. Time limits create finality.

## Petition Format

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the petition includes specific elements.

### Required Elements

- MRO identification (name, grade, DoD ID).
- The FITREP being challenged (date, type, RS, RO).
- Specific grounds for the appeal (cite MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7).
- Specific factual or procedural errors alleged.
- The remedy requested (correction, removal, etc.).
- Supporting documentation list.
- MRO signature and date.

### Format

Written petition. Typically formatted as a memorandum addressed to the PERB through the chain of command.

### Length

Concise and substantive. Long petitions diluted with non-substantive content lose impact. The PERB rewards focused, evidence-backed petitions.

## Supporting Documentation

The petition is only as strong as the supporting documentation.

### Common Supporting Documents

- Written counseling records (or lack thereof).
- Awards, citations, and recognition documents.
- Email or written communications with the RS or RO.
- Witness statements.
- Performance evaluations from other observers (peer assessments, third-party reports).
- Operational documents establishing facts.

### Documentation Best Practice

- Organize chronologically.
- Tab and label each exhibit.
- Reference exhibits in the petition narrative.
- Provide certified copies where possible.

## Routing Through the Chain of Command

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the petition routes through the chain of command before reaching the PERB.

### Step 1. MRO Submits to Unit Commander

The MRO submits the petition to the unit commander (typically the Battalion Commander or equivalent).

### Step 2. Commander's Endorsement

The commander provides an endorsement.

- Concur with the appeal.
- Non-concur with the appeal.
- Provide additional context or input.

The commander's endorsement is influential but not binding on the PERB.

### Step 3. Successive Endorsements

The petition routes through the chain to the General Officer level. Each echelon may provide an endorsement.

### Step 4. PES Receipt

The petition reaches HQMC PES. PES conducts initial procedural review and routes to the PERB.

### Step 5. PERB Adjudication

The PERB reviews and issues a written decision.

## PERB Adjudication Outcomes

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7.

### Affirmation

The PERB finds no substantive error. The FITREP stands as written. The MRO has no further administrative appeal (Board for Correction of Naval Records (BCNR) may be available for further appeal in some cases).

### Remand for Procedural Cure

The PERB finds a procedural failure (e.g., no counseling). The PERB remands the FITREP to the unit to cure the procedural defect. After cure, the FITREP is resubmitted.

### Partial Correction

The PERB finds specific factual errors. The PERB directs correction of those facts but allows the rest of the FITREP to stand.

### Full Removal

The PERB finds the FITREP fundamentally defective (substantial procedural failure, demonstrable bias, factual misrepresentation). The PERB directs removal from the official record.

### Decision Finality

The PERB decision is final within the PES framework. Further appeal is to the BCNR per separate procedures.

## What the PERB Cannot Do

### Re-Mark the FITREP

The PERB does NOT substitute its judgment for the RS's marks. The PERB asks whether the marks are procedurally and substantively correct, not whether they are the marks the PERB would have given.

### Substitute Judgment for RS

The PERB does NOT second-guess the RS's professional opinion about MRO performance. The PERB only addresses substantive errors.

### Reverse Comparative Assessment

The PERB does NOT change the comparative assessment in Section G unless the assessment reflects factual or procedural error.

### Resolve Personnel Disputes

The PERB does NOT adjudicate broader personnel disputes (relief for cause, command relationships, leadership conflicts). Those matters route through different processes.

## Best Practice for Petition Preparation

### Coordinate With Unit S-1 PES Manager

The PES Manager understands MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> procedural requirements and can identify procedural failures.

### Coordinate With Unit Legal

For petitions involving allegations of bias or misconduct by the RS or RO, coordinate with unit Legal. Legal can advise on the petition's legal basis and supporting documentation.

### Build the Documentation Early

Start collecting supporting documents as soon as the FITREP is received. Memory fades and documents become harder to obtain over time.

### Stay Factual

Avoid emotional or accusatory language. The PERB responds to evidence, not emotion.

### Be Specific

Vague claims of "unfairness" do not survive PERB review. Specific allegations with specific evidence drive outcomes.

### Set Realistic Expectations

PERB outcomes vary. A well-supported petition has a reasonable chance of remand or partial correction. Full removal is rare and reserved for the most defective FITREPs.

## After the PERB Decision

### If Affirmed

The FITREP stands. The MRO may consider BCNR appeal if appropriate. The MRO focuses on subsequent reporting periods to demonstrate sustained performance.

### If Remanded

The unit cures the procedural defect (e.g., conducts the missing counseling, provides the missing addendum opportunity). The cured FITREP is resubmitted. The MRO reviews and acknowledges.

### If Corrected

The corrected FITREP appears in the OMPF and on the MBS. The MRO verifies the correction was made.

### If Removed

The FITREP is removed from the official record. The MBS is updated to reflect the removal. Selection boards no longer see the removed report.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What is the PERB?

The Performance Evaluation Review Board at HQMC M&RA. Adjudicates FITREP appeals based on substantive errors per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> Chapter 7.

What grounds support a PERB appeal?

Factual error, procedural failure (missing counseling, etc.), RS or RO bias, or inconsistency with the RS profile. Disagreement with marks alone is not grounds.

How long do I have to file?

Within the time limit specified in MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />. Coordinate with the unit S-1 PES Manager for the current deadline.

What format does the petition take?

Written memorandum. Addresses the PERB through the chain of command. Specific factual or procedural errors. Supporting documentation. Specific remedy requested.

Can the PERB re-mark my FITREP?

No. The PERB does not substitute its judgment for the RS's marks. The PERB only addresses substantive errors.

What outcomes does the PERB issue?

Affirmation (no error), remand for procedural cure, partial correction of factual errors, or full removal of the FITREP.

What if my petition is denied?

The PERB decision is final within PES. Further appeal is to the BCNR per separate procedures.

How do I know whether to file?

Coordinate with the unit S-1 PES Manager and unit Legal. Identify specific factual or procedural errors. Build the supporting documentation. If the basis is strong, file. If the basis is solely disagreement with marks, the petition will be rejected.

Should I file a petition or focus on the next FITREP?

Both may be appropriate. The petition addresses the specific FITREP error. The next FITREP demonstrates sustained performance. A pattern of strong subsequent reports often offsets a single adverse FITREP without requiring PERB action.

Can the chain of command block my petition?

No. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the chain provides endorsements but cannot prevent the petition from reaching PERB. The endorsements are influential but the PERB makes the decision.
