## What BNA Is

Per 37 U.S.C. 402b and NDAA FY22 Section 605, the Basic Needs Allowance is a monthly supplemental cash allowance paid to service members with dependents whose total household income falls below the statutory threshold.

The allowance closes the gap between the household's gross income and the federal poverty guideline threshold. The intent is to help low-income service members with dependents meet basic household needs without relying on outside food assistance programs.

## Effective Date

Per NDAA FY22 Section 605, BNA was effective 1 January 2023. Marine Corps implementation guidance was issued through MARADMIN 037/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 037/23" /> in January 2023.

## Eligibility

Per 37 U.S.C. 402b and DoDI 1341.16<Citation source="DoDI 1341.16" />, a Marine is eligible if all of the following apply.

### Active-Duty Status

The Marine is on active duty in the Marine Corps. Active Reserve (AR) Marines on active duty for over 30 days are also eligible. SELRES Marines on inactive duty drill periods are NOT eligible.

### At Least One Dependent

The Marine has at least one dependent enrolled in DEERS. Common dependents include.

- Spouse.
- Children under 21 (or 23 if full-time student).
- Incapacitated dependent children of any age.
- Parents who meet dependency criteria.

### 12 Months Continuous Active-Duty Service

The Marine has at least 12 months of continuous active-duty service at the time of application. This requirement excludes most accession-year Marines from initial eligibility.

### Gross Household Income Below Threshold

The Marine's total gross household income falls below 150 percent of the federal poverty guideline for the household size. The Service Secretary extends the threshold up to 200 percent of FPG for Marines in designated high-cost areas.

### Excluded From Eligibility

- Marines without dependents.
- Marines with less than 12 months continuous active-duty service.
- Marines whose gross household income meets or exceeds the threshold.
- Reservists not on active duty for more than 30 days.

## The 150 Percent Federal Poverty Guideline Threshold

The federal poverty guideline (FPG) is published annually by the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS). The guideline scales by household size.

### How the Threshold Works

The BNA threshold is 150 percent of the FPG for the Marine's household size. Households below the threshold are eligible for BNA. Households at or above the threshold are not eligible.

### High-Cost Area 200 Percent Expansion

Per NDAA FY23, the Service Secretary may extend eligibility up to 200 percent of FPG for Marines stationed in designated high-cost areas. The expansion recognizes that the federal poverty guideline understates the cost of living in expensive locations.

### Annual Adjustment

The FPG is updated annually by HHS. The BNA threshold adjusts automatically with the FPG update. Marines whose income did not change but whose threshold rises (due to FPG adjustment) may become eligible at recertification.

## How Gross Household Income Is Calculated

Per DoDI 1341.16<Citation source="DoDI 1341.16" />, gross household income (GHI) is the total annual income of the Marine and all members of the household.

### Income Counted in GHI

- Marine's basic pay.
- Marine's special pays and incentives (HFP/IDP, FLPB, SDAP, etc.).
- Marine's bonuses (paid in the year of receipt).
- Spouse's wages and self-employment income.
- Other household members' income (if they contribute to household).
- Investment income (dividends, interest).
- Rental income.
- Other taxable income.

### Income Excluded From GHI

Per current DoD guidance, the following are NOT counted in GHI for BNA calculation.

- Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH).
- Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS).
- Combat zone tax exclusion (CZTE) amounts.
- Cost of Living Allowances (CONUS COLA, OCONUS COLA, TLA).
- One-time PCS allowances (DLA, MEA, TLE).
- Moving expense reimbursements.

The BAH exclusion is significant. BAH often comprises a large share of total compensation, and excluding it from GHI substantially increases the number of Marines who fall below the BNA threshold.

### Income Verification

The Marine certifies the gross household income on the BNA application. The unit S-1 and DFAS verify against pay records, W-2 documents, and tax filings. Misrepresentation is grounds for recoupment and possible disciplinary action.

## Monthly Allowance Calculation

Per 37 U.S.C. 402b, the BNA monthly allowance is calculated as.

### Formula

BNA Monthly Allowance = (Threshold - GHI) divided by 12.

Where.

- Threshold = 1.5 (or 2.0 for high-cost areas) multiplied by the FPG for the household size.
- GHI = annual gross household income (excluding BAH and other excluded items).

### Example Calculation

A Marine with a household of 4. Spouse and two children. Annual GHI of $36,000 (basic pay plus spouse wages, BAH excluded). FPG for household of 4 is approximately $32,150 (use current HHS rate).

- Threshold (150 percent of FPG) = $32,150 × 1.5 = $48,225.
- BNA monthly allowance = ($48,225 - $36,000) ÷ 12 = $1,018.75 per month.

The Marine receives approximately $1,019 per month in BNA. Verify all figures against the current FPG rates and the BNA self-screening tool.

### Tax Status

Per 37 U.S.C. 402b, the BNA is treated as taxable income for federal purposes. The Marine reports BNA on the W-2.

## Application Process

### BNA Self-Screening Tool

The DoD provides a BNA self-screening tool. Marines enter household size, gross household income, and station location. The tool indicates eligibility and the estimated monthly allowance.

The tool is available through Military OneSource and the DoD BNA portal. The Marine S-1 also assists Marines with the screening.

### Application Submission

Marines apply through.

- The BNA application portal at the DoD or DFAS website.
- The unit S-1 administrative office.
- DFAS Marine Corps Pay (1-888-332-7411).

### Required Documentation

- Verification of dependents (DEERS enrollment).
- Verification of gross household income (LES, spouse W-2 or paystubs, other income documents).
- Self-certification of income and household size.

### Approval and Payment Start

Once the application is approved, BNA appears on the Marine's LES as a separate entitlement. Payment is monthly with the regular pay cycle.

## Annual Recertification

Per DoDI 1341.16<Citation source="DoDI 1341.16" />, BNA recipients recertify annually. The recertification verifies continued eligibility based on.

- Continued active-duty service.
- Continued dependent enrollment in DEERS.
- Updated gross household income.

If the Marine's income has risen above the threshold or dependents have changed, BNA terminates or adjusts.

### Mid-Year Eligibility Changes

Marines report mid-year changes that affect BNA eligibility.

- Spouse starts or stops working.
- New dependent (birth, adoption, marriage).
- Loss of dependent (death, divorce, age out).
- Significant pay change (e.g., promotion, special duty assignment).

The Marine notifies the S-1 within 30 days of the change.

## Reading BNA on the LES

BNA appears on the LES under entitlements. The pay code identifies the monthly BNA amount. The Marine verifies.

- The amount matches the calculated allowance.
- The deductions reflect federal income tax withholding (BNA is taxable).

If the BNA amount is incorrect or missing, the Marine works with the S-1 to submit a pay correction through the established framework.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

Who qualifies for BNA?

Active-duty Marines with at least one dependent, at least 12 months continuous active-duty service, and gross household income below 150 percent of the federal poverty guideline (or 200 percent in designated high-cost areas).

Is BAH counted in my income for BNA?

No. BAH is excluded from gross household income for the BNA calculation. This exclusion significantly expands the number of Marines who qualify.

How is the monthly allowance calculated?

(Threshold minus gross household income) divided by 12. Threshold is 150 percent (or 200 percent) of the FPG for the household size.

Is BNA taxable?

Yes. Per 37 U.S.C. 402b, BNA is taxable income reported on the W-2.

How do I apply?

Through the BNA self-screening tool, the unit S-1, or DFAS Marine Corps Pay (1-888-332-7411). Submit the application with verification of dependents and income.

How often do I recertify?

Annually. Marines also report mid-year changes affecting eligibility within 30 days.

What if my income changes mid-year?

Notify the S-1 within 30 days. BNA adjusts or terminates based on the new income. Failure to report changes results in recoupment of overpayments.

What income counts as gross household income?

Basic pay, special pays, bonuses, spouse wages, investment income, and other taxable income. BAH, BAS, COLA, CZTE, and PCS allowances are excluded.

What if my Marine spouse and I are both active duty?

Dual-military couples may be eligible if combined GHI falls below the threshold. Each Marine submits a separate application. The household income calculation aggregates both pay streams.

Can I lose BNA mid-month if my situation changes?

BNA terminates at the end of the month in which eligibility ends. Recoupment applies if the Marine failed to report a qualifying change.
