## Start Here If You Are New to GTCC Compliance

The GTCC carries clear rules. Personal use is prohibited. Non-TDY charges are prohibited. Use during Permissive TAD or leave is prohibited. Late payment leads to suspension. Continued non-payment leads to cancellation. Willful misuse can lead to UCMJ action. The standard suspension threshold is 60 days of delinquency. Suspended cardholders cannot use the card until the bill is paid and the APC reinstates the account. Cancellation removes the card permanently. Reinstatement after cancellation requires command endorsement and is at the program's discretion. Page 11 entries document each incident. ## What Counts as Misuse

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4 and MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" />, misuse falls into several categories.

### Personal Use

Charging personal expenses to the GTCC. Examples.

- Souvenirs, gifts, or personal shopping.
- Personal entertainment (movies, sports, theme parks).
- Personal alcohol purchases.
- Family or dependent expenses.
- Personal supplies unrelated to TDY.

### Non-TDY Charges

Charging expenses outside the authorized TDY period. Examples.

- Charges at the PDS (use personal funds).
- Charges before TDY begins or after TDY ends.
- Charges during PTAD or leave (per MARADMIN 478/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 478/24" /> paragraph 3.E.4 for ELT cardholders, applies broadly per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4).

### Late Payment

Failure to pay the GTCC bill on time. Common causes.

- Voucher not submitted within 5 working days of TDY return.
- Split disbursement not configured or insufficient.
- Cardholder did not pay shortfall manually.

### Exceeding Limits

Spending beyond the credit limit (Standard or Restricted) without prior Mission Critical approval.

### Cash Advance Abuse

Excessive ATM cash advances or cash advances unrelated to authorized travel needs.

### Prohibited Merchant Categories

Charges at gambling establishments, adult entertainment, or other categorically prohibited merchants.

### Willful Misrepresentation

Knowingly submitting a fraudulent claim or making intentionally false statements about GTCC use.

## The 60-Day Delinquency Suspension Threshold

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4, the standard threshold for suspension is 60 days of delinquency on the GTCC bill.

### Day-by-Day Timeline

- Day 1. Statement issued by Citi.
- Day 25-30. Payment due (typical billing cycle).
- Day 31-60. Past-due alerts to cardholder and APC.
- Day 60+. Account suspended.

### What Happens at Suspension

- Card declines all transactions.
- Account is placed in suspended status by Citi at APC direction.
- Cardholder cannot use the card for new charges.
- Existing balance still accrues fees and interest (where applicable).

### Notification

The APC and cardholder receive notification of impending suspension. The cardholder has the opportunity to pay before the suspension takes effect.

## Page 11 Entries for Documented Misuse

Per MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" /> and standard administrative practice, documented misuse triggers a Page 11 entry.

### What a Page 11 Entry Captures

- Date of the incident.
- Specific misuse (personal use, late payment, prohibited merchant).
- Counseling provided by the chain of command.
- Cardholder acknowledgment.
- Corrective action required.

### Page 11 Impact

- Impacts the Marine's record reviewed for promotions, reenlistments, and special duty assignments.
- Documents a pattern of misuse if recurring.
- Supports adverse administrative actions if escalation is needed.

### Initial Counseling vs Page 11

First-incident misuse may be addressed through verbal counseling without a Page 11. Recurring misuse or significant single incidents trigger formal Page 11 entries.

## UCMJ Exposure

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4 and applicable UCMJ articles, willful or fraudulent misuse can support UCMJ action.

### Common UCMJ Articles Applicable to GTCC Misuse

- Article 92 (Failure to Obey a Lawful Order or Regulation). Applies to violation of GTCC use rules.
- Article 121 (Larceny). Applies to fraudulent claims involving GTCC charges.
- Article 132 (False or Unauthorized Pass Offenses). Applies to certain types of misrepresentation.
- Article 134 (General Article). Catch-all for service-discrediting conduct.

### UCMJ Process

The chain of command initiates the UCMJ process. The standard preliminary investigation, charge sheet, and disposition (NJP, court-martial, or administrative) framework applies. Adverse findings may include reduction in grade, forfeitures, restriction, confinement, and discharge characterization.

### Severity Threshold

UCMJ action is typically reserved for.

- Willful and substantial fraud (large dollar amounts).
- Patterns of misuse despite counseling.
- Misuse coordinated with other misconduct.

Single-incident inadvertent misuse is more often handled through Page 11 entries and counseling.

## Suspension Procedures

When the suspension threshold is reached.

### Step 1. APC Notification

The APC receives a delinquency report from Citi. The APC verifies the cardholder situation.

### Step 2. Pre-Suspension Counseling

The APC notifies the cardholder of impending suspension. The cardholder has typically 5-10 days to pay.

### Step 3. Suspension Action

If payment is not received, the APC directs Citi to suspend the account.

### Step 4. Notification of Suspension

The cardholder is notified of the suspension. The notification includes the reinstatement requirements.

### Step 5. Page 11 Entry

The chain of command issues a Page 11 entry documenting the suspension.

### Step 6. Continued Monitoring

The APC monitors for payment and tracks the timeline to potential cancellation.

## Cancellation Procedures

If the cardholder fails to pay despite suspension, the account is cancelled.

### Cancellation Threshold

Typically 120-180 days of continued delinquency, varies by program guidance and cardholder circumstances.

### What Happens at Cancellation

- The account is permanently closed by Citi.
- The card is cancelled and the cardholder must destroy the physical card.
- The cardholder cannot use the card for any further charges.
- The unpaid balance becomes a debt subject to collection (including DFAS garnishment if applicable).

### Page 11 Entry for Cancellation

The cancellation triggers a more substantial Page 11 entry documenting the program-level adverse action.

### Future Card Eligibility

A cancelled cardholder may face barriers to future GTCC issuance. Reinstatement after cancellation requires command endorsement and is at the program's discretion.

## Reinstatement Framework

After suspension or cancellation, the cardholder may pursue reinstatement.

### Reinstatement After Suspension

1. Cardholder pays the full delinquent balance.
2. APC verifies payment with Citi.
3. APC reinstates the account in CitiManager.
4. Cardholder retains the original card (if not yet cancelled) or receives a replacement.
5. Page 11 entry remains on the record.

Reinstatement after suspension is typically straightforward once payment is verified.

### Reinstatement After Cancellation

1. Cardholder pays the full delinquent balance and any associated fees.
2. The Marine submits a written reinstatement request through the chain of command to the APC.
3. The commander endorses the request with a recommendation.
4. The APC forwards the request to higher-level APCs (typically up to the TECOM HL3 APC for ELT cardholders or the equivalent service-level APC).
5. The reinstatement is approved or denied at the program's discretion.
6. If approved, a new application process is initiated.

Reinstatement after cancellation is not guaranteed and is more rigorous.

## Prevention. Best Practices for Compliance

The most effective compliance strategy is prevention.

### Use Split Disbursement Religiously

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4 and MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" />, split disbursement is mandatory. Verify the amount fully covers the GTCC bill before submitting every voucher. The GTCC Split Disbursement leaf covers the framework.

### Submit Vouchers Within 5 Working Days

Per JTR, voucher submission within 5 working days of return triggers the reimbursement and split disbursement quickly.

### Monitor CitiManager Monthly

Log in to CitiManager monthly. Review transactions, balance, and payment posting. Catch errors and unauthorized charges early.

### Set Account Alerts

Configure alerts for balance thresholds, payment due dates, and unusual transactions.

### Stop Card Use During PTAD or Leave

Per MARADMIN 478/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 478/24" /> paragraph 3.E.4 (ELT-specific) and DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4 (program-wide), GTCC use during PTAD or leave is prohibited. Pay personal expenses with personal funds during these periods.

### Coordinate Mission Critical Status

For long-term TDY, OCONUS PCS, or short-notice elevated travel, coordinate Mission Critical status and limit raises with the APC in advance.

### Communicate With the APC

Notify the APC of any concerns or issues before they escalate. The APC is the primary point of contact and can address problems before they reach suspension thresholds.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What is misuse?

Personal use, non-TDY charges, late payment, exceeding limits, cash advance abuse, charges at prohibited merchants, and willful misrepresentation. Use during PTAD or leave is also prohibited.

What is the standard suspension threshold?

60 days of delinquency on the GTCC bill. The card is suspended at the APC's direction.

What is a Page 11 entry?

An administrative entry on the Marine's record documenting the misuse. Reviewed for promotions, reenlistments, and special duty assignments. Pattern of entries supports adverse action.

Can I be UCMJ'd for GTCC misuse?

Yes, for willful or substantial misuse. Article 92 (failure to obey), Article 121 (larceny), and Article 134 (general article) are commonly applied. Single-incident inadvertent misuse is typically handled through counseling and Page 11.

How do I avoid suspension?

Use split disbursement. Submit vouchers within 5 working days. Monitor CitiManager monthly. Pay any shortfalls immediately. Communicate with the APC about any payment concerns.

What if I accidentally charged a personal expense?

Reimburse the GTCC immediately through CitiManager. Document the error. Do NOT claim the personal expense on the travel voucher. Notify the APC.

How do I get reinstated after suspension?

Pay the full delinquent balance. The APC verifies payment with Citi and reinstates the account. The original Page 11 entry remains.

How do I get reinstated after cancellation?

Pay the full delinquent balance and associated fees. Submit a written reinstatement request through the chain of command. The commander endorses. The APC forwards to higher-level APCs. Approval is at program discretion and not guaranteed.

What if my GTCC is suspended during a TDY?

You cannot use the suspended card. Use personal funds for the remainder of the TDY. The Government reimburses authorized expenses through the standard travel voucher process. Coordinate with the APC immediately to seek reinstatement.

What if my late payment was due to a DFAS processing delay?

Document the delay (DFAS confirmation, voucher approval timestamp). Notify the APC and Citi customer service. Citi can typically reverse late fees and prevent suspension when the delay is documented as a system issue.
