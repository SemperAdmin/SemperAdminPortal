## Start Here If You Are New to the Reenlistment Process

The reenlistment process has FIVE core eligibility criteria and FOUR primary steps. ELIGIBILITY requires (1) End of Active Service (EAS) within the cohort window, (2) Command recommendation, (3) MOS boatspace availability, (4) Physical fitness and body composition compliance, (5) No adverse material in the record. The 4-STEP PROCESS. Step 1. Contact your Career Counselor (your first stop). Step 2. Complete the package (NAVMC 11537<Citation source="NAVMC 11537" /> RELM plus medical, dental, S-1 legal, S-2 security, S-3 operations, SACO endorsements). Step 3. Return to Career Counselor for CO endorsement and electronic HQMC submission via Total Force Retention System (TFRS). Step 4. Action upon HQMC decision (accept the approved package OR decline without negative repercussions). Marines may also engage the PMOS Monitor for career conversation. Per MCO 1040.31<Citation source="MCO 1040.31" />.

This page walks you through reenlistment eligibility and the 4-step process from your seat as a Marine considering reenlistment. By the end you will know.

- The five eligibility criteria
- Step 1 (Career Counselor consultation)
- Step 2 (package completion, NAVMC 11537<Citation source="NAVMC 11537" /> RELM)
- Step 3 (CO endorsement, TFRS submission)
- Step 4 (HQMC decision and acceptance/decline)
- Required package components (medical, dental, legal, security, operations, SACO)
- The PMOS Monitor option for career conversation
- Common procedural errors

## Eligibility Criteria

### Criterion 1. EAS Within the Cohort Window

Each FY Retention Campaign MARADMIN defines the cohort. Example. The FY27 cohort is Marines with End of Current Contract (ECC) between 1 October 2026 and 30 September 2027 per MARADMIN 590/25<Citation source="MARADMIN 590/25" />.

#### How to Identify Your Cohort

- Check your contract end date in MCTFS or MOL.
- Compare to the current FY Retention Campaign MARADMIN.

### Criterion 2. Command Recommendation

The chain of command must recommend the Marine for reenlistment.

- Demonstrated performance and leadership.
- No significant adverse incidents.
- Alignment with unit retention priorities.

### Criterion 3. MOS Boatspace Availability

Boatspaces are the core control mechanism for reenlistment. Each MOS has specific quotas managed by HQMC.

- Closed PMOSs limit reenlistment.
- LATMOV available when PMOS is closed.

The Boatspaces and Allocations leaf covers the framework.

### Criterion 4. Physical Fitness and Body Composition

Marines must meet.

- PFT and CFT standards.
- Body composition standards per MCO 6110.3A<Citation source="MCO 6110.3A" /> and MARADMIN 066/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 066/26" />.

The Body Composition Reporting on Performance Evaluations leaf covers MARADMIN 066/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 066/26" />.

### Criterion 5. No Adverse Material

The record must be clear of disqualifying items including.

- Pending UCMJ action.
- Recent significant misconduct.
- Pattern of unsatisfactory FITREPs.

## The 4-Step Process

### Step 1. Contact Your Career Counselor

The first step in any retention action.

#### How to Find Your Career Counselor

- Through the unit S-1 or admin section.
- Posted in unit retention information.
- Often listed on unit organizational charts.

#### What to Discuss

- Your career intentions.
- Cohort identification and timeline.
- PMOS boatspace status.
- LATMOV opportunities.
- SRB eligibility.
- Duty station preferences (DSI for FTAP).
- Reenlistment vs extension trade-offs.

#### Set Up an Appointment

Schedule with the Career Counselor to begin the package.

### Step 2. Complete a Package

All requests begin with NAVMC 11537<Citation source="NAVMC 11537" /> (RELM). The Career Counselor guides completion.

#### What NAVMC 11537 (RELM) Covers

The lifeblood document for any request. Reenlistments. Extensions. Lateral Moves. Special Duty Assignment (SDA). Special Officer Program. And other career actions.

#### Required Supporting Documents

The Career Counselor identifies what is needed. Typical components.

- Medical readiness check.
- Dental readiness check.
- S-1 legal endorsement.
- S-2 security manager endorsement.
- S-3 operations endorsement.
- SACO (Substance Abuse Control Officer) endorsement.
- Comments from the chain of command up to the OIC.
- Additional documentation specific to the action (SDA checklists, financial checklists, etc.).

#### Why Multiple Endorsements

Each office verifies different aspects.

- Medical and dental confirm physical readiness.
- S-1 legal confirms no adverse legal items.
- S-2 confirms security clearance status.
- S-3 confirms unit operational requirements alignment.
- SACO confirms no substance abuse program assignment.

### Step 3. Return to Career Counselor

Once the Marine has completed all required paperwork.

#### Career Counselor Actions

- Reviews the complete package.
- Submits to the Commanding Officer for endorsement.
- Coordinates corrections if needed.

#### CO Endorsement

The Commanding Officer endorses the package, confirming.

- The Marine meets eligibility criteria.
- The unit recommends reenlistment.
- The package is complete.

#### Electronic Submission via TFRS

Once CO endorses, the Career Counselor submits the package electronically to HQMC (MMEA) through Total Force Retention System (TFRS).

#### Optional. Engage the PMOS Monitor

At any point during the process, the Marine may reach out to the PMOS Monitor to discuss assignment options. Methods.

- Microsoft Teams.
- Email.
- Phone.

The Monitor provides career and future options including duty station opportunities.

### Step 4. Action Request Upon Decision from HQMC

After HQMC review and decision, the request returns to the Career Counselor.

#### Two Options

#### Accept

If the Marine is ready to "Stay Marine," the Marine accepts the approved request.

#### Decline

If the Marine is not at that point of executing the request, the Marine may decline.

#### NO Negative Repercussions for Declining

There are NO negative repercussions from declining your request. You can resubmit another request up to HQMC.

#### Caveat. Specific DSIs or Assignments May Not Be Available

If the original approval included specific DSIs or assignments. Those same options and locations may NOT be available with future resubmissions.

#### Recommendation. Discuss With Career Counselor and PMOS Monitor

Marines are highly encouraged to discuss with the Career Counselor and PMOS Monitor before declining. The opportunity may not return.

## Required Package Components Detail

### Medical Readiness

- PHA (Periodic Health Assessment) current.
- No medical conditions disqualifying for continued service.
- No pending DES referral.

### Dental Readiness

- Dental classification current.
- No conditions disqualifying for deployment.

### Legal (S-1)

- No pending UCMJ actions.
- No civilian charges or convictions.
- No legal restrictions on continued service.

### Security (S-2)

- Security clearance status current.
- No suspensions or revocations.
- Clearance level appropriate for new MOS or assignment if applicable.

### Operations (S-3)

- Unit operational requirements assessment.
- Deployment status considerations.
- Training and qualification status.

### SACO

- No assignment to Marine Corps Substance Abuse Program.
- No pending substance abuse evaluation.
- No documented substance abuse incidents within disqualifying timeframes.

### Other Specific Documents

- SDA checklists for SDA reenlistments.
- Financial checklists for specific bonuses.
- LATMOV-specific qualifications (ASVAB scores, screening assessments).
- Special Officer Program documents if applicable.

## The PMOS Monitor

### What the PMOS Monitor Does

A subject matter expert at HQMC for your PMOS. Monitors.

- Manage PMOS boatspaces.
- Coordinate assignments.
- Provide career counseling within the PMOS.
- Make duty station decisions.

### When to Engage the Monitor

- During package preparation for assignment options discussion.
- When considering specific duty stations.
- Before declining an HQMC decision.
- For career path conversations.

### How to Engage the Monitor

Through the Career Counselor for contact information. Methods include MS Teams, email, or phone.

## TFRS Workflow

### Marine Submits

The Marine completes paperwork with the Career Counselor.

### Career Counselor Submits

The Career Counselor enters the package into TFRS for HQMC routing.

### CO Endorsement

CO endorsement is captured in TFRS.

### HQMC Review

MMEA processors review the package in TFRS. Decision is recorded.

### Return to Career Counselor

The decision flows back to the Career Counselor through TFRS. The Career Counselor delivers the decision to the Marine.

## Common Procedural Errors

### Error 1. Skipping the Career Counselor

Marines who try to handle reenlistment paperwork without the Career Counselor often miss requirements or submit incorrectly.

### Error 2. Late Engagement

Engaging 30 days before EAS gives no time to resolve issues. 12-18 months out is the recommended window.

### Error 3. Missing Endorsements

Each required endorsement is necessary. Missing any one delays processing.

### Error 4. Not Engaging PMOS Monitor

Many Marines skip the Monitor conversation. The Monitor may have career-shaping insights.

### Error 5. Declining Without Discussion

Declining can be the right choice but should follow discussion with the Career Counselor and Monitor. Specific DSIs and assignments may not be available with resubmissions.

### Error 6. Not Tracking Cohort

Marines should know their cohort and submission window from the FY Retention Campaign MARADMIN.

## Common Reenlistment Process Scenarios

### Scenario 1. FTAP Marine Reenlisting at EAS

First-term Marine completes 4-year contract, wants to reenlist.

- Step 1. Contact Career Counselor 12-18 months out.
- Step 2. Complete RELM and supporting documents.
- Step 3. CO endorses, submitted via TFRS.
- Step 4. HQMC approves with assignment. Marine accepts and reenlists.

### Scenario 2. STAP Marine Reenlisting in Closed PMOS

Career Marine at end of subsequent contract. PMOS closed.

- Step 1. Contact Career Counselor.
- Step 2. Discuss LATMOV options.
- Step 3. Submit LATMOV package via TFRS.
- Step 4. HQMC approves with new MOS designation and school assignment.

### Scenario 3. Marine Wants to Decline HQMC Decision

Marine receives HQMC approval but is unsure about reenlisting.

- Action. Discuss with Career Counselor and PMOS Monitor.
- Decision. Decline if not ready.
- Result. No negative repercussions. Can resubmit later. Specific DSIs and assignments from prior approval may not return.

### Scenario 4. Marine Has Pending Legal Issue

Marine wants to reenlist but has pending UCMJ action.

- Issue. Adverse material disqualifies.
- Action. Wait for resolution. Coordinate with Defense Counsel and Career Counselor.
- Result. Reenlist after resolution if cleared.

### Scenario 5. Marine Engages PMOS Monitor for Assignment Conversation

Marine wants specific duty station for next contract.

- Action. Through Career Counselor, contact PMOS Monitor.
- Discussion. Available billets, timing, career impact.
- Result. Marine submits with informed preferences.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1. Waiting Until 30 Days Before EAS

By then, the package processing time alone can miss the EAS. 12-18 months is the right window.

### Mistake 2. Bypassing the Career Counselor

The Career Counselor is your guide. Bypassing leads to incomplete or incorrect submissions.

### Mistake 3. Submitting With Incomplete Endorsements

Each required endorsement must be present. Missing one returns the package.

### Mistake 4. Not Engaging the PMOS Monitor

The Monitor has insights that affect your assignment options. Worth the conversation.

### Mistake 5. Misunderstanding Decline Implications

Declining is allowed without consequences but specific approved options may not return.

### Mistake 6. Not Verifying Boatspace

PMOS may close before submission. Verify boatspace status with Career Counselor.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What is the first step?

Contact your Career Counselor.

What is NAVMC 11537<Citation source="NAVMC 11537" />?

The Reenlistment / Extension / Lateral Move (RELM) request form. The lifeblood document for any retention action.

What endorsements are required?

Medical, dental, S-1 (legal), S-2 (security), S-3 (operations), SACO. Plus chain of command endorsement up to OIC.

What is TFRS?

Total Force Retention System. The digital portal where the Career Counselor submits the package to HQMC.

How does CO endorsement happen?

The Career Counselor routes the package to the CO. CO endorses confirming eligibility and unit recommendation. Recorded in TFRS.

Can I talk to the PMOS Monitor?

Yes. Through your Career Counselor for contact info. Methods include Teams, email, phone.

What if I want to decline the HQMC approval?

Decline. No negative repercussions. Can resubmit. Specific DSIs and assignments may not be available with resubmissions.

When should I start the process?

12-18 months before EAS. Early engagement allows time to resolve issues and secure desired options.

What disqualifies me?

EAS outside the cohort window. Lack of command recommendation. Closed PMOS without LATMOV. Failure to meet PFT/CFT or body composition. Adverse material in the record.

What is the SACO endorsement?

Substance Abuse Control Officer endorsement confirming no substance abuse program assignment.

How long does HQMC processing take?

Varies. Standard reenlistments typically process within several weeks. Coordinate with Career Counselor for current timelines.

What if my package is returned?

Address the noted issue and resubmit. Coordinate with Career Counselor.
