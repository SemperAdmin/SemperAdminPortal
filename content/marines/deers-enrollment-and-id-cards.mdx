## Start Here If You Are New to DEERS and USIP Cards

DEERS (DEFENSE ENROLLMENT ELIGIBILITY REPORTING SYSTEM) is the primary DoD system for verifying military eligibility. RAPIDS (REAL-TIME AUTOMATED PERSONNEL IDENTIFICATION SYSTEM) is the network of computers that updates DEERS and issues USIP (UNIFORMED SERVICES ID AND PRIVILEGES) cards. Schedule RAPIDS appointments via ID CARD OFFICE ONLINE at idco.dmdc.osd.mil. After any life event adding a dependent, complete RAPIDS appointment within 30 DAYS. Dependent must be present in person with original or certified documents. milConnect at milconnect.dmdc.osd.mil for online updates and verification. The DEERS data is fed by Marine Corps Manpower Information System updates from S-1/IPAC submissions. Per DoDI 1000.13<Citation source="DoDI 1000.13" /> and MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />.

## What DEERS Does

DEERS is the authoritative system that records.

- Service member identifying information.
- Dependent enrollments.
- Sponsor-dependent relationships.
- TRICARE eligibility.
- Commissary, exchange, and morale, welfare, and recreation eligibility.
- USIP card issuance history.

### Who Manages DEERS

- Defense Manpower Data Center (DMDC) at the DoD level.
- HQMC Dependency Determination Section for adjudications and validations affecting Marines.
- Marine Corps Manpower Information System feeds Marine-specific data.

### How DEERS Gets Updated

- S-1 and IPAC submissions through MCTFS.
- RAPIDS appointments at installation sites.
- Marine self-service via Marine Online and milConnect.
- HQMC Dependency Determination Section adjudications.

### Why DEERS Matters

- Without DEERS enrollment, a dependent cannot access TRICARE.
- USIP card cannot be issued without DEERS enrollment.
- Commissary, exchange, and MWR access requires valid USIP.
- Dependency-related entitlements (BAH, FSA, etc.) trace back to DEERS.

## What RAPIDS Does

RAPIDS is the network of computers at installation ID card sites that processes DEERS updates and issues USIP cards.

### What RAPIDS Sites Do

- Verify identity of sponsor and dependents.
- Enroll new dependents in DEERS.
- Update existing dependent information (address, name change, etc.).
- Issue new and replacement USIP cards.
- Activate TRICARE eligibility upon enrollment.
- Confirm secondary dependency adjudications from HQMC.

### Where RAPIDS Sites Are

- Most major installations have a RAPIDS site (S-1, MCCS, or PMO administrative section).
- Joint base sites serve all branches.
- Some recruiting and reserve sites have RAPIDS capability.
- Find sites and schedule via ID Card Office Online (idco.dmdc.osd.mil).

## The USIP Card and Eligibility Tiers

### What USIP Provides

- Photo identification of eligible dependents.
- TRICARE access at military and network providers.
- Commissary and exchange access.
- MWR access (gym, pool, recreation activities).
- Installation access (typically required for base entry).

### Eligibility Tiers

#### Active Duty Sponsor Dependents

- Spouse. Full USIP card.
- Children under 21. Full USIP card.
- Children 21-22 (full-time students). Full USIP card with student verification.
- Incapacitated adult children. Full USIP card with DD 137 approval.
- Wards (legal custody, under 21). Full USIP card.
- Parent, parent-in-law, stepparent (residing with sponsor). USIP card via DD 137.

#### Limited Dependents

- Parent, parent-in-law, stepparent (NOT residing with sponsor). NOT eligible for USIP card. May still qualify for BAH and travel allowance only.
- In-loco parentis. NEVER eligible for USIP card. May qualify for BAH only.

#### Former Spouses

- 20/20/20 former spouse. Full USIP card (former spouse type).
- 20/20/15 former spouse. USIP card limited to one year post-divorce.
- Other former spouse. NOT eligible.

### Card Expiration

- Spouse and adult child cards typically issued for 4 years or until age-out.
- Children under 10 issued without photo (caregiver photo on parent card).
- Children 10 and over issued with photo.
- Renewal required upon expiration.

## Scheduling RAPIDS Appointments

### ID Card Office Online (idco.dmdc.osd.mil)

The DMDC ID Card Office Online is the primary scheduling tool.

- Search for nearest RAPIDS site by ZIP code.
- View available appointment slots.
- Book appointment for sponsor or sponsor + dependent.
- Receive email confirmation.
- Cancel or reschedule online.

### Walk-In Versus Appointment

Most RAPIDS sites prefer appointments. Walk-in availability varies. For dependent enrollment, appointments are recommended.

### What If You Cannot Get An Appointment

- Check multiple nearby installations.
- Joint base sites often have more capacity.
- Some sites offer evening or Saturday hours.
- Reserve sites are an option for Reserve Marines and may have less wait.

## The 30-Day Expectation After Dependent Add

After submitting NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> to S-1 with the supporting documents, schedule the RAPIDS appointment within 30 DAYS.

### Why 30 Days

- TRICARE coverage gap risk if delayed.
- USIP card needed for installation access, commissary, MWR.
- DD Form 1172-2<Citation source="DD Form 1172-2" /> may need notarization if dependent cannot attend with sponsor.
- Family Care Plan validation may depend on DEERS enrollment.

### What Happens If You Wait

- Newborn TRICARE coverage automatic only 60 days.
- Spouse access to military installations limited without USIP.
- Pay system may not reflect dependent until DEERS enrollment.
- Audit trail shows delay.

## What To Bring To A RAPIDS Appointment

### For Sponsor (You)

- Your active duty USIP card (CAC) or alternate ID.
- DD Form 1172-2<Citation source="DD Form 1172-2" /> if dependent attending without you.

### For Spouse Add

- Original or certified marriage certificate.
- Spouse's birth certificate (certified).
- Spouse's Social Security card.
- Spouse's photo ID (driver's license, passport, etc.).

### For Child Add

- Original or certified birth certificate.
- Child's Social Security card (when issued).
- Child must be present (over age 10 with photo).

### For Adoption

- Final adoption decree (certified).
- Adoptive birth certificate (post-adoption issuance).

### For Custody Add

- Certified court order.
- Child's birth certificate.

### For Secondary Dependency

- Approval letter from HQMC Dependency Determination Section.
- DD Form 137<Citation source="DD Form 137" /> approved copy.
- Identifying documents per DD 137 type.

### For Card Replacement

- Lost. Police report if applicable, sponsor with you to verify.
- Damaged. Damaged card.
- Expired. Expired card.

## Card Replacement, Expiration, and Lost Card Procedures

### Renewal Before Expiration

- Schedule RAPIDS appointment 30 days before expiration.
- Bring expiring card.
- Bring original or certified documents if any information changed.

### Expired Card

- Card is no longer valid for any privilege.
- Schedule RAPIDS appointment ASAP.
- TRICARE may have grace period. Confirm with regional contractor.

### Lost or Stolen Card

- Report to local PMO if stolen.
- Notify sponsor command for tracking.
- Schedule RAPIDS appointment for replacement.
- Bring police report if stolen.

### Damaged Card

- Bring damaged card to RAPIDS appointment.
- New card issued same day in most cases.

## milConnect for Online Verification

### What milConnect Does

milConnect at milconnect.dmdc.osd.mil is the DMDC self-service portal.

- View your DEERS enrollment information.
- View dependent enrollments.
- Update contact information.
- View benefit eligibility.
- Update SGLI beneficiaries (for service member).
- Print eligibility letters for civilian providers.

### What You Can Do Online

- Update mailing and email address.
- Update phone numbers.
- Add or update emergency contacts.
- View TRICARE eligibility.

### What You CANNOT Do Online

- Initial dependent enrollment (requires RAPIDS).
- USIP card issuance (requires RAPIDS).
- Marriage, divorce, birth, custody updates (requires NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> through S-1).
- Secondary dependency approval (requires DD 137 through S-1 and HQMC).

## HQMC Dependency Determination Section Coordination

The HQMC Dependency Determination Section adjudicates complex DEERS questions and secondary dependency claims.

### What They Adjudicate

- DD Form 137<Citation source="DD Form 137" /> secondary dependency approvals.
- BAH eligibility determinations.
- Investigations of non-support complaints.
- USIP card eligibility for parent/parent-in-law/stepparent (residence determinations).

### How To Reach Them

- Through your S-1 and IPAC. Direct contact is uncommon for individual Marines.
- IPAC routes complex cases.
- Coordination with HQMC Manpower for Marine-specific cases.

### Marine and Family Programs Branch (MF) Connection

The HQMC Dependency Determination Section sits within the Marine and Family Programs Branch (MF), alongside Casualty Assistance, EFMP, CYP, SLP, Career Services, Education Services, Family Readiness Services (UPFRP), Marine for Life, and CREDO. Many of these programs may inform or follow from your DEERS-related decisions.

## Common Errors

### Wrong Documents Brought to RAPIDS

Photocopies are not enough. Original or certified documents with raised seal are required. Triple-check before the appointment.

### Dependent Not Present

For initial enrollment, the dependent (over age 10) must be present. Children under 10 may be enrolled with the sponsor only.

### Wrong DD Form 1172-2

If sponsor cannot attend with the dependent, DD Form 1172-2<Citation source="DD Form 1172-2" /> must be NOTARIZED. Unsigned or improperly notarized forms result in turn-away.

### Schedule Conflict With Marine Online Submission

The DEERS update may not appear immediately after S-1 submission. Allow 24-48 hours after IPAC processing before the RAPIDS appointment, or request the RAPIDS site verify with the IPAC system.

### Forgetting milConnect

Marines often forget that milConnect is available for many minor updates. Use it for address, phone, and emergency contact changes without scheduling a RAPIDS visit.

### Card Expiration Surprise

Marines often forget to check dependent card expiration. Set calendar reminders 60 days before expiration.

### Failure to Update After Custody Change

Custody changes require DEERS update. Failure can result in TRICARE coverage for ineligible person or denial for eligible person.

## Records To Keep

- Original or certified documents for all enrollments
- DEERS enrollment confirmation
- USIP card issuance receipt
- DD Form 1172-2<Citation source="DD Form 1172-2" /> (if used)
- TRICARE enrollment confirmation
- Card expiration calendar reminders

If you have a question about DEERS or USIP cards, your unit S-1 and the RAPIDS site are first contacts. For complex secondary dependency or eligibility questions, S-1 routes to the HQMC Dependency Determination Section.
