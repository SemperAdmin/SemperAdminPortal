## Start Here If You Are New to Special Pays

Beyond basic pay, BAH, and BAS, Marines see additional pay lines on the LES tied to specific qualifications, duties, or written agreements. These are special pays. They scale from a few hundred dollars per month for routine qualifications up to retention bonuses worth tens of thousands per year for officers in critical career fields.

This page walks you through special pays from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The two-category framework (bonuses under written agreement vs. recurring special pays for qualification or duty)
- The four officer bonuses Marine Corps officers see most often
- The career cap on combined bonus payments
- Command pay for select O-4 through O-6 commanders
- The recoupment risk if you fail to fulfill the conditions of a bonus agreement
- Where the other special pays (jump, dive, flight, demolition, SDA, sea, hardship, FLPP) will appear as future chapters arrive

Every fact on this page comes from DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 3 (March 2024 revision), Chapter 2 (repayment, February 2024), DoDI 1304.34 (General Bonus Authority for Officers), Title 37 U.S.C. Chapter 5, and current DFAS Marine Corps Pay guidance.

## What Special Pays Are

Special pays are payments above basic pay tied to specific service conditions. The two categories.

### Category 1. Bonuses Under Written Agreement

A Marine signs a written agreement with the Secretary of the Military Department in exchange for a bonus payment. The Marine agrees to.

- Remain on active duty for a specified period, OR
- Remain in an active status in a Reserve Component, OR
- Perform duty in a specified skill, with or without a specified qualification or credential, OR
- Perform duty at a specified location, OR
- Perform duty for a specified period of time.

The bonus is paid up front (or in installments) and earned over the period of obligated service. If the Marine fails to fulfill the conditions, the unearned portion is subject to recoupment per Volume 7A Chapter 2 (covered on the Pay Problems and Fixes page).

This category includes accession bonuses, retention bonuses, RC affiliation bonuses, and transfer bonuses.

### Category 2. Recurring Special Pays for Qualifications or Duties

A Marine receives a recurring monthly or annual payment tied to a current qualification (jump, dive, flight, demolition, foreign language) or current duty (Drill Instructor, Recruiter, MEU staff, sea time, hardship duty location). The payment continues while the qualification or assignment continues. No written agreement is required.

This category includes Special Duty Assignment Pay (SDA), jump pay, dive pay, flight pay, demolition pay, sea pay, hardship duty pay, foreign language proficiency pay (FLPP), and similar entitlements.

The substantive content for Category 2 special pays will populate as the relevant DoD FMR chapters arrive. This page will be expanded each time.

## Officer Bonuses (Volume 7A Chapter 3)

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 3 (March 2024) and DoDI 1304.34, the General Bonus Authority for Officers covers four officer bonuses, plus a Navy-only nuclear bonus track and a discretionary command pay.

### General Eligibility Rules (Apply to All Four Officer Bonuses)

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 2.1 and 2.2, the Secretary concerned pays officer bonuses to a Marine who.

- Accepts a commission or appointment as an officer
- Affiliates as an officer with a Reserve Component
- Agrees to remain on active duty or in active status for a specified period
- Transfers between Regular Component and Ready Reserve of the same service, or vice versa
- Transfers between Military Services (subject to gaining service Secretary approval)

The Marine must.

- Sign a written agreement specifying the period and conditions
- Successfully complete training and qualification (if training is the basis for the bonus)
- Qualify pursuant to additional criteria the Secretary concerned prescribes
- Not be already in receipt of an accession, retention, RC affiliation, or transfer bonus for the same activity, skill, or period of service per Title 37 U.S.C. Chapter 5
- Not exceed mandatory retirement or high-year tenure during the obligated period
- Not be already serving an existing service obligation (with narrow exceptions)

### Disqualifying Conditions

Per paragraph 2.3.1, an officer is not eligible for a bonus under DoDI 1304.34 if previously discharged or released from active duty or active status based on misconduct, substandard duty performance, or moral or professional dereliction.

Per paragraph 2.3.2 and Title 37 U.S.C. section 371, an officer is not allowed to receive a bonus and a Subchapter II incentive pay for the same activity, skill, or period.

### Career Cap on Combined Bonuses

Per paragraph 2.3.4, an officer is not allowed to receive more than $300,000 in combined bonus payments authorized under DoDI 1304.34 during the course of the officer's career. The cap drops to $150,000 for a non-Active Guard Reserve officer in the RC.

The Military Service is not authorized to exceed these caps unless specifically authorized by the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs (ASD M&RA).

### Bonus 1. Accession Bonus

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 3.0 through 3.7.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for a person (including an enlisted Service member) to accept a commission or appointment as an officer.
- Minimum service obligation. 4 years on active duty or in SELRES.
- Maximum amount. $60,000 total for the minimum 4-year period. The annual cap is $15,000.
- Restrictions per paragraph 3.3. Only one accession bonus per career. An officer reappointed after resignation or termination from an earlier period of military service is not eligible. An officer is not allowed to receive an accession bonus and a transfer bonus for the same period. An officer is not allowed to receive an accession bonus and an affiliation bonus for the same period.
- Disqualifiers per paragraph 3.2.4. Pre-commissioning compensation under Title 10 U.S.C. sections 2106, 2107, or 2107a (e.g., ROTC, USNA scholarship). Loan repayment programs under Title 10 U.S.C. sections 16201, 16301, 16302, or 16303. A prior accession bonus under Title 37 U.S.C. sections 324 or 330.

### Bonus 2. RC Affiliation Bonus

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 4.0 through 4.7.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for an officer to affiliate with the SELRES of a Military Service.
- Minimum service obligation. 3 years in SELRES, in a critical military skill or specific SELRES unit or grade with a manpower shortage.
- Maximum amount. $10,000 for the minimum 3-year obligation.
- Eligibility per paragraph 4.2. The officer must have fewer than 15 years of qualifying service toward retirement, must be serving in the Ready Reserve or Standby Reserve, OR be serving (or have served) in the Regular Component for more than 30 days and released under honorable conditions.
- Multiple affiliation bonuses per career allowed (per paragraph 4.3.1), though not for the same skill, grade, or unit.
- Affiliation bonus and transfer bonus together. Allowed if served consecutively. The combined obligation is at least 4 years (12 additional months over the affiliation alone) per paragraph 4.3.3.

### Bonus 3. Retention Bonus

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 5.0 through 5.7.

- Purpose. Voluntary extension of an officer's service in exchange for an agreement to remain in a designated military skill, career field, unit, or grade.
- Minimum service obligation. 2 years.
- Eligibility per paragraph 5.2. Must have completed at least 4 years of active commissioned service (or qualifying RC service per DoDI 1215.07).
- Maximum amount. $25,000 per year of obligated service for Regular Component officers. $12,000 per year for RC officers. Prorate for retention requests longer than 2 years at 1/12th the annual amount per month.
- Restrictions per paragraph 5.3. The officer must not have between 16 and 19 years of active commissioned service at the start of the bonus period (this is the gate to retirement-eligibility window). The officer must not be retirement-eligible with more than 22 years of active service. The bonus must not extend the officer's service beyond 25 years of active commissioned service.
- Post-9/11 GI Bill or Montgomery GI Bill-SELRES transfer to dependent allowed. The Secretary concerned determines whether the retention bonus and GI Bill transfer obligations run concurrently or consecutively.

### Bonus 4. Transfer Bonus

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 6.0 through 6.7. Two types: intra-service and inter-service.

#### Intra-Service Transfer Bonus

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for an officer to transfer from the Regular Component to the Ready Reserve, or vice versa, within the same Military Service.
- Eligibility per paragraph 6.1.3. The officer must have fulfilled all current service obligations satisfactorily, meet the qualification criteria for the gaining component, agree to remain in the new component for at least 2 years, and have fewer than 15 years of active service.
- Mobilization is not an intra-service transfer for bonus purposes.

#### Inter-Service Transfer Bonus

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for an officer to transfer to a different Military Service to meet manning requirements.
- Eligibility per paragraph 6.2.4. The officer must qualify for retention in the gaining service, have fulfilled all MSOs in the current service, meet the qualification criteria the gaining Secretary establishes, sign a written agreement to serve at least 3 years in the gaining service, and have fewer than 15 years of active service.
- Restriction per paragraph 6.3. An officer receiving separation pay per Title 10 U.S.C. Chapter 59 is not eligible for a transfer bonus.

#### Transfer Bonus Amount and Combinations

- Maximum amount per paragraph 6.4. $10,000.
- Affiliation and transfer bonus together (allowed under specific conditions). Adds 12 months of additional service obligation per paragraph 6.2.5.2, for a minimum 4-year combined MSO.

### Navy-Only Nuclear Officer Bonuses

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 7.0 through 7.4, the Navy pays additional special pays to nuclear-qualified officers.

- Nuclear Officer Accession Bonus. Up to $75,000 per OPNAVINST 7220.11J.
- Nuclear Officer Continuation Bonus (COBO). Up to $75,000 per 12-month period.
- Nuclear Career Annual Incentive Pay (AIP). Up to $25,000 per 12-month period of qualifying service per Title 37 U.S.C. section 333(d)(1)(B).
- Prototype Shift Engineer Incentive Pay (SEIP). Specific monthly rate per OPNAVINST 7220.11J.

These nuclear-track bonuses are Navy-only and do not apply to Marine Corps officers. Listed here for cross-service awareness.

### Command Pay

Per Chapter 3 paragraphs 8.0 through 8.4, command pay recognizes officers assigned as commanding officers in operational leadership positions of unusual responsibility critical to the Military Service.

- Eligibility per paragraph 8.2. The Secretary concerned establishes eligibility based on paygrade, level of responsibility, and operational nature of the assignment.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 8.3. $150 per month.
- Restrictions per paragraph 8.4. No more than 10 percent of the number of officers on active duty in pay grades O-4 through O-6 in a Military Service is allowed to receive command pay. Not authorized for more than one officer per unit (except during dates of assumption and relief from command).

The Marine Corps designates specific command billets eligible for command pay through internal directives. Battalion and squadron commanders, regimental and group commanders, and similar operational command billets typically qualify.

## Enlisted Bonuses (Volume 7A Chapter 9)

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 9 (August 2024), DoDI 1304.31, and Title 37 U.S.C. section 331, the active-duty enlisted bonus framework covers seven distinct bonuses tied to written agreements. This is the enlisted parallel to Chapter 3 (officer bonuses).

### Repayment Framework

Per Chapter 9 paragraph 1.3, an enlisted Marine who does not complete the agreed term of service or otherwise meet the conditions of service for the bonus is subject to the repayment provisions of Chapter 2. The early-discharge exception per Title 10 U.S.C. section 1171 applies. A Marine discharged 12 months or less before EAS is considered to have completed the term for which the bonus was paid (covered on the Pay Problems and Fixes page).

### Bonus 1. Enlistment Bonus (EB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 2.0 through 2.4.

- Purpose. For initial enlistees in the Regular Component for service in a designated military skill, career field, unit, grade, or other condition imposed by the Secretary concerned.
- Eligibility per paragraph 2.1. Possess HS diploma, completion certificate in lieu of HS diploma, or GED. Be an initial enlistee. Enlist for at least 2 years (or extend an initial obligation to a total of at least 2 years) in the Regular Component. Execute a written agreement specifying the bonus amount, payment method, period of obligated service, and designated skill/career field/unit/grade.
- Disqualifiers per paragraphs 2.1.5 and 2.1.6. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received an enlistment bonus, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received or be eligible to receive an SRB or CSRB.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 2.2. $75,000 for a minimum 2-year service obligation. This rate is authorized from 11 January 2024 through 10 January 2027.
- Method of payment per paragraph 2.4. Periodic installments or single lump sum. Initial payment is not paid before completion of basic recruit training. For specialties requiring formal training, the Service is allowed to withhold initial payment until skill qualification.

### Bonus 2. Selective Reenlistment Bonus (SRB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 3.0 through 3.7.

- Purpose. To mitigate significant personnel shortages or changing force structure requirements through targeted reenlistment incentives.
- Eligibility per paragraph 3.1. Pay grade E-3 or higher. Reenlist for at least 3 years OR voluntarily extend an enlistment for at least 1 year. Execute a written agreement.
- Indefinite reenlistment requirements per paragraph 3.1.8. Pay grade E-5 or above, 10 or more years of military service, sign agreement to remain on active duty for at least 3 years.
- Reenlistment window per paragraph 3.2.1. Re-entry or reenlistment is required no later than 3 months after discharge or release (or shorter period prescribed by the Secretary).
- Broken service / prior service re-entry SRB per paragraph 3.2.2. Veterans with more than 3-month but less than 4-year break in active duty are allowed to qualify under separate Service regulations.
- Disqualifiers per paragraph 3.2.7. The Marine is not eligible if previously discharged or released based on misconduct, substandard duty performance, or moral or professional dereliction.
- 28-year service ceiling per paragraph 3.2.8. SRB payments to a Marine are not allowed past 28 years of active duty unless waived by the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Military Personnel Policy.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 3.3. The statutory cap is $50,000 per year of obligated service. DoD policy limits the SRB to $30,000 per year of obligated service. The maximum single SRB is $180,000.
- Lifetime career cap per paragraph 3.3.2. Total combined SRB payments over a career are not allowed to exceed $360,000.
- Method of payment per paragraph 3.5. Lump sum or periodic installments. Initial installment after reenlistment with break in service is paid no earlier than 30 days after arrival at the first permanent duty station following reenlistment.

### Bonus 3. Critical Skill Retention Bonus (CSRB)

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 4.0 through 4.4.

- Purpose. For enlisted Marines qualified in a critical military skill designated by the Secretary of Defense.
- Eligibility per paragraph 4.1. Serving on active duty in a Regular Component, qualified in the critical skill, not on indefinite enlistment, reenlist or voluntarily extend for at least 1 year.
- 25-year service limit per paragraph 4.2.1. CSRB is not authorized for a Marine who has completed more than 25 years of active duty (or who will reach 25 years before the bonus period ends). Exception. Marines qualified in a special operations forces skill.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 4.3.2. $30,000 annually unless PDUSD M&RA grants an exception.
- Combined CSRB and SRB cap. The combined total of CSRB and SRB during an individual's career is not allowed to exceed $200,000 unless PDUSD M&RA authorizes an exception for the specific skill.
- Method of payment per paragraph 4.4. Single lump sum or periodic installments.

### Bonus 4. Prior Service Reenlistment Bonus

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 5.0 through 5.3.

- Purpose. For Marines who reenlist in a Regular Component after a break in active duty.
- Eligibility per paragraph 5.1. The Marine is not allowed to have previously received an SRB or CSRB. The Marine is not allowed to have more than 16 years of total military service. The Marine must have an honorable discharge for all previous periods. The Marine must reenlist for at least 3 years.
- Documentation. Original DD-214 (copy 1 or copy 4), or certified true-copy reproduction, or other official documentation verifying satisfactory participation.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 5.2. $20,000 per year of obligated service in a Regular Component.
- Method of payment per paragraph 5.3. Lump sum or periodic installments. The Marine Corps is not authorized to pay any portion of the bonus prior to completion of training. Marines already technically qualified receive their first installment no earlier than 30 days after arrival at the first permanent duty station.

### Bonus 5. Conversion Bonus

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 6.0 through 6.3.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for a Marine to convert to and serve in a designated military skill experiencing a shortage of trained and qualified personnel.
- Eligibility per paragraph 6.1. At time of agreement, pay grade E-6 or below with no more than 12 years of service. Have completed all service obligations from prior EB, SRB, or CSRB. Extend the existing enlistment to qualify if less than 3 years of active duty remaining. Execute a written agreement.
- Disqualifier per paragraph 6.1.6. The Marine is not allowed to be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 6.2. $10,000.
- Method of payment per paragraph 6.3. Lump sum or periodic installments. The Marine Corps is not allowed to pay any portion before completion of conversion training.

### Bonus 6. Transfer Between Military Services

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 7.0 through 7.2.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for an enlisted Marine to transfer to and serve in another Military Service.
- Eligibility per paragraph 7.1. Execute a written agreement with the gaining Service for a minimum 3-year period. Have satisfactorily completed any term of enlistment in the current service. Qualify for reenlistment in the gaining service. Not be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period. Not be in receipt of separation pay per Title 10 U.S.C. Chapter 59.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 7.2. $10,000 lump sum at time of transfer approval, or annual installments totaling no more than $10,000.

### Bonus 7. Transfer Between Components of a Military Service

Per Chapter 9 paragraphs 8.0 through 8.2.

- Purpose. Monetary incentive for a Marine to transfer between the Regular Component and the Ready Reserve (or vice versa) of the same service.
- Eligibility per paragraph 8.1. Execute a written agreement specifying period and conditions. Have satisfactorily completed all terms of enlistment in the current component. Qualify for reenlistment in the gaining component. Agree to remain in the gaining component for at least 2 years. Have fewer than 15 years of active service.
- Disqualifier per paragraph 8.1.3. The Marine is not allowed to be in receipt of an EB, retention bonus, affiliation bonus, or transfer bonus for the same period.
- Maximum amount per paragraph 8.2. $10,000.

### Career Cap Summary for Enlisted Bonuses

- Single EB. Up to $75,000 (current authorization period 11 Jan 2024 to 10 Jan 2027).
- Single SRB. Up to $180,000 (DoD policy of $30,000/year times the maximum 6-year obligation under SRB).
- Lifetime SRB. Up to $360,000.
- Lifetime combined CSRB and SRB. Up to $200,000 unless waived by PDUSD M&RA.
- Conversion Bonus. Up to $10,000 per agreement.
- Transfer Between Services. Up to $10,000.
- Transfer Between Components. Up to $10,000.
- Prior Service Reenlistment Bonus. Up to $20,000 per year of obligated service.

The various bonuses are not allowed to overlap for the same period. Each bonus has its own disqualifier list. EB disqualified by prior SRB or CSRB. SRB disqualified by current EB. Read the written agreement before signing to confirm the period covered and the disqualifying interactions.

## Service Obligations

Per Chapter 3 paragraph 2.8, an officer with an existing service obligation is not eligible for a new bonus, with four exceptions.

- The officer is serving the initial service obligation incurred at commissioning or appointment per DoDI 1304.25.
- The officer incurred a service obligation due to permanent change in duty station or promotion.
- The officer incurred a service obligation for attending professional military education or government-funded education courses (excluding undergraduate education).
- The officer incurred a service obligation as a result of transferring Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits.

For the first three categories, service obligations run concurrently with bonus obligations. For the GI Bill transfer category, the Secretary determines whether the obligations run concurrently or consecutively.

Outside the four exceptions, a new bonus is not paid until the prior service obligation completes. The new bonus service obligation begins on the date of the new agreement or completion of the prior obligation, whichever is later.

## Recoupment of Unearned Bonus

Per Chapter 3 paragraph 2.7 and Volume 7A Chapter 2, an officer who fails to complete the period of obligated service or other conditions specified in the written agreement repays any unearned portion of the bonus per Title 37 U.S.C. section 373 and Chapter 2.

The Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site covers the full recoupment framework, including the nine rules in Chapter 2 Table 2-1 governing when repayment is and is not sought, the discretionary cases under the Secretary's review, and the 5-year bankruptcy rule.

Key takeaways for an officer signing a bonus contract.

- Read the full written agreement before signing. The terms drive the recoupment math if conditions are not fulfilled.
- Document any service-affecting events (PCS, MOS change, medical, hardship) so the controlling Chapter 2 rule is identifiable.
- For service members directed to change MOS or assignment by the Marine Corps, recoupment is not sought per Chapter 2 paragraph 3.3.4.

## Special Duty Assignment Pay (SDAP)

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 8 (August 2025), Title 37 U.S.C. section 352, DoDI 1340.26, and MCO 7220.12R, Special Duty Assignment Pay (SDAP) is the most common recurring special pay enlisted Marines see beyond combat-zone entitlements.

### Purpose

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 1.1, SDAP is awarded to Service Members for the performance of duty in an assignment, location, or unit designated, where the assigned duties are determined to be.

- Extremely demanding, OR
- Involving an unusual degree of responsibility or difficulty, OR
- Requiring special qualifications.

The Marine Corps designates specific billets eligible for SDAP through MCO 7220.12R. Common SDAP-designated billets include.

- Drill Instructors at Marine Corps Recruit Depots (Parris Island, San Diego)
- Recruiters at Recruiting Stations
- Marine Security Guards (MSG) at U.S. Embassies and consulates
- Marine Expeditionary Unit (MEU) staff in specific billets
- Other billets designated by the Commandant of the Marine Corps through internal directives

The Marine Corps reviews and updates the SDAP-designated list periodically. Verify your billet's current SDAP designation through your S-1 or the controlling MCO 7220.12R amendments.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 2.2.1, SDAP is paid to a Marine who.

- Is entitled to basic pay under Title 37 U.S.C. section 204 (active duty) or compensation under Title 37 U.S.C. section 206 (Reserve component drill compensation), AND
- Performs duties in an assignment, location, or unit designated, under the conditions of service the Secretary concerned specifies.

Per paragraph 2.2.2, SDAP is paid in addition to any other pay or allowances the Marine is receiving, except where otherwise stated.

### Reserve Component SDAP Proration

Per paragraph 2.3.1, a Reserve Component Marine entitled to pay under Title 37 U.S.C. section 206 is paid SDAP proportionate to the inactive duty training (IDT) compensation received. The amount is 1/30th of the prescribed monthly rate of SDAP for each authorized period of IDT with pay.

Translation. A Reservist drilling on a standard weekend (typically four IDT periods) receives 4/30ths of the monthly SDAP rate for that drill weekend.

### Maximum Amount

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 2.4.

- A single SDAP rate is not allowed to exceed $750 per month per paragraph 2.4.2.
- The combined total of assignment and special duty pays a Marine receives is not allowed to exceed an average monthly amount of $3,500, unless ASD M&RA specifically authorizes more per paragraph 2.4.1.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments establish specific monthly rates within these caps. Marine Corps SDAP rates by designated billet are in MCO 7220.12R.

### Multiple SDAPs Allowed

Per paragraph 2.3.4, a Marine is allowed to receive multiple assignment and special duty pays. The restriction. A Marine is not allowed to receive multiple pays for the same purpose and period of service.

A Drill Instructor with a separate qualification-based special pay does not double-count for the same purpose. A Marine in two distinct SDAP-designated billets concurrently receives both rates as long as the duties are not overlapping.

### Discontinuance Rules

Per paragraph 2.3.2, the Secretary of the Military Department concerned is allowed to increase, decrease, or abolish SDAP for any skill or assignment at any time. Restrictions and limitations are established through Service regulations.

Per paragraph 2.3.3, SDAP should be for a specified period, not continuous. Back-to-back SDAP assignments are authorized as long as the Marine continues to meet the Service regulations and perform duties in the SDAP-designated assignment.

### Important Discontinuance of New Subchapter I Agreements

Per paragraph 2.2.3, awarding new agreements for assignment and special duty pays under Title 37 U.S.C. Chapter 5, Subchapter I, was discontinued after 1 October 2017. Existing agreements under Subchapter I remain in effect through their agreed-upon termination dates. New SDAP agreements are processed under Subchapter II authority.

### Written Agreement Requirements

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 3.2.

- Discretionary for monthly payments. The Secretary of the Military Department concerned is allowed to require a Marine to enter into a written agreement to qualify for monthly SDAP. If used, the agreement specifies the period and the monthly rate.
- Required for installment or lump sum payments. A written agreement is mandatory for any installment or lump sum SDAP payment. The agreement specifies the period, the amount of each installment or lump sum, and the repayment policy.

The Marine Corps SDAP for designated billets typically pays monthly without a separate written agreement. The orders to the SDAP-designated billet are the controlling documentation.

### Effect of SDAP on Other Computations

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 4.1, SDAP is NOT used in the computation for.

- Enlistment bonus (the bonus is calculated on basic pay, not SDAP)
- Severance pay
- Separation pay
- Cash settlement of accrued leave (terminal leave sell-back)

This means SDAP boosts your monthly take-home but does not lift the dollar amount of bonuses, separation pay, or terminal leave sell-back.

### Tax Treatment

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 4.2.

- SDAP IS subject to federal income tax withholding.
- SDAP is NOT subject to FICA (Social Security and Medicare) withholding.

The FICA exception is unique among regular pay items. SDAP shows on the LES with federal income tax withheld at the regular rate but no FICA deduction.

### Repayment

Per Chapter 8 paragraph 2.5, a Marine who receives SDAP under Title 37 U.S.C. section 352 and fails to fulfill the eligibility requirements is subject to the repayment provisions of Title 37 U.S.C. section 373. The Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site covers the recoupment framework in detail.

### Verifying SDAP on the LES

Pull the LES through MyPay each payday and verify.

- The SDAP line item shows the monthly rate prescribed in MCO 7220.12R for your designated billet.
- The rate matches your assignment orders (e.g., a Drill Instructor SDAP rate matches your DI orders to the Recruit Depot).
- Federal income tax is withheld on the SDAP amount.
- FICA is not withheld on the SDAP amount.

If SDAP is missing, the rate is wrong, or the entry stops before the assignment ends, work through your S-1 to surface the correction.

## Hazardous Duty Incentive Pay (HDIP) - Volume 7A Chapter 24

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 24 (June 2025), DoDI 1340.09, and Title 37 U.S.C., HDIP recognizes Marines performing hazardous duties beyond the routine. This chapter covers all hazardous duty incentive pays except flying duty (covered separately).

### General Eligibility

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 2.1, to receive HDIP a Marine must.

- Be entitled to basic pay under Title 37 U.S.C. section 204 or 206.
- Be serving under competent orders to perform the hazardous duty.
- Be in training or have completed qualifying training and meet the eligibility criteria for the duty.

### Multiple HDIPs Allowed

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 2.4, a Marine performing multiple hazardous duties is allowed up to three HDIP payments per month. Multiple HDIPs require orders directing the Marine to perform the multiple hazardous duties as part of the unit mission.

Aviation Incentive Pay (AvIP) recipients (officers) and Critical Skill Incentive Pay (CSIP) recipients (enlisted) are not eligible for flying-duty HDIP. Beyond flying-duty HDIP, both AvIP/CSIP recipients are allowed to receive two additional HDIPs under independent eligibility.

### Injury or Incapacity Continuation

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 2.5, a Marine qualified for HDIP who is temporarily unable to perform the hazardous duty due to injury or illness not from the Marine's own misconduct continues to receive HDIP for up to 6 months. The Secretary concerned (or designee) is allowed to extend payments up to an additional 6 months on a case-by-case basis.

### Parachute Duty (Jump Pay)

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 3.0.

- Eligibility per paragraph 3.1. Designated parachutists (including those undergoing training for designation), required by competent orders to engage in parachute jumping from an aircraft in aerial flight, meeting performance requirements per DoDI 1340.09 paragraph 3.4.d.
- Static line parachute jumping rate. $150 per month per paragraph 3.3.1.
- Military free fall (HALO/HAHO) parachute rate. $225 per month per paragraph 3.3.2.
- Restriction per paragraph 2.4.2. Only one type of parachute duty payment (regular or military free fall) authorized per qualifying period. When a Marine qualifies for both, the higher rate is paid.
- Disqualifying jumps per paragraph 3.2. Jumps performed while on leave, PCS, TDY/TAD not requiring parachute duty as essential, or RC ADT not requiring parachute duty.

### Demolition Duty

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 5.0.

- Eligibility per paragraph 5.1. Marine entitled to basic pay required by competent orders to perform demolition of explosives as a primary duty (or training for that duty), per Service regulations.
- Qualifying activities per paragraph 5.1.2. Demolition of objects/obstacles, recovery and disarming of failed explosives, demolition training (student or instructor) with live explosives, proficiency training with live explosives, experimental development with live explosives.
- Rate per paragraph 5.2. $150 per month.
- Common Marine Corps applicability. EOD (MOS 2336 enlisted, MOS 2305 officer) and demolition-qualified Marines in qualifying billets.

### Flight Deck Duty (FDHDIP)

Per Chapter 24 paragraph 4.0. The Secretary concerned is allowed to offer Flight Deck HDIP to Marines performing flight deck hazardous duty during flight operations on an aircraft carrier or other designated platform. Marine Corps applicability is limited to Marines on flight deck assignments aboard naval vessels.

### Other HDIP Categories

Chapter 24 also covers experimental stress duty (paragraph 6.0), toxic fuels duty (paragraph 7.0), toxic pesticides duty (paragraph 8.0), dangerous viruses or bacteria lab duty (paragraph 9.0), and chemical munitions duty (paragraph 10.0). These categories rarely apply to general Marine billets but populate for Marines in specialized research, EOD, or chemical-defense assignments.

## Diving Duty Pay - Volume 7A Chapter 11

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 11 (July 2025), diving duty HDIP recognizes Marines performing diving duty as a primary or secondary duty.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.2, a Marine entitled to basic pay is eligible for diving HDIP for periods during which they are.

- Assigned by competent orders to the duty of diving.
- Required to maintain proficiency as a diver by frequent and regular dives.
- Either performing diving duty in an assignment where diving is a primary duty, OR meeting proficiency requirements while serving in an assignment that includes diving other than as a primary duty.

### Marine Corps Qualification

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.4.2, Marines must be designated divers, assigned to diving duty under competent orders, and maintain qualifications for diving. Common Marine Corps qualifying communities include combatant divers (Reconnaissance, Force Recon), combat engineers in dive billets, and EOD-trained divers.

### Maximum Rate

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.5.1, diving duty HDIP is paid at a rate of not more than $240 per month. Specific rates vary by qualification level and Service-specific tables (Tables 11-2 through 11-9 in Chapter 11). The DTMO publishes current rates at the Diving Duty Pay Rates table on DFAS.MIL.

### Multiple HDIP Restriction

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.7.1, a Marine receiving diving HDIP is not allowed to receive more than two other HDIP payments in the same month (the three-HDIP-per-month total cap applies).

### Lapsed Qualification

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 2.7.2, no Marine is entitled to diving HDIP after diving qualifications have lapsed. Upon requalification, no payments are made for the period of the lapsed qualification.

### Master Diver Skill Incentive Pay (MDSIP)

Per Chapter 11 paragraph 3.0, Master Diver SIP is a separate incentive for senior diving billets. Specific eligibility and rates are in paragraph 3.0.

## Career Sea Pay (CSP) and CSP-Premium - Volume 7A Chapter 18

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 18 (May 2024), Career Sea Pay (CSP) recognizes the greater-than-normal rigors of sea duty assignment.

### CSP Eligibility

Per Chapter 18 paragraph 2.1, a Marine entitled to basic pay is entitled to CSP while serving on sea duty under regulations.

Sea duty per Chapter 18 paragraph 2.2.3 means duty performed under orders.

- While permanently assigned for duty to a ship, ship-based staff, or ship-based aviation unit and serving on a ship with a primary mission accomplished underway (paragraph 2.2.3.1).
- While temporarily assigned to a ship, ship-based staff, or ship-based aviation unit and serving on a ship with a primary mission accomplished underway (paragraph 2.2.3.2).
- While permanently or temporarily assigned for duty to a ship or ship-based staff and serving on a ship with a primary mission accomplished in port, only during periods the ship is away from homeport (more than 50 miles) (paragraph 2.2.3.3).
- While serving as off-crew of a two-crewed vessel (paragraph 2.2.3.4).

### CSP Rate Determination

Per Chapter 18 paragraph 3.1, CSP rates depend on Service, pay grade, and total Cumulative Sea Duty (CSD). All Marines in pay grades E-1 through O-6 are eligible. The DTMO publishes current rates.

CSD is the total time a Marine has been assigned to qualifying sea duty during the service career, regardless of pay grade.

### CSP-Premium (CSP-P) for 36+ Consecutive Months

Per Chapter 18 paragraph 2.2.2, a Marine entitled to CSP who has served 36 consecutive months of sea duty is also entitled to CSP-Premium for the 37th and each subsequent consecutive month of sea duty.

CSP-P is in addition to CSP. For some pay grades, CSP-P is included in the CSP rate tables and is not a separate item. When payable as a separate item, CSP-P accrues from the first day following completion of the 36th month of consecutive sea duty.

#### CSP-P as a Separate Item for Marines

Per Chapter 18 paragraph 3.2.1, the following Marine categories qualify for CSP-P as a separate item.

- All officers in pay grades O-1 through O-6.
- All warrant officers.
- All enlisted in pay grades E-1 through E-4.
- All enlisted in pay grades E-5 through E-9 with not more than 8 years of CSD.

For senior enlisted Marines (E-5 to E-9 with more than 8 years of CSD), CSP-P is built into the CSP rate table.

## Hardship Duty Pay (HDP) - Volume 7A Chapter 17

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 17 (December 2024), Hardship Duty Pay covers four distinct hardship situations Marines encounter.

### HDP - Location (HDP-L)

Per Chapter 17 paragraph 2.1.

- Purpose. Recognizes service at locations where living conditions are substantially below those normally found within the continental United States.
- Eligibility. All Marines permanently assigned or serving in TAD/TDY, deployed, or attached status for over 30 consecutive days in a designated area receive HDP-L. Marines on permanent reassignment qualify from the day of arrival at the new station.
- Rates. Vary by location. ASD M&RA promulgates locations and amounts at the rates shown in Chapter 17 Table 17-1. The DTMO publishes the current HDP-L list.
- Restriction. Marines performing duty temporarily in a designated area are NOT eligible for HDP-L during the first 30 days. On the 31st day, HDP-L is payable retroactive to the date the Marine reported.

### HDP - Mission (HDP-M)

Per Chapter 17 paragraph 2.2.

- Purpose. Recognizes permanent or temporary assignment when conditions are particularly arduous or require duties outside normal military operations.
- Eligibility. Established by ASD M&RA. Common HDP-M missions include duty with the Defense POW/MIA Accounting Agency (formerly DPMO/JTF-FA/CIL-Hawaii) on remains recovery in remote isolated areas (Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, North Korea, etc.).
- Rate. $150 per month.
- Payment. Full monthly rate without proration for any month during any part of which the Marine performs the qualifying mission.

### HDP - Tempo (HDP-T)

Per Chapter 17 paragraph 2.3.

- Purpose. Recognizes extended or excessive amounts of time outside the Marine's PDS.
- Eligibility. Service Secretaries establish criteria considering retention, quality of life, family separation.
- Maximum rate. $500 per month.
- Proration. Applied for Marines who do not satisfy eligibility for an entire month.

### HDP - Restriction of Movement (HDP-ROM)

Per Chapter 17 paragraph 2.4.

- Purpose. For Marines ordered by command to restriction of movement for self-monitoring due to exposure or suspected exposure to COVID-19.
- Eligibility. Effective 13 March 2020. Specific conditions in paragraph 2.4.2.

## Foreign Language Proficiency Bonus (FLPB) - Volume 7A Chapter 19

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 19 (February 2025) and Title 37 U.S.C. section 353(b), FLPB compensates Marines for maintaining certified proficiency in a critical foreign language or dialect.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 19 paragraph 2.1, a Marine is eligible for FLPB if all of these apply.

- Entitled to basic pay (Title 37 U.S.C. section 204) or qualifying RC compensation (section 206).
- Determined to have and is maintaining certified foreign language or dialect proficiency in a skill the Service Secretary designates as critical.
- Executes a written agreement with the Secretary specifying the FLPB amount, period, certification requirements, and repayment provision.

### Critical Language Skill Definition

Per Chapter 19 paragraph 2.1.5, a critical language skill is.

- Required by a Service language-related career field or military specialty designated by the Secretary, where primary duties require an Interagency Language Roundtable (ILR) skill level 2+ or higher (ILR skill level 2 and higher for special operations forces personnel).
- A foreign language on the DoD or Service Strategic Language List, or any foreign language for which the Service has language-designated requirements.

### Maximum Amount

Per Chapter 19 paragraph 2.5.1, FLPB is not allowed to exceed $12,000 per 1-year certification period. The Service Secretary is allowed to pay in monthly installments (not exceeding $1,000 per month aggregate) or as a lump sum during the certification period.

### Recertification

Annual DLPT (Defense Language Proficiency Test) recertification is required to maintain FLPB. Per Chapter 19 paragraph 2.6, the Service Secretary is allowed to waive certification requirements for Marines on contingency operation duty who are unable to schedule or complete the certification test, with a 180-day grace period after returning to recertify.

### RC Members

Per Chapter 19 paragraph 2.2, RC Marines must earn a minimum of 50 retirement points in each full anniversary year for that year to count toward verification of total qualifying service. Partial qualifying years prorate the FLPB at 2 percent per retirement point less than 50.

## Aviation Incentive Pays (AvIP, CSIP, Flying HDIP) - Volume 7A Chapter 22

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 22 (March 2024), DoDI 7730.67, and Title 37 U.S.C. sections 334(a), 351(a)(2), and 353, three pays cover flying duty.

### Flying Duty HDIP

Per Chapter 22 paragraph 2.0, the Secretary concerned is allowed to pay HDIP to Marines required by competent orders to participate in frequent and regular aerial flights as an aircrew member, or to non-aircrew members who otherwise meet the requirements.

- Eligibility per paragraph 2.1. Required by competent orders to participate frequently and regularly in sustained, powered, controlled aerial flights. Generally complete 4 hours per month (2 hours for RC) of aerial flights. Excess hours credit forward for up to 5 subsequent consecutive months. A 3-month grace period allows retroactive crediting of future hours to earlier monthly requirements.
- Restriction. Officers entitled to AvIP and enlisted Marines entitled to CSIP are NOT entitled to flying-duty HDIP for the same period.

### Aviation Incentive Pay (AvIP)

Per Chapter 22 paragraph 3.0 and Title 37 U.S.C. section 334(a). Monthly pay for officers performing qualified flying duty.

- Marine Corps officer AvIP rates published in Chapter 22 Table 22-10.
- Rates depend on years of aviation service and aviation career stage.
- Officer must be qualified as a pilot, naval flight officer, combat systems officer, or qualifying aviation specialty.

### Critical Skill Incentive Pay (CSIP)

Per Chapter 22 paragraph 5.0 and Title 37 U.S.C. section 353(a). Monthly pay for enlisted Marines in critical aviation skills.

- Currently authorized for Air Force enlisted flyers per Chapter 22 Table 22-11. Marine Corps enlisted aviation skill applicability depends on Service-specific designation.

## Aviation Bonus (AvB) - Volume 7A Chapter 20

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 20 (December 2025), DoDI 7730.67, and Title 37 U.S.C. section 334.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 20 paragraph 3.2, an officer must.

- Be eligible for AvIP at the time of incurring the initial AvB contractual obligation.
- Be in pay grade O-5 or below at the time of initial obligation.
- Serve in an aviation specialty designated as critical by the Secretary concerned.
- Have completed (or be within one year of completing) any service obligation incurred for undergraduate aviator training. Per Title 10 U.S.C. section 653, the minimum service obligation for fixed-wing jet pilots is 8 years. Other aircraft pilots and combat systems officers / naval flight officers are 6 years.
- Execute a written agreement to remain on AD or in active status in the SELRES for at least 1 year.

### Maximum Amounts

Per Chapter 20 paragraph 4.2.

- $35,000 per year for active obligated service of RC officers performing qualified flying duty.
- $35,000 per year for RC officers performing qualified UAS operator duty.
- $18,000 per year for all other RC officers performing qualified duty.

The ASD M&RA is allowed to update AvB amounts.

### Recoupment

Per the chapter and the Pay Problems and Fixes page recoupment framework, an officer who fails to complete the obligated service repays the unearned portion under Title 37 U.S.C. section 373 and Volume 7A Chapter 2.

## Continuation Pay (CP) - Volume 7A Chapter 66

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 66 (February 2024), Title 37 U.S.C. section 356, and Public Law 114-92 as amended, Continuation Pay is a one-time, mid-career bonus payment for Service Members covered by the Blended Retirement System (BRS).

### CP Is BRS-Only

CP is only payable to Marines under the BRS. Marines under the legacy High-Three retirement system are not eligible.

### Eligibility (Active Component)

Per Chapter 66 paragraph 2.2.1, an Active Component Marine is eligible for CP if all of these apply.

- Covered under the BRS.
- Has completed not less than 8 and not more than 12 Years of Service (YOS) computed from the PEBD.
- Eligible to enter into an agreement to serve at least 3 additional years in the AC.

### Eligibility (Reserve Component)

Per paragraph 2.2.2, an RC Marine is eligible if BRS-covered, has 8-12 YOS, is in SELRES or Ready Reserve in a status eligible for basic pay or inactive duty pay, and is eligible to commit to at least 3 additional years in the SELRES.

### Amount

Per Chapter 66 paragraph 2.3.

- AC member. Not less than 2.5 times and not more than 13 times the monthly basic pay of an AC member based on the current pay grade and YOS.
- RC member. Not less than 0.5 times and not more than 6 times the monthly basic pay of a member of the same grade and YOS on AD. RC members performing Active Guard and Reserve service are paid at the AC rate subject to AD service agreement.

### Obligated Service

Per Chapter 66 paragraph 2.4, the Marine serves not less than 3 years of additional service. Length is determined by the Secretary concerned. Obligated service runs concurrently with other service obligations unless other obligations specifically preclude concurrent obligation.

### Payment Method

Per Chapter 66 paragraph 2.5, the Marine is allowed to elect a single lump sum or up to four equal annual installment payments over 4 consecutive years.

### YOS Tier for Calculation

Per Chapter 66 paragraph 2.6.3.

- 8 to less than 10 YOS pays at the over-8-YOS rate.
- 10 to less than 12 YOS pays at the over-10-YOS rate.
- Exactly 12 YOS pays at the over-12-YOS rate.

## High-Deployment Allowance (HDA) - Volume 7A Chapter 65

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 65 (September 2024) and Title 10 U.S.C. section 991.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 65 paragraph 2.1, the Secretary concerned is allowed to pay a high-deployment allowance to a Marine for each month during which the Marine is deployed AND at any time during that month.

- Has been deployed for 191 or more consecutive days, OR
- Has been deployed 401 or more days out of the preceding 730 days.

For Reserve Marines on active duty, additional eligibility paths apply per paragraph 2.1.2 (specific contingency-operation activations).

### Maximum Amount

Per Chapter 65 paragraph 2.2.1, the rate is determined by the Secretary, not to exceed $1,000 per month.

### Tax Treatment

Per Chapter 65 paragraph 2.2.2, HDA is generally taxable income. If earned while serving within a combat zone, the allowance is eligible for the Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (covered on the Deployment Pays page).

### Deployment Definition

Per Chapter 65 paragraph 2.3.1, a Marine is "deployed" on any day on which, pursuant to orders, the Marine performs service for an operation at a location or under circumstances that make it impossible to spend off-duty time in the housing in which they reside when on garrison duty at the PDS or homeport.

A deployment begins when a unit, detachment, or individual leaves a homeport, station, or base (or enroute training location) to meet a SECDEF-approved operation. A deployment ends when most of the unit returns. Movement for training, readiness, or exercise (without operational deployment status) is not counted.

## Assignment Incentive Pay (AIP) - Volume 7A Chapter 15

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 15 (April 2024), AIP is a special pay for assignments at locations or in roles designated by Service Secretaries.

### General Framework

Per Chapter 15 paragraph 2.0, the Secretary concerned is allowed to pay AIP based on Service-specific needs. Each Service has its own AIP programs covering specific assignments, locations, or roles.

### Marine Corps AIP Programs

Per Chapter 15 paragraph 6.0, the Marine Corps AIP programs are documented separately within Marine Corps directives. Marines in qualifying billets receive AIP at the rate established for the specific program. Coordinate with the unit S-1 to verify eligibility for any specific assignment.

### Cross-Service AIP Examples

Other Services have detailed AIP programs covered in Chapter 15 paragraphs 3.0 (Army), 4.0 (Navy), and 5.0 (Air Force / Space Force). These cover specific high-demand billets (cyber, missile defense, drill instructor, joint special operations, recruiting, etc.). Marines in joint or cross-service assignments are sometimes eligible for the gaining Service's AIP under specific conditions.

### AIP in Lieu of PDMRA

Per Chapter 15 paragraph 7.0, AIP is sometimes offered in lieu of Post-Deployment Mobilization Respite Absence (PDMRA) for qualifying deployments. Specific eligibility tied to deployment-to-dwell ratios and Public Law 112-120 / 112-239 frameworks.

## Incentive for Qualified Members Extending OCONUS Tours - Volume 7A Chapter 14

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 14 (July 2024), this incentive applies to Marines who voluntarily extend OCONUS tours.

### Eligibility

Per Chapter 14 paragraph 2.1, a Marine is eligible if all of these apply.

- Entitled to basic pay.
- Has a specialty designated by the Secretary for this entitlement.
- Has completed a tour of duty at a designated OCONUS location.
- Executes an agreement to extend the tour for at least 1 year.

### Amount Options

Per Chapter 14 paragraph 2.2, when the Secretary accepts the extension agreement, the Marine becomes entitled to ONE of the following.

- Special pay in monthly installments not to exceed $80 per month, OR
- Annual bonus not to exceed $2,000 per year (lump sum or monthly installments).

### Special Rest and Recuperative (SR&R) Absence Alternative

Per Chapter 14 paragraph 3.0, in lieu of special pay or bonus, the Marine is allowed to elect.

- Up to 30 days of SR&R absence, OR
- Up to 15 days of SR&R plus round-trip transportation to the nearest port in the 48 contiguous States (for tours of 12 months or less), OR
- Up to 20 days of SR&R plus round-trip transportation (for tours longer than 12 months).

### Recoupment

Per paragraph 2.4, a Marine who enters the agreement and receives a bonus payment but does not complete the obligated extension is subject to recoupment under Volume 7A Chapter 2.

## Health Professions Officer (HPO) Special Pays

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 5, Health Professions Officer special pays cover medical, dental, and other health-care officer specialties (medical corps, dental corps, biomedical sciences, nurse corps, medical service corps).

The Marine Corps does not maintain a separate medical corps (Marines receive medical care from Navy medical personnel). Marine Corps applicability is limited to specific cross-service contexts. Coordinate with the Navy medical command structure for medical-officer pay questions tied to Navy personnel supporting Marine units.

## CONUS COLA - Volume 7A Chapter 67

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 67 (December 2024), the Cost of Living Allowance for the Continental United States compensates Marines stationed in designated high-cost CONUS areas.

### What CONUS COLA Is

CONUS COLA is a recurring monthly allowance that adjusts compensation upward when the Marine's PDS is in a designated high-cost area. The allowance is taxable income.

### High-Cost Area Designation

Per Chapter 67 paragraph 2.1, an area is a high-cost area for a fiscal year if the Uniformed Services cost of living for that area for the base period (the 12-month period ending on June 30 of the year in which the fiscal year begins) exceeds the average cost of living in CONUS for that base period by at least the threshold percentage. The Secretary of Defense establishes the threshold percentage, not less than 5 percent. The administering Secretaries are allowed to prescribe a higher threshold.

The DTMO publishes the current CONUS COLA areas and rates at https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Cost-of-Living-Allowance-CONUS/.

### With-Dependent vs. Without-Dependent Rates

Per Chapter 67 paragraphs 2.2 and 2.3.

- With-dependent rate. The Marine is authorized BAH at the with-dependent rate (or would be if Government quarters were not occupied). Excludes Marines authorized with-dependent BAH solely based on paying child support. A spouse who is also a Service Member on active duty is not a dependent for CONUS COLA.
- Without-dependent rate. The Marine is authorized BAH at the without-dependent rate, would be authorized without-dependent BAH if Government quarters were not occupied, or is authorized with-dependent BAH solely based on paying child support.

### Concurrent CONUS COLA and OCONUS COLA

Per Chapter 67 paragraph 3.3, concurrent payment of CONUS COLA and OCONUS COLA is allowed in narrow circumstances (e.g., a Marine PCSing between a CONUS high-cost area and an OCONUS high-cost area where dependent transitions span both locations).

### Eligibility During Special Circumstances

Per Chapter 67 paragraphs 5.0 and 6.0.

- Missing status, confinement, prolonged hospitalization, evacuated PDS. Specific rules apply per paragraphs 5.1 through 5.4.
- Reserve Component Marines on active duty. CONUS COLA continues during the active duty period under specific orders. Modified or amended orders adjust eligibility per paragraph 6.2.

## OCONUS COLA - Volume 7A Chapter 68

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A Chapter 68 (January 2025), Title 37 U.S.C. section 405, the OCONUS Cost of Living Allowance is a non-taxable allowance offsetting the higher prices of goods and services (excluding housing) in foreign countries, U.S. territories, Alaska, and Hawaii.

### What OCONUS COLA Is

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 1.1, OCONUS COLA equalizes purchasing power so a Marine is allowed to purchase the same level of goods and services OCONUS as in CONUS. Unlike CONUS COLA (which is taxable), OCONUS COLA is non-taxable.

### COLA Index

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 2.1.4, the OCONUS COLA index represents how much more expensive it is to purchase goods and services OCONUS compared to the same level of goods and services available in an average CONUS location. An index of 110 indicates the OCONUS area is 10 percent more expensive. An index of 100 indicates approximately equal cost (no COLA paid).

The DTMO publishes OCONUS COLA rates by location and pay grade at https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Overseas-Cost-of-Living-Allowance/.

### With-Dependent vs. Without-Dependent Rates

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 2.1.5, a Marine is with-dependent for OCONUS COLA if any of these apply.

- Authorized to have a dependent reside at or in the vicinity of the PDS OCONUS and the dependent resides there.
- Married to another Service Member on the effective date of PCS orders, where one Service Member later separates and remains near the active-duty Marine's former PDS.
- Joined by a dependent or acquires a dependent while serving OCONUS, with the dependent meeting specific command-sponsorship or residence criteria per paragraph 2.1.5.3.

The without-dependent rate applies in cases meeting paragraph 2.1.6 (no dependents, dependent not at PDS, dependent not command-sponsored, no legal custody / control of dependent).

### Command Sponsorship Requirement

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 2.2.3, a dependent must be command-sponsored for the Marine to receive OCONUS COLA based on the dependent's presence (with narrow exceptions noted in the chapter and the JTR).

### Unaccompanied Tour and Sea Duty

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 2.2.2, a Marine on an unaccompanied tour or "unusually arduous sea duty" with a dependent at the old PDS OCONUS or at an approved designated place OCONUS is eligible for OCONUS COLA at the with-dependent rate based on the dependent's location.

### Dual Military

Per Chapter 68 paragraph 2.2.1, when a Marine is married to another Service Member on active duty, the spouse on active duty is not considered a dependent for OCONUS COLA purposes.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

I am a Marine Corps officer thinking about taking a $50,000 retention bonus for 2 more years. How does the math work?

Per Volume 7A Chapter 3 paragraph 5.4, the retention bonus is up to $25,000 per year of obligated service for Regular Component officers. A 2-year extension at $25,000 per year produces a $50,000 maximum bonus. The bonus is paid per the Secretary concerned (typically lump sum at signing or annual installments). You earn the bonus over the 2-year period. If you fail to complete the obligation, the unearned portion is recouped per Chapter 2.

I am a Marine officer with 17 years of active commissioned service. Am I eligible for a retention bonus?

Per Chapter 3 paragraph 5.3.1, no. An officer with between 16 and 19 years of active commissioned service at the start of the additional obligated service is not eligible. The gate exists because of the proximity to retirement eligibility.

I received a $40,000 accession bonus when I commissioned. Is a retention bonus still available later?

Yes, subject to the cap. Per paragraph 2.3.4, the career cap on combined officer bonuses under DoDI 1304.34 is $300,000 (or $150,000 for non-AGR RC officers). A $40,000 accession bonus and subsequent retention bonuses are allowed as long as the cumulative total stays under the cap.

I am transferring from the Marine Corps Reserve to the Regular Marine Corps. Am I eligible for a transfer bonus?

The intra-service transfer bonus framework per paragraph 6.1 applies to transfers between the Regular Component and Ready Reserve of the same service. Verify with your S-1 and the Marine Corps Manpower whether your specific transfer qualifies under the current Marine Corps policy and the DFAS-published Duration of Authority table.

I am the executive officer of a Marine battalion. Do I get command pay?

No. Per Chapter 3 paragraph 8.4.2, command pay is not authorized for more than one officer per unit (except during dates of assumption and relief from command). The battalion commander receives command pay (if the billet is designated and meets the unit's eligibility criteria). The XO does not.

I am being involuntarily moved from my critical-skill MOS to another MOS by the Marine Corps. What happens to my retention bonus?

Per Volume 7A Chapter 2 paragraph 3.3.4 and Table 2-1 Rules 6 and 7, when the Marine Corps directs the MOS or assignment change (versus the Marine requesting it), repayment of the unearned portion is not sought. Document the directed nature of the change with orders or written direction. The Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site covers the recoupment framework in detail.

What is the difference between a bonus and a recurring special pay?

A bonus is paid against a written agreement specifying obligated service. A recurring special pay is paid monthly or annually for as long as the qualifying condition (qualification, billet, location, mission) holds. Recoupment applies only to bonuses, not to recurring special pays. If a Marine loses a qualification (e.g., parachutist status lapses), the recurring special pay (jump pay) stops but no recoupment of prior payments is sought.

I qualified for jump pay 6 months ago but it has not appeared on my LES. What do I do?

Document the qualification with the school certificate and assignment to a jump-status billet. Submit through your reporting unit (I&I S-1 with direct UD/MIPS access, or a battalion S-1 routing through their PAC). For the months missed, submit a back-pay request through the same channel. The Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site covers the missing-special-pay recovery flow.

- Leaders identify Marines whose qualifications should produce special pays, ensure documentation routes through the reporting unit on time, and coach Marines on bonus contract terms before signing.

Special pays add up. The four officer bonuses cap at $300,000 over a career. The recurring special pays add hundreds to a few thousand per month for qualifying Marines. Read every bonus contract before signing, verify qualifying special pays appear on the LES every month, and document everything in case the recoupment framework comes into play later.
