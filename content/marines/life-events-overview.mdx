## Start Here If You Are New to Life Events

LIFE EVENTS are dependent add and loss events that affect your DEERS enrollment, BAH, ID cards, and benefits. MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" /> (Marine Corps Dependency Determination and Support Program) is the governing order. NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> (Dependency Application/Record of Emergency Data) is the central document. You have 10 WORKING DAYS from the life event to submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with supporting documents to S-1. RAPIDS site for DEERS enrollment and ID card issuance within 30 days. Dependency category carries audit weight ($63M dependency audit category in IPAC inspections). Late submission delays BAH, ID cards, and TRICARE access. Common life events include marriage, birth, adoption, divorce, death of dependent, custody changes, and secondary dependency (DD Form 137<Citation source="DD Form 137" />) for parents, parents-in-law, stepparents, in-loco parentis, wards, students 21-22, and incapacitated adult children. Per MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" />.

## What MCO 1751.3 Covers

MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" /> establishes the Marine Corps Dependency Determination and Support Program. It governs.

- Dependent eligibility determinations
- Documentation requirements for dependent add and loss
- Submission timelines and processes
- BAH, FSA, and other entitlement triggers
- Annual dependency validation
- Audit and inspection requirements

The order works alongside DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 26 (Pay and Allowances), DoDI 1000.13<Citation source="DoDI 1000.13" /> (DoD Identification Cards), and MCO 1740.13<Citation source="MCO 1740.13" /> (Family Care Plan).

## The NAVMC 10922 - Central Document

NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> (Dependency Application/Record of Emergency Data) is the document used for nearly every life event.

### What NAVMC 10922 Captures

- Marine identifying information
- Current dependent roster
- Add or loss event details
- Supporting document attachments
- Member signature certifying accuracy
- S-1 endorsement section

### When NAVMC 10922 Is Required

- Marriage (spouse add)
- Birth or adoption (child add)
- Divorce (spouse loss)
- Death of dependent (loss)
- Custody change (add or loss)
- Secondary dependency add (DD 137 attached)
- Address change for dependents
- Annual dependency recertification

### Where NAVMC 10922 Goes

- S-1 IPAC for review and submission
- DEERS enrollment via RAPIDS site (Marine and dependent appearance often required)
- MCTFS update for BAH and entitlement adjustment
- OMPF for permanent record per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" />

## The 10 Working Day Rule

MCO 1751.3<Citation source="MCO 1751.3" /> requires submission of NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with supporting documents within 10 WORKING DAYS of the life event.

### Why 10 Working Days

- BAH adjustment timing depends on submission date
- DEERS enrollment delay impacts TRICARE access
- ID card issuance requires DEERS update first
- Dependency category audits track timeliness
- Late submission creates pay debts

### What Counts As Day Zero

- Marriage. Date on the marriage certificate.
- Birth. Date on the birth certificate.
- Adoption. Date on the adoption decree.
- Divorce. Date on the final divorce decree.
- Death. Date on the death certificate.
- Custody change. Date on the court order.

### What Counts As A Working Day

Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays. Weekends and federal holidays do NOT count.

### Consequences of Late Submission

- BAH delayed start or unrecouped overpayment
- Dependent ID card delayed
- DEERS enrollment delayed, blocking TRICARE
- SGLI beneficiary not updated
- Family Separation Allowance gap during deployment
- Audit findings against the unit and IPAC

## DEERS, MCTFS, and OMPF Integration

Three systems must align after every life event.

### DEERS (Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System)

The authoritative system for dependent eligibility. Updated at RAPIDS sites.

- Dependent enrollment after document review
- ID card issuance after enrollment
- TRICARE eligibility activation
- 30-day expectation for enrollment after event

### MCTFS (Marine Corps Total Force System)

The pay and personnel system. Updated by IPAC after S-1 submission.

- BAH start, stop, or adjustment
- FSA eligibility
- Dependency category updates
- LES reflection within one to two pay cycles

### OMPF (Official Military Personnel File)

The permanent record. Updated via OMPF document submission.

- NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> retained as historical record
- Supporting documents archived
- Audit trail for future verification
- Per MARADMIN 015/20<Citation source="MARADMIN 015/20" /> submission process

If any one of these three lags, downstream problems follow. Career Counselor and S-1 IPAC track all three.

## The S-1 IPAC Role

S-1 (battalion administrative section) and IPAC (Installation Personnel Administrative Center) work together.

### S-1 Battalion Level

- Initial document review for completeness
- NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> endorsement
- Marine counseling on supporting documents
- Routing to IPAC

### IPAC Installation Level

- DEERS enrollment processing
- MCTFS update
- OMPF document submission
- Pay adjustment validation

For most Marines, S-1 is the first stop. IPAC handles the back-end processing.

## Audit Weight

Dependency category is one of the highest-weight audit categories in IPAC inspections.

### What Auditors Check

- Timeliness of NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> submission (within 10 working days)
- Completeness of supporting documents
- DEERS-MCTFS alignment
- OMPF document presence
- Annual recertification compliance

### Why It Matters

- Findings against the unit and IPAC
- Pay debts identified during audit
- Career impact for S-1 personnel with poor case files
- Investigation triggers for repeated failures

The dependency category audit weight is reported at $63M annually across the Marine Corps. Errors at the unit level aggregate up.

## Common Failure Points

### Late Submission

Marines wait until they have time, miss the 10-day window, and lose entitlement timing.

Fix. Schedule S-1 appointment the same day you receive the event document.

### Missing Certified Copies

Originals or photocopies are not enough. CERTIFIED copies (raised seal or court-issued) are required.

Fix. Order extra certified copies at the time of the event (marriage license, court order, etc.).

### DEERS-MCTFS Misalignment

DEERS enrollment completes but MCTFS does not update, or vice versa. BAH or TRICARE breaks.

Fix. Marine confirms via Marine Online and milConnect within two pay cycles. Escalate to S-1 if mismatch persists.

### Failure to Update SGLI

Marriage, divorce, or birth without SGLI beneficiary update creates legal complications upon death.

Fix. Update SGLI beneficiary at every life event via Marine Online.

### OMPF Document Missing

NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> processed but never submitted to OMPF. Audit finding and retention reverification required.

Fix. Marine confirms OMPF document presence via Marine Online OMPF section after each event.
