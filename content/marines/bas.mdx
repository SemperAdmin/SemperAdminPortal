## Start Here If You Are New to BAS

BAS is the second-most-confused pay line on the LES after BAH. Officers draw one rate. Enlisted Marines draw a different (lower) rate. Marines on a meal card pay for the meals through their pay account. Marines in the field pay for meals whether they eat them or not. Marines on sea duty pay similarly. Get the BAS framework right and the LES makes sense. Get it wrong and you stop drawing food allowance you should be receiving.

This page walks you through BAS from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The officer BAS rate vs. the enlisted BAS and BAS II rates
- The continuity rules that keep BAS flowing across reenlistment, leave, and weekends
- Restrictions during basic training, excess leave, AWOL, and court-martial forfeiture
- Government-provided meals and mandatory pay-account collections in field duty, sea duty, EUM, and ESM
- The Family Supplemental Subsistence Allowance (FSSA) for OCONUS Marines under the poverty line
- The new Basic Needs Allowance (BNA) effective May 2025 at 200 percent of the Federal Poverty Guideline
- The common LES errors that produce BAS gaps

## What BAS Is

Per DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD 7000.14-R Volume 7A" /> Chapter 25, the Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS) is the monthly allowance for the cost of food. The rate depends on whether the Marine is an officer or an enlisted member, with a separate higher rate (BAS II) for narrow enlisted situations.

BAS is published in dollar amounts, not as a percentage of basic pay or a locality-based variable. The DTMO publishes BAS rates annually at https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Subsistence/. New rates take effect 1 January each year.

Per Title 26 U.S.C. section 134<Citation source="Title 26 U.S.C. section 134" />, BAS is excluded from federal taxable income. The full BAS amount is take-home, not subject to federal income tax or FICA.

## Why BAS Matters to You

Three reasons BAS carries weight beyond the dollar amount.

- Tax-free monthly allowance. BAS is paid every month and is fully tax-free. The dollar amount is small relative to basic pay, but it adds to disposable income.
- Driven by status changes. BAS starts and stops with status changes (basic training, meal card on/off, AWOL, court-martial, etc.). Errors compound when the status changes do not flow correctly through the LES.
- Field-duty and sea-duty interaction. Marines drawing BAS pay for meals through pay-account collection during field duty, sea duty, EUM, or ESM. Understanding the framework prevents surprise debts on the LES.

## Officer BAS

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.1.1, a single rate of BAS applies to all officers regardless of pay grade. Officer BAS is published in Table 25-1 and updates annually with the BAS rate change.

Officers do not have a BAS II rate. Officers do not transition to BAS II in the situations enlisted Marines do.

## Enlisted BAS

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.1.2, enlisted Marines draw one of two BAS rates.

### Standard Enlisted BAS

The default rate for active-duty enlisted Marines after completion of basic training. Per paragraph 2.1.2.1, this is the monthly standard rate payable unless the Marine qualifies for and is approved for BAS II.

### Enlisted BAS II

Per paragraph 2.1.2.2, BAS II is the monthly rate payable to enlisted Marines on duty at a permanent station, assigned to single (unaccompanied) Government quarters that lack adequate food storage or preparation facilities, when a Government mess is not available, and the Government cannot otherwise make meals available.

The BAS II rate is twice the standard enlisted BAS rate.

BAS II application varies by Service. The Marine Corps applies BAS II under specific MCO conditions. The Navy applies BAS II for vessels in pre-commissioning status under MILPERMAN 7220-182. The Air Force applies BAS II at specific designated locations.

### Continuity of Enlisted BAS

Per paragraph 2.1.3, enlisted BAS continues without interruption in two important cases.

- Reenlistment or recall to active duty at the same station within 24 hours of discharge or retirement (paragraph 2.1.3.1).
- Weekends, holidays, administrative absence, pass, or liberty (not including chargeable leave) (paragraph 2.1.3.2).

This continuity prevents short-status changes from triggering BAS interruption.

## Restrictions on BAS

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.3, Marines are not entitled to BAS in these situations.

- Undergoing Basic Military Training, including initial officer training (Officer Candidate School, Officer Training School), unless the Marine has continuous prior enlisted service. Recruits are subsisted-in-kind during basic training (paragraph 2.3.1.1).
- Excess leave status (paragraph 2.3.1.2).
- AWOL status in excess of 24 hours, unless the absence is excused as unavoidable (paragraph 2.3.1.3).
- Approved educational leave of absence not exceeding 2 years (paragraph 2.3.1.4).
- Marine without dependents training for or participating in Pan American games, Olympic games, or other authorized international amateur sport competitions where the sponsoring agency subsists the Marine (paragraph 2.3.1.5).
- Court-martial sentence imposing forfeiture of pay and allowances (paragraph 2.3.1.6).

Additionally per paragraph 2.3.2, BAS II is not authorized when a Government mess is temporarily closed for less than 14 days, or when the Marine is on leave, TAD/TDY, or hospitalized.

## Government Provided Meals and Pay Account Collections

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.4, a Marine receiving BAS pays for all meals or rations provided by or on behalf of the Government, except in narrow medical-recuperation cases.

This is a personal obligation. Payment options include cash to the Government mess, deduction from travel per diem, or collection from the pay account.

### Mandatory Pay Account Collection

Per paragraph 2.4.3, mandatory pay-account collection applies in six specific situations where it is not feasible to control dining access or collect cash.

- Field duty
- Sea duty
- Members traveling together with limited or no per diem travel
- Accession Pipeline Military Training
- Essential Unit Messing (EUM)
- Essential Station Messing (ESM)

In mandatory collection, the Marine is charged for all meals available, whether eaten or not. Collections are made day-for-actual-day, not on a 30-day month basis.

### When Mandatory Collection Does NOT Apply

Per paragraph 2.4.3.2, mandatory collection does not apply.

- During official leave, PCS status (including travel time and proceed time), military treatment facility care, or TAD/TDY (other than TAD/TDY to another mandatory-collection situation).
- When the Marine missed more than 20 percent of meals on a monthly basis due to assigned duties, as certified by the commander or designee. Collections are suspended or adjusted with a credit.

### Meal Rate Tiers

Two meal rates apply per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.4.4.

- Standard meal rate. The default rate for meals provided by or on behalf of the Government when the Marine is not authorized the discount rate.
- Discount meal rate. Applied to meals where the Marine is in a status that qualifies for the discount.

Pay-account collections in mandatory collection situations apply at 25 percent of the discount meal rate for the first and last day of assignment. The 25-percent reduced charge does not apply to leave periods.

### Combat Zone Medical Care Exception

Per paragraph 2.4.2, a Marine is not required to pay for meals provided while undergoing medical recuperation, therapy, or continuous care at a military treatment facility for an injury, illness, or disease incurred while on active duty.

- In support of Operation Enduring Freedom, Operation Iraqi Freedom, Operation New Dawn, or Operation Freedom's Sentinel.
- In any other operation designated by the Secretary of Defense as a combat operation or in an area designated by SECDEF as a combat zone.

## Specialized Terms

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.5, six specialized terms govern BAS treatment in non-routine situations.

### Essential Station Messing (ESM)

Per paragraph 2.5.1. Messing declared by the installation commander as essential to operate the Government mess efficiently and economically, or necessary for the health and safety of enlisted personnel permanently assigned to single quarters. Marines included in ESM are charged for all meals made available whether eaten or not, except for approved missed meals.

### Essential Unit Messing (EUM)

Per paragraph 2.5.2. Group messing declared by appropriate authority as necessary for operational readiness, military operations, or effective training where Marines are required to eat Government-furnished meals. Marines on EUM are in a travel status entitled to incidental travel reimbursement but not subsistence per diem. EUM applies to organizational units and operational elements, not to individual Marines.

### Field Duty

Per paragraph 2.5.3. Maneuvers, war games, field exercises, or similar operations where the Marine is assigned to a unit subsisted in a dining facility operated by or on behalf of the U.S. Government, or with an organization drawing field rations.

### Sea Duty

Per paragraph 2.5.4. Service performed in a self-propelled vessel with berthing and messing facilities in active status, in commission, or in service. Applies to permanent party and TAD/TDY personnel aboard. All Marines on sea duty (not in an authorized private mess) are charged for all meals made available whether eaten or not.

### Government Furnished Meals

Per paragraph 2.5.5. Meals or rations furnished without charge by a government contractor, foreign government, fellowship, grant, or intern program while the Marine is receiving basic pay. Subsistence provided on behalf of the Government is charged to the Marine unless the Marine is entitled to be subsisted-in-kind.

### Subsisted-in-Kind

Per paragraph 2.5.6. Meals or rations furnished at no charge to Marines not entitled to BAS from a Government Mess or who are subsisted at no charge on behalf of the Government. Recruits in basic training and certain trainees are subsisted-in-kind.

## BAS in Specific Marine Situations

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.2.2, four common Marine pay actions trigger specific BAS treatment.

### Authorized Leave (Including Convalescent Leave and Proceed Time)

Enlisted Marines on authorized leave (including proceed time, authorized delays enroute between duty stations chargeable as leave, and convalescent leave) are entitled to the standard enlisted BAS rate, regardless of the BAS rate authorized at the PDS (per paragraph 2.2.2.2).

### PCS Travel and TAD/TDY

Enlisted Marines performing PCS travel (including TAD/TDY) en route under orders away from their designated post of duty are entitled to the standard enlisted BAS rate, regardless of the BAS type authorized at the previous or subsequent PDS (per paragraph 2.2.2.3).

### BAS II at PDS, Then Hospitalization or Routine TAD/TDY

Enlisted Marines receiving BAS II at the PDS who are hospitalized or performing regular or permissive TAD/TDY (including field duty, sea duty, EUM, or limited per diem travel) revert to standard enlisted BAS for the period of hospitalization or absence (per paragraph 2.2.2.4).

### Days of Leave or PCS Travel

Per paragraph 2.2.2.5, enlisted Marines are entitled to the standard enlisted BAS rate for any day under orders for leave or PCS travel, including the day of commencement and the day of termination of the status.

## Family Supplemental Subsistence Allowance (FSSA)

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 3.0, FSSA is a supplemental allowance designed to bring a Marine's household income to a level that removes the need for benefits under the USDA Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

### Eligibility

Per paragraph 3.2, FSSA is payable to officers or enlisted members who meet ALL of these criteria.

- Serving on active duty and receiving BAS.
- Household income (including military income and any other household income) that would qualify for SNAP based on household size.
- At least one person in the household is a military dependent.
- Application made and certified at a specific payment level.
- After 30 September 2016, serving outside the United States, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, or Guam.

### Important Restriction

Per paragraph 3.2.5, FSSA is now restricted to OCONUS Marines. CONUS Marines who would otherwise qualify based on income do not qualify for FSSA. The CONUS food-insecurity gap is now addressed through the Basic Needs Allowance (BNA) covered below.

### Rate

Per paragraph 3.3, FSSA is a monthly entitlement payable in whole dollar amounts not to exceed $1,100. The amount is calculated to bring household income to 130 percent of the Federal Poverty Guidelines (FPG) but not to exceed the $1,100 cap.

If a Marine is receiving SNAP benefits and the SNAP allotment exceeds the FSSA calculation, the FSSA entitlement equals the lesser of the SNAP allotment value or $1,100.

For periods of less than a full month, FSSA is paid at 1/30 of the monthly amount per eligible day served.

### Non-Pay Status

Per paragraph 3.3, FSSA is not payable during periods in a non-pay status.

## Basic Needs Allowance (BNA)

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 4.0 and Title 37 U.S.C. section 402<Citation source="Title 37 U.S.C. section 402" />b, the Basic Needs Allowance (BNA) is the newer program addressing the gap FSSA does not cover (CONUS food insecurity for Marines with dependents).

### Effective Date and Threshold

Per paragraph 4.2.1, the BNA threshold is set at 200 percent of the Federal Poverty Guideline (FPG) for the Marine's geographical location, effective 12 May 2025. Earlier thresholds (covered in Table 25-3 of the chapter) used different percentages.

### Eligibility

Per paragraph 4.2.2.1, an active-duty Marine with dependents is eligible for BNA if all of these apply.

- Has completed Initial Entry Training (IET).
- Gross Household Income (GHI) during the most recent calendar year did not exceed 200 percent of the FPG (per location and household size).
- Voluntarily applied for BNA and provided required information.
- Not ineligible per paragraphs 4.2.2.2 or 4.2.2.3.

### Ineligibility

Per paragraph 4.2.2.2, a Marine is NOT eligible for BNA if any of these apply.

- GHI exceeded 200 percent of the FPG in the most recent calendar year.
- Has no dependents (BNA is dependent-required).
- Cadet at a Service Academy or other officer training program.
- Has not completed IET.
- Failed to submit a BNA application within 180 days of notification of potential eligibility.
- Elected in writing not to receive the allowance.

### Special Cases

#### Multiple Eligible Members in One Household

Per paragraph 4.2.2.3.1, if a household contains multiple Service Members determined eligible, only one allowance is paid. The household members jointly elect which eligible member receives the allowance.

#### Pay or Allowance Increases

Per paragraph 4.2.2.3.2, if a Marine receiving BNA gets a promotion or other permanent pay increase that pushes the annualized GHI above the threshold, the Marine becomes automatically ineligible. Payment terminates 60 calendar days after the date of ineligibility.

#### Disciplinary Action and Pay Reductions

Per paragraph 4.2.2.3.3, if a Marine's prior-year GHI was reduced due to a fine, forfeiture, or reduction in rank from disciplinary action under the UCMJ, the Marine is not eligible for BNA solely because of the disciplinary-driven reduction. A Marine reinstated to the previous pay grade is allowed to become eligible if otherwise qualifying.

#### Non-Pay Status

Per paragraph 4.2.2.4, a Marine in non-pay status is not eligible for BNA. Eligibility is allowed to resume when the Marine returns to pay status.

#### Household Size Changes via DEERS

Per paragraph 4.2.2.5, if a change in dependents reported in DEERS pushes the GHI above 200 percent of the FPG for the new household size, BNA terminates 60 calendar days after the change. The Marine reports household changes to the BNA certifying official for re-certification.

## Common BAS Errors and Fixes

Per Chapter 25 and the Pay Problems and Fixes page on this site, the five most common BAS errors.

### 1. BAS Stopped After Recruit Graduation Did Not Restart

Cause. Recruits are subsisted-in-kind during basic training (no BAS). After graduation, BAS should start automatically. If it does not, the reporting unit at the first PDS missed the BAS start. Fix. Submit through the S-1 immediately.

### 2. BAS Withheld for Government Quarters with No Mess

Cause. The reporting unit assumed the Marine is in ESM because of single Government quarters assignment, but the quarters lack adequate food preparation facilities and no Government mess is available. The Marine should draw BAS II, not standard BAS with collection. Fix. Coordinate with the unit's housing office and the S-1 to verify the BAS II eligibility per paragraph 2.1.2.2.

### 3. Field Duty Pay Account Collection Without Missed Meals Credit

Cause. Marine missed more than 20 percent of meals due to assigned duties (range duty, security shift, etc.) but the reporting unit charged for all meals. Per paragraph 2.4.3.2.2, the missed-meals credit applies. Fix. The commander or designee certifies the missed-meals percentage and routes the credit through the reporting unit.

### 4. Wrong BAS Rate After Officer Commissioning From Enlisted Ranks

Cause. A Marine commissioning from the enlisted ranks transitions from enlisted BAS (potentially BAS II) to officer BAS (single rate). The transition should occur on the commissioning date. If the LES still shows the enlisted rate after commissioning, the reporting unit missed the transition.

### 5. FSSA or BNA Not Started After Eligibility Confirmed

Cause. Marine certified eligible for FSSA (OCONUS) or BNA (after 12 May 2025) but the allowance never started on the LES. Fix. Confirm the certification date and route through the certifying official to the reporting unit.

## Verifying BAS on the LES

A monthly LES check catches BAS errors fast.

### Step 1. Pull Your LES

Through MyPay each payday.

### Step 2. Verify the BAS Line

For officers, confirm a single BAS rate matching the published Table 25-1 officer rate.

For enlisted, confirm one of these.

- Standard Enlisted BAS rate (default after basic training).
- Enlisted BAS II rate (for narrow eligible quarters situations, twice the standard rate).
- No BAS line if subsisted-in-kind (e.g., recruits in basic training).

For FSSA recipients (OCONUS only), confirm the FSSA line item with the certified amount.

For BNA recipients, confirm the BNA line item.

### Step 3. Check Pay Account Collections in Field Duty / Sea Duty / EUM / ESM

The Remarks Block annotates pay-account collections for meals during field duty, sea duty, EUM, or ESM. Verify the collection days match the actual duty days. Verify any missed-meals certification is reflected as a credit.

### Step 4. Resolve Discrepancies

Coordinate with your S-1. Standard correction routes through the reporting unit (I&I S-1 with direct UD/MIPS access, or a battalion S-1 routing through their PAC) to DFAS Marine Corps Pay.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

I am a Sergeant in the barracks. The chow hall is open. Why am I drawing BAS and not BAS II?

BAS II requires single Government quarters that lack adequate food storage or preparation AND no Government mess is available. If a chow hall is operational at your installation, BAS II is not authorized. You draw standard enlisted BAS and pay for meals at the chow hall through the meal-card system.

I commissioned last week. When does officer BAS start?

The transition from enlisted BAS to officer BAS is effective the date of commissioning. The first LES after commissioning should reflect officer BAS (with proration if mid-month).

I went on terminal leave. Will my BAS continue?

Yes. Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.2.2.2, enlisted Marines on authorized leave (including terminal leave, which is a form of chargeable annual leave) are entitled to the standard enlisted BAS rate. Officers continue at the officer BAS rate. BAS terminates on the actual separation date.

I am in the field for 30 days. The chow hall is open every day. Will I be charged for all meals?

Per paragraph 2.4.3.1.1, field duty triggers mandatory pay-account collection for all meals available. If you eat 80 percent of the meals due to operational tempo and the commander certifies the missed-meals percentage, you receive a credit per paragraph 2.4.3.2.2. Otherwise the collection is for all meals available.

I deployed and was in a combat zone hospital for 2 weeks. Was I charged for hospital meals?

No. Per paragraph 2.4.2, you are not required to pay for meals during medical recuperation or therapy at a military treatment facility for an injury, illness, or disease incurred while on active duty in support of designated combat operations or in a combat zone.

I am dual military and we both qualify for BNA. Do we both get it?

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 4.2.2.3.1, no. Only one allowance is paid per household. You and your spouse jointly elect which eligible member receives BNA.

I was on excess leave. Did I lose BAS?

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.3.1.2, yes. Excess leave terminates BAS for the duration of the excess leave period. BAS resumes when you return to a pay status.

I had a court-martial that imposed forfeiture of pay and allowances. Did I lose BAS too?

Per Chapter 25 paragraph 2.3.1.6, yes. A court-martial sentence imposing forfeiture of pay and allowances terminates BAS during the forfeiture period.

## Where to Go for Help

### Routing by Issue

- BAS rate verification and corrections. Your S-1 routes corrections through the reporting unit.
- BAS II eligibility verification (single quarters, no mess). Coordinate with the unit housing office and the S-1.
- Missed-meals credit during field duty / sea duty / EUM / ESM. The commander certifies the missed-meals percentage. The S-1 routes the credit.
- FSSA certification (OCONUS only). The Marine submits the application. The Service-designated certifying official approves.
- BNA certification (CONUS and OCONUS). The Marine voluntarily applies. The Service-designated certifying official certifies based on GHI and household size.

## Related Roles

- Leaders verify Marines' BAS on the LES during command checks, particularly after recruit graduation, commissioning, sea duty embarkation, and any field exercise, and route corrections to the S-1 quickly.
- Admin at S-1 builds documentation packages for BAS starts/stops, BAS II elections, missed-meals credits, and FSSA/BNA certifications.
- Commanders certify BAS-related pay actions (BAS II eligibility for single Government quarters lacking mess, missed-meals credits during field duty, ESM/EUM declarations) and oversee unit-level pay-record integrity.
