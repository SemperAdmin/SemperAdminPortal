## How to Use This Table

The locations listed below are **representative** of SLIP-eligible billets for each MOS. This is not a complete or current list of available assignments—that changes based on Marine Corps needs.

**Your PMOS monitor determines what actually counts as "difficult to fill" and what's open right now.** Use this table as a starting point. Then ask your monitor what's actually available for your specialty.

## What "Eligible" Means

An eligible location is one the Marine Corps considers **difficult to fill** or **critical** for your MOS. This typically includes:

- **Overseas postings** (farther from CONUS, longer separation)
- **Remote CONUS locations** (smaller bases, isolated areas)
- **Specialized billets** (require additional training or rare skillsets)

Your specific eligibility depends on:
- Your current rank (NCO vs. SNCO where noted)
- Your reenlistment zone (must be B, C, or D)
- Your career stage and time-on-station
- Current Marine Corps staffing needs

## Ask Your Monitor

Before you commit to a SLIP assignment, confirm with your PMOS monitor:

- **Is this billet actually open?** The table shows what *can* be SLIP-eligible, not what's available now.
- **What follow-on assignments are available?** The incentive is the follow-on. Know what you're working toward.
- **Do I rate SLIP right now?** Verify you meet all five eligibility gates (reenlistment zone, RE code, HSST, time-on-station, service obligation).
- **How long is the tour?** Typical SLIP assignments are 2–4 years. Confirm the length before you volunteer.
