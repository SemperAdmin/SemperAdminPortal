## What Is SLIP?

SLIP (Strategic Location Incentive Program) is a deal: you volunteer for a tour at a tough-to-fill duty station—overseas, remote, or requiring special skills—and in return, when that tour ends, you get to choose where you go next.

It's a way the Marine Corps fills critical billets while giving career Marines control over their next move.

## Why SLIP Matters to You

**You get agency.** Most assignments come down from your PMOS monitor based on Marine Corps needs. SLIP flips that: you volunteer, and your preferred follow-on duty station is locked in your orders before you leave.

**It's a retention tool.** If you're thinking about staying in, SLIP gives you a concrete incentive: harder tour now, better choice later.

**It applies to your whole second tour.** The incentive doesn't expire. Your follow-on assignment sticks with you for the full tour.

## Am I Eligible?

You rate SLIP if you meet ALL of these:

- **Reenlistment Zone B, C, or D.** You've completed your first enlistment and are eligible to sign another contract.
- **No RE3O code.** Your reenlistment code must be good. If you have RE3O (disqualifying), you're out.
- **Not slated for HSST or special duty.** You can't be identified for Headquarters screening (HSST) or already selected for a special duty assignment at the time you get SLIP orders.
- **Time-on-station requirement met.** You've been at your current duty station long enough per MCO 1300.8.
- **Enough service obligation remaining.** You have to commit to enough time to complete the SLIP assignment and your follow-on tour.

**Check with your PMOS monitor to confirm you rate it.**

## Which Billets Qualify?

Your MOS determines what counts as "hard to fill." Monitor discretion varies by community, but generally SLIP covers:

- **Overseas postings** (Okinawa, Guam, Bahrain)
- **Remote CONUS locations** (29 Palms, Bridgeport, Camp Lejeune certain units)
- **Billets requiring special training** (Intelligence, Cyber, Recon, Communications schoolhouse)

**Current eligible locations by MOS are listed in the companion reference page.**

The list is guidance only. Your monitor makes the final call on what's available and what counts as difficult to fill for your specialty.

## How the Program Works

**Step 1: Volunteer.** You express interest in a SLIP-eligible billet.

**Step 2: Get orders with a follow-on agreement.** Your orders include SLIP language that locks in your preferred follow-on duty station before you deploy or move.

**Step 3: Serve the tour.** Complete the assignment as ordered.

**Step 4: Get your follow-on assignment.** When the tour ends, you move to the duty station you requested, with no re-screening needed.

**Your DCC code.** Once you accept a SLIP assignment, you'll be assigned a Draw Case Code (DCC) of "DS" in the Marine Corps Total Force System (MCTFS). This code does two things: it locks you in place for your current tour and follow-on, and it prevents you from being offered other special programs until both tours are complete.

## Next Steps

**Talk to your PMOS monitor.** They maintain the list of current SLIP billets for your MOS and know which ones are actually open. The locations listed in this policy are representative, not exhaustive. Availability changes.

**Confirm your eligibility.** Walk through the five checks above with your monitor or your first sergeant to make sure you rate SLIP before your next reenlistment window.

**Ask about follow-on preferences.** If a SLIP billet interests you, your monitor can walk you through what follow-on assignments are available under SLIP for your community.
