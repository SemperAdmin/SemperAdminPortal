## Start Here If You Are New to IBA Types

Two types of Individually Billed Accounts (IBAs) exist. Standard and Restricted. The type you receive depends on your FICO credit score result during the application process. FICO 660 or above gets you a Standard IBA. FICO below 660 (or applying via the DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" /> alternate path) gets you a Restricted IBA. Default credit limits differ. Standard is $7,500 credit, $250 cash, $250 retail. Restricted is $4,000 credit, $250 cash, $100 retail. Both cards look identical. Limits on either card can be raised temporarily when validated by commanders for mission needs. Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4.

This page walks you through Standard vs Restricted IBAs from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The FICO 660 threshold for Standard vs Restricted assignment
- Default credit, cash, and retail limits for each type
- Temporary limit raises for mission needs
- DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" /> alternate credit path leading to Restricted IBA
- Identical card appearance regardless of type
- Why FICO score is not shared with the cardholder or APC

Every fact on this page comes from DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4, MCO 4600.40C<Citation source="MCO 4600.40C" />, DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" />, and the DTMO IBA Reference Library.

## How Citibank Determines Card Type

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4 and the DTMO application framework, Citibank performs a soft-pull credit check during the application process unless the applicant declines.

### Soft-Pull Credit Check

The soft-pull does NOT affect the applicant's credit score. The result determines card type.

- FICO 660 or above. Standard IBA.
- FICO below 660. Restricted IBA.

### FICO Score Confidentiality

The FICO score is NOT shared with the cardholder or the APC during the application process. Citibank communicates only the card type approval, not the underlying score.

### DD Form 2883 Alternate Path

If the applicant declines the soft-pull, the applicant completes DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" /> (Alternate Credit Worthiness Evaluation). All responses must be affirmative. Affirmative DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" /> results in a Restricted IBA.

## Standard IBA Default Limits

A Standard IBA carries the following default limits.

### Credit Limit

$7,500 total monthly credit limit. This is the maximum cumulative authorized charge balance per billing cycle.

### Cash Advance Limit

$250 per billing cycle. Cash advances are reserved for ATM withdrawals during travel when card use is impractical.

### Retail Purchase Limit

$250 per billing cycle. Retail purchases cover one-off authorized expenses (e.g., emergency travel supplies).

### Use Cases for Standard IBA

- Frequent TDY travelers with diverse expense profiles.
- Marines on extended TDY (over 30 days).
- Marines on PCS with substantial travel costs (POV mileage stops, en route lodging).

## Restricted IBA Default Limits

A Restricted IBA carries the following default limits.

### Credit Limit

$4,000 total monthly credit limit. Lower than Standard.

### Cash Advance Limit

$250 per billing cycle. Same as Standard.

### Retail Purchase Limit

$100 per billing cycle. Lower than Standard.

### Use Cases for Restricted IBA

- New cardholders with limited credit history (typical for entry-level Marines).
- Cardholders applying via DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" />.
- Cardholders rebuilding credit after past financial issues.

## Identical Card Appearance

Per DTMO, Standard and Restricted IBAs are identical in physical appearance. The card.

- Bears the cardholder's name.
- Shows the 16-digit account number.
- Carries the GSA SmartPay branding.
- Has the same expiration cycle.

The card type is NOT printed on the card. Cardholders verify their card type through CitiManager or by asking the APC.

## Temporary Limit Raises for Mission Needs

Per DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol 4, credit and cash limits on either card type can be raised temporarily when justified by mission needs. The increase is.

- Validated by the commander or supervisor.
- Coordinated through the APC.
- Bounded by a specific time period (typically the duration of the TDY or PCS).
- Reverted to the default limit at the end of the period.

### Common Mission Critical Status Triggers

- Long-term TDY with elevated lodging costs.
- OCONUS PCS with anticipated extended travel delays.
- Mission-critical short-notice deployments.
- Group travel coordination.

### Mission Critical Status Code

The APC places the account into Mission Critical (MC) status during the elevated-limit period. The status reverts to standard at the end of the mission window.

### How to Request a Limit Raise

1. Marine identifies the need for the higher limit (e.g., 30-day TDY at a $300/night locality).
2. Marine submits the request to the APC with supporting documentation (TDY orders, lodging estimate).
3. Commander or supervisor validates the request.
4. APC adjusts the limit in CitiManager.
5. Marine confirms the limit raise is active before incurring the elevated expenses.

## Cash Advance Use Rules

Cash advances on either card type are limited to.

- ATM withdrawals during travel when card use is impractical.
- Locations where merchants do not accept cards.
- Specific authorized expense categories per JTR (e.g., gratuities, small incidental expenses).

Cash advance fees apply (typically 3 percent of the advance amount). The Marine reconciles cash advances against the travel voucher with receipts where required.

### Mission Critical Cash Advance Increases

For OCONUS or remote TDY where cash use is operationally necessary, the APC may temporarily raise the cash advance limit. Same Mission Critical framework as credit limit raises.

## Reading Your Card Type in CitiManager

To verify your card type.

1. Log in to CitiManager at https://home.cards.citidirect.com/.
2. Navigate to Account Information.
3. Card type displays as "Standard" or "Restricted."

Or call Citi customer service at 1-800-200-7056 and ask the representative to confirm the card type.

## Upgrading From Restricted to Standard

A cardholder issued a Restricted IBA may request consideration for upgrade to Standard after demonstrating responsible use.

### Typical Upgrade Path

- 12 months of on-time payment history.
- No misuse incidents.
- No suspension or cancellation history.
- Documented improvement in credit profile (cardholder may request a re-evaluation).

The APC initiates the upgrade request to Citibank. Approval is at Citibank's discretion based on updated credit information.

### No Automatic Upgrade

Upgrades are NOT automatic. The cardholder requests the review through the APC.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

How do I know if my card is Standard or Restricted?

Log in to CitiManager and check Account Information, or call Citi customer service at 1-800-200-7056. The card itself does NOT display the type.

Can I see my FICO score from the GTCC application?

No. Citibank does not share the FICO score with the cardholder or the APC during the application process.

What if my Restricted card limit is too low for my upcoming TDY?

Request a temporary limit raise through your APC. Provide TDY orders and a lodging estimate. The commander validates the need. The APC raises the limit through Mission Critical status.

How long do temporary limit raises last?

Typically the duration of the mission (TDY length or PCS travel period). The limit reverts to the default at the end of the mission window.

Can my Restricted card be upgraded to Standard?

Yes, after demonstrating responsible use over 12 months of on-time payment with no misuse. Request the upgrade through your APC. Citibank reviews credit information and approves at its discretion. Not automatic.

What is the cash advance fee?

Approximately 3 percent of the cash advance amount. Cash advances are reserved for travel-related use only.

Why are the Restricted limits lower?

The Restricted limits reflect the elevated credit risk indicated by the FICO below 660 result or the DD Form 2883<Citation source="DD Form 2883" /> alternate path. Lower limits cap the Government's exposure.
