## Start Here If You Are New to CYP

CHILD AND YOUTH PROGRAMS (CYP) provide high-quality childcare and recreational programs to support eligible families at Marine Corps installations. CHILD DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS, available for children 6 WEEKS THROUGH 12 YEARS of age, include CHILD DEVELOPMENT CENTERS, SCHOOL AGE CARE, and FAMILY CHILD CARE HOMES. Programs offer FULL-DAY, PART-TIME, and HOURLY child care depending on location. YOUTH PROGRAMS serve children ages 6 TO 18 at many installation locations. Programs are designed to enrich children's SOCIAL-EMOTIONAL, COGNITIVE, AND PHYSICAL growth and development. CYP staff partner with parents to meet each individual child's needs in a safe, healthy, and nurturing environment. Request care through MCCS Child Care system. Coordinated with EFMP for special needs accommodation. Per DoDI 6060.02<Citation source="DoDI 6060.02" /> and Marine Corps Community Services policy.

## What CYP Is

CYP is the umbrella for childcare and youth programs delivered by MCCS at Marine Corps installations. CYP exists to.

- Support military readiness by providing reliable childcare for service members.
- Enrich child development with quality programming.
- Provide age-appropriate activities for children and youth.
- Reduce family stress associated with finding quality care.

### Why CYP Exists

- Marines often work non-standard hours.
- Frequent PCS disrupts civilian childcare arrangements.
- Quality childcare in some installation areas is scarce.
- Military families have unique needs (deployment, training absences, dual-military households).

## The Three Main Child Development Program Types

### Child Development Centers (CDCs)

Center-based childcare for ages 6 weeks through 5 years (pre-kindergarten).

- DoD-certified facilities.
- Typically offer full-day care during business hours.
- Programs structured around child development milestones.
- Some accept part-time enrollments.
- Hourly care may be available depending on capacity.

### School Age Care (SAC)

Before-school, after-school, and school-break programs for children K-6 (typically ages 5-12).

- Offered at installation school-age centers.
- Coordinated with local school district schedules.
- Summer and holiday camp programs.
- Homework support, recreation, and enrichment activities.

### Family Child Care (FCC) Homes

Smaller in-home childcare provided by certified FCC providers (typically a Marine spouse or military-affiliated person operating from their home).

- Smaller group sizes (typically up to 6 children).
- More flexible hours including evenings and weekends.
- Mixed-age groupings.
- Personalized care.
- Same DoD certification standards apply.

## Age Ranges Served

### CDC Age Ranges

- Infant. 6 weeks to 12 months.
- Pre-toddler. 12 to 24 months.
- Toddler. 24 to 36 months.
- Pre-school. 3 to 5 years.

### School Age Care

- Kindergarten through 6th grade.
- Approximately ages 5 to 12.

### Youth Programs

- Ages 6 to 18.
- Includes Middle School Programs (typically 11-13).
- Includes Teen Programs (typically 13-18).

## Youth Programs (Ages 6-18)

Youth Programs are offered at many installation locations and provide.

### What Youth Programs Cover

- After-school recreation.
- Sports leagues.
- Special interest clubs.
- Teen centers.
- Leadership development.
- Service learning and volunteer opportunities.
- Camps and outdoor activities.
- Educational support.

### Where Youth Programs Are Offered

- Installation Youth Centers (varies by installation).
- Coordinated with installation MCCS Recreation.
- Some programs partner with national organizations (Boys and Girls Clubs of America affiliations).

### Why Youth Programs Matter

- Older children also benefit from military community connection.
- Peer support during PCS and deployment.
- Structured activities reduce risk-taking behavior.
- College and career preparation.

## Full-Day, Part-Time, and Hourly Options

### Full-Day Care

- Most common for working parents.
- Typically 6 AM to 6 PM windows depending on installation.
- Year-round availability.
- Standard pricing.

### Part-Time Care

- 2-3 days per week or half-days.
- Useful for parents with non-standard schedules or for socialization purposes.
- Limited availability depending on enrollment.

### Hourly Care

- Drop-in care for short-term needs (medical appointments, single-day events, occasional needs).
- Limited capacity.
- May require advance scheduling.

### Evening and Weekend Care

- Generally NOT available at CDCs (which operate business hours).
- May be available through FCC homes.
- Some installations have specific evening programs for events.

## How to Request Care Through MCCS

### Step 1. Visit MilitaryChildCare.com

The DoD central system for requesting child care at military installations is MilitaryChildCare.com.

- Search by installation.
- Submit care request specifying needs (age, hours, type).
- Be placed on the wait list (most installations have wait lists).

### Step 2. Coordinate With Installation MCCS

- Visit MCCS website for your installation.
- Contact installation Resource and Referral office.
- Tour CDCs and meet staff if planning long-term enrollment.

### Step 3. Track Wait List

- Wait times vary widely by installation, age group, and program.
- Some installations have year-long wait lists for infant care.
- Others have shorter waits for older children.

### Step 4. Plan Backup Options

- Civilian childcare in the local community.
- Family Child Care homes (often shorter waits).
- Hourly care for emergency needs.

### When You Receive An Offer

- Accept promptly to secure spot.
- Complete enrollment paperwork.
- Provide medical and emergency information.
- Coordinate first-day orientation.

## Pricing and Subsidies

### DoD Subsidized Pricing

CYP fees are subsidized by DoD to make childcare affordable for military families.

- Fee structure based on Total Family Income (TFI).
- Higher-income families pay more, lower-income pay less.
- Across all categories, fees are typically below comparable civilian rates.

### Fee Categories

- Categories I through IX based on TFI bands.
- Annual fee schedule published by DoD.
- Fees recalculated when TFI changes (PCS, promotion, spouse income change).

### Multi-Child Discounts

Some discounts available for families with multiple children enrolled.

### Subsidy Programs

For families using off-base childcare due to no on-base availability or geographic constraints, subsidy programs may apply.

- Military Child Care in Your Neighborhood.
- Child Care Aware of America Fee Assistance Program.

## EFMP Coordination for Special Needs

### How CYP Accommodates Special Needs

CYP serves children with special needs in inclusive settings whenever possible.

- Trained staff for common conditions.
- Individual care plans.
- Coordination with EFMP and family.
- Accommodations per regulation.

### When CYP Cannot Accommodate

If a child's needs exceed CYP capacity, alternative options are explored.

- Specialized civilian childcare.
- Respite care through EFMP.
- TRICARE ECHO services.
- Community-based programs.

### Coordination Process

- EFMP enrollment first.
- Coordinate with installation EFMP Coordinator.
- CYP staff trained on EFMP family member needs.
- Individual planning for specific child needs.

See [Exceptional Family Member Program (EFMP)](/marines/exceptional-family-member-program).

## Common Waitlist Realities

### Infant Care Wait Lists

- Often the longest wait. Some installations have 6-12 month waits for infant slots.
- Plan ahead. Add to wait list as soon as pregnancy is confirmed if possible.

### Toddler and Pre-School Wait Lists

- Shorter than infant typically.
- Still varies by installation.

### School Age Care Availability

- Generally easier to secure than CDC slots.
- Summer programs may have different availability.

### Family Child Care Availability

- Often more immediate than CDC.
- Capacity varies based on certified providers in the area.

### Geographic Variation

Some installations have abundant CYP capacity (often older, larger installations). Others have severe shortages (often newer or expanding installations).

## Common Failure Points

### Late Wait List Sign-Up

Marines wait until they need care to sign up. Wait time means months without care.

Mitigation. Sign up via MilitaryChildCare.com as early as possible (during pregnancy or after PCS confirmation).

### Failure to Accept Offered Slot

Marines decline an offered slot waiting for a different age group or different facility. May lose place in line.

Mitigation. Take available care when offered if needed.

### No Backup Plan

Marines depend solely on CYP and have no civilian backup.

Mitigation. Identify backup civilian providers, occasional family/friend support, or FCC homes.

### Incorrect TFI Reporting

Marines underreport TFI to lower fees. Audit may catch this and create back-charges.

Mitigation. Report TFI accurately. Adjust as TFI changes.

### EFMP Coordination Missed

Marines do not communicate special needs to CYP. Care quality and safety affected.

Mitigation. Coordinate with EFMP and CYP from enrollment day one.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1. New Marine Just Married Expecting First Child

- Add to MilitaryChildCare.com wait list during pregnancy.
- Tour CDC at installation.
- Plan for backup civilian care if wait list is long.
- Confirm TRICARE pediatrician.

### Scenario 2. PCS to New Installation With Existing Children

- Add to MilitaryChildCare.com wait list at gaining installation as soon as PCS confirmed.
- Coordinate with losing installation CDC for transfer documentation.
- Identify civilian providers in gaining area as backup.

### Scenario 3. Single Parent After Divorce

- CYP critical for daily work.
- Coordinate with FRO and Family Care Plan.
- Combination of full-day care and SAC for school-age children.

### Scenario 4. Dual-Military Couple With Multiple Children

- Both spouses need reliable childcare.
- Multiple children means multiple wait lists.
- FCC homes often more flexible for non-standard hours.

### Scenario 5. EFMP Family With Special Needs Child

- Coordinate with EFMP first.
- CYP enrollment with individual care plan.
- TRICARE ECHO for additional services.
