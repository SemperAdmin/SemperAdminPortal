## Start Here If You Are New to the MBS

The Master Brief Sheet (MBS) is the aggregate display of all FITREPs in your career per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />. The MBS is the primary tool selection boards, monitors, and reenlistment authorities use to assess your performance over time. The MBS shows each FITREP date range, report type, RS, RO, marks, comparative assessment, and the Reporting Senior Profile providing context for how the marks should be read. Marines view the MBS through Marine Online (MOL). Verify accuracy before any promotion or selection board. Common errors include missing FITREPs (RS submitted but not loaded), incorrect dates, wrong RS or RO, and Section A administrative errors. MBS errors flow through the unit S-1 to HQMC PES for correction. Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> and HQMC PES guidance.

This page walks you through the MBS from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

## What the MBS Displays

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />, the MBS aggregates the Marine's FITREP history into a single profile.

### Per FITREP Information

For each FITREP on the record.

- Date range (reporting period start and end).
- Report type (Annual, Change of RS, Change of RO, etc.).
- Reporting Senior name and grade.
- Reviewing Officer name and grade.
- Marks for each of the six performance attributes.
- Comparative assessment in Section G.
- Days observed by RS and RO.
- Body composition status (per MARADMIN 066/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 066/26" /> directed comment selection).

### Aggregate Patterns

The MBS shows.

- Trends across time (improvement, sustainment, decline).
- Consistency across multiple RSs and ROs.
- Comparative assessment patterns.
- Adverse FITREPs and procedural notations.

### Reporting Senior Profile

For each RS who marked the Marine, the MBS shows the RS's broader marking profile. This provides context for how to read each FITREP's marks.

## How Selection Boards Use the MBS

Selection boards (promotion, retention, special duty selection) review the MBS as the primary performance assessment tool.

### Promotion Boards

Promotion boards review the MBS to.

- Assess the Marine's competitive standing against peers.
- Identify performance trends.
- Calibrate marks against the RS profile.
- Identify any adverse FITREPs and procedural cures.

### Selection Boards (B-Billets)

Selection boards for Recruiting Duty, Drill Instructor Duty, MSG, MARSOC, and other special duties review the MBS to.

- Identify candidates with strong sustained performance.
- Filter out candidates with adverse FITREPs.
- Assess fit for the special duty environment.

### Senior Enlisted Boards

Senior enlisted promotion boards (E-7 through E-9) place heavy emphasis on the MBS due to the limited number of selection slots.

## How Monitors Use the MBS

MMEA and MMOA monitors use the MBS during PCS slating.

### Slating Decisions

Monitors review the MBS to.

- Determine the Marine's competitive standing for desired assignments.
- Identify Marines with strong sustained performance for high-demand billets.
- Identify Marines with adverse patterns who need development assignments.

### Monitor Engagement

Marines should engage their monitor with knowledge of their MBS profile. Monitors recommend assignments based on what the MBS shows.

## How Reenlistment Authorities Use the MBS

Retention and reenlistment authorities review the MBS to.

- Assess the Marine's performance trajectory.
- Determine reenlistment eligibility.
- Calculate SRB tier eligibility based on quality.
- Identify Marines for non-standard retention paths.

## Accessing Your MBS

The MBS is available through Marine Online (MOL).

### Marine Online URL

https://www.mol.usmc.mil/.

### Login

CAC required. Sign in and navigate to the OMPF section.

### MBS Navigation

Within the OMPF, locate the Master Brief Sheet under the Performance Evaluation section. The MBS displays the aggregate view.

### Drill-Down to Individual FITREPs

From the MBS aggregate view, drill into individual FITREPs to see full content (Sections A through K).

## Common MBS Errors

### Missing FITREPs

The RS submitted a FITREP but it does not appear on the MBS. Common causes.

- Submission delays at PES quality control.
- Procedural failures returning the FITREP for correction.
- System errors during MCTFS upload.

### Incorrect Dates

The reporting period or report submission date is wrong on the MBS.

### Wrong RS or RO

The MBS shows the wrong Reporting Senior or Reviewing Officer name.

### Section A Administrative Errors

Wrong DOR, wrong MOS, wrong Service Component, or other Section A errors.

### Body Composition Errors (MARADMIN 066/26)

Per MARADMIN 066/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 066/26" /> effective 1 January 2026, height ("000"), weight ("000"), and body fat (blank) entries should be standardized. Errors in these fields require correction.

### Comparative Assessment Visibility

Marks displayed do not match the actual FITREP. Verify against the original FITREP.

## Error Correction Process

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> and HQMC PES guidance.

### Step 1. Identify the Error

Document the specific error. Compare the MBS display against the original FITREP and supporting records.

### Step 2. Coordinate With Unit S-1

The unit S-1 PES Manager is the primary point of contact for MBS error correction. The PES Manager.

- Verifies the error.
- Identifies the correction pathway.
- Coordinates with the Reporting Senior or Reviewing Officer if needed.
- Routes the correction request to HQMC PES.

### Step 3. PES Correction

HQMC PES reviews the correction request and processes the correction. Common timelines vary based on the type of error.

### Step 4. Verification

After PES processes the correction, the Marine verifies the corrected MBS through MOL.

## Verification Before Boards

### Before Any Selection Board

Verify the MBS at least 60 days before any board cycle.

### What to Check

- All FITREPs from the relevant reporting period are present.
- Dates and RS/RO are correct.
- Marks match the original FITREPs.
- Comparative assessment matches.
- Body composition entries are correct.
- No procedural notations or annotations are missing.
- The Reporting Senior Profile information is current.

### If Errors Are Found

Coordinate with the unit S-1 PES Manager immediately. Errors found close to a board cycle may not be corrected in time. Earlier verification gives more time for correction.

## The MBS and JEPES

Per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" /> and MCO 1616.1<Citation source="MCO 1616.1" />, the MBS in its current form aggregates FITREPs (E-5 and above). JEPES reports for E-1 through E-4 are administered separately through MCTFS.

### Junior Enlisted Records

For junior enlisted Marines (E-1 through E-4), the equivalent record is the JEPES history. View through MOL.

### MBS Activation at Sergeant Promotion

When a Marine promotes to Sergeant (E-5), the MBS framework activates. The first FITREP after promotion appears on the MBS. Subsequent FITREPs build the profile over time.

The JEPES leaf covers the junior enlisted framework in detail.

## Reporting Senior Profile on the MBS

The MBS displays the RS's broader marking profile to provide context for the marks given to the Marine.

### What the Profile Shows

- Distribution of marks given by this RS to all evaluated Marines.
- Comparative assessment distribution (MUE through MFE).
- Average marks vs Marine Corps-wide averages.
- Notable patterns.

### Why the Profile Matters

A G mark from an RS who routinely marks G is interpreted differently than a G mark from an RS with a balanced profile. Boards calibrate accordingly.

### MRO Awareness

The MRO does not need to track every nuance of the RS's profile but should understand the framework. A strong narrative supporting the marks is more compelling than the marks alone.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

What is the MBS?

The Master Brief Sheet. The aggregate display of all FITREPs in your career per MCO 1610.7B<Citation source="MCO 1610.7B" />. Selection boards, monitors, and reenlistment authorities review it.

How do I access my MBS?

Through Marine Online at https://www.mol.usmc.mil/. Sign in with CAC, navigate to the OMPF section, locate the Master Brief Sheet.

What does the MBS show?

Per FITREP information (dates, RS, RO, marks, comparative assessment, body composition). Aggregate patterns. The Reporting Senior Profile for each RS.

How do selection boards use the MBS?

Boards review the MBS to assess competitive standing, identify performance trends, calibrate marks against the RS profile, and identify any adverse FITREPs.

What if I find errors on my MBS?

Coordinate with the unit S-1 PES Manager. The PES Manager verifies the error and routes a correction request to HQMC PES.

When should I verify my MBS?

Before any promotion or selection board. Verify at least 60 days in advance to allow time for corrections.

What is the Reporting Senior Profile on the MBS?

The RS's broader marking pattern shown alongside each FITREP. Boards use it to calibrate how to read the marks given to you.

Does JEPES appear on the MBS?

JEPES reports for E-1 through E-4 are administered separately. The MBS framework activates at Sergeant promotion. Junior enlisted Marines view their JEPES history through MOL.

What is the most common MBS error?

Missing FITREPs (RS submitted but not loaded into the system). Compare your record of submitted FITREPs against the MBS display.

What if my MBS is wrong on a body composition entry?

Per MARADMIN 066/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 066/26" /> effective 1 January 2026, Section A Item 8 entries should be "000" / "000" / blank. Errors require coordination with the unit S-1 PES Manager and PES.

How long does an MBS correction take?

Varies by error type. Simple administrative corrections may process in days. Complex corrections involving the RS or RO chain may take weeks. Earlier identification gives more time.
