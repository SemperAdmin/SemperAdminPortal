## Start Here If You Are New to MF Education Services and Resilience

MF EDUCATION SERVICES AND RESILIENCE programs support Marine education, lifelong learning, and personal and family resilience. LEADERSHIP SCHOLAR PROGRAM (LSP) supports Marines pursuing TOP-TIER CIVILIAN EDUCATION at premier universities. VOLUNTARY EDUCATION PROGRAM (VolEd) provides TUITION ASSISTANCE (TA), academic counseling, and degree pursuit support. LIBRARY PROGRAM at installations supports academic and recreational reading and research. CHAPLAIN RELIGIOUS ENRICHMENT DEVELOPMENT OPERATION (CREDO) provides regionally based RETREATS to develop personal, relational, and spiritual resources for resilience. MARINE FOR LIFE NETWORK harnesses Marine veteran personal and professional networks for employment, housing, and education resources for transitioning and veteran Marines. Per MCO 1700.31<Citation source="MCO 1700.31" />, MCO 1500.55<Citation source="MCO 1500.55" />, and Marine Corps Community Services policy.

## Leadership Scholar Program (LSP)

LSP supports Marines pursuing top-tier civilian education at premier universities.

### What LSP Provides

- Information on top-tier graduate programs and selective undergraduate programs.
- Counseling on application strategy.
- Connection with admissions officers at participating universities.
- Resources on funding sources (GI Bill, scholarships).
- Mentorship from prior LSP participants.

### Who LSP Serves

- Active duty Marines pursuing graduate education.
- Officer Marines pursuing professional military education at civilian institutions.
- Marines competing for selective programs (Olmsted Scholar, etc.).

### Why LSP Matters

- Top-tier education enhances individual capability.
- Marine Corps benefits from broadly educated officers and SNCOs.
- Personal investment in lifelong learning.
- Career advancement opportunities.

### How To Engage LSP

- Through installation Education Center at MCCS.
- Through unit Career Counselor.
- Through HQMC programs and selection boards.

## Voluntary Education Program (VolEd) and Tuition Assistance

VolEd is the Marine Corps' voluntary education program providing access to off-duty civilian education.

### What VolEd Provides

- Tuition Assistance (TA) for off-duty courses.
- Academic counseling.
- Degree planning and progression tracking.
- Connection with accredited civilian and DoD-affiliated institutions.
- Testing services (CLEP, DSST for credit by examination).

### Tuition Assistance (TA) Basics

- Up to $4,500 per fiscal year per Marine.
- Up to $250 per semester hour or equivalent.
- For courses at accredited institutions.
- Bachelor's, master's, and certificate programs.
- Some restrictions on duplicate degree pursuit.

### TA Eligibility

- Active duty Marines and Reserve Marines on orders.
- Specific time-in-service and obligation requirements per MARADMIN.
- Approval through Education Center prior to course enrollment.

### TA Process

- Identify course at accredited institution.
- Apply for TA through Marine Online or Education Center.
- Approval prior to course start.
- Course completion required for TA payment.
- Failure to complete creates debt back to government.

### GI Bill Coordination

- TA used during active service.
- Post-9/11 GI Bill typically reserved for post-service use.
- Some Marines use both TA and GI Bill (with rules per MARADMIN).

### Coordination With Off-Duty Schedule

- TA courses are conducted off-duty.
- Class schedule must accommodate Marine's duty obligations.
- Unit support is encouraged but Marine has primary responsibility.

### Common VolEd Pursuits

- Bachelor's degree completion for SNCOs and senior Marines.
- Master's degree pursuit.
- Specific credentials and certificates.
- General education prerequisites.

## Library Program

The Library Program at Marine Corps installations supports academic, professional, and recreational reading.

### What Libraries Provide

- Academic and reference materials.
- Recreational reading collection.
- Internet access and computer workstations.
- Quiet study space.
- Children's programming.
- Online research databases.
- Inter-library loan with civilian and DoD libraries.

### Why Libraries Matter

- Free access to materials supports lifelong learning.
- Internet access for Marines without home internet.
- Quiet study for Marines pursuing TA-funded courses.
- Family programming for children's literacy.

### How To Access

- Visit installation library at MCCS facility.
- Online access for many resources via library website.
- Library card free for eligible patrons.

## CREDO (Chaplain Religious Enrichment Development Operation)

CREDO provides regionally based religious ministry designed to assist Marines and families in developing personal, relational, and spiritual resources for resilience.

### What CREDO Provides

- Personal Growth Retreats (PGR) for individual reflection and skill development.
- Marriage Enrichment Retreats (MER) for couples.
- Family Enrichment Retreats (FER) for families.
- Returning Warrior Workshops (RWW) for combat veterans.
- Other periodic offerings.

### Retreat Format

- Typically multi-day retreats (often weekend).
- Held at off-installation retreat facilities.
- Facilitated by trained chaplains and lay facilitators.
- Confidential and voluntary.

### Why CREDO Matters

- Resilience training reduces stress impact.
- Marriage enrichment reduces relationship breakdown.
- Returning combat veterans benefit from structured reflection time.
- Family dimension addresses whole-family well-being.

### Religious But Not Sectarian

CREDO is a chaplain corps program. While rooted in religious framework, programs are.

- Open to all faiths and no faith.
- Not proselytizing.
- Focused on personal and relational skill development.

### How To Attend

- Through installation chaplain office.
- Through unit chaplain.
- CREDO regional websites for retreat schedules.
- Marines and families self-register or are referred by chaplain.

### Cost

- No cost to attend.
- Travel reimbursement may apply for some events.

## Marine for Life Network

The Marine for Life Network harnesses the personal and professional networks of Marine Corps veterans and Marine-friendly resources.

### What Marine for Life Provides

- Connection with veteran Marines who have transitioned to civilian careers.
- Employment networking.
- Housing information for transitioning Marines.
- Educational resources and benefits guidance.
- Mentorship.

### Why Marine for Life Matters

- Veteran Marines often want to support transitioning Marines.
- Networks provide warmer connections than cold applications.
- "Once a Marine, always a Marine" institutionalized.

### How To Engage Marine for Life

- Through installation MCCS Career Resource Center.
- Through Marine for Life website and regional offices.
- For transitioning Marines, coordinate with TRP.

### Marine for Life Network Roles

- Marine for Life Network Coordinators at major employment centers.
- Veteran Marines volunteering as mentors.
- Marine-friendly employers.
- Marine veteran service organizations (MarSOA, others).

## Coordination Across Programs

### Marine Pursuing Degree

- VolEd for TA-funded courses.
- LSP if pursuing top-tier program.
- Library for study resources.
- CREDO for resilience during demanding academic periods.

### Marine Transitioning to Civilian Career

- Career Services (TRP, SkillBridge, COOL, USMAP).
- VolEd for additional credentials before separation.
- Marine for Life for veteran network.
- CREDO for transition resilience (Returning Warrior Workshop if combat veteran).

### Marine Family Resilience

- CREDO Family Enrichment Retreat.
- MCFTB workshops (under Family Readiness Services).
- Library children's programming.
- Coordinate with Behavioral Health if needed.

### Combat Veteran Reintegration

- CREDO Returning Warrior Workshop.
- Behavioral Health resources.
- Marine for Life network of veterans who have walked similar path.

## How To Access Education and Resilience Services

### Step 1. Visit Installation Education Center

- Located at MCCS facility.
- Walk-in and appointment availability.

### Step 2. Identify Your Need

- Tuition Assistance for college courses.
- Top-tier program pursuit (LSP).
- Library access.
- Resilience retreat (CREDO).
- Veteran network (Marine for Life).

### Step 3. Engage Specific Program

- Each program has dedicated staff.
- Coordinate across programs as relevant.

### Step 4. Online Resources

- MarineNet for Marine Corps online courses.
- DANTES.dod.mil for DoD-wide testing and education resources.
- VolEd online portals through installation Education Center.

## Why These Programs Matter

### Force Resilience

A resilient Marine and family handles operational stress, relocation, deployment, and major life events better. CREDO and family resilience programs build capacity.

### Continuous Learning

Marine Corps culture values continuous learning. VolEd, LSP, and Library Programs make learning accessible.

### Veteran Connection

Marine for Life Network institutionalizes the lifelong connection to the Marine Corps community.

### Family Readiness

Education and resilience are family programs as much as Marine programs. Spouse and children participation supports the whole family.

## Common Failure Points

### TA Underutilization

Marines do not use available TA for off-duty education.

Mitigation. Engage Education Center for course planning. Match courses to career goals.

### CREDO Skepticism

Marines skeptical of "religious" framing miss the resilience benefits.

Mitigation. CREDO is open to all. Give a single retreat a try.

### Marine for Life Underuse

Transitioning Marines apply cold to civilian jobs without leveraging veteran network.

Mitigation. Engage Marine for Life early in transition planning.

### Library Underuse

Marines view libraries as outdated. Miss free academic and recreational resources.

Mitigation. Visit library and discover modern services and online databases.

### Family Disengagement From Resilience Programs

Marine attends CREDO solo. Family resilience benefits diluted.

Mitigation. Engage family members in family-oriented programs.
