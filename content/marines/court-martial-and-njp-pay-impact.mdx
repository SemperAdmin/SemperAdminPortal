## Start Here If You Are New to Court-Martial and NJP Pay Impact

A court-martial sentence or non-judicial punishment changes the Marine's pay through forfeitures and reductions in grade. Forfeiture of pay deprives the Marine of stated amounts (partial) or all pay and allowances (total). Reduction in grade reduces basic pay to the lower-grade rate. Court-martial reductions take effect 14 days after sentence or upon convening authority approval (whichever is earlier). NJP follows Article 15 UCMJ limits per the Manual for Courts-Martial. Pay accrued BEFORE imposition is not subject to forfeiture. Suspended forfeitures and reductions can be restored if the Marine completes probation successfully.

This page walks you through court-martial and NJP pay impact from your seat as a General Marine. By the end you will know.

- The difference between partial and total forfeitures
- The 14-day effective date rule for court-martial reductions
- The Article 15 UCMJ limits on NJP forfeiture and reduction
- The grade-based forfeiture calculation when both reduction and forfeiture apply
- The pay restoration framework when sentences are set aside or suspensions vacated
- The Navy and Marine Corps non-concurrent NJP forfeiture rule

## Court-Martial Sentences and Pay

### Forfeiture of Pay (Partial)

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 2.1.1, a sentence to partial forfeiture of pay deprives the Marine of the amount of pay stated in the sentence for the months specified. The forfeiture begins on the effective date and continues monthly until the total amount is collected.

The Marine still receives the unforfeited portion of pay each month (basic pay minus the forfeiture amount, plus any allowances not subject to the forfeiture).

### Forfeiture of Pay (Total) for General Court-Martial

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 2.1.2, a general court-martial sentence to forfeiture of all pay does NOT include allowances unless the sentence states "all pay and allowances." A standard "forfeiture of all pay" leaves BAH and BAS intact.

A sentence to "forfeiture of all pay AND allowances" forfeits everything including BAH and BAS.

### Forfeiture Effective Date

Per paragraph 6.1, court-martial forfeitures apply to pay becoming due on and after the date forfeitures are effective. The effective date is governed by the convening authority's action and the appellate review timeline.

### Pay Accrued Before Imposition Is Protected

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 2.1.2 and Chapter 49 paragraph 3.2, pay accrued by the Marine BEFORE the imposition of forfeiture is not subject to forfeiture. The forfeiture applies prospectively only.

This means a Marine with $5,000 in net pay accumulated in a bank account at the time of NJP imposition keeps that $5,000. Future paychecks become subject to the forfeiture.

### Fines

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 2.2, a fine is a judgment imposed in a court-martial sentence. Fines are effective upon entry of judgment in general or special courts-martial. The Marine becomes liable to the United States for the fine amount.

A fine is collected through deductions from pay, often as a discretionary allotment-style collection by DFAS, until satisfied. Fines differ from forfeitures in that fines are a debt to the government, while forfeitures simply reduce the pay the Marine receives.

## Reductions in Grade

### Court-Martial Reduction

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 3.0, a court-martial sentence including a punitive discharge (dishonorable or bad conduct), confinement, or hard labor without confinement may automatically reduce the Marine to E-1 if permitted by Service regulations.

### Effective Date of Court-Martial Reduction

Per paragraph 3.1, a reduction in grade adjudged in a court-martial sentence takes effect 14 days AFTER the convening authority signs the action OR the date the sentence is approved by the convening authority, whichever is earlier.

This means a Marine has up to 14 days from sentencing before the reduction takes effect, and the basic pay remains at the higher grade rate during this window.

### Restoration of Pay if Sentence Set Aside

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 3.0, if the sentence reducing the Marine in grade is set aside or disapproved, or as finally approved is invalid for any reason, the rights, privileges, and property of which the Marine was deprived because of the reduction are restored. The Marine is entitled to the pay and allowances to which the Marine would have been entitled, for the period the reduction was in effect, had the reduction not been applied.

The restoration is retroactive. The Marine receives back pay at the higher grade for the period the (later-overturned) reduction was in effect.

## Non-Judicial Punishment (NJP) Pay Impact

### Article 15 UCMJ Authority

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 2.0, NJP punishments affecting pay are forfeiture of pay, reduction in grade, or a combination. Title 10 U.S.C. section 815<Citation source="Title 10 U.S.C. section 815" /> (Article 15 UCMJ) and the Manual for Courts-Martial Part V govern.

### Maximum Forfeiture Under NJP

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 3.1, the maximum forfeiture of pay under NJP is per the MCM Part V. Common NJP forfeiture limits include.

- E-3 and below. Up to 7 days pay (or fractional reduction not exceeding 7 days basic pay).
- E-4 and above. Up to half of one month basic pay for 2 months (i.e., one-half basic pay for 2 months).
- Officers. Up to half of one month basic pay for 2 months for company-grade NJP.

The actual maximums vary by NJP authority (E-1 to E-3 NJP imposed by company commander, E-4 and above by battalion commander, etc.) and the specific MCM Part V framework.

### Forfeiture Calculation When Both Reduction and Forfeiture Apply

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 3.2, when an NJP includes BOTH reduction in grade AND forfeiture of pay, the forfeiture of pay is based on the LOWER (reduced) grade.

### Whole Dollar Amount Requirement

Per paragraph 3.2, NJP forfeiture amounts must be expressed in whole dollar amounts only. Not in fractions of monthly pay or number of days pay.

### Multiple Months

If the forfeiture is for more than 1 month, the amount to be forfeited per month and the number of months must be stated.

## NJP Effective Dates

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 6.0, NJP forfeitures and reductions in grade, if unsuspended, are effective on the date the commander imposes the punishment.

### Marine Corps Non-Concurrent Forfeitures

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 6.0, for Marine Corps members, non-judicial forfeitures of pay do NOT run concurrently. If a forfeiture of pay is imposed by NJP while a prior forfeiture is still in effect, the new forfeiture does NOT begin until the prior forfeiture is completed.

This is a Service-specific rule that applies to Marines and Navy members.

### Pay Accrued Before Imposition

Per paragraph 6.0, pay accrued by the Marine before the imposition of NJP is not subject to forfeiture.

## Suspension of NJP Punishment

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 4.0 (cross-referencing Table 49-1), a commander suspends some or all of the NJP punishment as part of the imposition. The suspended portion does not take effect unless the suspension is vacated by misconduct during the probation period.

### Suspended Forfeiture

A suspended forfeiture means the forfeiture amount is NOT collected from pay during the suspension period. If the Marine completes the probation successfully, the suspended forfeiture is permanently set aside and never collected.

### Suspended Reduction in Grade

A suspended reduction means the Marine remains at the original grade and continues to receive the higher-grade basic pay. If the suspension is vacated for misconduct during probation, the reduction takes effect from the date of vacation.

### Vacating a Suspension

If the suspension is vacated, the punishment takes effect on the date of vacation. The Marine becomes subject to the forfeiture and/or reduction prospectively from that date.

## Restoration of Pay After Suspension

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 6.0, if a reduction in grade is suspended and the Marine is restored to the higher grade, the Marine is entitled to pay of the higher grade ONLY from the date of suspension. The Marine does NOT receive back pay for the period before the suspension started.

If a reduction in grade or forfeiture of pay is SET ASIDE (different from suspended), all rights, privileges, and property are restored, and the Marine is entitled to pay as though the punishment had never been imposed. This includes back pay for the affected period.

## Confinement and Pay

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 4.7, automatic forfeiture during certain court-martial confinements reduces the Marine to two-thirds basic pay during the confinement period (specific paragraph in Chapter 48). The exact framework is in the chapter and depends on the type of court-martial and the sentence.

A Marine in pre-trial confinement continues to receive normal pay until the court-martial sentence is final. Pre-trial confinement does NOT trigger forfeiture.

## Disposition of Forfeitures

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 11.0 and Chapter 49 paragraph 7.0, forfeitures of pay collected by court-martial or NJP are disposed of per the Service procedures. Generally, forfeitures revert to the U.S. Treasury or the Marine Corps Disposition of Forfeitures account.

## Pay During Appeals

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 5.0, a Marine who appeals NJP that the Marine considers unjust may be required to undergo the adjudged punishment pending determination of the appeal. The forfeiture and reduction take effect during the appeal unless suspended.

If the appeal is granted and the punishment is set aside, the pay is restored retroactively per the set-aside framework.

## Verifying Court-Martial or NJP Impact on the LES

A monthly LES check confirms forfeiture and reduction entries are correct.

### Step 1. Pull Your LES

Through MyPay each payday after NJP or court-martial imposition.

### Step 2. Verify the Pay Reduction

For reductions in grade, verify the basic pay rate matches the reduced grade.

For forfeitures, verify the LES shows the forfeiture amount as a deduction or shows the reduced net pay.

### Step 3. Verify the Effective Date

For court-martial reductions, verify the 14-day effective date.

For NJP forfeitures, verify the imposition date and (for Marines) that prior forfeitures completed before the new one begins.

### Step 4. Resolve Discrepancies

Coordinate with your S-1 and the unit Staff Judge Advocate. The reporting unit (I&I S-1 with direct UD/MIPS access, or a battalion S-1 routing through their PAC) routes through DFAS Marine Corps Pay.

## Common Questions Marines Ask

I received NJP with forfeiture of half basic pay for 2 months. How is it calculated?

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 3.2, the forfeiture is based on the grade you held at the time (or the reduced grade if reduction is also part of the NJP). For an E-4 with $3,000 monthly basic pay, half is $1,500 per month for 2 months totaling $3,000 forfeited. The amount must be expressed in whole dollars.

I had a prior NJP forfeiture still being collected. Will my new NJP forfeiture run concurrently?

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 6.0, no for Marine Corps members. NJP forfeitures do not run concurrently for Marines. The new forfeiture begins after the prior forfeiture completes.

I was reduced in grade by court-martial but the conviction was set aside on appeal. Do I get back pay?

Per Chapter 48 paragraph 3.0, yes. If the sentence is set aside, all rights and pay are restored as though the reduction had never been imposed. You receive back pay at the higher grade for the period the reduction was in effect.

My commander suspended my reduction at NJP. Do I get the lower grade pay or the higher grade pay during suspension?

Per Chapter 49 paragraph 6.0, you remain at the original (higher) grade and continue to receive the higher grade basic pay during the suspension period. If you complete the probation successfully, the reduction never takes effect.

I am in pre-trial confinement awaiting court-martial. Will my pay be reduced?

No. Per the general framework, pre-trial confinement does NOT trigger forfeiture. You continue to receive normal pay and allowances until the court-martial sentence is final.

What happens to my pay if I receive a Bad Conduct Discharge (BCD)?

Per Chapter 48, a BCD is a punitive discharge that may be accompanied by forfeitures and reductions. Pay continues at the reduced rate (and reduced grade) until the appellate review is complete and the discharge is final. Final separation from the Marine Corps occurs after the BCD is approved through the convening authority and any required higher review.
