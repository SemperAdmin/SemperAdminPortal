## Family Member Employment Assistance Program (FMEAP)

FMEAP is the installation-based employment assistance program for Marine spouses and family members.

### What FMEAP Provides

- Resume development and review.
- Interview preparation.
- Job search strategies.
- Career counseling.
- Resource library.
- Employment workshops.
- Connections with employers (career fairs, recruiter visits).

### Who FMEAP Serves

- Marine spouses (active duty and retired).
- Marine adult dependents.
- Sometimes Marines transitioning out (in coordination with TRP).

### Why FMEAP Matters

- Marine spouses face employment challenges due to frequent moves.
- License and credential portability across states is difficult for many professions.
- Spouse income is critical for many military family budgets.
- Career continuity is a frequent reason Marines separate.

### License and Credential Portability

Many states have enacted reciprocity provisions for military spouses. FMEAP helps spouses navigate.

- State-specific reciprocity rules.
- Application processes.
- Cost considerations and reimbursement programs.

### MyCAA (My Career Advancement Account)

DoD offers MyCAA, a financial assistance program for spouse education and credentialing.

- Up to $4,000 in tuition assistance.
- For licenses, certificates, and associate degrees.
- Specific eligibility based on sponsor's pay grade.
- FMEAP staff assist with applications.

### How To Access FMEAP

- Installation MCCS Career Resource Center.
- MCCS website for your installation.
- MyCAA portal at mycaa.militaryonesource.mil.

## SkillBridge (Cross-Reference)

SkillBridge is the DoD program that allows transitioning service members to participate in industry training, apprenticeships, and internships prior to separation, retirement, or EAS while continuing to receive military pay and benefits. Per NAVMC 1700<Citation source="NAVMC 1700" />.2B and MARADMIN 280/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 280/24" />, the Marine Corps applies a rank-based three-category framework. SkillBridge is at commander discretion and is not a Marine's entitlement.

### Brief Overview

- Rank-based day cap per MARADMIN 280/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 280/24" />. Cat I (E1-E5) up to 120 days. Cat II (E6-E7, WO-CWO3, O1-O4) up to 90 days. Cat III (E8-E9, CWO4-CWO5, O5 and above) up to 90 days.
- Marine continues receiving pay, BAH, and benefits, reported PTAD (Other) per MARADMIN 280/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 280/24" /> paragraph 3.b.
- No cost to the Marine.
- No cost to the employer.
- NCMIS is the Marine Corps SkillBridge system of record. Apply up to 365 days from EAS.
- Approval authority is LtCol+ for Cat I and II, General Officer for Cat III. Joint Marines need an O-6 endorsement from operational chain.
- Designed to ease transition to civilian employment, prioritized for those who require additional transition assistance.

### Detailed Coverage

For detailed Marine Corps SkillBridge process, eligibility, application, and approval per MARADMIN 280/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 280/24" />, MARADMIN 625/24<Citation source="MARADMIN 625/24" />, NAVMC 1700<Citation source="NAVMC 1700" />.2B, and MCO 1050.3J<Citation source="MCO 1050.3J" /> Chapter 5, see [Permissive TAD (PTAD) and SkillBridge](/marines/permissive-tad).

### How MF Career Services Supports SkillBridge

- Information on available SkillBridge programs.
- Resume and interview preparation for SkillBridge applications.
- Coordination with TRP for transition timing.
- Connection with industry partners.

## Credentialing Opportunities OnLine (COOL)

Marine Corps COOL helps Marines identify and pursue civilian credentials related to their MOS.

### What COOL Provides

- MOS-credential mapping (which civilian credentials apply to which MOS).
- Funding for credential exams and preparation.
- Information on credential providers and requirements.
- Career-related credential planning.

### Why COOL Matters

- Civilian credentials add resume value.
- Some credentials are required for civilian jobs in the same field.
- COOL funding reduces personal cost of pursuing credentials.

### Eligible Credentials

- Industry certifications (CompTIA, PMP, Six Sigma, etc.).
- Professional licenses (electrician, mechanic, healthcare, etc.).
- Vendor-specific certifications (Cisco, Microsoft, AWS, etc.).
- Trade-specific credentials.

### How To Access COOL

- COOL website for the Marine Corps at cool.osd.mil/usmc.
- Education Center at installation MCCS.
- Coordination with MOS Career Counselor.

### Funding Process

- Identify eligible credential.
- Apply for COOL funding.
- Pursue credential.
- Submit documentation upon completion.

## United Services Military Apprenticeship Program (USMAP)

USMAP enables Marines to translate military training and on-the-job experience into nationally recognized civilian apprenticeship credentials.

### What USMAP Provides

- Apprenticeship registration with the Department of Labor.
- Documentation of military experience as civilian apprenticeship hours.
- Journeyman certification upon completion of apprenticeship hours.
- No tuition cost.

### Who Benefits

- Marines whose MOS aligns with civilian apprenticeable trades.
- Common matches include mechanics, electricians, IT specialists, food service, healthcare support, and many others.

### Why USMAP Matters

- Civilian apprenticeship completion is a recognized credential.
- Reduces apprenticeship time when transitioning to civilian career.
- Validates military training for civilian employers.

### How To Enroll

- USMAP website at usmap.cnic.navy.mil.
- Coordinate with installation Education Center or Career Resource Center.
- MOS coordinator at unit.

### Documentation Requirements

- Hour tracking and supervisor verification.
- Quarterly progress reports.
- Final journeyman certification application.

## Transition Readiness Program (TRP) (Cross-Reference)

TRP is the Marine Corps' transition program for separating Marines per MCO 1700.31<Citation source="MCO 1700.31" /> and DoDI 1322.29<Citation source="DoDI 1322.29" />.

### Brief Overview

- Mandatory program for transitioning Marines.
- Includes Transition Readiness Seminar (TRS).
- Capstone event for transition readiness verification.
- Individual Transition Plan (ITP) development.
- VA benefits briefing.
- Resource referrals.

### Detailed Coverage

For detailed TRP process, timing, and components, see [Transition Readiness](/marines/transition-readiness) (in Separations and Retirement parent group when published).

### How MF Career Services Supports TRP

- Career counseling beyond TRS sessions.
- Resume and interview preparation.
- Connection with civilian employers and recruiters.
- COOL and USMAP coordination.
- SkillBridge coordination.

## Volunteer Program (Career Relevance)

The Volunteer Program is a UPFRP component, but volunteer service has clear career development value, especially for Marine spouses.

### Career Relevance

- Volunteer roles develop or maintain professional skills.
- Volunteer experience adds to resumes.
- Networking opportunities.
- References for future employment.

### Examples of Career-Relevant Volunteer Roles

- FRV (project management, communication, leadership skills).
- CYP volunteer (early childhood education exposure).
- Family Readiness Volunteer (event planning, communication).
- MCFTB volunteer instructor (training and facilitation skills).

### How To Volunteer

- Through unit FRO.
- Through installation MCCS Volunteer Coordinator.
- See [Family Readiness Services (UPFRP)](/marines/family-readiness-services).

## How To Access Career Services

### Step 1. Visit Installation MCCS Career Resource Center

- Located at most installations.
- Walk-in and appointment availability.

### Step 2. Identify Your Need

- Spouse employment (FMEAP).
- Transition to civilian career (TRP, SkillBridge).
- MOS-related credential (COOL).
- Apprenticeship credential (USMAP).
- Volunteer for skill development.

### Step 3. Engage Specific Program

- Each program has dedicated staff.
- Multiple programs may apply (e.g., transitioning Marine using TRP, SkillBridge, AND COOL).

### Step 4. Use Online Resources

- MyCAA portal for spouse education funding.
- COOL website for credential funding.
- USMAP website for apprenticeship.
- MilitaryOneSource.mil for cross-cutting resources.

## Coordination Across Programs

### Transitioning Marine Coordination

A transitioning Marine might engage.

- TRP for overall transition planning.
- SkillBridge for civilian internship.
- COOL for industry credential.
- USMAP for apprenticeship credential.
- PFMP for transition financial planning.
- FMEAP for spouse employment continuity.

### Spouse Employment Coordination

A spouse might engage.

- FMEAP for resume and job search.
- MyCAA for education funding.
- Volunteer Program for skills and networking.
- IR&R for community connection.

### Family Coordination

Career decisions often involve family. Coordination with FRO and other family programs ensures unit and family alignment.

## Common Failure Points

### Late Engagement

Marines and spouses wait until they need help to engage. Long lead time programs (TRP, SkillBridge) close to them.

Mitigation. Engage Career Services well in advance. TRP requires 12-month lead time for separating Marines.

### Single Program Use

Marines use one program and miss complementary programs.

Mitigation. Career counselor at MCCS connects across programs. Ask about all options.

### Unfunded Personal Spending

Marines pay for credentials or training out of pocket when COOL or MyCAA could have funded.

Mitigation. Check funding eligibility before paying.

### Spouse Career Neglected

Marine focuses on his or her own career, treats spouse career as afterthought.

Mitigation. Spouse employment is significant for family financial readiness. Engage FMEAP and MyCAA.

### Volunteer Burnout vs Career Strategy

Spouses volunteer extensively without strategic career intent.

Mitigation. Pair volunteer service with career development goals. Document for resume use.
