## Use This Page When

A Marine in your command makes an Unrestricted Report of sexual assault, or a Restricted Report converts to Unrestricted, or a third party reports an assault you become aware of. The trigger is the report becoming Unrestricted, regardless of the route.

Restricted Reports stay with the SARC and SAPR VA. The CO has no command response role under a Restricted Report and is not entitled to information about the case.

## First 24 Hours

| Action | Authority | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| SARC notification | MCO 1752.5C, DoDI 6495.02 | Within 24 hours of the Unrestricted Report. SARC handles SAPR VA assignment. |
| OSTC notification | MARADMIN 633/24 | Sexual assault is a covered offense. OSTC has exclusive referral authority. |
| Military Protective Order | DoDI 6400.06 | Written MPO when accused and victim have contact opportunity. SJA reviews wording. |
| Pretrial confinement determination | RCM 305 | Decision authority is yours. IRO review timeline starts at confinement. |
| Verify victim's SAPR VA engagement | MCO 1752.5C | Confirm a credentialed VA is assigned and has met or scheduled to meet with the victim. |
| Witness Victim Advocate (VWAP) coordination | MCO 5800.14 | VWAP handles court process notifications. Different role from SAPR VA. |
| Determine Expedited Transfer eligibility | MCO 1752.5C, DoDI 6495.02 | Victim has the right to request ET. Decision authority by victim's grade. |
| Legal hold flag in MCTFS for accused | MCO 5800.16 Vol. 12 | Prevents transfer, retirement, separation, reenlistment while case open. |

## 7 to 30-Day Routing

1. **First CMG attendance.** The Case Management Group is the multi-disciplinary forum for case oversight. Your attendance is non-delegable under MCO 1752.5C.
2. **72-hour victim update after each CMG.** Update the reporting Marine on case status within 72 hours of each CMG. Non-delegable. Document the update.
3. **Expedited Transfer decision if requested.** Approve or deny in writing with the rationale. Timelines under DoDI 6495.02.
4. **Climate touchpoint with the unit.** Coordinate with the SARC. Frame around safety and support, not the case.
5. **Personnel records review for both parties.** Prior reports, prior MPOs, history of contact.
6. **OSTC liaison continues.** Charging decisions, witness lists, evidence preservation all run through OSTC and trial counsel.

## Non-Delegable CO Actions Under MCO 1752.5C

These are personal to you. Substitution by XO is not authorized.

- Attend monthly CMG for open Unrestricted Reports.
- Update the reporting Marine on case status within 72 hours of each CMG.
- Decision to issue or rescind a Military Protective Order.
- Decision on Expedited Transfer request.
- Decision on pretrial confinement.

The 72-hour update rule is the most-missed item on this list. Track it on your calendar.

## SAPR VA Eligibility - The Appointment Trap

If a SAPR VA is on your unit roster, verify their eligibility is current.

| Requirement | Authority |
|------------|-----------|
| Background investigation favorable | DoDI 6495.03 |
| NSO and PMO checks complete | DoDI 6495.03 |
| Secret clearance current | DoDI 6495.03 |
| D-SAACP certification current | MCO 1752.5C |
| 40-hour SAPR training complete | DoDI 6495.03 |
| Written appointment letter from CO | MCO 1752.5C |

Appointing a VA before all six items are complete creates a procedural defect. Pre-existing VAs from your predecessor require re-appointment under your signature.

## Restricted vs Unrestricted - Stay Out of the Restricted Lane

Restricted Reports are confidential between the victim, the SARC, and the SAPR VA. You as CO are not entitled to know:

- The existence of any Restricted Report in your command
- The identity of the victim under a Restricted Report
- The identity of any accused
- The details of the assault

If you receive information you suspect came from a Restricted Report, stop. Contact the SARC. Do not act on the information until the report converts to Unrestricted by the victim's choice.

This rule exists because trust in the Restricted Report system is the gateway to victims later choosing to come forward Unrestricted.

## Common Failures

1. **Missing the 72-hour victim update.** Easy to defer. Non-delegable. Track it.
2. **Sending the XO to CMG.** Not authorized under MCO 1752.5C. CO attendance is the standard.
3. **Acting on Restricted Report information.** Lost trust. Possible procedural violation.
4. **Issuing the MPO verbally only.** Verbal is fine for the first hour. Written is required for enforcement.
5. **Not running the OSTC overlap.** Sexual assault is a covered offense. Both playbooks apply.
6. **Re-appointing a SAPR VA without rerunning eligibility.** D-SAACP and clearance gaps create program findings.
7. **Treating the SARC as advisory only.** The SARC is the program coordinator. They have authorities the CO does not.

## Documentation Set

By the end of the first 30 days, these documents are on the record.

- SARC notification timeline
- MPO if issued
- Expedited Transfer decision letter
- CMG minutes (SARC-maintained)
- 72-hour victim update memoranda for record
- OSTC notification email
- Legal hold flag in MCTFS
- SAPR VA appointment letter (if you re-appointed)
- VWAP appointment for the case

## When the Case Closes

Disposition by OSTC or by closure of the SAPR case closes the response phase. Sustain work continues.

- Remove the legal hold once SJA confirms closure.
- Confirm Expedited Transfer is executed or rescinded.
- Update CIRRAS for the victim and the accused.
- Conduct unit climate touchpoint with SARC.
- Re-verify all SAPR VAs on the unit roster for D-SAACP currency.
