## The Premise

The IGMC inspector walks the same FAC you have access to. The advantage you have over the inspector is time and continuity. You see the unit every day. The inspector sees it for two to four days.

Self-audit converts the time-and-continuity advantage into documented audit posture. A FAC walked quarterly is a FAC the unit knows cold by the time the inspector arrives.

## Pre-Walk Preparation

Before walking the FAC, the responsible officer or staff section assembles three things.

1. **The current FAC document.** Pulled from the IGMC CoRE portal. Do not work from a printed copy older than six months.
2. **The unit's prior inspection findings on this FAC.** Closed and open. Knowing what was wrong last time tells you where to look this time.
3. **The supporting documents and access.** Policy letters, appointment letters, SOPs, training records, related logs.

The pre-walk takes 30 minutes. The walk-through takes 60 to 90 minutes. The closure work after takes longer than both combined.

## The Walk-Through

A workable structure for a FAC walk-through.

### Step 1: Introductions and scope

Confirm the inspector (you or your delegate) and the FAC owner. Confirm the FAC scope. Read the FAC's stated purpose aloud. This forces clarity on what is being inspected.

### Step 2: Line-by-line

Walk each FAC item. For each:

- Ask the question as written
- Request the supporting document
- Observe the practice if observable
- Mark the response: meets standard, partial, does not meet, not applicable
- Note the specific evidence (file name, document date, observed behavior)

### Step 3: Section findings

After each FAC section, summarize the section findings. Confirm what was strong, what was weak, what is missing.

### Step 4: Overall record

After the FAC, the owner of the walk-through produces a brief findings record. Date, FAC walked, owner, items marked, severity assessment, recommended corrective action.

## The Findings Record

A minimal findings record captures:

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Walk-through date | When the FAC was walked |
| FAC walked | Program number, title, sponsor |
| Inspector (delegate) | XO, SgtMaj, or other designated walker |
| FAC owner | Responsible officer or staff section |
| Items meeting standard | Count and list |
| Items partial or non-compliant | Specific item, evidence, recommended fix |
| Items not applicable | Specific item, rationale |
| Corrective action assignments | Owner, due date, follow-up trigger |
| CO review and signature | After the walk-through |

The record is filed where the unit FAC walk-through tracker lives.

## Severity Assessment

Three rough severity tiers help prioritize correction.

| Severity | Definition | Correction Timeline |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| Critical | Direct violation with legal or readiness exposure | Immediate |
| Significant | Procedural defect or stale document | 30 days |
| Minor | Improvable practice without policy violation | Next quarter |

A critical finding requires the CO's attention before the next FAC walk-through is scheduled. Significant findings get assigned and tracked. Minor findings are batched for quarterly review.

## Corrective Action Discipline

Findings without corrective action discipline accumulate. Three rules.

1. **Every finding has an owner and a due date.** No exceptions.
2. **The XO or SgtMaj owns the corrective action tracker.** Reviewed at FPC or at a dedicated audit meeting.
3. **Closure is verified at the next FAC walk-through.** Not by paperwork. By direct observation of the fix holding.

A unit with a long-standing corrective action tracker showing repeat findings tells the inspector everything they need to know about audit posture.

## How to Sequence the FACs Across the Year

The portal currently tracks 32 inspection programs across the IGMC and MCAAT tracks. Walking all of them is a year-long discipline.

| Quarter | Focus FACs |
|---------|------------|
| Q1 | High-frequency-inspection FACs: SAPR, FPC, Records Management, Safety |
| Q2 | Materiel readiness: Supply, Maintenance, Communications |
| Q3 | Personnel and admin: Awards, FITREP, Promotions, Page 11 |
| Q4 | Legal and discipline: Military Justice, NJP, Pretrial Confinement, OSTC liaison |

Adjust to local IGMC schedule. If the announced inspection is in Q3, frontload the Q3 FACs into Q2 walk-throughs.

## Use of the Portal's Inspection Surface

The Semper Admin Portal has an inspections surface at `/inspections/igmc` and `/inspections/mcaat`. The portal tracks 32 programs across both tracks at the time of this writing.

A workable pattern: open the portal's inspection page for the FAC you are walking. Use the portal as a cross-reference for the FAC line items, related policies, and links to your portal's own pages on the underlying topics.

This gives the walk-through three layers of reference at once:

1. The actual FAC from the IGMC CoRE portal
2. The portal's summary of the FAC
3. The portal's domain pages on the underlying topic

## What the Inspector Asks Beyond the FAC

The FAC line items are the floor. Inspectors often go beyond.

| Inspector approach | What it reveals |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Ask the Marine, not the leader | Does the person doing the work know the standard |
| Sample three random files | Whether the document discipline is consistent or selective |
| Ask for a specific date or event | Whether the records-by-date discipline holds |
| Ask "what would you do if X happened tomorrow" | Whether the SOP is in the person's head or only on paper |
| Walk the actual space | Whether posted policy letters and emergency contacts are current |

Self-audit prepares for these too. The walk-through should not be a paper exercise. Ask the Marine. Sample the files. Pose the hypothetical.

## Common Failures

1. **Walk-through done by junior officer in isolation.** Findings not surfaced to CO. Surprises at inspection.
2. **FAC walked once a year, two weeks before inspection.** No time to correct findings.
3. **Findings recorded but no closure tracking.** Same finding present at next walk-through.
4. **The walk-through is paper only.** No actual observation of practice. Marines cannot articulate the program.
5. **Findings marked "not applicable" without rationale.** Inspector challenges. The walk-through loses credibility.
6. **No CO review of findings.** Corrective actions assigned but never reviewed by command.
7. **Repeated inspection findings on the same item.** Visible pattern. Higher headquarters notices.

## Decision Framework Before Closing a Walk-Through

Before signing off, the walk-through inspector confirms:

1. Every FAC line item received a marking
2. Every partial or non-compliant item has an owner and due date
3. The findings record is filed
4. The CO is briefed on critical or significant findings
5. The next walk-through of this FAC is scheduled
6. Prior open findings on this FAC are either closed or escalated
