## Use This Page When the Phone Rings

Five incident types account for the majority of command response calls outside the battle rhythm. Each has a separate playbook page with the 90-second checklist, 24-hour actions, and 7-day routing.

This page is the triage step. Read it once now. Bookmark the role-tree branch. When the call comes, open the right page directly.

## Decision Tree

Walk these questions in order. Stop at the first yes.

1. **Is the alleged conduct on the OSTC covered offense list?** (Murder, manslaughter, sexual assault, child sexual offenses, kidnapping, domestic violence, stalking, retaliation, child pornography, substantiated sexual harassment, or conspiracy/solicitation/attempt of any of these.) → Open `covered-offense-incident`. OSTC has exclusive referral authority. Do not act before notifying.
2. **Is the report a SAPR Unrestricted Report?** → Open `sapr-unrestricted-report`. SARC notification is the first action. Many SAPR cases also fall under OSTC. Both playbooks apply.
3. **Was the Marine arrested off-base or by civilian law enforcement?** → Open `off-base-arrest`. PMO liaison and the MCTFS legal hold are the first actions.
4. **Did the Marine attempt suicide, express suicidal ideation, or get admitted to inpatient mental health or substance abuse care?** → Open `suicide-attempt-response`. Notification to command under DoDI 6490.08 is triggered. Warm hand-off and CIRRAS update follow.
5. **None of the above but a Marine is in crisis or significantly impaired?** → Default to the suicide-attempt-response checklist for the warm hand-off pattern, then escalate per the Force Preservation topic.

When two playbooks apply, run both. SAPR Unrestricted Reports often involve covered offenses. Suicide attempts often involve substance abuse referrals. The playbooks are designed to layer.

## What Every Playbook Has in Common

Each page is structured the same way. Read the section matching the time you have.

| Section | Use When |
|---------|---------|
| 90-second checklist | The phone is in your hand. You need the first three actions now. |
| 24-hour actions | After the immediate notifications. Before the next watch turns over. |
| 7-day routing | The legal hold, the records, the boards, the documentation. |
| Non-delegable actions | What you, the CO, must do personally. |
| Common failures | The mistakes which taint the record or the proceeding. |

## Non-Delegable Actions by Playbook

A reference summary. Each playbook page has the full list with citations.

| Playbook | Non-Delegable CO Actions |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Covered Offense | OSTC notification verification, legal hold orders, CMG attendance |
| SAPR Unrestricted Report | CMG attendance, 72-hour victim case-status update |
| Off-Base Arrest | Decision on confinement (pretrial), MCTFS flag review |
| Suicide Attempt Response | First conversation with the Marine if reachable, warm hand-off coordination, command-directed evaluation decision |

## Who You Notify Before You Act

Different incidents trigger different first calls. Get the order right.

| Incident | First External Call | Second |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| OSTC covered offense | Servicing LSSS/T to confirm OSTC jurisdiction | OSTC regional inbox (see OSTC Covered Offenses page) |
| SAPR Unrestricted Report | Installation SARC | SAPR VA assigned to victim |
| Off-base arrest | Civilian agency holding the Marine | Supporting SJA |
| Suicide attempt or hospitalization | Embedded behavioral health (if assigned) or medical provider | SgtMaj/SEA and immediate chain |

The Marine's family is not on this list by accident. Family notification protocol varies by incident type and by Marine's documented preferences. Each playbook page covers the specific rule.

## What This Page Is Not

This page does not replace the playbook content. It routes you to it.

The playbooks do not replace legal counsel, the SARC, the behavioral health provider, or the Sergeant Major. They give you the questions to ask and the actions to take in the first hour. The trained professionals in your chain own the disposition.

If you have time before the next call, read each of the five playbook pages once. The pattern is the same on every page. Familiarity is the difference between 60-second triage and 6-minute confusion.
