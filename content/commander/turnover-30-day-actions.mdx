## The 30-Day Window Rule

Section 4 puts a hard deadline on actions that incoming COs habitually defer. The Master Turnover Checklist is explicit: do NOT use "all policies remain in effect." Every policy letter must be re-issued under your signature.

The 30-day window covers four distinct areas: command policy letters, written appointments, fund control training, and readiness setup including FPC and CMT.

## A. Command Policy Letters

Every policy letter terminates with the predecessor. Re-issue under your signature within 30 days. Do not back-date. Effective date is the date of your signature.

|--------|-----------|
| Command Safety Policy Statement | MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" /> Ch 2 Para 0204 |
| Command Philosophy / Commander's Intent Letter | Commander's Leadership Handbook (USMCU 2016) |

The "all policies remain in effect" pattern is a common failure. It is administratively convenient and substantively incorrect. The policy letter is your statement of expectation as commander. Predecessor signatures do not bind your command.

## Common Failures on Section 4

1. **Predecessor policy letters left in force.** Letters dated under outgoing CO continue to be referenced. New CO has not signed any. Substantively void.
2. **SAPR VA appointed before D-SAACP certification complete.** Appointment letter signed; eligibility screening incomplete. Voids the appointment and exposes the program to inspection finding.
3. **Tier 1 fund control training treated as new-CO orientation.** Comptroller briefs once, no documentation. MARADMIN 350/11<Citation source="MARADMIN 350/11" /> requires record at command level on the 3-year cycle.
4. **CMT cadence not adjusted to biennial.** Unit continues annual non-core CMT under prior MCO. MCTIMS still shows annual. MARADMIN 199/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 199/26" /> supersedes.
5. **CIRRAS warm handoff treated as briefed-not-documented.** New CO inherits a list of names without the underlying CIRRAS entries updated. Para 4.b.(3)(g) standard is documentation, not briefing.

## 30-Day Sign-Off Checklist

Before Day 30 closes, confirm:

1. All seven core policy letters signed under my name with current dates
2. Required written appointments signed for every applicable billet
3. Tier 1 fund control training documented within 3 years
4. DON Budget Execution Course completed
5. MarineNet DRRS-MC policy course completed
6. First FPC chaired with CIRRAS
7. UPFRP enrollment audited (NAVMC 11654<Citation source="NAVMC 11654" /> or 11657 for every Marine)
8. CMT cadence confirmed against MCO 1500.63A<Citation source="MCO 1500.63A" /> in MCTIMS
9. First random urinalysis schedule approved

A "no" on any of these is a 60-day problem. Resolve before the 30-day mark or accept the schedule slip is on the record.
