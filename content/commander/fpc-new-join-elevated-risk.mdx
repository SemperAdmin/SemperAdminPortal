## The Rule

Per MCO 1500.60A Para 4.b.(3)(g)(3): all Marines transferred with a LOW risk determination must automatically be assigned ELEVATED risk for 90 days from date joined in MCTFS. The Marine must be discussed during FPCs throughout this period.

This is not a discretionary call. The rule applies by transfer, not by individual assessment. A Marine you have never met, with no incidents, no flags, no concerns of any kind, joins your unit at LOW. The rule elevates them to ELEVATED for 90 days regardless.

## Why the Rule Exists

The Suicide Prevention guide identifies transitions as one of the most common high-risk windows for Marines. The recurring framing: "Transitions are often a high-stress period." Every PCS scenario in the guide ends with the same direction to update FPC and CIRRAS.

The 90-day elevation is the Corps' systemic response to the pattern. The receiving command does not get to decide whether the transition was stressful. The rule treats every transition as stressful by default until 90 days of unit-level observation prove otherwise.

## What ELEVATED Triggers in Your Unit

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| CIRRAS profile | Status set to ELEVATED on the join date in MCTFS |
| FPC briefing | Marine discussed at every monthly council during the 90-day window |
| Sponsor assignment | Marine Corps Sponsorship Program sponsor assigned before arrival per NAVMC 1320 |
| Small-unit leader check-in | Documented check-in cadence at the squad or section level |
| Resource exposure | First-week orientation to CCP, MFLC, chaplain, OSCAR, MIP if applicable |
| Lethal-means conversation | Conducted by SNCO per the Suicide Prevention guide barracks/off-base scenarios |
| Initial counseling | Within the first 30 days of joining |

The 90-day clock starts at the date joined in MCTFS, not the date of orders.

## What Does NOT Override the Rule

The 90-day elevation is not waived by any of these.

- Marine's stated wellness or stability
- Inbound CIRRAS warm handoff showing low risk
- Brief from prior CO saying "this Marine is solid"
- Marine being a senior NCO or SNCO
- Marine being on follow-on tour at the same installation
- Marine being a high performer

The rule is by transfer, not by Marine.

## What ELEVATED Means Operationally

ELEVATED is not adverse. It is heightened leadership attention.

- The Marine is on the FPC list and receives 30-minute monthly discussion.
- The small-unit leader has a documented check-in cadence.
- The Marine knows their sponsor and has been oriented to base resources.
- Any incident, no matter how minor, is recorded in CIRRAS without waiting for a pattern.
- The Marine is paired with a mentor if no local relationships exist (per the Suicide Prevention guide E1-E4 scenario).

A Marine on ELEVATED status during the 90-day window who has zero incidents and is fully integrated reverts to LOW at the 90-day mark. A Marine with any indicators during the window stays ELEVATED or escalates to HIGH.

## The FPC Discussion Pattern

Each new join gets a structured discussion at the monthly FPC. The Suicide Prevention guide gives the rough template through its scenarios.

1. **Identity and grade.** Name, rank, MOS, billet.
2. **Origin and transition context.** Where transferred from. Why. Family location. PCS family separation status.
3. **Sponsor assignment status.** Sponsor name, date contact made, sponsor's status report.
4. **First-week integration.** Check-in completed, S-1 onboarding done, S-4 issued, BAH or barracks status.
5. **Resource exposure.** What was the Marine introduced to? CCP, MFLC, chaplain, OSCAR.
6. **Any indicators since join.** Behavioral observations from squad leader, ER visits, pay issues, family issues.
7. **Stressor inventory.** Active stressors documented per Suicide Prevention guide critical-stressor list.
8. **Leadership action plan.** What the chain is doing in the next 30 days.
9. **Resource gaps.** What resources the Marine does not yet have but should.

## Sponsor Program Integration

The Marine Corps Sponsorship Program is the front-loaded version of the new-join elevation. A sponsor assigned before arrival, with documented pre-arrival contact, materially reduces the integration risk.

A new join arriving without a sponsor assigned is itself a documented FPC discussion item. The chain's failure to sponsor is the unit's risk, not the Marine's.

## Sponsorship and the Warm Handoff

Two records cover the same transition.

| Record | Owner | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| CIRRAS warm handoff | Outgoing CO before transfer | Force preservation context |
| Sponsor pre-arrival contact | Receiving unit sponsor | Logistical and social integration |

Together they constitute the transition documentation. A receiving CO who finds neither has been done is looking at a system failure on both ends.

## What "Discuss During FPCs" Means

The MCO 1500.60A text says the Marine "must be discussed during FPCs throughout this period." Three minimum FPCs over 90 days. Each one must touch the new join.

The discussion is not a status check disguised as a name read aloud. The pattern above gives the substance.

A FPC where the new-join list is read without discussion is a procedural compliance with no substantive effect. The MCO assumes substance.

## When the 90 Days End

At the 90-day mark, the chain reassesses based on actual observation.

| Indicator During Window | Likely Status at 90 Days |
|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Full integration, no flags, sponsor reported smooth transition | Return to LOW |
| Minor adjustment issues, one or two flags, resolved | Continue ELEVATED for 30 to 60 days, then reassess |
| Multiple flags, behavioral changes, stressors mounting | Maintain ELEVATED or escalate to HIGH |
| Any suicide-related event in window | HIGH and full Incident Playbook response |

The 90-day automatic period is the floor, not the ceiling. Many Marines remain ELEVATED beyond 90 days based on what the unit observes in practice.

## Common Failures

1. **Treating the rule as a default the receiving CO is authorized to override.** The MCO does not give the receiving CO override authority. Elevation is automatic.
2. **CIRRAS profile not updated to ELEVATED for the 90-day window.** The receiving unit imports MCTFS LOW and does not apply the elevation rule. Direct violation.
3. **New joins listed at FPC without substantive discussion.** Compliance theater. The MCO assumes substance.
4. **No sponsor assigned before arrival.** Common failure. Suicide Prevention guide PCS scenario directly addresses this.
5. **Lethal-means conversation skipped for new joins.** The Suicide Prevention guide explicitly directs SNCOs to have this conversation. Treats firearms, expired medications, and unsecured access points the same.
6. **De-escalation before 90 days based on Marine's own report.** Marines new to the unit reliably under-report stress to avoid appearing weak. The 90-day window is to let the chain observe, not to let the Marine declare wellness.
7. **PCS family separation not in active stressors.** Family in transit or at prior installation is a stressor by definition per the Suicide Prevention guide. Document it.

## Edge Cases

### Returning from deployment

A Marine returning from deployment to your unit (not PCS) does not automatically trigger the 90-day rule by transfer logic. Reintegration is its own discipline (see the Reintegration scenarios in the Suicide Prevention guide). Apply elevated attention per the guide pattern.

### Marine joined HIGH from prior unit

The 90-day rule does not downshift the HIGH determination. HIGH stays HIGH and is reassessed on the merits, not on the calendar.

### Marine joined ELEVATED for reasons documented in warm handoff

The 90-day automatic period does not reset the warm-handoff context. The Marine remains ELEVATED for the stated reasons. The 90-day rule overlays additional attention for the transition itself.

### Multiple joins in same month

The FPC discussion pattern applies to each one individually. Do not batch new joins into a single "all our new joins are doing fine" entry. Each one is discussed.
