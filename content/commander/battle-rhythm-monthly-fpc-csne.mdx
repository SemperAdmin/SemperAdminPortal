## What This Page Covers

This page is the steady-state monthly procedure for the FPC, the urinalysis approval, the DRRS-MC submission, and the Commander Standing Notification Events (CSNEs) driving out-of-cycle Readiness Boards.

For the deeper FPC framework (council role, attendance, SOP requirements, CIRRAS access rules), see the dedicated Force Preservation Council topic. This page is the recurring monthly procedure.

## The Monthly FPC in Steady-State

The first FPC happens within 30 days of CoC. The 12 to 24 FPCs in the period afterward follow the same shape.

### The standing checklist

| Step | Action | Who |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Confirm CO is chairing or document unavailability with MfR | CO |
| 2 | Open with prior council action items review | CO |
| 3 | New joins (90-day elevated window) | CO with S-1 |
| 4 | Marines flagged in CIRRAS at elevated or high risk | Owner of each profile |
| 5 | Reintegration cases (post-attempt, post-treatment, post-court) | Small-unit leader |
| 6 | CMG/SAPR overlap cases | CO |
| 7 | Cross-cutting trends or unit climate observations | XO, SgtMaj |
| 8 | Action items and assignments | All |
| 9 | CIRRAS update audit | CO |

A FPC where the new-join list is read without substantive discussion is procedural compliance with no effect. The MCO assumes substance.

### Recurring outputs

- CIRRAS profile updates for every Marine reviewed
- Council minutes
- MfR if CO did not chair
- Updated action item tracker

### Recurring inputs

- Marines briefed at last council for follow-up
- New joins since last council
- DoDI 6490.08 notifications received from providers since last council
- Open SAPR cases from CMG with FPC overlap
- Incidents since last council from Incident Playbook responses

## Random Urinalysis Schedule

You approve the monthly random urinalysis schedule per MCO 5300.17 and DoDI 1010.01. The unit SACO or UPL drafts the schedule. You review and approve.

### What approval covers

- Number of Marines tested
- Selection method (random per SACO procedures)
- Date and time blocks
- Special-population coverage if applicable

### Documentation

Signed schedule retained per local SOP. Test results documented in CIRRAS profile for any flagged Marine for the next FPC.

### Failure mode

The schedule is treated as a SACO-only document. CO signs without review. Selection methodology drifts. Inspector finding at IGMC FAC #31.

## DRRS-MC Reporting

You submit the monthly DRRS-MC readiness report with required commander's remarks per MCO 3000.13B.

### The report covers

- C-Level (overall capability)
- P-Level (personnel)
- S-Level (supply)
- R-Level (training)
- T-Level (equipment condition)

### Commander's remarks

Required when:

- Any subjective override applied to a category
- Trend change from prior month
- Specific issues affecting readiness (open FLIPLs, supply discrepancies, training shortfalls)
- New CSNE triggered or resolved

Remarks are not optional when an override is applied. The MCO requires rationale.

### Submission discipline

The DRRS-MC report is monthly. Submission window is set by higher headquarters. Late or missing submission is visible at HHQ level.

## CIRRAS Audit

Each monthly FPC audits CIRRAS for currency. The Force Preservation page details the discipline. The monthly version of the audit is:

- Every elevated or high profile reviewed for staleness (no update in 90 days = stale)
- Every new join confirmed at ELEVATED for the 90-day window
- Every profile-level access user confirmed as uniformed (quarterly full audit)
- Every FPC decision from the council carried into the affected profile within 5 days

A monthly cadence where CIRRAS is touched only at the FPC misses real-time event-driven updates. Profiles are updated when events happen, not waited for the monthly review.

## Commander Standing Notification Events (CSNEs)

Per the Commanders Readiness Handbook, CSNEs are predetermined readiness triggers requiring an out-of-cycle Readiness Board, regardless of the monthly cadence.

### Typical CSNEs

| Event | Triggering Threshold |
|-------|----------------------|
| Personnel readiness drop | P-Level falls below acceptable threshold for assigned mission |
| Supply readiness drop | S-Level falls below acceptable threshold |
| Equipment readiness drop | T-Level degradation crosses subjective override threshold |
| Training readiness drop | R-Level cannot support next training event |
| FLIPL above threshold | Open FLIPL value exceeds CO approval authority |
| Loss of mission-critical equipment | Inoperable or destroyed |
| AA&E discrepancy | Serial-number mismatch or unaccounted item |
| Significant injury or fatality affecting unit readiness | Per local SOP |
| Loss of certified personnel in a unique billet | Per local SOP |

### What CSNE Triggers

When a CSNE fires, you convene an out-of-cycle Readiness Board within the timeline set in your published FPC SOP and Readiness Board SOP. The board reviews the trigger, documents impact, and adjusts DRRS-MC reporting as needed.

The next monthly DRRS-MC report annotates the CSNE in commander's remarks.

### Documentation

Each CSNE generates:

- Memorandum for Record on the trigger
- Out-of-cycle Readiness Board minutes
- DRRS-MC annotation
- Adjustment to monthly FPC agenda if force-preservation implications

## Monthly Pattern Worth Establishing

A workable cadence the first 90 days after assumption:

- Week 1: SACO drafts urinalysis schedule. CO reviews and approves.
- Week 2: DRRS-MC report drafted by S-3 with commander's remarks reviewed by CO. Submitted.
- Week 3: FPC convened. CMG runs same day or back-to-back where possible.
- Week 4: CIRRAS audit. Action items from FPC tracked to completion.

Adjust to fit local rhythm. The constraint is: each item happens monthly.

## Common Failures

1. **CO approves urinalysis schedule without review.** SACO drift in methodology. Findings on IGMC FAC #31.
2. **DRRS-MC commander remarks left blank.** Subjective override applied without rationale. MCO 3000.13B violation.
3. **FPC and CMG scheduled to conflict.** Both monthly, both non-delegable. Coordinate the calendar.
4. **CIRRAS profiles updated only at FPC.** Misses event-driven updates between councils.
5. **CSNE-triggered Readiness Board not convened.** Trigger fired but cadence not adjusted. DRRS-MC report continues without addressing the trigger.
6. **MfR retained less than 3 years.** Filed and lost. MCO 1500.60A Para 4.b.(3)(l) violation.
7. **DRRS-MC submission late.** HHQ visibility on the deviation. Recurring lateness is documented.
