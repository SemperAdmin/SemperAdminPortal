## Three Pathways

A Marine is reduced in grade through one of three distinct pathways. Each has its own procedural rules.

| Pathway | Trigger | Maximum Reduction | Authority |
|---------|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| NJP under Article 15 | Adjudicated misconduct at NJP | One grade for E-4 and below (company grade NJP) | Imposing CO |
| Administrative reduction | Unsuitability for current grade | Per MCO P1400.32D | Battalion or higher CO |
| Court-martial sentence | Conviction at court-martial | Per the sentence | Convening authority |

The procedural rules differ. A reduction valid under one pathway is not necessarily valid under another.

## Pathway 1: NJP Reduction

Article 15 NJP allows the imposing CO to reduce a Marine in grade as part of the punishment. Authority limits depend on the imposing CO's grade.

### Authority limits by imposing CO grade

| Imposing CO | Maximum Reduction at NJP |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| O-3 / Capt company commander | One grade for E-4 and below |
| O-4 / Maj or above | One grade |
| O-5 / LtCol battalion CO | One grade |
| Flag officer | Per UCMJ Article 15 expanded limits |

Officer punishments are more limited. Consult the Manual for Courts-Martial for specifics on officer NJP.

### Procedural rules

The NJP reduction is documented in the UPB (NAVMC 10132) and on a Page 11 entry. The imposing CO signs the UPB. The reduction is effective on the date of the UPB unless suspended.

### Pay impact timing

| Reduction type | Pay change effective |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Reduction in grade, immediate | Next pay period after UD/MIPS posts |
| Reduction in grade, suspended | No pay change unless suspension vacated |
| Forfeiture of pay (NJP) | Next pay period after UD/MIPS posts |

Coordinate timing with S-1 to ensure pay impact matches the intent of the punishment.

### Appeal

The Marine has 5 days from NJP to appeal to the next superior commander. During the appeal period, the reduction stands but the Marine retains the appeal right. The superior commander has authority to set aside, mitigate, or affirm.

A reduction set aside on appeal is restored. The Marine returns to the prior grade with back pay.

## Pathway 2: Administrative Reduction

Administrative reduction is for Marines who do not meet the standards required for their current grade but where NJP is not the appropriate vehicle. Examples include failure to maintain qualifications, pattern of poor performance, or substandard conduct not warranting punitive action.

### Authority

Per MCO P1400.32D, administrative reduction authority resides at the battalion level for one-grade reductions of junior enlisted Marines. Multi-grade reductions or reductions of senior Marines require higher authority.

### Procedural rules

The Marine receives:

1. Written notification of the proposed reduction
2. Specific basis for the action
3. Opportunity to consult counsel
4. Opportunity to submit written rebuttal
5. Right to elect a board where the grade and action qualify
6. Right to appeal the decision through the chain

Skipping any of these is a procedural defect. The decision is voidable on review.

### Documentation

The administrative reduction is documented through:

- Written reduction directive signed by the CO
- Page 11 entry under MCO P1070.12
- OMPF entry via IPAC processing
- Pay status update via UD/MIPS

### Pay impact

Administrative reductions affect pay starting the next pay period after UD/MIPS processing, similar to NJP reductions.

## Pathway 3: Court-Martial Reduction

When a Marine is convicted at court-martial, the sentence often includes reduction in grade. The reduction takes effect per the sentence terms after convening authority action.

### Convening authority action

After conviction, the convening authority reviews the case and takes "action" on the sentence under RCM 1109 / the Military Justice Act provisions. The action options:

- Approve the sentence as adjudged
- Disapprove part of the sentence
- Defer reduction pending appellate review where applicable
- Mitigate the sentence

### Documentation

Court-martial reductions are documented through:

- Convening authority action memorandum
- Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12
- OMPF entry via IPAC
- Pay status update

## Promotion Eligibility After Reduction

A reduced Marine's promotion eligibility resets. The Marine returns to the promotion zone for the new grade and accrues time-in-grade from the reduction effective date.

Specific waiting periods after reduction vary by grade and reduction reason. MCO P1400.32D has the rules.

## When Reductions Become Permanent

| Pathway | Final on |
|---------|----------|
| NJP reduction | Date of UPB unless suspended. Final after appeal period or appeal decision. |
| Administrative reduction | Date of the directive after appeal period |
| Court-martial reduction | Date of convening authority action, subject to appellate review |

A suspended reduction is not in effect. Suspension is liftable (vacated) on subsequent misconduct during the suspension period, restoring the reduction.

## Reductions Outside the Three Pathways

Some grade changes look like reductions but are not.

- **Voluntary reversion** to retain a Marine for specific service - rare, requires M&RA process
- **Date-of-rank adjustment** due to records correction - administrative, not punitive
- **Grade restoration** from prior reduction set aside - administrative

These do not follow the three pathways above. Consult MCO P1400.32D for the specific rule.

## Common Failures

1. **Reduction at NJP exceeds CO authority.** Authority limits are by grade. A multi-grade reduction at company commander NJP is invalid.
2. **Administrative reduction without notification or rebuttal opportunity.** Procedural defect. Marine challenges and wins.
3. **Reduction documented but not routed to OMPF.** Lost at later separation processing.
4. **Pay change timing mismatch.** Reduction effective on date X but pay continues at old grade until UD/MIPS processes. Coordinate with S-1.
5. **Page 11 entry missing.** Reduction adjudged but no Page 11 documenting the basis. Future personnel decisions lose context.
6. **Reduction during appeal period acted on as final.** Wait for appeal period to close before treating as final.
7. **Confusing administrative reduction with NJP.** Each has different procedural requirements. Mismatch creates defect.

## Decision Framework Before Reducing

Before signing any reduction directive:

1. Which pathway applies?
2. Do I have signature authority for this pathway and grade?
3. Has the Marine been notified and given rebuttal opportunity (administrative) or appeal advisement (NJP)?
4. Has SJA reviewed the case where the reduction is contested or multi-grade?
5. Is the basis documented (prior counseling, evidence, witnesses, prior NJP record)?
6. Will the reduction route through S-1 to OMPF correctly?
7. When does pay change and does the timing match my intent?

A "no" on any of these means delay until corrected.
