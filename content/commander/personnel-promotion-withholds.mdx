## Three Commander Actions on Promotions

The CO interacts with promotion decisions in three ways.

| Action | Trigger | Effect |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Promotion withhold | Pending adverse action or investigation | Marine's promotion delayed pending resolution |
| Promotion removal recommendation | Conduct or performance issue post-selection | Recommend removal from selection list |
| Response to delay | Higher-headquarters delay | CO advocates for or against the delay |

A withhold is unit-level. A removal is M&RA-level via recommendation. A delay is set higher than the CO.

## Promotion Withhold

A CO has authority to withhold a Marine's promotion when there is pending adverse action or investigation affecting promotion eligibility. The withhold is administrative, not disciplinary.

### When a withhold is appropriate

| Trigger | Withhold Rationale |
|---------|--------------------|
| NJP pending | Outcome risks reduction or unsuitability finding |
| Court-martial referred | Conviction would change promotion eligibility |
| AdSep processing initiated | Outcome risks removal from the Corps |
| OSTC covered-offense case open | Disposition pending |
| Command investigation underway | Findings risk affecting eligibility |
| Substandard performance under documented review | Pattern requires resolution |
| Failure to maintain qualification required for the next grade | Standards issue |
| Pending PERB appeal on a prior matter | Resolution affects record |

### Procedure

1. **Written notification to the Marine.** Specific basis. Effective date of withhold. Expected duration.
2. **Opportunity for the Marine to consult counsel and submit rebuttal.**
3. **CO signs the withhold directive.**
4. **Documentation in MOL promotion module and via S-1.**
5. **Page 11 entry per MCO P1070.12.**
6. **Notification up the chain per local SOP.**
7. **Periodic review (typically every 30 days) of the basis.**

### Resolution paths

A withhold resolves in one of three ways:

- **Underlying action favorable to the Marine.** Withhold lifted. Marine promoted with backdated effective date if appropriate.
- **Underlying action adverse but not disqualifying.** Withhold lifted. Marine promoted with current effective date.
- **Underlying action disqualifying.** Withhold leads to removal recommendation or other adverse action.

### Common withhold failures

- Withhold imposed without written notification. Procedural defect.
- Withhold imposed without specific basis. Defect grounds.
- Withhold left in place after the underlying issue resolved. Marine challenges and wins.
- Withhold imposed on hearsay rather than verified fact. Defect.

## Promotion Removal Recommendation

A Marine selected for promotion but not yet promoted is subject to removal recommendation if conduct or performance disqualifies them.

### Authority

The CO recommends removal. The decision authority is M&RA per MCO P1400.32D. The path:

1. CO identifies the basis
2. CO notifies the Marine in writing
3. Marine has opportunity to consult counsel and submit rebuttal
4. CO submits removal package via the chain
5. M&RA / Commandant adjudicates
6. Result: Marine promoted (no removal), or Marine removed from selection list

### When removal is appropriate

- NJP imposed between selection and promotion with adverse impact
- Court-martial conviction between selection and promotion
- AdSep proceeding initiated and characterizing as adverse
- Substantiated misconduct discovered after selection
- Failure to maintain qualifications required for the next grade
- Pattern of substandard performance documented after selection

### Procedural rules

- Notification in writing
- Specific basis with supporting documentation
- Opportunity to consult counsel
- Rebuttal opportunity per MCO P1400.32D
- SJA review of the package before submission
- Routing through the chain

A removal recommendation without complete procedural support is sent back. The Marine remains on the selection list and the unit retains the obligation.

## Response to a Promotion Delay

Higher headquarters or M&RA sometimes delays a promotion administratively for reasons outside the unit's view (record completeness, board action timing, statutory requirements).

### CO's role

- Receive notification of the delay
- Communicate the delay to the Marine
- Confirm whether the delay is for reasons the unit needs to address (e.g., missing record items, expired qualification)
- Coordinate with S-1 and MMEA / MMPR on resolution
- Track the timeline

A delay resolving at higher level is not the CO's call. A delay caused by a unit-level records gap is the CO's responsibility to clear.

## OSTC Overlap on Promotion Actions

Per MARADMIN 633/24, Marines under OSTC case authority on a covered offense are not eligible for promotion change processing without OSTC and SJA coordination. The withhold path is the typical mechanism.

A Marine alleged to have committed a covered offense, with OSTC reviewing the case, should have a promotion withhold in place pending OSTC disposition. The withhold is administrative. The OSTC review is the underlying action.

## Marine's Appeal Path

Marines retain appeal rights on adverse promotion actions.

| Action | Appeal Path |
|--------|-------------|
| Withhold | Chain of command, then PERB if applicable |
| Removal | PERB or BCNR depending on procedural stage |
| Promotion eligibility dispute | MMEA / MMPR review |
| Records-based promotion issue | BCNR for record correction |

The Performance Evaluation Review Board (PERB) handles certain adverse personnel record matters. The Board for Correction of Naval Records (BCNR) handles record correction at a higher level.

## Documentation Set

For any promotion adverse action, the documentation includes:

- Written notification to Marine
- Rebuttal received from Marine (or signed declination)
- CO's adverse action directive
- Page 11 entry
- OMPF routing record via IPAC
- MOL promotion module entry
- SJA coordination memorandum (where applicable)
- Higher-headquarters routing record for removal cases

A withhold or removal without this documentation set is challengeable on procedural grounds.

## Common Failures

1. **Verbal withhold.** Without written notification and basis, the action is voidable.
2. **Removal recommendation without SJA review.** Procedural risk and likely return.
3. **Marine promoted while withhold remained on record.** Administrative confusion. The promotion stands or fails depending on circumstances.
4. **Withhold based on hearsay.** Defect grounds.
5. **Failure to notify chain.** Higher headquarters surprised at the next promotion board.
6. **OSTC overlap missed.** Marine under OSTC review promoted without coordination. MARADMIN 633/24 violation.
7. **Removal package incomplete.** Returned for completion. Marine in limbo.

## Decision Framework Before Initiating

Before signing any promotion adverse action:

1. Is there a specific, documented basis?
2. Have I notified the Marine in writing?
3. Has the Marine had opportunity to consult counsel and submit rebuttal?
4. For removal: has SJA reviewed the package?
5. Is there OSTC overlap requiring coordination?
6. Is the documentation routed to S-1, OMPF, and MOL?
7. What is the Marine's appeal path?

A "no" on any of these is grounds to delay until corrected.

## What This Topic Does Not Cover

- **Selection board actions.** Promotion selection happens at HQMC. The unit influences via FITREP, not by direct action on the board.
- **PERB appeals.** PERB is a process the Marine drives with unit support. Different procedural set.
- **Statutory promotion limits.** Time-in-grade, time-in-service requirements come from law and MCO P1400.32D. The CO does not waive them.
- **Promotion ceremonies.** Process and pomp, not adverse action.
