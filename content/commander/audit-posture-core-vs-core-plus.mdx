## Why Two Programs

The IGMC inspection program separates required-everywhere coverage from selective scope expansion. CoRE is the floor. CoRE+ adds layers driven by higher headquarters emphasis, unit mission, or trend findings across the Marine Corps.

A CO needs to know which program a given FAC falls under because the cadence, the scope expectation, and the corrective action visibility differ.

## CoRE - Core Required Coverage

CoRE is the standing program for required functional areas applicable across O-5 and O-6 commands. The CoRE FAC set is published on the IGMC portal and is consistent across units of the same type.

### Typical CoRE coverage

The CoRE coverage area generally includes the foundational compliance programs every Marine command runs.

- Records Management (NAVMC 10030, MarineNet RM training, CDRM)
- Equal Opportunity and Anti-Harassment
- SAPR program execution (when applicable to the unit level)
- Force Preservation Council
- Substance Abuse Program
- Safety and Mishap Reporting
- Records administration (UPB, FITREP, Page 11)
- Awards program
- Operational Security (OPSEC)
- Antiterrorism (ATO)
- Information Assurance / Security Manager
- Voting Assistance
- Family Readiness Program
- Substance Abuse Control Officer

### CoRE cadence

CoRE FACs roll on an annual or near-annual cycle on the IGMC inspection schedule. A unit should expect CoRE inspection coverage every cycle.

## CoRE+ - Extended and Mission-Specific Coverage

CoRE+ adds FACs driven by:

- Higher headquarters emphasis (MARFOR-specific FACs)
- Unit mission (aviation-specific, recruiting-specific, MSC/MSE-specific)
- Trend findings (areas where Marine Corps-wide audits have surfaced systemic issues)
- New policy implementation (when a MARADMIN or MCO change creates new audit content)

### Typical CoRE+ coverage

The CoRE+ coverage area shifts more than CoRE. Examples surfaced in CoRE+ scope at various times:

- Aviation Safety Officer program (aviation units)
- Recruiting Station leadership climate
- MAGTF integration readiness
- Cyber readiness and ISSM compliance depth
- Fund Control Tier 1 and Tier 2 training currency
- Capstone records management at Regiment / Group level and above
- Marine Forces Reserve mobilization readiness
- Wounded Warrior Regiment specific FACs

### CoRE+ cadence

CoRE+ FACs are scheduled by direction. They pair with a CoRE cycle visit or stand alone. The supporting IG announces the CoRE+ scope as part of the inspection notification.

## Scope by Command Type

The portal's inspections schema flags applicability levels including "All O-5 and O-6 Level Commands" and similar. The right way to determine FAC applicability is to check the FAC's stated applicability on the IGMC CoRE portal.

### O-5 commands (Battalion / Squadron)

- Full CoRE FAC scope applies
- CoRE+ FACs apply where unit mission or HHQ direction triggers them
- Inspection visits typically pair multiple FACs per visit

### O-6 commands (Regiment / Group / MEU)

- Full CoRE FAC scope plus Capstone records management
- CoRE+ FACs more frequently applicable due to broader mission scope
- Higher headquarters audits supplement IGMC visits

### Below O-5

Some FACs apply selectively. The applicability statement on each FAC is the controlling reference.

## Why the Distinction Matters for Your Audit Posture

Three reasons the CoRE vs CoRE+ distinction affects how you prepare.

1. **CoRE you walk every quarter.** CoRE is the floor. The full CoRE set should be walked at least once per year, ideally on a rotating quarterly schedule.
2. **CoRE+ you walk on notification.** When supporting IG announces CoRE+ scope, the relevant FACs jump to priority. Knowing they exist lets you pre-position the self-audit.
3. **Findings carry different weight.** Repeat findings on CoRE items are visible across multiple cycles. CoRE+ findings often surface emerging issues. Both matter, but the framing differs.

## How to Identify the FAC's Program

Each FAC on the IGMC CoRE portal has metadata indicating:

- Program number
- Title
- Sponsor (the HQMC staff section owning the FAC content)
- Applicability level (which commands the FAC applies to)
- Category (CoRE, CoRE+, or other)
- Last revision date

The portal's inspections surface at `/inspections/igmc` exposes the same metadata for the FACs it tracks. Use the category field as the quick check.

## Scope Changes Worth Tracking

CoRE+ scope shifts more than CoRE. Tracking the shifts is part of audit posture.

- New MARADMIN published creates a new FAC obligation. Watch for FAC updates on the CoRE portal.
- HHQ inspection emphasis shifts annually. The supporting IG typically previews focus areas.
- Mission change at the unit (new equipment, new mission set) triggers new applicable FACs.
- Personnel changes at the Inspector General office sometimes shift emphasis.

A CO who reads the IGMC CoRE portal quarterly notices the changes early. A CO who reads it only at announced inspections misses the lead time.

## Coordinating With the Supporting IG on Scope

Before an announced inspection, the supporting IG confirms:

- Specific FACs in scope for the visit
- Whether the visit is CoRE only, CoRE+ only, or both
- Time on each FAC
- Documents to pre-stage
- Personnel availability for interview

A unit asking these questions early gets clearer expectations. A unit waiting until the inspector arrives gets surprised by scope.

## Common Misunderstandings

1. **Treating CoRE as the only program.** Missing CoRE+ scope at announcement. Lost lead time.
2. **Assuming CoRE+ is optional.** Once scoped by supporting IG, CoRE+ is graded like CoRE.
3. **Thinking the FAC list is static.** FACs revise. Older revisions cited at audit are stale.
4. **Skipping the applicability check.** Walking FACs not applicable to the unit wastes time. Skipping applicable FACs creates findings.
5. **Treating CoRE+ as exotic.** CoRE+ is often where new MARADMIN compliance lives. Worth quarterly attention.

## The Portal as Reference Aid

The portal's inspections surface tracks 32 IGMC programs at the time of this writing. The portal is a reference aid, not a substitute for the IGMC CoRE portal itself.

Use the portal to:

- See which FACs the portal has digested into structured form
- Cross-reference FAC content with the portal's domain pages on the underlying topic
- Trace from a FAC item to the supporting MCO or MARADMIN

Use the IGMC CoRE portal for:

- The actual current FAC document
- The applicability statement
- The sponsor's revision history
- Any FAC not yet tracked by the portal
