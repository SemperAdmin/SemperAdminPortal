## What Section 6 Covers

Section 6 is the sustainment phase. Three groups of actions: 90-120 day post-CoC actions, annual recurring requirements, and the monthly and quarterly battle rhythm. These are the actions that, once established, define what command actually looks like day to day.

The 90-120 day actions complete the formal turnover. Annual and battle rhythm actions are the operational floor for the rest of your tenure.

## 90 to 120 Days Post-CoC

| Action | Authority |
|--------|-----------|
| Conduct CCA with DEOCS within 90 days. Document corrective action POA&M. Annotate in annual FITREP. | MARADMIN 468/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 468/23" /> Para 4; MCO 5354.1F<Citation source="MCO 5354.1F" /> |
| O5/O6: complete safety climate survey (Ground CAS or MCAS for aviation) to establish baseline. | MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" /> Para 060402 |
| Formally accept supply account or report discrepancies. 60-day window from assumption. Receive and review Supply Officer's endorsement report. | MCO 4400.201<Citation source="MCO 4400.201" /> Vol. 3 |
| NAVMC 10030<Citation source="NAVMC 10030" />: Complete and sign Commander's Records Management Acknowledgement Agreement within 90 days. Provide to CDRM for upload to CROSS. Enforce annual RM training (MarineNet M01RMT0700) for all personnel. | MCBul 5210 (29 Aug 2025) Para 3.b.(1)(a), Note 1; MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" /> |
| FPC SOP: Publish Force Preservation Council Standard Operating Procedures within 90 days of assuming command. Available to all assigned Marines. | MCO 1500.60A<Citation source="MCO 1500.60A" /> Para 4.b.(3)(n) |
| METL Review and HHQ Submission: Update Mission Essential Task List and submit to Higher Headquarters for approval. | MCO 3500.26A<Citation source="MCO 3500.26A" /> |
| SAPR Command Policy Statement published (if not done within first 30 days). | MCO 1752.5C<Citation source="MCO 1752.5C" /> Appendix B |
| Receive MMEA retention briefing. Confirm FTAP/STAP missions, boatspaces, CRP selectees. | MMEA Commanders Retention Toolkit |
| CDRM notification to HQMC ARDB if Regiment/Group level or above. 90-day window from appointment. | MCO 5210.11F<Citation source="MCO 5210.11F" />; CROSS End User Guide v1.7 |
| Submit Assumption of Command letter with any additional discrepancies. Provide negative response if no discrepancies found. | CMC White Letter 3-17; MCO 4400.201<Citation source="MCO 4400.201" /> Vol. 3 |
| Verify fiscal/appropriations law training and Budget Execution Course completion are current within 3 years for you and all Tier 1 Fund Control Personnel. | MARADMIN 350/11<Citation source="MARADMIN 350/11" /> Para 2 |

The 60-day Supply formal acceptance is the deadline that binds you to the count. Failing to formally accept within 60 days from assumption means the account remains contested. Discrepancies must be reported in writing during this window.

The "negative response if no discrepancies" rule under CMC White Letter 3-17 is often missed. Silence is not compliance. A negative response statement is required.

## Annual Recurring Requirements

| Action | Authority |
|--------|-----------|
| DEOCS/CCA: annually between 1 August and 30 November. Begin DEOCS by 31 October. Annotate in FITREP. | MARADMIN 468/23<Citation source="MARADMIN 468/23" /> Para 6 |
| Safety climate survey annually (O5/O6). | MCO 5100.29C<Citation source="MCO 5100.29C" /> Para 060402 |
| SAPR Annual Training: all personnel complete rank-appropriate training by credentialed SAPR VA or SARC. | MCO 1752.5C<Citation source="MCO 1752.5C" />; SAPR Smart Pack |
| Non-core CMT: biennial per MARADMIN 199/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 199/26" />. Track in MCTIMS. Core CMT remains annual. | MARADMIN 199/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 199/26" />; MCO 1500.63A<Citation source="MCO 1500.63A" /> |
| Annual full unit inventory synchronized with CMR. Wall-to-wall inventory with RO spot checks. | 2016 Commanders Materiel Readiness Handbook |
| Revalidate ALL key appointment letters. D-SAACP currency for SAPR VAs. Fund control training currency for CO and Tier 1 fiscal staff. | MCO 5800.16<Citation source="MCO 5800.16" /> Vol. 12; MARADMIN 350/11<Citation source="MARADMIN 350/11" /> |

The 1 Aug to 30 Nov annual DEOCS window is fixed. Beginning later than 31 October risks missing the FITREP annotation requirement.

## Ongoing Battle Rhythm: Monthly and Quarterly

| Action | Authority |
|--------|-----------|
| Attend monthly CMG for open SAPR Unrestricted Reports. NON-DELEGABLE. | MCO 1752.5C<Citation source="MCO 1752.5C" />; DoDI 6495.02<Citation source="DoDI 6495.02" /> |
| Update Marine who reported sexual assault on case status within 72 hours of each CMG. NON-DELEGABLE. | MCO 1752.5C<Citation source="MCO 1752.5C" /> |
| Chair monthly Force Preservation Council (FPC). CO reviews and validates all risk determinations. CIRRAS mandatory. If unavailable: Memorandum for Record required, retain 3 years. | MCO 1500.60A<Citation source="MCO 1500.60A" /> Para 4.b.(3)(b); 4.b.(3)(l) |
| Verify new joins (within last 90 days) have ELEVATED risk status in CIRRAS. Monitor during FPCs until 90-day window closes. | MCO 1500.60A<Citation source="MCO 1500.60A" /> Para 4.b.(3)(g)(3) |
| Approve random urinalysis schedule. | MCO 5300.17<Citation source="MCO 5300.17" />; DoDI 1010.01<Citation source="DoDI 1010.01" /> |
| Submit DRRS-MC readiness report with required commander's remarks. | MCO 3000.13B<Citation source="MCO 3000.13B" />; Commanders Readiness Handbook |
| QUARTERLY: Complete CMR reconciliations. Submit unfunded equipment deficiencies. Review GTCC delinquency. | 2016 Commanders Materiel Readiness Handbook; DoDI 5154.31<Citation source="DoDI 5154.31" /> Vol. 4 |
| QUARTERLY: Notify ARD Records of any changes to Capstone officials. Quarterly ETMS2 tasker update. | MCBul 5210 (29 Aug 2025) Para 3.b.(1)(a)(3) |

CMG attendance and the 72-hour case-status update to the reporting Marine are explicitly non-delegable under MCO 1752.5C<Citation source="MCO 1752.5C" />. XO substitution is not authorized.

The FPC unavailability rule under MCO 1500.60A<Citation source="MCO 1500.60A" /> Para 4.b.(3)(l) is a documentation requirement. Memorandum for Record stating why CO did not chair, retained 3 years. Habitual delegation to XO without MfR support is a finding.

## Joint Certification Block

The final page of the Master Turnover Checklist v4.0 is a joint certification.

### Outgoing CO Certifies

- Legal Foundation (Art. 0807)
- Pre-Departure Admin
- Supply Turnover
- Military Justice
- Readiness Turnover

Outgoing CO signs at or before the Change of Command ceremony.

### Incoming CO Certifies

- Pre-CoC Preparation
- Day 0-7
- Within 30 Days
- 90-120 Days
- Annual Requirements
- Battle Rhythm Established

Incoming CO signs after all 120-day obligations are fulfilled. Outgoing CO has departed command well before the incoming CO signs their block.

Both certifications, when complete, constitute the official record of command turnover.

## What This Document Is Not

The Master Turnover Checklist v4.0 is explicit on its own scope:

- Not exhaustive
- Verify all policy against MCPEL before use
- Contact supporting SJA, G-1 or S-1, and Inspector General for current guidance

Treat this section as the operational floor for the first 120 days of command. Higher headquarters and unit-specific SOPs add requirements on top. Updates to MARADMINs and MCOs occur during your command tour. Re-verify annually at minimum.

## Sustainment Self-Check

Quarterly, run through these questions:

1. CCA/DEOCS POA&M tracked and annotated for FITREP?
2. SAPR VA D-SAACP currency confirmed for every appointed VA?
3. Tier 1 fund control training current within 3 years?
4. CMT cadence in MCTIMS consistent with MCO 1500.63A<Citation source="MCO 1500.63A" /> and MARADMIN 199/26<Citation source="MARADMIN 199/26" />?
5. CIRRAS entries current for all Marines on the high-risk list?
6. Monthly CMG attended and case-status updates documented?
7. NAVMC 10030<Citation source="NAVMC 10030" /> RM training tracked for all personnel?
8. CMR quarterly reconciliations complete?
9. METL re-validated against HHQ updates?

Any "no" is the next quarter's first priority.
