## What Bridging Means

You stand between the Marine and the S-1, PAC, IPAC, or higher admin chain. Bridging is the procedural work of getting a package from the section into the right hand at the right step.

Three jobs.

- Decide whether to walk or send.
- Build a complete package before routing.
- Follow up to acceptance and to closure.

A Marine who shows up to S-1 with an incomplete package goes home. A Marine who shows up to the wrong office goes home. A Marine sent without a leader follow-up cadence ends up in a queue with no advocate.

## When the Bridge Fails

- Wrong office. Marine PCSes to S-1 with a question only PAC handles.
- Incomplete package. Marine carries an envelope missing two signatures.
- No handoff. Marine drops paperwork at a counter without naming a contact.
- No follow-up. Package sits two weeks while the Marine assumes it is moving.
- No escalation when stalled. Section accepts a 30-day delay because nobody pushed.

Each failure costs the Marine. Pay drops. Records lag. Boards see gaps. The fix is upstream of the package.
