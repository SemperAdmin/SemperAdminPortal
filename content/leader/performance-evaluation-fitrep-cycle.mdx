## Three Roles in the Cycle

- Reporting Senior, RS. Writes the report. First substantive evaluator.
- Reviewing Officer, RO. Reviews the RS submission, concurs or non-concurs, adds RO comparative assessment.
- Marine Reported On, MRO. The Marine receiving the evaluation.

The senior and junior relationship for performance evaluation purposes establishes the moment the new RS or new MRO checks aboard. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7 Ch 3" />

## Report Types

| Type | Trigger | Window |
|---|---|---|
| Annual | Anniversary of last report | Per MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" /> windows |
| Change of Reporting Senior | RS departs or MRO transfers | Inside the window after change |
| Change of Duty | MRO billet change, even within the same RS | Per the policy window |
| Reserve | Reserve duty thresholds met | Per MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" /> reserve provisions |
| Other Occasional | Specific triggers per MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" /> | Per policy |

The policy windows are not suggestions. A late report harms the MRO's record more than it harms the writer.

## The Initial Counseling Tie-In

MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" /> requires the initial counseling between RS and MRO. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7 Ch 3" /> MCO 1500.61<Citation source="MCO 1500.61" /> paragraph 4.c.(2) lists this counseling as one of the eight mandatory occasions. <Citation source="MCO 1500.61 4.c.(2)" /> The counseling branch covers the session mechanics. The 30-day deadline applies.

A FitRep cycle without the initial counseling is a system already failing.

## Calendar Discipline

Build a section calendar for every MRO under your responsibility.

| MRO | RS | RO | Last report end | Next report due | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Review weekly. A report inside 60 days of due is the time to start drafting, not the time to start panicking.

## When Reports Stack

Multiple MROs hit annual dates within the same window. Plan for it.

- Schedule the writing block on the calendar 45 days out.
- Pull notebook entries during the writing block, not when the report is due.
- Sit with each MRO before submission, not after.

A writer with 8 reports due in one month and no writing block scheduled produces 8 weak reports.

## Common Cycle Failures

- RS and MRO never sat for the initial counseling. The first FitRep arrives without context.
- Annual report drafted in the last 7 days. Quality drops. The MRO senses it.
- Occasional report skipped because the trigger felt minor. APES tracks the gap. The MRO eventually feels it at a board.
- Submission misses the window. PFC and OMPF retain the lateness. Future reviewers see it.
