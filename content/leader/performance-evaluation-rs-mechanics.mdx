## Read the Sections in Order

The FitRep is a structured document. Filling sections out of order produces internal contradictions you and the RO will both have to walk back.

Order of operations.

1. Section H billet description.
2. Section I attributes.
3. Section J duty descriptions if applicable.
4. Section K certifications and acknowledgments.
5. RS comparative assessment.
6. Narrative supporting the marks.

## Section H Billet Description

Describe what the MRO does, not who the MRO is. Three sentences.

- Position and primary duty.
- Scope of responsibility, people supervised, equipment owned.
- Key collateral or special assignments.

Section H frames every other section. A weak Section H produces marks the RO cannot defend.

## Attribute Marks

Per MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" />, each attribute carries a behavioral anchor for each mark level. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7 Ch 3" /> Score against the anchor, not against the curve in your section.

Three rules.

- Document the evidence for any mark above or below the anchor for "meets standard."
- Internal consistency. A high mark in leadership with a low mark in mission accomplishment carries explanation in the narrative.
- No surprises. Every mark the MRO sees should land where the counseling sessions across the cycle pointed.

## Comparative Assessment

The RS comparative assessment ranks the MRO against everyone the RS has reported on at this grade. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7 Ch 3" /> The assessment carries weight at boards. Inflation here corrupts the system across the Corps.

Three honest checks.

- How many MROs at this grade have I reported on?
- Where in that distribution does this MRO actually sit?
- What evidence sustains the placement?

A new RS with two MROs reported on cannot place a new MRO at the top of a long career distribution. Build the distribution honestly across years.

## Narrative

The narrative supports the marks. Three rules.

- Specific events. Date, action, outcome.
- One paragraph per attribute or per duty area.
- No fluff. The board reader scans for substance.

Avoid generic phrases. Avoid the same superlative on every MRO you report on. The board reader notices.

## Pre-Submission Sit-Down

The MRO sees the report before submission. The conversation looks like the JEPES sit-down at a higher rank. Walk the marks, walk the rationale, hand the MRO the rebuttal pathway if disagreement persists.

Submission with no pre-brief is the leading complaint at MRO redress.

## Where Submission Lands

APES routes the report to the RO. The RO either concurs and submits or non-concurs and returns. Plan for at least one revision cycle if the RO is engaged in the work.

## Common RS Failures

- Section H written last. Marks and narrative drift away from the actual billet.
- Mark inflation across the section. The RO learns to discount everything the RS submits.
- Comparative assessment placed without the distribution discipline. Board readers spot the pattern.
- Narrative full of phrases recycled from the last 10 reports. The MRO reads as anonymous.
