## What Makes a Report Adverse

MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" /> Chapter 4 defines the criteria. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7 Ch 4" /> The triggers include marks below standard on attributes, narrative content describing significant deficiencies, and specific patterns of concern in performance.

If you are unsure whether the report qualifies as adverse, treat it as adverse and run the procedural overlay. The cost of running the overlay on a non-adverse report is small. The cost of skipping the overlay on an adverse report is the report coming back at redress.

<Callout variant="danger" title="Procedural defects defeat the substance">
A FitRep with valid adverse marks and skipped notification or statement opportunity comes back at redress every time. The substance does not save a procedurally defective report. Run the overlay.
</Callout>

## The Notification Step

The MRO has the right to be notified of the adverse content before final submission. Notification is in writing. The notification covers:

- The specific marks or narrative content the RS considers adverse.
- The basis for the adverse evaluation. Specific events, dates, actions.
- The MRO's right to submit a written statement.
- The deadline for the statement.

Document the notification in the leader notebook with date and method. Verbal notification is not sufficient.

## The Statement Opportunity

The MRO has a right to submit a written statement to be filed with the report. The statement is not a rebuttal in the legal sense. The statement is the MRO's perspective filed alongside the RS write-up.

- Provide the policy window for statement preparation.
- Do not pressure the MRO to waive the statement.
- Do not pre-screen the statement content. The MRO writes what the MRO writes.
- File the statement with the report submission per APES routing.

A waived statement is documented. A waiver under pressure becomes a redress vulnerability.

## The Submission

The report submits with the MRO statement attached if filed. The RO reviews both. The chain processes per MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" />. Routing through S-1 and the appropriate level of the chain follows the policy.

## When Adverse Marks Surprise the MRO

If the MRO sees adverse marks for the first time at notification, the counseling cadence has failed across the cycle. Surprise indicates:

- Counseling sessions skipped or vacant of substance.
- Performance feedback withheld during the cycle.
- The cycle drifted to adverse without intervention.

The fix is the next cycle's counseling discipline, not the current report. Adjust your cadence.

## What Adverse Triggers Beyond the Report

An adverse FitRep can trigger additional command actions per the chain. Coordinate with S-1 and the commander before submission to ensure routing aligns with any disciplinary, screening, or administrative process running in parallel.

## Common Adverse Failures

- Notification verbal, not written. Redress finds the procedural gap.
- Statement window compressed below policy. Redress finds it.
- Statement read and informally returned for revision. Redress finds it.
- Adverse marks rendered without the cycle's counseling history to support them. Redress chips at the substance.
