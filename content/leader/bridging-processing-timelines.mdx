## Standard Windows

| Package | Standard PAC Window | Where to Verify |
|---|---|---|
| NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> dependent add | 10 working days | LES BAH change, DEERS, ID card |
| BAH start | 10 working days | LES |
| ID card issuance | 30 days from RAPIDS appt | RAPIDS receipt, ID card |
| OMPF entry from FitRep approval | 30 to 60 days | Marine Online OMPF |
| Award entry post-ceremony | 30 to 60 days | Marine Online Awards Branch |
| Page 11 OMPF entry | 30 days | Marine Online OMPF |
| Travel voucher payment | 14 days from approved voucher | LES travel pay |
| Reenlistment package | 30 to 60 days through chain | Approval message |
| Promotion warrant | 30 days post-board | OMPF, ceremony |

These are working days under nominal load. PCS season, deployment surge, and end-of-year audit periods extend the windows.

## The Five-Day UD Reporting Standard

A common section-leader anchor is the five working day reporting cycle for source documents into the Unit Diary. Once a source document arrives at the section, the admin section has five working days to report the action to MCTFS via UD/MIPS so the system reflects reality.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Verify with your local SOP">
The five working day cycle is the ideal. Local IPAC, S-1, or PAC standard operating procedures often adjust the window for unit-specific volume, audit cycles, or regional support arrangements. Confirm the active reporting standard with your supporting admin office before briefing Marines against a specific number.
</Callout>

## Brief the Marine Honestly

Tell the Marine the real window. Do not promise tomorrow.

- "Expect 10 working days."
- "I will check in with you at day 7."
- "If we are past 14 working days, we walk back to S-1 together."

A Marine briefed to expect 3 days who waits 10 calls you weak on follow-through. A Marine briefed to expect 10 who gets it in 5 thinks the system worked.

## When to Push at Day 7

A 7-day check is not a complaint to S-1. It is a section discipline.

- Pull the package status from S-1 contact.
- Confirm the package is in the queue.
- Identify any blocking issue.
- Coordinate corrections if needed.

Most blocking issues at day 7 are correctable. Most blocking issues at day 14 cost time you do not have.

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate when the package sits 14 days past the standard window with no movement.

- First. Named S-1 contact.
- Second. S-1 chief or OIC.
- Third. Battalion or unit S-1 if the section sits one level down.
- Fourth. Through the chain to commander engagement if the issue is structural.

Escalation is not an attack on S-1. The S-1 has volume. Your section's volume sits inside that. Escalation surfaces a stuck package the S-1 also wants to clear.

## Where Verification Lands

Each package type verifies in a different system.

- LES. Pay-impacting changes.
- DEERS. Dependent enrollment.
- Marine Online OMPF. Records entries.
- Marine Online Awards Branch. Awards entries.
- RAPIDS. ID card issuance.
- DTS. Travel voucher status.

Brief the Marine on which system to check and at what date.
