## The 24-Month Mark

At 24 months from EAS or retirement.

- Open the transition conversation.
- Surface intent. Reenlist, separate, retire.
- If separating or retiring, brief the timeline ahead.
- Begin planning education and credential pursuit using time still available.
- Coordinate with the career planner if reenlistment is on the table.

A Marine 24 months out who has never had a transition conversation has lost half the runway.

## The 12-Month Mark

Pre-Separation Counseling per the VOW Act starts at 365 days from EAS. <Citation source="VOW Act" />

- Verify the Marine schedules the Pre-Separation Counseling.
- Coordinate TRP enrollment.
- Coordinate any pre-EAS leave (terminal, transition).
- Brief the Marine on the medical, dental, and records audit cadence ahead.

Many Marines are surprised at the 12-month mark by the volume of work needed before EAS. Brief early.

## The 6-Month Mark

At 6 months from EAS.

- TRP completion verified.
- Credential plan documented.
- Final medical and dental exams scheduled.
- Records audit running.
- Terminal leave planned.
- Family preparing for the transition.

A Marine 6 months out without these in motion is behind schedule.

## TRP Coordination

Transition Readiness Program is mandatory per VOW Act and DoDI 1322.29<Citation source="DoDI 1322.29" />.

- Verify TRP enrollment date.
- Verify TRP completion.
- Brief the Marine on the value beyond the requirement.
- Coordinate with TRP staff for any specific Marine needs.

TRP covers VA benefits, employment workshop, financial planning, and pathway-specific tracks (employment, education, entrepreneurship, vocational training).

## Records Audit Before EAS

The records cleanup at the records-cleanup leaf applies in particular to Marines transitioning.

- All FitReps current.
- All awards entered.
- DD-214 components reviewed.
- Discharge characterization confirmed.
- Any pending administrative actions resolved.

A Marine who EASes with records gaps fights those gaps post-service for years.

## Terminal Leave Coordination

Terminal leave allows the Marine to start the next chapter while still on active duty pay.

- Calculate available leave balance.
- Schedule terminal leave dates.
- Submit the leave request.
- Coordinate sign-out with terminal leave aligned.

Brief the Marine on the practical mechanics. Some Marines mismanage terminal leave and either lose pay or miss the start of civilian employment.

## Family Transition

Family carries the transition with the Marine.

- Coordinate with Family Readiness on transition resources.
- Brief on healthcare transition (TRICARE to civilian).
- Brief on housing transition.
- Brief on the dependent ID and DEERS transition.

Family considerations often drive the transition timing more than the Marine's preference.

## Retirement-Specific Touchpoints

Marines retiring carry additional steps.

- Retirement physical at 6 to 12 months.
- VA disability claim at 6 months (BDD program).
- Retired pay election (REDUX, BRS continuation pay return).
- Survivor Benefit Plan election.
- Retirement ceremony coordination.

Each touchpoint has its own window. Coordinate with the career planner and retiree services.

## Common Transition Timeline Failures

- 24-month conversation never happened.
- TRP enrollment deferred until 90 days from EAS.
- Records audit run too late, gaps persist.
- Terminal leave mismanaged, civilian employment delayed.
- Family transition planning skipped.

## Where to Go Next

- Education planning for the TA and GI Bill use during the runway.
- Civilian credential matching for the post-service preparation.
- Records cadence and pre-board audit for the parallel records work.
