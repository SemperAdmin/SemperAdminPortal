## Approach With Sensitivity

A Marine going through a divorce or custody change is dealing with personal disruption. The documentation work matters but the human work matters more.

- Acknowledge the personal nature.
- Respect privacy on the underlying causes.
- Focus on the procedural support.
- Refer to chaplain or MFLC if helpful.
- Refer to legal for the legal side.

The documentation closes administrative loops. It does not close the human loop.

## Wait for the Final Decree

Common mistake: submitting paperwork on the petition or on the separation agreement.

- Petition is the start. Not actionable.
- Separation agreement covers the in-between. Not divorce.
- Final decree is the actionable document.

Submit the NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> with the certified final decree, not earlier documents.

## Required Documents Post-Decree

| Document | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Final divorce decree | Court | Certified, final |
| Custody order | Court | If children involved |
| NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> | Section, S-1 | Within 10 working days of decree |

Photocopies fail. Certified.

## What Changes on Submission

- BAH rate may change. With-dependent to without if no children, or stays with-dependent if Marine retains custody.
- Spouse loses ID card and DEERS access.
- TRICARE for spouse ends per CHCBP transition rules.
- SGLI beneficiary may need update.
- RED-S contact information may need update.

Walk each change with the Marine.

## Custody Considerations

If children are involved.

- The custody order documents the arrangement.
- BAH may stay at with-dependent rate if Marine has primary or shared custody.
- Family Care Plan may apply if the Marine becomes a sole parent.
- TRICARE for children continues per dependent enrollment.

A Marine becoming a sole parent due to divorce triggers the Family Care Plan requirement per MCO 1754.9A<Citation source="MCO 1754.9A" />.

## SGLI Beneficiary

Many Marines list spouse as primary SGLI beneficiary. Post-divorce.

- Marine reviews the beneficiary list.
- Marine decides whether to update.
- The decision is the Marine's.

A Marine post-divorce who never updates SGLI may have the ex-spouse as primary beneficiary at death.

## RED-S Update

The ex-spouse may have been the primary next of kin on RED-S. Post-divorce.

- Marine reviews the contact list.
- Marine updates as appropriate.
- Section verifies the update landed.

## Spouse ID and Base Access

After the divorce.

- Spouse turns in the ID card.
- DEERS deactivates the spouse.
- Base access for the spouse ends per local policy.

The Marine coordinates the ID return. Some bases require the Marine to physically retrieve the card.

## When the Marine Is in Crisis

A Marine in active divorce proceedings may carry significant emotional load.

- Refer to chaplain or MFLC.
- Coordinate with Force Preservation if risk indicators surface.
- Adjust counseling cadence.
- Watch for performance impact.

A Marine grieving a divorce who no one acknowledges drifts further.

## Common Divorce and Custody Failures

- Marine submits paperwork on petition or separation agreement, not final decree.
- BAH change runs slow because the Marine kept paperwork private.
- SGLI beneficiary never updated. Ex-spouse remains primary.
- RED-S still lists ex-spouse as PNOK.
- Family Care Plan gap surfaces at deployment alert.
