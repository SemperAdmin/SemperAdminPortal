## The Cadence Table

| Rank tier | Initial | First follow-on | Recurring minimum | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lance Corporal and below | Day 30 | Every 30 days | Every 30 days | NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> 2001.3 |
| Corporal through Colonel | Day 30 | Day 120, 90 days post-ICS | Every 6 months | NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> 2001.2 |
| Reservist | Day 30 | Every 3 months | Every 3 months plus once during AT | NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> 2001.3.f |

The 6-month figure is the floor, not the target. Drive a session more often when performance shifts or events stack up.

## Event-Driven Sessions

Per MCO 1500.61<Citation source="MCO 1500.61" /> paragraph 4.c.(2), a session fires on each of these.<Citation source="MCO 1500.61 4.c.(2)" />

- New senior or junior relationship is established.
- A FitRep is issued.
- A JEPES evaluation is rendered (the system replaced Pro and Con marks for the junior enlisted tier).
- Marine becomes eligible for promotion.
- Marine joins a new unit.
- Marine executes a PCS.
- Marine is assigned to Force Preservation.
- Marine takes a major change in billet responsibility.

Stack non-mandatory events on top.

- Birth or adoption.
- Marriage.
- Selection to a school or special program.
- Loss in the family.
- Disciplinary investigation initiated.
- Deployment alert.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Force Preservation triggers a session">
Once a Marine is flagged into Force Preservation, the leader counseling session is mandatory. Coordinate with the Force Preservation Council and the chaplain before the session.
</Callout>

## Lance Corporal Sessions Run Different

NCOs own the 30-day cadence for L/Cpl and below.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 2001.3.b" /> Sessions run 10 to 15 minutes. Blanket-on-the-footlocker style.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 2001.3.d" /> Strengths, weaknesses, one or two targets, set the next date.

Topics for the L/Cpl tier.

- LES review and pay literacy.
- Off-duty education and MarineNet progress.
- Family or personal goals.
- Upcoming events touching the Marine's life.

The cadence is non-negotiable. Missed L/Cpl sessions are the leading cause of new-Marine drift.
