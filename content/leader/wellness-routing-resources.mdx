## The Core Resource Set

| Resource | Use | Confidentiality |
|---|---|---|
| Chaplain | Spiritual, moral, life-transition, broad confidential support | Chaplain confidentiality |
| Behavioral Health | Clinical mental health, treatment | Clinical with exceptions |
| MFLC | Non-medical short-term counseling | Non-medical counselor |
| Family Advocacy Program (FAP) | Domestic violence, child abuse | Per program |
| SAPR | Sexual assault prevention and response | Restricted or unrestricted reporting |
| DAPA | Substance abuse prevention | Per program |
| NMCRS | Emergency financial assistance | Per program |
| Command Financial Specialist (CFS) | Confidential financial counseling | Per program |
| Personal Financial Management (PFMP) | Base-level financial counseling | Per program |
| EFMP | Special needs family coordination | Administrative |
| School Liaison Officer (SLO) | Dependent education | Administrative |
| Family Readiness Officer (FRO) | Unit family support | Administrative |
| Force Fitness Instructor (FFI) | Physical fitness coaching | Administrative |
| Career Planner | Reenlistment, lateral move, retention | Administrative |
| TRP | Transition Readiness Program | Administrative |
| Education Office | Voluntary education and credentials | Administrative |
| Legal Assistance | Personal legal matters | Attorney-client |

## Building the Section Contact List

For each resource, capture.

- Name of point of contact.
- Phone number (office and cell if appropriate).
- Email.
- Office location.
- Hours and walk-in availability.
- Referral process.

Print and laminate. Keep in the leader notebook. Update annually or when contacts change.

## When Multiple Resources Apply

Many indicators fit more than one resource.

- Marine in financial distress also showing mental health risk. Refer both CFS and Behavioral Health.
- Marine in family conflict also showing alcohol use. Refer both FAP/MFLC and DAPA.
- Marine returning from deployment with multiple readjustment indicators. Behavioral Health, Chaplain, FRO.

Refer to the primary indicator's resource first. Layer the secondary as the Marine engages.

## When the Marine Resists One Resource

If a Marine declines Behavioral Health, offer Chaplain.

- Lower threshold.
- Broader confidentiality.
- May refer to Behavioral Health with the Marine's consent later.

The Chaplain can be a bridge. Some Marines will not walk into Behavioral Health alone but will walk into the Chapel.

## Confidentiality Awareness

Brief the Marine on the confidentiality of each resource.

- Some are clinical with mandatory reporting on specific indicators.
- Some are non-medical without mandatory reporting.
- Some are administrative without confidentiality protection.
- Some have special protections (SAPR restricted reporting).

A Marine choosing the right resource for the situation depends on accurate confidentiality understanding.

## Routing Without Assumption

Some indicators look like one thing and are another.

- Financial distress may be hiding gambling addiction.
- Mental health withdrawal may be grief.
- Performance decline may be substance abuse.
- Family stress may be domestic violence.

Refer to the most likely fit. Trust the resource professional to identify other layers and refer accordingly.

## Coordinating With Chain

Some referrals warrant chain awareness, others do not.

- SAPR. Specific reporting protocols protect the survivor's choices.
- Behavioral Health. Chain awareness as appropriate to operational need.
- FAP. Chain awareness per program protocols.
- Financial. Chain awareness if operational impact (security clearance, deployment).

Brief the Marine on chain awareness before referring.

## Quarterly Section Resource Brief

Once a quarter, brief the section.

- The resource set.
- Specific points of contact.
- Confidentiality of each.
- Common indicators and the right routing.
- The Marine's choice in engaging.

Marines who know the resources before they need them use them earlier.

## Common Routing Failures

- Section leader does not know the resource set.
- Marine referred to wrong resource for the indicator.
- Confidentiality not briefed, Marine avoids the resource.
- Single referral made, no follow-up.
- Chain not coordinated when chain involvement matters.
