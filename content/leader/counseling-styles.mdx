## The Three Styles

Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 4001, three styles cover the field.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4001" />

### Directive

You analyze, diagnose, and lay out the plan. The Marine receives. Senior-centered.

- Use when the Marine is new, inexperienced, or unsure.
- Use when speed matters and the issue is concrete.
- Risk - the Marine never owns the problem or the solution.
- Risk - you fix the symptom and miss the cause.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Directive is not an order">
Directive counseling uses suggestion, explanation, persuasion, and demonstration. It pulls the Marine into the decision.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4001.1.f" /> An order shuts the conversation down.
</Callout>

### Nondirective

You ask questions, listen, and draw the Marine into self-analysis. Junior-centered.

- Use when the Marine has the maturity to think through the issue.
- Use when the issue is attitude, motivation, or long-term direction.
- Risk - the session rambles without senior guidance.
- Risk - the Marine without skill at self-reflection produces nothing.

The advantage of nondirective work is the Marine builds the muscle to solve future problems alone.

### Collaborative

A blend. You shift between directive and nondirective inside one session.

- Default style for most senior or junior pairings.
- Use directive moments for facts and standards.
- Use nondirective moments for attitude, motivation, and goals.
- Read the Marine in real time and adjust.

## Choosing the Style

| Marine condition | Issue type | Primary style |
|---|---|---|
| New, inexperienced | Concrete skill gap | Directive |
| Mid-experience, drifting | Attitude or focus | Nondirective |
| Performing, on-track | Goal setting | Collaborative |
| Struggling, defensive | Performance failure | Collaborative leaning directive |
| Mature, self-aware | Career direction | Nondirective |

## Two Failure Modes

- Locked-in directive. You run every session as a one-way brief. Marines stop owning anything. Performance plateaus.
- Locked-in nondirective. You ask open questions to a Lance Corporal with no framework. The session goes nowhere. Set the framework first, then open the questions.

## Style Versus Personality

The style is a tool, not a personality. Stay yourself. A senior performing an artificial style reads as insincere.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4001.4.a" /> Pick the style the situation requires, then deliver it in your voice.
