## What Wellness Covers

Beyond the formal Force Preservation Council referrals, wellness work covers the section's daily watchfulness.

- Financial distress indicators.
- Mental health risk indicators.
- Fraternization risk indicators.
- Substance abuse indicators.
- Family stress indicators.
- Command climate concerns.

You see these in counseling sessions, in daily section life, in performance shifts. The earlier you see them, the easier the intervention.

## The Spot-Route-Follow-Up Pattern

Three jobs.

- Spot. Recognize the indicator before it becomes the incident.
- Route. Connect the Marine with the right resource.
- Follow up. Verify the resource was used and the indicator is shifting.

Spotting without routing leaves the Marine alone with the problem. Routing without follow-up assumes the Marine engaged the resource.

## The Resource Map

| Indicator | Primary Resource | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Financial distress | Command Financial Specialist | Personal Financial Management Program, NMCRS |
| Mental health risk | Behavioral Health | Chaplain, MFLC |
| Substance abuse | DAPA | Substance Abuse Counseling Center |
| Domestic violence | Family Advocacy Program | Chaplain, legal |
| Sexual assault | SAPR Victim Advocate | SAPR program |
| Suicidal ideation | Behavioral Health, Chaplain | 988 / Veterans Crisis Line |
| Family stress | Family Readiness, MFLC | Chaplain |
| Spiritual concern | Chaplain | MFLC |
| Career or transition stress | Career Planner, TRP | Education Office |

The section's job is awareness of which resource fits which indicator.

## When the Indicator Is Acute

Acute indicators (suicidal ideation expressed, domestic violence in progress, immediate safety threat) do not wait for normal cadence.

- Engage immediate resource. Behavioral Health, chaplain, or 988.
- Coordinate with chain immediately.
- Document the action taken.
- Stay with the Marine until the resource has them.

The Force Preservation referral comes alongside, not before, the urgent action.

## When the Indicator Is Chronic

Chronic indicators (sustained financial distress, ongoing family stress, recurring discipline) follow the regular Force Preservation referral process.

- Document the pattern.
- Coordinate with chain.
- Submit the referral.
- Implement council recommendations.
- Track to closure.

## Confidentiality and Boundaries

Many wellness referrals carry confidentiality protections.

- Behavioral Health holds clinical confidentiality with specific exceptions.
- Chaplain holds chaplain confidentiality, broader than clinical.
- MFLC holds non-medical counselor confidentiality.
- SAPR has restricted reporting options.

Brief the Marine on the confidentiality protections of each resource before referring. A Marine who fears command involvement may avoid resources that could have helped.

## Documenting Wellness Work

In the leader notebook.

- Indicator observed.
- Resource referred to.
- Marine's response.
- Follow-up dates.

Do not document specific clinical content. Document the leadership work.

## Pattern Recognition Across the Section

Multiple Marines presenting similar indicators may surface a section or unit pattern.

- Multiple financial distress cases. Section may need broader financial brief.
- Multiple mental health referrals. Section may have an environmental issue.
- Multiple substance issues. Command may need to engage at unit level.

Surface patterns to the chain. Section work is leadership work, not just individual work.

## Common Wellness Failures

- Indicator dismissed because the Marine seemed fine.
- Referral made but never followed up.
- Acute indicator treated as chronic, urgency lost.
- Confidentiality not briefed, Marine avoids resources.
- Pattern recognition skipped.
