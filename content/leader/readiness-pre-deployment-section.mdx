## Why Pre-Deployment Section Readiness Matters

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10 and MCAP Enclosure 3, deployment success depends on members arriving ready. As section leader, you ensure this happens.

A Marine who reports for deployment unable to deploy.

- Wastes mobilization slot
- Disrupts unit timeline
- Creates additional admin burden
- May trigger involuntary actions

Your section readiness work prevents these problems.

## Five Readiness Domains

Per MCRAMM Chapter 10.

### Medical Readiness

- Current PHA (annual)
- Class 1 or Class 2 dental status
- Current immunizations
- No deployment-limiting medical conditions
- Specific deployment medications addressed

### Dental Readiness

- Class 1 (no treatment needed) or Class 2 (treatment can wait)
- Class 3 conditions blocked deployment
- Treatment current

### Administrative Readiness

- Current MCTFS data
- Current dependent data and DEERS enrollment
- Current SGLI election (recommended max coverage)
- Current RED with valid contacts
- Member personal records current
- Will and power of attorney updated

### Training Readiness

- MOS qualification current
- Required training complete (SAPR, INFOSEC, specific deployment training)
- PFT and CFT scores current
- Annual rifle qualification current
- Specific MOS training requirements met

### Family Readiness

- Family Care Plan if applicable
- Family briefed on deployment possibility
- Family support resources identified
- Member's family contact information current

## Performance Steps

### Step 1 - Receive Mobilization Notice

When unit gets alerted.

- Identify members in alert
- Establish timeline
- Begin section preparation

### Step 2 - Audit Each Marine

For each Marine in your section.

- Pull MCTFS readiness data
- Cross-check with MyMedical or equivalent
- Identify specific gaps per domain
- Document gaps

### Step 3 - Address Gaps

For each identified gap.

- Medical: Schedule appointments, address conditions
- Dental: Schedule treatment for Class 3
- Administrative: Update records, file documents
- Training: Complete required training
- Family: Coordinate Family Care Plan

### Step 4 - Track to Closure

Establish tracking for each Marine.

| Marine | Medical | Dental | Admin | Training | Family | Ready |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|----------|--------|-------|
| ...    | ...     | ...    | ...   | ...      | ...    | ...   |

Update weekly.

### Step 5 - Verify Final Readiness

Before reporting date.

- All gaps closed
- Each Marine cleared
- Documentation complete
- Brief commander on section status

### Step 6 - Brief Commander

Periodic readiness reports.

- Section ready vs not-ready members
- Specific gaps remaining
- Specific recommendations
- Resource needs

## Domain-Specific Coaching

### Medical Coaching

For Marines with medical needs.

- Brief on MTF appointment process
- Coordinate Reserve Health Readiness Program
- Specific civilian provider coordination if needed
- Track to completion

### Dental Coaching

For Marines in Class 3.

- Brief on Reserve Dental Care for deployment
- TDP enrollment if not enrolled
- Specific treatment coordination
- Track to Class 1 or Class 2

### Administrative Coaching

For Marines with admin gaps.

- Walk through specific updates needed
- Coordinate with S-1 for processing
- Specific document requirements
- Verify completion

### Training Coaching

For Marines missing training.

- Identify specific gaps
- Coordinate completion options
- AT or specific training periods
- Track to completion

### Family Coaching

For Marines requiring FCP.

- Brief on FCP requirements
- Identify caregiver
- Coordinate documentation
- Verify completion

## Family Care Plan Coordination

Per Marine Corps policy, specific Marines need Family Care Plan.

### Who Needs FCP

- Single parent
- Dual-military couple
- Specific other family situations

### FCP Components

- Caregiver identified
- Care arrangements during mobilization
- Financial arrangements
- Specific contingency plans

### Your Coaching Role

- Identify Marines requiring FCP
- Brief on requirements
- Coordinate completion
- Verify documentation

## Common Section Readiness Issues

### PHA Overdue

Marine's annual PHA overdue.

- Schedule promptly
- Coordinate MTF or provider
- Track to completion

### Class 3 Dental

Marine in Class 3 status.

- Coordinate treatment
- Reserve Dental Care if needed
- Track until Class 1 or Class 2

### Required Training Gaps

Marine missing required training.

- Identify gaps (SAPR, INFOSEC, specific training)
- Coordinate completion
- Online vs in-person options

### Admin Gaps

Marine's records out of date.

- Specific items to update
- Coordinate via S-1
- Verify completion

### Family Issues

Marine's family situation creating concerns.

- Specific support resources
- Family Readiness Officer engagement
- Specific accommodations

### Member's Personal Affairs

Marine without will or power of attorney.

- Coordinate with legal services
- Specific personal affairs preparation
- Member acknowledges

## Pre-Deployment Counseling

Specific counseling for each Marine.

### Initial Brief

When mobilization announced.

- Mobilization details
- Timeline expectations
- Specific preparation needed
- Family briefing required

### Mid-Prep Check-In

Approximately 30 days into prep.

- Status of gap closure
- Specific concerns
- Family preparation
- Personal affairs

### Final Pre-Reporting Brief

Just before reporting.

- All gaps closed
- Family briefed
- Personal affairs ready
- Specific final coordination

## Coordination Touchpoints

Section readiness touches.

- **MTF**: For medical and dental
- **Reserve Health Readiness Program**: Reserve-specific medical
- **S-1 admin**: For admin updates
- **Career Planner**: For training coordination
- **Family Readiness Officer**: For family programs
- **Legal Services**: For will and POA
- **Commander**: For section readiness briefings

## Common Pre-Deployment Pitfalls

### Late Notification

Marines learn of mobilization too late.

- Maximize available time
- Prioritize critical gaps
- Coordinate with command on extensions

### Family Surprise

Family unaware of deployment possibility.

- Brief Marine to brief family
- Family Readiness Officer engagement
- Specific family resources

### Specific Marine Unwilling

Marine doesn't want to deploy.

- Specific procedures
- SJA coordination if needed
- Specific consequences

### Resource Constraints

Limited resources for medical or dental.

- Prioritize critical gaps
- Specific resource coordination
- Coordinate with command

## Related Roles

Section readiness affects every level.

- **Marines** maintain personal readiness across five domains.
- **Admin** at I&I and PAC processes administrative actions.
- **Commanders** receive section readiness briefings and make decisions.

Your section's readiness is the unit's readiness.
