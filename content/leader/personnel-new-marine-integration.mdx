## Why New Marine Integration Matters

The first 90 days at a new unit shape a Marine's experience. Per MCO 5000.14D<Citation source="MCO 5000.14D" /> Enclosure 3, leaders own the integration of new Marines into their sections.

Strong integration produces.

- Higher retention
- Faster productivity
- Better section cohesion
- Stronger family connection
- Better morale

Weak integration produces.

- Higher attrition
- Slower productivity
- Member feeling unsupported
- Family disconnection
- Lower morale

## Two Types of New Marines

Different integration approaches.

### Non-Prior Service (NPS)

First-tour Marines fresh from MOS school.

- Recent boot camp graduates
- New to drilling life (for SMCR)
- Often young and inexperienced
- Need significant orientation

### Prior Service (PS)

Marines with previous active duty or other service.

- Often experienced Marines
- Familiar with military but new to your unit
- May need orientation to unit culture
- Different integration approach

## Performance Steps

### Step 1 - Pre-Arrival Coordination

Before Marine arrives.

- Notification from S-1 of incoming Marine
- Pull member's records
- Review prior service history
- Identify mentor
- Prepare welcome packet

### Step 2 - Welcome Marine

When Marine arrives.

- Personal welcome
- Tour of section spaces
- Introduce to section leadership
- Brief on initial expectations

### Step 3 - Initial Counseling

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" /> (IRAM).

- Within first 30 days
- Page 11 documenting initial counseling
- Brief on section expectations
- Set initial goals
- Member acknowledges

### Step 4 - Section Expectations Brief

Specific section standards.

- Performance expectations
- Conduct expectations
- Specific section culture
- Specific section history

### Step 5 - Pair with Mentor

For new Marine.

- Identify appropriate mentor
- Brief mentor on responsibilities
- Establish mentor-mentee relationship
- Set first meeting

### Step 6 - Family Integration

For Marine with family.

- Welcome family
- Brief family on unit
- Connect with Family Readiness Officer
- Provide family resources

### Step 7 - Track Integration Progress

Throughout first 90 days.

- 30-day check-in
- 60-day check-in
- 90-day formal review
- Adjust as needed

## Initial Counseling Per IRAM

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" />.

### Required Elements

- Welcome and introductions
- Brief on section
- Brief on commander's intent
- Set initial goals
- Identify member's career goals
- Address any immediate concerns
- Member signs Page 11

### Topics to Cover

- Section history and traditions
- Section standard operating procedures
- Performance expectations
- Conduct expectations
- Career counseling overview
- Available support resources
- Member's specific situation

### Follow-Up

- Schedule next counseling
- Specific follow-up actions
- Track member's progress

## Mentorship Pairing

Per Marine Corps leadership best practices.

### Selecting Mentor

For NPS Marine.

- Slightly senior rank typically (NCO mentoring junior enlisted)
- Same MOS or similar specialty
- Strong performer
- Willing and able mentor

For PS Marine.

- Mentor with similar experience level
- Knowledge of unit culture
- Helps PS member integrate

### Mentor Responsibilities

- Available for questions
- Career guidance
- Section knowledge transfer
- Personal support
- Specific check-ins

### Setting Up Relationship

- Introduce mentor and mentee formally
- Set first meeting time
- Establish meeting cadence
- Specific initial topics

- Family's connection to unit

- Family Readiness Officer assignment
- Family briefing scheduled

- Specific local resources

## 30-60-90 Day Tracking

### 30-Day Check-In

- Brief member on first 30 days
- Identify any issues
- Verify integration progress
- Page 11 if significant

### 60-Day Check-In

- Continued integration assessment
- Specific career discussion
- Mentor relationship check
- Family integration check

### 90-Day Formal Review

- Page 11 documenting first 90 days
- Member's performance assessment
- Career counseling
- Goal setting for next quarter
- Specific recommendations

## Common New Marine Issues

### NPS Marine Overwhelmed

Recent boot camp graduate finding civilian-Reserve life challenging.

- Specific support
- Connect with peers
- Mentor engagement
- Family support

### PS Marine Adjustment

PS Marine adjusting to new unit culture.

- Brief on unit culture
- Specific orientation
- Mentor pairing with similar background
- Avoid micromanaging experienced Marine

### Family Concerns

New Marine's family struggling.

- Family Readiness Officer engagement
- Specific resources
- Specific support
- Coordinate with member

### Civilian Job Conflicts

Member's civilian employment creating issues.

- USERRA brief
- Specific accommodations
- Coordinate with command

### MOS Concerns

Member uncertain about MOS or section.

- Career counseling
- Specific options discussion
- Lateral move consideration if appropriate

## Section Welcome Standards

What every new Marine should experience.

### First Day

- Personal welcome by section leadership
- Tour of spaces
- Introduction to mentor
- Brief on immediate expectations
- Family briefing scheduled

### First Week

- Initial counseling
- Section expectations brief
- Member's initial assignments
- Mentor first meeting
- Family welcome

### First Month

- 30-day check-in
- Continued integration
- Specific issues addressed
- Member feeling welcomed

### First 90 Days

- Member fully integrated
- Performing in section role
- Family connected
- Career goals established

## Related Roles

New Marine integration connects through chain.

- **Marines** (new arrivals) integrate into section.
- **Admin** at I&I processes initial admin.
- **Commanders** receive integration briefings.

Your integration work sets the conditions for the new Marine's career success in your unit.
