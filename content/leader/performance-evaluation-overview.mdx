## The Two Systems

| System | Audience | Owner | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| JEPES | Pvt through Sgt (E-1 to E-5) | Section leader, NCO | Per current MARADMIN |
| FitRep | SSgt and above (E-6 to O-10) | Reporting Senior, Reviewing Officer | Annual plus event-driven |

JEPES replaced the Pro and Con marks system for the junior enlisted tier. FitRep is unchanged in structure under MCO 1610.7<Citation source="MCO 1610.7" />. <Citation source="MCO 1610.7" />

<Callout variant="warning" title="Verify the active JEPES MARADMIN">
The JEPES program rolled out through MARADMIN updates that supersede each other on cycle. Confirm the active MARADMIN number with S-1 before citing JEPES policy in counseling notes or appeals. The structure below is stable, the citation number is not.
</Callout>

## Your Role on the Writer Side

If you write reports, you own:

- The billet description and accomplishments narrative.
- Honest, observable evaluation marks.
- The on-time submission into APES or MROWS.
- The pre-submission counseling with the Marine Reported On.

If the Marine reported on is your direct subordinate, you are the writer.

## Your Role on the Reviewer Side

If you review reports, you own:

- Reading every word the writer submitted.
- Concurring or non-concurring with substantive justification.
- Adding the Reviewing Officer comparative assessment where required.
- Returning a non-concurrence to the writer with specific corrections.

A SNCO reviewing an NCO's JEPES write-up sits in the reviewer role. A field grade officer reviewing a SNCO's FitRep sits in the reviewer role.

## How Counseling Connects

MCO 1500.61<Citation source="MCO 1500.61" /> paragraph 4.c.(2) lists FitRep issuance and JEPES rendering among the eight mandatory counseling occasions. <Citation source="MCO 1500.61 4.c.(2)" /> Every report you submit triggers a counseling session. The counseling branch covers the conversation. This branch covers the report mechanics.

## Common Failure Points

- Writer submits without sitting with the Marine first. The Marine reads the report cold. Trust drops.
- Writer carries a halo from one event into every mark. Read the counseling pitfalls leaf for the counter.
- Reviewer rubber-stamps. The writer learns to inflate. The system breaks for the entire section.
- Late submission into APES. Records lag. Promotion boards misfire on incomplete records.
