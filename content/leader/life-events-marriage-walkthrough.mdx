## Pre-Wedding Brief

When you learn a Marine is engaged, brief on the documentation sequence.

- Get the certified marriage certificate from the issuing county within 7 days post-wedding.
- Submit NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> within 10 working days of the marriage.
- Schedule RAPIDS appointment for spouse ID inside 30 days.
- Verify BAH change on next LES.
- Update SGLI beneficiary if changing.

A Marine engaged who hears this brief shows up post-wedding ready to execute. A Marine who learns this for the first time after the wedding loses the 10-day window.

## Required Documents

| Document | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Certified marriage certificate | Issuing county | Original or certified copy with seal |
| Spouse SSN | Spouse | Verify the number |
| Spouse photo ID | Spouse | For DEERS verification |
| NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> | Section, S-1 | Member and spouse signatures, witness |

Photocopies fail. Certified means embossed seal or color stamp.

## Walking the NAVMC 10922

Sit with the Marine to complete the form.

- Member identifying data verified.
- Marriage event data captured (date, location).
- Spouse data captured (name, SSN, date of birth, citizenship).
- Member and spouse signatures.
- Witness signature.

Do not send the Marine to S-1 with a partially completed form.

## RAPIDS Coordination

The spouse ID card requires a RAPIDS appointment.

- The Marine schedules.
- The Marine and spouse both attend.
- DEERS enrollment happens at the appointment.
- ID card issued at the appointment.

Coach the Marine to schedule inside 30 days of the marriage.

## BAH Verification

BAH at the with-dependent rate kicks in effective the marriage date.

- Verify the LES at the next pay cycle reflects the with-dependent BAH.
- If the LES does not reflect the change, walk to S-1 with the Marine.
- The change effective date should be the marriage date, not the submission date.

A BAH change taking 60 days to reflect on LES has a routing problem. Push.

## SGLI Beneficiary Update

The marriage may or may not change the Marine's SGLI beneficiary preferences. Brief the Marine.

- Current SGLI beneficiary on file.
- Whether the spouse should be added or replace the existing.
- Children from prior relationships affecting the choice.
- The decision is the Marine's.

The Marine submits the SGLV-8286 update through S-1.

## RED-S Update

Post-marriage, RED-S contact information needs an update if the spouse becomes the primary next of kin.

- Coach the Marine to update on Marine Online.
- Verify the update landed.

## Common Marriage Walkthrough Failures

- Marine submits photocopy of the marriage certificate. Returns from S-1.
- 10-day window slips because the Marine waited for "free time."
- Spouse ID appointment scheduled 60 days out, DEERS gap blocks medical care.
- BAH change submitted but the section never verifies the LES.
- SGLI never updated despite the Marine intending to.
