## What Fraternization Covers

Per current Marine Corps policy, fraternization covers relationships across ranks that compromise the chain of command, fairness, good order, or authority. The policy applies to.

- Officer to enlisted relationships.
- SNCO to junior enlisted relationships.
- Mentorship relationships outside the chain that drift toward inappropriate.
- Business relationships between seniors and juniors.
- Personal relationships affecting duty.

The policy adapts to the Corps. Specific prohibited conduct lives in MCO 1700.32<Citation source="MCO 1700.32" /> and reinforced through orders and case law.

## Indicators You Watch For

In the section.

- Off-duty social interactions between Marines of substantially different rank that exclude peers.
- Senior Marine showing favoritism to a junior in section assignments.
- Marines from different rank tiers riding together off-duty regularly without unit reason.
- Marines from different rank tiers sharing housing without unit reason.
- Senior Marine engaging in business arrangements (loans, sales, services) with a junior.
- Pattern of senior Marine intervening on junior's behalf outside normal chain.
- Social media indicating relationships outside policy bounds.

A single observation may have a non-fraternization explanation. A pattern warrants attention.

## Addressing Early

When you observe an indicator.

- Pull the senior aside privately.
- Describe what you observed.
- Brief on the policy.
- Confirm the senior understands.
- Document the conversation.

Most early conversations resolve the issue without escalation. The senior corrects the conduct. The pattern stops.

## When the Pattern Persists

If observed again after the initial conversation.

- Document with date, observation, and prior conversation.
- Coordinate with chain.
- Brief legal on the pattern.
- The chain may direct formal investigation or admin action.

Continued conduct after a leader's brief signals deliberate disregard.

## Specific Higher-Risk Patterns

Three patterns warrant immediate escalation.

- Sexual or romantic relationship across ranks. Violates policy and may violate UCMJ Article 134 depending on circumstances.
- Power-imbalance relationship suggesting coercion. May involve other UCMJ articles.
- Business relationship affecting duty assignments or fairness.

These patterns engage legal and command immediately, not the leader's informal conversation.

## When You Are the One Concerned About

Self-check.

- Are you maintaining bright-line professional conduct with juniors?
- Are you treating section members fairly across rank?
- Are you engaging in any conduct you would not want documented?

A leader's example sets the section's posture. Self-discipline upstream prevents downstream problems.

## When the Junior Reports

If a junior Marine reports concerning conduct involving a senior.

- Take the report seriously.
- Document.
- Coordinate with chain and legal immediately.
- Protect the junior from retaliation.

Junior Marines report concerning conduct only with significant courage. The system has to honor that.

## Common Fraternization Recognition Failures

- Pattern dismissed as friendship.
- Initial conversation skipped, jumping straight to escalation when pattern resurfaces.
- Conversation held but never documented.
- Junior's report not taken seriously.
- Leader's own conduct not self-checked.
