## Five Career Conversations

The leader runs five conversations with every Marine across a career.

- Reenlistment. Reup or transition decision at the end of each contract.
- Lateral move. MOS change to support career growth or Corps need.
- Transition. EAS approach planning at the 24, 12, and 6 month marks.
- Education. TA, GI Bill, MarineNet, MCU planning.
- Credential matching. Civilian-side qualifications the Marine should pursue.

Each conversation has a window. Miss the window and the Marine loses the leverage of the timing.

## Career Phase Awareness

| Phase | Key Conversations |
|---|---|
| First-term L/Cpl through Cpl | Initial career goals, PME, first reenlistment |
| Mid-career Sgt through SSgt | Lateral moves, advanced PME, second reenlistment |
| Senior SNCO | Senior PME, billet variety, retirement timeline |
| Career officer | Resident schools, joint duty, command opportunities |

Section leaders read each Marine's career phase and run the right conversation.

## Coordinate, Do Not Replicate

Career coaching coordinates with the unit Career Planner, the TRP, and education services.

- Career Planner owns the formal reenlistment conversation.
- TRP owns the formal transition counseling at 365 days from EAS.
- Education services own the TA and GI Bill mechanics.

Your role pulls the Marine into those conversations at the right time and supports the Marine's decision-making with the personal relationship the formal channels do not have.

## Career Intent in the Notebook

For each Marine, document.

- Stated career goal.
- Reenlistment intent if known.
- Education plan if articulated.
- Lateral move interest.
- EAS intent and post-Corps plan.

Update at every counseling cycle. The intent shifts as the Marine grows.

## When the Marine Has No Plan

A Marine without an articulated career plan is the Marine you spend the most time with.

- Counseling sessions surface goals through questioning.
- The six functional areas Future leaf provides the framework.
- The SMART goals leaf provides the structure.

Walk the Marine through the question of where they want to be in 5 years. Build the plan from there.

## When the Marine's Plan Diverges From the Section's Need

Sometimes the Marine wants a path the section or the Corps cannot support.

- Lateral move to an over-strength MOS.
- Education plan that conflicts with deployment cycle.
- Transition timeline that conflicts with billet need.

Surface the conflict early. Coordinate alternatives. Some conflicts resolve. Some require the Marine to choose.

## Common Career Coaching Failures

- Reenlistment conversation deferred until 30 days from EAS. Marine has no leverage.
- Transition counseling skipped or delegated entirely to TRP.
- Education plan not surfaced until the Marine is at the Education Office alone.
- Lateral move opportunities missed because no one asked.
- Marine retires or separates without a documented plan.

## Where to Go Next

- Reenlistment conversation for the FTAP and STAP windows.
- Lateral move advising for the MOS change path.
- Transition timeline for the EAS approach.
- Education planning for the TA and GI Bill use.
- Civilian credential matching for the post-service preparation.
