## What Allotments Do

Allotments deduct from the Marine's pay and route to a designated recipient.

- Family support (spouse, ex-spouse per court order, dependent care).
- Savings (TSP outside the standard, banks).
- Debt repayment (auto loans, mortgages where allotment is the payment method).
- Unit charitable contributions.
- Insurance premiums.
- Other allowed types per FMR Volume 7A Chapter 42.

The Marine controls allotments. The section reviews for currency.

## Annual Allotment Review

At annual counseling.

- Pull the LES allotment section.
- Walk each allotment with the Marine.
- For each, confirm: still active need, correct amount, correct recipient.
- Identify any allotment to discontinue.

A Marine with an allotment to a paid-off auto loan is a Marine losing money every month to a lender taking it.

## Common Allotments to Discontinue

- Auto loan paid off but allotment still running.
- Subscription service no longer used.
- Insurance policy lapsed but premium still deducting.
- Court-ordered support that has expired (children aged out).
- Charity gift the Marine no longer wishes to support.

The Marine submits the discontinuation per FMR procedures.

## Life-Event-Driven Allotment Changes

Marriage.

- Spouse may need allotment for shared finances.
- Existing allotment to a parent or other family member may shift priority.

Divorce.

- Court-ordered spousal support allotment may begin.
- Existing spousal allotment may end.

Birth.

- Childcare allotment may begin.
- Savings allotment for the child's future may begin.

Death of a dependent.

- Existing dependent-related allotments end.

Each life event prompts an allotment review.

## Court-Ordered Support

Court-ordered child or spousal support runs through allotment per the court order.

- The Marine submits the allotment per court order documentation.
- Failure to submit creates legal exposure.
- Adjusting the allotment without court order modification creates legal exposure.

Coordinate with legal if questions arise.

## Coaching the Marine on Adding or Removing

The Marine's responsibility. Section coaches.

- Brief on the allotment process via myPay or NSIPS.
- Brief on the timing (allotment changes typically take effect the following pay period).
- Confirm the Marine knows how to verify the change.

## Special Pay Considerations

Some allotments depend on special pay. If special pay ends, the allotment may run against base pay.

- Brief the Marine on the impact.
- Adjust the allotment if needed.

## Common Allotment Review Failures

- Annual review skipped, dead allotments run for years.
- Court-ordered support not started timely.
- Court-ordered support continued past expiration.
- Marine never briefed on self-management.
- Section assumes allotments are clean because the LES looks normal.
