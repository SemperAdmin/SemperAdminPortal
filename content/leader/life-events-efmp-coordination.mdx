## What EFMP Does

Exceptional Family Member Program enrolls Marines whose family members have specific medical, educational, or developmental needs.

- Limits PCS assignments to locations where the family's needs can be supported.
- Connects the family with services at the duty station.
- Protects the family from assignment to locations without appropriate support.

A Marine with EFMP-eligible family members who is not enrolled has a system gap that surfaces at the worst time, usually a PCS to an unsupportable location.

## Eligibility Indicators

The EFMP coordinator determines eligibility. Common indicators surface in counseling.

- Family member with chronic medical condition requiring specialist care.
- Family member with developmental delay or special education needs.
- Family member with mental health condition requiring ongoing care.
- Family member with mobility or accessibility needs.

If the Marine mentions any of these, route to the EFMP coordinator for an enrollment evaluation.

## Surfacing in Counseling

Use the family functional area in counseling sessions.

- Ask about family medical conditions.
- Ask about school situations for school-age children.
- Ask about specialist care needs.
- Listen for anything affecting PCS choice or duty station preference.

A Marine who has not heard of EFMP and has an eligible family member has been carrying the burden alone.

## Coordinating Enrollment

The Marine drives the enrollment with EFMP coordinator support.

- Refer the Marine to the EFMP coordinator.
- Brief the Marine on what the coordinator will need (medical documentation, school records, specialist letters).
- Coordinate any leave needed for medical appointments supporting the application.
- Track the enrollment to completion.

## Enrollment Levels

Per current EFMP policy, enrollment levels classify the family's support needs.

- Active enrollment. The family receives services and assignment limitations apply.
- Inactive enrollment. Family is enrolled but does not currently require limitations.

Verify the Marine's level annually. Levels change as family conditions change.

## PCS Coordination

When PCS orders are pending or under consideration.

- Coordinate with EFMP coordinator on assignment options.
- Verify the proposed location supports the family's needs.
- Coordinate with the gaining EFMP coordinator before the move.
- Resolve any assignment conflicts before the orders execute.

A PCS to a location without EFMP support for the family's needs is a PCS the chain has to walk back, often after the family has already moved.

## Briefing the Chain

The chain may not know which Marines have EFMP enrollments. Brief at the appropriate level.

- Section leader to company-level on EFMP-enrolled Marines in the section.
- Chain awareness for assignment decisions.
- Coordination with S-3 on deployment considerations.

EFMP does not block deployment but may affect family support during the deployment.

## When the Family's Needs Change

EFMP is not static.

- New diagnosis. Update the enrollment.
- Resolution or improvement. Re-evaluate the level.
- Birth of additional special needs family member. Add to enrollment.
- Family member ages out or transitions out of services. Update.

Annual review with the coordinator catches the changes.

## Common EFMP Failures

- Marine never told about EFMP, family member eligible but not enrolled.
- PCS orders cut without EFMP coordination, family arrives to no support.
- Enrollment level not updated as family's needs change.
- Chain unaware of EFMP-driven constraints.
- Deployment surprises family already stressed by special needs.
