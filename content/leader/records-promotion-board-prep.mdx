## Why Board Prep Matters

Promotion to E-6 (Staff Sergeant) and above requires HQMC selection board. Per MCO P1400.32<Citation source="MCO P1400.32" /> Volume 2, the board reviews each candidate's record. Strong preparation produces stronger outcomes. Weak preparation costs Marines opportunities.

For E-2 through E-5, promotion is via composite score cutting score. Your prep here is just as important.

## Your Role in Board Prep

You bridge between Marine and the promotion process.

### For Composite Score Promotions (E-2 through E-5)

- Verify eligibility per cycle
- Optimize composite score factors
- Address documentation gaps
- Ensure required training complete

### For SNCO Selection Boards (E-6 and above)

- Verify records 90+ days before board
- Optimize Master Brief Sheet (MBS)
- Coordinate fitness reports
- Coach Marine on appearance

### For Officer Selection Boards

- Similar coordination for officers
- Officer-specific factors
- Coordinate fitness reports
- Career planning

## Composite Score Optimization (E-2 through E-5)

For Marines eligible for cutting score promotion.

### Composite Score Factors

Per MCO P1400.32<Citation source="MCO P1400.32" /> Volume 2.

| Factor | Description | Optimization |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Time in Service | Points per month TIS | Automatic |
| Time in Grade | Points per month TIG | Automatic |
| Conduct Marks | Quarterly conduct | Maintain 4.0 or higher |
| Proficiency Marks | Quarterly proficiency | Maintain 4.0 or higher |
| Education | High school, college credit | Pursue education |
| Awards | Personal and unit awards | Submit deserved awards |
| Rifle Score | Annual qualification | Train and qualify high |
| PFT | Annual physical fitness | Train and score high |
| CFT | Annual combat fitness | Train and score high |

Each factor adds points. Higher composite = above cutting score = promotion.

### Section-Level Composite Score Coaching

For each Marine.

- Pull current composite score from MOL
- Identify weak factors
- Develop improvement plan
- Track improvement

### Common Score Boosters

Quick wins.

- Posting earned but unposted awards
- Submitting school certificates
- Resubmitting Pro/Con marks if missed
- Improving PFT/CFT during cycle

## SNCO Selection Board Preparation

For E-6 and above. Board convenes annually or biennially.

### Records to Verify

90 days before board.

- Master Brief Sheet (MBS) accurate
- All fitness reports posted
- All awards posted
- All school certificates posted
- All Page 11 entries appropriate
- Specific MOS qualifications current

### Member-to-Member Audit

Pair board candidates with each other.

- Cross-check each other's records
- Identify gaps
- Coordinate corrections

### Pre-Board Counseling

Brief candidate on board process.

- Board reviews MBS and OMPF
- Specific factors weighted
- Member's professional history
- Specific recommendations

### Optimize Fitness Report Inputs

For SNCO and officer candidates.

- Reviewing Officer (RO) and Reviewing Officer's RO have time
- Coordinate fitness report quality
- Specific section recommendations

## Performance Steps

### Step 1 - Identify Approaching Eligibility

For each Marine in your section.

- Pull current grade and TIG/TIS
- Calculate eligibility timeline
- Identify upcoming boards

### Step 2 - Audit Eligibility Roster

For each promotion cycle.

- Pull eligibility roster from MOL
- Verify each member on roster
- Identify members expected but not on roster
- Coordinate discrepancies with S-1

### Step 3 - Composite Score Review

For E-2 through E-5 candidates.

- Pull current composite scores
- Identify weak factors
- Develop improvement plan with member
- Track improvement

### Step 4 - SNCO Records Review

For E-6 board candidates.

- Pull MBS and review
- Identify gaps
- Coordinate corrections via S-1
- Pre-board audit pair

### Step 5 - Coach Member on Board

For SNCO candidates.

- Brief on board process
- Discuss expectations
- Address specific concerns
- Build candidate confidence

### Step 6 - Coordinate Required Training

For specific promotion requirements.

- Career schools (NCO Academy, Career Course)
- MOS-specific schools
- Required certifications
- Schedule before board if possible

### Step 7 - Track Outcomes

After board or cutting score.

- Promoted: Coordinate warrant and pay change
- Not promoted: Counsel on next cycle preparation
- Document outcomes for section tracker

## Common Board Prep Issues

### Composite Score Too Low

Member below cutting score.

- Identify specific factors
- Address quickly
- Wait for next cycle if not addressable

### Missing Documentation

Member's records have gaps.

- Coordinate corrections via S-1
- Specific procedures per gap type
- Time required for processing

### Pro/Con Mark Gaps

Member missing quarters.

- Submit retroactive marks if appropriate
- Specific procedures per gap
- Coordinate with command

### Education Credits Not Posted

Member completed school but not posted.

- Submit school cert via S-1
- OMPF posting via UD/MIPS
- Verify before next cycle

### Awards Not Posted

Member earned awards not in record.

- Submit award documentation
- Specific procedures
- May affect composite score

### Member Not Recommending

Commander not recommending.

- Specific Page 11 documenting
- Member acknowledges
- Coordinate alternate options

### Mentorship Connection

- Specific mentorship opportunities
- Career network building

## Will Not Promote Process

Per MCO P1070.12<Citation source="MCO P1070.12" /> and P1400.32.

### When to Use

Member should not be promoted.

- Specific performance concerns
- Specific disciplinary issues
- Specific other reasons

### Process

- Page 11 documenting reason
- Member acknowledges
- Submit "Will Not Promote" via MOL
- Member's record reflects

### Counsel Member

- Brief on reasons
- Specific improvement opportunities
- Future board considerations
- Maintain professional relationship

## Coordination Touchpoints

Promotion prep touches.

- **S-1 admin chief**: For records corrections
- **Career planner**: For career counseling
- **Commander**: For recommendations
- **HQMC career schools**: For training
- **MCRSC**: For Reserve-specific issues

## Related Roles

Promotion prep connects across roles.

- **Marines** verify their records and prepare for boards.
- **Admin** at S-1 processes records and submits warrants.
- **Commanders** sign warrants and make recommendations.

Your prep work directly affects your section's promotion success.
