## Mentorship Is Voluntary

Per MCO 1500.61<Citation source="MCO 1500.61" /> paragraph 4.c.(1)(d), mentorship is voluntary. <Citation source="MCO 1500.61 4.c.(1)(d)" /> A directed mentorship is no mentorship. The Marine gets a check-the-box relationship. Both sides resent it.

Your job is to propose, not to assign.

## When to Propose a Mentorship

Mentorship fits when:

- The Marine has cleared early integration and is in a development phase.
- A specific career path or life decision sits ahead.
- A peer mentor is not the right fit.
- A mentor outside the immediate chain offers commonality.

Mentorship does not fit when:

- The Marine is in early integration. Sponsor and immediate chain handle that.
- The relationship would cross fraternization lines.
- The proposed mentor has no capacity.

## Proposing the Pairing

Brief both sides separately.

- Brief the prospective mentor on the Marine's situation, the development goal, and the ask. Confirm the mentor has capacity.
- Brief the Marine on the prospective mentor and the rationale. Confirm the Marine wants the relationship.
- If both agree, introduce them and step back.
- If either declines, do not push.

The introduction is the leader's last touch. The relationship belongs to the two parties.

## Setting Initial Conditions

When you introduce the pair, set:

- Frequency expectation. Monthly is typical.
- Format. In-person, phone, written.
- Topic frame. The development goal anchors the early conversations.
- Expiration check. Revisit at 90 days to confirm the relationship works.

Do not script the conversations. The mentor and the Marine own the content.

## The 90-Day Audit

At 90 days, ask the Marine.

- Has the mentorship met monthly?
- Has the relationship felt useful?
- Where has it helped most?
- Should it continue?

Ask the mentor parallel questions. If both want it to continue, step back. If either has reservations, talk through whether to dissolve or adjust.

## When Mentorship Fails

Common failure modes.

- Forced pairing. Both sides going through the motions.
- Wrong commonality. Mentor and Marine have nothing real to discuss.
- Mentor capacity drains. Mentor stops showing up.
- Marine stops asking questions. Relationship withers.

Dissolve a failing mentorship cleanly. Do not let it drift.

## Where Mentorship Fits in the Counseling Cycle

Counseling is mandatory. Mentorship is supplementary. The mentor does not replace the section leader's counseling cadence. The mentor adds a perspective the section leader cannot, often outside the immediate chain.

The Marine carrying both regular counseling and a working mentorship gets development from two angles.
