## Indicators You Watch For

Financial distress shows in patterns.

- Payday loan or high-rate credit cycling.
- GTCC delinquency or suspension.
- Repeated allotment changes attempting to manage cash flow.
- Marine asking peers to lend money.
- Selling personal items to make ends meet.
- Bankruptcy paperwork at the unit.
- Garnishment surfacing on LES.
- Family member calls to the unit about money.
- Performance shifts you trace back to financial stress at home.

A single indicator may be a moment. Multiple indicators or a pattern is distress.

## The First Conversation

Approach the Marine in private. Drop the formality.

- Acknowledge what you have observed.
- Ask, do not assume the cause.
- Brief the resources.
- Leave the Marine with options, not directives.

The Marine in financial distress often carries shame. Lead with respect.

## The Resource Set

| Resource | Use |
|---|---|
| Command Financial Specialist (CFS) | Confidential financial counseling at the unit |
| Personal Financial Management Program (PFMP) | Base-level financial counseling and education |
| Navy-Marine Corps Relief Society (NMCRS) | Emergency assistance loans and grants |
| Chaplain | Spiritual support, sometimes emergency funds |
| Legal | Court-related financial matters |

For most Marines, the CFS or PFMP is the first stop.

## NMCRS Emergency Assistance

If the Marine has an acute need (rent due tomorrow, car payment to keep transportation, family emergency).

- Refer to NMCRS.
- NMCRS provides loans, grants, or budget counseling.
- Process is typically same-day for genuine emergencies.

A Marine choosing payday loans because they did not know NMCRS existed is a Marine the section did not brief well.

## Coordinating With Command

Some financial issues require chain awareness.

- Bankruptcy. Coordinate with legal and command.
- Garnishment. Coordinate with S-1.
- Court-ordered support delinquency. Coordinate with legal.
- Continuing pattern triggering Force Preservation. Coordinate with chain.

Brief the chain at the appropriate level.

## Briefing the Section on Prevention

Quarterly section brief on financial wellness.

- TSP and savings contribution patterns.
- Emergency fund building.
- Allotment best practices.
- Resource access (CFS, PFMP, NMCRS).
- Common pitfalls.

A section brief that prevents one Marine from a payday loan cycle saves that Marine months of recovery.

## When the Pattern Persists

A Marine receiving CFS counseling who continues showing distress indicators warrants Force Preservation referral.

- Document the pattern.
- Coordinate with chain.
- Submit the referral.
- Implement council recommendations.

Sustained financial distress affects performance, deployment readiness, and family stability.

## Common Financial Distress Failures

- Indicator dismissed as Marine being immature with money.
- CFS referral made but never followed up.
- Marine pursues payday loans because resources never briefed.
- Spouse calling unit treated as a complaint rather than an indicator.
- Section never briefs prevention.
