## Resolutions Are For Civilians, Missions Are For Marines

Discipline beats motivation. Motivation fades. A SMART mission survives the bad week.

A mission you have no way to define is a mission already lost. SMART forces the definition.

## The Five Tests

### S - Specific

Define the exact target. No fluff. No generalities.

- Weak. "I want to get better at land nav."
- Strong. "I will master terrain association and pace counts on lensatic and GPS."

### M - Measurable

Numbers. Time, weight, dollars, scores.

- Weak. "Improve the run."
- Strong. "Lower the 3-mile run from 21:00 to 19:30 by 1 August."

With no number, you have no measure. With no measure, you have no management.

### A - Achievable

A reality check against MOS, OpTempo, deployment cycle, and field schedule.

- Push the Marine. Do not break them.
- Account for upcoming field ops, deployments, and schools.
- Reject "gimme" goals the Marine clears in a week.

### R - Relevant

Tie the goal to the Marine, the unit, or the long-term career path. The "so what" test.

- Does the goal make the Marine stronger?
- Does it serve the unit?
- Does it advance PME, billet readiness, or transition?

### T - Time-Bound

A drop-dead date. A goal without a date is a wish.

## The Mission-Order Sentence

Combine the five tests into one sentence.

> By [Date], I will [Specific Action] as measured by [Metric] in order to [Relevance].

## Worked Examples Across the Six F's

| F | Goal sentence |
|---|---|
| Fitness | "By the June PFT, I will achieve 285 or higher by training 5 days a week to remain competitive for the promotion board." |
| Finances | "By December 2026, I will save $5,000 in TSP by setting a $400 monthly allotment to build a deployment-ready emergency fund." |
| Fighter | "By 1 October 2026, I will complete Spanish Level 1 on MarineNet ahead of the SOUTHCOM deployment to support unit interoperability." |
| Family | "By 30 June 2026, I will refresh the NAVMC 10922<Citation source="NAVMC 10922" /> and Family Care Plan within 10 working days of every dependent change to keep my section deployable." |
| Fidelity | "By the next heritage observance, I will brief the platoon on the Battle of Belleau Wood for 20 minutes to reinforce unit identity." |
| Future | "By EAS minus 18 months, I will complete the Transition Readiness Program with a signed credential plan to land a job inside 60 days of separation." |

## Friction Points and Support

A SMART goal collapses without a plan for friction.

- Identify the primary obstacle. OpTempo, budget, discipline, family.
- Name an accountability partner. Peer or NCO who checks the metric on a fixed cadence.
- Set quarterly milestones inside any annual goal.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Carry the mission-order voice into every session">
Use the mission-order sentence as the standard format for Future-area targets in every counseling session. The Marine builds fluency with the format and the goals get sharper over time.
</Callout>

## Where SMART Lives in the Counseling Cycle

- Initial Counseling Session. Build the first 3 to 5 SMART targets across the F's.
- Follow-on. Review prior targets, retire the completed, modify the slipping, add the new.
- Event-driven. A PCS, a promotion, a new billet resets the target list.
