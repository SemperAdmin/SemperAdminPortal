## Education Categories

| Program | Use |
|---|---|
| Tuition Assistance (TA) | Active duty pursuit of college courses |
| Post-9/11 GI Bill | Higher education during or after service |
| Montgomery GI Bill | Alternative GI Bill option |
| MarineNet | Free distance learning, MOS and PME |
| MCI | Free distance learning courses |
| MCU | Marine Corps University courses, PME |
| Credentialing programs | Civilian credentials with DoD funding |

A Marine's education plan often stacks multiple programs across years.

## Tuition Assistance

TA covers most of the cost of college courses for active duty.

- Eligibility per current MARADMIN.
- Annual fiscal year cap on dollars.
- Per-course cap.
- Approval through Education Office before enrollment.
- Grade requirements to keep eligibility.

A Marine using TA every fiscal year through a 5-year contract can accumulate substantial credit. Most Marines underutilize.

## GI Bill Timing

The Post-9/11 GI Bill has critical timing decisions.

- During service. Available for use, but TA often more cost-effective if the Marine can take it on duty time.
- Transfer of benefits to spouse or children. Requires service obligation. Decide early.
- After service. Full benefit including monthly housing allowance and book stipend.
- Yellow Ribbon Program. Adds private school coverage.

A Marine surfacing the GI Bill conversation at 6 months from EAS has missed the transfer window if they wanted to give it to a family member.

## MarineNet and MCI

Free distance learning. Use cases.

- PME progression for promotion eligibility.
- MOS skill development.
- Off-duty professional development.
- TA prerequisite courses.

Track section MarineNet completion as part of training requirements oversight.

## MCU and Resident PME

For SNCOs and officers, resident PME progression is career-shaping.

- Sgts Course, Career Course, Advanced Course (enlisted).
- TBS, MOS school, Career Course, Command and Staff College (officer).
- Coordinate timing with billet cycles.

Marines selected for resident PME typically have strong APES records. Education planning supports the path to selection.

## Credentialing

Many MOSes have civilian credentials available with DoD funding through programs like COOL.

- Specific credentials per MOS.
- Application through the Education Office.
- Timing during service or transition.
- Stackability with the GI Bill.

A Marine separating with civilian-recognized credentials transitions into employment faster.

## Coordinating With the Education Office

The Education Office owns the formal education planning conversation.

- Refer Marines for personalized planning.
- Coordinate with the Office on TA approvals.
- Receive Office briefings on program changes.
- Support Marines through enrollment.

The leader supports. The Office plans. The Marine executes.

## Common Education Planning Failures

- Marine never told about TA eligibility.
- TA approved but Marine never enrolls.
- GI Bill transfer window missed.
- MarineNet completion drifts, PME blocks promotion.
- Credentialing programs unknown to Marine and section.
