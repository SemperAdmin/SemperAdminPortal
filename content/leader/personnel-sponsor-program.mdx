## Why Sponsorship Matters

Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" />, the Marine Corps Sponsorship Program (MCSP) sets the Marine's first impression of the new unit.<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> A strong sponsor saves the Marine 60 days of administrative friction and three weeks of housing search anxiety. A weak sponsor leaves the Marine to figure it out alone.

You own the sponsor assignment. The sponsor owns the Marine's check-in. The unit's Sponsorship Coordinator (SC) coordinates the program above the section level.

## The 10-Working-Day Welcome Aboard Rule

Per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" />, Sponsors prepare Welcome Aboard letters for inbound Marines within 10 working days of sponsorship assignment. Sample letters live in MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" /> enclosures (4) for CONUS, (5) for accompanied OCONUS, and (6) for unaccompanied OCONUS. The Section's audit point is the 10-working-day mark.

## NAVMC 11791

The unit provides NAVMC 11791<Citation source="NAVMC 11791" /> (Sponsorship Questionnaire) to newly arrived Marines. Completion is optional but encouraged. The unit retains returned questionnaires for two years per the records schedule cited in MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" />.

A section running quarterly Welcome Aboard reviews using NAVMC 11791<Citation source="NAVMC 11791" /> builds a feedback loop on sponsor effectiveness.

## Assignment Criteria

Match on:

- Rank tier. Cpl gets a Sgt sponsor. Sgt gets a SSgt sponsor. SNCO gets a peer or one rank above.
- MOS. Same MOS where possible, same shop where not.
- Family status. Married Marine gets a married sponsor. Marine with kids gets a sponsor with kids. Single Marine gets a single peer.
- Geography. Sponsor lives in the same housing area or knows it.

Mismatched sponsorship loses the leverage of shared experience.

## Sponsor Brief Before Contact

Before the sponsor reaches out to the inbound Marine, brief the sponsor on:

- Inbound Marine's rank, MOS, family status, expected report date.
- Sponsor responsibilities per MCO 1320.11H<Citation source="MCO 1320.11H" />.
- Section expectations on cadence of contact.
- Required handoff items (welcome aboard packet, housing brief, in-processing checklist).
- Section's standard for a "good" sponsor experience.

A sponsor who learns the duties from a checklist after first contact has already missed the first impression.

## Inside the First 7 Days

Verify sponsor contact happened.

- Sponsor reached out to the inbound Marine.
- Sponsor answered initial questions.
- Sponsor sent the in-processing checklist.
- Sponsor coordinated meet-and-greet for the report date.

If contact has not happened, replace the sponsor. Do not let a weak start drag.

## At the New Marine's Day-30 Counseling

During the initial counseling session at day 30, ask:

- Did your sponsor reach out before report?
- Did your sponsor walk you through check-in?
- Did your sponsor introduce you to the section?
- Where did the sponsor relationship help most?
- Where did it fall short?

Document the answer. Use the feedback to assign the next sponsor better.

## Common Sponsor Failures

- Assignment late, sponsor learns of duty after the Marine has already arrived.
- Mismatched assignment, sponsor has no shared experience.
- No section brief to the sponsor on duties.
- No follow-up at day 7 or day 30.
- Sponsor never replaced when the relationship is not working.
