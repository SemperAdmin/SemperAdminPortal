## Pre-Session Self-Check

Run before every session.

- Is the room neutral, quiet, free of interruptions?
- Are you mentally clear of unrelated tasks?
- Do you have records, notes, and a written agenda?
- Have you checked your bias against this Marine?
- Have you set time aside, no calls, no walk-ins?

A weak self-check produces a weak session.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 5002.2" />

## Junior Pitfalls

Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 5001.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 5001" />

| Pattern | Signal | Counter |
|---|---|---|
| Nervousness | Fidgeting, eye avoidance, tight voice | Open with praise, slow the pace, confirm the agenda |
| Blanket agreement | "Yes" to every prompt | Switch to open-end questions, ask for an opinion |
| Disagreement | Pushes back on an evaluation | Treat as legitimate, explore with questions |
| Persistent argument | Denies and rebuts continuously | Separate emotion from facts, refocus on objective events |
| Blame-shifting | Points at peers, leadership, OpTempo | Get facts on the table, redirect to ownership |
| Discouragement | Withdrawn, flat affect | Anchor on a recent strength, look for the underlying cause |

## Senior Pitfalls

Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 5002.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 5002" />

| Pattern | Signal | Counter |
|---|---|---|
| Halo effect | One trait coloring every judgment | Force-list specific events from notebook, weigh each on its own |
| Personal bias | Stereotype shaping the read | Pull peer comparison data, audit your own pattern over months |
| Inflexible style | Same directive script every session | Read the Marine, switch styles inside the session |
| Monologue | You are doing 70 percent of the talking | Pause, ask a probing question, hand the floor back |
| Emotionally charged words | Marine reacts defensively to wording | Strip emotion from the language, focus on facts |
| Jumping to conclusions | Generalization from a single event | Build the conclusion from three or more concrete events |
| Vague targets | "Work on it," "do better" | Rewrite with action verb, object, standard |
| No follow-up | Marine never sees you check the targets | Schedule the next session before the current one ends |

## Two Combined Failure Modes

- Marine arrives nervous. You open with criticism. The session ends with the Marine more guarded than at the start. The pre-session self-check would have caught the bias toward leading with the negative.
- Marine arrives ready. You arrive unprepared. The agenda dies in the first five minutes. The Marine reads the lack of prep as a lack of care.

<Callout variant="danger" title="The follow-up debt">
A counseling session with no follow-up is a documented broken promise. Marines remember the targets you set. They notice when you never check. Burn through too many of those and the leader notebook becomes paper drill.<Citation source="MCO 1500.61 5.b.(1)" />
</Callout>
