## Why AT Section Planning Matters

AT is the year's primary block of active duty training for SELRES. Per MCRAMM Chapter 7, AT serves member's annual obligation, unit collective training, and operational readiness validation.

A well-planned AT.

- Achieves clear training objectives
- Maximizes time for individual and section development
- Prepares unit for mobilization potential
- Validates MOS proficiency
- Builds section cohesion

A poorly planned AT wastes 12-14 days of valuable training time.

## Your Role in Section AT Planning

Each phase has specific tasks.

### Pre-AT Planning (60-90 days out)

- Define section training objectives
- Coordinate scheduling per member
- Identify resources needed
- Coordinate with unit AT plan

### Pre-AT Preparation (30 days out)

- Verify each Marine's readiness
- Address any gaps
- Coordinate travel
- Brief Marines on AT expectations

### During AT Execution

- Lead section daily
- Track training accomplishment
- Address issues as they arise
- Coordinate with unit leadership

### Post-AT Documentation

- Document training completion
- Submit awards if earned
- Update training records
- Lessons learned for next AT

## Pre-AT Planning Steps

### Step 1 - Define Section Objectives

What does your section need to accomplish?

- Specific MOS training
- Specific equipment training
- Specific certification renewals
- Specific section-level objectives
- Specific individual development

Write objectives down. Brief section leadership for alignment.

### Step 2 - Coordinate Scheduling

For each Marine in your section.

- Verify AT availability
- Identify any conflicts
- Coordinate civilian employer notification
- Coordinate family preparation

### Step 3 - Identify Resources

For section objectives.

- Training space requirements
- Equipment needs
- Specific training aids
- Coordination with other sections

### Step 4 - Coordinate with Unit AT Plan

Your section's plan within unit's overall AT.

- Specific block of training time
- Specific resource sharing
- Specific dependencies
- Coordinate with unit operations

## Pre-AT Preparation Steps

- Medical issues addressed
- Required training completed

- Specific issues resolved

### Step 3 - Coordinate Travel

For Marines requiring travel.

- Per diem coordination

### Step 4 - Brief Marines

Pre-AT brief for section.

- AT objectives and expectations

## Common AT Pitfalls

### No Specific Objectives

AT becomes "showing up" rather than purposeful training.

- Define specific objectives in advance
- Brief section on objectives
- Track accomplishment

### Poor Pre-AT Preparation

Marines arrive unprepared.

- Pre-AT readiness verification critical
- Address gaps before AT starts
- Set clear expectations

### Underutilized Time

Time wasted during AT.

- Plan training to fill time
- Identify productive activities
- Avoid unnecessary downtime

### No Post-AT Follow-Up

Training accomplished but not documented.

- Document each completed task
- Update records
- Submit awards earned

## Related Roles

AT planning connects through chain.

- **Marines** complete AT and contribute to section objectives.
- **Admin** at I&I processes AT orders and pay.
- **Commanders** approve AT plans and review outcomes.

Your section's AT execution determines unit AT success.
