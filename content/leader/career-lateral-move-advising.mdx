## When to Surface Lateral Move

A Marine fits the lateral move conversation when.

- Performance is solid but the Marine seems disengaged from the MOS.
- The Marine has talent or interest aligning with a different MOS.
- The current MOS is over-strength.
- Promotion outlook in the current MOS is poor.
- Family needs an MOS-driven duty station change.

Lateral moves are not for Marines failing the current MOS. They are for Marines whose talent fits better elsewhere.

## The 18-Month Lead Time

Lateral moves take time. Open the conversation at the next reenlistment window or earlier.

- Identification at 18 months from decision point.
- Coordination with career planner at 12 months.
- Application at 6 to 9 months.
- School selection and orders at 3 to 6 months.

A Marine surfacing lateral move interest at 6 months from EAS has compressed all of this.

## The Conversation

In counseling sessions.

- What do you enjoy about your current MOS?
- What frustrates you?
- If you could pick any MOS in the Corps, which one?
- What experience or interest pulls you that direction?
- Have you talked to anyone in that MOS?

Listen for the gap between what the Marine does and what the Marine wants to do.

## Career Planner Coordination

The career planner owns the formal lateral move process.

- Brief the planner on the Marine's interest.
- Receive the planner's eligibility assessment.
- Coordinate the application timing.
- Support the Marine through the package.

Lateral move opportunities follow current MARADMIN guidance on which MOSes accept moves and what eligibility requires.

## Eligibility Considerations

Common factors.

- Time in current MOS (often a minimum requirement).
- Performance in current MOS.
- ASVAB or specific test scores for the gaining MOS.
- Medical and physical qualifications for the gaining MOS.
- Security clearance level if the gaining MOS requires.

A Marine whose performance is weak in the current MOS rarely qualifies for a competitive lateral move.

## Family and Geographic Considerations

Lateral moves often drive PCS to the gaining MOS school and subsequent assignment.

- Brief the Marine and family on the timeline.
- Coordinate with EFMP if family has special needs.
- Discuss the impact on the Marine's transition timeline if reenlistment also in play.

The lateral move can extend the Marine's career commitment as part of the package.

## When the Move Approves

After approval.

- Coordinate the school assignment.
- Coordinate the duty status during the transition.
- Brief the Marine on the gaining MOS expectations.
- Connect the Marine with peers in the gaining MOS.

Send the Marine into the new MOS with awareness, not just orders.

## When the Move Denies

Disapproval is part of the process.

- Document the denial reason.
- Coordinate with the career planner on alternatives.
- Reassess in 6 to 12 months if eligibility shifts.
- Support the Marine's continued performance in the current MOS.

A Marine whose lateral move denied who continues to perform is a Marine the section keeps developed.

## Common Lateral Move Failures

- Marine surfaces interest at 6 months from EAS, no time to execute.
- Career planner not engaged early.
- Section pushes Marine toward a lateral move the Marine has not chosen.
- Family considerations not surfaced.
- Disapproval treated as a relationship failure.
