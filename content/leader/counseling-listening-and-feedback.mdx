## Six Question Types

Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 4003.1, six question types cover the counseling field.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4003.1" />

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Closed-end | Yes or no | "Did you take the test?" |
| Open-end | Free response | "How is the work going?" |
| Echo | Repeat a word | "You got no response?" |
| Probing | Ask for cause | "Why do you think this happened?" |
| Interpretive | Clarify meaning | "Does this mean you have to start over?" |
| Confirming | Gain commitment | "So your platoon is ready?" |

Default to open-end during the body of the session. Save closed-end for facts and final commitment.

## Active Listening

Listening is half the work. Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 4003.2, active listening reads the verbal and the nonverbal.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4003.2" />

Verbal cues to watch.

- Tone shifts higher or lower.
- Hesitation or stammer over a topic.
- Generalities replacing specifics.
- Volume drop.

Nonverbal cues to watch.

- Eye avoidance.
- Slumped or rigid posture.
- Clenched fists.
- Foot tapping.

When you catch a cue, surface it.

- Restate. "You said the work feels heavier this month."
- Paraphrase. "Working with the new section sergeant has been harder than you expected."
- Support. "I understand why you reached the conclusion."

## Four Feedback Rules

Per NAVMC 2795<Citation source="NAVMC 2795" /> paragraph 4003.3, feedback works inside four constraints.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4003.3" />

1. Specific. Anchor to events and actions, not the person or personality.
2. Balanced. Strengths and successes alongside weaknesses. Praise is the strongest motivator on the floor.
3. Target-anchored. Feedback ties to the targets set at the last session.
4. Timely. Close to the event, while memory is fresh.

## Praise Is Not Optional

A counseling session weighted only on deficiencies builds a Marine who hides from sessions.<Citation source="NAVMC 2795 4003.3.b.(2)" /> Use praise where the Marine has earned it. Use praise as a deliberate tactic, not an afterthought.

## Pitfall - Confusing Feedback for Lecturing

If your feedback runs longer than the Marine's response, you have shifted from counseling to lecturing. Cut the feedback to a paragraph. Hand the conversation back.
