## What GTCC Delinquency Costs

Government Travel Charge Card delinquency runs through stages.

- 30 days past due. Account flagged.
- 60 days past due. Card suspended.
- 90 days past due. Account in delinquent status, command notified.
- 120 days past due. Salary offset may begin per policy.
- Continued delinquency. Potential UCMJ exposure for misuse.

A Marine with a delinquent GTCC carries financial, professional, and legal exposure.

## Identifying Delinquency Early

S-1 or the unit Agency Program Coordinator (APC) provides regular GTCC status reports.

- Pull the report monthly.
- Identify any Marine with past-due balance.
- Identify any Marine approaching the next stage.
- Coordinate intervention before the next stage hits.

A Marine at 25 days past due has a different fix path than a Marine at 95 days past due.

## Briefing the Marine

When you identify the delinquency.

- Pull the Marine into a private conversation.
- Confirm the delinquency status.
- Identify the cause (voucher not submitted, voucher rejected, personal financial issue).
- Coordinate the fix path.

The conversation is leader to Marine, not punishment. Punishment surfaces only if pattern or misuse is established.

## Common Delinquency Causes

- Travel voucher not submitted post-trip. Marine paid the GTCC bill out of pocket waiting for reimbursement.
- Voucher submitted but rejected, Marine did not resubmit.
- Voucher approved but split disbursement not set, GTCC charges remain outstanding.
- Personal use of GTCC outside official travel (potential misuse).
- Personal financial issue affecting GTCC payment.

Each cause has a different fix.

## Voucher Not Submitted

The fastest fix.

- Walk the Marine through DTS voucher submission.
- Coordinate with S-1 if voucher is past the policy window.
- Once approved, payment routes to GTCC.
- Verify card restoration.

## Split Disbursement Not Set

A common gap. Default split disbursement routes the GTCC-charged portion of voucher payment directly to the GTCC. If not set.

- Travel pay arrives at the Marine's bank account.
- The Marine then has to pay the GTCC manually.
- Payment delays produce delinquency.

Coach the Marine to set split disbursement on every voucher.

## Personal Use of GTCC

GTCC is for official travel only. Personal use is misuse.

- Brief the Marine on the policy.
- Document the misuse.
- Coordinate with the chain on appropriate action.
- Continued misuse may produce administrative or UCMJ action.

## Personal Financial Issue

If the Marine cannot pay due to personal financial distress.

- Refer to Command Financial Specialist or PFMP.
- Coordinate with chaplain or NMCRS for emergency assistance.
- Coordinate a payment plan with GTCC issuing bank if needed.
- Surface as Force Preservation indicator if pattern persists.

A Marine with sustained financial distress has more going on than the GTCC. The GTCC is the visible symptom.

## Tracking Restoration

After the payment plan engages.

- Verify GTCC payment posted.
- Verify card status changes from suspended to active.
- Verify no continued delinquency.
- Coordinate with APC on closure.

## Common Delinquency Failures

- Section monitors GTCC reports late or not at all.
- Marine briefed but no fix path coordinated.
- Voucher resubmission deferred, balance continues to age.
- Misuse pattern not addressed, escalates.
- Personal financial distress treated as a GTCC issue, root cause never surfaced.

## Where to Go Next

- Pay problems escort for the broader pay error walk-through.
- Personal financial management program referral.
- Force preservation referral if patterns escalate.
