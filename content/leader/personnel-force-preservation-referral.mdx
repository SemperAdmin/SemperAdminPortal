## What Force Preservation Does

Per MCO 1500.60<Citation source="MCO 1500.60" />, Force Preservation Councils review Marines exhibiting indicators of risk and coordinate intervention before the indicators escalate to crisis or separation. <Citation source="MCO 1500.60 Ch 3" />

The council pulls together S-1, command, chaplain, behavioral health, legal, and other resources. The leader provides the initial referral and the documented observations.

## Indicators That Warrant Referral

The active MARADMIN and MCO 1500.60<Citation source="MCO 1500.60" /> list the qualifying indicators. Common patterns include:

- Repeated unauthorized absence or disciplinary actions.
- Substance abuse incidents.
- Financial distress with command involvement.
- Mental health concerns with command awareness.
- Family disruption affecting performance.
- Suicidal ideation reported or observed.
- Domestic violence indicators.
- Pattern of poor performance after a stable history.

A single indicator does not always warrant a referral. A pattern across two or more indicators does.

## What You Document Before Referral

The Force Preservation Council needs facts.

- Specific dates and events.
- Observable behaviors. Not interpretations.
- Counseling sessions where the Marine raised or you raised concerns.
- Resource referrals already attempted (chaplain, MFLC, financial counselor).
- Marine's stated awareness of the concerns.

A referral built on observations beats a referral built on interpretations. The council reads facts.

## Coordinate With the Chain Before Routing

Before submitting the referral.

- Brief your immediate senior.
- Brief the company-level leader.
- Confirm the chain agrees the referral is the right move.
- Walk the documentation up the chain so each level knows the basis.

A referral that surprises the chain reads as a section running outside the chain's awareness.

## After the Council Reviews

The council typically returns:

- A determination of risk level.
- Recommended interventions and resource referrals.
- A monitoring cadence.
- A reporting back schedule.

You implement the recommendations at the section level.

- Schedule the resource appointments.
- Adjust the Marine's counseling cadence.
- Track the monitoring marks.
- Report back to the council on cadence.

## When the Marine Is in Acute Crisis

A Marine in acute crisis (suicidal ideation expressed, domestic violence in progress, immediate safety concerns) does not wait for the next council meeting.

- Coordinate with chaplain or behavioral health immediately.
- Engage the chain.
- Use the Force Preservation referral as a documentation step alongside the urgent action.

Do not delay urgent care for the council process.

## Common Referral Failures

- Indicators ignored until the crisis.
- Documentation thin or interpretation-heavy.
- Referral submitted without chain awareness.
- No follow-up on council recommendations.
- Marine never told the referral occurred.
