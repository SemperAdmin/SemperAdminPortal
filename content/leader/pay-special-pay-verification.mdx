## Special Pay Categories

Common to track.

- Hazardous Duty Incentive Pay (HDIP).
- Hostile Fire Pay or Imminent Danger Pay (HFP/IDP).
- Sea Pay.
- Aviation Incentive Pay (flight pay).
- Foreign Language Proficiency Bonus (FLPB).
- Special Duty Assignment Pay (SDAP).
- Career Sea Pay Premium.
- Diving Duty Pay.
- Hazardous Duty Pay.

Each has eligibility criteria, start dates, stop dates, and rate tables. The active rate sits in DoD FMR Volume 7A<Citation source="DoD FMR Volume 7A" />.

## Identifying Eligibility

Surface eligibility through.

- Billet assignment that triggers the pay.
- Qualification earned (flight wings, dive, language).
- Duty location (sea duty, hostile fire zone, foreign language area).
- Special duty designation (drill instructor, recruiter, MSG).

A Marine arrives in your section already eligible. A Marine earns eligibility while in your section. A Marine loses eligibility on PCS or qualification expiration.

## Start Date Verification

When eligibility triggers.

- Verify the package routed to S-1.
- Verify the LES reflects the special pay starting on the eligibility date.
- Verify the rate matches the FMR table.
- Catch retroactive missing pay if start date pre-dated the LES entry.

A Marine eligible for HDIP from January 1 whose LES shows the pay starting February 15 has 6 weeks of missing pay.

## Continuing Eligibility

Some special pays require continuing qualification.

- FLPB requires periodic language testing.
- Flight pay requires continuing flight time.
- Dive pay requires continuing dive currency.
- SDAP requires continuing assignment.

Quarterly verification catches expirations the Marine forgot to address.

## Stop Date Verification

When eligibility ends.

- PCS out of the qualifying duty.
- Qualification expiration without renewal.
- Billet change.
- Reassignment out of the special duty.

Verify the stop date is correct. A pay that continues past the stop date may produce overpayment recovery later.

## Brief the Marine

Marines often do not know their special pay eligibility in detail.

- Brief on the active special pays during initial counseling.
- Brief on continuing requirements (testing, currency).
- Brief on stop triggers.

A Marine briefed sees the special pay on the LES and verifies it monthly.

## Coordinating Corrections

When the LES is wrong.

- Pull source documentation.
- Submit correction request through S-1.
- Track to resolution.
- Verify on next LES.

Special pay corrections often require Branch coordination beyond S-1.

## Common Special Pay Failures

- Eligibility triggers but package never routes.
- Rate calculated against old FMR table.
- Continuing qualification expires without notice.
- Stop date missed, overpayment surfaces months later.
- Marine never briefed, never self-verifies.
