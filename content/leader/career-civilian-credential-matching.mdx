## What Credential Matching Does

Each MOS develops skills that civilian employers recognize when documented through a recognized credential.

- Aviation maintenance maps to FAA certifications.
- Communications maps to CompTIA, Cisco, and similar IT credentials.
- Logistics maps to APICS, Six Sigma, project management.
- Medical maps to civilian EMT, RN, and specialist certifications.
- Mechanical maps to ASE and trade certifications.
- Administrative maps to office management and HR credentials.

A Marine separating with these credentials in hand has demonstrable, civilian-recognized capability.

## The Marine Corps COOL Program

Marine Corps COOL or its current equivalent maps MOSes to civilian credentials and provides funding.

- Credential identified for the Marine's MOS.
- Application through the Education Office.
- Funding for exam fees, study materials, and prep.
- Specific eligibility per current program guidance.

Pull the active program details from the Education Office.

## When to Pursue Credentials

During service is best.

- More time on duty for study.
- TA and credentialing program funding available.
- The Marine still works in the field daily.
- The credential supports continued service options too.

Post-service pursuit is harder. The Marine often has employment, family, and time pressures the credentialing process exposes.

## Section-Level Coaching

In counseling sessions.

- Ask about civilian career interest.
- Surface the credentials matching the Marine's MOS and interest.
- Coordinate with Education Office on the path.
- Track progress.

A Marine whose MOS doors close at separation often had unmet credential opportunity during service.

## Specific Credential Categories

Trade and technical.

- ASE for mechanical.
- FAA for aviation.
- Various trade unions and associations for skilled trades.
- IT vendor certs (CompTIA, Cisco, AWS, Microsoft).

Healthcare.

- EMT and paramedic.
- Pharmacy tech.
- LPN, RN with degree pathway.
- Specialty technician roles.

Business and management.

- PMP for project management.
- Six Sigma green and black belts.
- APICS for supply chain.
- HR credentials.

Pull the active mapping from Education Office for each Marine's MOS.

## SkillBridge and Transition

DoD SkillBridge allows transitioning Marines to participate in civilian work or training in the final months before EAS.

- Application typically 6 months out.
- Requires command approval.
- Marine remains on active duty pay during the SkillBridge period.
- Provides direct employment exposure.

Coordinate SkillBridge as part of the transition timeline.

## Documenting Credentials

Each credential earned.

- Record in the leader notebook.
- Verify entry in MOL or relevant records.
- Brief the Marine on inclusion in DD-214 supporting documentation.
- Coordinate documentation with the Education Office.

A Marine separating without documentation of earned credentials has done the work but cannot prove it on the resume.

## Common Credential Matching Failures

- Marine never told about COOL or successor program.
- Credentialing pursued at the last 90 days, no time to complete.
- Funding application incomplete.
- Credential earned but never documented.
- Section unaware of the MOS-to-credential mapping.
