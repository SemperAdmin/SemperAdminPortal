## What Family Readiness Covers

Beyond the Marine's individual readiness, family readiness covers.

- Family Care Plans for sole-parent and dual-military Marines.
- RED-S (Record of Emergency Data) contact information current.
- Dependent enrollment in DEERS current.
- Spouse contact for unit communications.
- Special needs (EFMP) coordination.
- Deployment readiness briefings.

Gaps surface when the deployment alert drops or a casualty notification fires.

## Family Care Plan Audit

Per MCO 1754.9A<Citation source="MCO 1754.9A" />, sole-parent and dual-military Marines maintain a current Family Care Plan. <Citation source="MCO 1754.9A" />

- Identify sole-parent and dual-military Marines in the section.
- Verify Family Care Plan on file with S-1.
- Verify the plan is current (annual review).
- Verify guardian and alternate guardian designated.
- Verify financial provisions documented.

A Family Care Plan that has not been reviewed in two years is a plan that has not survived life changes.

## RED-S Currency

Every Marine has a Record of Emergency Data. The Marine owns the data accuracy.

- Primary next of kin.
- Secondary next of kin.
- Beneficiary designations (SGLI separate).
- Emergency contact information.

Section coaches Marines to update RED-S after.

- Marriage.
- Divorce.
- Death of a designated contact.
- Move of designated contact.
- Birth or adoption.

Stale RED-S surfaces during casualty notification or emergency leave processing. By then, the gap costs the family.

## Spouse and Family Contact

Maintain a section-level family contact roster.

- Spouse name and contact information.
- Children names and ages.
- Special situations (deployed parent, ill family member, custody arrangements).

The roster supports unit communications, deployment briefings, and family readiness events. Coordinate with the Family Readiness Officer.

## EFMP Coordination

Marines with Exceptional Family Member Program enrollment require additional coordination.

- Verify enrollment current.
- Verify duty station appropriateness for family needs.
- Coordinate with EFMP coordinator for upcoming PCS.
- Coordinate with the unit on assignment limitations.

EFMP gaps surface late and PCS cycles can be derailed by misalignment.

## Deployment Briefings

Family briefings before deployment cover.

- Communication plans.
- Pay and allotment continuity.
- DEERS and ID card validity through the deployment.
- Power of attorney if needed.
- Family Readiness Officer contact.
- Resource referrals (chaplain, MFLC, MCCS).

Coordinate with the Family Readiness Officer on the briefing schedule.

## Common Family Readiness Failures

- Family Care Plan deferred year after year.
- RED-S last updated before a marriage ended.
- DEERS gap discovered at TRICARE appointment, not at the section.
- EFMP not surfaced until PCS orders cannot be executed.
- Family deployment briefing skipped, communications break down.
