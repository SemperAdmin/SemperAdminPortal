NAVMC 11000 Marine Verified Apr 14, 2026

NAVMC 11000 FAMILY DOCUMENTS

10-day window for marriage, baby, divorce, and death updates. The Record of Emergency Data and Dependency form keeps your pay, benefits, and notification correct. Late or wrong updates create audit findings and pay errors.

Effective2024-09-01
AuthorityMCO 1900.16
Reading time6 min
Source check
Always verify against MCO 1900.16 Vol 2 Ch 4 and the current NAVMC 11000 form before action. This summary aggregates open-source policy as of April 2026.

Why it matters

Your NAVMC 11000 record drives BAHMCO 7220.56, SGLI beneficiary payouts38 USC 1969, casualty notification, and dependent ID issuance. If your data is wrong on the day a real-world event happens, your family is at risk.

The 10-day rule

Per MCO 1900.16, you have 10 calendar days from the qualifying event to update your record. The clock starts on the event date, not the date you receive paperwork.

  • Marriage - 10 days from ceremony
  • Birth or adoption - 10 days from birth or court order
  • Divorce - 10 days from final decree
  • Death of dependent - 10 days from date of death

What you submit

Marriage

Provide the certified marriage certificate, your spouse's birth certificate, and the spouse's SSN card. Your S-1 or PAC verifies the originals and returns them. Photocopies are kept on file for OMPF inclusion.

Birth or adoption

Provide the certified birth certificate or court adoption order. The hospital-issued souvenir certificate does not satisfy the requirement.

Common errors

Audit hot spots
BAH-with-dependent rate paid after divorce is the largest single audit finding category in this area. Update before the next pay period to avoid recoupment.
